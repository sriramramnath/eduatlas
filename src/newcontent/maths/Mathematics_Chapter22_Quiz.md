## PART A: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS

### Question 1 [1 mark]

What is the gradient of the line $y = -3x + 5$?

A) $5$  
B) $-5$  
C) $3$  
D) $-3$

**Answer: D**

**Explanation:** In the equation $y = mx + c$, the coefficient of $x$ is the gradient. Here, $m = -3$.

---

### Question 2 [1 mark]

The line $y = 2x - 4$ crosses the $y$-axis at:

A) $(2, 0)$  
B) $(0, -4)$  
C) $(-4, 0)$  
D) $(0, 2)$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The $y$-intercept is found by setting $x = 0$. When $x = 0$: $y = 2(0) - 4 = -4$. So the point is $(0, -4)$.

---

### Question 3 [1 mark]

What is the gradient of the line passing through $(1, 3)$ and $(4, 9)$?

A) $2$  
B) $3$  
C) $\frac{1}{2}$  
D) $6$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} = \frac{9 - 3}{4 - 1} = \frac{6}{3} = 2$$

---

### Question 4 [1 mark]

A line parallel to $y = 4x - 7$ has gradient:

A) $-7$  
B) $-\frac{1}{4}$  
C) $4$  
D) $\frac{1}{4}$

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Parallel lines have the same gradient. The gradient of $y = 4x - 7$ is $4$.

---

### Question 5 [1 mark]

The gradient of a line perpendicular to $y = \frac{1}{3}x + 2$ is:

A) $3$  
B) $-3$  
C) $\frac{1}{3}$  
D) $-\frac{1}{3}$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** For perpendicular lines, $m_1 \times m_2 = -1$. If $m_1 = \frac{1}{3}$, then $m_2 = -\frac{1}{m_1} = -3$.

### Question 6 [1 mark]

Which equation represents a horizontal line?

C) $y = 3x$  
D) $y = 3x + 1$

**Explanation:** A horizontal line has the form $y = c$ (constant). All points have the same $y$-coordinate.

### Question 8 [1 mark]

What is the $x$-intercept of the line $y = 2x - 6$?

A) $(0, -6)$  
B) $(3, 0)$  
C) $(-3, 0)$  
D) $(6, 0)$

**Answer: B**

### Question 9 [1 mark]

The turning point of $y = (x - 3)^2 + 2$ is:

A) $(3, 2)$  
B) $(-3, 2)$  
C) $(3, -2)$  
D) $(-3, -2)$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** In completed square form $y = a(x-h)^2 + k$, the turning point is $(h, k)$. Here, $h = 3$ and $k = 2$.

### Question 10 [1 mark]

When solving $y = x + 1$ and $y = 2x - 1$ graphically, the solution is:

A) $(1, 2)$  
B) $(2, 3)$  
C) $(0, 1)$  
D) $(2, 1)$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Setting the equations equal: $x + 1 = 2x - 1$, so $x = 2$. Then $y = 2 + 1 = 3$. Solution: $(2, 3)$.

### Question 11 [1 mark]

The distance between points $(0, 0)$ and $(3, 4)$ is:

A) $5$  
B) $7$  
C) $12$  
D) $25$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$d = \sqrt{(3-0)^2 + (4-0)^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = \sqrt{25} = 5$$

### Question 12 [1 mark]

The graph of $y = \frac{2}{x}$ has asymptotes at:

A) $x = 2$ and $y = 2$  
B) $x = 0$ and $y = 0$  
C) $x = 2$ and $y = 0$

**Explanation:** For $y = \frac{k}{x}$, the asymptotes are always the coordinate axes: $x = 0$ (y-axis) and $y = 0$ (x-axis).

## PART B: TRUE/FALSE QUESTIONS

### Question 13 [1 mark each]

**Statement (a):** The line $y = 5$ is a horizontal line passing through all points with $y$-coordinate $5$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** The equation $y = c$ represents a horizontal line where all points have $y$-coordinate equal to $c$.

---

**Statement (b):** A line with gradient $-2$ slopes upward from left to right.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A line with gradient $-2$ slopes **downward** from left to right. Negative gradients always indicate downward slopes.

---

**Statement (c):** Parallel lines have the same $y$-intercept.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Parallel lines have the **same gradient** but can have different $y$-intercepts.

---

**Statement (d):** The point $(0, c)$ is always the $y$-intercept of $y = mx + c$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** When $x = 0$, $y = m(0) + c = c$, so the point $(0, c)$ is on the line and on the $y$-axis.

---

**Statement (e):** The line $x = -4$ has gradient zero.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The line $x = -4$ is **vertical**, so its gradient is **undefined**, not zero.

---

**Statement (f):** If two lines are perpendicular and one has gradient $\frac{2}{3}$, the other has gradient $-\frac{3}{2}$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Perpendicular gradients are negative reciprocals: $-\frac{1}{\frac{2}{3}} = -\frac{3}{2}$.

---

**Statement (g):** The graph of $y = x^2 - 4$ crosses the $x$-axis at $(2, 0)$ and $(-2, 0)$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Setting $y = 0$: $x^2 - 4 = 0$, so $x^2 = 4$ and $x = \pm 2$. The $x$-intercepts are $(2, 0)$ and $(-2, 0)$.

---

**Statement (h):** The graph of $y = \frac{1}{x}$ passes through the origin.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The graph of $y = \frac{1}{x}$ never passes through the origin. It has asymptotes at both axes, meaning $x$ and $y$ can never be zero.

---

**Statement (i):** Two different straight lines can intersect at most once.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Two distinct straight lines either intersect at exactly one point (if they have different gradients), are parallel (never intersect), or are the same line (infinite intersections).

---

**Statement (j):** The turning point of $y = -x^2 + 4$ is a maximum point.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Since the coefficient of $x^2$ is negative ($a = -1 < 0$), the parabola opens downward, giving a maximum at the turning point.

### Question 31 [6 marks]

**(a)** On the same axes, sketch the graphs of:
- $y = x^2$ for $-3 \leq x \leq 3$
- $y = x + 2$ for $-2 \leq x \leq 4$ [4 marks]

**(b)** Write down the coordinates of the points where the graphs intersect. [2 marks]

**Solution:**

**(a) Sketch:**

**For $y = x^2$:**
- Parabola, minimum at $(0, 0)$
- Points: $(-3, 9)$, $(-2, 4)$, $(-1, 1)$, $(0, 0)$, $(1, 1)$, $(2, 4)$, $(3, 9)$

**For $y = x + 2$:**
- Straight line, gradient $1$, $y$-intercept $(0, 2)$
- Points: $(-2, 0)$, $(0, 2)$, $(2, 4)$, $(4, 6)$

[M1 for parabola shape, M1 for correct points, M1 for line, M1 for both on same axes]

**(b) Intersection points:**

Set $x^2 = x + 2$:
$$x^2 - x - 2 = 0$$
$$(x - 2)(x + 1) = 0$$
$$x = 2 \text{ or } x = -1$$

When $x = 2$: $y = 2^2 = 4$
When $x = -1$: $y = (-1)^2 = 1$

**Answer:** $\boxed{(-1, 1) \text{ and } (2, 4)}$ [A1, A1]

## PART D: EXAM FOCUS & STRATEGY

### Frequently Tested Question Types

1. **Finding equations of lines** (gradient and intercept, two points, parallel/perpendicular)
2. **Sketching graphs** with all key features labeled
3. **Graphical solutions** of simultaneous equations
4. **Midpoint and distance** calculations
5. **Interpreting graphs** to solve problems

---

### Typical Problem-Solving Patterns

| Pattern | Key Steps |
|---------|-----------|
| "Find the equation..." | Extract $m$, find $c$, write $y = mx + c$ |
| "Sketch the graph..." | Find intercepts, turning point, draw smooth curve |
| "Solve graphically..." | Plot both, read intersection |
| "Perpendicular bisector..." | Find midpoint, perpendicular gradient, equation |

---

### Command Word Quick Guide

| Word | Action |
|------|--------|
| **Sketch** | Show shape + key points |
| **Plot** | Draw accurately |
| **Find** | Calculate with working |
| **Write down** | No working needed |
| **Determine** | Show full working |

---

### Tips for Full Marks

1. ✅ **Show all working** - method marks available
2. ✅ **Label all points** on sketches
3. ✅ **Check your answer** by substitution
4. ✅ **Use appropriate scales** for graphs
5. ✅ **Draw smooth curves** (not straight segments for curves)
6. ✅ **Read scales carefully** when extracting values

---

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correction |
|-----------|---------------|
| Wrong gradient sign | Check if line goes up (+) or down (-) |
| Missing $y$-intercept calculation | Complete all steps |
| Forgetting to label points | Always write coordinates |
| Straight curves | Use smooth strokes |
| Wrong scale reading | Check axis scale first |
| Only one intersection | Check for multiple solutions |
