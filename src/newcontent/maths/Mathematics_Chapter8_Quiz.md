## SECTION A: MULTIPLE CHOICE QUESTIONS (10 marks)

**Answer all questions. Select the correct option.**

---

**Q1.** Convert 4500 mm to metres.

A) 0.45 m  
B) 4.5 m  
C) 45 m  
D) 450 m

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
$1 \text{ m} = 1000 \text{ mm}$  
$4500 \div 1000 = 4.5 \text{ m}$

---

**Q2.** Convert 2.5 m² to cm².

A) 250 cm²  
B) 2500 cm²  
C) 25000 cm²  
D) 250000 cm²

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
Area conversion: $1 \text{ m}^2 = 10000 \text{ cm}^2$  
$2.5 \times 10000 = 25000 \text{ cm}^2$

---

**Q3.** A block has volume 500 cm³ and mass 1.2 kg. What is its density in g/cm³?

A) 0.24 g/cm³  
B) 2.4 g/cm³  
C) 24 g/cm³  
D) 0.0024 g/cm³

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Mass = 1.2 kg = 1200 g  
$\text{Density} = \frac{1200}{500} = 2.4 \text{ g/cm}^3$

---

**Q4.** A car travels 180 km in 2 hours 30 minutes. What is its average speed?

A) 60 km/h  
B) 72 km/h  
C) 75 km/h  
D) 90 km/h

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
Time = 2.5 hours  
$\text{Speed} = \frac{180}{2.5} = 72 \text{ km/h}$

---

**Q5.** Convert 54 km/h to m/s.

A) 15 m/s  
B) 194.4 m/s  
C) 5.4 m/s  
D) 54 m/s

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**  
$\text{Speed (m/s)} = \frac{\text{Speed (km/h)}}{3.6} = \frac{54}{3.6} = 15 \text{ m/s}$

---

**Q6.** A force of 200 N acts on an area of 50 cm². What is the pressure in N/m²?

A) 4 N/m²  
B) 400 N/m²  
C) 40000 N/m²  
D) 400000 N/m²

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
Area = $50 \text{ cm}^2 = 0.005 \text{ m}^2$  
$\text{Pressure} = \frac{200}{0.005} = 40000 \text{ N/m}^2$

---

**Q7.** Water has density 1 g/cm³. What is this in kg/m³?

A) 0.001 kg/m³  
B) 1 kg/m³  
C) 100 kg/m³  
D) 1000 kg/m³

**Answer: D**

**Explanation:**  
$1 \text{ g/cm}^3 = 1000 \text{ kg/m}^3$

---

**Q8.** A container holds 3.5 litres. What is this volume in cm³?

A) 35 cm³  
B) 350 cm³  
C) 3500 cm³  
D) 35000 cm³

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**  
$1 \text{ litre} = 1000 \text{ cm}^3$  
$3.5 \times 1000 = 3500 \text{ cm}^3$

---

**Q9.** A person travels 30 miles. Approximately how many kilometres is this?

A) 18.75 km  
B) 48 km  
C) 30 km  
D) 19 km

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**  
$1 \text{ mile} \approx 1.6 \text{ km}$  
$30 \times 1.6 = 48 \text{ km}$

---

**Q10.** A substance has density 8 g/cm³ and mass 200 g. What is its volume?

A) 25 cm³  
B) 0.04 cm³  
C) 1600 cm³  
D) 40 cm³

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**  
$\text{Volume} = \frac{\text{Mass}}{\text{Density}} = \frac{200}{8} = 25 \text{ cm}^3$

## SECTION B: TRUE/FALSE QUESTIONS (10 marks)

**State whether each statement is TRUE or FALSE. For FALSE statements, provide the correction.**

---

**Q1.** 1 m² = 100 cm²

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** 1 m² = 10000 cm² (Area conversion requires squaring the linear factor: $100^2 = 10000$)

---

**Q2.** 1 litre = 1000 ml

**Answer: TRUE**

---

**Q3.** When converting from m³ to cm³, multiply by 1000000.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

**Q4.** To convert km/h to m/s, multiply by 3.6.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** To convert km/h to m/s, divide by 3.6 (or multiply by $\frac{5}{18}$)

---

**Q5.** Average speed = (initial speed + final speed) ÷ 2

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Average speed = Total Distance ÷ Total Time

---

**Q6.** Pressure = Force × Area

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Pressure = Force ÷ Area ($P = \frac{F}{A}$)

---

**Q7.** 1 cm³ = 1 ml

**Answer: TRUE**

---

**Q8.** A density of 2 g/cm³ is equivalent to 2000 kg/m³.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

**Q9.** When converting from a larger unit to a smaller unit, you divide.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** When converting from larger to smaller unit, you multiply (you need more of the smaller units)

---

**Q10.** A cube with sides 10 cm has a volume of 1 litre.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Volume = $10 \times 10 \times 10 = 1000 \text{ cm}^3 = 1 \text{ litre}$

### Question 1 (3 marks)

A rectangular field measures 150 m by 80 m.

(a) Calculate the area of the field in m². **[1 mark]**

(b) Convert your answer to km². **[2 marks]**

---

**Mark Scheme Solution:**

**(a) Area calculation [1 mark]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Area = $150 \times 80$ | M1 |
| A1 | = $12000 \text{ m}^2$ | A1 |

**Answer: $12000 \text{ m}^2$**

**(b) Conversion to km² [2 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | $1 \text{ km}^2 = 1000000 \text{ m}^2$ (or conversion stated) | M1 |
| M1 | $12000 \div 1000000$ | M1 (dep) |
| A1 | = $0.012 \text{ km}^2$ | A1 |

**Answer: $0.012 \text{ km}^2$**

(a) Calculate the volume of the block in cm³. **[2 marks]**

(b) Find the density of the metal in g/cm³. **[2 marks]**

---

**Mark Scheme Solution:**

**(b) Density calculation [2 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |

### Question 3 (5 marks)

A train travels from Station A to Station B at an average speed of 90 km/h. The journey takes 1 hour 40 minutes.

(a) Calculate the distance from Station A to Station B. **[2 marks]**

(b) On the return journey, the train travels at an average speed of 75 km/h. Calculate the time taken for the return journey, in hours and minutes. **[3 marks]**

---

**Mark Scheme Solution:**

**(a) Distance calculation [2 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Time = $1 \text{ hr } 40 \text{ min} = \frac{5}{3}$ hours or $1.667$ hours | M1 |
| M1 | Distance = $90 \times \frac{5}{3}$ | M1 |
| A1 | = $150 \text{ km}$ | A1 |

**Answer: 150 km**

**(b) Return journey time [3 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Time = $\frac{\text{Distance}}{\text{Speed}}$ | M1 |
| M1 | Time = $\frac{150}{75} = 2$ hours | M1 |
| A1 | = 2 hours 0 minutes | A1 |

**Answer: 2 hours 0 minutes**

### Question 5 (5 marks)

A man drives from Town P to Town Q at a speed of 60 km/h and returns from Q to P at a speed of 40 km/h. The distance between the towns is 120 km.

(a) Calculate the total time for the round trip in hours. **[3 marks]**

(b) Calculate the average speed for the entire journey. **[2 marks]**

---

**Mark Scheme Solution:**

**(a) Total time [3 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Time P→Q = $\frac{120}{60} = 2$ hours | M1 |
| M1 | Time Q→P = $\frac{120}{40} = 3$ hours | M1 |
| A1 | Total time = $2 + 3 = 5$ hours | A1 |

**Answer: 5 hours**

**(b) Average speed [2 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Total distance = $120 + 120 = 240$ km | M1 |
| M1 | Average speed = $\frac{240}{5}$ | M1 |
| A1 | = $48 \text{ km/h}$ | A1 |

**Answer: 48 km/h**

**Note:** Average speed ≠ $\frac{60 + 40}{2} = 50$ km/h (common error)

### Question 7 (6 marks)

A student cycles to school. She cycles at 12 km/h for 15 minutes, then walks at 5 km/h for 8 minutes.

(a) Convert 15 minutes to hours. **[1 mark]**

(b) Calculate the total distance travelled. **[3 marks]**

(c) Calculate the average speed for the whole journey. **[2 marks]**

---

**Mark Scheme Solution:**

**(a) Time conversion [1 mark]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | 15 minutes = $\frac{15}{60}$ hours | M1 |
| A1 | = $0.25$ hours | A1 |

**Answer: 0.25 hours (or $\frac{1}{4}$ hour)**

**(b) Total distance [3 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Distance cycling = $12 \times \frac{15}{60} = 3$ km | M1 |
| M1 | 8 min = $\frac{8}{60} = \frac{2}{15}$ hours | M1 |
| M1 | Distance walking = $5 \times \frac{2}{15} = \frac{2}{3}$ km | M1 |
| A1 | Total distance = $3 + \frac{2}{3} = \frac{11}{3}$ km or $3.67$ km | A1 |

**Answer: $\frac{11}{3}$ km or $3.67$ km (to 3 sf)**

**(c) Average speed [2 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Total time = $15 + 8 = 23$ min = $\frac{23}{60}$ hours | M1 |
| M1 | Average speed = $\frac{\frac{11}{3}}{\frac{23}{60}} = \frac{11}{3} \times \frac{60}{23}$ | M1 |
| A1 | = $\frac{220}{23} \approx 9.57 \text{ km/h}$ | A1 |

**Answer: 9.57 km/h (to 3 sf)**

### Question 8 (5 marks)

A box weighs 150 N and has a base of dimensions 30 cm by 20 cm.

(a) Calculate the area of the base in m². **[2 marks]**

(b) Calculate the pressure exerted by the box on the floor in N/m². **[2 marks]**

(c) If the box is turned so that it stands on its smallest face (20 cm by 10 cm), will the pressure increase or decrease? Explain your answer. **[1 mark]**

---

**Mark Scheme Solution:**

**(a) Area in m² [2 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | $30 \text{ cm} = 0.3 \text{ m}$, $20 \text{ cm} = 0.2 \text{ m}$ | M1 |
| A1 | Area = $0.3 \times 0.2 = 0.06 \text{ m}^2$ | A1 |

**Answer: $0.06 \text{ m}^2$**

**(b) Pressure [2 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Pressure = $\frac{\text{Force}}{\text{Area}} = \frac{150}{0.06}$ | M1 |
| A1 | = $2500 \text{ N/m}^2$ (or 2500 Pa) | A1 |

**Answer: $2500 \text{ N/m}^2$ (or 2500 Pa)**

**(c) Effect of turning [1 mark]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | The pressure will **increase** because the area is smaller and Pressure = Force ÷ Area. Smaller area means higher pressure for the same force. | E1 |

**Answer: Pressure increases (smaller area = higher pressure)**

A cylindrical can has radius 4 cm and height 12 cm.

### Question 10 (6 marks)

A car travels from City A to City B, a distance of 270 km.

(a) The journey takes 3 hours 45 minutes. Calculate the average speed in km/h. **[3 marks]**

(b) Convert your answer to m/s. **[2 marks]**

(c) A different car completes the same journey at an average speed of 75 km/h. How much longer (in minutes) does the first car take compared to this car? **[3 marks]**

---

**Mark Scheme Solution:**

**(a) Average speed [3 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Time = $3$ h $45$ min = $3 + \frac{45}{60} = 3.75$ hours | M1 |
| M1 | Speed = $\frac{270}{3.75}$ | M1 |
| A1 | = $72 \text{ km/h}$ | A1 |

**Answer: 72 km/h**

**(b) Convert to m/s [2 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Speed = $\frac{72}{3.6}$ m/s or $\frac{72 \times 1000}{3600}$ m/s | M1 |
| A1 | = $20 \text{ m/s}$ | A1 |

**Answer: 20 m/s**

**(c) Time difference [3 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Time for second car = $\frac{270}{75} = 3.6$ hours | M1 |
| M1 | 3.6 hours = $3$ h $36$ min | M1 |
| M1 | First car time = $3$ h $45$ min | M1 |
| A1 | Difference = $45 - 36 = 9$ minutes | A1 |

**Answer: 9 minutes longer**

---

### 🎯 Typical Problem-Solving Patterns

#### Pattern 1: Multi-Unit Conversion
**Structure:** Given quantity in one unit → Convert → Apply formula → Convert answer

**Strategy:**
1. Identify the unit given and unit required
2. Convert ALL quantities to consistent units BEFORE calculating
3. Calculate the answer
4. Check if answer needs conversion to different unit

---

#### Pattern 2: Compound Measure Problems
**Structure:** Given two of {variable1, variable2, result} → Find the third

**Strategy:**
1. Write down the formula (use triangle if needed)
2. Cover the unknown variable
3. Substitute the known values
4. Calculate and check units

---

#### Pattern 3: Two-Part Journey (Average Speed)
**Structure:** Journey with two parts at different speeds → Find average speed

**Strategy:**
1. Find distance and time for EACH part separately
2. Calculate total distance and total time
3. Average speed = Total Distance ÷ Total Time
4. NEVER average the two speeds!

---

###  Command Word Guidance

| Command Word | What It Means | How to Answer |
|--------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Calculate** | Work out the value | Show all working, give final answer with units |
| **Convert** | Change from one unit to another | Show conversion factor, give answer in new unit |
| **Find** | Determine the value | Same as calculate |
| **Explain** | Give reason(s) | Use because/since, refer to formulas |
| **Compare** | Identify similarities/differences | State both values and the comparison |
| **Show that** | Prove the given statement | Full working must lead to the stated result |

---

### 🏆 How to Gain Full Marks

#### For 1-2 mark questions:
- Show the calculation clearly
- Give the answer with correct units
- Check for reasonableness

#### For 3-4 mark questions:
- Show all steps clearly
- Include unit conversions where needed
- State formulas used
- Give answer to appropriate accuracy (usually 3 sf)
- Include units in final answer

#### For 5-6 mark questions:
- Clear structure with labeled parts
- Show all working (method marks available)
- Check each step as you go
- Make clear what each calculation represents
- Final answer clearly stated with units
- Check answer makes sense in context

---

### ⚡ Last-Minute Checklist

Before submitting your paper, check:

- [ ] All units are consistent in calculations
- [ ] Area conversions use squared factors
- [ ] Volume conversions use cubed factors
- [ ] Average speed = Total Distance ÷ Total Time
- [ ] Speed conversions: km/h ↔ m/s (÷3.6 or ×3.6)
- [ ] Density: mass/volume (not volume/mass)
- [ ] Pressure: force/area (not force × area)
- [ ] All answers have units
- [ ] Answers rounded appropriately (usually 3 sf)

---

###  A* Student Tips

1. **Draw the triangle** for compound measures if you're unsure of the formula

2. **Convert first, then calculate** - avoid mixing units mid-calculation

3. **Check reasonableness** - a car at 500 km/h or density of 0.0001 g/cm³ should prompt a review

4. **Show working even for "easy" questions** - you might make an arithmetic error and lose all marks otherwise

5. **Label your working** - writing "Time = " or "Distance = " helps you and the examiner

6. **Use exact values** where possible (fractions rather than decimals) until the final answer

7. **Common conversion shortcuts:**
   - Area: m² → cm² add 4 zeros (×10,000)
   - Volume: m³ → cm³ add 6 zeros (×1,000,000)
   - Capacity: cm³ = ml directly
