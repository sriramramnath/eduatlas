### Question 1
What is $\frac{3}{8} + \frac{1}{4}$?

A) $\frac{4}{12}$

B) $\frac{5}{8}$

C) $\frac{4}{8}$

D) $\frac{1}{2}$

**Answer: B**

**Working:**
$$\frac{3}{8} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{3}{8} + \frac{2}{8} = \frac{5}{8}$$

**Explanation:** First find a common denominator (8), then convert $\frac{1}{4} = \frac{2}{8}$, then add numerators.

---

### Question 2
Calculate $\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{9}{10}$

A) $\frac{11}{30}$

B) $\frac{3}{5}$

C) $\frac{18}{30}$

D) $\frac{5}{3}$

**Answer: B**

**Working:**
$$\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{9}{10} = \frac{\cancel{2}^{1}}{\cancel{3}^{1}} \times \frac{\cancel{9}^{3}}{\cancel{10}^{5}} = \frac{3}{5}$$

**Explanation:** Cross-cancel: 2 divides 10 (giving 5), 3 divides 9 (giving 3). Then multiply.

---

### Question 3
What is $5\frac{2}{7}$ as an improper fraction?

A) $\frac{7}{7}$

B) $\frac{10}{7}$

C) $\frac{35}{7}$

D) $\frac{37}{7}$

**Answer: D**

**Working:**
$$5\frac{2}{7} = \frac{5 \times 7 + 2}{7} = \frac{35 + 2}{7} = \frac{37}{7}$$

**Explanation:** Multiply whole number by denominator, add numerator, keep denominator.

---

### Question 4
Calculate $4 + 3 \times 2$

A) 14

B) 10

C) 12

D) 8

**Answer: B**

**Working:**
$$4 + 3 \times 2 = 4 + 6 = 10$$

**Explanation:** BIDMAS - multiplication before addition. Common mistake is to add first: $7 \times 2 = 14$ (wrong).

---

### Question 5
Calculate $\frac{5}{6} \div \frac{2}{3}$

A) $\frac{10}{18}$

B) $\frac{5}{4}$

C) $\frac{5}{9}$

D) $\frac{4}{5}$

**Answer: B**

**Working:**
$$\frac{5}{6} \div \frac{2}{3} = \frac{5}{6} \times \frac{3}{2} = \frac{5 \times 3}{6 \times 2} = \frac{15}{12} = \frac{5}{4}$$

**Explanation:** KCF rule - Keep $\frac{5}{6}$, Change ÷ to ×, Flip $\frac{2}{3}$ to $\frac{3}{2}$. Simplify at end.

---

### Question 6
Round 7.846 to 2 decimal places.

A) 7.84

B) 7.85

C) 7.8

D) 7.83

**Answer: B**

**Working:**
Look at third decimal digit: 6 (which is ≥ 5), so round up.
$$7.846 \rightarrow 7.85$$

**Explanation:** For 2 d.p., look at digit 3. Since 6 ≥ 5, round the 4 up to 5.

---

### Question 7
How many significant figures does 0.00450 have?

A) 2

B) 3

C) 5

D) 6

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** 
- Leading zeros (0.00) are NOT significant
- All non-zero digits (4, 5) are significant  
- Trailing zero after decimal (0) IS significant
- Total: 3 significant figures

---

### Question 8
Calculate $(12 - 4) \times 3 + 2$

A) 26

B) 14

C) 10

D) 28

**Answer: A**

**Working:**
$$(12 - 4) \times 3 + 2 = 8 \times 3 + 2 = 24 + 2 = 26$$

**Explanation:** Brackets first, then multiplication, then addition.

---

### Question 9
Find $\frac{2}{5}$ of 35.

A) 7

B) 14

C) 17.5

D) 70

**Answer: B**

**Working:**
$$\frac{2}{5} \times 35 = \frac{2 \times 35}{5} = \frac{70}{5} = 14$$

**Explanation:** "Of" means multiply. Alternative: $\frac{35}{5} = 7$, then $7 \times 2 = 14$.

---

### Question 10
Round 4873 to 2 significant figures.

A) 4800

B) 4870

C) 4900

D) 5000

**Answer: C**

**Working:**
- First 2 sig figs: 4, 8
- Look at 3rd digit: 7 (≥ 5), so round up
- $48 \rightarrow 49$
- Answer: 4900

**Explanation:** 7 rounds the 8 up to 9. Fill with zeros to maintain place value.

## SECTION B: True/False Questions (10 marks)

**For each statement, state whether it is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correction.**

---

### Question 11
$\frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{2}{9}$

**FALSE**

**Correction:** 
$$\frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{2}{6} + \frac{1}{6} = \frac{3}{6} = \frac{1}{2}$$

The denominators should NOT be added together. Find a common denominator first.

---

### Question 12
When dividing fractions, you should flip both fractions.

**FALSE**

**Correction:** Only flip the SECOND fraction. Use KCF: Keep the first fraction, Change ÷ to ×, Flip the second fraction.

Example: $\frac{3}{4} \div \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{2}{1} = \frac{3}{2}$

---

### Question 13
$3 + 4 \times 5 = 35$

**FALSE**

**Correction:** 
$$3 + 4 \times 5 = 3 + 20 = 23$$

Multiplication comes before addition (BIDMAS).

---

### Question 14
The number 0.0304 has 3 significant figures.

**TRUE**

The significant figures are 3, 0, 4. Leading zeros are not significant, but the zero between 3 and 4 is significant.

---

### Question 15
When rounding 3.95 to 1 decimal place, the answer is 3.9.

**FALSE**

**Correction:** 3.95 rounded to 1 d.p. is **4.0**

Since the second decimal (5) is ≥ 5, round up. The 9 becomes 10, so carry 1 to get 4.0.

---

### Question 16
$\frac{4}{7} \times \frac{7}{4} = 1$

**TRUE**

$$\frac{4}{7} \times \frac{7}{4} = \frac{28}{28} = 1$$

A fraction multiplied by its reciprocal equals 1.

---

### Question 17
$2\frac{1}{3} = \frac{7}{3}$

**TRUE**

$$2\frac{1}{3} = \frac{2 \times 3 + 1}{3} = \frac{6 + 1}{3} = \frac{7}{3}$$

---

### Question 18
Division and multiplication should be done from left to right.

**TRUE**

Division and multiplication have equal priority in BIDMAS, so work from left to right.

Example: $12 \div 3 \times 2 = 4 \times 2 = 8$ (not $12 \div 6 = 2$)

---

### Question 19
The fraction $\frac{17}{5}$ as a mixed number is $3\frac{2}{5}$.

**TRUE**

$$17 \div 5 = 3 \text{ remainder } 2$$

Therefore $\frac{17}{5} = 3\frac{2}{5}$

---

### Question 20
When asked to give an answer to 2 decimal places, writing 3.5 instead of 3.50 is acceptable.

**FALSE**

**Correction:** If asked for 2 decimal places, you MUST write 3.50 (with the placeholder zero). This shows the precision of your answer.

**Question 22** [2 marks]

Work out $2\frac{1}{3} \times 1\frac{1}{2}$

Give your answer as a mixed number.

**M1** Converting to improper fractions: $\frac{7}{3} \times \frac{3}{2}$ OR setting up correct multiplication

**A1** $3\frac{1}{2}$ (cao - correct answer only)

**Working:**
$$2\frac{1}{3} \times 1\frac{1}{2} = \frac{7}{3} \times \frac{3}{2} = \frac{21}{6} = \frac{7}{2} = 3\frac{1}{2}$$

**Alternative with cross-cancellation:**
$$\frac{7}{\cancel{3}^{1}} \times \frac{\cancel{3}^{1}}{2} = \frac{7}{2} = 3\frac{1}{2}$$

**Question 23** [2 marks]

Calculate $15 - 3 \times 4 + 2$

Show each step of your working.

**M1** Multiplication done first: $3 \times 4 = 12$

**A1** Correct answer: 5

**Working:**
$$15 - 3 \times 4 + 2 = 15 - 12 + 2 = 3 + 2 = 5$$

Note: Must work left to right for subtraction/addition: $15 - 12 = 3$, then $3 + 2 = 5$

**Question 25** [3 marks]

Calculate $\frac{2}{3} \div \frac{4}{9} \times \frac{1}{2}$

Give your answer as a fraction in its simplest form.

**M1** Correct division as multiplication: $\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{9}{4}$

**M1** Complete calculation set up: $\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{9}{4} \times \frac{1}{2}$

**A1** $\frac{3}{4}$ (cao)

**Working:**
$$\frac{2}{3} \div \frac{4}{9} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{9}{4} \times \frac{1}{2}$$

With cross-cancellation:
$$= \frac{\cancel{2}^{1}}{3} \times \frac{9}{\cancel{4}^{2}} \times \frac{1}{\cancel{2}^{1}} = \frac{1}{3} \times \frac{9}{2} \times \frac{1}{1} = \frac{9}{6} = \frac{3}{2}$$

Wait - let me recalculate:
$$\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{9}{4} = \frac{18}{12} = \frac{3}{2}$$
$$\frac{3}{2} \times \frac{1}{2} = \frac{3}{4}$$

Answer: $\frac{3}{4}$

**Question 26** [4 marks]

A recipe requires $\frac{3}{4}$ kg of flour to make 12 cakes.

(a) How much flour is needed to make 8 cakes? [2]

(b) Maria has $2\frac{1}{4}$ kg of flour. How many complete cakes can she make? [2]

**(a) M1** Method to find flour per cake OR set up proportion

**M1/A1** $\frac{1}{2}$ kg or 0.5 kg

**Working:**
Flour for 1 cake: $\frac{3}{4} \div 12 = \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{1}{12} = \frac{3}{48} = \frac{1}{16}$ kg

Flour for 8 cakes: $\frac{1}{16} \times 8 = \frac{8}{16} = \frac{1}{2}$ kg

OR: $\frac{8}{12} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{2}{3} \times \frac{3}{4} = \frac{6}{12} = \frac{1}{2}$ kg

**(b) M1** Method to find number of batches

**A1** 36 cakes

**Working:**
$2\frac{1}{4} = \frac{9}{4}$ kg

Number of $\frac{3}{4}$ kg portions: $\frac{9}{4} \div \frac{3}{4} = \frac{9}{4} \times \frac{4}{3} = 3$

Number of cakes: $3 \times 12 = 36$ cakes

**Question 27** [4 marks]

(a) Calculate $(2.7 + 1.8) \times 4.2 - 3.5$ [2]

Give your answer to 2 decimal places.

(b) Explain why your answer to part (a) is different from $2.7 + 1.8 \times 4.2 - 3.5$ [2]

**(a) M1** Correct order of operations shown

**A1** 14.17

**Working:**
$$(2.7 + 1.8) \times 4.2 - 3.5 = 4.5 \times 4.2 - 3.5$$
$$= 18.9 - 3.5 = 15.4$$

Wait - let me recalculate: $4.5 \times 4.2 = 18.9$, $18.9 - 3.5 = 15.40$

Answer: **15.40** (to 2 d.p.)

**(b) M1** Recognition that brackets change order of operations

**A1** Clear explanation

**Explanation:**
Without brackets: $2.7 + 1.8 \times 4.2 - 3.5$
$= 2.7 + 7.56 - 3.5$ (multiplication first)
$= 6.76$

With brackets: $(2.7 + 1.8) \times 4.2 - 3.5 = 15.40$

The brackets force addition to be done first, giving a different result. This demonstrates the importance of BIDMAS.

**Question 28** [5 marks]

A school has 1200 students.

- $\frac{2}{5}$ of the students are boys
- $\frac{1}{4}$ of the boys play football
- $\frac{1}{3}$ of the girls play tennis

How many students play football or tennis?

**M1** Finding number of boys: $\frac{2}{5} \times 1200 = 480$ boys

**M1** Finding number of girls: $1200 - 480 = 720$ girls

**M1** Finding boys who play football: $\frac{1}{4} \times 480 = 120$ boys

**M1** Finding girls who play tennis: $\frac{1}{3} \times 720 = 240$ girls

**A1** Total: $120 + 240 = 360$ students

**Full Working:**

**Boys:**
$$\frac{2}{5} \times 1200 = \frac{2400}{5} = 480 \text{ boys}$$

**Girls:**
$$1200 - 480 = 720 \text{ girls}$$

**Boys playing football:**
$$\frac{1}{4} \times 480 = 120$$

**Girls playing tennis:**
$$\frac{1}{3} \times 720 = 240$$

**Total playing football or tennis:**
$$120 + 240 = 360 \text{ students}$$

**Question 29** [6 marks]

(a) Express $4\frac{5}{6} - 2\frac{1}{3}$ as a mixed number. [3]

(b) Find the value of $\frac{3}{4} \div \frac{5}{8} + \frac{1}{2} \times 3$ [3]

Give your answer as an improper fraction in its simplest form.

**(a) M1** Convert to improper fractions: $\frac{29}{6} - \frac{7}{3}$

**M1** Find common denominator: $\frac{29}{6} - \frac{14}{6}$

**A1** $2\frac{1}{2}$

**Working:**
$$4\frac{5}{6} = \frac{29}{6}, \quad 2\frac{1}{3} = \frac{7}{3} = \frac{14}{6}$$
$$\frac{29}{6} - \frac{14}{6} = \frac{15}{6} = \frac{5}{2} = 2\frac{1}{2}$$

**(b) M1** Division converted to multiplication: $\frac{3}{4} \times \frac{8}{5}$

**M1** Following BIDMAS correctly

**A1** $\frac{11}{5}$

**Working:**
$$\frac{3}{4} \div \frac{5}{8} + \frac{1}{2} \times 3$$
$$= \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{8}{5} + \frac{3}{2}$$
$$= \frac{6}{5} + \frac{3}{2}$$
$$= \frac{12}{10} + \frac{15}{10}$$
$$= \frac{27}{10} = \frac{27}{10}$$

Let me recalculate:
$\frac{3}{4} \times \frac{8}{5} = \frac{24}{20} = \frac{6}{5}$ (this is correct)

$\frac{1}{2} \times 3 = \frac{3}{2}$ (this is correct)

$\frac{6}{5} + \frac{3}{2} = \frac{12}{10} + \frac{15}{10} = \frac{27}{10}$

Answer: $\frac{27}{10}$

**Question 30** [6 marks]

A water tank contains 360 litres of water.

On Monday, $\frac{1}{4}$ of the water is used.

On Tuesday, $\frac{1}{3}$ of the remaining water is used.

On Wednesday, 45 litres of water are added to the tank.

(a) How much water is in the tank at the end of Monday? [1]

(b) How much water is in the tank at the end of Tuesday? [2]

(c) How much water is in the tank at the end of Wednesday? [1]

(d) What fraction of the original amount is now in the tank? Give your answer in its simplest form. [2]

**(a) M1/A1** 270 litres

**Working:**
Water used Monday: $\frac{1}{4} \times 360 = 90$ litres
Water remaining: $360 - 90 = 270$ litres

**(b) M1** Finding $\frac{1}{3}$ of remaining water

**A1** 180 litres

**Working:**
Water used Tuesday: $\frac{1}{3} \times 270 = 90$ litres
Water remaining: $270 - 90 = 180$ litres

**(c) M1/A1** 225 litres

**Working:**
$180 + 45 = 225$ litres

**(d) M1** Method to find fraction

**A1** $\frac{5}{8}$

**Working:**
$$\frac{225}{360} = \frac{225 \div 45}{360 \div 45} = \frac{5}{8}$$

OR:
$$\frac{225}{360} = \frac{25}{40} = \frac{5}{8}$$
