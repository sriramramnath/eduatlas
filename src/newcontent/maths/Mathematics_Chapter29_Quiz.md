### Question 1
The derivative of $y = x^4$ is:

A) $4x^3$  
B) $x^3$  
C) $4x^5$  
D) $\frac{x^3}{4}$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Apply the power rule: multiply by the power (4), then reduce the power by 1. So $\frac{dy}{dx} = 4x^{4-1} = 4x^3$.

---

### Question 2
What is the derivative of $y = 3x^2 - 5x + 7$?

A) $6x - 5$  
B) $6x + 5$  
C) $3x - 5$  
D) $6x^2 - 5$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Differentiate each term separately:
- $\frac{d}{dx}(3x^2) = 6x$
- $\frac{d}{dx}(-5x) = -5$
- $\frac{d}{dx}(7) = 0$

So $\frac{dy}{dx} = 6x - 5$.

---

### Question 3
The gradient of the curve $y = x^3 - 2x$ at the point where $x = 2$ is:

A) 10  
B) 4  
C) 8  
D) 12

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** 
- $\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 - 2$
- At $x = 2$: $\frac{dy}{dx} = 3(2)^2 - 2 = 12 - 2 = 10$

### Question 4
At a stationary point on a curve:

A) $\frac{dy}{dx} > 0$  
B) $\frac{dy}{dx} < 0$  
C) $\frac{dy}{dx} = 0$  
D) $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 0$

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** By definition, a stationary point occurs where the gradient is zero, i.e., $\frac{dy}{dx} = 0$. The second derivative helps classify the point, but the defining condition is that the first derivative equals zero.

---

### Question 5
For a curve with $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} > 0$ at a stationary point, the point is a:

A) Maximum  
B) Minimum  
C) Point of inflection  
D) Cannot be determined

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** When the second derivative is positive at a stationary point, the curve curves upward (like a smile/U-shape), indicating a minimum point.

### Question 6
The displacement $s$ metres of a particle is given by $s = 2t^3 - 3t$. The velocity $v$ m/s is:

A) $v = 6t^2 - 3$  
B) $v = 6t^2$  
C) $v = 2t^2 - 3$  
D) $v = 6t - 3$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Velocity is the derivative of displacement: $v = \frac{ds}{dt} = \frac{d}{dt}(2t^3 - 3t) = 6t^2 - 3$.

### Question 7
A particle is "at rest" when:

A) Displacement = 0  
B) Velocity = 0  
C) Acceleration = 0  
D) Time = 0

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** "At rest" means the particle has zero velocity. The displacement and acceleration can still be non-zero when the particle is momentarily stationary.

### Question 8
The derivative of $y = \frac{1}{x^2}$ is:

A) $\frac{1}{x}$  
B) $-\frac{1}{x^2}$  
C) $-\frac{2}{x^3}$  
D) $\frac{2}{x^3}$

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Rewrite as $y = x^{-2}$, then apply the power rule: $\frac{dy}{dx} = -2x^{-3} = -\frac{2}{x^3}$.

### Question 9
The curve $y = x^3 - 6x^2 + 9x$ has a stationary point at $x = 1$. What type is it?

A) Maximum  
B) Minimum  
C) Point of inflection  
D) Cannot determine

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Find the second derivative: $\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 - 12x + 9$, so $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 6x - 12$. At $x = 1$: $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 6 - 12 = -6 < 0$, therefore it's a maximum.

### Question 10
A function is increasing when:

A) $\frac{dy}{dx} > 0$  
B) $\frac{dy}{dx} < 0$  
C) $\frac{dy}{dx} = 0$  
D) $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} > 0$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** A function is increasing when its gradient (derivative) is positive. This means $y$ increases as $x$ increases.

### Question 12
On a velocity-time graph, the area under the curve represents:

A) Velocity  
B) Acceleration  
C) Displacement  
D) Speed

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** The area under a velocity-time graph gives the displacement. This is because distance = velocity × time, which corresponds to the area under the curve.

### Question 13
The stationary points of $y = x^3 - 3x$ occur when $x$ equals:

A) $x = 0$ only  
B) $x = 1$ and $x = -1$  
C) $x = 3$ only  
D) $x = 0$ and $x = 3$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Find where $\frac{dy}{dx} = 0$: $\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 - 3 = 0$, so $x^2 = 1$, giving $x = 1$ or $x = -1$.

### Question 14
If $\frac{dy}{dx} = -4$ at a point on a curve, the curve at this point is:

A) Increasing steeply  
B) Decreasing steeply  
C) Horizontal  
D) Vertical

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** A negative derivative means the curve is decreasing (going downhill from left to right). The magnitude $|-4| = 4$ indicates it's decreasing relatively steeply.

### Question 15
The acceleration of a particle with velocity $v = 3t^2 - 6t + 2$ m/s is:

A) $a = 6t - 6$ m/s²  
B) $a = 3t - 6$ m/s²  
C) $a = t^3 - 3t^2$ m/s²  
D) $a = 6t + 2$ m/s²

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Acceleration is the derivative of velocity: $a = \frac{dv}{dt} = \frac{d}{dt}(3t^2 - 6t + 2) = 6t - 6$ m/s².

# Section B: True/False Statements

**For each statement, state whether it is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.**

---

### Question 1
**Statement:** The derivative of a constant is always zero.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** A constant function has no rate of change - it doesn't vary at all. Therefore, its derivative is zero. For example, $\frac{d}{dx}(5) = 0$.

---

### Question 2
**Statement:** The second derivative of $y = x^3$ is $6x$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** 
- First derivative: $\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2$
- Second derivative: $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 6x$

---

### Question 3
**Statement:** A stationary point is always a maximum or minimum.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A stationary point can be a maximum, minimum, OR a point of inflection. At a point of inflection, the gradient is zero, but the curve continues in the same direction.

---

### Question 4
**Statement:** When velocity is negative, the particle is moving backwards.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Negative velocity means motion in the negative direction. If we define forward as the positive direction, then negative velocity means moving backward.

---

### Question 5
**Statement:** The derivative of $y = 5x^3$ is $15x^2$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Using the power rule with coefficient: $\frac{d}{dx}(5x^3) = 5 \times 3x^2 = 15x^2$.

---

### Question 6
**Statement:** A maximum point has $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} > 0$.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A maximum point has $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} < 0$ (negative). This indicates the curve is curving downward at the maximum. A minimum has $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} > 0$.

---

### Question 7
**Statement:** The gradient of a curve at any point equals the gradient of the tangent at that point.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This is the fundamental definition. The gradient of a curve at a point is defined as the gradient of the tangent line drawn at that point.

---

### Question 8
**Statement:** If $v > 0$ and $a < 0$, the particle is slowing down.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** When velocity and acceleration have opposite signs, the particle is decelerating (slowing down). Here, positive velocity means moving forward, but negative acceleration means decelerating.

---

### Question 9
**Statement:** The derivative of $y = x^2 + 5x$ is $2x + 5x$.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The derivative of $y = x^2 + 5x$ is $2x + 5$. The derivative of $5x$ is 5, not $5x$.

---

### Question 10
**Statement:** At a minimum point, the function changes from decreasing to increasing.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** At a minimum, the gradient changes from negative (before) to zero (at the point) to positive (after). This means the function was decreasing, reaches its lowest point, then starts increasing.

---

### Question 11
**Statement:** The derivative $\frac{dy}{dx}$ gives the rate of change of $y$ with respect to $x$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This is the definition of the derivative. It measures how quickly $y$ changes as $x$ changes - the instantaneous rate of change.

---

### Question 12
**Statement:** A particle with zero acceleration has zero velocity.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A particle with zero acceleration has **constant velocity**, not zero velocity. Zero acceleration means the velocity is not changing, but the velocity itself could be any constant value.

Differentiate $y = 4x^3 - 2x^2 + 6$.

**Solution:**

| Step | Working | Marks |
|------|---------|-------|

### Question 2 [2 marks]
Find the gradient of the curve $y = 2x^3 - x^2 + 3x - 1$ at the point where $x = 2$.

**Solution:**

| Step | Working | Marks |
|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | $\frac{dy}{dx} = 6x^2 - 2x + 3$ | M1 |
| 2 | At $x = 2$: $\frac{dy}{dx} = 6(4) - 2(2) + 3$ | |
| 3 | $= 24 - 4 + 3 = 23$ | A1 |

**Answer:** The gradient at $x = 2$ is **23**.

### Question 3 [3 marks]
Find the coordinates of the stationary point on the curve $y = x^2 - 6x + 10$ and determine its nature.

**Solution:**

| Step | Working | Marks |
|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | $\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x - 6$ | M1 |
| 2 | Set $\frac{dy}{dx} = 0$: $2x - 6 = 0$, so $x = 3$ | M1 |
| 3 | $y = (3)^2 - 6(3) + 10 = 9 - 18 + 10 = 1$ | |
| 4 | $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 2 > 0$, so minimum | A1 |

**Answer:** Stationary point is at **$(3, 1)$** and it is a **minimum**.

**Solution:**

### Question 5 [4 marks]
Find and classify the stationary points on the curve $y = x^3 - 3x^2 - 9x + 5$.

**Solution:**

| Step | Working | Marks |
|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | $\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 - 6x - 9$ | M1 |
| 2 | $3x^2 - 6x - 9 = 0$ | |

### Question 6 [4 marks]
A stone is thrown upwards and its height $h$ metres after $t$ seconds is given by $h = 20t - 5t^2$.

(b) Find the maximum height reached by the stone. [2 marks]

**Solution:**

**(a)**

| Step | Working | Marks |
|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | $v = \frac{dh}{dt} = 20 - 10t$ | M1 |
| 2 | At $t = 2$: $v = 20 - 20 = 0$ m/s | A1 |

**Answer:** Velocity at $t = 2$ is **0 m/s** (momentarily at rest).

**(b)**

| Step | Working | Marks |
|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | Maximum height when $v = 0$: $20 - 10t = 0$, so $t = 2$ | M1 |
| 2 | $h = 20(2) - 5(4) = 40 - 20 = 20$ m | A1 |

**Answer:** Maximum height is **20 metres**.

### Question 7 [5 marks]
The curve $y = x^3 + ax^2 + bx + c$ passes through the point $(0, 5)$ and has stationary points at $x = 1$ and $x = 3$.

(a) Find the values of $a$, $b$ and $c$. [4 marks]

(b) Determine the nature of each stationary point. [1 mark]

**Solution:**

**(a)**

| Step | Working | Marks |
|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | $\frac{dy}{dx} = 3x^2 + 2ax + b$ | M1 |
| 2 | At stationary points: $3x^2 + 2ax + b = 0$ | |
| 3 | Roots are $x = 1$ and $x = 3$ | |
| 4 | So $3x^2 + 2ax + b = 3(x-1)(x-3) = 3(x^2 - 4x + 3) = 3x^2 - 12x + 9$ | M1 |
| 5 | Comparing: $2a = -12$, so $a = -6$ | |
| 6 | $b = 9$ | M1 |
| 7 | Since curve passes through $(0, 5)$: $c = 5$ | A1 |

**Answer:** $a = -6$, $b = 9$, $c = 5$

**(b)**

| Step | Working | Marks |
|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 6x + 2a = 6x - 12$ | |
| 2 | At $x = 1$: $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = -6 < 0 \Rightarrow$ **maximum** | |
| 3 | At $x = 3$: $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = 6 > 0 \Rightarrow$ **minimum** | A1 |

**Answer:** Maximum at $x = 1$, minimum at $x = 3$.

### Question 8 [5 marks]

(a) Find the times when the particle is at rest. [2 marks]

**(b)**

|------|---------|-------|
| 1 | $a = \frac{dv}{dt} = 6t - 12$ | M1 |

**(c)**

# Exam Focus & Strategy

## Frequently Tested Question Types

### Type 1: Basic Differentiation
- Apply power rule to polynomials
- Find gradient at a specific point
- Identify increasing/decreasing regions

### Type 2: Stationary Points
- Find coordinates by setting $\frac{dy}{dx} = 0$
- Classify using $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2}$ or sign testing
- Sketch curves showing turning points

### Type 3: Motion Problems
- Find velocity from displacement
- Find acceleration from velocity
- Determine when at rest ($v = 0$)
- Find direction of motion
- Find maximum/minimum values

### Type 4: Applied Rate of Change
- Real-world contexts (profit, temperature, volume)
- Find when rate equals specific value
- Interpret meaning of derivatives

---

## Typical Problem-Solving Patterns

### Pattern 1: Finding Gradient at a Point
```
1. Differentiate to find dy/dx
2. Substitute the x-value
3. Result = gradient at that point
```

### Pattern 2: Finding and Classifying Stationary Points
```
1. Differentiate to find dy/dx
2. Set dy/dx = 0 and solve for x
3. Find y-coordinates
4. Find d²y/dx²
5. Evaluate d²y/dx² at each x
6. Classify: > 0 is minimum, < 0 is maximum
```

### Pattern 3: Motion Problems
```
1. s(t) given → v = ds/dt, a = dv/dt
2. "At rest" → solve v = 0
3. "Direction" → check sign of v
4. "Max/min displacement" → find stationary points of s
5. "Max/min velocity" → find stationary points of v
```

---

## Command Word Guidance

| Command Word | What You Must Do |
|-------------|------------------|
| **Differentiate** | Find the derivative; no need to evaluate |
| **Find** | Calculate numerical value |
| **Determine** | Find with clear justification shown |
| **Classify** | State type (max/min/inflection) |
| **Sketch** | Draw showing key features |
| **Explain** | Give reasons for your answer |
| **Verify** | Check that a given value works |

## Common Exam Mistakes

| ❌ Common Error | ✅ Correct Approach |
|----------------|---------------------|
| $\frac{d}{dx}(5) = 5$ | $\frac{d}{dx}(5) = 0$ |
| $\frac{d}{dx}(3x^2) = 6x^2$ | $\frac{d}{dx}(3x^2) = 6x$ |
| $\frac{d}{dx}(x^3) = 3x$ | $\frac{d}{dx}(x^3) = 3x^2$ |
| Maximum when $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} > 0$ | Maximum when $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} < 0$ |
| "At rest" when $s = 0$ | "At rest" when $v = 0$ |
| Finding only x-coordinate | Find BOTH coordinates of stationary point |
| Not classifying the point | Always state "maximum" or "minimum" |

## Key Formulas to Memorize

$$\boxed{\frac{d}{dx}(x^n) = nx^{n-1}}$$

$$\boxed{\text{Stationary point: } \frac{dy}{dx} = 0}$$

$$\boxed{\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} > 0 \Rightarrow \text{Minimum}, \quad \frac{d^2y}{dx^2} < 0 \Rightarrow \text{Maximum}}$$

$$\boxed{v = \frac{ds}{dt}, \quad a = \frac{dv}{dt}}$$

$$\boxed{\text{At rest: } v = 0}$$

---

## Time-Saving Tips

1. **Memorize common derivatives:**
   - $x^2 \to 2x$
   - $x^3 \to 3x^2$
   - $x^4 \to 4x^3$

2. **For motion problems, write down:**
   - $s \xrightarrow{\text{differentiate}} v \xrightarrow{\text{differentiate}} a$

3. **Check your classification:**
   - Maximum = "hill top" = frown shape = $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} < 0$
   - Minimum = "valley bottom" = smile shape = $\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} > 0$

4. **Always find y-coordinates** for stationary points - marks are lost by only finding x!
