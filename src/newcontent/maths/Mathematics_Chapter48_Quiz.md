### Question 1

Given $\mathbf{a} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$, what is $\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}$?

A) $\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}$

B) $\begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ -6 \end{pmatrix}$

C) $\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -6 \end{pmatrix}$

D) $\begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}$

**Answer: A**

$$\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} -1 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 + (-1) \\ -2 + 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}$$

Add corresponding components: $x$-components together, $y$-components together.

### Question 2

What is the magnitude of the vector $\begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ -8 \end{pmatrix}$?

A) 10

B) 14

C) 2

D) 100

**Answer: A**

$$\left|\begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ -8 \end{pmatrix}\right| = \sqrt{6^2 + (-8)^2} = \sqrt{36 + 64} = \sqrt{100} = 10$$

Use Pythagorean theorem. Note: $(-8)^2 = 64$, not $-64$.

### Question 3

If $\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix}$, what is $-3\mathbf{v}$?

A) $\begin{pmatrix} -6 \\ -15 \end{pmatrix}$

B) $\begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ 15 \end{pmatrix}$

C) $\begin{pmatrix} -6 \\ 15 \end{pmatrix}$

D) $\begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ -15 \end{pmatrix}$

**Answer: A**

$$-3\mathbf{v} = -3\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -3 \times 2 \\ -3 \times 5 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -6 \\ -15 \end{pmatrix}$$

Multiply each component by $-3$.

### Question 4

Points $A$ and $B$ have coordinates $(1, 3)$ and $(5, 7)$ respectively. What is $\vec{AB}$?

A) $\begin{pmatrix} -4 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix}$

B) $\begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ 10 \end{pmatrix}$

C) $\begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$

D) $\begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix}$

**Answer: C**

$$\vec{AB} = \begin{pmatrix} x_B - x_A \\ y_B - y_A \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 - 1 \\ 7 - 3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$$

Remember: End point minus start point.

### Question 5

If $\mathbf{a} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{b} = \begin{pmatrix} -6 \\ -3 \end{pmatrix}$, which statement is true?

A) $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ are equal vectors

B) $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ are parallel

C) $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ are perpendicular

D) $|\mathbf{a}| = |\mathbf{b}|$

**Answer: B**

Check if $\mathbf{a} = k\mathbf{b}$ for some $k$:

$$\begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} = k\begin{pmatrix} -6 \\ -3 \end{pmatrix}$$

From $x$-component: $4 = -6k \Rightarrow k = -\frac{2}{3}$

Check $y$-component: $2 = -3 \times \left(-\frac{2}{3}\right) = 2$ ✓

Since $\mathbf{a} = -\frac{2}{3}\mathbf{b}$, the vectors are parallel.

### Question 6

Given $\vec{AB} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix}$, what is $\vec{BA}$?

A) $\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix}$

B) $\begin{pmatrix} -3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$

C) $\begin{pmatrix} -3 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix}$

D) $\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$

**Answer: B**

$$\vec{BA} = -\vec{AB} = -\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$$

Reversing the direction of a vector means negating both components.

### Question 7

If $\mathbf{p} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\mathbf{q} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$, what is $2\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q}$?

A) $\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -6 \end{pmatrix}$

B) $\begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix}$

C) $\begin{pmatrix} 7 \\ -6 \end{pmatrix}$

D) $\begin{pmatrix} 7 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix}$

**Answer: A**

$$2\mathbf{p} = 2\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}$$

$$2\mathbf{p} - \mathbf{q} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 - 3 \\ -2 - 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ -6 \end{pmatrix}$$

### Question 8

The vector $\mathbf{v} = \begin{pmatrix} 12 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix}$ has magnitude:

A) 13

B) 17

C) $\sqrt{17}$

D) 7

**Answer: A**

$$|\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{12^2 + 5^2} = \sqrt{144 + 25} = \sqrt{169} = 13$$

This is a classic Pythagorean triple (5, 12, 13).

### Question 9

Point $P$ has position vector $\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}$. Point $Q$ is such that $\vec{PQ} = \begin{pmatrix} -2 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix}$. What are the coordinates of $Q$?

A) $(1, 7)$

B) $(5, -3)$

C) $(5, 7)$

D) $(1, -3)$

**Answer: A**

$$\vec{OQ} = \vec{OP} + \vec{PQ} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} -2 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 1 \\ 7 \end{pmatrix}$$

So $Q$ has coordinates $(1, 7)$.

### Question 10

If $M$ is the midpoint of $AB$ where $\vec{OA} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\vec{OB} = \begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ 8 \end{pmatrix}$, what is $\vec{OM}$?

A) $\begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix}$

B) $\begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 12 \end{pmatrix}$

C) $\begin{pmatrix} 8 \\ 12 \end{pmatrix}$

D) $\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}$

**Answer: A**

$$\vec{OM} = \frac{\vec{OA} + \vec{OB}}{2} = \frac{\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} 6 \\ 8 \end{pmatrix}}{2} = \frac{\begin{pmatrix} 8 \\ 12 \end{pmatrix}}{2} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 6 \end{pmatrix}$$

### Question 12

If $\mathbf{a} = \begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}$, find the unit vector in the direction of $\mathbf{a}$.

A) $\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -1 \end{pmatrix}$

B) $\begin{pmatrix} \frac{2}{\sqrt{5}} \\ \frac{-1}{\sqrt{5}} \end{pmatrix}$

C) $\begin{pmatrix} \frac{4}{\sqrt{6}} \\ \frac{-2}{\sqrt{6}} \end{pmatrix}$

D) $\begin{pmatrix} \frac{4}{20} \\ \frac{-2}{20} \end{pmatrix}$

**Answer: B**

## SECTION B: TRUE OR FALSE

**For each statement, state whether it is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correction.**

---

### Question 1

$\vec{AB} = \vec{BA}$ for any two points $A$ and $B$.

**FALSE**

**Correction:** $\vec{AB} = -\vec{BA}$

The vectors have the same magnitude but opposite directions.

---

### Question 2

The magnitude of a vector is always positive (or zero).

**TRUE**

Magnitude represents the length of a vector and is calculated using $|\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2} \geq 0$.

---

### Question 3

If $\mathbf{a} = k\mathbf{b}$ for some scalar $k$, then $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ are parallel.

**TRUE**

This is the definition of parallel vectors: one is a scalar multiple of the other.

---

### Question 4

Two vectors with equal magnitudes are equal vectors.

**FALSE**

**Correction:** Two vectors are equal if they have the same magnitude AND the same direction.

Example: $\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}$ both have magnitude 5 but are not equal.

---

### Question 5

The vector $\begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$ is called the zero vector.

**TRUE**

This vector has zero magnitude and no specific direction.

---

### Question 6

If $\vec{OA} = \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\vec{OB} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ 7 \end{pmatrix}$, then $\vec{AB} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$.

**TRUE**

$\vec{AB} = \vec{OB} - \vec{OA} = \begin{pmatrix} 5 \\ 7 \end{pmatrix} - \begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 3 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$

---

### Question 7

Multiplying a vector by $-1$ does not change the vector.

**FALSE**

**Correction:** Multiplying by $-1$ reverses the direction of the vector while keeping the same magnitude.

$-\mathbf{a}$ is the negative of $\mathbf{a}$, pointing in the opposite direction.

---

### Question 8

If $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ are parallel, then $|\mathbf{a}| = |\mathbf{b}|$.

**FALSE**

**Correction:** Parallel vectors have the same (or opposite) direction, but may have different magnitudes.

Example: $\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$ and $\begin{pmatrix} 4 \\ 8 \end{pmatrix}$ are parallel but have different magnitudes ($\sqrt{20}$ and $\sqrt{80}$).

---

### Question 9

The unit vector in the direction of $\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$ is $\begin{pmatrix} 0.6 \\ 0.8 \end{pmatrix}$.

**TRUE**

$|\mathbf{v}| = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = 5$

Unit vector = $\frac{1}{5}\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0.6 \\ 0.8 \end{pmatrix}$

Checking: $\sqrt{0.6^2 + 0.8^2} = 1$ ✓

---

### Question 10

If points $A$, $B$, and $C$ are collinear, then $\vec{AB}$ is parallel to $\vec{BC}$.

**TRUE**

If three points lie on a straight line, the vectors between consecutive points must be parallel (they lie on the same line).

---

### Question 11

The midpoint of two points with position vectors $\mathbf{a}$ and $\mathbf{b}$ has position vector $\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}$.

**FALSE**

**Correction:** The midpoint has position vector $\frac{\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}}{2}$

The sum $\mathbf{a} + \mathbf{b}$ gives a vector twice as far as needed.

---

### Question 12

$2\mathbf{a} + 3\mathbf{a} = 5\mathbf{a}$ for any vector $\mathbf{a}$.

**TRUE**

This is the distributive property. Vector coefficients add just like scalar coefficients:

$2\mathbf{a} + 3\mathbf{a} = (2+3)\mathbf{a} = 5\mathbf{a}$

## SECTION D: EXAM FOCUS & STRATEGY

###  Frequently Tested Question Types

1. **Basic Vector Operations** (Grades 4-5)
   - Addition and subtraction of column vectors
   - Scalar multiplication
   - Finding magnitudes

2. **Geometric Problems** (Grades 5-7)
   - Finding vectors between points
   - Midpoint and section formula applications
   - Proving collinearity

3. **Vector Proofs** (Grades 7-9)
   - Proving geometric properties
   - Working with ratios
   - Expressing vectors in terms of others

### 🟡 Typical Problem-Solving Patterns

**Pattern 1: Expressing One Vector in Terms of Others**
1. Identify the path from start to end point
2. Write the path as a sum of known vectors
3. Simplify and collect like terms

**Pattern 2: Proving Collinearity**
1. Calculate two vectors sharing a common point
2. Show one is a scalar multiple of the other
3. State: "Both vectors are parallel and share point ___, therefore collinear"

**Pattern 3: Finding Ratios**
1. Express vectors in terms of position vectors
2. Set up equation based on given relationship
3. Solve for unknown ratio or position

### 🟢 Command Word Guidance

| Command Word | What Examiners Expect |
|--------------|----------------------|
| **Find** | Direct calculation; show working |
| **Show that** | Full working leading to given result |
| **Prove** | Logical steps with reasons |
| **Express** | Write in terms of given variables |
| **Determine** | Find with clear justification |
| **Calculate** | Numerical answer with working |

### 🔵 How to Gain Full Marks

1. **Use correct notation consistently**
   - Underline vectors: $\underline{a}$
   - Use column vectors: $\begin{pmatrix} x \\ y \end{pmatrix}$
   - Never write vectors without notation

2. **Show all working**
   - "Show that" questions require complete reasoning
   - Even if answer is obvious, write the steps

3. **Check your arithmetic**
   - Watch negative signs
   - Double-check subtraction
   - Verify magnitude calculations

4. **Write clear conclusions**
   - "Therefore, points A, B, C are collinear"
   - "Thus, vectors a and b are parallel"

5. **Label diagrams when provided**
   - Mark vectors with arrows
   - Indicate directions clearly
