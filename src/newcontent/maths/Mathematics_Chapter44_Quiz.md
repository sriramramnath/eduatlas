### Question 1 (1 mark)

In a right-angled triangle, the side opposite the 30° angle is 5 cm. What is the length of the hypotenuse?

**A** 2.5 cm  
**B** 5.8 cm  
**C** 10 cm  
**D** 8.7 cm

**Answer:** C

**Explanation:**
$$\sin 30° = \frac{5}{H}$$
$$H = \frac{5}{\sin 30°} = \frac{5}{0.5} = 10 \text{ cm}$$

---

### Question 2 (1 mark)

Which trigonometric ratio would you use to find the adjacent side when given the hypotenuse and the angle?

**A** $\sin \theta = \frac{A}{H}$  
**B** $\cos \theta = \frac{A}{H}$  
**C** $\sin \theta = \frac{H}{A}$  
**D** $\cos \theta = \frac{H}{A}$

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** Cosine relates the adjacent side to the hypotenuse: $\cos \theta = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$

---

### Question 3 (1 mark)

Calculate $\sin 45°$ to 3 decimal places.

**A** 0.500  
**B** 0.707  
**C** 0.866  
**D** 1.000

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** $\sin 45° = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \approx 0.707$

### Question 4 (1 mark)

In triangle ABC, angle B = 90°, AB = 6 cm and BC = 8 cm. What is the value of $\cos A$?

**A** $\frac{3}{5}$  
**B** $\frac{4}{5}$  
**C** $\frac{3}{4}$  
**D** $\frac{4}{3}$

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:**
- First find AC using Pythagoras: $AC = \sqrt{6^2 + 8^2} = 10 \text{ cm}$
- $\cos A = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}} = \frac{AB}{AC} = \frac{6}{10} = \frac{3}{5}$

### Question 5 (1 mark)

What is the value of angle $\theta$ if $\sin \theta = 0.6$?

**A** 30.0°  
**B** 36.9°  
**C** 53.1°  
**D** 60.0°

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:** $\theta = \sin^{-1}(0.6) = 36.9°$ (to 1 d.p.)

### Question 6 (1 mark)

A right-angled triangle has a hypotenuse of 20 cm and one angle of 60°. What is the length of the adjacent side?

**A** 10 cm  
**B** 17.3 cm  
**C** 8.7 cm  
**D** 15.4 cm

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:**
$$\cos 60° = \frac{A}{20}$$
$$A = 20 \times \cos 60° = 20 \times 0.5 = 10 \text{ cm}$$

### Question 7 (1 mark)

Which statement is correct?

**A** $\sin 30° = \cos 60°$  
**B** $\sin 30° = \cos 30°$  
**C** $\sin 60° = \cos 30°$ is false  
**D** $\sin 45° = \cos 60°$

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** 
- $\sin 30° = 0.5$
- $\cos 60° = 0.5$
- Therefore $\sin 30° = \cos 60°$ ✓

### Question 8 (1 mark)

If $\cos \theta = \frac{4}{5}$, what is $\theta$ to the nearest degree?

**A** 37°  
**B** 41°  
**C** 53°  
**D** 49°

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:** $\theta = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{4}{5}\right) = \cos^{-1}(0.8) = 36.9° \approx 37°$

### Question 9 (1 mark)

In a right-angled triangle, the hypotenuse is 15 cm and the adjacent side is 12 cm. What is the angle between them?

**A** 36.9°  
**B** 53.1°  
**C** 41.4°  
**D** 48.6°

**Answer:** A

**Explanation:**
$$\cos \theta = \frac{12}{15} = 0.8$$
$$\theta = \cos^{-1}(0.8) = 36.9°$$

### Question 10 (1 mark)

Which calculation finds the hypotenuse when the opposite side is 7 cm and the angle is 35°?

**A** $H = 7 \times \sin 35°$  
**B** $H = \frac{7}{\sin 35°}$  
**C** $H = 7 \times \cos 35°$  
**D** $H = \frac{7}{\cos 35°}$

**Answer:** B

**Explanation:**
$$\sin 35° = \frac{7}{H}$$

### Question 16 (1 mark)

**Statement:** If $\sin \theta = 0.5$, then $\theta = 30°$ is the only possible angle.

**Answer:** FALSE (in context of IGCSE 0-180°)

**Correction:** $\theta = 30°$ or $\theta = 150°$ (though IGCSE focuses on acute angles in right triangles, where only $30°$ applies)

### Question 17 (1 mark)

**Statement:** When finding the hypotenuse using sine, you multiply the opposite by $\sin \theta$.

**Answer:** FALSE

**Correction:** When finding the hypotenuse using sine, you **divide** the opposite by $\sin \theta$: $H = \frac{O}{\sin \theta}$

**Answer:** TRUE

### Question 19 (1 mark)

**Answer:** TRUE

### Question 20 (1 mark)

**Statement:** For a right-angled triangle with angle $\theta$, if the hypotenuse is 1, then the opposite side equals $\cos \theta$.

**Answer:** FALSE

**Correction:** If the hypotenuse is 1, then the opposite side equals $\sin \theta$, and the adjacent side equals $\cos \theta$.

### Question 21 (3 marks)

In triangle PQR, angle Q = 90°, PQ = 8 cm and angle P = 40°.

Calculate the length of PR.

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Identify the sides
- PR is the hypotenuse (opposite the right angle)
- PQ = 8 cm is adjacent to angle P

**Step 2:** Choose the correct ratio
$$\cos 40° = \frac{8}{PR}$$

**Step 3:** Rearrange and calculate

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | Correct trigonometric ratio selected ($\cos 40° = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}}$) |
| M1 | Correct rearrangement: $PR = \frac{8}{\cos 40°}$ |
| A1 | $PR = \frac{8}{0.766...} = 10.4 \text{ cm (to 1 d.p.)}$ |

**Answer:** PR = 10.4 cm (to 1 d.p.)

---

### Question 22 (3 marks)

A ladder of length 5 m rests against a vertical wall. The ladder makes an angle of 65° with the horizontal ground.

Calculate how far the foot of the ladder is from the wall.

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Draw diagram and identify sides
- Ladder (5 m) is the hypotenuse
- Distance from wall is the adjacent side (to 65°)

**Step 2:** Apply cosine ratio
$$\cos 65° = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{5}$$

**Step 3:** Solve

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | $\cos 65° = \frac{x}{5}$ |
| M1 | $x = 5 \times \cos 65°$ |
| A1 | $x = 5 \times 0.4226... = 2.11 \text{ m (to 2 d.p.)}$ |

**Answer:** 2.11 m (to 2 d.p.)

### Question 23 (4 marks)

In triangle ABC, AB = 12 cm, BC = 5 cm and angle B = 90°.

**(a)** Calculate the length of AC. (2 marks)
**(b)** Calculate the size of angle ACB. (2 marks)

**Solution:**

**(a)** Finding AC using Pythagoras:

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | $AC^2 = 12^2 + 5^2 = 144 + 25 = 169$ |
| A1 | $AC = \sqrt{169} = 13 \text{ cm}$ |

**Answer (a):** AC = 13 cm

**(b)** Finding angle ACB:

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | $\sin(\text{ACB}) = \frac{5}{13}$ or $\cos(\text{ACB}) = \frac{12}{13}$ |
| A1 | $\text{ACB} = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{5}{13}\right) = 22.6°$ (to 1 d.p.) |

**Answer (b):** Angle ACB = 22.6° (to 1 d.p.)

### Question 24 (4 marks)

A ship sails from port A on a bearing of 055° for 20 km to reach point B.

Calculate how far:
**(a)** east of A the ship is (2 marks)
**(b)** north of A the ship is (2 marks)

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Interpret the bearing
- Bearing 055° means 55° clockwise from North
- The angle between the path and the East direction is: $90° - 55° = 35°$

Or: The angle between the path and the North direction is $55°$

**Better approach:** Draw North line at A, then the path AB at 55° to it.

**(a)** Distance East:

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | East distance = $20 \times \sin 55°$ |
| A1 | East distance = $20 \times 0.819... = 16.4 \text{ km (to 1 d.p.)}$ |

**Answer (a):** 16.4 km east

**(b)** Distance North:

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | North distance = $20 \times \cos 55°$ |
| A1 | North distance = $20 \times 0.574... = 11.5 \text{ km (to 1 d.p.)}$ |

**Answer (b):** 11.5 km north

### Question 25 (5 marks)

In the diagram, ABC is a right-angled triangle with AC = 15 cm and angle BAC = 38°.

Point D lies on BC such that AD is perpendicular to BC.

**(a)** Calculate the length of BC. (2 marks)
**(b)** Calculate the length of AD. (3 marks)

**Solution:**

**(a)** Finding BC:

First, find AB and BC.

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | $\cos 38° = \frac{AB}{15}$ so $AB = 15 \times \cos 38° = 11.82 \text{ cm}$ |
| M1 | $\sin 38° = \frac{BC}{15}$ so $BC = 15 \times \sin 38° = 9.24 \text{ cm}$ |

**Answer (a):** BC = 9.24 cm (to 2 d.p.)

**(b)** Finding AD:

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | In triangle ABD: $\sin(\text{angle ABD}) = \frac{AD}{AB}$ |
| M1 | angle ABD = $90° - 38° = 52°$ |
| M1 | $AD = AB \times \sin 52° = 11.82 \times \sin 52° = 9.32 \text{ cm}$ |
| A1 | AD = 9.32 cm (to 2 d.p.) |

**Answer (b):** AD = 9.32 cm (to 2 d.p.)

### Question 26 (4 marks)

Two ships leave port P at the same time.
- Ship A sails on a bearing of 040° for 25 km
- Ship B sails on a bearing of 100° for 30 km

Calculate the distance between the two ships.

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Find the positions
- Ship A: 25 km at bearing 040°
- Ship B: 30 km at bearing 100°

**Step 2:** Find North and East components

**For Ship A:**
- North: $25 \times \cos 40° = 19.15$ km
- East: $25 \times \sin 40° = 16.07$ km

**For Ship B:**
- Bearing 100°: angle from North is 100° clockwise
- North: $30 \times \cos 100° = 30 \times (-0.174) = -5.21$ km (actually south)
- East: $30 \times \sin 100° = 29.54$ km

**Step 3:** Find relative position

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | North difference: $19.15 - (-5.21) = 24.36$ km |
| M1 | East difference: $29.54 - 16.07 = 13.47$ km |
| M1 | Distance = $\sqrt{24.36^2 + 13.47^2}$ |
| A1 | Distance = $\sqrt{593.4 + 181.4} = \sqrt{774.8} = 27.8$ km |

**Answer:** 27.8 km (to 1 d.p.)

### Question 27 (3 marks)

Prove that $\sin \theta + \cos \theta$ has its maximum value when $\theta = 45°$.

**Solution:**

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | At $\theta = 45°$: $\sin 45° + \cos 45° = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} + \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{2}} = \sqrt{2} \approx 1.414$ |
| M1 | At $\theta = 0°$: $\sin 0° + \cos 0° = 0 + 1 = 1$ |
| M1 | At $\theta = 90°$: $\sin 90° + \cos 90° = 1 + 0 = 1$ |

Since $\sqrt{2} > 1$, the maximum value occurs at $\theta = 45°$.

**Answer:** Maximum value = $\sqrt{2}$ when $\theta = 45°$

### Question 28 (5 marks)

A rectangular field ABCD has AB = 80 m and BC = 60 m.
A diagonal path AC runs across the field.

**(a)** Calculate the length of the diagonal AC. (2 marks)
**(b)** Calculate the angle that AC makes with AB. (2 marks)
**(c)** A tree is planted at point E on AC such that the perpendicular distance from E to AB is 30 m. Calculate AE. (1 mark)

**Solution:**

**(a)** Finding AC:

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | $AC = \sqrt{80^2 + 60^2} = \sqrt{6400 + 3600} = \sqrt{10000}$ |
| A1 | $AC = 100 \text{ m}$ |

**Answer (a):** AC = 100 m

**(b)** Finding angle CAB:

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | $\tan(\text{CAB}) = \frac{60}{80} = \frac{3}{4}$ OR $\sin(\text{CAB}) = \frac{60}{100} = 0.6$ |
| A1 | $\text{CAB} = \tan^{-1}(0.75) = 36.9°$ |

**Answer (b):** 36.9° (to 1 d.p.)

**(c)** Finding AE:

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | $\sin(\text{CAB}) = \frac{30}{AE}$, so $AE = \frac{30}{\sin 36.9°}$ |
| A1 | $AE = \frac{30}{0.6} = 50 \text{ m}$ |

**Answer (c):** AE = 50 m

### Question 30 (6 marks)

The diagram shows a triangle ABC with AB = 10 cm, angle BAC = 50° and angle ABC = 70°.

**(a)** Calculate the length of BC. (3 marks)
**(b)** Calculate the area of triangle ABC. (3 marks)

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Find angle ACB
$$\text{Angle ACB} = 180° - 50° - 70° = 60°$$

This is NOT a right-angled triangle, but we can draw an altitude from C to AB.

Let D be the foot of the perpendicular from C to AB.

**(a)** Finding BC:

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | In right triangle ADC: $CD = AC \times \sin 50°$ |
| M1 | In right triangle BDC: $CD = BC \times \sin 70°$ |
| M1 | Using sine rule (or area formula): $\frac{BC}{\sin 50°} = \frac{10}{\sin 60°}$ |
| A1 | $BC = \frac{10 \times \sin 50°}{\sin 60°} = \frac{10 \times 0.766}{0.866} = 8.85 \text{ cm}$ |

**Answer (a):** BC = 8.85 cm (to 2 d.p.)

**(b)** Finding the area:

| Mark | Working |
|------|---------|
| M1 | $\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times AB \times AC \times \sin 50°$ OR find height CD first |
| M1 | Find AC: $\frac{AC}{\sin 70°} = \frac{10}{\sin 60°}$, so $AC = \frac{10 \times \sin 70°}{\sin 60°} = 10.9 \text{ cm}$ |
| M1 | $\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 10.9 \times \sin 50° = 41.8 \text{ cm}^2$ |
| A1 | Area = 41.8 cm² (to 1 d.p.) |

**Answer (b):** Area = 41.8 cm² (to 1 d.p.)

## SECTION D: Exam Focus & Strategy

---

### Frequently Tested Question Types

1. **Basic trigonometry calculations** (2-3 marks)
   - Finding a side given angle and another side
   - Finding an angle given two sides
   - Always draw and label the triangle first

2. **Word problems with real-world context** (3-4 marks)
   - Ladder problems
   - Height/distance problems
   - Bearings problems
   - Identify the right angle and label from the angle given

3. **Multi-step problems** (4-6 marks)
   - Combining trigonometry with Pythagoras
   - Problems requiring two trigonometric calculations
   - Area calculations involving heights found by trigonometry

4. **Exact value questions** (1-2 marks)
   - Non-calculator paper
   - Know sin, cos for 30°, 45°, 60°

---

### Typical Problem-Solving Patterns

| Pattern | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| Find side with angle given | Identify O, A, H → Choose ratio → Multiply or divide |
| Find angle with sides given | Identify O, A, H → Calculate ratio → Use inverse function |
| Word problem | Draw diagram → Mark knowns → Apply trigonometry |
| Multi-step | Break into parts → Solve each part → Combine results |

---

### Command Word Guidance

| Command Word | What It Means |
|--------------|---------------|
| **Calculate** | Show working and give numerical answer |
| **Find** | Similar to calculate - show method |
| **Show that** | Work towards the given result |
| **Determine** | Use mathematical reasoning |
| **Work out** | Calculate with working shown |

---

### How to Gain Full Marks

#### ✅ Essential Checklist:

1. **Show ALL working**
   - Write the formula first
   - Show substitution
   - Show rearrangement
   - Show final answer with units

2. **Correct labelling**
   - Mark the right angle
   - Label H, O, A clearly
   - Show which angle you're using

3. **Appropriate accuracy**
   - Don't round intermediate values
   - Give final answer to specified accuracy (usually 1 or 3 significant figures, or 1 decimal place)
   - Include units

4. **Calculator checks**
   - Verify DEGREE mode
   - Use brackets for complex calculations
   - Check answer is reasonable

#### 📋 Mark Scheme Awareness:

| Mark Type | How to Earn It |
|-----------|----------------|
| **M1 (Method)** | Correct formula/ratio selection and substitution |
| **A1 (Accuracy)** | Correct numerical answer |
| **B1 (Independent)** | Correct statement without method shown |
| **C1 (Communication)** | Clear explanation or correct units |

---

### Last-Minute Tips

- ✅ Check calculator mode (DEGREES not RADIANS)
- ✅ Always label sides relative to YOUR angle
- ✅ Remember: Hypotenuse is ALWAYS the longest side
- ✅ Show working: $O = H \times \sin \theta$ or $H = \frac{O}{\sin \theta}$
- ✅ For inverse: $\theta = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{O}{H}\right)$
- ✅ Exact values: $\sin 30° = 0.5$, $\cos 60° = 0.5$
- ✅ Include units in final answer
- ✅ Check: side lengths cannot exceed hypotenuse
