## Quick Revision Synopsis

## 1. STRAIGHT-LINE GRAPHS

### Key Formula

$$\boxed{y = mx + c}$$

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| $m$ | Gradient (slope) |
| $c$ | Y-intercept |

---

### 📐 Gradient Formula

$$m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} = \frac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}}$$

---

### ⚡ Quick Rules: Gradient Interpretation

| Gradient | Line Direction |
|----------|----------------|
| $m > 0$ | ↗️ Upward (left to right) |
| $m < 0$ | ↙️ Downward (left to right) |
| $m = 0$ | ↔️ Horizontal |
| Undefined | ↕️ Vertical |

---

### 🎯 Finding the Equation

| Given | Method |
|-------|--------|
| Gradient $m$ + Y-intercept $c$ | Substitute directly into $y = mx + c$ |
| Gradient $m$ + Point $(x_1, y_1)$ | Use $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$ |
| Two points | Calculate $m$ first, then find $c$ |

---

###  Special Lines

| Type | Equation | Gradient |
|------|----------|----------|
| Horizontal | $y = c$ | $m = 0$ |
| Vertical | $x = k$ | Undefined |
| Through origin | $y = mx$ | $m$ (any) |

---

### 🔗 Parallel and Perpendicular Lines

| Relationship | Rule |
|--------------|------|
| **Parallel** | $m_1 = m_2$ (same gradient) |
| **Perpendicular** | $m_1 \times m_2 = -1$ |

**Perpendicular gradient:** $m_2 = -\frac{1}{m_1}$

---

### 📍 Coordinate Formulas

**Midpoint:**
$$M = \left(\frac{x_1 + x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2}\right)$$

**Distance:**
$$d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$$

---

## 2. SKETCHING GRAPHS

### 📊 Key Features to Show

| Feature | How to Find |
|---------|-------------|
| Y-intercept | Set $x = 0$ |
| X-intercept(s) | Set $y = 0$ |
| Turning point (quadratics) | $x = -\frac{b}{2a}$ |

---

### 📈 Graph Shape Summary

| Function | Shape | Key Features |
|----------|-------|--------------|
| $y = mx + c$ | Straight line | Gradient $m$, intercept $(0, c)$ |
| $y = ax^2 + bx + c$ | Parabola | $a>0$: ∪, $a<0$: ∩ |
| $y = x^3$ | S-curve | Through origin |
| $y = \frac{k}{x}$ | Hyperbola | Asymptotes at axes |
| $y = ka^x$ | Exponential | Horizontal asymptote $y=0$ |

---

### 🌟 Quadratic Key Points

**Turning Point:**
$$x = -\frac{b}{2a}$$

**Completed Square Form:** $y = a(x-h)^2 + k$
- Turning point: $(h, k)$

---

### ⚡ Quick Shape Recognition

| Equation | Opens | Vertex |
|----------|-------|--------|
| $y = x^2$ | Upward | $(0,0)$ |
| $y = -x^2$ | Downward | $(0,0)$ |
| $y = (x-2)^2$ | Upward | $(2,0)$ |
| $y = x^2 + 3$ | Upward | $(0,3)$ |
| $y = 2x^2$ | Upward (steeper) | $(0,0)$ |

---

### 📉 Reciprocal Graphs ($y = \frac{k}{x}$)

| $k$ Value | Quadrants |
|-----------|-----------|
| $k > 0$ | I and III |
| $k < 0$ | II and IV |

**Asymptotes:** $x = 0$ and $y = 0$

---

## 3. SIMULTANEOUS EQUATIONS (GRAPHICAL)

### 🎯 Core Concept

**Solution = Point of Intersection**

---

###  Method Steps

1. **Plot both graphs** on same axes
2. **Identify intersection point(s)**
3. **Read coordinates** $(x, y)$

---

### 📊 Types of Solutions

| Situation | Solutions |
|-----------|-----------|
| Two lines (different gradients) | **1** intersection |
| Parallel lines | **0** intersections |
| Same line | **Infinite** points |
| Line and parabola (crossing) | **2** intersections |
| Line and parabola (tangent) | **1** intersection |
| Line and parabola (missing) | **0** intersections |

## 🚨 COMMON EXAM ERRORS

| Mistake | Correction |
|---------|------------|
| Wrong sign for gradient | Check if line goes up (+) or down (-) |
| Swapping $x$ and $y$ | Remember: $(x, y)$ order |
| Not labeling axes | Always write $x$ and $y$ on axes |
| Forgetting to find $c$ | Complete all working for full equation |
| Drawing straight curves | Use smooth curves for quadratics |
| Reading wrong scale | Check scale carefully before reading |
