### Question 1
**Simplify: $4x + 3x - 2x$**

A) $5x$

B) $9x$

C) $6x$

D) $5x^2$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$4x + 3x - 2x = (4 + 3 - 2)x = 5x$$
Combine the coefficients: $4 + 3 = 7$, then $7 - 2 = 5$. The variable part $x$ remains unchanged.

---

### Question 2
**Simplify: $3a + 2b - a + 5b$**

A) $2a + 7b$

B) $4a + 7b$

C) $2a - 3b$

D) $4ab$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
- Collect $a$ terms: $3a - a = 2a$
- Collect $b$ terms: $2b + 5b = 7b$
- Result: $2a + 7b$

---

### Question 3
**Simplify: $5x^2 + 3x - 2x^2 + x$**

A) $3x^2 + 4x$

B) $7x^2 + 4x$

C) $3x^2 + 2x$

D) $7x^3$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
- $x^2$ terms: $5x^2 - 2x^2 = 3x^2$
- $x$ terms: $3x + x = 4x$
- Result: $3x^2 + 4x$

*Note: $x^2$ and $x$ are NOT like terms (different powers).*

### Question 12
**Which of the following are like terms?**

A) $3x$ and $3x^2$

B) $2ab$ and $2a$

C) $5y$ and $-2y$

D) $4x$ and $4y$

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**
Like terms must have the same variable with the same power.
- $5y$ and $-2y$ both have $y^1$ ✓
- $3x$ has $x^1$ but $3x^2$ has $x^2$ ✗
- $2ab$ has both $a$ and $b$, but $2a$ only has $a$ ✗
- $4x$ has $x$ but $4y$ has $y$ ✗

### Question 16
**Statement:** $3x + 5x = 8x^2$

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** $3x + 5x = 8x$

**Explanation:** When adding like terms, add the coefficients but keep the variable the same. You do NOT multiply the powers.

---

### Question 17
**Statement:** $x^2$ and $x$ are like terms.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** $x^2$ and $x$ are NOT like terms because they have different powers ($x^2$ vs $x^1$).

**Explanation:** Like terms must have the same variable raised to the same power.

---

### Question 18
**Statement:** $-3(x - 2) = -3x + 6$

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:**
$$-3(x - 2) = -3 \times x + (-3) \times (-2) = -3x + 6$$
Negative × negative = positive, so $(-3)(-2) = +6$.

---

### Question 19
**Statement:** To solve $2x + 3 = 11$, first subtract 3 from both sides.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:**
$$2x + 3 = 11$$
$$2x + 3 - 3 = 11 - 3$$
$$2x = 8$$
$$x = 4$$

The correct first step is to subtract 3 (remove the constant first).

---

### Question 20
**Statement:** $5x - 2x = 3$

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** $5x - 2x = 3x$

**Explanation:** When subtracting like terms, subtract the coefficients: $5 - 2 = 3$, keeping $x$ as the variable. The result is $3x$, not just $3$.

---

### Question 21
**Statement:** $(-2)^2 = -4$

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** $(-2)^2 = 4$

**Explanation:**
$(-2)^2 = (-2) \times (-2) = 4$
Negative × negative = positive.
*Note: $-2^2 = -4$ (different meaning - square of 2, then negative).*

---

### Question 22
**Statement:** $4(x + 2) = 4x + 8$ shows correct expansion.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:**
$$4(x + 2) = 4 \times x + 4 \times 2 = 4x + 8$$
Every term inside the bracket is multiplied by 4.

---

### Question 23
**Statement:** If $3x = 18$, then $x = 6$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:**
$$3x = 18$$
$$x = \frac{18}{3}$$
$$x = 6$$

*Check: $3 \times 6 = 18$ ✓*

---

### Question 24
**Statement:** $2a + 3b = 5ab$

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** $2a + 3b = 2a + 3b$ (cannot be simplified further)

**Explanation:** $2a$ and $3b$ are NOT like terms (different variables). They cannot be combined by addition.

---

### Question 25
**Statement:** "5 less than $x$" is written as $x - 5$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** "Less than" means we subtract FROM $x$. So "5 less than $x$" means $x - 5$, not $5 - x$.

## EXAM FOCUS & STRATEGY

### Frequently Tested Question Types

1. **Simplifying expressions** (1-2 marks)
   - Identify like terms
   - Combine coefficients

2. **Expanding brackets** (2-3 marks)
   - Multiply ALL terms
   - Watch negative signs

3. **Solving equations** (2-4 marks)
   - Show every step
   - Balance method
   - Check your answer

4. **Forming and solving equations** (4-6 marks)
   - Define variable clearly
   - Form correct expression
   - Solve and check

### Command Words Guidance

| Command | Action Required |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Simplify** | Collect like terms, give final answer |
| **Expand** | Remove brackets, show working |
| **Expand and simplify** | Expand then collect like terms |
| **Solve** | Find the value of the variable |
| **Evaluate** | Substitute and calculate |
| **Find** | Determine the value |

### How to Gain Full Marks

1. **Show all working** - every step on a new line
2. **Write clearly** - examiners must follow your method
3. **Check answers** - substitute back into original
