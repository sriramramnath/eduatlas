### Question 1
What is the period of the function $y = \sin(2\theta)$?

A) $180°$

B) $360°$

C) $720°$

D) $90°$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** The period of $y = \sin(b\theta)$ is $\frac{360°}{b}$. Here $b = 2$, so period $= \frac{360°}{2} = 180°$.

---

### Question 2
What is the value of $\sin 150°$?

A) $-\frac{1}{2}$

B) $\frac{1}{2}$

C) $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$

D) $-\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** $150°$ is in the 2nd quadrant where sine is positive. $\sin 150° = \sin(180° - 30°) = \sin 30° = \frac{1}{2}$.

---

### Question 3
For what values of $\theta$ is $\tan\theta$ undefined in the range $0° \leq \theta \leq 360°$?

A) $0°$ and $180°$

B) $90°$ and $270°$

C) $180°$ and $360°$

D) $45°$ and $225°$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** $\tan\theta = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta}$. This is undefined when $\cos\theta = 0$, which occurs at $\theta = 90°$ and $\theta = 270°$.

### Question 4
In triangle $ABC$, angle $A = 40°$, angle $B = 65°$, and side $a = 10$ cm. Which rule should be used to find side $b$?

A) SOHCAHTOA

B) Cosine Rule

C) Sine Rule

D) Area formula

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** We have two angles and one side (AAS), so the Sine Rule is appropriate. $\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B}$.

---

### Question 5
In triangle $PQR$, $p = 8$ cm, $q = 10$ cm, and angle $R = 60°$. Which formula should be used to find side $r$?

A) $r = \sqrt{p^2 + q^2}$

B) $r = \sqrt{p^2 + q^2 - 2pq\sin R}$

C) $r^2 = p^2 + q^2 - 2pq\cos R$

D) $\frac{r}{\sin R} = \frac{p}{\sin P}$

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** We have two sides and the included angle (SAS), so the Cosine Rule applies: $r^2 = p^2 + q^2 - 2pq\cos R$.

### Question 6
The area of a triangle with sides 6 cm and 8 cm and included angle $30°$ is:

A) $24 \text{ cm}^2$

B) $12 \text{ cm}^2$

C) $48 \text{ cm}^2$

D) $6 \text{ cm}^2$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Area $= \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C = \frac{1}{2} \times 6 \times 8 \times \sin 30° = \frac{1}{2} \times 6 \times 8 \times \frac{1}{2} = 12 \text{ cm}^2$.

### Question 7
Which statement about the graphs of $y = \sin\theta$ and $y = \cos\theta$ is TRUE?

A) Both have the same period and different amplitudes

B) Both have the same amplitude and different periods

C) Both have the same period and the same amplitude

D) $\cos\theta$ has twice the period of $\sin\theta$

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Both $y = \sin\theta$ and $y = \cos\theta$ have period $360°$ and amplitude $1$.

### Question 8
If $\cos\theta = -0.8$ and $0° \leq \theta \leq 180°$, what is $\theta$?

A) $36.9°$

B) $53.1°$

C) $126.9°$

D) $143.1°$

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** $\cos\theta = -0.8$. Reference angle $= \cos^{-1}(0.8) = 36.9°$. Since cosine is negative and $0° \leq \theta \leq 180°$, the angle is in the 2nd quadrant: $\theta = 180° - 36.9° = 143.1°$. Let me verify: $\cos 126.9° = -\cos 53.1° = -0.6$, so answer C is incorrect. Let me recalculate: $\cos^{-1}(-0.8) = 143.1°$. So the correct answer should be D.

**Corrected Answer: D**

**Correction:** $\theta = \cos^{-1}(-0.8) = 143.1°$ (since cosine is negative, the angle must be between $90°$ and $180°$).

### Question 9
The transformation $y = 3\cos\theta$ from $y = \cos\theta$ is:

A) A vertical stretch by factor 3

B) A horizontal stretch by factor 3

C) A translation up by 3 units

D) A translation right by 3 units

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** The coefficient $3$ in front of $\cos\theta$ causes a vertical stretch by factor $3$. The amplitude changes from $1$ to $3$.

### Question 10
In triangle $ABC$, if $a = 7$ cm, $b = 8$ cm, and $c = 9$ cm, what type of angle is angle $C$?

A) Acute

B) Right-angled

C) Obtuse

D) Cannot be determined

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Using the Cosine Rule: $\cos C = \frac{a^2 + b^2 - c^2}{2ab} = \frac{49 + 64 - 81}{2 \times 7 \times 8} = \frac{32}{112} = \frac{2}{7} > 0$. Since $\cos C > 0$, angle $C$ is acute.

### Question 11
What is the maximum value of $y = 5\sin\theta - 2$?

A) $5$

B) $7$

C) $3$

D) $-7$

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Maximum value of $\sin\theta$ is $1$. So maximum of $y = 5\sin\theta - 2$ is $5(1) - 2 = 3$.

### Question 12
Which equation gives the correct relationship for finding angle $A$ in triangle $ABC$ with sides $a = 5$ cm, $b = 7$ cm, and $c = 10$ cm?

A) $\sin A = \frac{5 \times \sin B}{7}$

B) $\cos A = \frac{7^2 + 10^2 - 5^2}{2 \times 7 \times 10}$

C) $a^2 = b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\sin A$

D) $\cos A = \frac{5^2 + 7^2 - 10^2}{2 \times 5 \times 7}$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** With three sides (SSS), use Cosine Rule to find an angle. $\cos A = \frac{b^2 + c^2 - a^2}{2bc}$ where $a$ is the side opposite angle $A$.

**Statement:** The period of $y = \tan\theta$ is $360°$.

**Answer: FALSE**

### Question 14
**Statement:** When using the Sine Rule to find an angle given two sides and a non-included angle, there is always exactly one solution.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** There may be two solutions (the ambiguous case). Always check if $180° - \theta_1$ gives a valid second angle.

---

### Question 15
**Statement:** The cosine rule can only be used for obtuse-angled triangles.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The cosine rule can be used for any triangle, whether acute, right-angled, or obtuse.

---

### Question 16
**Statement:** $\sin(180° - \theta) = \sin\theta$

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This is a standard trigonometric identity. Sine is positive in both the 1st and 2nd quadrants.

---

### Question 17
**Statement:** The graph of $y = \cos\theta$ is the graph of $y = \sin\theta$ shifted $90°$ to the right.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The graph of $y = \cos\theta$ is the graph of $y = \sin\theta$ shifted $90°$ to the LEFT (or $\cos\theta = \sin(\theta + 90°)$).

---

### Question 18
**Statement:** If $\cos A < 0$ in a triangle, then angle $A$ is obtuse.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** In a triangle, angles range from $0°$ to $180°$. Cosine is negative only for angles between $90°$ and $180°$ (obtuse angles).

---

### Question 19
**Statement:** The area of a triangle can be found using the formula $\text{Area} = ab\sin C$ where $a$ and $b$ are any two sides.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The formula is $\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2}ab\sin C$, and angle $C$ must be the included angle between sides $a$ and $b$.

---

### Question 20
**Statement:** The Sine Rule states that $\frac{\sin A}{a} = \frac{\sin B}{b} = \frac{\sin C}{c}$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This is an equivalent form of the Sine Rule: $\frac{a}{\sin A} = \frac{b}{\sin B} = \frac{c}{\sin C}$.

---

### Question 21
**Statement:** When solving $\cos\theta = 0.5$ in the range $0° \leq \theta \leq 360°$, the solutions are $\theta = 60°$ and $\theta = 300°$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** $\cos^{-1}(0.5) = 60°$. Cosine is positive in the 1st and 4th quadrants, so the second solution is $360° - 60° = 300°$.

---

### Question 22
**Statement:** The amplitude of $y = -2\sin\theta$ is $-2$.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The amplitude is $|{-2}| = 2$. Amplitude is always positive; the negative sign indicates a reflection.

### Question 23 [3 marks]

Solve the equation $\sin\theta = -0.6$ for $0° \leq \theta \leq 360°$.

**Solution:**

**[M1]** Find the principal value:
$$\sin^{-1}(0.6) = 36.9°$$

**[M1]** Identify quadrants where sine is negative:
- Sine is negative in the 3rd and 4th quadrants
- $\theta_1 = 180° + 36.9° = 216.9°$
- $\theta_2 = 360° - 36.9° = 323.1°$

**[A1]** **Answer:** $\theta = 216.9°$ or $\theta = 323.1°$ (to 1 d.p.)

---

### Question 24 [4 marks]

The graph of $y = \sin\theta$ is transformed to give the graph of $y = 2\sin(3\theta) - 1$.

Describe fully the two transformations that have been applied.

**Solution:**

**Analysis of $y = 2\sin(3\theta) - 1$:**

**[M1]** The coefficient $2$ causes a **vertical stretch** by factor $2$.
- The amplitude changes from $1$ to $2$.

**[M1]** The coefficient $3$ inside the angle causes a **horizontal stretch** by factor $\frac{1}{3}$.
- The period changes from $360°$ to $\frac{360°}{3} = 120°$.

**[M1]** The $-1$ causes a **translation downward** by $1$ unit.

**[A1]** **Answer:** Vertical stretch by factor $2$, horizontal stretch by factor $\frac{1}{3}$ (period becomes $120°$), and translation down by $1$ unit.

### Question 25 [5 marks]

In triangle $ABC$, $AB = 12$ cm, $BC = 8$ cm, and angle $ABC = 72°$.

(a) Calculate the length of $AC$. [3 marks]

(b) Calculate the area of triangle $ABC$. [2 marks]

**Solution:**

**(a) Finding length $AC$:**

**[M1]** Identify that we have SAS, so use the Cosine Rule:
$$AC^2 = AB^2 + BC^2 - 2 \times AB \times BC \times \cos B$$

**[M1]** Substitute values:
$$AC^2 = 12^2 + 8^2 - 2 \times 12 \times 8 \times \cos 72°$$
$$AC^2 = 144 + 64 - 192 \times 0.3090...$$
$$AC^2 = 208 - 59.33...$$
$$AC^2 = 148.67...$$

**[A1]** $AC = \sqrt{148.67...} = 12.2$ cm (to 1 d.p.)

**(b) Finding the area:**

**[M1]** Use the area formula:
$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times AB \times BC \times \sin B$$
$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 12 \times 8 \times \sin 72°$$

**[A1]** $\text{Area} = 48 \times 0.9511... = 45.7 \text{ cm}^2$ (to 1 d.p.)

### Question 26 [6 marks]

In triangle $PQR$, $PQ = 14$ cm, $QR = 18$ cm, and $PR = 10$ cm.

(a) Calculate the size of angle $PQR$. [3 marks]

(b) Calculate the size of the largest angle in the triangle. [3 marks]

**Solution:**

**(a) Finding angle $PQR$:**

**[M1]** Use the Cosine Rule for finding an angle:
$$\cos Q = \frac{PQ^2 + QR^2 - PR^2}{2 \times PQ \times QR}$$

**[M1]** Substitute values:
$$\cos Q = \frac{14^2 + 18^2 - 10^2}{2 \times 14 \times 18}$$
$$\cos Q = \frac{196 + 324 - 100}{504}$$
$$\cos Q = \frac{420}{504} = \frac{5}{6}$$

**[A1]** $Q = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{5}{6}\right) = 33.6°$ (to 1 d.p.)

**(b) Finding the largest angle:**

**[M1]** The largest angle is opposite the longest side. The longest side is $QR = 18$ cm, so we need angle $P$.

**[M1]** Use the Cosine Rule:
$$\cos P = \frac{PQ^2 + PR^2 - QR^2}{2 \times PQ \times PR}$$
$$\cos P = \frac{14^2 + 10^2 - 18^2}{2 \times 14 \times 10}$$
$$\cos P = \frac{196 + 100 - 324}{280}$$
$$\cos P = \frac{-28}{280} = -0.1$$

**[A1]** $P = \cos^{-1}(-0.1) = 95.7°$ (to 1 d.p.)

### Question 27 [5 marks]

In triangle $XYZ$, $XY = 9$ cm, $XZ = 11$ cm, and angle $Y = 42°$.

Find the two possible values of angle $Z$.

**Solution:**

**[M1]** We have SSA (two sides and non-included angle), so use the Sine Rule:
$$\frac{XY}{\sin Z} = \frac{XZ}{\sin Y}$$
$$\frac{9}{\sin Z} = \frac{11}{\sin 42°}$$

**[M1]** Rearrange to find $\sin Z$:
$$\sin Z = \frac{9 \times \sin 42°}{11}$$
$$\sin Z = \frac{9 \times 0.6691...}{11}$$
$$\sin Z = 0.5475...$$

**[M1]** Find the principal value:
$$Z_1 = \sin^{-1}(0.5475...) = 33.2°$$

**[M1]** Check for the second solution (ambiguous case):
$$Z_2 = 180° - 33.2° = 146.8°$$

**Verification:**
- For $Z_1 = 33.2°$: $Y + Z_1 = 42° + 33.2° = 75.2° < 180°$ ✓
- For $Z_2 = 146.8°$: $Y + Z_2 = 42° + 146.8° = 188.8° > 180°$ ✗

**[A1]** **Answer:** $Z = 33.2°$ only (to 1 d.p.)

*Note: The second solution would make the angle sum exceed $180°$, so only one valid solution exists.*

### Question 28 [4 marks]

A triangle has sides of length $5$ cm, $7$ cm, and $8$ cm.

Show that the area of the triangle is $10\sqrt{3} \text{ cm}^2$.

**Solution:**

**[M1]** Find an angle using the Cosine Rule. Let's find the angle opposite side $8$:
$$\cos C = \frac{5^2 + 7^2 - 8^2}{2 \times 5 \times 7}$$
$$\cos C = \frac{25 + 49 - 64}{70}$$
$$\cos C = \frac{10}{70} = \frac{1}{7}$$

**[M1]** Find $\sin C$:
$$\sin C = \sqrt{1 - \cos^2 C} = \sqrt{1 - \frac{1}{49}} = \sqrt{\frac{48}{49}} = \frac{4\sqrt{3}}{7}$$

**[M1]** Calculate the area:
$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 5 \times 7 \times \sin C$$
$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 5 \times 7 \times \frac{4\sqrt{3}}{7}$$

**[A1]** $\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 5 \times 4\sqrt{3} = 10\sqrt{3} \text{ cm}^2$ ✓

### Question 29 [6 marks]

The diagram shows triangle $ABC$ with $AB = 15$ cm, $AC = 20$ cm, and the area of the triangle is $120 \text{ cm}^2$.

(a) Calculate the size of angle $BAC$. [3 marks]

(b) Calculate the length of $BC$, given that angle $BAC$ is obtuse. [3 marks]

**Solution:**

**(a) Finding angle $BAC$:**

**[M1]** Use the area formula:
$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times AB \times AC \times \sin A$$
$$120 = \frac{1}{2} \times 15 \times 20 \times \sin A$$
$$120 = 150 \sin A$$

**[M1]** Solve for $\sin A$:
$$\sin A = \frac{120}{150} = 0.8$$

**[A1]** Since angle $BAC$ is obtuse:
$$A = 180° - \sin^{-1}(0.8) = 180° - 53.1° = 126.9°$$

**(b) Finding length $BC$:**

**[M1]** Use the Cosine Rule with the obtuse angle:
$$BC^2 = AB^2 + AC^2 - 2 \times AB \times AC \times \cos A$$
$$BC^2 = 15^2 + 20^2 - 2 \times 15 \times 20 \times \cos 126.9°$$

**[M1]** Calculate:
$$BC^2 = 225 + 400 - 600 \times (-0.6)$$
$$BC^2 = 625 + 360 = 985$$

**[A1]** $BC = \sqrt{985} = 31.4$ cm (to 1 d.p.)

### Question 30 [5 marks]

Two ships leave a port at the same time.
- Ship $A$ travels on a bearing of $050°$ at a speed of $20$ km/h.
- Ship $B$ travels on a bearing of $120°$ at a speed of $15$ km/h.

Calculate the distance between the two ships after $3$ hours.

**Solution:**

**[M1]** Calculate distances travelled:
- Distance $A = 20 \times 3 = 60$ km
- Distance $B = 15 \times 3 = 45$ km

**[M1]** Find the angle between their paths:
- The angle between bearings $050°$ and $120°$ is $120° - 50° = 70°$

**[M1]** Use the Cosine Rule to find the distance between ships:
$$d^2 = 60^2 + 45^2 - 2 \times 60 \times 45 \times \cos 70°$$
$$d^2 = 3600 + 2025 - 5400 \times 0.342...$$
$$d^2 = 5625 - 1847.2...$$
$$d^2 = 3777.8...$$

**[A1]** $d = \sqrt{3777.8...} = 61.5$ km (to 1 d.p.)

**[A1]** **Answer:** The ships are $61.5$ km apart after $3$ hours.

## EXAM FOCUS & STRATEGY

### Frequently Tested Question Types

1. **Triangle Solving** (4-6 marks)
   - Given 3 pieces of information
   - Find remaining sides and angles
   - May combine sine rule, cosine rule, and area formula

2. **Word Problems with Bearings** (4-6 marks)
   - Draw clear diagrams
   - Convert bearings to angles
   - Apply appropriate rules

3. **Graph Transformations** (2-4 marks)
   - Describe transformations
   - Identify period and amplitude changes

4. **Solving Trigonometric Equations** (2-4 marks)
   - Find all solutions in a given range
   - Watch for multiple solutions

5. **Area Problems** (3-5 marks)
   - Calculate area directly
   - Find angle or side given area

---

### Typical Problem-Solving Patterns

| Pattern | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| Given SAS | Cosine Rule for 3rd side, Area formula for area |
| Given AAS | Sine Rule for missing side |
| Given SSA | Sine Rule (check ambiguous case) |
| Given SSS | Cosine Rule for angles |
| Given area | Rearrange area formula to find angle |

### How to Gain Full Marks

✅ **M1 (Method marks):** Show correct formula and substitution

✅ **A1 (Accuracy marks):** Give correct answer to specified accuracy

✅ **Show working:** No working = no marks for wrong answer

✅ **Check units:** Include cm, cm², degrees (°) as appropriate

✅ **Multiple solutions:** Always check for second solution with Sine Rule

✅ **Verification:** Check angle sum = 180°, largest angle opposite longest side

---

1. **Exact form:** Leave answers in exact form ($\sqrt{3}$, $\frac{1}{2}$) when appropriate

4. **Verification:** Always verify solutions are valid

5. **Presentation:** Clear diagrams, labeled sides and angles, neat working
