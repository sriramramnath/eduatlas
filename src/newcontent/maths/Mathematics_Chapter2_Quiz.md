### Question 1
Write 0.00038 in standard form.

A) $3.8 \times 10^4$

B) $3.8 \times 10^{-4}$

C) $38 \times 10^{-5}$

D) $3.8 \times 10^{-3}$

**Answer: B**

**Working:**
- Move decimal point 4 places right to get 3.8
- Since original number is small (< 1), power is negative
- $0.00038 = 3.8 \times 10^{-4}$

**Explanation:** Count the places the decimal moves to get a number between 1 and 10. For small numbers, the power is negative.

---

### Question 2
Calculate $(2.5 \times 10^3) \times (4 \times 10^5)$

A) $10 \times 10^8$

B) $1 \times 10^9$

C) $6.5 \times 10^8$

D) $1 \times 10^8$

**Answer: B**

**Working:**
$$(2.5 \times 10^3) \times (4 \times 10^5) = (2.5 \times 4) \times 10^{3+5} = 10 \times 10^8$$

Since $10$ is not between 1 and 10:
$$10 \times 10^8 = 1 \times 10^1 \times 10^8 = 1 \times 10^9$$

**Explanation:** Multiply the coefficients, add the powers. Then adjust to ensure the coefficient is between 1 and 10.

### Question 3
Find 23% of 400.

A) 92

B) 9.2

C) 920

D) 46

**Answer: A**

**Working:**
$$23\% \text{ of } 400 = 0.23 \times 400 = 92$$

**Alternative (non-calculator):**
- 10% of 400 = 40
- 20% of 400 = 80
- 1% of 400 = 4
- 3% of 400 = 12
- 23% = 80 + 12 = 92

### Question 4
A jacket costs £84. Its price is increased by 15%. What is the new price?

A) £96.60

B) £99.00

C) £12.60

D) £71.40

**Answer: A**

**Working:**
Using multiplier: $1 + 0.15 = 1.15$
$$£84 \times 1.15 = £96.60$$

**Two-step method:**
- Increase = 15% of £84 = $0.15 \times 84 = £12.60$
- New price = $84 + 12.60 = £96.60$

### Question 5
A car's value decreases from £15,000 to £12,750. What is the percentage decrease?

A) 15%

B) 17.5%

C) 12.5%

D) 25%

**Answer: A**

**Working:**
$$\text{Decrease} = 15,000 - 12,750 = £2,250$$
$$\text{Percentage decrease} = \frac{2,250}{15,000} \times 100\% = 15\%$$

**Explanation:** Always divide by the ORIGINAL value, not the new value.

---

### Question 6
After a 20% increase, a population is 48,000. What was the original population?

A) 38,400

B) 40,000

C) 57,600

D) 36,000

**Answer: B**

**Working:**
After 20% increase, the new value = 120% of original
$$48,000 = 1.20 \times \text{original}$$
$$\text{Original} = \frac{48,000}{1.20} = 40,000$$

**Explanation:** This is a reverse percentage problem. Divide by the multiplier 1.20.

---

### Question 7
What is the multiplier for a 35% decrease?

A) 1.35

B) 0.35

C) 0.65

D) 0.75

**Answer: C**

**Working:**
$$\text{Multiplier for decrease} = 1 - \frac{35}{100} = 1 - 0.35 = 0.65$$

**Explanation:** For a decrease, the multiplier is less than 1. A 35% decrease leaves 65% of the original.

### Question 8
£3,000 is invested at 5% compound interest per year. What is the value after 2 years?

A) £3,300

B) £3,307.50

C) £3,150

D) £3,225

**Answer: B**

**Working:**
$$A = 3000 \times 1.05^2$$
$$A = 3000 \times 1.1025 = £3,307.50$$

**Explanation:** Compound interest uses $P(1+r)^n$, not $P(1+rn)$ which is simple interest.

### Question 9
Express 27 minutes as a percentage of 2 hours.

A) 13.5%

B) 22.5%

C) 27%

D) 54%

**Answer: B**

**Working:**
2 hours = 120 minutes
$$\frac{27}{120} \times 100\% = \frac{9}{40} \times 100\% = 22.5\%$$

**Explanation:** Convert to the same units first, then express as a percentage.

### Question 10
Calculate $(6 \times 10^7) \div (3 \times 10^{-2})$. Give your answer in standard form.

A) $2 \times 10^5$

B) $2 \times 10^9$

C) $2 \times 10^{-9}$

D) $0.5 \times 10^9$

**Answer: B**

**Working:**
$$\frac{6 \times 10^7}{3 \times 10^{-2}} = \frac{6}{3} \times 10^{7-(-2)} = 2 \times 10^{7+2} = 2 \times 10^9$$

**Explanation:** When dividing, subtract the powers. Remember: subtracting a negative is adding.

### Question 11
$4,500,000 = 4.5 \times 10^6$

**TRUE**

### Question 12
When converting 0.00073 to standard form, the answer is $7.3 \times 10^4$.

**FALSE**

**Correction:** $0.00073 = 7.3 \times 10^{-4}$

Small numbers (< 1) have NEGATIVE powers in standard form.

---

### Question 13
The multiplier for a 25% increase is 1.25.

**TRUE**

$$1 + \frac{25}{100} = 1.25$$

---

### Question 14
If a price decreases by 30%, the new price is 30% of the original price.

**FALSE**

**Correction:** If a price decreases by 30%, the new price is **70%** of the original price.

$$100\% - 30\% = 70\%$$

---

### Question 15
To find the original price after a 20% decrease, divide the new price by 0.8.

**TRUE**

After a 20% decrease, the new price = 80% of original = $0.8 \times \text{original}$

So: $\text{original} = \frac{\text{new price}}{0.8}$

---

### Question 16
$0.15 = 150\%$

**FALSE**

**Correction:** $0.15 = 15\%$

To convert decimal to percentage, multiply by 100: $0.15 \times 100 = 15\%$

---

### Question 17
Compound interest always gives a higher return than simple interest over the same period.

**TRUE**

Compound interest earns interest on interest, leading to exponential growth. Simple interest only earns interest on the principal.

For example, £1000 at 10% for 3 years:
- Simple: $1000 \times 1.30 = £1300$
- Compound: $1000 \times 1.10^3 = £1331$

---

### Question 18
$(3 \times 10^4) + (5 \times 10^4) = 8 \times 10^8$

**FALSE**

**Correction:** $(3 \times 10^4) + (5 \times 10^4) = 8 \times 10^4$

When adding standard form with the same power, add the coefficients but keep the power the same.

---

### Question 19
Finding 35% of a number and then subtracting it from the original gives the same result as multiplying by 0.65.

**TRUE**

35% decrease means the new value = 100% - 35% = 65% of original

$65\% = 0.65$, so multiplying by 0.65 gives the correct result.

---

### Question 20
The percentage change formula divides by the new value.

**FALSE**

**Correction:** The percentage change formula divides by the **ORIGINAL** value.

$$\text{Percentage Change} = \frac{\text{change}}{\text{original}} \times 100\%$$

**Question 27** [4 marks]

(a) Express $5 \times 10^6 + 3 \times 10^5$ in standard form. [2]

(b) Calculate $(4 \times 10^{-3}) \times (2.5 \times 10^7)$ [2]

**A1** $5.3 \times 10^6$

**Working:**
$$5 \times 10^6 = 50 \times 10^5$$
$$50 \times 10^5 + 3 \times 10^5 = 53 \times 10^5 = 5.3 \times 10^6$$

**(b) M1** Correct multiplication of coefficients OR powers

**A1** $1 \times 10^5$ or $10^5$

**Working:**
$$(4 \times 10^{-3}) \times (2.5 \times 10^7) = (4 \times 2.5) \times 10^{-3+7}$$
$$= 10 \times 10^4$$
$$= 1 \times 10^5$$

**Question 31** [6 marks]

A company's profit was £2.4 million in 2019.

In 2020, the profit increased by 15%.

In 2021, the profit decreased by 8%.

(a) Calculate the profit in 2020. [1]

(b) Calculate the profit in 2021. [2]

(c) Calculate the overall percentage change in profit from 2019 to 2021. [2]

(d) Explain why the overall percentage change is not 15% - 8% = 7%. [1]

**(a) A1** £2.76 million

$$2.4 \times 1.15 = £2.76 \text{ million}$$

**(b) M1** Correct method (decrease from 2020 value)

**A1** £2.5392 million (accept £2.54 million or £2,539,200)

$$2.76 \times 0.92 = £2.5392 \text{ million}$$

**(c) M1** Correct percentage change calculation

**A1** 5.8% increase

$$\text{Change} = 2.5392 - 2.4 = 0.1392 \text{ million}$$

**(d) A1** Clear explanation

**Acceptable answers:**

**Question 32** [6 marks]

The distance from Earth to the Sun is approximately $1.5 \times 10^8$ km.

The distance from Earth to the Moon is approximately $3.8 \times 10^5$ km.

(a) Calculate how many times further the Sun is from Earth compared to the Moon. Give your answer to the nearest whole number. [2]

(b) Light travels at approximately $3 \times 10^5$ km/s. Calculate how long it takes light to travel from the Sun to Earth. Give your answer in minutes, to the nearest minute. [2]

(c) The distance from Earth to Mars is approximately $2.25 \times 10^8$ km. Express the distance from Earth to the Moon as a percentage of the distance from Earth to Mars. [2]

**(a) M1** Correct division of standard form values

**A1** 395 (or 394 or 395)

**Working:**
$$\frac{1.5 \times 10^8}{3.8 \times 10^5} = \frac{1.5}{3.8} \times 10^3 = 0.395 \times 10^3 \approx 395$$

**(b) M1** Correct division for time

**A1** 8 minutes (to nearest minute)

**Working:**
$$\text{Time} = \frac{\text{distance}}{\text{speed}} = \frac{1.5 \times 10^8}{3 \times 10^5} = 500 \text{ seconds}$$
$$\frac{500}{60} = 8.33... \text{ minutes} \approx 8 \text{ minutes}$$

**(c) M1** Correct percentage calculation

**A1** 0.17% (accept 0.169%)

**Working:**
$$\frac{3.8 \times 10^5}{2.25 \times 10^8} \times 100 = \frac{3.8}{2.25 \times 10^3} \times 100$$
$$= \frac{380}{2250} = \frac{38}{225} = 0.169\%$$
