### Question 1 [1 mark]

In a right-angled triangle, the side opposite the angle θ has length 8 cm and the adjacent side has length 5 cm. What is the value of $\tan \theta$?

**A)** 0.625

**B)** 1.6

**C)** $\frac{5}{8}$

**D)** $\sqrt{39}$

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$\tan \theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}} = \frac{8}{5} = 1.6$$

The tangent ratio is always opposite divided by adjacent.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- A: This is $\frac{5}{8}$, the inverse (would be $\cot \theta$)
- C: This would give $\tan \theta = 0.625$ (reversed)
- D: This relates to Pythagoras, not tangent

### Question 2 [1 mark]

A right-angled triangle has an angle of 42° and an adjacent side of 15 cm. What is the length of the opposite side?

**A)** $15 \times \tan 42°$

**B)** $15 \div \tan 42°$

**C)** $15 \times \sin 42°$

**D)** $15 \div \cos 42°$

**Correct Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
Using $\tan \theta = \frac{O}{A}$:
$$O = A \times \tan \theta = 15 \times \tan 42°$$

To find the opposite side, we multiply the adjacent side by the tangent of the angle.

**Calculation:** $O = 15 \times 0.9004... = 13.5$ cm (1 d.p.)

### Question 3 [1 mark]

What is the value of $\tan 45°$?

**A)** 0

**B)** 0.5

**C)** 1

**D)** $\sqrt{2}$

**Correct Answer: C**

**Explanation:**
In a 45°-45°-90° triangle, the two legs are equal. If opposite = adjacent, then:
$$\tan 45° = \frac{O}{A} = \frac{x}{x} = 1$$

This is a standard value that should be memorized.

### Question 4 [1 mark]

If $\tan \theta = 2.5$, what is the value of θ to the nearest degree?

**A)** 22°

**B)** 68°

**C)** 45°

**D)** 88°

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$\theta = \tan^{-1}(2.5) = 68.198...° ≈ 68°$$

Since tan 45° = 1 and the value is 2.5 > 1, the angle must be greater than 45°. Between the options, only 68° is reasonable.

### Question 5 [1 mark]

In triangle PQR, angle Q = 90°, PQ = 12 cm, and QR = 5 cm. What is $\tan \angle P$?

**A)** $\frac{5}{12}$

**B)** $\frac{12}{5}$

**C)** $\frac{5}{13}$

**D)** $\frac{12}{13}$

**Correct Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
```
    Q ─────────── R
    │╲
    │ ╲
 5cm│  ╲
    │   ╲
    │ P  ╲
    └──────
      12cm
```

For angle P:
- Opposite = QR = 5 cm
- Adjacent = PQ = 12 cm
- Hypotenuse = PR = 13 cm (by Pythagoras)

$$\tan P = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}} = \frac{5}{12}$$

### Question 6 [1 mark]

The angle of elevation from a point to the top of a building is 35°. The horizontal distance from the point to the building is 40 m. Which expression gives the height of the building?

**A)** $40 \times \tan 35°$

**B)** $40 \div \tan 35°$

**C)** $40 \times \sin 35°$

**D)** $40 \div \cos 35°$

**Correct Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
```
    Building
       │ ╲
    h  │  ╲
       │35°╲
       └─────●
         40m
```

For angle of elevation:
- Height (h) = opposite
- Distance (40m) = adjacent

$$\tan 35° = \frac{h}{40}$$
$$h = 40 \times \tan 35°$$

### Question 7 [1 mark]

Which statement about $\tan \theta$ is correct?

**A)** $\tan \theta$ is always less than 1

**B)** $\tan \theta$ is always positive for acute angles

**C)** $\tan 90° = 1$

**D)** $\tan \theta = \tan(90° - \theta)$

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
For acute angles (0° < θ < 90°), both opposite and adjacent are positive lengths, so $\tan \theta = \frac{O}{A}$ is always positive.

**Why other options are wrong:**
- A: $\tan 60° = 1.732 > 1$, so tan can exceed 1
- C: $\tan 90°$ is undefined (adjacent = 0)
- D: $\tan(90° - \theta) = \cot \theta$, not $\tan \theta$

A ladder makes an angle of 72° with the ground and reaches 4.8 m up a wall. How far is the foot of the ladder from the wall?

**A)** $4.8 \times \tan 72°$

**B)** $4.8 \div \tan 72°$

**C)** $4.8 \times \sin 72°$

**D)** $4.8 \div \sin 72°$

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
```
    Wall
      │ ╲
  4.8m│  ╲ Ladder
      │72°╲
      └────
        d
```

For angle 72°:
- Height (4.8 m) = opposite
- Distance (d) = adjacent

$$\tan 72° = \frac{4.8}{d}$$
$$d = \frac{4.8}{\tan 72°}$$

### Question 9 [1 mark]

If the opposite side is 18 cm and the adjacent side is 12 cm, what is the angle θ to 1 decimal place?

**A)** 33.7°

**B)** 41.8°

**C)** 48.2°

**D)** 56.3°

**Correct Answer: D**

**Explanation:**
$$\tan \theta = \frac{18}{12} = 1.5$$
$$\theta = \tan^{-1}(1.5) = 56.3° \text{ (to 1 d.p.)}$$

Since tan θ > 1, the angle must be greater than 45°.

### Question 10 [1 mark]

From a cliff 60 m high, a boat is observed at an angle of depression of 25°. What is the horizontal distance from the cliff to the boat?

**A)** $60 \times \tan 25°$

**B)** $60 \div \tan 25°$

**C)** $60 \times \sin 25°$

**D)** $60 \div \sin 25°$

**Correct Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
The angle of depression equals the angle of elevation at the boat (alternate angles).

For the triangle formed:
- Height (60 m) = opposite
- Distance (d) = adjacent

$$\tan 25° = \frac{60}{d}$$
$$d = \frac{60}{\tan 25°} = 128.7 \text{ m (1 d.p.)}$$

## SECTION B: TRUE OR FALSE (10 Statements)

**For each statement, state TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correction.**

---

### Statement 1

"The tangent ratio involves the hypotenuse and the opposite side."

**FALSE**

**Correction:** The tangent ratio involves the opposite and adjacent sides only: $\tan \theta = \frac{O}{A}$. The hypotenuse is not used in the tangent ratio.

---

### Statement 2

"$\tan 30° \approx 0.577$"

**TRUE**

This is a standard value that can be verified using a calculator.

---

### Statement 3

"The angle of elevation and angle of depression are always equal when two objects are at the same height."

**FALSE**

**Correction:** The angle of elevation and angle of depression are equal when considering the same two points, regardless of height. They are equal because they are alternate angles formed by parallel horizontal lines.

---

### Statement 4

"To find the adjacent side, multiply the opposite side by $\tan \theta$."

**FALSE**

**Correction:** To find the adjacent side when given the opposite and angle: $A = \frac{O}{\tan \theta}$ (divide, not multiply).

---

### Statement 5

"$\tan^{-1}(1) = 45°$"

**TRUE**

Since $\tan 45° = 1$, therefore $\tan^{-1}(1) = 45°$.

---

### Statement 6

"The tangent of an angle is always less than 1 for angles less than 45°."

**TRUE**

For 0° < θ < 45°: opposite < adjacent, so $\tan \theta = \frac{O}{A} < 1$.

For θ = 45°: O = A, so $\tan 45° = 1$.

For 45° < θ < 90°: opposite > adjacent, so $\tan \theta > 1$.

---

### Statement 7

"If $\tan \theta = 0.75$, then $\theta = 36.9°$ to 1 d.p."

**TRUE**

$$\theta = \tan^{-1}(0.75) = 36.869...° = 36.9° \text{ (1 d.p.)}$$

---

### Statement 8

"The inverse tangent function ($\tan^{-1}$) is used to find a side length."

**FALSE**

**Correction:** The inverse tangent function ($\tan^{-1}$) is used to find an **angle** when given the opposite and adjacent sides. To find a side, we use the regular tangent function.

---

### Statement 9

"In a right-angled triangle, $\tan \theta$ can be greater than 10."

**TRUE**

As the angle approaches 90°, tan θ increases rapidly. For example, $\tan 85° ≈ 11.4$ and $\tan 89° ≈ 57.3$.

---

### Statement 10

"The labels 'opposite' and 'adjacent' depend on which angle in the triangle is being considered."

**TRUE**

The opposite and adjacent sides are always relative to a specific angle. If you change the angle you're working with, you must relabel the sides accordingly.

### Question 11 [2 marks]

Calculate the length of side $x$ in the triangle below.

```
        ╱│
       ╱ │
      ╱  │ x
     ╱   │
    ╱38° │
   ──────┘
     16 cm
```

Give your answer to 1 decimal place.

**Step 1: Identify sides**
- Opposite = $x$
- Adjacent = 16 cm
- Angle = 38°

**Step 2: Write the formula** **[M1]**
$$\tan 38° = \frac{x}{16}$$

**Step 3: Rearrange and calculate**
$$x = 16 \times \tan 38°$$
$$x = 16 \times 0.7812...$$
$$x = 12.5 \text{ cm (1 d.p.)}$$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** $x = 12.5$ cm

### Question 12 [2 marks]

Find the value of angle θ in this triangle. Give your answer to the nearest degree.

```
        ╱│
       ╱ │
      ╱  │ 20 cm
     ╱   │
    ╱ θ  │
   ──────┘
     7 cm
```

**Step 1: Identify sides** **[M1]**
- Opposite = 20 cm
- Adjacent = 7 cm

**Step 2: Calculate the ratio**
$$\tan \theta = \frac{20}{7} = 2.857...$$

**Step 3: Apply inverse tangent**
$$\theta = \tan^{-1}(2.857...)$$

$$\theta = 71° \text{ (nearest degree)}$$ **[A1]**

### Question 13 [3 marks]

A vertical flagpole is supported by a rope attached to the top and pegged into the ground 6 m from the base. The rope makes an angle of 58° with the ground.

(a) Calculate the height of the flagpole. [2]

(b) Calculate the length of the rope. [1]

Give your answers to 1 decimal place.

**Diagram:**
```
Flagpole
    │╲
    │ ╲ Rope
 h  │  ╲
    │58°╲
    └────●
      6m
```

**(a) Height of flagpole [2 marks]**

For angle 58°:
- Height (h) = opposite
- Distance (6 m) = adjacent

$$\tan 58° = \frac{h}{6}$$ **[M1]**
$$h = 6 \times \tan 58°$$
$$h = 6 \times 1.6003...$$
$$h = 9.6 \text{ m (1 d.p.)}$$ **[A1]**

**(b) Length of rope [1 mark]**

Using Pythagoras:
$$\text{Rope} = \sqrt{6^2 + 9.6^2} = \sqrt{36 + 92.16} = \sqrt{128.16} = 11.3 \text{ m (1 d.p.)}$$ **[A1]**

**Answers:** (a) 9.6 m  (b) 11.3 m

### Question 15 [4 marks]

A surveyor wants to find the height of a tower. She stands 50 m from the base of the tower and measures the angle of elevation to the top as 54°. She also measures the angle of elevation to a point on the tower to be 28°.

Calculate the height of the section of the tower between these two points.

**Diagram:**
```
        Tower
           │
           │ Top
        h₂ │ ●
           │ │
           │ │ Section height
        h₁ │ ●
           │ │
           │ │
           └─┴───● Surveyor
              50m
```

**Step 1: Find total height (h₂)** **[M1]**
$$\tan 54° = \frac{h_2}{50}$$
$$h_2 = 50 \times \tan 54° = 50 \times 1.376... = 68.8 \text{ m}$$

**Step 2: Find height to lower point (h₁)** **[M1]**
$$\tan 28° = \frac{h_1}{50}$$
$$h_1 = 50 \times \tan 28° = 50 \times 0.5317... = 26.6 \text{ m}$$

**Step 3: Find section height** **[M1]**
$$\text{Section} = h_2 - h_1 = 68.8 - 26.6 = 42.2 \text{ m}$$ **[A1]**

**Answer:** The height of the section is 42.2 m.

**Alternative working:**
$$\text{Section} = 50(\tan 54° - \tan 28°) = 50(1.376 - 0.532) = 42.2 \text{ m}$$

### Question 16 [4 marks]

A road rises uniformly from sea level. After driving 800 m horizontally, the road is 120 m above sea level.

(a) Calculate the angle of inclination of the road. [2]

(b) If a car drives 2 km along this road, how much does it rise vertically? [2]

Give your answers to 1 decimal place.

**(a) Angle of inclination [2 marks]**

$$\tan \theta = \frac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}} = \frac{120}{800} = 0.15$$ **[M1]**
$$\theta = \tan^{-1}(0.15) = 8.5° \text{ (1 d.p.)}$$ **[A1]**

**(b) Vertical rise for 2 km [2 marks]**

Note: 2 km = 2000 m is the **horizontal** distance.

$$\tan 8.5° = \frac{h}{2000}$$ **[M1]**
$$h = 2000 \times \tan 8.5°$$
$$h = 2000 \times 0.1493...$$
$$h = 298.6 \text{ m (1 d.p.)}$$ **[A1]**

**Answers:** (a) 8.5°  (b) 298.6 m

### Question 17 [5 marks]

A ship sails from port A on a bearing of 065° for 12 km to reach point B. It then changes course and sails on a bearing of 155° for 8 km to reach port C.

(a) Draw a diagram showing the journey. [1]

(b) Calculate how far North of port A the ship is after the first leg of the journey. [2]

(c) Calculate how far East of port A the ship is after the first leg of the journey. [2]

**(a) Diagram [1 mark]**
```
         N
         │
         │    B
         │   ╱
         │  ╱ 12 km
         │ ╱65°
         │╱
    A────┴───────
              E
```
**[B1]** for correct bearing and distances

**(b) Distance North of A [2 marks]**

The bearing of 065° means the angle from North is 65°.
For the right triangle:
- Distance North (adjacent to 65°) = 12 × cos 65°

Or using tangent approach:
$$\tan 65° = \frac{\text{East}}{\text{North}}$$

Using: $\text{North} = 12 \times \cos 65°$ **[M1]**
$$\text{North} = 12 \times 0.4226... = 5.1 \text{ km (1 d.p.)}$$ **[A1]**

**(c) Distance East of A [2 marks]**

$$\tan 65° = \frac{\text{East}}{\text{North}}$$
$$\text{East} = \text{North} \times \tan 65°$$
$$\text{East} = 5.07... \times \tan 65°$$

Or directly: $\text{East} = 12 \times \sin 65°$ **[M1]**
$$\text{East} = 12 \times 0.9063... = 10.9 \text{ km (1 d.p.)}$$ **[A1]**

**Answers:** (b) 5.1 km North  (c) 10.9 km East

### Question 18 [6 marks]

**Problem:** A communications tower has two support cables attached to its top. One cable is anchored 30 m from the base and makes an angle of 62° with the ground. The other cable is anchored on the opposite side, 45 m from the base.

(a) Calculate the height of the tower. [2]

(b) Calculate the angle that the second cable makes with the ground. [2]

(c) Calculate the total length of both cables. [2]

Give your answers to 1 decimal place.

**Diagram:**
```
           Tower
              │ ╲
              │  ╲ Cable 1
           h  │   ╲
              │62° ╲
         ─────┴──────●
         30m    │
                │ 45m
                │
                ● Cable 2
```

**(a) Height of tower [2 marks]**

Using Cable 1 information:
$$\tan 62° = \frac{h}{30}$$ **[M1]**
$$h = 30 \times \tan 62°$$
$$h = 30 \times 1.8807...$$
$$h = 56.4 \text{ m (1 d.p.)}$$ **[A1]**

**(b) Angle for Cable 2 [2 marks]**

For Cable 2:
- Opposite = h = 56.4 m
- Adjacent = 45 m

$$\tan \theta_2 = \frac{56.4}{45} = 1.2533...$$ **[M1]**
$$\theta_2 = \tan^{-1}(1.2533...) = 51.4° \text{ (1 d.p.)}$$ **[A1]**

**(c) Total length of cables [2 marks]**

**Cable 1 length:**
$$L_1 = \sqrt{30^2 + 56.4^2} = \sqrt{900 + 3180.96} = \sqrt{4080.96} = 63.9 \text{ m}$$

**Cable 2 length:**
$$L_2 = \sqrt{45^2 + 56.4^2} = \sqrt{2025 + 3180.96} = \sqrt{5205.96} = 72.2 \text{ m}$$

**Total:** $L_1 + L_2 = 63.9 + 72.2 = 136.1 \text{ m}$ **[A1]**

**Alternative method for Cable lengths:**
$$L_1 = \frac{30}{\cos 62°} = 63.9 \text{ m}$$
$$L_2 = \frac{45}{\cos 51.4°} = 72.2 \text{ m}$$

**Answers:** (a) 56.4 m  (b) 51.4°  (c) 136.1 m

###  Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Calculator not in degree mode** - Always check!
2. **Wrong sides identified** - Label O, A, H before starting
3. **Mixing up multiply/divide** - Remember: multiply for O, divide for A
4. **Forgetting units** - Always include (cm, m, km, etc.)
5. **Rounding too early** - Use calculator memory
6. **Not reading the question** - Check what you're asked to find
