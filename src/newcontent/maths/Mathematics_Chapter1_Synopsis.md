# Quick Reference Card

## 1. Fractions - Essential Rules

### Types of Fractions
| Type | Form | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| Proper | numerator < denominator | $\frac{3}{4}$ |
| Improper | numerator ≥ denominator | $\frac{7}{4}$ |
| Mixed | whole + fraction | $1\frac{3}{4}$ |

### Conversions

**Mixed → Improper:**
$$a\frac{b}{c} = \frac{a \times c + b}{c}$$

**Improper → Mixed:**
- Divide: $\frac{17}{5} = 17 \div 5 = 3$ remainder $2 = 3\frac{2}{5}$

---

## 2. Fraction Operations - Quick Rules

### Addition & Subtraction
- **Same denominator:** Add/subtract numerators
  $$\frac{a}{c} + \frac{b}{c} = \frac{a+b}{c}$$

- **Different denominators:** Find LCD, convert, then add/subtract
  $$\frac{2}{3} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{8}{12} + \frac{3}{12} = \frac{11}{12}$$

### Multiplication
- Multiply numerators, multiply denominators
- **Cross-cancel** to simplify before multiplying
  $$\frac{4}{9} \times \frac{3}{8} = \frac{\cancel{4}^{1}}{\cancel{9}^{3}} \times \frac{\cancel{3}^{1}}{\cancel{8}^{2}} = \frac{1}{6}$$

### Division - KCF Rule
- **K**eep first fraction
- **C**hange ÷ to ×
- **F**lip second fraction
  $$\frac{3}{4} \div \frac{2}{5} = \frac{3}{4} \times \frac{5}{2} = \frac{15}{8}$$

---

## 3. BIDMAS - Order of Operations

| Order | Operation | Examples |
|:-----:|-----------|----------|
| 1 | **B**rackets | $(3+4)$ |
| 2 | **I**ndices | $2^3, \sqrt{9}$ |
| 3 | **D**ivision / **M**ultiplication | Work left → right |
| 4 | **A**ddition / **S**ubtraction | Work left → right |

> **Remember:** D&M have equal priority (left to right), A&S have equal priority (left to right)

---

## 4. Rounding Rules

### Decimal Places
- Look at the **next digit**
- **5 or more** → round up
- **4 or less** → round down

| Number | 1 d.p. | 2 d.p. | 3 d.p. |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| 3.847 | 3.8 | 3.85 | 3.847 |
| 7.295 | 7.3 | 7.30 | 7.295 |

### Significant Figures
| Number | 1 s.f. | 2 s.f. | 3 s.f. |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| 4738 | 5000 | 4700 | 4740 |
| 0.00638 | 0.006 | 0.0064 | 0.00638 |
| 3.200 | 3 | 3.2 | 3.20 |

### Significant Figure Rules
- **All non-zero digits** are significant
- **Zeros between non-zeros** are significant
- **Leading zeros** are NOT significant
- **Trailing zeros after decimal** ARE significant

## 5. Key Exam Reminders

✅ **Always simplify** final fraction answers

✅ **Show all working** - marks are for method

✅ **Don't round** until final answer in multi-step calculations

✅ **Keep placeholder zeros** - 7.30 not 7.3 for 2 d.p.

✅ **Check answers** - does the result make sense?

---

## 6. Common Mistakes Checklist

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---------|---------|
| $\frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{2}{7}$ | $\frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{4} = \frac{7}{12}$ |
| $\frac{2}{3} \div \frac{1}{2} = \frac{1}{3}$ | $\frac{2}{3} \times \frac{2}{1} = \frac{4}{3}$ |
| $3 + 4 \times 2 = 14$ | $3 + 4 \times 2 = 11$ |
| Round intermediate steps | Keep full precision |
| 7.3 for 2 d.p. question | 7.30 |

## 7. Speed Calculations

### Finding a Fraction of a Number
$$\frac{3}{5} \text{ of } 40 = \frac{3}{5} \times 40 = 24$$

### Quick LCD Method
- For denominators 3 and 4: LCD = 12
- For denominators 6 and 8: LCD = 24
- Check: both original denominators divide the LCD exactly
