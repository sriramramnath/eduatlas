### Question 1
A circle has a circumference of $16\pi$ cm. What is its radius?

A) 4 cm  
B) 8 cm  
C) 16 cm  
D) 32 cm

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$C = 2\pi r$$
$$16\pi = 2\pi r$$
$$r = 8 \text{ cm}$$

---

### Question 2
A sector of a circle has radius 6 cm and angle 60°. What is the length of its arc?

A) $\pi$ cm  
B) $2\pi$ cm  
C) $6\pi$ cm  
D) $12\pi$ cm

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{Arc length} = \frac{60}{360} \times 2\pi \times 6 = \frac{1}{6} \times 12\pi = 2\pi \text{ cm}$$

---

### Question 3
What is the area of a sector with radius 9 cm and angle 120°?

A) $9\pi$ cm²  
B) $18\pi$ cm²  
C) $27\pi$ cm²  
D) $81\pi$ cm²

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{Sector area} = \frac{120}{360} \times \pi \times 9^2 = \frac{1}{3} \times 81\pi = 27\pi \text{ cm}^2$$

---

### Question 4
A cone has radius 3 cm and vertical height 4 cm. What is its slant height?

A) 5 cm  
B) 6 cm  
C) 7 cm  
D) 12 cm

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$l = \sqrt{r^2 + h^2} = \sqrt{3^2 + 4^2} = \sqrt{9 + 16} = \sqrt{25} = 5 \text{ cm}$$

---

### Question 5
A sphere has a surface area of $100\pi$ cm². What is its radius?

A) 5 cm  
B) 10 cm  
C) 25 cm  
D) 50 cm

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$4\pi r^2 = 100\pi$$
$$r^2 = 25$$
$$r = 5 \text{ cm}$$

---

### Question 6
Two similar cylinders have radii in the ratio 2:3. What is the ratio of their volumes?

A) 2:3  
B) 4:9  
C) 8:27  
D) 16:81

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{Linear scale factor } k = \frac{3}{2}$$
$$\text{Volume ratio} = k^3 = \left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^3 = \frac{27}{8} = 8:27$$

### Question 7
A cone has radius 5 cm and slant height 13 cm. What is its volume?

A) $\frac{100\pi}{3}$ cm³  
B) $100\pi$ cm³  
C) $\frac{300\pi}{3}$ cm³  
D) $300\pi$ cm³

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$h = \sqrt{l^2 - r^2} = \sqrt{169 - 25} = \sqrt{144} = 12 \text{ cm}$$
$$V = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h = \frac{1}{3}\pi \times 25 \times 12 = \frac{300\pi}{3} = 100\pi \text{ cm}^3$$

Note: Answer A equals $\frac{100\pi}{3} = \frac{300\pi}{9}$, so correct is $100\pi$ cm³.

**Correct Answer: B** (The volume is $100\pi$ cm³)

### Question 8
Two similar shapes have areas of 20 cm² and 45 cm². What is the linear scale factor from the smaller to the larger?

A) $\frac{3}{2}$  
B) $\frac{4}{3}$  
C) $\frac{9}{4}$  
D) $\frac{3}{4}$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$k^2 = \frac{A_2}{A_1} = \frac{45}{20} = \frac{9}{4}$$
$$k = \sqrt{\frac{9}{4}} = \frac{3}{2}$$

### Question 9
A hemisphere has radius 7 cm. What is its total surface area?

A) $98\pi$ cm²  
B) $147\pi$ cm²  
C) $196\pi$ cm²  
D) $49\pi$ cm²

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{TSA} = 3\pi r^2 = 3\pi \times 49 = 147\pi \text{ cm}^2$$

### Question 10
A cylindrical tank has internal radius 2 m and height 5 m. What is its capacity in litres?

A) $20\pi$ litres  
B) $2000\pi$ litres  
C) $20000\pi$ litres  
D) $200000\pi$ litres

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**
$$V = \pi r^2 h = \pi \times 2^2 \times 5 = 20\pi \text{ m}^3$$
$$= 20\pi \times 1000000 \text{ cm}^3$$
$$= 20\pi \times 1000 \text{ litres} = 20000\pi \text{ litres}$$

### Question 11
The angle at the centre of a circle is 110°. What is the angle at the circumference subtended by the same arc?

A) 55°  
B) 110°  
C) 220°  
D) 35°

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
By the circle theorem, angle at centre = $2 \times$ angle at circumference.
$$\text{Angle at circumference} = \frac{110°}{2} = 55°$$

---

### Question 12
Two similar cones have volumes in the ratio 8:125. What is the ratio of their heights?

A) 2:5  
B) 4:25  
C) 8:125  
D) $\sqrt{2}:\sqrt{5}$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$k^3 = \frac{125}{8}$$
$$k = \sqrt[3]{\frac{125}{8}} = \frac{5}{2}$$
$$\text{Ratio of heights} = 2:5$$

## SECTION B: TRUE/FALSE QUESTIONS

## Instructions
State whether each statement is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.

---

### Question 1
**Statement:** The area of a circle with diameter $d$ is $\frac{\pi d^2}{4}$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Since $r = \frac{d}{2}$:
$$A = \pi r^2 = \pi\left(\frac{d}{2}\right)^2 = \frac{\pi d^2}{4}$$

---

### Question 2
**Statement:** The curved surface area of a cone is $\pi r h$, where $r$ is the radius and $h$ is the vertical height.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The curved surface area of a cone is $\pi r l$, where $l$ is the **slant height**.

---

### Question 3
**Statement:** If two shapes are similar with linear scale factor $k$, then the ratio of their areas is $k^2$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This is the fundamental relationship between linear and area scale factors.

---

### Question 4
**Statement:** A segment is the region bounded by two radii and an arc.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A **sector** is the region bounded by two radii and an arc. A **segment** is the region bounded by a chord and an arc.

---

### Question 5
**Statement:** The volume of a pyramid is always one-third of the volume of a prism with the same base and height.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** $V_{pyramid} = \frac{1}{3} \times \text{base area} \times \text{height}$

---

### Question 6
**Statement:** If the radius of a sphere is doubled, its volume becomes 8 times larger.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Volume scales with $r^3$. If $r$ is multiplied by 2, volume is multiplied by $2^3 = 8$.

---

### Question 7
**Statement:** The angle in a semicircle is 180°.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The angle in a semicircle is **90°** (a right angle).

---

### Question 8
**Statement:** Two tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are equal in length.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This is a fundamental circle theorem.

---

### Question 9
**Statement:** The total surface area of a hemisphere is $2\pi r^2$.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The total surface area of a hemisphere is $3\pi r^2$ (curved surface $2\pi r^2$ plus flat circular base $\pi r^2$).

---

### Question 10
**Statement:** Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral add up to 90°.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral add up to **180°**.

---

### Question 11
**Statement:** If the area scale factor between two similar shapes is 16, then the linear scale factor is 4.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** $k^2 = 16$, so $k = 4$.

---

### Question 12
**Statement:** The perimeter of a sector equals the arc length plus twice the radius.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Perimeter = Arc + $r$ + $r$ = Arc + $2r$

## Question 1 (3 marks)

A sector of a circle has radius 8 cm and perimeter 20 cm.

Calculate the angle of the sector.

### Mark Scheme Solution

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Let angle = $\theta$ cm | 1 |
| M1 | Arc length = $20 - 2(8) = 4$ cm | 1 |
| A1 | $\frac{\theta}{360} \times 2\pi \times 8 = 4$ | 1 |

$$\theta = \frac{4 \times 360}{16\pi} = \frac{90}{\pi} = 28.6° \text{ (3 s.f.)}$$

**Answer: 28.6°** (accept $28.6°$ or $\frac{90}{\pi}$ degrees)

## Question 2 (4 marks)

A solid consists of a cone mounted on a cylinder.
- Both have radius 5 cm
- The cylinder has height 12 cm
- The cone has vertical height 9 cm

Calculate the total volume of the solid. Give your answer in terms of $\pi$.

### Mark Scheme Solution

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | $V_{cylinder} = \pi \times 5^2 \times 12$ | 1 |
| A1 | $V_{cylinder} = 300\pi$ cm³ | 1 |
| M1 | $V_{cone} = \frac{1}{3}\pi \times 5^2 \times 9$ | 1 |
| A1 | $V_{cone} = 75\pi$ cm³, Total = $375\pi$ cm³ | 1 |

**Working:**

$$V_{cylinder} = \pi \times 5^2 \times 12 = 300\pi \text{ cm}^3$$

$$V_{cone} = \frac{1}{3}\pi \times 5^2 \times 9 = \frac{225\pi}{3} = 75\pi \text{ cm}^3$$

$$V_{total} = 300\pi + 75\pi = 375\pi \text{ cm}^3$$

**Answer: $375\pi$ cm³**

## Question 3 (4 marks)

A water tank is a cylinder with radius 1.2 m and height 2.5 m.

(a) Calculate the volume of the tank in m³. Give your answer correct to 3 significant figures. **[2 marks]**

(b) The tank is filled at a rate of 20 litres per minute. How long does it take to fill the tank from empty? Give your answer in hours and minutes. **[2 marks]**

### Mark Scheme Solution

**(a)**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | $V = \pi \times 1.2^2 \times 2.5$ | 1 |
| A1 | $V = 3.6\pi = 11.3$ m³ (3 s.f.) | 1 |

$$V = \pi \times 1.2^2 \times 2.5 = 3.6\pi = 11.3 \text{ m}^3 \text{ (3 s.f.)}$$

**Answer: 11.3 m³**

**(b)**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Volume = $11300$ litres OR $V = 3.6\pi \times 1000$ litres | 1 |
| A1 | Time = $11310 ÷ 20 = 565$ min = **9 h 25 min** | 1 |

$$V = 11.31 \times 1000 = 11310 \text{ litres}$$
$$\text{Time} = \frac{11310}{20} = 565.5 \text{ minutes}$$
$$= 9 \text{ hours } 25.5 \text{ minutes} \approx 9 \text{ h } 25 \text{ min}$$

**Answer: 9 hours 25 minutes**

## Question 4 (5 marks)

Two similar cones, A and B, have volumes $64\pi$ cm³ and $125\pi$ cm³ respectively.

The height of cone A is 8 cm.

(a) Find the linear scale factor from cone A to cone B. **[2 marks]**

(b) Find the height of cone B. **[1 mark]**

(c) Find the ratio of the curved surface areas of cone A to cone B. **[2 marks]**

### Mark Scheme Solution

**(a)**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | $k^3 = \frac{125\pi}{64\pi} = \frac{125}{64}$ | 1 |
| A1 | $k = \sqrt[3]{\frac{125}{64}} = \frac{5}{4}$ | 1 |

$$k^3 = \frac{V_B}{V_A} = \frac{125}{64}$$
$$k = \frac{5}{4} = 1.25$$

**Answer: $\frac{5}{4}$ or 1.25**

**(b)**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | $h_B = 8 \times \frac{5}{4}$ | 1 |
| A1 | $h_B = 10$ cm | 1 |

$$h_B = 8 \times \frac{5}{4} = 10 \text{ cm}$$

**Answer: 10 cm**

**(c)**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Area ratio = $k^2$ | 1 |
| A1 | Ratio = $\left(\frac{4}{5}\right)^2 : \left(\frac{5}{4}\right)^2 = 16:25$ | 1 |

$$\text{Area ratio} = k^2 = \left(\frac{4}{5}\right)^2 : \left(\frac{5}{4}\right)^2 = 16 : 25$$

**Answer: 16:25**

## Question 5 (6 marks)

In the diagram, O is the centre of the circle. Points A, B, and C lie on the circumference.

- Angle AOB = 140°
- OA = OB = 10 cm

(a) Calculate the area of sector AOB. **[2 marks]**

(b) Calculate the area of triangle AOB. **[2 marks]**

(c) Calculate the area of the minor segment cut off by chord AB. **[2 marks]**

### Mark Scheme Solution

**(a)**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Area = $\frac{140}{360} \times \pi \times 10^2$ | 1 |
| A1 | Area = $\frac{350\pi}{9}$ cm² or $122.2$ cm² | 1 |

$$\text{Sector area} = \frac{140}{360} \times \pi \times 100 = \frac{350\pi}{9} \text{ cm}^2$$

**Answer: $\frac{350\pi}{9}$ cm² or $122$ cm²**

**(b)**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Area = $\frac{1}{2} \times 10 \times 10 \times \sin(140°)$ | 1 |
| A1 | Area = $50\sin(140°) = 32.1$ cm² | 1 |

$$\text{Triangle area} = \frac{1}{2}r^2\sin\theta = \frac{1}{2} \times 100 \times \sin(140°)$$
$$= 50 \times 0.643 = 32.1 \text{ cm}^2 \text{ (3 s.f.)}$$

**Answer: 32.1 cm²**

**(c)**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Segment = Sector - Triangle | 1 |
| A1 | $\frac{350\pi}{9} - 32.1 = 90.1$ cm² | 1 |

$$\text{Segment area} = \frac{350\pi}{9} - 32.1 = 122.2 - 32.1 = 90.1 \text{ cm}^2$$

**Answer: 90.1 cm²**

## Question 6 (5 marks)

A hollow spherical shell has outer radius 8 cm and inner radius 6 cm.

(a) Calculate the volume of material used to make the shell. Give your answer in terms of $\pi$. **[3 marks]**

(b) The material has density 4.5 g/cm³. Calculate the mass of the shell in kilograms. **[2 marks]**

### Mark Scheme Solution

**(a)**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | $V_{outer} = \frac{4}{3}\pi \times 8^3$ | 1 |
| M1 | $V_{inner} = \frac{4}{3}\pi \times 6^3$ | 1 |
| A1 | $V_{material} = \frac{4}{3}\pi(512 - 216) = \frac{1184\pi}{3}$ cm³ | 1 |

$$V_{outer} = \frac{4}{3}\pi \times 512 = \frac{2048\pi}{3} \text{ cm}^3$$

$$V_{inner} = \frac{4}{3}\pi \times 216 = \frac{864\pi}{3} \text{ cm}^3$$

$$V_{material} = \frac{2048\pi - 864\pi}{3} = \frac{1184\pi}{3} \text{ cm}^3$$

**Answer: $\frac{1184\pi}{3}$ cm³**

**(b)**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Mass = Volume × Density | 1 |
| A1 | $m = \frac{1184\pi}{3} \times 4.5 = 5577$ g = **5.58 kg** | 1 |

$$m = \frac{1184\pi}{3} \times 4.5 = 5576.8 \text{ g} = 5.58 \text{ kg}$$

**Answer: 5.58 kg**

## Question 7 (6 marks)

A solid metal cone has base radius 6 cm and height 8 cm.

The cone is melted down and recast into small spherical balls of radius 0.5 cm.

How many complete balls can be made?

### Mark Scheme Solution

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | $V_{cone} = \frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$ | 1 |
| A1 | $V_{cone} = \frac{1}{3}\pi \times 36 \times 8 = 96\pi$ cm³ | 1 |
| M1 | $V_{sphere} = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$ | 1 |
| A1 | $V_{sphere} = \frac{4}{3}\pi \times 0.125 = \frac{\pi}{6}$ cm³ | 1 |
| M1 | Number = $\frac{96\pi}{\pi/6}$ | 1 |
| A1 | Number = $96 \times 6 = 576$ balls | 1 |

**Working:**

$$V_{cone} = \frac{1}{3}\pi \times 6^2 \times 8 = \frac{1}{3}\pi \times 36 \times 8 = 96\pi \text{ cm}^3$$

$$V_{sphere} = \frac{4}{3}\pi \times 0.5^3 = \frac{4}{3}\pi \times 0.125 = \frac{\pi}{6} \text{ cm}^3$$

$$\text{Number} = \frac{96\pi}{\pi/6} = 96 \times 6 = 576$$

**Answer: 576 balls**

## Question 8 (5 marks)

Two similar jugs have capacities 500 ml and 1500 ml.

The smaller jug has height 14 cm and a base area of 40 cm².

(a) Find the height of the larger jug. **[2 marks]**

(b) Find the base area of the larger jug. **[2 marks]**

(c) The smaller jug is filled with water. How many times must it be filled to completely fill the larger jug? **[1 mark]**

### Mark Scheme Solution

**(a)**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | $k^3 = \frac{1500}{500} = 3$ | 1 |
| A1 | $k = \sqrt[3]{3}$, $h = 14\sqrt[3]{3} = 20.1$ cm | 1 |

$$k^3 = \frac{1500}{500} = 3$$
$$k = \sqrt[3]{3} \approx 1.442$$
$$h_{large} = 14 \times 1.442 = 20.2 \text{ cm}$$

**Answer: 20.2 cm** (accept $14\sqrt[3]{3}$)

**(b)**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| M1 | Area ratio = $k^2 = (\sqrt[3]{3})^2 = 3^{2/3}$ | 1 |
| A1 | Base area = $40 \times 3^{2/3} = 83.2$ cm² | 1 |

$$k^2 = 3^{2/3} \approx 2.08$$
$$\text{Base area} = 40 \times 2.08 = 83.2 \text{ cm}^2$$

**Answer: 83.2 cm²**

**(c)**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| A1 | $\frac{1500}{500} = 3$ times | 1 |

**Answer: 3 times**

## Frequently Tested Question Types

### 1. Arc and Sector Problems (High Frequency)
**Pattern:**
- Given: radius and angle → Find arc length and sector area
- Given: arc/sector → Find radius or angle
- Combined with perimeter questions

**Key Skills:**
- Correct substitution into formulas
- Working backwards from arc/sector to find radius
- Adding radii for perimeter of sector

---

### 2. Volume of Compound Solids (High Frequency)
**Pattern:**
- Identify component shapes
- Calculate individual volumes
- Add or subtract as appropriate

**Key Skills:**
- Recognising component shapes
- Correct formula selection
- Handling hollow objects (subtract inner from outer)

---

### 3. Surface Area of Compound Solids (Medium-High Frequency)
**Pattern:**
- Only external surfaces counted
- Hidden/joined faces excluded

**Key Skills:**
- Visualising the 3D shape
- Identifying which faces contribute to surface area
- Careful counting

---

### 4. Similarity Problems (Medium-High Frequency)
**Pattern:**
- Direct: Given linear SF → find area/volume ratio
- Reverse: Given area/volume ratio → find linear SF
- Applied: Models, maps, shadows

**Key Skills:**
- Correct use of $k$, $k^2$, $k^3$
- Working in both directions
- Applying to real-world contexts
