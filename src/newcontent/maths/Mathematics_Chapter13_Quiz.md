### Question 1
**Factorise completely:** $4x^2 - 16$

| Option | Answer |
|--------|--------|
| A | $(2x + 4)(2x - 4)$ |
| B | $4(x + 2)(x - 2)$ |
| C | $4(x + 4)(x - 4)$ |
| D | $(4x + 16)(x - 1)$ |

**Answer:** **B**

**Explanation:** 
- Take out common factor 4: $4(x^2 - 4)$
- Recognise difference of two squares: $4(x + 2)(x - 2)$
- Option A is not fully factorised (2 can still come out)

### Question 2
**Factorise:** $x^2 - 11x + 28$

| Option | Answer |
|--------|--------|
| A | $(x - 4)(x - 7)$ |
| B | $(x + 4)(x - 7)$ |
| C | $(x - 4)(x + 7)$ |
| D | $(x + 4)(x + 7)$ |

**Answer:** **A**

**Explanation:** 
- We need two numbers with product 28 and sum -11
- Both numbers must be negative (since sum is negative and product is positive)
- $-4 \times -7 = 28$ and $-4 + (-7) = -11$ ✓

### Question 3
**Factorise:** $2x^2 - 5x - 12$

| Option | Answer |
|--------|--------|
| A | $(2x + 3)(x - 4)$ |
| B | $(2x - 3)(x + 4)$ |
| C | $(2x + 4)(x - 3)$ |
| D | $(2x - 4)(x + 3)$ |

**Answer:** **A**

**Explanation:**
- Using AC method: $a \times c = 2 \times (-12) = -24$
- Numbers with product -24 and sum -5: **-8 and +3**
- Split: $2x^2 - 8x + 3x - 12$
- Group: $2x(x - 4) + 3(x - 4) = (2x + 3)(x - 4)$

### Question 5
**Simplify:** $\frac{3x^2 - 12}{6x + 12}$

| Option | Answer |
|--------|--------|
| A | $\frac{x-2}{2}$ |
| B | $\frac{x+2}{2}$ |
| C | $\frac{x-2}{4}$ |
| D | $\frac{x-2}{2(x+2)}$ |

**Answer:** **A**

**Explanation:**
- Factorise numerator: $3x^2 - 12 = 3(x^2 - 4) = 3(x+2)(x-2)$
- Factorise denominator: $6x + 12 = 6(x + 2)$
- $\frac{3(x+2)(x-2)}{6(x+2)} = \frac{3(x-2)}{6} = \frac{x-2}{2}$

### Question 9
**Solve the simultaneous equations:**
$$\begin{cases} 2x + y = 10 \\ 3x - y = 5 \end{cases}$$

| Option | Answer |
|--------|--------|
| A | $x = 3, y = 4$ |
| B | $x = 4, y = 3$ |

**Answer:** **A**

**Explanation:**

### Question 10
**Solve the simultaneous equations:**
$$\begin{cases} y = x^2 - 2x + 5 \\ y = 2x + 2 \end{cases}$$

| Option | Answer |
|--------|--------|
| A | $(1, 4)$ only |
| B | $(3, 8)$ only |
| C | $(1, 4)$ and $(3, 8)$ |
| D | No solutions |

**Answer:** **C**

**Explanation:**
- Substitute: $2x + 2 = x^2 - 2x + 5$
- Rearrange: $x^2 - 4x + 3 = 0$
- Factorise: $(x - 1)(x - 3) = 0$
- $x = 1$: $y = 2(1) + 2 = 4$
- $x = 3$: $y = 2(3) + 2 = 8$

### Question 11
**Which expression is equivalent to:** $\frac{x^2-16}{x^2-4x} \times \frac{x}{x+4}$

| Option | Answer |
|--------|--------|
| A | $\frac{x+4}{x-4}$ |
| B | $\frac{x-4}{x}$ |
| C | $\frac{x+4}{x}$ |
| D | $1$ |

**Answer:** **B**

**Explanation:**
- Factorise: $\frac{(x+4)(x-4)}{x(x-4)} \times \frac{x}{x+4}$
- Cancel: $\frac{\cancel{(x+4)}\cancel{(x-4)}}{\cancel{x}\cancel{(x-4)}} \times \frac{\cancel{x}}{\cancel{x+4}}$
- $= \frac{x-4}{x}$

# Section B: True/False Questions

**Instructions:** State whether each statement is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.

---

### Question 1
**Statement:** $\frac{x+3}{x} = 3$ when simplifying algebraic fractions.

**Answer:** **FALSE**

**Correction:** Cannot cancel terms - only factors. The expression $\frac{x+3}{x}$ cannot be simplified further.

---

### Question 2
**Statement:** $x^2 - 9 = (x-3)^2$

**Answer:** **FALSE**

**Correction:** $x^2 - 9 = (x+3)(x-3)$ (difference of two squares), whereas $(x-3)^2 = x^2 - 6x + 9$ (perfect square).

---

### Question 3
**Statement:** When factorising $x^2 + bx + c$ where $c > 0$ and $b < 0$, both brackets contain negative numbers.

**Answer:** **TRUE**

**Explanation:** Since $c > 0$ (product of factors is positive) and $b < 0$ (sum of factors is negative), both factors must be negative.

---

### Question 4
**Statement:** $4x^2 - 25 = (4x + 5)(4x - 5)$

**Answer:** **FALSE**

**Correction:** $4x^2 - 25 = (2x)^2 - 5^2 = (2x + 5)(2x - 5)$

---

### Question 5
**Statement:** When solving $\frac{3}{x-2} = \frac{4}{x+1}$, cross-multiplication gives $3(x+1) = 4(x-2)$.

**Answer:** **TRUE**

**Explanation:** Cross-multiplication: numerator₁ × denominator₂ = numerator₂ × denominator₁

---

### Question 6
**Statement:** The solution to $x^2 + 6x + 9 = 0$ is $x = -3$ only.

**Answer:** **TRUE**

**Explanation:** $x^2 + 6x + 9 = (x+3)^2 = 0$, so $x = -3$ is the repeated root.

---

### Question 7
**Statement:** When subtracting algebraic fractions, the negative sign only applies to the first term of the numerator.

**Answer:** **FALSE**

**Correction:** The negative sign applies to ALL terms in the numerator being subtracted. For example: $\frac{a}{c} - \frac{b+d}{c} = \frac{a - (b+d)}{c} = \frac{a - b - d}{c}$

---

### Question 8
**Statement:** The LCD of $\frac{1}{x}$ and $\frac{1}{x^2}$ is $x^2$.

**Answer:** **TRUE**

**Explanation:** The lowest common multiple of $x$ and $x^2$ is $x^2$.

---

### Question 9
**Statement:** A linear equation and a quadratic equation can have at most 2 solutions.

**Answer:** **TRUE**

**Explanation:** When substituting a linear equation into a quadratic, the resulting quadratic has at most 2 solutions.

---

### Question 10
**Statement:** Factorising $3x^2 - 12$ completely gives $3(x+2)(x-2)$.

**Answer:** **TRUE**

**Explanation:** 
- Factor out 3: $3(x^2 - 4)$
- Apply difference of two squares: $3(x+2)(x-2)$

---

### Question 11
**Statement:** $\frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} = x + 1$ for all values of $x$.

**Answer:** **FALSE**

**Correction:** $\frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} = x + 1$ for all values of $x$ **except $x = 1$** (where the expression is undefined).

---

### Question 12
**Statement:** When solving simultaneous equations $2x + y = 7$ and $3x - 2y = 0$, the method of elimination requires multiplying the first equation by 2.

**Answer:** **TRUE**

**Explanation:** To eliminate $y$, we need the same coefficient. Multiplying the first equation by 2 gives $4x + 2y = 14$, which can be added to $3x - 2y = 0$.

## Method Marks (M) vs Accuracy Marks (A)

- **M marks** are for correct method even if final answer is wrong
- **A marks** require correct answer (depend on M marks)
- **Always show working** to earn method marks
