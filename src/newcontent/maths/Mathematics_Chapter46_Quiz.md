### Question 1
In the diagram, $PT$ is a tangent to the circle at point $A$. Chord $AB$ divides the circle into two segments. The angle between tangent $PT$ and chord $AB$ is $48°$. What is the angle in the alternate segment?

**A)** $24°$  
**B)** $48°$  
**C)** $96°$  
**D)** $132°$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** By the alternate segment theorem, the angle between a tangent and a chord equals the angle in the alternate segment. Therefore, the angle in the alternate segment is $48°$.

### Question 2
Two chords $AB$ and $CD$ intersect at point $P$ inside a circle. If $AP = 3$ cm, $PB = 8$ cm, and $CP = 4$ cm, what is the length of $PD$?

**A)** $5$ cm  
**B)** $6$ cm  
**C)** $7$ cm  
**D)** $10$ cm

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Using the intersecting chords theorem:
$$AP \times PB = CP \times PD$$
$$3 \times 8 = 4 \times PD$$
$$24 = 4 \times PD$$
$$PD = 6 \text{ cm}$$

### Question 3
From an external point $P$, a tangent $PT$ of length 15 cm touches a circle. A secant from $P$ meets the circle at $A$ and $B$, where $PA = 9$ cm. What is the length of $PB$?

**A)** $18$ cm  
**B)** $25$ cm  
**C)** $20$ cm  
**D)** $16$ cm

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Using the tangent-secant theorem:
$$PT^2 = PA \times PB$$
$$15^2 = 9 \times PB$$
$$225 = 9 \times PB$$
$$PB = 25 \text{ cm}$$

### Question 8
In the alternate segment theorem, the alternate segment is:
**A)** The segment containing the tangent  
**B)** The segment opposite to where the tangent meets the chord  
**C)** The larger segment only  
**D)** The smaller segment only

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The alternate segment is specifically the segment that does NOT contain the tangent. It is the segment on the opposite side of the chord from where the tangent touches.

### Question 9
$XY$ is a tangent at $A$ to a circle with centre $O$. Chord $AB$ subtends an angle of $130°$ at the centre. What is the angle between tangent $XY$ and chord $AB$?

**A)** $65°$  
**B)** $50°$  
**C)** $130°$  
**D)** $25°$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Angle at centre = 2 × angle at circumference.
$$\angle AOB = 130°$$
$$\angle ACB = \frac{130°}{2} = 65°$$
By alternate segment theorem, angle between tangent and chord equals angle in alternate segment = $65°$.

### Question 10
If two chords intersect at the centre of a circle, and each chord is divided into segments of lengths $a$, $b$ and $c$, $d$ respectively, what is the relationship between the segments?

**A)** $a = c$ and $b = d$  
**B)** $a + b = c + d$  
**C)** $a \times b = c \times d$  
**D)** $a \times d = b \times c$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** When chords intersect at the centre, each chord is a diameter (or segments are equal because they're radii). Therefore, the segments on each chord are equal: $a = b$ and $c = d$ on each individual chord through the centre.

## SECTION B: True/False Statements (10 marks)

### Statement 1
"The angle between a tangent and a chord is always equal to the angle in the alternate segment."

**Answer: TRUE**

This is the exact statement of the alternate segment theorem.

---

### Statement 2
"For intersecting chords, the products of the segments on opposite sides are equal."

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** For intersecting chords, the products of segments on the **same chord** are equal: $AP \times PB = CP \times PD$. The segments being multiplied are from the same chord, not opposite sides.

---

### Statement 3
"The tangent-secant theorem states that $PT = PA \times PB$ where $PT$ is the tangent length."

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The tangent-secant theorem states that $PT^2 = PA \times PB$. The tangent length is squared, not equal to the product.

---

### Statement 4
"In the alternate segment theorem, the alternate segment is the segment that does NOT contain the tangent."

**Answer: TRUE**

This is the correct definition of the alternate segment.

---

### Statement 5
"When two chords intersect inside a circle, if one chord is bisected, the other chord must also be bisected."

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** If one chord is bisected at the intersection point, the product relationship becomes: $\frac{AP^2}{2} = CP \times PD$. The other chord is only bisected if it passes through the centre of the circle.

---

### Statement 6
"A tangent is always perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact."

**Answer: TRUE**

This is a fundamental circle theorem.

---

### Statement 7
"The alternate segment theorem can only be used when the angle in the alternate segment is acute."

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The alternate segment theorem works for any angle. The angle in the alternate segment can be acute, right, or obtuse depending on the configuration of the chord.

---

### Statement 8
"For external secants, the formula $PA \times PB = PC \times PD$ uses the full length from the external point to each intersection with the circle."

**Answer: TRUE**

This correctly describes the secant-secant theorem for external intersection.

---

### Statement 9
"If a tangent and a secant are drawn from the same external point, the tangent length is always greater than the external part of the secant."

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The tangent length can be greater or smaller than the external part of the secant. The relationship is $PT^2 = PA \times PB$, which does not imply any direct comparison between $PT$ and $PA$.

---

### Statement 10
"The alternate segment theorem can be proved using the angle in a semicircle theorem and angles in the same segment theorem."

**Answer: TRUE**

The proof of the alternate segment theorem uses these two theorems along with the tangent-radius perpendicular property.

### Question 11 (3 marks)

Two chords $AB$ and $CD$ intersect at point $P$ inside a circle. Given that $AP = 6$ cm, $PB = 4$ cm, and $CD = 11$ cm, find the lengths of $CP$ and $PD$.

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Apply intersecting chords theorem
$$AP \times PB = CP \times PD$$
$$6 \times 4 = CP \times PD$$
$$CP \times PD = 24$$

**Step 2:** Use the relationship between $CP$, $PD$, and $CD$
Since $CP + PD = CD = 11$ cm, we need two numbers that multiply to 24 and sum to 11.

**Step 3:** Solve the system
Let $CP = x$, then $PD = 11 - x$
$$x(11 - x) = 24$$
$$11x - x^2 = 24$$
$$x^2 - 11x + 24 = 0$$
$$(x - 3)(x - 8) = 0$$
$$x = 3 \text{ or } x = 8$$

**Answer:** $CP = 3$ cm and $PD = 8$ cm (or vice versa)

**Mark Allocation:**
- M1: Correct use of intersecting chords theorem
- M1: Setting up equation with sum of segments
- A1: Correct values for both segments

### Question 13 (5 marks)

From an external point $P$, two lines are drawn to a circle. One is a tangent $PT$ of length 10 cm. The other is a secant $PAB$ where $PA = 5$ cm.

(a) Find the length $PB$. [2 marks]

(b) Calculate the length $AB$. [1 mark]

(c) If another secant $PCD$ is drawn where $PC = 4$ cm, find $PD$. [2 marks]

**Solution:**

**(a) Finding $PB$**

**Step 1:** Apply tangent-secant theorem
$$PT^2 = PA \times PB$$

**Step 2:** Substitute values
$$10^2 = 5 \times PB$$
$$100 = 5 \times PB$$
$$PB = 20 \text{ cm}$$

**Answer:** $PB = 20$ cm

**(b) Finding $AB$**

$$AB = PB - PA = 20 - 5 = 15 \text{ cm}$$

**Answer:** $AB = 15$ cm

**(c) Finding $PD$**

**Step 1:** Apply secant-secant theorem
$$PA \times PB = PC \times PD$$

**Step 2:** Substitute values
$$5 \times 20 = 4 \times PD$$
$$100 = 4 \times PD$$
$$PD = 25 \text{ cm}$$

**Answer:** $PD = 25$ cm

**Mark Allocation:**
- (a) M1: Correct theorem; A1: Correct calculation
- (b) A1: Correct subtraction
- (c) M1: Correct theorem; A1: Correct calculation

### Question 14 (6 marks)

$AB$ is a tangent to a circle at $T$. $OTC$ is a diameter, where $O$ is the centre. Chord $TD$ is drawn such that $\angle OTD = 35°$.

(a) Find $\angle OTB$. [1 mark]

(b) Find $\angle TDC$. [2 marks]

(c) Find $\angle BT D$. [3 marks]

**Solution:**

**(a) Finding $\angle OTB$**

**Step 1:** Tangent is perpendicular to radius
Since $AB$ is tangent at $T$ and $OT$ is a radius:
$$\angle OTB = 90°$$

**Answer:** $\angle OTB = 90°$

**(b) Finding $\angle TDC$**

**Step 1:** Recognise angle in semicircle
Since $OTC$ is a diameter and $D$ is on the circumference:
$$\angle TDC = 90°$$

**Answer:** $\angle TDC = 90°$

**(c) Finding $\angle BTD$**

**Step 1:** Find $\angle BTC$ (angle between tangent and diameter)
Since $AB$ is tangent at $T$ and $OTC$ is a diameter:
$$\angle BTC = 90°$$

**Step 2:** Find $\angle CTD$
In right triangle $TDC$:
$$\angle DTC + \angle TCD = 90°$$
We need more information or use alternate segment.

**Step 3:** Apply alternate segment theorem
Angle between tangent $AB$ and chord $TD$ equals angle in alternate segment.

$$\angle BTD = \angle TCD$$

In triangle $TDC$:
$$\angle DTC + \angle TCD + 90° = 180°$$
$$\angle DTC + \angle TCD = 90°$$

Also, $\angle DTC = \angle OTD = 35°$ (same angle)
Therefore: $35° + \angle TCD = 90°$
$$\angle TCD = 55°$$

**Step 4:** Find $\angle BTD$
By alternate segment theorem:
$$\angle BTD = \angle TCD = 55°$$

**Answer:** $\angle BTD = 55°$

**Mark Allocation:**
- (a) A1: Recognising tangent ⊥ radius
- (b) M1: Recognising semicircle; A1: Correct angle
- (c) M1: Using alternate segment; M1: Finding angle in triangle; A1: Correct angle

### Question 15 (7 marks)

Prove that when two chords $AB$ and $CD$ intersect at point $P$ inside a circle, $AP \times PB = CP \times PD$.

**Solution:**

**Proof:**

| Step | Statement | Reason |
|------|-----------|--------|
| 1 | Join $AC$ and $BD$ | Construction |
| 2 | $\angle CAP = \angle BDP$ | Angles in same segment (subtended by arc $CB$) |
| 3 | $\angle ACP = \angle DBP$ | Angles in same segment (subtended by arc $AD$) |
| 4 | $\angle APC = \angle BPD$ | Vertically opposite angles |
| 5 | Triangles $APC$ and $DPB$ are similar | AAA similarity |
| 6 | $\frac{AP}{DP} = \frac{CP}{BP}$ | Corresponding sides of similar triangles |
| 7 | $AP \times BP = CP \times DP$ | Cross-multiplication |

$\blacksquare$ QED

**Mark Allocation:**
- M1: Construction (joining points)
- M1: Identifying angles in same segment
- M1: Recognising vertically opposite angles
- M1: Stating similar triangles with reason
- M1: Setting up ratio from similar triangles
- M1: Cross-multiplication
- A1: Correct conclusion

### Problem-Solving Patterns

**Pattern 1: Angle Chain**
1. Start with tangent-radius (90°)
2. Apply angle at centre theorem
3. Use alternate segment
4. Complete with triangle angle sum

**Pattern 2: Length Chain**
1. Identify intersection type
2. Apply appropriate product theorem
3. Use algebra for unknown
4. Check reasonableness

**Pattern 3: Proof Construction**
1. Draw auxiliary lines
2. Identify similar triangles
3. Use angle properties
4. Conclude with algebra

### Command Word Guidance

| Command Word | What to Do |
|--------------|------------|
| **Find** | Calculate and give answer |

### How to Gain Full Marks

#### For Angle Questions
1. **Always state the theorem** you are using (M1)
2. **Show substitution** of values (M1)
3. **Give final answer** with degree symbol (A1)
4. **Check reasonableness** - angles should be between 0° and 180°

#### For Length Questions
1. **Write the formula** clearly (M1)
2. **Substitute values** (M1)
3. **Show calculation** (M1)
4. **State answer with units** (A1)

#### For Proof Questions
1. **Draw construction** lines clearly (M1)
2. **State each step** with reason (M1 each)
3. **Use correct notation** ($\angle$, $\triangle$) (Accuracy)
4. **End with QED** or $\blacksquare$ (Completion)
