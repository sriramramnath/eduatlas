# Section B: True/False Questions

**Instructions:** State whether each statement is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.

---

### Question 1

**Statement:** When comparing products, the one with the lowest total price always offers the best value.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** When comparing products, the one with the lowest unit price (cost per gram, per litre, etc.) offers the best value, not necessarily the lowest total price.

---

### Question 2

**Statement:** Net pay is calculated by adding all deductions to gross pay.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Net pay is calculated by subtracting all deductions from gross pay:
$$\text{Net Pay} = \text{Gross Pay} - \text{Deductions}$$

---

### Question 3

**Statement:** If the exchange rate is £1 = $1.25, then to convert $100 to pounds, you divide $100 by 1.25.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

### Question 4

**Statement:** Personal allowance is the amount of income on which you pay the basic rate of tax.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Personal allowance is the amount of income you can earn tax-free. Taxable income starts after the personal allowance is subtracted.

---

### Question 5

**Statement:** Time-and-a-half overtime means you are paid 1.5 times your normal hourly rate.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

### Question 6

**Statement:** When a bank buys foreign currency from you, it uses a rate that gives you more of your home currency.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** When a bank buys foreign currency from you, it uses a rate that gives you LESS of your home currency. This is how banks make profit on exchange.

---

### Question 7

**Statement:** Commission is always calculated as a fixed amount regardless of sales value.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Commission is usually calculated as a percentage of sales value:
$$\text{Commission} = \text{Percentage Rate} \times \text{Sales Value}$$

---

### Question 8

**Statement:** If someone earns above the higher tax threshold, ALL their income is taxed at 40%.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Only the income ABOVE the higher tax threshold is taxed at 40%. Income in lower bands is still taxed at the lower rates (0%, 20%, etc.).

---

### Question 9

**Statement:** When converting from pounds to a foreign currency, you multiply by the exchange rate.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

### Question 10

**Statement:** A "3 for 2" offer means you pay for 3 items but only receive 2 items.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A "3 for 2" offer means you pay for 2 items but receive 3 items (the cheapest one is free).

---

### Question 11

**Statement:** Gross annual salary divided by 52 gives the weekly gross pay.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

### Question 12

**Statement:** If the pound strengthens against the dollar, British tourists will get fewer dollars for their pounds.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** If the pound strengthens against the dollar, British tourists will get MORE dollars for their pounds. A stronger pound means each pound buys more foreign currency.

# Section D: Exam Focus & Strategy

## Frequently Tested Question Types

### 1. Unit Price Comparisons
**Frequency:** Very High
**Typical format:** Compare 2-3 products with different quantities
**Key skill:** Calculate cost per unit and compare

### 2. Tax Calculations with Multiple Bands
**Frequency:** High
**Typical format:** Calculate tax for income spanning multiple bands
**Key skill:** Split income correctly across bands

### 3. Currency Exchange with Fees
**Frequency:** High
**Typical format:** Convert currency with commission/fees
**Key skill:** Include all costs in final calculation

### 4. Best Deal Comparison
**Frequency:** Medium-High
**Typical format:** Compare multiple offers/discounts
**Key skill:** Calculate total cost for required quantity

---

## Typical Problem-Solving Patterns

### Pattern 1: The Unit Price Decision
```
1. Identify quantities and prices
2. Convert to same units if needed
3. Calculate unit price for each option
4. Compare and conclude
```

### Pattern 2: The Tax Band Problem
```
1. Calculate taxable income (Gross - Allowance)
2. Draw a table showing bands
3. Allocate income to each band
4. Calculate tax for each band
5. Sum all taxes
```

### Pattern 3: The Currency Conversion
```
1. Identify direction (home→foreign or foreign→home)
2. Choose correct operation (multiply or divide)
3. Apply any fees/commission
4. State final amount with currency symbol
```

### Pattern 4: The Total Cost Comparison
```
1. For each option, calculate ALL costs
2. Include hidden costs (fees, extra charges)
3. Compare totals
4. State which is cheaper with reason
```

- [ ] Show tax band allocation (1-2 marks)
- [ ] Show tax calculation for each band (1-2 marks)

- [ ] State final net pay if asked (1 mark)

### For Currency Questions:
- [ ] Identify correct rate (1 mark)
- [ ] Show correct operation (multiply/divide) (1 mark)
- [ ] Calculate commission/fees (1 mark)
- [ ] Give final answer with currency symbol (1 mark)

---

## Mark Scheme Insights

### Method Marks (M)
- Given for correct approach
- Can be earned even if answer is wrong
- Must show the calculation

### Accuracy Marks (A)
- Given for correct final answer
- Must be based on correct method
- Check rounding requirements

### Process Marks (P)
- Given for organizing work correctly
- Tables, clear steps, logical flow
- Helps examiner follow your working

---

## Final Exam Tips

✅ **Always show working** - Even if unsure, write down what you know

✅ **Use tables** - Great for organizing tax bands and comparisons

✅ **Label your work** - Make it clear what each calculation is for

✅ **Check units** - Ensure consistent units throughout

✅ **State conclusions** - Don't just leave the answer; say what it means

✅ **Show currency symbols** - £, $, € to show you understand

✅ **Round at the end** - Keep accuracy until final answer

✅ **Check reasonableness** - Net pay should be less than gross; unit prices should be reasonable
