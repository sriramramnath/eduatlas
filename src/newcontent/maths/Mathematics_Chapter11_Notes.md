## 1. SIMPLIFYING ALGEBRAIC EXPRESSIONS (COLLECTING LIKE TERMS)

### 1.1 What is an Algebraic Expression?

An **algebraic expression** is a combination of numbers, variables (letters), and operations (+, −, ×, ÷).

**Examples:**
- $3x + 5$ is an expression
- $2a - 3b + 7$ is an expression
- $x^2 + 2x - 1$ is an expression

> **Key Distinction:** An expression does NOT have an equals sign (=). An equation DOES have an equals sign.

### 1.2 Understanding Terms

A **term** is a single number, variable, or numbers and variables multiplied together.

**Example:** In the expression $3x^2 + 5xy - 7y + 2$
- $3x^2$ is a term (coefficient 3, variable $x^2$)
- $5xy$ is a term (coefficient 5, variables $x$ and $y$)
- $-7y$ is a term (coefficient −7, variable $y$)
- $2$ is a term (called a **constant term**)

### 1.3 What are Like Terms?

**Like terms** have the **same variable(s) raised to the same power(s)**.

| Like Terms | Why They Are Like Terms |
|------------|------------------------|
| $3x$ and $-5x$ | Both have $x^1$ |
| $2ab$ and $7ab$ | Both have $ab$ |
| $4y^2$ and $-y^2$ | Both have $y^2$ |
| $5$ and $-3$ | Both are constants |

| NOT Like Terms | Why They Are NOT Like Terms |
|----------------|----------------------------|
| $3x$ and $3x^2$ | Different powers of $x$ |
| $2ab$ and $2a$ | Different variables |
| $4x$ and $4y$ | Different variables |

>  **COMMON MISTAKE:** $x^2$ and $x$ are NOT like terms. You cannot add or subtract them!

### 1.4 The Rule for Collecting Like Terms

**Only like terms can be combined (added or subtracted).**

When collecting like terms:
1. Identify all like terms
2. Add or subtract their coefficients
3. Keep the variable part the same

### 1.5 Worked Examples

#### Example 1: Simple Collection
**Simplify:** $3x + 2x$

**Solution:**
$$3x + 2x = (3 + 2)x = 5x$$

**Why:** Both terms have $x$, so add the coefficients: $3 + 2 = 5$.

---

#### Example 2: Multiple Variables
**Simplify:** $4a + 3b - 2a + 5b$

**Solution:**
$$4a + 3b - 2a + 5b$$

Step 1: Collect the $a$ terms:
$$4a - 2a = 2a$$

Step 2: Collect the $b$ terms:
$$3b + 5b = 8b$$

Step 3: Combine:
$$\boxed{2a + 8b}$$

>  **TIP:** Always write terms in alphabetical order: $a$ before $b$.

---

#### Example 3: Including Powers
**Simplify:** $5x^2 + 3x - 2x^2 + 7x$

**Solution:**
$$5x^2 + 3x - 2x^2 + 7x$$

Step 1: Collect $x^2$ terms:
$$5x^2 - 2x^2 = 3x^2$$

Step 2: Collect $x$ terms:
$$3x + 7x = 10x$$

Step 3: Combine:
$$\boxed{3x^2 + 10x}$$

>  **Remember:** $x^2$ and $x$ are different powers – they CANNOT be combined!

---

#### Example 4: Constants and Multiple Variables
**Simplify:** $7xy + 3x - 2xy - 5 + x - 4$

**Solution:**
$$7xy + 3x - 2xy - 5 + x - 4$$

Step 1: Collect $xy$ terms:
$$7xy - 2xy = 5xy$$

Step 2: Collect $x$ terms:
$$3x + x = 4x$$

Step 3: Collect constants:
$$-5 - 4 = -9$$

Step 4: Combine:
$$\boxed{5xy + 4x - 9}$$

---

#### Example 5: With Negative Coefficients
**Simplify:** $-3a + 5b - a - 7b + 2a$

**Solution:**
$$-3a + 5b - a - 7b + 2a$$

Step 1: Collect $a$ terms:
$$-3a - a + 2a = -4a + 2a = -2a$$

Step 2: Collect $b$ terms:
$$5b - 7b = -2b$$

Step 3: Combine:
$$\boxed{-2a - 2b}$$

>  **TIP:** $-2a - 2b$ can also be written as $-2(a + b)$ if factorising is required.

---

### 1.6 Key Exam Points

| Point | Explanation |
|-------|-------------|
| **Always check powers** | $x^2$ ≠ $x$ – different powers means different terms |
| **Watch the signs** | A minus sign belongs to the term following it |
| **Order matters for neatness** | Write in order: powers first, then alphabetically |
| **Coefficients of 1** | $x$ means $1x$, $-x$ means $-1x$ |

## 2. EXPRESSIONS WITH BRACKETS

### 2.1 Understanding Brackets

Brackets (parentheses) are used to **group terms together**. They indicate that the operation outside the bracket applies to **everything inside**.

**Key Point:** In expressions with brackets, you may need to either:
- **Simplify inside** the brackets first (if possible)
- **Expand** the brackets (covered in Section 3)

### 2.2 When Can We Simplify Inside Brackets?

You can simplify inside brackets when there are like terms within.

#### Example 6: Simplifying Inside Brackets
**Simplify:** $2(x + 3) + 4(x + 2)$

**Solution:** You cannot simplify inside each bracket (only one term with $x$ in each).

But if we had: $2(x + x + 3)$, this could become $2(2x + 3)$.

---

### 2.3 Writing Expressions from Words

This is a **frequently tested** skill in IGCSE exams.

| Words | Expression |
|-------|------------|
| "The sum of $x$ and $5$" | $x + 5$ |
| "The product of $3$ and $y$" | $3y$ |
| "5 more than $x$" | $x + 5$ |
| "5 less than $x$" | $x - 5$ |
| "Twice the value of $a$" | $2a$ |
| "$x$ squared" | $x^2$ |
| "The sum of $x$ and $y$, all squared" | $(x + y)^2$ |

>  **COMMON MISTAKE:** "5 less than $x$" is $x - 5$, NOT $5 - x$!

---

### 2.4 Substituting into Expressions

**Substitution** means replacing variables with numerical values.

#### Example 7: Basic Substitution
**Find the value of $3x + 7$ when $x = 4$**

**Solution:**
$$3x + 7 = 3(4) + 7 = 12 + 7 = 19$$

---

#### Example 8: Substitution with Brackets
**Find the value of $2(x + 5)$ when $x = 3$**

**Method 1: Substitute first**
$$2(3 + 5) = 2(8) = 16$$

**Method 2: Expand first**
$$2(x + 5) = 2x + 10$$
$$2(3) + 10 = 6 + 10 = 16$$

Both methods give the same answer! ✓

---

#### Example 9: Substitution with Negative Values
**Find the value of $5 - 2x$ when $x = -3$**

**Solution:**
$$5 - 2x = 5 - 2(-3) = 5 + 6 = 11$$

>  **CAREFUL:** $-2(-3) = +6$ (negative × negative = positive)

---

#### Example 10: Substitution with Powers
**Find the value of $x^2 + 3x - 7$ when $x = -2$**

**Solution:**
$$x^2 + 3x - 7 = (-2)^2 + 3(-2) - 7$$
$$= 4 + (-6) - 7$$
$$= 4 - 6 - 7$$
$$= \boxed{-9}$$

>  **COMMON MISTAKE:** $(-2)^2 = 4$ but $-2^2 = -4$. Brackets matter!

---

### 2.5 Formulae and Expressions

A **formula** shows the relationship between variables. It has an equals sign.

#### Example 11: Using a Formula
**The area of a rectangle is given by $A = lw$ where $l$ is length and $w$ is width. Find $A$ when $l = 8$ cm and $w = 5$ cm.**

**Solution:**
$$A = lw = 8 \times 5 = 40 \text{ cm}^2$$

## 3. EXPANDING SINGLE BRACKETS

### 3.1 What is Expanding?

**Expanding** means removing brackets by multiplying each term inside the bracket by the term outside.

### 3.2 The Golden Rule for Expanding

$$\boxed{a(b + c) = ab + ac}$$

**Every term inside the bracket is multiplied by the term outside.**

>  **COMMON MISTAKE:** Forgetting to multiply ALL terms inside the bracket!

### 3.3 Step-by-Step Method

1. Multiply the outside term by the first term inside
2. Multiply the outside term by the second term inside
3. Continue until all terms are multiplied
4. Simplify if possible

### 3.4 Worked Examples

#### Example 12: Basic Expansion
**Expand:** $3(x + 4)$

**Solution:**
$$3(x + 4) = 3 \times x + 3 \times 4 = \boxed{3x + 12}$$

---

#### Example 13: Expansion with Subtraction
**Expand:** $5(2x - 3)$

**Solution:**
$$5(2x - 3) = 5 \times 2x + 5 \times (-3) = \boxed{10x - 15}$$

>  **TIP:** Think of the $-3$ as $+(-3)$, so $5 \times (-3) = -15$

---

#### Example 14: Negative Outside the Bracket
**Expand:** $-2(x + 5)$

**Solution:**
$$-2(x + 5) = -2 \times x + (-2) \times 5 = \boxed{-2x - 10}$$

>  **WATCH THE SIGNS:** Both terms become negative!

---

#### Example 15: Negative Outside with Subtraction Inside
**Expand:** $-3(2x - 4)$

**Solution:**
$$-3(2x - 4) = -3 \times 2x + (-3) \times (-4)$$
$$= -6x + 12$$
$$= \boxed{-6x + 12}$$

> **Check the signs carefully:**
> - $-3 \times 2x = -6x$ (negative × positive = negative)
> - $-3 \times (-4) = +12$ (negative × negative = positive)

---

#### Example 16: Variable Outside the Bracket
**Expand:** $x(x + 7)$

**Solution:**
$$x(x + 7) = x \times x + x \times 7 = \boxed{x^2 + 7x}$$

---

#### Example 17: Variable Outside with Multiple Terms
**Expand:** $2x(3x - 4)$

**Solution:**
$$2x(3x - 4) = 2x \times 3x + 2x \times (-4)$$
$$= 6x^2 - 8x$$
$$= \boxed{6x^2 - 8x}$$

> **Remember:** $2x \times 3x = 6x^2$ (multiply coefficients, add powers of same variable)

---

#### Example 18: Three Terms Inside the Bracket
**Expand:** $4(2x + 3y - 5)$

**Solution:**
$$4(2x + 3y - 5) = 4 \times 2x + 4 \times 3y + 4 \times (-5)$$
$$= 8x + 12y - 20$$
$$= \boxed{8x + 12y - 20}$$

---

#### Example 19: Expansion and Simplification
**Expand and simplify:** $3(x + 2) + 4(x - 5)$

**Solution:**

Step 1: Expand each bracket
$$3(x + 2) = 3x + 6$$
$$4(x - 5) = 4x - 20$$

Step 2: Add the results
$$3x + 6 + 4x - 20$$

Step 3: Collect like terms
$$3x + 4x + 6 - 20 = 7x - 14$$

$$\boxed{7x - 14}$$

---

#### Example 20: Expansion with Subtraction of Brackets
**Expand and simplify:** $5(2x + 1) - 2(x - 3)$

**Solution:**

Step 1: Expand each bracket
$$5(2x + 1) = 10x + 5$$
$$-2(x - 3) = -2x + 6$$

>  **CAREFUL:** The minus sign before the 2 belongs to the 2!

Step 2: Combine
$$10x + 5 - 2x + 6$$

Step 3: Collect like terms
$$10x - 2x + 5 + 6 = 8x + 11$$

$$\boxed{8x + 11}$$

---

### 3.5 Sign Rules Summary Table

| Outside | Inside | Result |
|---------|--------|--------|
| $+$ | $+$ | $+$ |
| $+$ | $-$ | $-$ |
| $-$ | $+$ | $-$ |
| $-$ | $-$ | $+$ |

### 3.6 Common Exam Errors

| Error | Wrong | Correct |
|-------|-------|---------|
| Forgetting to multiply all terms | $3(x + 4) = 3x + 4$ ✗ | $3(x + 4) = 3x + 12$ ✓ |
| Sign error with negative outside | $-2(x - 3) = -2x - 6$ ✗ | $-2(x - 3) = -2x + 6$ ✓ |
| Forgetting $x \times x = x^2$ | $x(x + 3) = x + 3x$ ✗ | $x(x + 3) = x^2 + 3x$ ✓ |

## 4. SOLVING EQUATIONS

### 4.1 What is an Equation?

An **equation** is a mathematical statement that two expressions are equal, shown by the equals sign (=).

**Examples:**
- $x + 3 = 7$
- $2x - 5 = 11$
- $3(x + 2) = 21$

### 4.2 What Does "Solve" Mean?

To **solve** an equation means to find the **value of the variable** that makes the equation true.

**Example:** The solution to $x + 3 = 7$ is $x = 4$ because $4 + 3 = 7$ ✓

### 4.3 The Balance Method

Think of an equation as a **balance scale**. Whatever you do to one side, you must do to the other side to keep it balanced.

| Operation | What you do | Example |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Add | Add same to both sides | $x - 3 = 5$ → add 3 to both sides |
| Subtract | Subtract same from both sides | $x + 2 = 7$ → subtract 2 from both sides |
| Multiply | Multiply both sides by same | $\frac{x}{3} = 4$ → multiply both sides by 3 |
| Divide | Divide both sides by same | $3x = 12$ → divide both sides by 3 |

### 4.4 Inverse Operations

Each operation has an **inverse** (opposite) that "undoes" it.

| Operation | Inverse Operation |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Add $a$ | Subtract $a$ |
| Subtract $a$ | Add $a$ |
| Multiply by $a$ | Divide by $a$ |
| Divide by $a$ | Multiply by $a$ |

---

## 4.5 ONE-STEP EQUATIONS

A **one-step equation** requires only one operation to solve.

### Type 1: $x + a = b$ (Addition)

**Method:** Subtract $a$ from both sides.

#### Example 21:
**Solve:** $x + 7 = 12$

**Solution:**
$$x + 7 = 12$$
$$x + 7 - 7 = 12 - 7$$
$$x = 5$$

**Check:** $5 + 7 = 12$ ✓

---

### Type 2: $x - a = b$ (Subtraction)

**Method:** Add $a$ to both sides.

#### Example 22:
**Solve:** $x - 5 = 9$

**Solution:**
$$x - 5 = 9$$
$$x - 5 + 5 = 9 + 5$$
$$x = 14$$

**Check:** $14 - 5 = 9$ ✓

---

### Type 3: $ax = b$ (Multiplication)

**Method:** Divide both sides by $a$.

#### Example 23:
**Solve:** $4x = 20$

**Solution:**
$$4x = 20$$
$$\frac{4x}{4} = \frac{20}{4}$$
$$x = 5$$

**Check:** $4 \times 5 = 20$ ✓

---

### Type 4: $\frac{x}{a} = b$ (Division)

**Method:** Multiply both sides by $a$.

#### Example 24:
**Solve:** $\frac{x}{3} = 8$

**Solution:**
$$\frac{x}{3} = 8$$
$$\frac{x}{3} \times 3 = 8 \times 3$$
$$x = 24$$

**Check:** $\frac{24}{3} = 8$ ✓

---

### Type 5: Equations with Negative Numbers

#### Example 25:
**Solve:** $x + (-5) = 3$

**Solution:**
$$x + (-5) = 3$$
$$x - 5 = 3$$
$$x = 3 + 5$$
$$x = 8$$

**Check:** $8 + (-5) = 3$ ✓

---

#### Example 26:
**Solve:** $-3x = 15$

**Solution:**
$$-3x = 15$$
$$\frac{-3x}{-3} = \frac{15}{-3}$$
$$x = -5$$

**Check:** $-3 \times (-5) = 15$ ✓

---

## 4.6 TWO-STEP EQUATIONS

A **two-step equation** requires two operations to solve.

### Standard Form: $ax + b = c$

**Method:**
1. First, eliminate the constant term (add/subtract)
2. Then, eliminate the coefficient (multiply/divide)

>  **TIP:** Work with the addition/subtraction FIRST, then multiplication/division.

---

#### Example 27: Basic Two-Step
**Solve:** $3x + 5 = 17$

**Solution:**

Step 1: Subtract 5 from both sides
$$3x + 5 - 5 = 17 - 5$$
$$3x = 12$$

Step 2: Divide both sides by 3
$$\frac{3x}{3} = \frac{12}{3}$$
$$x = 4$$

**Check:** $3(4) + 5 = 12 + 5 = 17$ ✓

---

#### Example 28: With Subtraction
**Solve:** $2x - 7 = 13$

**Solution:**

Step 1: Add 7 to both sides
$$2x - 7 + 7 = 13 + 7$$
$$2x = 20$$

Step 2: Divide both sides by 2
$$\frac{2x}{2} = \frac{20}{2}$$
$$x = 10$$

**Check:** $2(10) - 7 = 20 - 7 = 13$ ✓

---

#### Example 29: With Negative Coefficient
**Solve:** $-4x + 3 = 19$

**Solution:**

Step 1: Subtract 3 from both sides
$$-4x + 3 - 3 = 19 - 3$$
$$-4x = 16$$

Step 2: Divide both sides by -4
$$\frac{-4x}{-4} = \frac{16}{-4}$$
$$x = -4$$

**Check:** $-4(-4) + 3 = 16 + 3 = 19$ ✓

---

#### Example 30: Variable on the Right
**Solve:** $14 = 3x + 2$

**Solution:**

Step 1: Subtract 2 from both sides
$$14 - 2 = 3x + 2 - 2$$
$$12 = 3x$$

Step 2: Divide both sides by 3
$$\frac{12}{3} = \frac{3x}{3}$$
$$4 = x$$

Therefore: $x = 4$

**Check:** $14 = 3(4) + 2 = 12 + 2 = 14$ ✓

---

#### Example 31: With Fractions
**Solve:** $\frac{x}{4} + 2 = 7$

**Solution:**

Step 1: Subtract 2 from both sides
$$\frac{x}{4} + 2 - 2 = 7 - 2$$
$$\frac{x}{4} = 5$$

Step 2: Multiply both sides by 4
$$\frac{x}{4} \times 4 = 5 \times 4$$
$$x = 20$$

**Check:** $\frac{20}{4} + 2 = 5 + 2 = 7$ ✓

---

#### Example 32: With Coefficient as Fraction
**Solve:** $\frac{2x}{3} = 8$

**Solution:**

Step 1: Multiply both sides by 3
$$2x = 24$$

Step 2: Divide both sides by 2
$$x = 12$$

**Check:** $\frac{2(12)}{3} = \frac{24}{3} = 8$ ✓

---

### 4.7 Equations with Brackets

**Method:** Expand the brackets first, then solve as a two-step equation.

#### Example 33:
**Solve:** $3(x + 4) = 27$

**Method 1: Expand first**
$$3x + 12 = 27$$
$$3x = 27 - 12$$
$$3x = 15$$
$$x = 5$$

**Method 2: Divide first**
$$\frac{3(x + 4)}{3} = \frac{27}{3}$$
$$x + 4 = 9$$
$$x = 5$$

Both methods give $x = 5$ ✓

---

#### Example 34:
**Solve:** $5(2x - 3) = 25$

**Solution:**

Step 1: Expand
$$10x - 15 = 25$$

Step 2: Add 15 to both sides
$$10x = 40$$

Step 3: Divide by 10
$$x = 4$$

**Check:** $5(2(4) - 3) = 5(8 - 3) = 5(5) = 25$ ✓

---

### 4.8 Equations with Unknown on Both Sides

**Method:**
1. Move all terms with $x$ to one side
2. Move all constant terms to the other side
3. Solve

#### Example 35:
**Solve:** $4x + 3 = 2x + 11$

**Solution:**

Step 1: Subtract $2x$ from both sides
$$4x - 2x + 3 = 2x - 2x + 11$$
$$2x + 3 = 11$$

Step 2: Subtract 3 from both sides
$$2x = 8$$

Step 3: Divide by 2
$$x = 4$$

**Check:** $4(4) + 3 = 19$ and $2(4) + 11 = 19$ ✓

---

#### Example 36:
**Solve:** $5x - 2 = 3x + 8$

**Solution:**

Step 1: Subtract $3x$ from both sides
$$5x - 3x - 2 = 8$$
$$2x - 2 = 8$$

Step 2: Add 2 to both sides
$$2x = 10$$

Step 3: Divide by 2
$$x = 5$$

**Check:** $5(5) - 2 = 23$ and $3(5) + 8 = 23$ ✓

---

### 4.9 Word Problems Leading to Equations

#### Example 37:
**I think of a number. I add 7 to it and multiply the result by 3. The answer is 36. Find the number.**

**Solution:**
Let the number be $x$.

$$3(x + 7) = 36$$

Expand:
$$3x + 21 = 36$$

Subtract 21:
$$3x = 15$$

Divide by 3:
$$x = 5$$

**The number is 5.**

**Check:** $5 + 7 = 12$, $12 \times 3 = 36$ ✓

---

#### Example 38:
**A rectangle has a perimeter of 28 cm. The length is 2 cm more than the width. Find the dimensions.**

**Solution:**
Let the width be $x$ cm.
Then the length is $(x + 2)$ cm.

Perimeter formula: $P = 2(\text{length} + \text{width})$

$$2(x + x + 2) = 28$$
$$2(2x + 2) = 28$$
$$4x + 4 = 28$$
$$4x = 24$$
$$x = 6$$

**Width = 6 cm, Length = 8 cm**

**Check:** $P = 2(8 + 6) = 2(14) = 28$ cm ✓

### Command Words Guide

| Command Word | What It Means | How to Answer |
|--------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Simplify** | Write in simplest form | Collect like terms, remove brackets |
| **Expand** | Remove brackets | Multiply out, DON'T collect like terms unless asked |
| **Expand and simplify** | Remove brackets then simplify | Do both operations |
| **Solve** | Find the value of the variable | Show all working, check your answer |
| **Calculate** | Work out the numerical value | Use BODMAS |
| **Find** | Determine a value | Show method clearly |
| **Show that** | Prove the given result | Every step must be shown |
| **Factorise** | Put back into brackets | Opposite of expanding |

---

### How to Gain Full Marks

#### For Simplifying:
1. ✅ Identify all like terms clearly
2. ✅ Show the collection of each type of term
3. ✅ Write the final answer clearly

#### For Expanding:
1. ✅ Show each multiplication separately
2. ✅ Check signs carefully
3. ✅ For "expand and simplify", show collecting like terms

#### For Solving Equations:
1. ✅ Show EVERY step on a new line
2. ✅ Use the balance method (same operation on both sides)
3. ✅ Check your answer by substitution
4. ✅ Write the final answer clearly: $x = ...$

---

### Common Exam Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correct Approach |
|------------|---------------------|
| $3x + 5 = 8x$ | Cannot add $3x$ and $5$ - they're not like terms |
| $x^2 + x = 2x$ | Cannot combine $x^2$ and $x$ - different powers |
| $-2(x - 3) = -2x - 6$ | Check signs: $-2 \times (-3) = +6$ |
| $3(x + 4) = 3x + 4$ | Must multiply ALL terms inside |
| Forgetting to check | Always verify by substitution |

---

### Time-Saving Tips for A* Students

1. **Mental maths:** Practise quick mental calculations for coefficients
2. **Sign awareness:** Always check the sign of each term
3. **Organisation:** Write each step clearly on a new line
4. **Check answers:** A quick substitution check saves marks
5. **Look for shortcuts:** In $3(x + 2) = 21$, divide by 3 first (not expand)
