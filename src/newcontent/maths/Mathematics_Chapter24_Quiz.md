### Question 1

The graph of $y = x^2$ is:

A) A straight line  
B) A parabola opening upwards  
C) A parabola opening downwards  
D) A circle  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The graph of $y = x^2$ is a parabola. Since the coefficient of $x^2$ is positive ($a = 1 > 0$), the parabola opens upwards (U-shaped).

**Key Point:** $a > 0$ means the parabola opens upwards.

---

### Question 2

What is the y-intercept of the graph $y = x^2 - 3x + 5$?

A) $(5, 0)$  
B) $(0, 5)$  
C) $(0, -3)$  
D) $(0, 2)$  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The y-intercept occurs when $x = 0$. For a quadratic $y = ax^2 + bx + c$, the y-intercept is always at $(0, c)$.

Here, $c = 5$, so the y-intercept is $(0, 5)$.

**Key Point:** y-intercept = $(0, c)$ where $c$ is the constant term.

---

### Question 3

Which of the following parabolas opens downwards?

A) $y = x^2 + 2x$  
B) $y = 3x^2 - 1$  
C) $y = -x^2 + 4x - 3$  
D) $y = 0.5x^2$  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** A parabola opens downwards when the coefficient of $x^2$ (the value of $a$) is negative.

- $y = x^2 + 2x$: $a = 1 > 0$ → opens up
- $y = 3x^2 - 1$: $a = 3 > 0$ → opens up
- $y = -x^2 + 4x - 3$: $a = -1 < 0$ → **opens down** ✓
- $y = 0.5x^2$: $a = 0.5 > 0$ → opens up

**Key Point:** Check the sign of $a$. Negative $a$ means opens downwards.

---

### Question 4

The x-coordinate of the vertex of $y = x^2 - 6x + 4$ is:

A) 3  
B) -3  
C) 6  
D) -6  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** The x-coordinate of the vertex is given by $x = -\frac{b}{2a}$.

Here, $a = 1$ and $b = -6$.

$$x = -\frac{-6}{2(1)} = \frac{6}{2} = 3$$

**Key Point:** Vertex x-coordinate = $-\frac{b}{2a}$

### Question 5

Which parabola is narrower than $y = x^2$?

A) $y = 0.5x^2$  
B) $y = -0.5x^2$  
C) $y = 2x^2$  
D) $y = \frac{1}{3}x^2$  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** A parabola is narrower than $y = x^2$ when $|a| > 1$.

- $y = 0.5x^2$: $|a| = 0.5 < 1$ → wider
- $y = -0.5x^2$: $|a| = 0.5 < 1$ → wider
- $y = 2x^2$: $|a| = 2 > 1$ → **narrower** ✓
- $y = \frac{1}{3}x^2$: $|a| = \frac{1}{3} < 1$ → wider

**Key Point:** $|a| > 1$ gives a narrower parabola.

### Question 6

The axis of symmetry of $y = x^2 - 8x + 7$ is:

A) $x = 4$  
B) $x = -4$  
C) $x = 8$  
D) $x = 7$  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** The axis of symmetry is $x = -\frac{b}{2a}$.

Here, $a = 1$ and $b = -8$.

$$x = -\frac{-8}{2(1)} = \frac{8}{2} = 4$$

**Key Point:** Axis of symmetry is always $x = -\frac{b}{2a}$

### Question 7

If the discriminant $b^2 - 4ac = 0$, how many x-intercepts does the parabola have?

A) 0  
B) 1  
C) 2  
D) 3  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** 
- If $b^2 - 4ac > 0$: Two distinct x-intercepts
- If $b^2 - 4ac = 0$: **One** x-intercept (the vertex touches the x-axis)
- If $b^2 - 4ac < 0$: No x-intercepts

**Key Point:** Discriminant = 0 means the parabola touches the x-axis at exactly one point.

### Question 8

What is the value of $y$ when $x = -2$ in the equation $y = x^2 - 3x + 1$?

A) -3  
B) 11  
C) 3  
D) -1  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** 
$$y = (-2)^2 - 3(-2) + 1$$
$$y = 4 + 6 + 1$$
$$y = 11$$

**Key Point:** Remember $(-2)^2 = 4$, not $-4$.

### Question 9

The solutions of $x^2 - 9 = 0$ are:

A) $x = 9$ only  
B) $x = -9$ only  
C) $x = 3$ or $x = -3$  
D) $x = 9$ or $x = -9$  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** 
$$x^2 - 9 = 0$$
$$x^2 = 9$$
$$x = \pm\sqrt{9}$$
$$x = 3 \text{ or } x = -3$$

**Key Point:** When solving $x^2 = k$ (where $k > 0$), there are always two solutions: $x = \pm\sqrt{k}$

### Question 10

Which graph has a maximum point?

A) $y = x^2 + 2x + 1$  
B) $y = 3x^2 - 5$  
C) $y = -x^2 + 6x - 5$  
D) $y = \frac{1}{2}x^2$  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** A parabola has a maximum point when it opens downwards, which occurs when $a < 0$.

- $y = x^2 + 2x + 1$: $a = 1 > 0$ → minimum
- $y = 3x^2 - 5$: $a = 3 > 0$ → minimum
- $y = -x^2 + 6x - 5$: $a = -1 < 0$ → **maximum** ✓
- $y = \frac{1}{2}x^2$: $a = 0.5 > 0$ → minimum

**Key Point:** $a < 0$ means maximum point; $a > 0$ means minimum point.

### Question 11

The graph of $y = (x - 2)^2$ is the graph of $y = x^2$ shifted:

A) Up by 2 units  
B) Down by 2 units  
C) Right by 2 units  
D) Left by 2 units  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** The graph of $y = (x - h)^2$ is $y = x^2$ shifted **right** by $h$ units.

For $y = (x - 2)^2$, the vertex is at $(2, 0)$, so the graph is shifted right by 2 units.

**Key Point:** $y = (x - h)^2$ shifts right by $h$ units; $y = (x + h)^2$ shifts left by $h$ units.

### Question 12

When solving $x^2 - 5x + 4 = 2$ graphically, what line should be drawn?

A) $y = 0$  
B) $y = 2$  
C) $x = 0$  
D) $x = 2$  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** To solve $x^2 - 5x + 4 = 2$ graphically:

1. Draw $y = x^2 - 5x + 4$
2. Draw the horizontal line $y = 2$
3. Find where they intersect

The x-coordinates of the intersection points are the solutions.

**Key Point:** To solve $ax^2 + bx + c = k$, draw the line $y = k$.

### Question 13

The vertex of $y = x^2 + 4x + 7$ is:

A) $(2, 3)$  
B) $(-2, 3)$  
C) $(2, 11)$  
D) $(-2, 11)$  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** 
$$x = -\frac{b}{2a} = -\frac{4}{2(1)} = -2$$

Substitute $x = -2$:
$$y = (-2)^2 + 4(-2) + 7 = 4 - 8 + 7 = 3$$

Vertex: $(-2, 3)$

**Key Point:** Always calculate both coordinates of the vertex.

### Question 14

For the equation $y = 2x^2 - 8x + 6$, the y-intercept is:

A) $(0, 6)$  
B) $(0, 2)$  
C) $(0, -8)$  
D) $(6, 0)$  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** The y-intercept occurs when $x = 0$.

$$y = 2(0)^2 - 8(0) + 6 = 0 - 0 + 6 = 6$$

y-intercept: $(0, 6)$

Alternatively, for $y = ax^2 + bx + c$, the y-intercept is $(0, c)$. Here $c = 6$.

**Key Point:** y-intercept is always $(0, c)$.

### Question 15

Which statement about $y = -3x^2$ is correct?

A) The graph opens upwards  
B) The graph is wider than $y = x^2$  
C) The vertex is at $(0, 0)$  
D) The graph has no vertex  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** 
- $a = -3 < 0$ → opens **downwards** (not up)
- $|a| = 3 > 1$ → **narrower** (not wider)
- The vertex is at $(0, 0)$ ✓
- Every parabola has a vertex

**Key Point:** $y = ax^2$ always has vertex at $(0, 0)$.

## SECTION B: True/False Questions

**Instructions:** State whether each statement is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correction.

---

### Question 16

The graph of $y = ax^2 + bx + c$ is always a parabola.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** The graph of any quadratic function $y = ax^2 + bx + c$ (where $a \neq 0$) is always a parabola.

---

### Question 17

If $a > 0$ in $y = ax^2 + bx + c$, the parabola has a maximum point.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** If $a > 0$, the parabola opens upwards and has a **minimum** point. A maximum point occurs when $a < 0$.

---

### Question 18

The y-intercept of $y = 2x^2 - 5x + 3$ is at $(0, 3)$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** The y-intercept occurs when $x = 0$. For $y = ax^2 + bx + c$, the y-intercept is $(0, c)$. Here $c = 3$, so the y-intercept is $(0, 3)$.

---

### Question 19

The axis of symmetry of a parabola is always a horizontal line.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The axis of symmetry of a parabola $y = ax^2 + bx + c$ is always a **vertical line** with equation $x = -\frac{b}{2a}$.

---

### Question 20

When drawing a quadratic graph, points should be joined with straight lines.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Points on a quadratic graph should be joined with a **smooth curve**, not straight lines. Parabolas are smooth curves.

---

### Question 21

The x-intercepts of a parabola are also called the roots of the quadratic equation.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** The x-intercepts are where $y = 0$, which means $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$. The solutions to this equation are the roots, and they correspond to the x-coordinates of the x-intercepts.

---

### Question 22

If the discriminant is negative, the parabola crosses the x-axis twice.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** If the discriminant $b^2 - 4ac < 0$ (negative), the parabola **does not cross the x-axis** at all (no real roots).

---

### Question 23

The value $(-3)^2 = -9$.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** $(-3)^2 = (-3) \times (-3) = 9$. When you square a negative number, the result is always **positive**.

---

### Question 24

The parabola $y = 2x^2$ is narrower than $y = x^2$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** When $|a| > 1$, the parabola is narrower. Since $|2| = 2 > 1$, $y = 2x^2$ is narrower than $y = x^2$.

---

### Question 25

To find the vertex of $y = x^2 - 4x + 1$, first calculate $x = -\frac{-4}{2} = 2$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** The x-coordinate of the vertex is $x = -\frac{b}{2a}$. Here $b = -4$ and $a = 1$, so:
$$x = -\frac{-4}{2(1)} = \frac{4}{2} = 2$$

---

### Question 26

The graph of $y = x^2 + 3$ is the graph of $y = x^2$ shifted left by 3 units.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The graph of $y = x^2 + 3$ is the graph of $y = x^2$ shifted **upwards** by 3 units. Adding a positive constant shifts the graph up.

---

### Question 27

Every parabola has exactly one vertex.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** A parabola has exactly one turning point, which is called the vertex. It is either a maximum or minimum point.

---

### Question 28

If $a = 0$ in $y = ax^2 + bx + c$, the graph is still a parabola.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** If $a = 0$, the equation becomes $y = bx + c$, which is a **straight line**, not a parabola. For a quadratic, we require $a \neq 0$.

---

### Question 29

To solve $x^2 - 4 = 0$ graphically, find where $y = x^2 - 4$ crosses the x-axis.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** The solutions to $x^2 - 4 = 0$ are the x-values where the graph of $y = x^2 - 4$ crosses the x-axis (where $y = 0$).

---

### Question 30

The vertex of $y = -x^2 + 6x$ is a maximum point.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Since $a = -1 < 0$, the parabola opens downwards, so the vertex is a maximum point.

## 📌 Frequently Tested Question Types

### 1. Drawing Quadratic Graphs
- Complete a table of values
- Plot points accurately
- Draw smooth curve through points

### 2. Finding Key Features
- y-intercept from equation
- x-intercepts from graph or equation
- Vertex from graph or formula
- Axis of symmetry

### 3. Solving Equations Graphically
- $ax^2 + bx + c = 0$ (x-intercepts)
- $ax^2 + bx + c = k$ (intersection with horizontal line)
- Comparing two graphs

### 4. Maximum/Minimum Problems
- Identify from sign of $a$
- Calculate vertex coordinates
- State maximum/minimum value

### 5. Describing Transformations
- Effect of changing $a$ (width, direction)
- Effect of changing $c$ (vertical shift)
- Effect of changing $b$ (horizontal position)

## 📌 Typical Problem-Solving Patterns

### Pattern 1: "Draw the graph of $y = ax^2 + bx + c$"
1. Complete table of values (5-7 points minimum)
2. Calculate each y-value carefully (watch negatives!)
3. Plot all points accurately
4. Draw smooth curve through points
5. Label the graph

### Pattern 2: "Find the vertex"
1. Use formula $x = -\frac{b}{2a}$
2. Substitute x-value to find y
3. Write as ordered pair $(x, y)$
4. State if maximum or minimum (check sign of $a$)

### Pattern 3: "Solve graphically"
1. Identify what line to draw ($y = 0$, $y = k$, or another function)
2. Find intersection points
3. Read off x-coordinates
4. State answer with appropriate accuracy

### Pattern 4: "Show that the curve has no x-intercepts"
1. Calculate discriminant $b^2 - 4ac$
2. Show it's negative
3. Conclude: no real roots, therefore no x-intercepts

## 📌 How to Gain Full Marks

### For Table Completion:
- ✅ Show working for each calculation
- ✅ Check arithmetic (especially negative numbers)
- ✅ Verify pattern in results (symmetry about vertex)

### For Drawing Graphs:
- ✅ Plot all points accurately
- ✅ Use small, clear dots
- ✅ Draw smooth curve (no straight lines!)
- ✅ Label axes
- ✅ Extend curve beyond outer points if appropriate

### For Finding Vertex:
- ✅ Show use of formula $x = -\frac{b}{2a}$
- ✅ Show substitution to find y
- ✅ Give answer as coordinates $(x, y)$

### For Graphical Solutions:
- ✅ Draw line clearly (use ruler for straight lines)
- ✅ Show reading with dotted lines to axes
- ✅ Give x-values to required accuracy
- ✅ State both solutions if there are two

### For Discriminant Questions:
- ✅ Write formula $b^2 - 4ac$
- ✅ Substitute values correctly
- ✅ Calculate result
- ✅ Interpret (positive = 2 roots, zero = 1 root, negative = no roots)

## 📌 Common Mistakes to Avoid

| ❌ Mistake | ✅ Correct Approach |
|------------|---------------------|
| $(-x)^2 = -x^2$ | $(-x)^2 = x^2$ (squaring negative gives positive) |
| Joining points with straight lines | Use smooth curve |
| Forgetting units or labels | Label axes, include units |
| Only finding x-coordinate of vertex | Calculate both x and y |
| Wrong direction of parabola | Check sign of $a$ |
| Reading wrong values from graph | Check scale carefully |
| Not checking discriminant | Use $b^2 - 4ac$ to check for roots |

** Key Takeaways:**
- Quadratic graphs are always parabolas

- Vertex formula: $x = -\frac{b}{2a}$
- y-intercept = $(0, c)$

- Discriminant tells you about x-intercepts
