### Question 1 [1 mark]

In a right-angled triangle, the two shorter sides are 6 cm and 8 cm. What is the length of the hypotenuse?

A) 10 cm  
B) 12 cm  
C) 14 cm  
D) $\sqrt{28}$ cm

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$c^2 = 6^2 + 8^2 = 36 + 64 = 100$$
$$c = \sqrt{100} = 10 \text{ cm}$$

This is the 6-8-10 Pythagorean triple (multiple of 3-4-5).

### Question 15 [1 mark]

**Statement:** The angle in a semicircle is always 90°.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This is a fundamental circle theorem. Any angle subtended by a diameter at the circumference is 90°.

---

### Question 16 [1 mark]

**Statement:** Opposite angles in any quadrilateral sum to 180°.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Opposite angles in a **cyclic quadrilateral** (one inscribed in a circle) sum to 180°. This is not true for all quadrilaterals.

---

### Question 17 [1 mark]

**Statement:** A tangent to a circle is perpendicular to the radius at the point of contact.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This is a fundamental property of tangents and circles.

---

### Question 18 [1 mark]

**Statement:** Two tangents drawn from an external point to a circle are equal in length.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This can be proven using congruent triangles (both right-angled, share hypotenuse, radii equal).

---

### Question 19 [1 mark]

**Statement:** The angle at the circumference is twice the angle at the center when both are subtended by the same arc.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The angle at the **center** is **twice** the angle at the **circumference** (not the other way around).

### Question 21 [1 mark]

**Statement:** Angles in the same segment of a circle are supplementary.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Angles in the same segment are **equal** (not supplementary). Opposite angles in a cyclic quadrilateral are supplementary.

### Question 22 [1 mark]

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This is a fundamental chord property and creates a right-angled triangle for problem-solving.

## Question 29 [6 marks]

Prove that the angle in a semicircle is a right angle.

---

### Mark Scheme

| Mark | Description |
|------|-------------|
| M1 | Draws diagram showing triangle inscribed in semicircle |
| M1 | States that the angle at center for a semicircle is 180° |
| M1 | References angle at center theorem: angle at center = 2 × angle at circumference |
| A1 | Shows that angle at circumference = 180° ÷ 2 = 90° |
| R1 | Clear conclusion |
| C1 | Communication mark for clear, logical presentation |

**Solution:**

**Given:** A triangle $ABC$ inscribed in a circle with $AC$ as diameter.

**To prove:** $\angle ABC = 90°$

**Construction:** Join $B$ to the center $O$.

**Proof:**

In triangle $AOB$:
- $OA = OB$ (radii)
- $\therefore \angle OAB = \angle OBA = x$

In triangle $BOC$:
- $OB = OC$ (radii)
- $\therefore \angle OCB = \angle OBC = y$

In triangle $ABC$:
$$\angle ABC = \angle OBA + \angle OBC = x + y$$

Since angles in a triangle sum to 180°:
$$\angle BAC + \angle ACB + \angle ABC = 180°$$
$$x + y + (x + y) = 180°$$
$$2x + 2y = 180°$$
$$x + y = 90°$$

Therefore:
$$\angle ABC = x + y = 90°$$

**Q.E.D.**
