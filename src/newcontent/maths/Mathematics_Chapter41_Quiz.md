### Question 1 [1 mark]

In triangle $PQR$, $\angle P = 38°$ and $\angle Q = 74°$. What is the size of $\angle R$?

A) $68°$  
B) $112°$  
C) $58°$  
D) $78°$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
Using the angle sum of a triangle:
$$\angle R = 180° - (38° + 74°)$$
$$\angle R = 180° - 112° = 68°$$

### Question 2 [1 mark]

In an isosceles triangle, the vertex angle is $40°$. What is the size of each base angle?

A) $60°$  
B) $70°$  
C) $80°$  
D) $140°$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
In an isosceles triangle, base angles are equal. Let each base angle = $x$:
$$40° + x + x = 180°$$
$$2x = 140°$$
$$x = 70°$$

### Question 3 [1 mark]

The exterior angle of a regular polygon is $36°$. How many sides does the polygon have?

A) 8  
B) 10  
C) 12  
D) 15

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$n = \frac{360°}{\text{exterior angle}} = \frac{360°}{36°} = 10$$

The polygon has 10 sides (a decagon).

### Question 4 [1 mark]

In a parallelogram, one angle is $65°$. What is the size of the adjacent angle?

A) $65°$  
B) $115°$  
C) $130°$  
D) $295°$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
Adjacent angles in a parallelogram are supplementary (sum to $180°$):
$$\text{Adjacent angle} = 180° - 65° = 115°$$

### Question 6 [1 mark]

Two similar triangles have a scale factor of $3:5$. If the area of the smaller triangle is $18$ cm², what is the area of the larger triangle?

A) $30$ cm²  
B) $50$ cm²  
C) $54$ cm²  
D) $90$ cm²

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{Area ratio} = (\text{length ratio})^2 = \left(\frac{5}{3}\right)^2 = \frac{25}{9}$$
$$\text{Larger area} = 18 \times \frac{25}{9} = 2 \times 25 = 50 \text{ cm}^2$$

### Question 7 [1 mark]

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{Exterior angle} = 180° - 156° = 24°$$
$$n = \frac{360°}{24°} = 15$$

### Question 8 [1 mark]

In triangle $ABC$, an exterior angle at $A$ measures $127°$. If $\angle B = 58°$, what is $\angle C$?

A) $69°$  
B) $58°$  
C) $127°$  
D) $69°$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
Using exterior angle theorem:
$$\text{Exterior angle} = \angle B + \angle C$$
$$127° = 58° + \angle C$$
$$\angle C = 127° - 58° = 69°$$

### Question 10 [1 mark]

In a kite $PQRS$ with $PQ = PS$ and $QR = SR$, which angles are equal?

A) $\angle P$ and $\angle R$  
B) $\angle Q$ and $\angle S$  
C) $\angle P$ and $\angle Q$  
D) $\angle R$ and $\angle S$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
In a kite with two pairs of adjacent equal sides, the angles between unequal sides are equal. Since $PQ = PS$ and $QR = SR$, the equal angles are $\angle P$ and $\angle R$.

### Question 11 [1 mark]

A hexagon has five angles: $115°, 130°, 110°, 140°, 95°$. What is the sixth angle?

A) $90°$  
B) $110°$  
C) $130°$  
D) $150°$

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{Sum of interior angles} = (6-2) \times 180° = 720°$$
$$\text{Sixth angle} = 720° - (115° + 130° + 110° + 140° + 95°)$$
$$= 720° - 590° = 130°$$

### Question 12 [1 mark]

Two similar triangles have corresponding sides in the ratio $4:7$. If the larger triangle has perimeter $42$ cm, what is the perimeter of the smaller triangle?

A) $16$ cm  
B) $24$ cm  
C) $28$ cm  
D) $36$ cm

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{Scale factor} = \frac{4}{7}$$
$$\text{Smaller perimeter} = 42 \times \frac{4}{7} = 6 \times 4 = 24 \text{ cm}$$

### Question 13 [1 mark]

**Statement:** The sum of the exterior angles of any polygon is $(n-2) \times 180°$.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The sum of the exterior angles of any polygon is always $360°$, regardless of the number of sides. The formula $(n-2) \times 180°$ gives the sum of interior angles.

---

### Question 14 [1 mark]

**Statement:** In a parallelogram, the diagonals are perpendicular to each other.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** In a parallelogram, diagonals bisect each other but are NOT necessarily perpendicular. Diagonals are perpendicular only in a rhombus or square.

---

### Question 15 [1 mark]

**Statement:** If two triangles have the same three angles, they are similar.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This is the AAA similarity condition. Triangles with equal corresponding angles are similar (AAA criterion).

---

### Question 16 [1 mark]

**Statement:** The interior angle of a regular hexagon is $120°$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{Interior angle} = \frac{(6-2) \times 180°}{6} = \frac{720°}{6} = 120°$$

---

### Question 17 [1 mark]

**Statement:** When constructing a perpendicular bisector, the compass radius must be exactly half the length of the line segment.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The compass radius must be MORE than half the length of the line segment. If it's exactly half, the arcs won't intersect.

---

### Question 18 [1 mark]

**Statement:** In similar shapes, if the scale factor is $k$, then the area ratio is $k^2$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Area is a 2-dimensional quantity, so the scale factor is squared when comparing areas of similar shapes.

---

### Question 19 [1 mark]

**Statement:** A trapezium has two pairs of parallel sides.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A trapezium has exactly ONE pair of parallel sides. Two pairs of parallel sides defines a parallelogram.

---

### Question 20 [1 mark]

**Statement:** The exterior angle of a triangle equals the sum of the two interior opposite angles.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This is the exterior angle theorem. The exterior angle equals the sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles.

---

### Question 21 [1 mark]

**Statement:** All rectangles are parallelograms.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** A rectangle has all the properties of a parallelogram (opposite sides parallel and equal, opposite angles equal, diagonals bisect each other) plus the additional property that all angles are $90°$.

---

### Question 22 [1 mark]

**Statement:** To construct a $60°$ angle, you need to construct an equilateral triangle.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** The standard construction for a $60°$ angle uses the fact that all angles in an equilateral triangle are $60°$. You draw arcs with equal radii to create the equilateral triangle.

In triangle $ABC$, $AB = AC$ (isosceles). The exterior angle at $A$ is $110°$.

**(a)** Find the size of $\angle BAC$. [1 mark]

**(b)** Find the sizes of $\angle ABC$ and $\angle ACB$. [3 marks]

**(a)** Finding $\angle BAC$ [1 mark]

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| $\angle BAC + 110° = 180°$ | M1 | Using angles on straight line |
| $\angle BAC = 70°$ | A1 | Correct answer |

**(b)** Finding base angles [3 marks]

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| Since $AB = AC$, base angles are equal | M1 | Identifying isosceles property |
| Let $\angle ABC = \angle ACB = x$ | M1 | Setting up equation |
| $70° + x + x = 180°$ | M1 | Using angle sum |
| $2x = 110°$ | | |
| $x = 55°$ | A1 | Correct answer |

**Answer:** $\angle BAC = 70°$, $\angle ABC = \angle ACB = 55°$

### Question 30 [6 marks]

A vertical pole of height $3$ m casts a shadow of length $4$ m. At the same time, a nearby tree casts a shadow of $20$ m.

**(a)** Use similar triangles to find the height of the tree. [3 marks]

**(b)** If the angle of elevation of the sun is $\theta$, find $\theta$ to the nearest degree. [3 marks]

**(a)** Finding tree height [3 marks]

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| Let tree height = $h$ | | |
| Triangles formed are similar (same sun angle) | M1 | Recognizing similarity |
| $\frac{h}{3} = \frac{20}{4} = 5$ | M1 | Setting up proportion |
| $h = 3 \times 5 = 15$ m | A1 | Correct answer |

**(b)** Finding angle of elevation [3 marks]

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| $\tan \theta = \frac{\text{height}}{\text{shadow}} = \frac{3}{4}$ | M1 | Using tangent ratio |
| $\tan \theta = 0.75$ | M1 | Correct substitution |
| $\theta = \tan^{-1}(0.75) = 36.87° \approx 37°$ | A1 | Correct to nearest degree |

## Typical Problem-Solving Patterns

### Pattern 1: Angle Chasing
1. Identify the shape (triangle/quadrilateral/polygon)
2. List all known angles
3. Apply appropriate angle sum formula
4. Work systematically to find unknown angles

### Pattern 2: Similar Triangles
1. Identify corresponding sides/angles
2. Calculate or state the scale factor
3. Apply scale factor to find missing lengths
4. For area: square the scale factor

### Pattern 3: Polygon Problems
1. Determine if regular or irregular
2. Apply appropriate formula
3. Check answer makes sense (interior < 180° for convex)
