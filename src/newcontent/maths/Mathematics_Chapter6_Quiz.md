## Question 1

If $y$ is directly proportional to $x$ and when $x = 5$, $y = 15$, what is the value of $y$ when $x = 8$?

A) 20  
B) 24  
C) 40  
D) 12

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
- $y \propto x \Rightarrow y = kx$
- $15 = k \times 5 \Rightarrow k = 3$
- $y = 3x$
- When $x = 8$: $y = 3 \times 8 = 24$

---

## Question 2

If $y$ is inversely proportional to $x$ and when $x = 4$, $y = 12$, what is the value of $y$ when $x = 6$?

A) 8  
B) 18  
C) 6  
D) 9

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
- $y \propto \frac{1}{x} \Rightarrow y = \frac{k}{x}$
- $k = xy = 4 \times 12 = 48$
- $y = \frac{48}{x}$
- When $x = 6$: $y = \frac{48}{6} = 8$

---

## Question 3

Evaluate $16^{-\frac{1}{2}}$.

A) $-4$  
B) $\frac{1}{4}$  
C) $4$  
D) $-\frac{1}{4}$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
- $16^{-\frac{1}{2}} = \frac{1}{16^{\frac{1}{2}}}$
- $16^{\frac{1}{2}} = \sqrt{16} = 4$
- Therefore: $\frac{1}{4}$

---

## Question 4

What is the value of $27^{\frac{2}{3}}$?

A) 6  
B) 9  
C) 18  
D) 3

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
- $27^{\frac{2}{3}} = (\sqrt[3]{27})^2$
- $\sqrt[3]{27} = 3$ (since $3^3 = 27$)
- $3^2 = 9$

## Question 5

Which graph represents direct proportion?

A) A curve passing through $(0,0)$  
B) A straight line passing through $(0,0)$  
C) A straight line with $y$-intercept $(0, 2)$  
D) A curve with asymptotes at $x = 0$ and $y = 0$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
- Direct proportion gives $y = kx$
- This is a straight line through the origin
- The $y$-intercept must be $0$

## Question 6

Evaluate $\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{-2}$.

A) $\frac{4}{9}$  
B) $-\frac{4}{9}$  
C) $\frac{9}{4}$  
D) $-\frac{9}{4}$

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**
- $\left(\frac{2}{3}\right)^{-2} = \left(\frac{3}{2}\right)^2$
- $= \frac{9}{4}$

## Question 7

If $y \propto x^2$ and when $x = 3$, $y = 18$, what is $k$?

A) 2  
B) 6  
C) 9  
D) 54

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
- $y = kx^2$
- $18 = k \times 3^2 = k \times 9$
- $k = \frac{18}{9} = 2$

## Question 8

The time $T$ to complete a task is inversely proportional to the number of workers $n$. If 4 workers take 6 hours, how long would 8 workers take?

A) 12 hours  
B) 4 hours  
C) 3 hours  
D) 2 hours

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**
- $T \propto \frac{1}{n} \Rightarrow T = \frac{k}{n}$
- $k = Tn = 6 \times 4 = 24$
- When $n = 8$: $T = \frac{24}{8} = 3$ hours

Simplify $a^3 \times a^{-5}$.

A) $a^{-2}$  
B) $a^{-15}$  
C) $a^8$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
- $a^3 \times a^{-5} = a^{3+(-5)} = a^{-2}$

## Question 10

What is $64^{-\frac{2}{3}}$?

A) $\frac{1}{16}$  
B) $\frac{1}{8}$  
C) $-16$  
D) $\frac{1}{4}$

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
- First find $64^{\frac{2}{3}}$
- $64^{\frac{1}{3}} = 4$ (since $4^3 = 64$)
- $64^{\frac{2}{3}} = 4^2 = 16$
- $64^{-\frac{2}{3}} = \frac{1}{16}$

## Question 11

For inverse proportion $y = \frac{k}{x}$, which statement is TRUE?

A) The graph is a straight line  
B) $xy$ is constant  
C) $\frac{y}{x}$ is constant  
D) The graph passes through $(0, k)$

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
- For inverse proportion: $y = \frac{k}{x}$
- Rearranging: $xy = k$ (constant)
- The graph is a curve (hyperbola), not a straight line
- $\frac{y}{x}$ is NOT constant for inverse proportion

D) 81

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
- $9^{\frac{3}{2}} = (\sqrt{9})^3$
- $\sqrt{9} = 3$
- $3^3 = 27$

# Section B: True or False

**Instructions:** State whether each statement is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.

---

## Question 1

**Statement:** If $y$ is directly proportional to $x$, then the graph of $y$ against $x$ is a straight line that passes through the origin.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

## Question 2

**Statement:** For direct proportion, the product $xy$ is always constant.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** For direct proportion, the **ratio** $\frac{y}{x}$ is constant. The product $xy$ is constant for **inverse** proportion.

---

## Question 3

**Statement:** $4^{-\frac{1}{2}} = -2$

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** $4^{-\frac{1}{2}} = \frac{1}{4^{\frac{1}{2}}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{4}} = \frac{1}{2}$

A negative index means reciprocal, not a negative number.

---

## Question 4

**Statement:** The graph of $y = \frac{k}{x}$ (for $k > 0$) is a curve that never touches either axis.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

## Question 5

**Statement:** $\left(\frac{3}{5}\right)^{-1} = -\frac{3}{5}$

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** $\left(\frac{3}{5}\right)^{-1} = \frac{5}{3}$

A negative index on a fraction means we take the reciprocal (flip the fraction).

---

## Question 6

**Statement:** $27^{\frac{1}{3}} = 9$

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** $27^{\frac{1}{3}} = \sqrt[3]{27} = 3$

The cube root of 27 is 3 (since $3^3 = 27$), not 9.

---

## Question 7

**Statement:** In inverse proportion, when one quantity doubles, the other halves.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

## Question 8

**Statement:** $a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}$ for all values of $a$.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** $a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}$ for all values of $a$ **except** $a = 0$ (division by zero is undefined).

---

## Question 9

**Statement:** When evaluating $a^{\frac{m}{n}}$, you should calculate the power ($m$) before taking the root ($n$).

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** When evaluating $a^{\frac{m}{n}}$, you should take the **root first** (denominator $n$), then raise to the **power** (numerator $m$).

Memory aid: **Denominator = Down = root**

---

## Question 10

**Statement:** For the equation $y = kx$, the gradient of the graph equals $k$.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

## Question 11

**Statement:** If $y \propto \frac{1}{x^2}$, then $x^2 y = k$ is constant.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

## Question 12

**Statement:** $16^{\frac{2}{4}} = 16^{\frac{1}{2}} = 4$

**Answer: TRUE**

**Note:** $\frac{2}{4} = \frac{1}{2}$, so $16^{\frac{2}{4}} = 16^{\frac{1}{2}} = \sqrt{16} = 4$

## Question 1 [3 marks]

$y$ is directly proportional to $x$. When $x = 12$, $y = 30$.

(a) Find the equation connecting $y$ and $x$. [2 marks]

(b) Find the value of $x$ when $y = 45$. [1 mark]

### Mark Scheme Solution

**(a) Finding the equation [2 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| Write proportionality | $y \propto x$ | M1 |
| Form equation | $y = kx$ | |
| Substitute values | $30 = k \times 12$ | M1 |
| Find $k$ | $k = \frac{30}{12} = 2.5$ | A1 |
| Final equation | $y = 2.5x$ | A1 |

**Answer:** $y = 2.5x$ or $y = \frac{5x}{2}$

**(b) Finding $x$ when $y = 45$ [1 mark]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| Substitute $y = 45$ | $45 = 2.5x$ | M1 |
| Solve for $x$ | $x = \frac{45}{2.5} = 18$ | A1 |

**Answer:** $x = 18$

The time taken to travel a fixed distance is inversely proportional to the speed.

(a) When the speed is 40 km/h, the time is 3 hours. Find the time when the speed is 60 km/h. [3 marks]

(b) Explain what happens to the time if the speed is doubled. [1 mark]

### Mark Scheme Solution

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| State relationship | $T \propto \frac{1}{S}$ | M1 |
| Form equation | $T = \frac{k}{S}$ | |
| Find constant | $k = TS = 3 \times 40 = 120$ | M1 |
| Write equation | $T = \frac{120}{S}$ | |
| Find time for 60 km/h | $T = \frac{120}{60} = 2$ hours | A1 |

**Answer:** 2 hours

**(b) [1 mark]**

| Explanation | Mark |
|-------------|------|
| If speed is doubled, time is halved | A1 |
| (Inverse proportion: as one doubles, other halves) | |

**Answer:** The time is halved.

## Question 3 [5 marks]

Evaluate without using a calculator. Show all your working.

(a) $32^{\frac{3}{5}}$ [2 marks]

(b) $125^{-\frac{2}{3}}$ [3 marks]

### Mark Scheme Solution

**(a) $32^{\frac{3}{5}}$ [2 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |

**(b) $125^{-\frac{2}{3}}$ [3 marks]**

| Find cube root | $\sqrt[3]{125} = 5$ (since $5^3 = 125$) | M1 |
| Square the result | $5^2 = 25$ | M1 |
| Final answer | $\frac{1}{25}$ | A1 |

**Answer:** $\frac{1}{25}$

## Question 4 [6 marks]

$y$ is inversely proportional to $x^2$. When $x = 2$, $y = 5$.

(a) Find the equation connecting $y$ and $x$. [2 marks]

(b) Find the value of $y$ when $x = 4$. [2 marks]

(c) Find the value of $x$ when $y = 20$. [2 marks]

### Mark Scheme Solution

**(a) Finding the equation [2 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| State proportionality | $y \propto \frac{1}{x^2}$ | M1 |
| Form equation | $y = \frac{k}{x^2}$ | |
| Substitute values | $5 = \frac{k}{2^2} = \frac{k}{4}$ | M1 |
| Find $k$ | $k = 20$ | A1 |
| Final equation | $y = \frac{20}{x^2}$ | A1 |

**Answer:** $y = \frac{20}{x^2}$

**(b) Finding $y$ when $x = 4$ [2 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| Substitute $x = 4$ | $y = \frac{20}{4^2} = \frac{20}{16}$ | M1 |
| Simplify | $y = \frac{5}{4} = 1.25$ | A1 |

**Answer:** $y = \frac{5}{4}$ or $1.25$

**(c) Finding $x$ when $y = 20$ [2 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| Substitute $y = 20$ | $20 = \frac{20}{x^2}$ | M1 |
| Multiply by $x^2$ | $20x^2 = 20$ | |
| Divide by 20 | $x^2 = 1$ | |
| Take square root | $x = 1$ (positive value) | A1 |

**Answer:** $x = 1$

## Question 5 [4 marks]

Simplify fully: $\frac{a^{\frac{1}{2}} \times a^{-\frac{1}{4}}}{a^{\frac{3}{4}}}$

### Mark Scheme Solution

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| Multiply numerator | $a^{\frac{1}{2}} \times a^{-\frac{1}{4}} = a^{\frac{1}{2} + (-\frac{1}{4})}$ | M1 |
| Add indices | $= a^{\frac{2}{4} - \frac{1}{4}} = a^{\frac{1}{4}}$ | M1 |
| Divide by denominator | $a^{\frac{1}{4}} \div a^{\frac{3}{4}} = a^{\frac{1}{4} - \frac{3}{4}}$ | M1 |
| Subtract indices | $= a^{-\frac{2}{4}} = a^{-\frac{1}{2}}$ | A1 |
| Alternative form | $= \frac{1}{\sqrt{a}}$ | |

**Answer:** $a^{-\frac{1}{2}}$ or $\frac{1}{\sqrt{a}}$

## Question 7 [6 marks]

(a) Evaluate $64^{\frac{1}{2}} \times 64^{\frac{1}{3}}$. Give your answer as a power of 2. [3 marks]

(b) Show that $4^{-\frac{3}{2}} = \frac{1}{8}$. [3 marks]

### Mark Scheme Solution

**(a) [3 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| Use index law | $64^{\frac{1}{2}} \times 64^{\frac{1}{3}} = 64^{\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{3}}$ | M1 |
| Add fractions | $= 64^{\frac{3}{6} + \frac{2}{6}} = 64^{\frac{5}{6}}$ | M1 |
| Express 64 as power of 2 | $64 = 2^6$ | |
| Substitute | $(2^6)^{\frac{5}{6}} = 2^{6 \times \frac{5}{6}} = 2^5$ | A1 |

**Answer:** $2^5$

**(b) [3 marks]**

| Step | Working | Mark |
|------|---------|------|
| Convert negative index | $4^{-\frac{3}{2}} = \frac{1}{4^{\frac{3}{2}}}$ | M1 |
| Evaluate $4^{\frac{3}{2}}$ | $\sqrt{4} = 2$, then $2^3 = 8$ | M1 |
| So $4^{\frac{3}{2}} = 8$ | | |
| Final answer | $\frac{1}{4^{\frac{3}{2}}} = \frac{1}{8}$ | A1 |

**QED**

# Section D: Exam Focus & Strategy

## Frequently Tested Question Types

### Type 1: Finding the Equation
**Pattern:** Given one pair of values, find the equation.

**Strategy:**
1. Identify direct or inverse proportion
2. Write $y = kx$ or $y = \frac{k}{x}$
3. Substitute to find $k$
4. State final equation

**Example:** "$y$ is directly proportional to $x$. When $x = 6$, $y = 24$. Find the equation."

---

### Type 2: Finding Unknown Values
**Pattern:** Given equation and one value, find the other.

**Strategy:**
1. Substitute the known value
2. Solve for the unknown
3. Check answer makes sense

---

### Type 3: Graph-Based Questions
**Pattern:** Use graph to find $k$ or interpret proportionality.

**Strategy:**
1. Read a point from the graph
2. Calculate $k$ using the coordinates
3. Use $k$ for further calculations

---

### Type 4: Real-World Context
**Pattern:** Word problems involving proportion.

**Strategy:**
1. Identify the variables
2. Determine proportion type
3. Find $k$ using given information
4. Answer the question asked

---

### Type 5: Evaluating Indices
**Pattern:** Calculate values with fractional/negative indices.

**Strategy:**
1. For $a^{\frac{m}{n}}$: root first, then power
2. For $a^{-n}$: take reciprocal
3. Show clear working steps

---

## Command Word Guidance

| Command Word | What to Do |
|--------------|------------|
| **Find** | Calculate and give a numerical answer |
| **Show that** | Work must lead to the given result |
| **Simplify** | Reduce to simplest form |
| **Evaluate** | Calculate the value |
| **Sketch** | Draw the main features, don't plot precisely |
| **Explain** | Give a reason using mathematical terms |

---

## How to Gain Full Marks

### For Proportion Questions (4+ marks)
1. ✅ Write the proportionality statement
2. ✅ Show the equation with $k$
3. ✅ Show substitution to find $k$
4. ✅ State the value of $k$
5. ✅ Write the final equation
6. ✅ Show substitution for finding unknowns
7. ✅ Give final answer with units if applicable

### For Index Questions (2-3 marks)
1. ✅ Show the conversion (negative → reciprocal)
2. ✅ Show intermediate steps
3. ✅ Give final answer in requested form

### For Graph Questions
1. ✅ Label axes clearly
2. ✅ Show key points (origin, given point)
3. ✅ Draw correct shape (straight line or curve)

## Mark Scheme Tips

| Tip | Explanation |
|-----|-------------|
| **M1 (Method)** | Awarded for correct method, even with calculation error |
| **A1 (Accuracy)** | Awarded for correct answer |
| **B1 (Independent)** | Awarded independently of other marks |
| **Follow through** | Marks for correct working from earlier error |

### Common Mark Allocation Patterns

**3-mark question:**
- M1: Correct method/setup
- M1: Correct substitution/calculation
- A1: Correct final answer

**4-mark question:**
- M1: Statement of proportionality
- M1: Finding $k$
- A1: Correct equation
- A1: Correct answer to question
