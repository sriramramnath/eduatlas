### Question 1

On a distance-time graph, what does a horizontal line represent?

A) The object is moving at constant speed  
B) The object is accelerating  
C) The object is stationary  
D) The object is returning to the start  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** A horizontal line on a distance-time graph means the distance is not changing over time. This indicates the object is stationary (not moving).

**Key Point:** Horizontal line = zero gradient = zero speed = stationary.

---

### Question 2

A distance-time graph shows a straight line from (0, 0) to (10, 80). What is the speed?

A) 8 m/s  
B) 80 m/s  
C) 0.125 m/s  
D) 800 m/s  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** 
$$\text{Speed} = \frac{\text{change in distance}}{\text{change in time}} = \frac{80 - 0}{10 - 0} = \frac{80}{10} = 8 \text{ m/s}$$

**Key Point:** Speed = gradient of distance-time graph = rise ÷ run.

---

### Question 3

On a speed-time graph, the gradient represents:

A) Distance  
B) Speed  
C) Acceleration  
D) Time  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** On a speed-time graph, the vertical axis is speed and the horizontal axis is time. Therefore:
$$\text{Gradient} = \frac{\text{change in speed}}{\text{change in time}} = \text{acceleration}$$

**Key Point:** Speed-time gradient = acceleration.

---

### Question 4

Convert 72 km/h to m/s.

A) 20 m/s  
B) 259.2 m/s  
C) 7.2 m/s  
D) 0.72 m/s  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$72 \text{ km/h} = 72 \times \frac{5}{18} = 72 \div 3.6 = 20 \text{ m/s}$$

**Key Point:** To convert km/h to m/s, divide by 3.6 (or multiply by 5/18).

---

### Question 5

A car travels at an average speed of 60 km/h for 2 hours 30 minutes. How far does it travel?

A) 120 km  
B) 150 km  
C) 62.5 km  
D) 180 km  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{Time} = 2.5 \text{ hours}$$
$$\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} = 60 \times 2.5 = 150 \text{ km}$$

**Key Point:** Distance = Speed × Time. Convert time to hours first.

---

### Question 6

On a speed-time graph, what does the area under the graph represent?

A) Acceleration  
B) Speed  
C) Distance travelled  
D) Time  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** The area under a speed-time graph equals distance travelled because:
$$\text{Area} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} = \text{Distance}$$

**Key Point:** Area under speed-time graph = distance.

---

### Question 7

A car accelerates from 10 m/s to 30 m/s in 5 seconds. What is the acceleration?

A) 2 m/s²  
B) 4 m/s²  
C) 6 m/s²  
D) 8 m/s²  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$a = \frac{v - u}{t} = \frac{30 - 10}{5} = \frac{20}{5} = 4 \text{ m/s}^2$$

**Key Point:** Acceleration = (final speed − initial speed) ÷ time.

---

### Question 8

A speed-time graph shows a horizontal line at 25 m/s for 10 seconds. How far does the object travel in this time?

A) 250 m  
B) 2.5 m  
C) 25 m  
D) 10 m  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{Distance} = \text{Speed} \times \text{Time} = 25 \times 10 = 250 \text{ m}$$

Or, using area: $\text{Area} = 25 \times 10 = 250 \text{ m}$

**Key Point:** For constant speed, distance = speed × time = area of rectangle.

---

### Question 9

A person walks 2 km in 30 minutes, rests for 10 minutes, then walks 1 km in 20 minutes. What is the average speed for the whole journey?

A) 3 km/h  
B) 2 km/h  
C) 4 km/h  
D) 6 km/h  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{Total distance} = 2 + 1 = 3 \text{ km}$$
$$\text{Total time} = 30 + 10 + 20 = 60 \text{ minutes} = 1 \text{ hour}$$
$$\text{Average speed} = \frac{3}{1} = 3 \text{ km/h}$$

**Key Point:** Average speed = total distance ÷ total time (NOT average of individual speeds).

---

### Question 10

A speed-time graph shows a straight line sloping downwards. This indicates:

A) Constant speed  
B) Acceleration  
C) Deceleration  
D) Stationary  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** A line sloping downwards (negative gradient) on a speed-time graph means the speed is decreasing over time, which is deceleration.

**Key Point:** Negative gradient on speed-time graph = deceleration.

---

### Question 11

A train decelerates from 40 m/s to rest in 8 seconds. What is the deceleration?

A) 5 m/s²  
B) 320 m/s²  
C) 32 m/s²  
D) 0.2 m/s²  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$a = \frac{v - u}{t} = \frac{0 - 40}{8} = \frac{-40}{8} = -5 \text{ m/s}^2$$

The deceleration is **5 m/s²** (given as a positive value).

**Key Point:** Deceleration is stated as a positive value, though acceleration is negative.

---

### Question 12

Which of the following is NOT true about distance-time graphs?

A) A steeper line means greater speed  
B) The gradient represents speed  
C) A curved line means constant speed  
D) A horizontal line means the object is stationary  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** A curved line on a distance-time graph means the speed is **changing** (accelerating or decelerating), not constant. A straight line represents constant speed.

**Key Point:** Curved distance-time graph = changing speed.

---

### Question 13

Convert 15 m/s to km/h.

A) 5.4 km/h  
B) 54 km/h  
C) 4.17 km/h  
D) 150 km/h  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$15 \text{ m/s} = 15 \times 3.6 = 54 \text{ km/h}$$

**Key Point:** To convert m/s to km/h, multiply by 3.6.

---

### Question 14

A speed-time graph shows acceleration from rest to 20 m/s in 4 seconds. What distance is travelled during this acceleration?

A) 80 m  
B) 40 m  
C) 20 m  
D) 5 m  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{Area (triangle)} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height} = \frac{1}{2} \times 4 \times 20 = 40 \text{ m}$$

**Key Point:** Distance = area under speed-time graph. For acceleration from rest, it's a triangle.

---

### Question 15

A cyclist travelling at 12 m/s accelerates at 2 m/s² for 5 seconds. What is the final speed?

A) 22 m/s  
B) 10 m/s  
C) 14 m/s  
D) 60 m/s  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:**
$$v = u + at = 12 + (2 \times 5) = 12 + 10 = 22 \text{ m/s}$$

**Key Point:** Final speed = initial speed + (acceleration × time).

## SECTION B: True/False Questions

**Instructions:** State whether each statement is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correction.

---

### Question 16

On a distance-time graph, the gradient represents acceleration.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** On a distance-time graph, the gradient represents **speed**.

---

### Question 17

A horizontal line on a speed-time graph means the object is stationary.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A horizontal line on a speed-time graph means the object is travelling at **constant speed**. (A horizontal line on a distance-time graph means stationary.)

---

### Question 18

The area under a speed-time graph gives the distance travelled.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Area = Speed × Time = Distance

---

### Question 19

To convert km/h to m/s, you multiply by 3.6.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** To convert km/h to m/s, you **divide by 3.6** (or multiply by 5/18). Multiplying by 3.6 converts m/s to km/h.

---

### Question 20

Average speed is calculated by dividing total distance by total time.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** $\text{Average speed} = \frac{\text{total distance}}{\text{total time}}$

---

### Question 21

A curved distance-time graph indicates that the object is moving at constant speed.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A curved distance-time graph indicates that the speed is **changing** (the object is accelerating or decelerating). A straight line indicates constant speed.

---

### Question 22

Deceleration is always stated as a positive value in exam answers.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** In exams, deceleration is given as a positive value (e.g., "deceleration of 3 m/s²" even though acceleration is -3 m/s²).

---

### Question 23

On a speed-time graph, a steeper positive gradient means greater acceleration.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Gradient = acceleration, so steeper gradient = greater acceleration.

---

### Question 24

The formula v = u + at can be used to find final speed when acceleration is constant.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This is one of the key equations for uniform acceleration: $v = u + at$

---

### Question 25

On a distance-time graph, a negative gradient means the object is moving backwards (towards the starting point).

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** A negative gradient means distance from start is decreasing, so the object is returning towards the starting point.

---

### Question 26

When finding average speed, you should average all the individual speeds.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Average speed = total distance ÷ total time. You should **NOT** average individual speeds.

---

### Question 27

To find instantaneous speed from a curved distance-time graph, you draw a tangent and find its gradient.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** The gradient of the tangent to the curve at a point gives the instantaneous speed at that moment.

---

### Question 28

The units of acceleration are m/s.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The units of acceleration are **m/s²** (metres per second squared).

---

### Question 29

If a car travels 100 km in 2 hours, its average speed is 50 km/h.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** $\text{Average speed} = \frac{100}{2} = 50 \text{ km/h}$

---

### Question 30

A speed-time graph with a line from (0, 10) to (5, 30) shows deceleration.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The line has a positive gradient (speed increasing from 10 to 30 m/s), so it shows **acceleration**, not deceleration.

The distance-time graph below shows a cyclist's journey:

(c) Calculate the average speed for the whole journey. [1 mark]

**(a) Speed during section A [2 marks]**

| Mark | Response |

| A1 | 0.4 km/min or 24 km/h (cao with correct unit) |

**Question 32** [5 marks]

A car's speed-time graph shows:

- Section A: Accelerates from rest to 18 m/s in 6 seconds
- Section B: Travels at constant speed for 10 seconds  
- Section C: Decelerates to rest in 4 seconds

(a) Calculate the acceleration in Section A. [2 marks]

(b) Calculate the total distance travelled. [2 marks]

(c) Calculate the average speed for the whole journey. [1 mark]

**(a) Acceleration in Section A [2 marks]**

| Mark | Response |
|------|----------|
| M1 | Correct method: $\frac{18 - 0}{6}$ or $\frac{18}{6}$ |
| A1 | 3 m/s² (cao with correct unit) |

**Answer:** $a = \frac{18}{6} = 3 \text{ m/s}^2$

---

**(b) Total distance [2 marks]**

| Mark | Response |
|------|----------|
| M1 | Method to find at least one area (triangle, rectangle, or triangle) or method for total |
| A1 | 234 m (cao) |

**Working:**
- Section A (triangle): $\frac{1}{2} \times 6 \times 18 = 54 \text{ m}$
- Section B (rectangle): $10 \times 18 = 180 \text{ m}$
- Section C (triangle): $\frac{1}{2} \times 4 \times 18 = 36 \text{ m}$
- Total: $54 + 180 + 36 = 270 \text{ m}$

**Correct Answer: 270 m**

---

**(c) Average speed [1 mark]**

| Mark | Response |
|------|----------|
| A1 | $\frac{270}{20} = 13.5 \text{ m/s}$ (ft from their distance and time) |

**Question 33** [6 marks]

A train travels from Station A to Station C, passing through Station B.

- From A to B: Travels 60 km at an average speed of 80 km/h
- Stops at B for 5 minutes
- From B to C: Travels 45 km at an average speed of 90 km/h

(a) Calculate the time taken to travel from A to B. [2 marks]

(b) Calculate the time taken to travel from B to C. [2 marks]

(c) Calculate the average speed for the whole journey from A to C. [2 marks]

**(a) Time from A to B [2 marks]**

| Mark | Response |
|------|----------|
| M1 | Correct method: $\frac{60}{80}$ or $60 \div 80$ |
| A1 | 0.75 hours or 45 minutes (cao with correct unit) |

**Working:**
$$t = \frac{d}{s} = \frac{60}{80} = 0.75 \text{ h} = 45 \text{ min}$$

---

**(b) Time from B to C [2 marks]**

| Mark | Response |
|------|----------|
| M1 | Correct method: $\frac{45}{90}$ or $45 \div 90$ |
| A1 | 0.5 hours or 30 minutes (cao with correct unit) |

**Working:**
$$t = \frac{d}{s} = \frac{45}{90} = 0.5 \text{ h} = 30 \text{ min}$$

---

**(c) Average speed A to C [2 marks]**

| Mark | Response |
|------|----------|
| M1 | Correct method: $\frac{60 + 45}{0.75 + \frac{5}{60} + 0.5}$ or $\frac{105}{\text{total time}}$ |
| A1 | 76.4 km/h (accept 76.36... or 76 km/h) |

**Working:**
$$\text{Total distance} = 60 + 45 = 105 \text{ km}$$
$$\text{Total time} = 45 + 5 + 30 = 80 \text{ min} = \frac{80}{60} \text{ h} = \frac{4}{3} \text{ h}$$
$$\text{Average speed} = \frac{105}{\frac{4}{3}} = 105 \times \frac{3}{4} = 78.75 \text{ km/h}$$

**Correct Answer: 78.75 km/h**

**Question 35** [6 marks]

A runner is training for a race. The distance-time graph for one training session shows:

- Runs 4 km in 20 minutes
- Rests for 10 minutes
- Runs a further 2 km in 15 minutes
- Rests for 5 minutes
- Runs back to the start, covering 6 km in 30 minutes

(a) Calculate the running speed during the first section. [2 marks]

(b) Convert your answer to part (a) to m/s. [1 mark]

(c) Calculate the average speed for the whole training session. [2 marks]

(d) Explain why the average speed is less than the average of the three running speeds. [1 mark]

**(a) Running speed (first section) [2 marks]**

| Mark | Response |
|------|----------|
| M1 | Correct method: $\frac{4}{20}$ or $4 \div \frac{1}{3}$ or $\frac{4 \text{ km}}{20 \text{ min}}$ |
| A1 | 0.2 km/min or 12 km/h (cao with unit) |

**Working:**
$$\text{Speed} = \frac{4}{20} = 0.2 \text{ km/min} = 0.2 \times 60 = 12 \text{ km/h}$$

---

**(b) Convert to m/s [1 mark]**

| Mark | Response |
|------|----------|
| A1 | $12 \times \frac{5}{18} = 3.33 \text{ m/s}$ or $12 \div 3.6 = 3.33 \text{ m/s}$ |

---

**(c) Average speed [2 marks]**

| Mark | Response |
|------|----------|
| M1 | $\frac{4 + 2 + 6}{20 + 10 + 15 + 5 + 30}$ or $\frac{12}{80}$ |
| A1 | 0.15 km/min or 9 km/h |

**Working:**
$$\text{Total distance} = 4 + 2 + 6 = 12 \text{ km}$$
$$\text{Total time} = 20 + 10 + 15 + 5 + 30 = 80 \text{ min} = \frac{4}{3} \text{ h}$$
$$\text{Average speed} = \frac{12}{\frac{4}{3}} = 12 \times \frac{3}{4} = 9 \text{ km/h}$$

---

**(d) Explanation [1 mark]**

| Mark | Response |
|------|----------|
| A1 | The average speed includes the rest periods when speed = 0, which lowers the overall average. OR The rest periods add time without adding distance. |

**Question 36** [7 marks]

A car accelerates uniformly from 8 m/s to 24 m/s over a distance of 256 m.

(a) Calculate the time taken for this acceleration. [3 marks]

(b) Calculate the acceleration. [2 marks]

(c) After reaching 24 m/s, the car continues at this speed for 30 seconds. Calculate the total distance travelled from the start. [2 marks]

**(a) Time taken [3 marks]**

| Mark | Response |
|------|----------|
| M1 | Uses $s = \frac{(u+v)t}{2}$ or finds average speed $\frac{8+24}{2} = 16$ |
| M1 | Rearranges: $t = \frac{2s}{u+v}$ or $\frac{256}{16}$ |
| A1 | 16 seconds (cao) |

**Working:**
$$\text{Average speed} = \frac{8 + 24}{2} = 16 \text{ m/s}$$
$$t = \frac{\text{distance}}{\text{average speed}} = \frac{256}{16} = 16 \text{ s}$$

---

**(b) Acceleration [2 marks]**

| Mark | Response |
|------|----------|
| M1 | $\frac{24 - 8}{16}$ or $\frac{16}{16}$ |
| A1 | 1 m/s² (cao with unit) |

**Working:**
$$a = \frac{v - u}{t} = \frac{24 - 8}{16} = \frac{16}{16} = 1 \text{ m/s}^2$$

---

**(c) Total distance [2 marks]**

| Mark | Response |
|------|----------|
| M1 | $256 + (24 \times 30)$ or method to find distance at constant speed |
| A1 | 976 m (cao, ft from their values) |

**Working:**
$$\text{Further distance} = 24 \times 30 = 720 \text{ m}$$
$$\text{Total distance} = 256 + 720 = 976 \text{ m}$$

## 📌 Frequently Tested Question Types

### 1. Distance-Time Graph Interpretation
- Find speed from gradient
- Describe motion in different sections
- Compare speeds
- Identify stationary periods

### 2. Speed-Time Graph Interpretation
- Find acceleration from gradient
- Calculate distance from area
- Identify constant speed sections
- Describe acceleration/deceleration

### 3. Multi-Stage Journey Problems
- Calculate average speed
- Find total distance/time
- Draw graphs from descriptions

### 4. Unit Conversion
- km/h to m/s and vice versa
- Minutes to hours

### 5. Word Problems
- Real-world applications (trains, cars, runners)
- Find unknown speed, distance, or time

---

## 📌 Typical Problem-Solving Patterns

### Pattern 1: "Find the speed from the distance-time graph"
1. Read off two points on the line
2. Calculate gradient = rise ÷ run = Δd ÷ Δt
3. Include units

### Pattern 2: "Find the distance from the speed-time graph"
1. Identify shapes under the graph
2. Use area formulas: triangle, rectangle, trapezium
3. Add all areas together
4. Include units (metres)

### Pattern 3: "Calculate average speed"
1. Find total distance
2. Find total time (include rest periods!)
3. Divide: total distance ÷ total time
4. NOT average of individual speeds

### Pattern 4: "Describe the motion"
1. Identify each section
2. State what happens (stationary, constant speed, accelerating, decelerating)
3. Use correct terminology

## 📌 Command Word Guidance

| Command Word | What Examiners Expect |
|--------------|----------------------|
| **Calculate** | Show working, give numerical answer with units |
| **Find** | Same as calculate – show full working |
| **Describe** | Give a written explanation of what happens |
| **Explain** | Give reasons why something happens |
| **Compare** | State similarities and/or differences |
| **Determine** | Work out with clear logical steps |
| **Sketch** | Draw showing key features (not exact plot) |

## 📌 How to Gain Full Marks

### Always:
- ✅ Show all working clearly
- ✅ Include units in final answers
- ✅ Check scale on graph axes
- ✅ Label your answers (a), (b), etc.
- ✅ Use correct terminology

### For Graph Questions:
- ✅ Quote coordinates when calculating gradients
- ✅ State the formula you're using
- ✅ Show substitution and calculation
- ✅ Break complex areas into simple shapes

### For Word Problems:
- ✅ Write down what you know
- ✅ Show the formula
- ✅ Check unit conversions
- ✅ Check answer is sensible

### Common Mistakes to Avoid:
- ❌ Averaging speeds for average speed
- ❌ Forgetting to include rest periods
- ❌ Missing units
- ❌ Confusing distance-time and speed-time graphs
- ❌ Reading wrong values from axes
