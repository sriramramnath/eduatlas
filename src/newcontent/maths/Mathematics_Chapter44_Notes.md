# Topic: Sine and Cosine Ratios

## 1. Introduction to Trigonometric Ratios

### 1.1 What are Trigonometric Ratios?

Trigonometric ratios are relationships between the **sides and angles** of a **right-angled triangle**. The three primary ratios are:

| Ratio | Abbreviation | Definition |
|-------|--------------|------------|
| **Sine** | sin | $\frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$ |
| **Cosine** | cos | $\frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$ |
| **Tangent** | tan | $\frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Adjacent}}$ |

> **Note:** This unit focuses specifically on **sine and cosine ratios**.

---

### 1.2 Labelling a Right-Angled Triangle

Before applying trigonometric ratios, you must correctly label the triangle:

```
                    B
                   /|\
                  / | \
                 /  |  \
        Hypotenuse   |   Opposite
               /    |    \
              /     |     \
             /      |      \
            A-------+--------C
               Adjacent
```

**Key Points:**
- The **hypotenuse** is always the longest side (opposite the right angle)
- **Opposite** is the side directly across from the angle you're working with
- **Adjacent** is the side next to (touching) the angle you're working with

>  **Common Mistake:** The labels "opposite" and "adjacent" change depending on which angle you're using. Always identify YOUR angle first!

---

### 1.3 The SOHCAHTOA Mnemonic

This is an essential memory aid for trigonometric ratios:

| Letter | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| **S** | $\sin \theta = \frac{\textbf{O}pposite}{\textbf{H}ypotenuse}$ |
| **C** | $\cos \theta = \frac{\textbf{A}dajacent}{\textbf{H}ypotenuse}$ |
| **T** | $\tan \theta = \frac{\textbf{O}pposite}{\textbf{A}dajacent}$ |

**Memory trick:** "Some Old Hags Can't Always Hide Their Old Age"

## 2. The Sine Ratio

### 2.1 Definition

$$\sin \theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$$

The sine ratio relates the **angle** to the **opposite side** and the **hypotenuse**.

### 2.2 When to Use Sine

Use the sine ratio when you have:
- **Finding a side:** You know the angle and either the opposite or hypotenuse
- **Finding an angle:** You know both the opposite and hypotenuse

### 2.3 Worked Examples - Finding a Side Using Sine

---

**Example 1: Finding the Opposite Side**

Find the length of side $x$ in the triangle below:

```
              A
             /|
            / |
           /  | x
          /   |
         /30° |
        B-----C
         12 cm
```

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Label the sides relative to the 30° angle
- Hypotenuse = 12 cm (opposite the right angle)
- Opposite = $x$ (opposite to the 30° angle)

**Step 2:** Choose the correct ratio
- We have: Hypotenuse, need Opposite
- Use: $\sin \theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$

**Step 3:** Substitute values
$$\sin 30° = \frac{x}{12}$$

**Step 4:** Solve for $x$
$$x = 12 \times \sin 30°$$

**Step 5:** Calculate
$$x = 12 \times 0.5 = 6 \text{ cm}$$

✅ **Answer:** $x = 6 \text{ cm}$

---

**Example 2: Finding the Hypotenuse**

Find the length of side $y$:

```
              A
             /|
            / |
           /  | 8 cm
          /   |
         /45° |
        B-----C
           y
```

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Label the sides relative to the 45° angle
- Opposite = 8 cm
- Hypotenuse = $y$

**Step 2:** Choose the correct ratio
$$\sin \theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$$

**Step 3:** Substitute values
$$\sin 45° = \frac{8}{y}$$

**Step 4:** Rearrange (multiply both sides by $y$, then divide by $\sin 45°$)
$$y = \frac{8}{\sin 45°}$$

**Step 5:** Calculate
$$y = \frac{8}{0.707...} = 11.3 \text{ cm (to 1 d.p.)}$$

✅ **Answer:** $y = 11.3 \text{ cm (to 1 d.p.)}$

---

### 2.4 Worked Examples - Finding an Angle Using Sine

---

**Example 3: Finding an Angle**

Find angle $\theta$ in the triangle:

```
              A
             /|
            /θ|
           /  | 7 cm
          /   |
         /    |
        B-----C
         10 cm
```

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Label the sides
- Opposite = 7 cm
- Hypotenuse = 10 cm

**Step 2:** Choose the ratio and substitute
$$\sin \theta = \frac{7}{10} = 0.7$$

**Step 3:** Use the inverse sine function
$$\theta = \sin^{-1}(0.7)$$

**Step 4:** Calculate
$$\theta = 44.4° \text{ (to 1 d.p.)}$$

✅ **Answer:** $\theta = 44.4°$ (to 1 d.p.)

## 4. Choosing Between Sine and Cosine

### 4.1 Decision Flowchart

```
                     START
                       |
                       ▼
              Identify known sides
                       |
                       ▼
              Is the HYPOTENUSE known?
                    /        \
                  YES         NO
                   |           |
                   ▼           ▼
           Which side is   Use Pythagoras
           the other?      (if right angle)
              /    \
         OPPOSITE  ADJACENT
            |          |
            ▼          ▼
         Use SINE   Use COSINE
```

### 4.2 Quick Selection Guide

| Given Information | Finding | Use |
|-------------------|---------|-----|
| Angle + Hypotenuse | Opposite | **sin** |
| Angle + Opposite | Hypotenuse | **sin** |
| Angle + Hypotenuse | Adjacent | **cos** |
| Angle + Adjacent | Hypotenuse | **cos** |
| Opposite + Hypotenuse | Angle | **sin⁻¹** |
| Adjacent + Hypotenuse | Angle | **cos⁻¹** |

## 6. Multi-Step Problems

### 6.1 Combining Trigonometry with Other Topics

**Example 7: Problem Involving Area**

A triangle ABC has AB = 12 cm, AC = 10 cm and angle BAC = 40°. Find the area of the triangle.

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Draw a perpendicular from C to AB

```
        A
       /|\
      / | \
  10 /  |  \ 
    /   |h  \
   /40° |    \
  B-----+-----C
      12 cm
```

**Step 2:** Find height $h$ using sine
$$\sin 40° = \frac{h}{10}$$
$$h = 10 \times \sin 40° = 6.428 \text{ cm}$$

**Step 3:** Calculate area
$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times \text{base} \times \text{height}$$
$$\text{Area} = \frac{1}{2} \times 12 \times 6.428 = 38.6 \text{ cm}^2 \text{ (to 1 d.p.)}$$

✅ **Answer:** Area = 38.6 cm² (to 1 d.p.)

---

**Example 8: Problem with Bearings**

A ship sails from port A for 15 km on a bearing of 060° to point B. How far east of A is the ship?

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Draw a diagram

```
           N
           |
           |
           A-----> East
          /|
         / |
        /  | x (East distance)
   15km/   |
      /    |
     /60°  |
    B------+
```

**Step 2:** Identify the angle from North
- Bearing of 060° means 60° clockwise from North
- The angle from the east direction is: 90° - 60° = 30°... No wait, let me reconsider.

Actually, for bearing 060°:
- The angle from North going clockwise is 60°
- The angle between AB and the East direction is: 60° - 0° = 60°? No...

Let me reconsider the geometry:
- Bearing 060° means the direction is 60° clockwise from North
- The angle between the path and the eastward direction is 60°

So if we drop a perpendicular eastward from B:

```
           N
           |
           A-----> East
          /|
         / |
    15km/  | x (distance east)
       /   |
      /60° |
     B-----+
```

**Step 2:** Using the diagram correctly
- The angle at A between AB and the East direction = 60°
- AB = 15 km (hypotenuse of the right triangle)
- We need the adjacent side (east distance)

**Step 3:** Use cosine
$$\cos 60° = \frac{x}{15}$$
$$x = 15 \times \cos 60° = 15 \times 0.5 = 7.5 \text{ km}$$

✅ **Answer:** The ship is 7.5 km east of A.

**Example 9: 3D Problem (Ladder Problem)**

A 6m ladder leans against a vertical wall. The ladder makes an angle of 70° with the ground. How far up the wall does the ladder reach?

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Draw a diagram

```
    Wall
      |
      |
      |  height (x)
      |
      +-------+
     /|
    / |
   /  |
  /70°|
 +----+
Ground (d)
```

**Step 2:** Identify what we need
- Ladder = hypotenuse = 6 m
- We need the height (opposite to 70°)

**Step 3:** Use sine
$$\sin 70° = \frac{x}{6}$$
$$x = 6 \times \sin 70°$$
$$x = 6 \times 0.9397 = 5.64 \text{ m}$$

✅ **Answer:** The ladder reaches 5.64 m up the wall (to 2 d.p.)

## 7. Problem-Solving Strategies

### 7.1 Standard Approach for Any Trigonometry Problem

1. **Draw a diagram** - Always sketch the situation
2. **Mark the right angle** - Essential for identifying the hypotenuse
3. **Label all sides** - Relative to the angle you're using
4. **Mark what you know** - Given values
5. **Mark what you need** - Unknown value
6. **Choose your ratio** - Use SOHCAHTOA
7. **Write the equation** - Substitute values
8. **Solve** - Rearrange if necessary
9. **Calculate** - Use your calculator
10. **Check** - Does the answer make sense?

### 7.2 Checking Your Answer

**Methods to verify:**
- **Sense check:** Is a side longer than the hypotenuse? It shouldn't be!
- **Magnitude check:** sin and cos values are between -1 and 1
- **Recalculate:** Work backwards to check

## 8. Common Mistakes and How to Avoid Them

### 8.1 Calculator Errors

| Mistake | How to Avoid |
|---------|--------------|
| Wrong mode (radians instead of degrees) | Always check calculator shows "D" or "DEG" |
| Forgetting brackets | Use brackets: $\sin(30)$ not $\sin 30$ when there's more to the calculation |
| Inverse function confusion | $\sin^{-1}$ finds the angle, $\sin$ uses the angle |

>  **Critical Check:** Make sure your calculator is in **DEGREE mode** before any trigonometry calculation!

### 8.2 Labelling Errors

| Mistake | How to Avoid |
|---------|--------------|
| Wrong "opposite" side | The opposite is ALWAYS opposite to the angle you're using |
| Mixing up adjacent and opposite | Adjacent TOUCHES the angle, opposite does NOT |
| Forgetting the hypotenuse | It's always opposite the right angle (the longest side) |

### 8.3 Algebraic Errors

| Mistake | Example | Correction |
|---------|---------|------------|
| Multiplying instead of dividing | $\sin 30° = \frac{x}{5} \rightarrow x = 5 \times \sin 30°$ ✓ | This is correct! |
| Wrong rearrangement | $\sin 30° = \frac{5}{x} \rightarrow x = 5 \times \sin 30°$ ✗ | Should be $x = \frac{5}{\sin 30°}$ |

## 9. Exam-Style Questions

### 9.1 Basic Application (2-3 marks)

**Question:** Find the length marked $x$:

```
        A
       /|
      / |
     /  | x
    /   |
   /50° |
  B-----C
   8 cm
```

**Solution:**
$$\sin 50° = \frac{x}{8}$$
$$x = 8 \times \sin 50° = 8 \times 0.766 = 6.13 \text{ cm (to 2 d.p.)}$$

---

### 9.2 Finding an Angle (2-3 marks)

**Question:** A ladder of length 5 m leans against a wall. The foot of the ladder is 2 m from the wall. Find the angle the ladder makes with the ground.

**Solution:**
$$\cos \theta = \frac{2}{5} = 0.4$$
$$\theta = \cos^{-1}(0.4) = 66.4° \text{ (to 1 d.p.)}$$

---

### 9.3 Multi-Step Problem (4-5 marks)

**Question:** In triangle PQR, angle Q = 90°, PQ = 8 cm, and angle P = 35°. Calculate:
(a) The length QR
(b) The length PR

**Solution:**

**(a)** To find QR (opposite to angle P):
$$\tan 35° = \frac{QR}{8}$$
$$QR = 8 \times \tan 35° = 8 \times 0.700 = 5.60 \text{ cm}$$

**(b)** To find PR (hypotenuse):
Method 1 - Using cosine:
$$\cos 35° = \frac{8}{PR}$$
$$PR = \frac{8}{\cos 35°} = \frac{8}{0.819} = 9.77 \text{ cm}$$

Method 2 - Using Pythagoras:
$$PR = \sqrt{8^2 + 5.60^2} = \sqrt{64 + 31.36} = \sqrt{95.36} = 9.77 \text{ cm}$$

## 10. Key Formula Summary

| Formula | When to Use |
|---------|-------------|
| $\sin \theta = \frac{O}{H}$ | Finding opposite or hypotenuse (or angle if O and H known) |
| $\cos \theta = \frac{A}{H}$ | Finding adjacent or hypotenuse (or angle if A and H known) |
| $\theta = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{O}{H}\right)$ | Finding angle when O and H are known |
| $\theta = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{A}{H}\right)$ | Finding angle when A and H are known |
