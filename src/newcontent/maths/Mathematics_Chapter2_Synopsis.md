## Concise Synopsis: Standard Form, Percentages, and Multipliers

---

# Quick Reference Card

## 1. Standard Form

### Definition
$$\text{Standard Form: } a \times 10^n$$
- $a$ is between **1 and 10** (i.e., $1 \leq a < 10$)
- $n$ is an **integer**

### Converting Rules

| Type | Rule | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| Large numbers → Standard form | Count places moved LEFT; positive power | $45,000 = 4.5 \times 10^4$ |
| Small numbers → Standard form | Count places moved RIGHT; negative power | $0.00047 = 4.7 \times 10^{-4}$ |
| Standard form → Ordinary | Positive power: move RIGHT | $3.7 \times 10^5 = 370,000$ |
| Standard form → Ordinary | Negative power: move LEFT | $5.2 \times 10^{-3} = 0.0052$ |

### Calculations

| Operation | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| Multiply | $(a \times 10^m) \times (b \times 10^n) = (a \times b) \times 10^{m+n}$ |
| Divide | $(a \times 10^m) \div (b \times 10^n) = \frac{a}{b} \times 10^{m-n}$ |
| Add/Subtract | **Must have same power** - convert first |

> **Quick Check:** Always verify $1 \leq a < 10$ in final answer!

## 2. Percentages - Core Conversions

| Fraction | Decimal | Percentage |
|:--------:|:-------:|:----------:|
| $\frac{1}{10}$ | 0.1 | 10% |
| $\frac{1}{5}$ | 0.2 | 20% |
| $\frac{1}{4}$ | 0.25 | 25% |
| $\frac{1}{3}$ | 0.333... | 33$\frac{1}{3}$% |
| $\frac{1}{2}$ | 0.5 | 50% |
| $\frac{3}{4}$ | 0.75 | 75% |

### Conversion Rules
- **% → Decimal:** ÷ 100 (e.g., 35% = 0.35)
- **Decimal → %:** × 100 (e.g., 0.075 = 7.5%)
- **% → Fraction:** Write over 100, simplify (e.g., 36% = $\frac{36}{100} = \frac{9}{25}$)

---

## 3. Finding a Percentage of a Quantity

### Formula
$$x\% \text{ of } y = \frac{x}{100} \times y$$

### Non-Calculator Building Blocks

| Find... | Method |
|---------|--------|
| 50% | ÷ 2 |
| 10% | ÷ 10 |
| 5% | Half of 10% |
| 1% | ÷ 100 |
| 25% | Half of 50% |
| 20% | Double 10% |

**Example:** 17% of £350
- 10% = £35
- 5% = £17.50
- 1% = £3.50
- 17% = £35 + £17.50 + £3.50 = **£56** (or build as 10% + 5% + 1% + 1%)

### Express as Percentage
$$\text{Percentage} = \frac{\text{part}}{\text{whole}} \times 100\%$$

## 4. Percentage Increase & Decrease

### Multipliers - Quick Reference

| Change | Multiplier Formula | Examples |
|--------|-------------------|----------|
| Increase by $x\%$ | $1 + \frac{x}{100}$ | +20% → 1.20, +5% → 1.05 |
| Decrease by $x\%$ | $1 - \frac{x}{100}$ | -20% → 0.80, -5% → 0.95 |

### Two Methods

**Method 1: Two-Step**
- Calculate the percentage of original
- Add (increase) or subtract (decrease)

**Method 2: Multiplier (Recommended)**
$$\text{New value} = \text{Original} \times \text{Multiplier}$$

### Percentage Change Formula
$$\text{Percentage Change} = \frac{\text{change}}{\text{original}} \times 100\%$$

> **Key Point:** Always divide by the ORIGINAL value!

---

## 5. Reverse Percentages

### Concept
Finding the **original** value when you know the value AFTER a percentage change.

### Method
1. Identify what percentage the new value represents
2. Divide by the corresponding multiplier

| After Change | New Value Represents | To Find Original |
|:------------:|:-------------------:|:----------------:|
| Increase by 20% | 120% of original | Divide by 1.20 |
| Decrease by 15% | 85% of original | Divide by 0.85 |
| Increase by 8% | 108% of original | Divide by 1.08 |

**Example:** Price after 25% increase is £75
$$\text{Original} = \frac{75}{1.25} = £60$$

> **Common Mistake:** Don't find 25% of £75 and subtract!

## 6. Compound Interest & Depreciation

### Formula
$$A = P\left(1 + \frac{r}{100}\right)^n$$

| Variable | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| $A$ | Final amount |
| $P$ | Principal (starting amount) |
| $r$ | Rate per period |
| $n$ | Number of periods |

### For Depreciation
$$A = P\left(1 - \frac{r}{100}\right)^n$$

**Example:** £5,000 at 4% compound interest for 3 years
$$A = 5000 \times 1.04^3 = 5000 \times 1.124864 = £5,624.32$$

## 7. Key Exam Reminders

✅ **Standard form:** $1 \leq a < 10$ always

✅ **Negative power** for small numbers (< 1)

✅ **Multiplier for increase** > 1 (e.g., 1.15 for +15%)

✅ **Multiplier for decrease** < 1 (e.g., 0.85 for -15%)

✅ **Reverse percentages:** DIVIDE by multiplier, don't add/subtract

✅ **Percentage change:** Always divide by ORIGINAL value

✅ **Compound interest:** Use powers for multiple periods

✅ **Show all working** - method marks available

## 8. Common Mistakes Checklist

| ❌ Wrong | ✅ Right |
|---------|---------|
| $23 \times 10^4$ (not standard form) | $2.3 \times 10^5$ |
| $0.005 = 5 \times 10^3$ | $5 \times 10^{-3}$ |
| Original = New + 20% | Original = New ÷ 1.20 |
| $\frac{\text{new} - \text{original}}{\text{new}}$ | $\frac{\text{new} - \text{original}}{\text{original}}$ |
| Simple interest for compound | $P(1+r)^n$ for compound |
| Adding 10% then 10% = +20% | $1.10^2 = 1.21$ (+21%) |

### Checking Your Answer
- **Increase:** New value should be LARGER than original
- **Decrease:** New value should be SMALLER than original
- **Reverse increase:** Original should be SMALLER than new
- **Reverse decrease:** Original should be LARGER than new
