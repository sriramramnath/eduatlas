### Question 1

Which type of data is "shoe size"?

- A) Qualitative
- B) Continuous
- C) Discrete
- D) Categorical

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Shoe size can only take specific values (e.g., 6, 6.5, 7, 7.5) with gaps between possible values. Although shoe sizes include half sizes, they are still discrete because you cannot have a shoe size of 6.73 for example. The values are countable and finite.

### Question 2

A school has 240 boys and 360 girls. A stratified sample of 50 students is to be taken. How many boys should be in the sample?

- A) 15
- B) 20
- C) 25
- D) 30

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
Using the stratified sampling formula:
$$\text{Number of boys} = \frac{240}{240 + 360} \times 50 = \frac{240}{600} \times 50 = 20$$

The total population is 600, and boys make up $\frac{240}{600} = \frac{2}{5}$ of the population, so $\frac{2}{5} \times 50 = 20$ boys.

### Question 3

The mean of five numbers is 12. Four of the numbers are 10, 15, 8, and 11. What is the fifth number?

- A) 12
- B) 14
- C) 16
- D) 18

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**
If the mean of 5 numbers is 12, then the total sum is $5 \times 12 = 60$.
Sum of known numbers: $10 + 15 + 8 + 11 = 44$
Fifth number = $60 - 44 = 16$

### Question 4

Which average is most affected by extreme outliers?

- A) Mean
- B) Median
- C) Mode
- D) Range

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** The mean uses all values in its calculation, so extreme values pull the mean towards them. For example, in the set {2, 3, 4, 5, 100}, the mean is 22.8, which is not representative of most values, while the median (4) remains unaffected by the outlier.

### Question 5

In a pie chart, a category with frequency 24 out of a total of 96 should have what angle?

- A) 45°
- B) 60°
- C) 90°
- D) 120°

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{Angle} = \frac{24}{96} \times 360° = \frac{1}{4} \times 360° = 90°$$

### Question 6

For a histogram with unequal class widths, what is plotted on the y-axis?

- A) Frequency
- B) Frequency density
- C) Cumulative frequency
- D) Class width

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** For histograms with unequal class widths, we must use frequency density on the y-axis so that the area of each bar (frequency density × class width) represents the frequency. This ensures visual comparisons are fair.

### Question 7

The numbers 3, 7, 7, 8, 12, 15 have:

- A) No mode
- B) One mode: 7
- C) Two modes: 7 and 8
- D) Mode = 8.7

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The mode is the most frequently occurring value. In this data set, 7 appears twice while all other values appear only once. Therefore, the mode is 7.

### Question 8

A cumulative frequency curve shows the 25th percentile at a value of 34 and the 75th percentile at a value of 58. What is the interquartile range?

- A) 12
- B) 24
- C) 34
- D) 58

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
$$\text{IQR} = Q_3 - Q_1 = 58 - 34 = 24$$

The interquartile range is the difference between the upper and lower quartiles.

### Question 9

Which of the following is an example of secondary data?

- A) Data collected from your own survey
- B) Data from an experiment you conducted
- C) Population figures from a government website
- D) Measurements you recorded in class

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Secondary data is data that has already been collected by someone else for a different purpose. Government statistics on a website were collected by the government, not by you, making this secondary data. All other options involve primary data collection.

### Question 10

What is the median of: 4, 9, 2, 7, 5, 8?

- A) 5
- B) 6
- C) 6.5
- D) 7

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
First, arrange in order: 2, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9

With 6 values (even number), the median is the average of the 3rd and 4th values.
$$\text{Median} = \frac{5 + 7}{2} = 6$$

### Question 11

A company claims "Average salary is £32,000" but most employees earn around £22,000. Which average might the company be using?

- A) Mode
- B) Median
- C) Mean
- D) Range

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** The mean is pulled upward by very high salaries (e.g., executives earning much more), giving a higher "average" than what most employees actually earn. This is a common way to present data misleadingly. The median would give a better representation of what a "typical" employee earns.

### Question 12

For grouped data with class intervals 10-20, 20-30, 30-40, what is the midpoint of the second class?

- A) 20
- B) 25
- C) 30
- D) 50

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:**
The midpoint is calculated as: $\frac{\text{lower bound} + \text{upper bound}}{2}$
$$\text{Midpoint} = \frac{20 + 30}{2} = 25$$

## SECTION B: TRUE OR FALSE

**Instructions:** State whether each statement is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correction.

---

### Question 1

**Statement:** In a histogram, there should always be gaps between the bars.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** In a histogram, there should be **no gaps** between the bars because histograms represent continuous data. Gaps are only used in bar charts for discrete or qualitative data.

---

### Question 2

**Statement:** The mean is always the best measure of average to use.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The mean is **not always the best** measure. When there are extreme outliers or the data is skewed, the **median** is more appropriate. For categorical data, only the **mode** can be used.

---

### Question 3

**Statement:** The median is the middle value when data is arranged in order.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

### Question 4

**Statement:** A stratified sample ensures that each subgroup in the population is represented proportionally.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

### Question 5

**Statement:** If a value increases from 20% to 25%, the percentage increase is 5%.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The percentage increase is **25%**, calculated as:
$$\frac{25 - 20}{20} \times 100\% = \frac{5}{20} \times 100\% = 25\%$$

The confusion is between "percentage points" (5 percentage points) and "percentage increase" (25%).

---

### Question 6

**Statement:** The mode can be used for both qualitative and quantitative data.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

### Question 7

**Statement:** For grouped data, the exact mean can be calculated using the class midpoints.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** For grouped data, we can only **estimate** the mean using class midpoints, because we don't know the actual values within each class interval. The calculation gives an **estimated mean**, not an exact value.

---

### Question 8

**Statement:** The range is not affected by outliers.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The range **is affected by outliers** because it is calculated using the maximum and minimum values. If either extreme is an outlier, the range will be distorted. The **interquartile range (IQR)** is not affected by outliers.

---

### Question 9

**Statement:** In a pie chart, all angles must add up to 360°.

**Answer: TRUE**

---

### Question 10

**Statement:** Primary data is always more reliable than secondary data.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Primary data is **not always more reliable**. While primary data can be collected specifically for your purpose, it may still have biases or errors in collection. Secondary data from reputable sources (like government statistics) may be more reliable than poorly collected primary data.

---

### Question 11

**Statement:** A truncated y-axis on a graph always makes differences appear smaller.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A truncated y-axis makes differences appear **larger** (exaggerated), not smaller. By cutting off the bottom portion of the axis, small differences are given more visual prominence.

---

### Question 12

**Statement:** The interquartile range represents the spread of the middle 50% of the data.

**Answer: TRUE**

### Question 8 [5 marks]

**A histogram is drawn to show the distribution of journey times. The class 0-10 minutes has frequency 20 and a bar of width 2cm and height 4cm. The class 20-40 minutes has frequency 30.**

**(a) Calculate the frequency density for the 0-10 minute class. [2 marks]**

**(a) [2 marks]**

**Compare the two data sets using appropriate statistical measures.**

**Data Set A:** 12, 15, 18, 19, 21, 23, 24, 26, 28

**Data Set B:** 8, 14, 17, 20, 20, 22, 24, 28, 32

## Problem-Solving Patterns

### Pattern 1: Missing Value Questions
**"The mean of n values is X. One value is missing..."**
- Calculate total: Total = n × mean
- Subtract known values to find missing one

### Pattern 2: Comparison Questions
**"Compare the two distributions..."**
- Calculate a measure of location (mean or median)
- Calculate a measure of spread (range or IQR)
- Write comparison using context

### Pattern 3: From Diagrams
**"Use the cumulative frequency curve to find..."**
- Median: read at n/2
- Q₁: read at n/4
- Q₃: read at 3n/4
- IQR: Q₃ - Q₁
