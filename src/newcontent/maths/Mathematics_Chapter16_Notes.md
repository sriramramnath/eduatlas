## PART 1: PROPORTION IN ALGEBRA

## 1. UNDERSTANDING PROPORTION

### 1.1 What is Proportion?

> **KEY DEFINITION**: **Proportion** describes the relationship between two quantities where they change in a consistent way relative to each other.

**Why is proportion important?**
- Models real-world relationships (speed, cost, physics)
- Forms equations from word problems
- Foundation for understanding functions
- Essential for problem-solving in science

---

### 1.2 Types of Proportion

There are two main types of proportion:

| Type | Relationship | Symbol |
|------|--------------|--------|
| **Direct Proportion** | As one quantity increases, the other increases at the same rate | $\propto$ |
| **Inverse Proportion** | As one quantity increases, the other decreases | $\propto \frac{1}{...}$ |

---

## 2. DIRECT PROPORTION

### 2.1 Definition and Formula

> **KEY DEFINITION**: Two quantities $y$ and $x$ are **directly proportional** if $y$ increases at the same rate as $x$. We write $y \propto x$.

**Formula:**
$$y \propto x \quad \Rightarrow \quad y = kx$$

where $k$ is the **constant of proportionality** (a fixed number).

---

### 2.2 Key Characteristics of Direct Proportion

- $y = kx$ is a straight line through the **origin** (0, 0)
- When $x$ doubles, $y$ doubles
- When $x$ triples, $y$ triples
- The ratio $\frac{y}{x} = k$ is **constant**

---

### 2.3 Finding the Constant of Proportionality

**Method:**
1. Write the proportion formula: $y = kx$
2. Substitute known values of $x$ and $y$
3. Solve for $k$
4. Write the complete equation

---

#### **Worked Examples**

**Example 1:** $y$ is directly proportional to $x$. When $x = 4$, $y = 12$. Find the equation connecting $y$ and $x$.

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Write the proportion formula
$$y = kx$$

**Step 2:** Substitute the known values
$$12 = k \times 4$$

**Step 3:** Solve for $k$
$$k = \frac{12}{4} = 3$$

**Step 4:** Write the complete equation
$$y = 3x$$

---

**Example 2:** Using the equation from Example 1, find $y$ when $x = 7$.

**Solution:**
$$y = 3x = 3 \times 7 = 21$$

**Answer:** $y = 21$

---

**Example 3:** $y$ is directly proportional to $x$. When $x = 15$, $y = 45$. Find $x$ when $y = 63$.

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Find $k$
$$y = kx$$
$$45 = k \times 15$$
$$k = 3$$

**Step 2:** Write the equation
$$y = 3x$$

**Step 3:** Find $x$ when $y = 63$
$$63 = 3x$$
$$x = \frac{63}{3} = 21$$

**Answer:** $x = 21$

---

### 2.4 Direct Proportion with Powers

> **KEY FORMULA:** If $y \propto x^n$, then $y = kx^n$

**Common variations:**
- $y \propto x^2$ means $y = kx^2$ (quadratic proportion)
- $y \propto \sqrt{x}$ means $y = k\sqrt{x}$ (square root proportion)

---

**Example 4:** $y$ is directly proportional to $x^2$. When $x = 3$, $y = 18$. Find the equation and the value of $y$ when $x = 5$.

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Write the formula
$$y = kx^2$$

**Step 2:** Find $k$
$$18 = k \times 3^2$$
$$18 = k \times 9$$
$$k = 2$$

**Step 3:** Write the equation
$$y = 2x^2$$

**Step 4:** Find $y$ when $x = 5$
$$y = 2 \times 5^2 = 2 \times 25 = 50$$

**Answer:** $y = 2x^2$, and when $x = 5$, $y = 50$

---

**Example 5:** $y \propto \sqrt{x}$. When $x = 9$, $y = 15$. Find $y$ when $x = 25$.

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Write the formula
$$y = k\sqrt{x}$$

**Step 2:** Find $k$
$$15 = k\sqrt{9}$$
$$15 = k \times 3$$
$$k = 5$$

**Step 3:** Write the equation
$$y = 5\sqrt{x}$$

**Step 4:** Find $y$ when $x = 25$
$$y = 5\sqrt{25} = 5 \times 5 = 25$$

**Answer:** $y = 25$

---

### 2.5 Recognising Direct Proportion from Data

**To check if data shows direct proportion:**
- Calculate $\frac{y}{x}$ for each pair
- If all values are equal (constant), it's direct proportion
- The constant equals $k$

**Example 6:** Does this table show direct proportion?

| $x$ | 2 | 4 | 6 | 8 |
|-----|---|---|---|---|
| $y$ | 6 | 12 | 18 | 24 |

**Solution:**
Calculate $\frac{y}{x}$ for each pair:
- $\frac{6}{2} = 3$
- $\frac{12}{4} = 3$
- $\frac{18}{6} = 3$
- $\frac{24}{8} = 3$

All ratios are equal to 3, so $y \propto x$ with $k = 3$.

**Answer:** Yes, $y = 3x$

---

## 3. INVERSE PROPORTION

### 3.1 Definition and Formula

> **KEY DEFINITION**: Two quantities $y$ and $x$ are **inversely proportional** if their product is constant. We write $y \propto \frac{1}{x}$.

**Formula:**
$$y \propto \frac{1}{x} \quad \Rightarrow \quad y = \frac{k}{x}$$

where $k$ is the constant of proportionality.

**Alternative form:**
$$y \times x = k$$

---

### 3.2 Key Characteristics of Inverse Proportion

- When $x$ doubles, $y$ halves
- When $x$ triples, $y$ becomes one-third
- The product $xy = k$ is **constant**
- The graph is a hyperbola (curved line)

---

### 3.3 Finding the Constant of Proportionality

**Method:**
1. Write the formula: $y = \frac{k}{x}$ or $xy = k$
2. Substitute known values
3. Solve for $k$
4. Write the complete equation

---

#### **Worked Examples**

**Example 7:** $y$ is inversely proportional to $x$. When $x = 4$, $y = 6$. Find the equation connecting $y$ and $x$.

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Write the formula
$$y = \frac{k}{x}$$

**Step 2:** Substitute known values
$$6 = \frac{k}{4}$$

**Step 3:** Solve for $k$
$$k = 6 \times 4 = 24$$

**Step 4:** Write the equation
$$y = \frac{24}{x}$$

**Answer:** $y = \frac{24}{x}$

---

**Example 8:** Using the equation from Example 7, find $y$ when $x = 8$.

**Solution:**
$$y = \frac{24}{8} = 3$$

**Answer:** $y = 3$

> 📝 **Note:** When $x$ doubled from 4 to 8, $y$ halved from 6 to 3. This confirms inverse proportion.

---

**Example 9:** $y$ is inversely proportional to $x$. When $x = 10$, $y = 5$. Find $x$ when $y = 2$.

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Find $k$
$$y = \frac{k}{x}$$
$$5 = \frac{k}{10}$$
$$k = 50$$

**Step 2:** Write the equation
$$y = \frac{50}{x}$$

**Step 3:** Find $x$ when $y = 2$
$$2 = \frac{50}{x}$$
$$2x = 50$$
$$x = 25$$

**Answer:** $x = 25$

---

### 3.4 Inverse Proportion with Powers

> **KEY FORMULA:** If $y \propto \frac{1}{x^n}$, then $y = \frac{k}{x^n}$

**Common variations:**
- $y \propto \frac{1}{x^2}$ means $y = \frac{k}{x^2}$
- $y \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{x}}$ means $y = \frac{k}{\sqrt{x}}$

---

**Example 10:** $y$ is inversely proportional to the square of $x$. When $x = 2$, $y = 9$. Find $y$ when $x = 3$.

**Solution:**

**Step 1:** Write the formula
$$y = \frac{k}{x^2}$$

**Step 2:** Find $k$
$$9 = \frac{k}{2^2}$$
$$9 = \frac{k}{4}$$
$$k = 36$$

**Step 3:** Write the equation
$$y = \frac{36}{x^2}$$

**Step 4:** Find $y$ when $x = 3$
$$y = \frac{36}{3^2} = \frac{36}{9} = 4$$

**Answer:** $y = 4$

---

## 4. COMPARING DIRECT AND INVERSE PROPORTION

### 4.1 Summary Table

| Feature | Direct Proportion | Inverse Proportion |
|---------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Symbol** | $y \propto x$ | $y \propto \frac{1}{x}$ |
| **Equation** | $y = kx$ | $y = \frac{k}{x}$ |
| **Alternative** | $\frac{y}{x} = k$ | $xy = k$ |
| **Graph** | Straight line through origin | Hyperbola (curve) |
| **When $x$ doubles** | $y$ doubles | $y$ halves |
| **When $x$ halves** | $y$ halves | $y$ doubles |

---

### 4.2 How to Determine Which Type

**Read the wording carefully:**

| Phrase | Type |
|--------|------|
| "$y$ is directly proportional to $x$" | $y = kx$ |
| "$y$ varies directly as $x$" | $y = kx$ |
| "$y$ is proportional to $x$" | $y = kx$ |
| "$y$ is inversely proportional to $x$" | $y = \frac{k}{x}$ |
| "$y$ varies inversely as $x$" | $y = \frac{k}{x}$ |
| "$y$ is proportional to the reciprocal of $x$" | $y = \frac{k}{x}$ |

---

## 5. REAL-WORLD PROPORTION PROBLEMS

### 5.1 Problem-Solving Strategy

**Step-by-Step Approach:**
1. **Read carefully** – Identify the type of proportion
2. **Define variables** – Let $x$ and $y$ represent the quantities
3. **Write the formula** – $y = kx$ or $y = \frac{k}{x}$ (or with powers)
4. **Find $k$** – Use given values
5. **Write the complete equation**
6. **Solve for the unknown**
7. **Check reasonableness** – Does the answer make sense?

---

### 5.2 Worked Examples

**Example 11: Direct Proportion (Cost)**

The cost $C$ of fuel is directly proportional to the number of litres $L$. 20 litres cost £28.

(a) Find the equation connecting $C$ and $L$.
(b) Find the cost of 35 litres.
(c) How many litres can be bought for £49?

**Solution:**

**(a)** 
$$C = kL$$
$$28 = k \times 20$$
$$k = \frac{28}{20} = 1.4$$
$$C = 1.4L$$

**(b)**
$$C = 1.4 \times 35 = £49$$

**(c)**
$$49 = 1.4L$$
$$L = \frac{49}{1.4} = 35 \text{ litres}$$

---

**Example 12: Inverse Proportion (Time and Workers)**

The time $t$ days to complete a project is inversely proportional to the number of workers $w$. 5 workers take 12 days.

(a) Find the equation connecting $t$ and $w$.
(b) How long would 8 workers take?

**Solution:**

**(a)**
$$t = \frac{k}{w}$$
$$12 = \frac{k}{5}$$
$$k = 60$$
$$t = \frac{60}{w}$$

**(b)**
$$t = \frac{60}{8} = 7.5 \text{ days}$$

---

**Example 13: Direct Proportion with Square (Physics)**

The distance $d$ travelled by a falling object is directly proportional to the square of the time $t$. An object falls 80 m in 4 seconds.

(a) Find the equation.
(b) How far does it fall in 6 seconds?
(c) How long does it take to fall 180 m?

**Solution:**

**(a)**
$$d = kt^2$$
$$80 = k \times 4^2$$
$$80 = 16k$$
$$k = 5$$
$$d = 5t^2$$

**(b)**
$$d = 5 \times 6^2 = 5 \times 36 = 180 \text{ m}$$

**(c)**
$$180 = 5t^2$$
$$t^2 = 36$$
$$t = 6 \text{ seconds}$$

---

**Example 14: Inverse Proportion with Square (Physics)**

The intensity $I$ of light is inversely proportional to the square of the distance $d$ from the source. At 2 m, the intensity is 100 units.

(a) Find the equation.
(b) Find the intensity at 5 m.

**Solution:**

**(a)**
$$I = \frac{k}{d^2}$$
$$100 = \frac{k}{2^2}$$
$$100 = \frac{k}{4}$$
$$k = 400$$
$$I = \frac{400}{d^2}$$

**(b)**
$$I = \frac{400}{5^2} = \frac{400}{25} = 16 \text{ units}$$

---

## 6. COMMON MISTAKES IN PROPORTION

>  **EXAM WARNING – TOP ERRORS:**

| Mistake | Wrong | Correct |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Confusing direct and inverse | Using $y = kx$ for inverse proportion | Use $y = \frac{k}{x}$ for inverse |
| Forgetting to square | $y \propto x^2$ → $y = kx$ | $y \propto x^2$ → $y = kx^2$ |
| Not substituting correctly | $18 = k \times 3$ (when $y \propto x^2$ and $x = 3$) | $18 = k \times 3^2 = 9k$ |
| Wrong rearrangement | $y = \frac{k}{x}$ → $k = \frac{y}{x}$ | $k = xy$ |
| Forgetting units | Giving numerical answer only | Include units in final answer |

# PART 2: INDICES LAWS

---

## 7. UNDERSTANDING INDICES

### 7.1 What are Indices?

> **KEY DEFINITION**: **Indices** (also called powers or exponents) show how many times a number is multiplied by itself.

**Notation:** $a^n$ means $a$ multiplied by itself $n$ times.

**Example:**
$$a^3 = a \times a \times a$$
$$5^4 = 5 \times 5 \times 5 \times 5 = 625$$

---

### 7.2 Key Terminology

| Term | Meaning | Example |
|------|---------|---------|
| **Base** | The number being multiplied | In $5^3$, base = 5 |
| **Index/Power/Exponent** | How many times to multiply | In $5^3$, index = 3 |
| **$a^n$** | "$a$ to the power $n$" | $5^3$ = "five to the power three" |
| **$a^2$** | "$a$ squared" | $5^2$ = "five squared" |
| **$a^3$** | "$a$ cubed" | $5^3$ = "five cubed" |

---

## 8. THE FIVE INDICES LAWS

### 8.1 Law 1: Multiplying Powers (Same Base)

> **KEY FORMULA:**
> $$a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$$

**Rule:** When multiplying powers with the **same base**, **add** the indices.

**Derivation:**
$$a^m \times a^n = \underbrace{a \times a \times \cdots \times a}_{m \text{ times}} \times \underbrace{a \times a \times \cdots \times a}_{n \text{ times}}$$
$$= \underbrace{a \times a \times \cdots \times a}_{(m+n) \text{ times}} = a^{m+n}$$

---

#### **Worked Examples**

**Example 15:** Simplify $a^3 \times a^5$

**Solution:**
$$a^3 \times a^5 = a^{3+5} = a^8$$

---

**Example 16:** Simplify $x^4 \times x^2 \times x^3$

**Solution:**
$$x^4 \times x^2 \times x^3 = x^{4+2+3} = x^9$$

---

**Example 17:** Simplify $3^4 \times 3^6$

**Solution:**
$$3^4 \times 3^6 = 3^{4+6} = 3^{10}$$

---

**Example 18:** Simplify $2x^3 \times 5x^4$

**Solution:**
$$2x^3 \times 5x^4 = 2 \times 5 \times x^3 \times x^4 = 10x^{3+4} = 10x^7$$

> 📝 **Note:** Multiply the coefficients (2 × 5 = 10), add the powers of $x$.

---

**Example 19:** Simplify $x^2y \times xy^3$

**Solution:**
$$x^2y \times xy^3 = x^2 \times x \times y \times y^3 = x^{2+1} \times y^{1+3} = x^3y^4$$

---

### 8.2 Law 2: Dividing Powers (Same Base)

> **KEY FORMULA:**
> $$a^m \div a^n = a^{m-n}$$

**Rule:** When dividing powers with the **same base**, **subtract** the indices.

**Derivation:**
$$\frac{a^m}{a^n} = \frac{\overbrace{a \times a \times \cdots \times a}^{m \text{ times}}}{\underbrace{a \times a \times \cdots \times a}_{n \text{ times}}} = \underbrace{a \times a \times \cdots \times a}_{(m-n) \text{ times}} = a^{m-n}$$

---

#### **Worked Examples**

**Example 20:** Simplify $a^7 \div a^3$

**Solution:**
$$a^7 \div a^3 = a^{7-3} = a^4$$

---

**Example 21:** Simplify $\frac{x^{10}}{x^4}$

**Solution:**
$$\frac{x^{10}}{x^4} = x^{10-4} = x^6$$

---

**Example 22:** Simplify $\frac{15x^8}{3x^3}$

**Solution:**
$$\frac{15x^8}{3x^3} = \frac{15}{3} \times \frac{x^8}{x^3} = 5x^{8-3} = 5x^5$$

---

**Example 23:** Simplify $\frac{12a^6b^5}{4a^2b^3}$

**Solution:**
$$\frac{12a^6b^5}{4a^2b^3} = \frac{12}{4} \times \frac{a^6}{a^2} \times \frac{b^5}{b^3} = 3a^{6-2}b^{5-3} = 3a^4b^2$$

---

### 8.3 Law 3: Power of a Power

> **KEY FORMULA:**
> $$(a^m)^n = a^{mn}$$

**Rule:** When raising a power to another power, **multiply** the indices.

**Derivation:**
$$(a^m)^n = \underbrace{a^m \times a^m \times \cdots \times a^m}_{n \text{ times}} = \underbrace{a \times a \times \cdots \times a}_{m \times n \text{ times}} = a^{mn}$$

---

#### **Worked Examples**

**Example 24:** Simplify $(x^3)^4$

**Solution:**
$$(x^3)^4 = x^{3 \times 4} = x^{12}$$

---

**Example 25:** Simplify $(a^2)^5$

**Solution:**
$$(a^2)^5 = a^{2 \times 5} = a^{10}$$

---

**Example 26:** Simplify $(2^3)^4$

**Solution:**
$$(2^3)^4 = 2^{3 \times 4} = 2^{12}$$

---

**Example 27:** Simplify $(x^2y^3)^4$

**Solution:**
$$(x^2y^3)^4 = (x^2)^4 \times (y^3)^4 = x^{2 \times 4} \times y^{3 \times 4} = x^8y^{12}$$

---

**Example 28:** Simplify $(3a^4)^3$

**Solution:**
$$(3a^4)^3 = 3^3 \times (a^4)^3 = 27 \times a^{4 \times 3} = 27a^{12}$$

---

### 8.4 Law 4: Power of Zero

> **KEY FORMULA:**
> $$a^0 = 1 \quad \text{(for any } a \neq 0\text{)}$$

**Rule:** Any non-zero number to the power zero equals 1.

**Derivation using Law 2:**
$$a^m \div a^m = a^{m-m} = a^0$$
But also: $a^m \div a^m = 1$
Therefore: $a^0 = 1$

---

#### **Worked Examples**

**Example 29:** Evaluate $5^0$

**Solution:**
$$5^0 = 1$$

---

**Example 30:** Evaluate $(17)^0$

**Solution:**
$$(17)^0 = 1$$

---

**Example 31:** Simplify $x^0$

**Solution:**
$$x^0 = 1 \quad \text{(assuming } x \neq 0\text{)}$$

---

**Example 32:** Simplify $3x^0$

**Solution:**
$$3x^0 = 3 \times 1 = 3$$

---

### 8.5 Law 5: Negative Indices

> **KEY FORMULA:**
> $$a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}$$

**Rule:** A negative index means "one over" (reciprocal).

**Derivation using Law 2:**
$$a^0 \div a^n = a^{0-n} = a^{-n}$$
But also: $a^0 \div a^n = \frac{1}{a^n}$
Therefore: $a^{-n} = \frac{1}{a^n}$

---

#### **Worked Examples**

**Example 33:** Evaluate $2^{-3}$

**Solution:**
$$2^{-3} = \frac{1}{2^3} = \frac{1}{8}$$

---

**Example 34:** Evaluate $5^{-2}$

**Solution:**
$$5^{-2} = \frac{1}{5^2} = \frac{1}{25}$$

---

**Example 35:** Express $x^{-4}$ with a positive index

**Solution:**
$$x^{-4} = \frac{1}{x^4}$$

---

**Example 36:** Express $\frac{1}{x^3}$ with a negative index

**Solution:**
$$\frac{1}{x^3} = x^{-3}$$

---

**Example 37:** Express $\frac{3}{x^2}$ with a negative index

**Solution:**
$$\frac{3}{x^2} = 3x^{-2}$$

---

## 9. FRACTIONAL INDICES

### 9.1 Square Root as an Index

> **KEY FORMULA:**
> $$a^{\frac{1}{2}} = \sqrt{a}$$

**Rule:** An index of $\frac{1}{2}$ means square root.

**Derivation:**
$$a^{\frac{1}{2}} \times a^{\frac{1}{2}} = a^{\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2}} = a^1 = a$$
So $a^{\frac{1}{2}}$ is a number that multiplied by itself gives $a$, which is $\sqrt{a}$.

---

**Example 38:** Evaluate $9^{\frac{1}{2}}$

**Solution:**
$$9^{\frac{1}{2}} = \sqrt{9} = 3$$

---

**Example 39:** Evaluate $25^{\frac{1}{2}}$

**Solution:**
$$25^{\frac{1}{2}} = \sqrt{25} = 5$$

---

### 9.2 Cube Root as an Index

> **KEY FORMULA:**
> $$a^{\frac{1}{3}} = \sqrt[3]{a}$$

**Rule:** An index of $\frac{1}{3}$ means cube root.

**Derivation:**
$$a^{\frac{1}{3}} \times a^{\frac{1}{3}} \times a^{\frac{1}{3}} = a^{\frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{3} + \frac{1}{3}} = a^1 = a$$

---

**Example 40:** Evaluate $8^{\frac{1}{3}}$

**Solution:**
$$8^{\frac{1}{3}} = \sqrt[3]{8} = 2$$

---

**Example 41:** Evaluate $27^{\frac{1}{3}}$

**Solution:**
$$27^{\frac{1}{3}} = \sqrt[3]{27} = 3$$

---

### 9.3 General Fractional Indices

> **KEY FORMULA:**
> $$a^{\frac{m}{n}} = \sqrt[n]{a^m} = (\sqrt[n]{a})^m$$

**Rule:** The denominator is the root, the numerator is the power.

**Two methods:**
1. Take the root first, then raise to the power
2. Raise to the power first, then take the root

---

#### **Worked Examples**

**Example 42:** Evaluate $8^{\frac{2}{3}}$

**Solution (Method 1):**
$$8^{\frac{2}{3}} = (\sqrt[3]{8})^2 = 2^2 = 4$$

**Solution (Method 2):**
$$8^{\frac{2}{3}} = \sqrt[3]{8^2} = \sqrt[3]{64} = 4$$

---

**Example 43:** Evaluate $16^{\frac{3}{2}}$

**Solution:**
$$16^{\frac{3}{2}} = (\sqrt{16})^3 = 4^3 = 64$$

---

**Example 44:** Evaluate $27^{\frac{4}{3}}$

**Solution:**
$$27^{\frac{4}{3}} = (\sqrt[3]{27})^4 = 3^4 = 81$$

---

### 9.4 Negative Fractional Indices

> **KEY FORMULA:**
> $$a^{-\frac{m}{n}} = \frac{1}{a^{\frac{m}{n}}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt[n]{a^m}}$$

---

**Example 45:** Evaluate $4^{-\frac{1}{2}}$

**Solution:**
$$4^{-\frac{1}{2}} = \frac{1}{4^{\frac{1}{2}}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{4}} = \frac{1}{2}$$

---

**Example 46:** Evaluate $8^{-\frac{2}{3}}$

**Solution:**
$$8^{-\frac{2}{3}} = \frac{1}{8^{\frac{2}{3}}} = \frac{1}{4}$$

---

## 10. COMBINED INDICES PROBLEMS

### 10.1 Complex Simplifications

**Example 47:** Simplify $(2x^3)^4 \times 3x^2$

**Solution:**
$$(2x^3)^4 \times 3x^2 = 2^4 \times (x^3)^4 \times 3x^2$$
$$= 16 \times x^{12} \times 3x^2$$
$$= 48x^{14}$$

---

**Example 48:** Simplify $\frac{(x^4)^3}{x^5 \times x^2}$

**Solution:**
$$\frac{(x^4)^3}{x^5 \times x^2} = \frac{x^{12}}{x^7} = x^{12-7} = x^5$$

---

**Example 49:** Simplify $\frac{12a^5b^3}{4a^2b} \times \frac{a^3b^2}{ab^4}$

**Solution:**
$$\frac{12a^5b^3}{4a^2b} \times \frac{a^3b^2}{ab^4} = \frac{12}{4} \times a^{5-2} \times b^{3-1} \times a^3 \times b^2 \times a^{-1} \times b^{-4}$$
$$= 3 \times a^3 \times b^2 \times a^3 \times b^2 \times a^{-1} \times b^{-4}$$
$$= 3 \times a^{3+3-1} \times b^{2+2-4}$$
$$= 3a^5b^0 = 3a^5$$

---

**Example 50:** Express $\frac{x^3y^{-2}}{x^{-1}y^4}$ with positive indices only

**Solution:**
$$\frac{x^3y^{-2}}{x^{-1}y^4} = x^{3-(-1)} \times y^{-2-4} = x^4y^{-6} = \frac{x^4}{y^6}$$

---

## 11. COMMON MISTAKES IN INDICES

>  **EXAM WARNING – TOP ERRORS:**

| Mistake | Wrong | Correct |
|---------|-------|---------|
| Adding instead of multiplying | $(x^3)^2 = x^5$ | $(x^3)^2 = x^6$ |
| Multiplying bases | $2^3 \times 3^2 = 6^5$ | Keep separate: $8 \times 9 = 72$ |
| Wrong sign in division | $\frac{x^5}{x^2} = x^3$ ✓ but $\frac{x^2}{x^5} = x^3$ | $\frac{x^2}{x^5} = x^{-3} = \frac{1}{x^3}$ |
| Forgetting zero power | $x^0 = 0$ | $x^0 = 1$ |
| Wrong fractional index | $8^{\frac{1}{3}} = \frac{8}{3}$ | $8^{\frac{1}{3}} = 2$ |
| Not simplifying coefficients | $3^2 \times 3^3 = 3^5$ only | $3^5 = 243$ (if asked for value) |

## 15. PRACTICE TIPS FOR A* STUDENTS

### 15.1 Quick Self-Check Questions

Before answering any question, ask yourself:
1. Is this direct or inverse proportion?
2. What is the correct formula?
3. Do I need to square/square root?
4. For indices: am I multiplying, dividing, or taking a power?
5. Is the final answer in the correct form?

### 15.2 Common Exam Traps to Avoid

1. **Confusing "inversely proportional"** – always check the wording
2. **Forgetting to square when $y \propto x^2$** – substitute the squared value
3. **Adding indices when multiplying bases** – only apply laws with SAME base
4. **Not simplifying fully** – check coefficients and powers
5. **Missing units** – always include units in word problem answers

### 15.3 Verification Methods

- **Proportion check:** Substitute your equation back with original values
- **Indices check:** Calculate numerical values for simple cases
- **Reasonableness check:** Does the answer make sense in context?
