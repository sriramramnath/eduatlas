## Quick Reference - Trigonometric Ratios

### The Two Key Formulas

| Ratio | Formula | Use When... |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| **Sine** | $\sin \theta = \frac{\text{Opposite}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$ | O and H are involved |
| **Cosine** | $\cos \theta = \frac{\text{Adjacent}}{\text{Hypotenuse}}$ | A and H are involved |

## 🎯 Side Labelling Rules

| Side | How to Identify |
|------|-----------------|
| **Hypotenuse** | Longest side; opposite the right angle |
| **Opposite** | Across from the angle you're using |
| **Adjacent** | Touching/next to the angle you're using |

>  **Remember:** Labels change depending on which angle you use!

---

## 📊 Decision Table - Which Ratio to Use?

### Finding a Side:

| You Have | You Need | Use This Formula |
|----------|----------|------------------|
| Angle + Hypotenuse | Opposite | $O = H \times \sin \theta$ |
| Angle + Opposite | Hypotenuse | $H = \frac{O}{\sin \theta}$ |
| Angle + Hypotenuse | Adjacent | $A = H \times \cos \theta$ |
| Angle + Adjacent | Hypotenuse | $H = \frac{A}{\cos \theta}$ |

### Finding an Angle:

| You Have | Formula |
|----------|---------|
| Opposite + Hypotenuse | $\theta = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{O}{H}\right)$ |
| Adjacent + Hypotenuse | $\theta = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{A}{H}\right)$ |

## 🔢 Exact Values (MEMORISE!)

| Angle | $\sin \theta$ | $\cos \theta$ |
|-------|---------------|---------------|
| **0°** | 0 | 1 |
| **30°** | $\frac{1}{2}$ | $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ |
| **45°** | $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$ | $\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$ |
| **60°** | $\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$ | $\frac{1}{2}$ |
| **90°** | 1 | 0 |

## ⚡ Quick Steps

### Finding a Side:
1. Label sides (H, O, A) relative to the given angle
2. Choose ratio: sin (if O/H) or cos (if A/H)
3. Substitute values into formula
4. Solve (multiply if finding O or A; divide if finding H)
5. Give answer to required accuracy

### Finding an Angle:
1. Label sides (H, O, A)
2. Choose ratio based on known sides
3. Calculate the ratio value (decimal or fraction)
4. Use inverse: $\sin^{-1}$ or $\cos^{-1}$
5. Give angle to required accuracy (usually 1 d.p.)

##  Common Exam Mistakes

| Mistake | How to Avoid |
|---------|--------------|
| Calculator in radians | Check for "D" or "DEG" mode |
| Wrong side labels | Always label from YOUR angle |
| Multiplying when should divide | Check: if finding H, divide by sin/cos |
| Premature rounding | Keep exact values until final answer |
| Missing units | Always include cm, m, km etc. |

##  Rearrangement Quick Guide

| Equation Form | Rearranged For | Result |
|---------------|----------------|--------|
| $\sin \theta = \frac{O}{H}$ | Finding O | $O = H \times \sin \theta$ |
| $\sin \theta = \frac{O}{H}$ | Finding H | $H = \frac{O}{\sin \theta}$ |
| $\cos \theta = \frac{A}{H}$ | Finding A | $A = H \times \cos \theta$ |
| $\cos \theta = \frac{A}{H}$ | Finding H | $H = \frac{A}{\cos \theta}$ |

## 🎯 Key Rules

### Rule 1: Hypotenuse Check
- The hypotenuse is always the **longest** side
- If your calculated side is longer than the hypotenuse, something's wrong!

### Rule 2: Value Bounds
- $\sin \theta$ and $\cos \theta$ values are always between **-1 and 1**
- If you get a value outside this range, check your calculation

### Rule 3: Angle Size
- Larger angle = larger opposite side
- If angle increases, opposite side increases

### Rule 4: Complementary Angles
- $\sin \theta = \cos(90° - \theta)$
- $\cos \theta = \sin(90° - \theta)$

##  Exam Tips

1. **Always show working** - Marks are given for method
2. **State the formula first** - Shows understanding
3. **Don't round early** - Keep accuracy until the end
4. **Check calculator mode** - Must be in DEGREES
5. **Use brackets** - $\sin(30)$ to be safe
6. **Write inverse correctly** - $\sin^{-1}(x)$ not $\sin(x)^{-1}$
