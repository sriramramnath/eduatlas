### Question 1
Point A(3, −2) is translated by vector $\begin{pmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix}$. What are the coordinates of A'?

**A** (−1, 3)  
**B** (7, −7)  
**C** (−1, 3)  
**D** (1, 3)

**Answer: C (−1, 3)**

**Explanation:**
- Translation by $\begin{pmatrix} -4 \\ 5 \end{pmatrix}$ means: move 4 units left, 5 units up
- A(3, −2) → A'(3 + (−4), −2 + 5) = A'(−1, 3)

**Mark scheme awarding:**
- M1: Correct application of translation (adding vector components)
- A1: Correct answer (−1, 3)

### Question 2
Point P(−2, 4) is reflected in the line $x = 3$. What are the coordinates of P'?

**A** (8, 4)  
**B** (−2, 2)  
**C** (8, 4)  
**D** (−8, 4)

**Answer: A (8, 4)**

**Explanation:**
Using formula for reflection in $x = a$: $(x, y) \rightarrow (2a - x, y)$
- $a = 3$, so $(x, y) \rightarrow (6 - x, y)$
- P(−2, 4) → P'(6 − (−2), 4) = P'(8, 4)

**Alternative method:**
- Distance from $x = 3$ to P: 3 − (−2) = 5 units
- P' is same distance on other side: 3 + 5 = 8

**Mark scheme awarding:**
- M1: Correct method (formula or distance counting)
- A1: Correct answer (8, 4)

### Question 3
Point Q(2, −3) is rotated 90° anticlockwise about the origin. What are the coordinates of Q'?

**A** (3, 2)  
**B** (−3, −2)  
**C** (3, −2)  
**D** (−2, 3)

**Answer: C (3, −2)**

**Explanation:**
For 90° anticlockwise about origin: $(x, y) \rightarrow (-y, x)$
- Q(2, −3) → Q'(−(−3), 2) = Q'(3, 2)

**Wait, let me recalculate:**
- $(x, y) \rightarrow (-y, x)$
- (2, −3) → (−(−3), 2) = (3, 2)

**Answer: A (3, 2)**

**Memory trick:** ACW rotation: x becomes −y, y becomes x

**Mark scheme awarding:**
- M1: Correct rotation rule identified
- A1: Correct answer (3, 2)

### Question 4
A triangle with area 6 cm² is enlarged by scale factor 3. What is the area of the image?

**A** 18 cm²  
**B** 36 cm²  
**C** 54 cm²  
**D** 9 cm²

**Answer: C (54 cm²)**

**Explanation:**
Area scale factor = (linear scale factor)²
- $k = 3$, so area factor = $k^2 = 9$
- New area = $6 \times 9 = 54$ cm²

**Common mistake:** Using $k = 3$ directly to get 18 cm²

**Mark scheme awarding:**
- M1: Recognising area factor = $k^2$
- M1: Correct calculation
- A1: 54 cm²

### Question 5
Shape S is reflected in the line $y = x$, then reflected in the line $y = -x$. The single transformation equivalent to this is:

**A** Rotation 90° clockwise about origin  
**B** Rotation 180° about origin  
**C** Rotation 90° anticlockwise about origin  
**D** Reflection in the x-axis

**Answer: B (Rotation 180° about origin)**

**Explanation:**
Two reflections in perpendicular lines through origin = rotation 180° about origin.

**Verification:**
- Point (2, 3) reflected in $y = x$: (3, 2)
- Then reflected in $y = -x$: (−2, −3)
- This equals 180° rotation: (2, 3) → (−2, −3) ✓

**Mark scheme awarding:**
- M1: Recognising perpendicular mirror lines or testing with a point
- A1: Correct equivalent transformation

### Question 6
Point R(4, 1) is enlarged by scale factor −2, centre (0, 0). What are the coordinates of R'?

**A** (−8, −2)  
**B** (8, 2)  
**C** (−2, −0.5)  
**D** (−4, −1)

**Answer: A (−8, −2)**

**Explanation:**
For enlargement SF $k$ with centre at origin: $(x, y) \rightarrow (kx, ky)$
- $k = -2$
- R(4, 1) → R'(−2 × 4, −2 × 1) = R'(−8, −2)

**Note:** Negative scale factor means the image is on the opposite side of the centre.

**Mark scheme awarding:**
- M1: Correct formula for enlargement about origin
- A1: Correct answer (−8, −2)

### Question 7
Point T(1, 5) is rotated 180° about the point (−2, 3). What are the coordinates of T'?

**A** (−5, 1)  
**B** (−1, −5)  
**C** (−5, 1)  
**D** (5, −1)

**Answer: A (−5, 1)**

**Explanation:**
For 180° rotation about $(a, b)$: $(x, y) \rightarrow (2a - x, 2b - y)$
- Centre = (−2, 3), so $a = -2$, $b = 3$
- T(1, 5) → T'(2(−2) − 1, 2(3) − 5)
- = T'(−4 − 1, 6 − 5) = T'(−5, 1)

**Mark scheme awarding:**
- M1: Correct formula or method
- A1: Correct answer (−5, 1)

### Question 8
Which transformation does NOT preserve orientation?

**A** Translation  
**B** Enlargement with positive scale factor  
**C** Rotation 180°  
**D** Reflection

**Answer: D (Reflection)**

**Explanation:**
- Translation: preserves orientation ✓
- Enlargement (positive SF): preserves orientation ✓
- Rotation 180°: actually preserves orientation (object flips twice)
- Reflection: reverses orientation ✗

**Note:** A reflection creates a mirror image, which reverses the "handedness" of the shape.

**Mark scheme awarding:**
- A1: Correct identification of reflection

### Question 9
A shape is translated by vector $\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}$, then by vector $\begin{pmatrix} -5 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix}$. What single vector describes this transformation?

**A** $\begin{pmatrix} -2 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}$  
**B** $\begin{pmatrix} 2 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}$  
**C** $\begin{pmatrix} -2 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix}$  
**D** $\begin{pmatrix} 8 \\ -6 \end{pmatrix}$

**Answer: A $\begin{pmatrix} -2 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}$**

**Explanation:**
Add the translation vectors:
$$\begin{pmatrix} 3 \\ -2 \end{pmatrix} + \begin{pmatrix} -5 \\ 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 3 + (-5) \\ -2 + 4 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} -2 \\ 2 \end{pmatrix}$$

**Mark scheme awarding:**
- M1: Adding vectors correctly
- A1: Correct answer

### Question 10
Point M is invariant under a rotation of 90° about point N. What can we conclude?

**A** M and N are the same point  
**B** M lies on the x-axis  
**C** M is any point on the shape  
**D** M is 1 unit from N

**Answer: A (M and N are the same point)**

**Explanation:**
Under a rotation, the only invariant point is the centre of rotation itself. Therefore, if point M is invariant (doesn't move), M must be the centre of rotation N.

**Mark scheme awarding:**
- A1: Understanding that only the centre is invariant in rotation

## SECTION B: TRUE/FALSE QUESTIONS (10 Questions)

### Question 11
**Statement:** A translation by vector $\begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$ leaves all points invariant.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** A translation of zero moves every point by zero distance. Therefore, all points remain unchanged (invariant).

---

### Question 12
**Statement:** A reflection in the line $y = 0$ followed by another reflection in the line $y = 0$ results in a rotation of 180°.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A reflection followed by the same reflection results in **no change** (identity transformation). Each point returns to its original position.

**Reasoning:** Reflection is self-inverse. Applying the same reflection twice cancels the effect.

---

### Question 13
**Statement:** An enlargement with scale factor $k = -1$ is equivalent to a rotation of 180° about the centre of enlargement.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** 
- For $k = -1$: $(x, y) \rightarrow (a + (-1)(x-a), b + (-1)(y-b)) = (2a-x, 2b-y)$
- For 180° rotation about $(a, b)$: $(x, y) \rightarrow (2a-x, 2b-y)$

Both transformations produce the same result.

---

### Question 14
**Statement:** If a shape is enlarged by scale factor 2, the perimeter of the image is twice the perimeter of the original.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Linear dimensions (length, perimeter) scale by factor $k$.
- If perimeter = $P$, then new perimeter = $k \times P = 2P$

Note: Area would scale by $k^2 = 4$.

---

### Question 15
**Statement:** In a rotation of 90° clockwise, every point moves the same distance.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Points at different distances from the centre travel different arc lengths. Only the angle is the same (90°).

**Reasoning:** The arc length = radius × angle. Points further from the centre travel greater distances.

---

### Question 16
**Statement:** When reflecting in the line $y = x$, the coordinates of any point swap places.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** The transformation rule for reflection in $y = x$ is:
$$(x, y) \rightarrow (y, x)$$

The coordinates literally swap positions.

---

### Question 17
**Statement:** A shape and its image under enlargement are always congruent.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A shape and its image under enlargement are **similar**, not congruent (unless $k = 1$ or $k = -1$).

**Reasoning:** Congruent means identical in size. Enlargement changes the size unless $k = \pm 1$.

---

### Question 18
**Statement:** The inverse of a rotation of 90° anticlockwise about point P is a rotation of 90° clockwise about the same point P.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** To "undo" a 90° anticlockwise rotation, we rotate 90° clockwise. Both rotations must have the same centre.

---

### Question 19
**Statement:** All points on the line $y = 3$ are invariant when a shape is reflected in the line $y = 3$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Points on the mirror line are always invariant under reflection. The line $y = 3$ is the mirror line, so all points on this line remain fixed.

---

### Question 20
**Statement:** Two reflections in parallel lines separated by distance $d$ is equivalent to a translation of distance $2d$.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** 
- First reflection: moves point to distance $d$ on other side of first line
- Second reflection: moves point another $d$ on other side of second line
- Total movement = $2d$

Direction is perpendicular to the mirror lines.

### Question 22 (4 marks)
Rectangle R is reflected in the line $x = 2$ to give rectangle R', then R' is reflected in the line $x = 6$ to give rectangle R''.

**(a)** Find the coordinates of R'' if R has vertices at (0, 0), (1, 0), (1, 3), and (0, 3). **(3 marks)**

**(b)** Describe the single transformation that maps R directly to R''. **(1 mark)**

**(a)** Step-by-step transformation:

**First reflection in $x = 2$:**
Using $(x, y) \rightarrow (4-x, y)$:
- (0, 0) → (4, 0)
- (1, 0) → (3, 0)
- (1, 3) → (3, 3)
- (0, 3) → (4, 3)

**Second reflection in $x = 6$:**
Using $(x, y) \rightarrow (12-x, y)$:
- (4, 0) → (8, 0)
- (3, 0) → (9, 0)
- (3, 3) → (9, 3)
- (4, 3) → (8, 3)

**R'' vertices: (8, 0), (9, 0), (9, 3), (8, 3)**

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Correct reflection formula
- M1: Applied to first reflection correctly
- A1: All final coordinates correct

**(b)** Two reflections in parallel lines = translation.

Distance between lines = $6 - 2 = 4$

Translation vector = $\begin{pmatrix} 2 \times 4 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 8 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$

**Verification:** (0, 0) → (8, 0) ✓

**Translation by vector $\begin{pmatrix} 8 \\ 0 \end{pmatrix}$**

**Mark scheme:**
- A1: Correct single transformation with correct vector

### Question 23 (5 marks)
Shape S is rotated 90° clockwise about the point (1, −1) to give shape S'.

**(a)** Find the image of point P(3, 2) under this rotation. **(3 marks)**

**(b)** Describe fully how to find the centre of rotation given a shape and its image after a 90° clockwise rotation. **(2 marks)**

**(a)** Rotation 90° clockwise about (1, −1):

**Method:**
1. Translate P so centre becomes origin: $(3-1, 2-(-1)) = (2, 3)$
2. Apply 90° clockwise rotation: $(x, y) \rightarrow (y, -x)$
   - $(2, 3) \rightarrow (3, -2)$
3. Translate back: $(3 + 1, -2 + (-1)) = (4, -3)$

**P'(4, −3)**

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Translate to origin correctly
- M1: Apply correct rotation rule
- A1: Correct final coordinates

**(b)** **Method to find centre of rotation:**

1. Draw lines joining two pairs of corresponding points (e.g., A to A' and B to B')
2. Construct the perpendicular bisector of each line
3. The intersection of the perpendicular bisectors is the centre of rotation

**Alternative method:**
1. Use compasses: set radius equal to distance from a point to its image
2. Draw arcs from both the original point and its image
3. The intersection gives the centre

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Mention perpendicular bisectors or geometric construction
- A1: Clear explanation of finding intersection

### Question 26 (8 marks)
Shape X has area 20 cm² and perimeter 24 cm.

Shape Y is obtained from Shape X by an enlargement with scale factor $k$.

**(a)** Find the area of Shape Y when $k = 3$. **(2 marks)**

**(b)** Find the perimeter of Shape Y when $k = \frac{1}{2}$. **(2 marks)**

**(c)** The area of Shape Y is 45 cm². Find the value of $k$. **(2 marks)**

**(d)** Shape Y is obtained by two successive enlargements with the same centre O, with scale factors 2 and $m$. The single equivalent enlargement has scale factor 6. Find $m$. **(2 marks)**

**(a)** Area factor = $k^2 = 3^2 = 9$

Area of Y = $20 \times 9 = 180$ cm²

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Area factor = $k^2$
- A1: 180 cm²

**(b)** Perimeter scales by factor $k$

Perimeter of Y = $24 \times \frac{1}{2} = 12$ cm

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Perimeter factor = $k$
- A1: 12 cm

**(c)** Area factor = $\frac{45}{20} = \frac{9}{4}$

$$k^2 = \frac{9}{4}$$

$$k = \frac{3}{2} = 1.5$$

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Setting up equation
- A1: $k = 1.5$ (accept $\frac{3}{2}$)

**(d)** Combined scale factor = $2 \times m = 6$

$$m = \frac{6}{2} = 3$$

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Recognising scale factors multiply
- A1: $m = 3$
