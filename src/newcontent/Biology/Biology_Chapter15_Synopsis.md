# Chapter 15: Human Influences on the Environment - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Human Population Growth

**Causes of increase:**
- Improved food production
- Better medical care
- Better sanitation
- Vaccinations
- Reduced infant mortality

**Impacts:**
- Increased resource demand
- Habitat destruction
- Pollution
- Climate change

---

## 2. Deforestation

### Causes
- Agriculture (crops, cattle)
- Logging (timber, paper)
- Urban development
- Mining

### Consequences
| Impact | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|
| **Biodiversity loss** | Habitat destruction |
| **Climate change** | Less CO₂ absorbed |
| **Soil erosion** | No roots to hold soil |
| **Flooding** | No trees to absorb water |
| **Disruption of water cycle** | Less transpiration |

---

## 3. Eutrophication Process

```
1. Excess nitrates/phosphates enter water
         ↓
2. Algal bloom (rapid growth)
         ↓
3. Sunlight blocked
         ↓
4. Underwater plants die
         ↓
5. Bacteria decompose dead material
         ↓
6. Bacteria use up oxygen
         ↓
7. Fish and aquatic organisms die
         ↓
8. Ecosystem collapse
```

### Sources
- Fertilisers
- Sewage
- Animal waste

---

## 4. Global Warming

### Greenhouse Gases
| Gas | Main Source |
|-----|-------------|
| CO₂ | Fossil fuels, deforestation |
| Methane | Livestock, rice, landfill |
| N₂O | Fertilisers |

### Enhanced Greenhouse Effect
1. Sun's radiation warms Earth
2. Earth radiates infrared
3. Greenhouse gases absorb infrared
4. More gases = more warming

### Consequences
- Rising temperatures
- Melting ice caps
- Rising sea levels
- Extreme weather
- Habitat loss

### Solutions
- Renewable energy
- Energy efficiency
- Reduce fossil fuels
- Afforestation

---

## 5. Pollution Types

| Type | Causes | Effects |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Air** | Vehicles, industry | Respiratory disease, acid rain |
| **Water** | Sewage, chemicals | Kills aquatic life |
| **Land** | Landfill, chemicals | Soil contamination |

---

## 6. Invasive Species

**Definition**: Non-native species causing harm

**Examples:**
- Grey squirrel → outcompetes red squirrel
- Japanese knotweed → damages buildings
- Cane toad → toxic to predators

**Problems:**
- Outcompete natives
- Predate on natives
- Introduce disease
- Reduce biodiversity

---

## 7. Conservation

### In-situ (in natural habitat)
- National parks
- Nature reserves
- Protected areas

### Ex-situ (outside habitat)
- Zoos
- Botanic gardens
- Seed banks

### Why conserve?
- Ethical reasons
- Ecological reasons (ecosystem stability)
- Economic reasons (medicine, tourism)

---

## 8. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Deforestation** | Permanent removal of trees |
| **Eutrophication** | Nutrient enrichment → oxygen depletion |
| **Greenhouse effect** | Warming by greenhouse gases |
| **Invasive species** | Harmful non-native species |
| **Sustainable** | Meeting needs without harming future |

---

## 9. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Eutrophication: nutrients → algae → O₂ depletion
2. CO₂ is main greenhouse gas
3. Deforestation reduces CO₂ absorption
4. In-situ = in habitat; Ex-situ = outside
5. Greenhouse effect is NATURAL; enhanced is problem

### Common Mistakes
- Not sequencing eutrophication correctly
- Forgetting greenhouse effect is natural
- Confusing in-situ and ex-situ
- Not naming specific pollutants
- Missing steps in processes
