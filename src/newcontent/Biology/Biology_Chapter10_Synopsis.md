# Chapter 10: Plants and Food - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Photosynthesis Equation

**Word Equation:**
Carbon dioxide + Water → (light + chlorophyll) → Glucose + Oxygen

**Chemical Equation:**
6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂

---

## 2. Leaf Structure

| Structure | Adaptation for Photosynthesis |
|-----------|------------------------------|
| **Broad, flat shape** | Large surface area for light absorption |
| **Thin** | Short distance for gas diffusion |
| **Transparent upper epidermis** | Allows light to reach mesophyll |
| **Waxy cuticle** | Reduces water loss |
| **Palisade mesophyll** | Many chloroplasts near light source |
| **Spongy mesophyll** | Air spaces for gas exchange |
| **Stomata** | Allow CO₂ in, O₂ out |
| **Veins (xylem)** | Bring water to leaf |

---

## 3. Limiting Factors

| Factor | Effect on Rate | Graph Shape |
|--------|---------------|-------------|
| **Light intensity** | Rate increases then plateaus | Curve that flattens |
| **CO₂ concentration** | Rate increases then plateaus | Curve that flattens |
| **Temperature** | Rate increases to optimum then decreases | Bell curve |

### Key Points
- At any time, only ONE factor is limiting
- Increasing a non-limiting factor has no effect
- Identifying the limiting factor is essential

---

## 4. Uses of Glucose

| Use | Form | Purpose |
|-----|------|---------|
| **Respiration** | Glucose | Energy release |
| **Storage** | Starch | Insoluble storage compound |
| **Structure** | Cellulose | Cell walls |
| **Transport** | Sucrose | Phloem transport |
| **Lipids** | Fats/oils | Energy storage (seeds) |
| **Proteins** | Amino acids | Growth, enzymes |

---

## 5. Mineral Requirements

| Mineral | Role | Deficiency Symptoms |
|---------|------|---------------------|
| **Nitrate (NO₃⁻)** | Amino acids, proteins, DNA, chlorophyll | Stunted growth, yellow leaves |
| **Magnesium (Mg²⁺)** | Chlorophyll molecule | Yellowing between veins (chlorosis) |

---

## 6. Testing for Photosynthesis

### Starch Test (Iodine)
1. Boil leaf in water (kills cells)
2. Boil in ethanol (removes chlorophyll)
3. Rinse in water (softens leaf)
4. Add iodine solution
5. **Blue-black = starch present**

### Experiments
| Factor | How to Test | Expected Result |
|--------|-------------|-----------------|
| Light | Cover part of leaf | No starch in covered area |
| CO₂ | Use soda lime | No starch without CO₂ |
| Chlorophyll | Variegated leaf | Starch only in green areas |

---

## 7. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Photosynthesis** | Making glucose from CO₂ and H₂O using light |
| **Chlorophyll** | Green pigment absorbing light energy |
| **Limiting factor** | Factor limiting rate of a process |
| **Stomata** | Pores for gas exchange |
| **Palisade mesophyll** | Photosynthetic cells near leaf surface |

---

## 8. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Chlorophyll is ESSENTIAL - absorbs light energy
2. Plants respire ALL the time; photosynthesise only in light
3. Glucose → Starch for storage (insoluble)
4. Nitrate needed for proteins; Magnesium for chlorophyll
5. Only ONE limiting factor at a time

### Common Mistakes
- Forgetting chlorophyll in equation
- Saying plants "breathe"
- Confusing glucose and starch
- Not explaining WHY factors limit
- Forgetting plants respire at night
