## 1. Major Endocrine Glands

| Gland | Location | Hormone(s) | Main Function |
|-------|----------|------------|---------------|
| Pituitary | Base of brain | GH, TSH, FSH, LH, ADH | Master gland; controls other glands |
| Thyroid | Neck | Thyroxine | Controls metabolic rate |
| Adrenal | Above kidneys | Adrenaline | Fight or flight response |
| Pancreas | Abdomen | Insulin, Glucagon | Blood glucose regulation |
| Ovaries | Female pelvis | Oestrogen, Progesterone | Female reproduction |
| Testes | Scrotum | Testosterone | Male reproduction |

## 2. Blood Glucose Regulation

### When Blood Glucose is HIGH
1. Detected by β-cells in pancreas
2. Insulin released
3. Effects:
   - Glucose taken up by cells
   - Glucose → Glycogen (glycogenesis) in liver
   - Glucose → Fat
4. Blood glucose DECREASES

### When Blood Glucose is LOW
1. Detected by α-cells in pancreas
2. Glucagon released
3. Effects:
   - Glycogen → Glucose (glycogenolysis) in liver
   - Glucose made from amino acids/fats (gluconeogenesis)
4. Blood glucose INCREASES

### Negative Feedback
- High glucose → Insulin → Glucose decreases
- Low glucose → Glucagon → Glucose increases

---

## 3. Diabetes Mellitus

| Feature | Type 1 | Type 2 |
|---------|--------|--------|
| **Cause** | Autoimmune; β-cells destroyed | Insulin resistance |
| **Insulin produced?** | No | Yes, but cells don't respond |
| **Age of onset** | Usually childhood | Usually adulthood |
| **Treatment** | Insulin injections | Diet, exercise, medication |
| **Prevention** | Cannot prevent | Can delay/prevent with lifestyle |

---

## 4. Adrenaline (Fight or Flight)

| Effect | Purpose |
|--------|---------|
| Increased heart rate | More blood to muscles |
| Dilated bronchioles | More oxygen intake |
| Glucose release from liver | Energy for muscles |
| Dilated pupils | Better vision |
| Blood diverted from gut to muscles | Priority to muscles |

---

## 5. Thyroxine

**Source**: Thyroid gland (neck)
**Function**: Controls metabolic rate (BMR)

### Control by Negative Feedback
- Low thyroxine → Hypothalamus releases TRH → Pituitary releases TSH → Thyroid produces thyroxine → Levels rise
- High thyroxine → Inhibits TSH release → Less thyroxine produced → Levels fall

---

## 6. Key Hormones Summary

| Hormone | Produced by | Effect |
|---------|-------------|--------|
| Insulin | Pancreas β-cells | Lowers blood glucose |
| Glucagon | Pancreas α-cells | Raises blood glucose |
| Adrenaline | Adrenal medulla | Fight or flight response |
| Thyroxine | Thyroid | Increases metabolic rate |
| Testosterone | Testes | Male development |
| Oestrogen | Ovaries | Female development |
| ADH | Pituitary | Increases water reabsorption |

---

## 7. Role of the Liver

- Stores glycogen
- Converts glucose → glycogen (when insulin present)
- Converts glycogen → glucose (when glucagon present)
- Produces glucose from amino acids/fats

---

## 8. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Hormone** | Chemical messenger from endocrine gland |
| **Endocrine gland** | Ductless gland secreting into blood |
| **Target organ** | Organ with receptors for specific hormone |
| **Negative feedback** | Change triggers response that reverses change |
| **Glycogenesis** | Glucose → Glycogen |
| **Glycogenolysis** | Glycogen → Glucose |

---

## 9. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Insulin LOWERS glucose; Glucagon RAISES glucose
2. Type 1 = no insulin; Type 2 = insulin resistance
3. Liver stores glycogen and converts glucose ↔ glycogen
4. Blood glucose controlled by negative feedback
5. Adrenaline prepares body for fight or flight

### Common Mistakes
- Confusing glycogen with glucose
- Saying insulin "converts" glucose (it stimulates the process)
- Forgetting to mention liver
- Not mentioning negative feedback
- Mixing up Type 1 and Type 2 diabetes
