## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
Which of the following is NOT one of the seven life processes?

A) Respiration  
B) Photosynthesis  
C) Excretion  
D) Sensitivity  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The seven life processes are Movement, Respiration, Sensitivity, Growth, Reproduction, Excretion, and Nutrition (MRS GREN). Photosynthesis is a process carried out by plants but is not one of the seven fundamental life processes that characterise all living organisms.

---

### Question 2
What is the function of the ribosomes in a cell?

A) To control what enters and leaves the cell  
B) To release energy through respiration  
C) To synthesise proteins  
D) To store genetic material  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Ribosomes are small organelles found in the cytoplasm or attached to the rough endoplasmic reticulum. They are the site of protein synthesis, where amino acids are assembled into proteins according to the instructions from DNA. The cell membrane controls entry/exit (A), mitochondria release energy (B), and the nucleus stores genetic material (D).

---

### Question 3
Which structure is found in plant cells but NOT in animal cells?

A) Mitochondrion  
B) Cell membrane  
C) Chloroplast  
D) Ribosome  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Chloroplasts are organelles containing chlorophyll where photosynthesis occurs. They are found only in plant cells (specifically in green parts). Mitochondria, cell membranes, and ribosomes are found in both plant and animal cells.

---

### Question 4
The movement of water molecules from a region of higher water potential to a region of lower water potential through a partially permeable membrane is called:

A) Diffusion  
B) Osmosis  
C) Active transport  
D) Transpiration  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Osmosis specifically refers to the movement of water molecules through a partially permeable membrane from a region of higher water potential (dilute solution) to lower water potential (concentrated solution). Diffusion (A) does not require a membrane, active transport (C) requires energy and moves against the gradient, and transpiration (D) is the loss of water vapour from plant leaves.

---

### Question 5
Which of the following correctly describes active transport?

A) It requires no energy input  
B) It moves substances from high to low concentration  
C) It requires carrier proteins and ATP  
D) It only occurs in plant cells  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Active transport moves substances against a concentration gradient (from low to high concentration) and requires both carrier proteins in the cell membrane and energy in the form of ATP. It occurs in both plant and animal cells (e.g., mineral uptake in roots, glucose absorption in intestines).

---

### Question 6
What happens to an enzyme when it is denatured?

A) It becomes more active  
B) Its active site changes shape permanently  
C) It is converted to a different protein  
D) It becomes dormant but can be reactivated  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Denaturation causes the enzyme's three-dimensional structure to change, altering the shape of the active site so the substrate can no longer fit. This is permanent and irreversible - the enzyme cannot function again. Denaturation is caused by high temperatures or extreme pH values.

---

### Question 7
Red blood cells are specialised for their function. Which adaptation allows them to carry more oxygen?

A) They have a nucleus  
B) They have a biconcave disc shape  
C) They contain many mitochondria  
D) They have a thick cell wall  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The biconcave disc shape increases the surface area to volume ratio, allowing more efficient oxygen diffusion. Additionally, mature red blood cells have no nucleus, creating more space for haemoglobin. They do not have mitochondria or cell walls.

---

### Question 8
Which enzyme works best in the acidic conditions of the stomach?

A) Amylase  
B) Lipase  
C) Pepsin  
D) Maltase  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Pepsin is a protease enzyme produced in the stomach that works optimally at pH 2 (acidic conditions). The stomach produces hydrochloric acid which creates this acidic environment. Amylase and maltase work best at neutral pH, while pancreatic lipase works in slightly alkaline conditions in the small intestine.

---

### Question 9
A plant cell placed in a concentrated sugar solution will:

A) Absorb water and become turgid  
B) Lose water and become plasmolysed  
C) Remain unchanged  
D) Absorb sugar and burst  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: In a concentrated sugar solution (low water potential), water will move out of the plant cell by osmosis. The vacuole shrinks and the cell membrane pulls away from the cell wall - this is called plasmolysis. The cell becomes flaccid and then plasmolysed. The cell wall prevents the cell from bursting.

---

### Question 10
What is the main difference between egestion and excretion?

A) Egestion removes metabolic waste; excretion removes undigested food  
B) Egestion removes undigested food; excretion removes metabolic waste  
C) They are the same process with different names  
D) Egestion occurs in plants; excretion occurs in animals  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Egestion is the removal of undigested food from the digestive system (faeces). This food never entered the body's cells. Excretion is the removal of metabolic waste products that were produced by cells, such as carbon dioxide from respiration and urea from protein breakdown.

---

### Question 11
Root hair cells are specialised for absorption. Which feature increases the surface area for water uptake?

A) Large nucleus  
B) Many chloroplasts  
C) Long, thin extension (root hair)  
D) Thick cell wall  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The root hair is a long, thin extension of the root hair cell that significantly increases the surface area for water and mineral ion absorption from the soil. Root hair cells do not have chloroplasts (they're underground and don't photosynthesise).

---

### Question 12
Which of the following factors would decrease the rate of diffusion?

A) Increasing the temperature  
B) Increasing the concentration gradient  
C) Increasing the distance over which diffusion occurs  
D) Increasing the surface area  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Diffusion is slower over longer distances because particles take longer to move across. Increasing temperature (A) gives particles more kinetic energy, increasing rate. Steeper concentration gradient (B) and larger surface area (D) both increase the rate of diffusion.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Respiration and breathing are the same process."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Respiration and breathing are different processes. Breathing is the mechanical process of moving air into and out of the lungs. Respiration is the chemical process occurring in all cells where glucose is broken down to release energy. Breathing is part of the respiratory system but is not the same as cellular respiration.

---

### Statement 2
"All enzymes are proteins."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: All enzymes are proteins made up of amino acid chains folded into specific three-dimensional shapes. This is why factors like temperature and pH affect enzyme activity - they affect the protein structure.

---

### Statement 3
"Osmosis requires energy from respiration."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Osmosis is a passive process that does NOT require energy input. Water molecules move down a water potential gradient (from high to low water potential) due to their kinetic energy. Active transport, not osmosis, requires energy from respiration in the form of ATP.

---

### Statement 4
"Animal cells burst when placed in distilled water."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Distilled water has a higher water potential than the cytoplasm of animal cells. Water enters the cell by osmosis. Since animal cells lack a cell wall, there is nothing to prevent the cell from swelling and eventually bursting (lysis).

---

### Statement 5
"The cell wall in plant cells is made of cellulose."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Plant cell walls are composed primarily of cellulose, a complex carbohydrate (polysaccharide) that provides structural support. Cellulose forms strong fibres that give plant cells their rigid shape.

---

### Statement 6
"Active transport can move substances from high concentration to low concentration."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Active transport moves substances from low concentration to high concentration (against the concentration gradient). Movement from high to low concentration is diffusion, which is passive and does not require energy.

---

### Statement 7
"An enzyme can catalyse many different types of reactions."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Enzymes are specific, meaning each enzyme can only catalyse one type of reaction (or reactions with very similar substrates). This specificity is due to the unique shape of the active site, which only fits certain substrate molecules (lock and key model).

---

### Statement 8
"Mitochondria are the site of protein synthesis."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Mitochondria are the site of aerobic respiration, where energy is released from glucose. Protein synthesis occurs at ribosomes, which can be free in the cytoplasm or attached to the rough endoplasmic reticulum.

---

### Statement 9
"A higher temperature always increases enzyme activity."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Temperature increases enzyme activity only up to the optimum temperature (usually around 37-40°C for human enzymes). Above this temperature, the enzyme denatures and activity decreases rapidly. The relationship forms a bell-shaped curve, not a continuously increasing line.

---

### Statement 10
"Guard cells control the opening and closing of stomata."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Guard cells are pairs of specialised cells surrounding stomata (pores) on leaf surfaces. When guard cells take up water and become turgid, they curve apart, opening the stomatal pore. When they lose water and become flaccid, the pore closes. This controls gas exchange and water loss.

---

### Statement 11
"Diffusion requires a partially permeable membrane."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Diffusion does NOT require a membrane. It is the net movement of particles from high to low concentration and can occur in any medium (gas, liquid, or through membranes). Osmosis specifically requires a partially permeable membrane because it involves the selective movement of water molecules.

---

### Statement 12
"Palisade mesophyll cells contain many chloroplasts to maximise photosynthesis."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Palisade mesophyll cells are found in the upper part of the leaf where they receive maximum light. They contain many chloroplasts arranged near the cell surface to capture as much light energy as possible for photosynthesis.

### Question 5 (3 marks)
A student placed potato cylinders in three different solutions:
- Solution A: Distilled water
- Solution B: 0.5 M sugar solution
- Solution C: 1.0 M sugar solution

Predict what would happen to the mass of each potato cylinder and explain your answers.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- Solution A: Mass increases because water enters the potato cells by osmosis (water moves from higher water potential in solution to lower water potential in potato) [1]
- Solution B: Slight increase or no change (depending on water potential of potato) [1]
- Solution C: Mass decreases because water leaves the potato cells by osmosis (water moves from higher water potential in potato to lower water potential in concentrated solution) [1]

### Question 9 (3 marks)
Explain why a plant cell does not burst when placed in distilled water, but an animal cell does.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- In both cells, water enters by osmosis because distilled water has higher water potential [1]
- In animal cells, there is no cell wall so the cell swells and bursts (lysis) [1]
- In plant cells, the strong cellulose cell wall prevents bursting by providing structural support; the cell becomes turgid instead [1]

## Part D: Extended Response Questions (Higher Tier)

### Question 13 (6 marks)
Describe how substances move across cell membranes, including named examples of where each type of transport occurs in the human body.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**

**Diffusion [1-2 marks]:**
- Movement from high to low concentration [1]
- Example: oxygen diffusing from alveoli into blood capillaries; CO₂ diffusing from blood into alveoli [1]

**Osmosis [1-2 marks]:**
- Movement of water from high to low water potential through partially permeable membrane [1]
- Example: water absorption in large intestine; water reabsorption in kidney tubules [1]

**Active transport [1-2 marks]:**
- Movement against concentration gradient using ATP and carrier proteins [1]
- Example: glucose absorption in small intestine; mineral ion uptake in kidney tubules [1]

*Maximum 6 marks for comprehensive answer with all three types described and examples given*

### Question 14 (5 marks)
A student investigated the effect of temperature on the activity of the enzyme amylase. They measured how long it took for amylase to break down starch at different temperatures.

Describe how the student should carry out this investigation to obtain valid results.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- Use water baths at different temperatures (e.g., 10°C, 20°C, 30°C, 40°C, 50°C, 60°C) [1]
- Keep all other variables constant: same volume/concentration of amylase and starch [1]
- Add amylase to starch and start timing [1]
- Test for starch at regular intervals using iodine solution (blue-black = starch present; brown = starch digested) [1]
- Record time taken for starch to disappear completely [1]
- Repeat at each temperature to calculate mean [1]

*Maximum 5 marks*
