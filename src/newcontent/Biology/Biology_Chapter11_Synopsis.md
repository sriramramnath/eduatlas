# Chapter 11: Transport in Plants - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Xylem vs Phloem

| Feature | Xylem | Phloem |
|---------|-------|--------|
| **Transports** | Water + minerals | Sugars + amino acids |
| **Direction** | Upward only | Both directions (source → sink) |
| **Cells** | Dead | Living |
| **End walls** | None | Sieve plates |
| **Nucleus** | No | Companion cells have nucleus |
| **Walls** | Thick, lignified | Thin |
| **Energy needed?** | No (passive) | Yes (active) |

---

## 2. Xylem Adaptations

| Adaptation | Function |
|------------|----------|
| Hollow tubes | Continuous water flow |
| No end walls | No barrier to flow |
| Thick lignified walls | Strength, waterproof |
| Dead cells | Nothing blocks flow |
| Narrow tubes | Capillary action |
| Pits | Water exchange between vessels |

---

## 3. Phloem Structure

- **Sieve tube elements**: Form tubes; sieve plates connect cells
- **Companion cells**: Nucleus, mitochondria; support sieve tubes
- **Plasmodesmata**: Connect companion cells to sieve tubes
- **Living cells**: For active transport

---

## 4. Water Uptake

### Root Hair Cells
- Increase surface area for absorption
- Thin walls for rapid uptake
- Absorb water by osmosis

### Pathways
- **Apoplast**: Through cell walls (main pathway)
- **Symplast**: Through cytoplasm via plasmodesmata
- **Casparian strip**: Blocks apoplast; forces through cytoplasm

---

## 5. Transpiration

### Definition
Loss of water VAPOUR from leaves through STOMATA

### Factors Affecting Rate

| Factor | Effect | Reason |
|--------|--------|--------|
| Temperature ↑ | Rate ↑ | Faster evaporation |
| Humidity ↓ | Rate ↑ | Greater concentration gradient |
| Wind ↑ | Rate ↑ | Removes water vapour |
| Light ↑ | Rate ↑ | Stomata open wider |

### Transpiration Pull
1. Water evaporates from mesophyll
2. Water vapour exits through stomata
3. Creates tension in xylem
4. Pulls water up from roots

---

## 6. Translocation

### Definition
Transport of ORGANIC substances (sucrose) in PHLOEM

### Source and Sink
- **Source**: Where sugars made/released (leaves, storage organs)
- **Sink**: Where sugars used/stored (roots, fruits, growing tips)

### Process
1. Sucrose loaded into phloem (active transport)
2. Water enters by osmosis → high pressure
3. Sucrose unloaded at sink
4. Water leaves → low pressure
5. Flow from high to low pressure (mass flow)

---

## 7. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Xylem** | Tissue transporting water upward |
| **Phloem** | Tissue transporting sugars |
| **Transpiration** | Water vapour loss through stomata |
| **Translocation** | Sugar transport in phloem |
| **Source** | Where sugars made/released |
| **Sink** | Where sugars used/stored |
| **Cohesion** | Water-water attraction |
| **Adhesion** | Water-xylem wall attraction |

---

## 8. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Xylem = dead; Phloem = living
2. Xylem = water UP; Phloem = sugars BOTH ways
3. Transpiration = water VAPOUR through STOMATA
4. Translocation requires ENERGY (active loading)
5. Root hair cells increase surface area

### Common Mistakes
- Confusing xylem and phloem
- Saying "water loss" not "water vapour loss"
- Forgetting translocation needs energy
- Not mentioning root hair cells
- Confusing transpiration and translocation
