# Chapter 12: Chemical Coordination in Plants - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Plant Hormones Overview

| Hormone | Function |
|---------|----------|
| **Auxin (IAA)** | Cell elongation; tropisms; apical dominance |
| **Gibberellins** | Stem elongation; seed germination |
| **Cytokinins** | Cell division; delay ageing |
| **Abscisic acid** | Closes stomata; seed dormancy |
| **Ethylene** | Fruit ripening |

---

## 2. Tropisms

| Tropism | Stimulus | Shoot Response | Root Response |
|---------|----------|----------------|---------------|
| **Phototropism** | Light | Positive (towards) | Negative (away) |
| **Gravitropism** | Gravity | Negative (upward) | Positive (downward) |

---

## 3. Phototropism Mechanism

1. Light hits one side of shoot tip
2. Auxin moves to SHADED side
3. More auxin = more cell elongation (in shoots)
4. Shaded side grows faster
5. Shoot bends TOWARDS light

---

## 4. Gravitropism Mechanism

**In Roots:**
1. Statoliths settle to bottom
2. Auxin moves to LOWER side
3. More auxin = LESS cell elongation (in roots)
4. Upper side grows faster
5. Root bends DOWNWARDS

**In Shoots:**
1. Auxin moves to lower side
2. More auxin = MORE cell elongation (in shoots)
3. Lower side grows faster
4. Shoot bends UPWARDS

---

## 5. Key Difference

| Tissue | Effect of HIGH auxin |
|--------|---------------------|
| **Shoot cells** | MORE elongation |
| **Root cells** | LESS elongation |

---

## 6. Commercial Uses

| Use | Hormone | Application |
|-----|---------|-------------|
| **Rooting powder** | Auxin | Root cuttings faster |
| **Selective weedkiller** | Synthetic auxin | Kills broad-leaved weeds |
| **Fruit ripening** | Ethylene | Controlled ripening of fruit |
| **Seedless fruit** | Auxin/gibberellin | Parthenocarpy |
| **Breaking dormancy** | Gibberellin | Stimulate germination |

---

## 7. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Tropism** | Growth movement towards/away from stimulus |
| **Phototropism** | Response to light |
| **Gravitropism** | Response to gravity |
| **Auxin** | Hormone controlling cell elongation |
| **Apical dominance** | Shoot tip suppresses side buds |

---

## 8. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Plants use hormones, NOT nerves
2. Auxin promotes shoot elongation; inhibits root elongation
3. Light causes auxin to move to SHADED side
4. Gravitropism: auxin to LOWER side
5. Same hormone can have opposite effects

### Common Mistakes
- Saying plants have nervous system
- Confusing phototropism with gravitropism
- Forgetting auxin inhibits root growth
- Not explaining differential growth
- Wrong direction of auxin movement
