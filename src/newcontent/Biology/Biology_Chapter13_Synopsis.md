# Chapter 13: Reproduction in Plants - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Asexual vs Sexual Reproduction

| Feature | Asexual | Sexual |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Parents | One | Two (usually) |
| Gametes | Not involved | Fuse during fertilisation |
| Offspring | Genetically identical | Genetically different |
| Speed | Faster | Slower |
| Variation | None | High |

---

## 2. Parts of a Flower

| Part | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Sepals** | Protect bud |
| **Petals** | Attract pollinators |
| **Anther** | Produces pollen (male gametes) |
| **Filament** | Holds anther |
| **Stigma** | Receives pollen |
| **Style** | Connects stigma to ovary |
| **Ovary** | Contains ovules (female gametes) |

---

## 3. Pollination

### Definition
Transfer of pollen from **anther to stigma**

### Types
| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Self-pollination** | Same flower or same plant |
| **Cross-pollination** | Different plant of same species |

---

## 4. Insect vs Wind Pollination

| Feature | Insect-pollinated | Wind-pollinated |
|---------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Petals | Large, bright | Small/absent |
| Scent | Present | Absent |
| Nectar | Present | Absent |
| Pollen | Sticky, spiky | Light, smooth |
| Quantity | Small | Large |
| Stigma | Inside flower | Exposed, feathery |
| Examples | Rose, orchid | Grass, maize |

---

## 5. Fertilisation Process

1. Pollen lands on stigma
2. Pollen tube grows down style
3. Enters ovule through micropyle
4. Male nucleus fuses with egg cell
5. Forms zygote → embryo
6. Ovule → seed; Ovary → fruit

---

## 6. Seed Structure

| Part | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Testa** | Protective seed coat |
| **Cotyledons** | Food store |
| **Plumule** | Embryonic shoot |
| **Radicle** | Embryonic root |
| **Micropyle** | Water entry point |
| **Hilum** | Attachment scar |

---

## 7. Germination Conditions

| Condition | Why Needed |
|-----------|------------|
| **Water** | Activates enzymes; rehydrates seed |
| **Oxygen** | Aerobic respiration |
| **Temperature** | Enzyme activity |

**Note**: Light is NOT essential for most seeds!

---

## 8. Germination Process

1. Water absorbed (imbibition)
2. Enzymes activated
3. Stored food broken down
4. Radicle emerges (root)
5. Plumule emerges (shoot)
6. First leaves photosynthesise

---

## 9. Seed Dispersal Methods

| Method | Adaptation | Examples |
|--------|------------|----------|
| **Wind** | Wings, hairs | Dandelion, sycamore |
| **Animals (external)** | Hooks, spines | Burdock |
| **Animals (internal)** | Fleshy fruit | Berries |
| **Water** | Buoyant | Coconut |
| **Self** | Explosive | Pea pods |

### Why Disperse?
- Reduce competition
- Colonise new areas
- Avoid overcrowding

---

## 10. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Pollination** | Pollen transfer anther → stigma |
| **Fertilisation** | Fusion of male + female gametes |
| **Germination** | Seed → seedling development |
| **Dispersal** | Seeds spread from parent |

---

## 11. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Pollination ≠ Fertilisation
2. Wind-pollinated: no petals, no nectar, light pollen
3. Seeds need water, oxygen, warmth (NOT soil!)
4. Testa = seed coat; Fruit = ovary wall
5. Cotyledons = food store in seed

### Common Mistakes
- Confusing pollination and fertilisation
- Saying wind-pollinated flowers have petals
- Saying seeds need soil to germinate
- Not linking adaptations to functions
- Confusing testa and fruit
