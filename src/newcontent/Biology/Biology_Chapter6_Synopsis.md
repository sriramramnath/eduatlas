# Chapter 6: Coordination - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Nervous vs Hormonal Coordination

| Feature | Nervous System | Hormonal System |
|---------|---------------|-----------------|
| Signal type | Electrical impulses | Chemical (hormones) |
| Transmission | Along nerves | Through bloodstream |
| Speed | Fast | Slow |
| Duration | Short | Long |
| Area affected | Localised | Widespread |
| Example | Reflex action | Insulin/glucose regulation |

---

## 2. Central Nervous System (CNS)

### Brain Parts

| Part | Function |
|------|----------|
| **Cerebrum** | Conscious activities: thinking, memory, reasoning |
| **Cerebellum** | Balance, posture, coordination |
| **Medulla** | Involuntary activities: heart rate, breathing |

### Spinal Cord
- Carries impulses between brain and body
- Contains reflex centres
- Protected by vertebrae

---

## 3. Types of Neurons

| Type | Function | Direction |
|------|----------|-----------|
| **Sensory** | Carry impulses from receptors | To CNS |
| **Relay** | Connect sensory to motor neurons | Within CNS |
| **Motor** | Carry impulses to effectors | From CNS |

---

## 4. Synapse

**Definition**: Junction between two neurons

**How it works**:
1. Impulse arrives at presynaptic ending
2. Neurotransmitter released into synaptic cleft
3. Neurotransmitter diffuses across gap
4. Binds to receptors on postsynaptic membrane
5. New impulse generated

**Key points**:
- One-way transmission
- Neurotransmitter is chemical, not electrical
- Enzymes break down neurotransmitter

---

## 5. Reflex Arc

**Sequence**: Stimulus → Receptor → Sensory neuron → Relay neuron → Motor neuron → Effector → Response

### Example: Hot Object Reflex
1. Heat detected by receptors in skin
2. Sensory neuron carries impulse to spinal cord
3. Relay neuron in spinal cord
4. Motor neuron carries impulse to muscle
5. Muscle contracts, hand withdrawn
6. (Brain informed but NOT involved in reflex)

| Structure | Function |
|-----------|----------|

| **Iris** | Controls pupil size |
| **Pupil** | Allows light to enter |
| **Lens** | Focuses light on retina |

## 7. Pupil Reflex

| Condition | Circular Muscles | Radial Muscles | Pupil Size |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|------------|
| **Bright light** | Contract | Relax | Constricts (smaller) |
| **Dim light** | Relax | Contract | Dilates (larger) |

## 10. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Stimulus** | Detectable change in environment |
| **Receptor** | Detects stimuli |
| **Effector** | Produces response (muscle/gland) |
| **Synapse** | Junction between neurons |
| **Reflex** | Rapid, automatic response |
| **Hormone** | Chemical messenger from endocrine gland |

---

## 11. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Reflex = rapid, automatic, involuntary
2. Sensory neurons → CNS; Motor neurons → from CNS
3. Synapses use neurotransmitters (chemical)
4. Bright light: circular muscles contract, pupil constricts
5. Near objects: ciliary muscles contract, lens becomes rounder

### Common Mistakes
- Confusing sensory and motor neurons
- Saying electrical impulse crosses synapse
- Forgetting reflexes are involuntary
- Mixing up circular and radial muscles
- Saying brain controls reflexes (spinal cord does)
