## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
Where are sperm cells produced?

A) Epididymis  
B) Prostate gland  
C) Testes  
D) Seminal vesicles  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Sperm cells are produced in the testes through the process of spermatogenesis. The epididymis stores mature sperm, the prostate gland produces seminal fluid, and seminal vesicles produce fluid rich in fructose. The testes also produce testosterone.

---

### Question 2
What is the function of the acrosome in a sperm cell?

A) To provide energy for movement  
B) To contain enzymes that digest the egg's protective layers  
C) To propel the sperm forward  
D) To store the chromosomes  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The acrosome is a cap-like structure at the head of the sperm containing digestive enzymes (including hyaluronidase). These enzymes are released when the sperm reaches the egg and help the sperm penetrate the egg's protective layers (zona pellucida and corona radiata).

---

### Question 3
Where does fertilisation normally occur in humans?

A) Uterus  
B) Ovary  
C) Oviduct (Fallopian tube)  
D) Vagina  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Fertilisation typically occurs in the ampulla (widest part) of the oviduct (Fallopian tube). The egg is released from the ovary during ovulation and is swept into the oviduct, where it may encounter sperm. Implantation occurs in the uterus.

---

### Question 4
Which hormone triggers ovulation?

A) FSH  
B) Oestrogen  
C) LH  
D) Progesterone  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Luteinising hormone (LH) triggers ovulation. A surge in LH levels around day 14 of the menstrual cycle causes the mature follicle to rupture and release the egg from the ovary. FSH stimulates follicle development, oestrogen thickens the uterine lining, and progesterone maintains the lining after ovulation.

---

### Question 5
The placenta:

A) Allows maternal and fetal blood to mix  
B) Allows exchange of materials between mother and fetus  
C) Produces sperm  
D) Stores eggs  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The placenta allows exchange of materials (oxygen, nutrients, carbon dioxide, wastes) between mother and fetus through diffusion. Importantly, maternal and fetal blood do NOT mix - they remain separated by a thin barrier. The placenta also produces hormones to maintain pregnancy.

---

### Question 6
Which structure carries oxygenated blood from the placenta to the fetus?

A) Umbilical artery  
B) Umbilical vein  
C) Aorta  
D) Vena cava  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The umbilical vein carries oxygenated blood and nutrients from the placenta to the fetus. The umbilical artery carries deoxygenated blood and waste products from the fetus to the placenta. This is opposite to the naming convention in the rest of the body, where arteries carry oxygenated blood away from the heart.

---

### Question 7
The contraceptive pill prevents pregnancy mainly by:

A) Killing sperm  
B) Preventing ovulation  
C) Blocking the cervix  
D) Preventing implantation  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The combined oral contraceptive pill contains oestrogen and progesterone, which prevent ovulation by inhibiting the release of FSH from the pituitary gland. Without FSH, follicles do not develop, and the LH surge needed for ovulation does not occur. The pill also thickens cervical mucus and thins the uterine lining.

---

### Question 8
How many chromosomes does a human zygote contain?

A) 23  
B) 46  
C) 92  
D) 12  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A human zygote contains 46 chromosomes (the diploid number). It is formed by the fusion of a sperm (with 23 chromosomes, haploid) and an egg (with 23 chromosomes, haploid). The zygote therefore has the full complement of 46 chromosomes - 23 from each parent.

---

### Question 9
During which days of a 28-day menstrual cycle does menstruation typically occur?

A) Days 1-5  
B) Days 7-14  
C) Days 14-21  
D) Days 21-28  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: Menstruation typically occurs during days 1-5 of the menstrual cycle. This is when the thickened uterine lining (endometrium) breaks down and is shed through the vagina. Ovulation occurs around day 14, and the luteal phase follows from days 15-28.

---

### Question 10
What is the function of progesterone in the menstrual cycle?

A) To stimulate follicle development  
B) To trigger ovulation  
C) To maintain the uterine lining  
D) To cause menstruation  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Progesterone, produced by the corpus luteum after ovulation, maintains the thickened uterine lining (endometrium) in preparation for possible implantation of an embryo. If pregnancy does not occur, progesterone levels fall, causing the uterine lining to break down (menstruation).

---

### Question 11
IVF (In Vitro Fertilisation) involves:

A) Fertilisation inside the body  
B) Fertilisation outside the body  
C) Using donor sperm only  
D) Natural conception  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: In Vitro Fertilisation (IVF) involves fertilisation outside the body ("in vitro" means "in glass"). Eggs are collected from the ovaries and mixed with sperm in a laboratory dish. After fertilisation and early embryo development, the embryo is transferred to the uterus for implantation.

---

### Question 12
The corpus luteum produces:

A) FSH  
B) LH  
C) Oestrogen and progesterone  
D) Testosterone  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The corpus luteum (formed from the ruptured follicle after ovulation) produces progesterone and some oestrogen. These hormones maintain the uterine lining. If pregnancy doesn't occur, the corpus luteum degenerates, causing hormone levels to drop and menstruation to begin. FSH and LH are produced by the pituitary gland.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Sperm are produced in the epididymis."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Sperm are produced in the TESTES (specifically in the seminiferous tubules). The epididymis is where sperm are stored and mature after production. Sperm take about 74 days to develop in the testes and then spend about 12 days maturing in the epididymis.

---

### Statement 2
"Fertilisation results in a diploid zygote."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Fertilisation involves the fusion of two haploid gametes (sperm and egg, each with 23 chromosomes). The resulting zygote has 46 chromosomes (23 pairs), making it diploid. This zygote will divide by mitosis to form an embryo.

---

### Statement 3
"The umbilical artery carries oxygenated blood to the fetus."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: The umbilical ARTERY carries deoxygenated blood FROM the fetus TO the placenta. The umbilical VEIN carries oxygenated blood FROM the placenta TO the fetus. This is opposite to the normal naming convention, where arteries carry blood away from the heart.

---

### Statement 4
"Oestrogen causes the uterine lining to thicken."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: During the follicular phase of the menstrual cycle, oestrogen produced by the developing follicle causes the endometrium (uterine lining) to thicken and become more vascular, preparing it for possible implantation of an embryo.

---

### Statement 5
"The placenta allows maternal and fetal blood to mix."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Maternal and fetal blood do NOT mix in the placenta. They remain separated by a thin barrier. Materials (oxygen, nutrients, wastes) diffuse across this barrier, but blood cells do not cross. This separation prevents immune reactions and protects the fetus from some pathogens.

---

### Statement 6
"LH stimulates follicle development in the ovary."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: FSH (Follicle-Stimulating Hormone) stimulates follicle development in the ovary. LH (Luteinising Hormone) triggers ovulation and causes the ruptured follicle to develop into the corpus luteum. Both hormones are produced by the pituitary gland.

---

### Statement 7
"A vasectomy involves cutting the sperm ducts."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: A vasectomy is a surgical sterilisation procedure for males. It involves cutting and sealing the sperm ducts (vas deferens), preventing sperm from being ejaculated. Sperm are still produced but cannot leave the reproductive system and are reabsorbed by the body.

---

### Statement 8
"The amniotic fluid protects the fetus from physical damage."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: The amniotic fluid surrounds the fetus in the amniotic sac, providing a protective cushion against physical shocks and impacts. It also maintains a constant temperature, allows the fetus to move, and supports lung development as the fetus "breathes" the fluid.

---

### Statement 9
"Human eggs are motile and swim towards sperm."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Human eggs are NOT motile - they cannot swim. After ovulation, the egg is moved along the oviduct towards the uterus by the beating of cilia on the cells lining the oviduct and by muscular contractions. Sperm must swim towards the egg.

---

### Statement 10
"Progesterone inhibits the release of FSH and LH."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: High levels of progesterone (and oestrogen) during the luteal phase inhibit the release of FSH and LH from the pituitary gland through negative feedback. This prevents the development of new follicles while the corpus luteum is active.

---

### Statement 11
"The scrotum keeps testes cooler than body temperature."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: The scrotum holds the testes outside the body cavity, keeping them about 2-3°C cooler than core body temperature. This lower temperature is essential for normal sperm production. Muscles in the scrotum can raise or lower the testes to regulate temperature.

---

### Statement 12
"Ovulation occurs on day 28 of the menstrual cycle."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Ovulation typically occurs around DAY 14 of a 28-day menstrual cycle. This is triggered by the LH surge. Day 28 is typically the last day before menstruation begins again. The timing of ovulation is important for fertility awareness and contraception.
