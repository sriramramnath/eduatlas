## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What is the function of the anther in a flower?

A) To receive pollen grains  
B) To produce and store pollen grains  
C) To attract insects  
D) To protect the flower bud  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The anther is the male part of the stamen that produces and stores pollen grains. The anther is supported by the filament. Pollen contains the male gametes. The stigma receives pollen, petals attract insects, and sepals protect the bud.

---

### Question 2
Cross-pollination occurs when:

A) Pollen is transferred from anther to stigma of the same flower  
B) Pollen is transferred between flowers of different plants of the same species  
C) Pollen is transferred between different species  
D) Pollen is transferred from stigma to anther  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Cross-pollination is the transfer of pollen from the anther of a flower on one plant to the stigma of a flower on a DIFFERENT plant of the SAME species. This introduces genetic variation. Transfer within the same flower or plant is self-pollination.

---

### Question 3
Which feature is characteristic of wind-pollinated flowers?

A) Brightly coloured petals  
B) Scent  
C) Large quantities of light pollen  
D) Nectar  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Wind-pollinated flowers produce large quantities of light, smooth pollen that can be carried by the wind. They do not need to attract insects, so they lack bright petals, scent, and nectar. Insect-pollinated flowers have these features.

---

### Question 4
After fertilisation, the ovule develops into:

A) A fruit  
B) A seed  
C) A flower  
D) A leaf  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: After fertilisation, the ovule develops into a seed. The zygote within the ovule develops into an embryo, and the ovule wall becomes the seed coat (testa). The ovary develops into the fruit.

---

### Question 5
Which condition is NOT essential for seed germination?

A) Water  
B) Oxygen  
C) Soil  
D) Suitable temperature  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Seeds can germinate without soil (e.g., on moist paper towels). The essential conditions are water (to rehydrate the seed and activate enzymes), oxygen (for aerobic respiration), and a suitable temperature (for enzyme activity). Light is also not essential for most seeds.

---

### Question 6
The radicle is:

A) The embryonic root  
B) The embryonic shoot  
C) The seed coat  
D) The food store  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: The radicle is the embryonic root that develops into the root system of the new plant. It is the first structure to emerge during germination. The plumule is the embryonic shoot, the testa is the seed coat, and cotyledons are the food stores.

---

### Question 7
Asexual reproduction in plants produces:

A) Genetically identical offspring  
B) Genetically different offspring  
C) Offspring with twice the chromosome number  
D) Offspring with half the chromosome number  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: Asexual reproduction involves only one parent and does not involve the fusion of gametes. Therefore, the offspring are genetically identical clones of the parent. There is no genetic variation in asexually produced offspring.

---

### Question 8
Insect-pollinated flowers typically have:

A) Small, dull petals  
B) No scent  
C) Sticky or spiky pollen  
D) Exposed anthers  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Insect-pollinated flowers have sticky or spiky pollen that adheres to the bodies of visiting insects. They also typically have large, bright petals, scent, and nectar to attract insects. Their anthers are inside the flower, not exposed.

---

### Question 9
Double fertilisation in flowering plants involves:

A) Two pollen grains fertilising one ovule  
B) One male nucleus fusing with egg cell, another with polar nuclei  
C) Fertilisation occurring twice in the same flower  
D) Two eggs being fertilised  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Double fertilisation is unique to flowering plants. One male nucleus fuses with the egg cell to form the zygote (which becomes the embryo), while another male nucleus fuses with the polar nuclei to form the endosperm (food store for the developing embryo).

---

### Question 10
Seed dispersal is important because it:

A) Allows seeds to find nutrients  
B) Reduces competition between parent and offspring  
C) Helps seeds avoid predators  
D) Increases the seed size  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Seed dispersal is important primarily because it moves offspring away from the parent plant, reducing competition for resources like light, water, and nutrients. It also allows colonisation of new areas and can help avoid predators and pathogens near the parent.

---

### Question 11
The testa of a seed:

A) Stores food  
B) Protects the embryo  
C) Produces enzymes  
D) Absorbs water  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The testa is the hard, protective seed coat that surrounds and protects the embryo inside the seed. It prevents damage and drying out. Cotyledons store food, the embryo produces enzymes during germination, and the micropyle is important for water absorption.

---

### Question 12
During germination, the first structure to emerge from the seed is:

A) The plumule  
B) The radicle  
C) The cotyledons  
D) The testa  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The radicle (embryonic root) is the first structure to emerge from the seed during germination. It grows downward (positive gravitropism) to anchor the seedling and absorb water and minerals. The plumule emerges later.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Pollination is the same as fertilisation."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Pollination is the TRANSFER of pollen from anther to stigma. Fertilisation is the FUSION of male and female gametes to form a zygote. Pollination must occur BEFORE fertilisation can happen. They are two separate stages in the reproductive process.

---

### Statement 2
"Wind-pollinated flowers have large, brightly coloured petals."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Wind-pollinated flowers have SMALL or NO petals, and they are NOT brightly coloured. They don't need to attract insects. Insect-pollinated flowers have large, brightly coloured petals to attract pollinators.

---

### Statement 3
"After fertilisation, the ovary develops into a fruit."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: After fertilisation, the ovary wall develops into the fruit, which protects the seeds and often aids in their dispersal. The ovules inside the ovary develop into seeds. This is why fruits contain seeds.

---

### Statement 4
"Self-pollination produces more genetic variation than cross-pollination."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: CROSS-pollination produces MORE genetic variation than self-pollination. Cross-pollination combines genetic material from two different plants, creating variation. Self-pollination uses genetic material from only one plant, producing offspring identical or very similar to the parent.

---

### Statement 5
"Seeds need light to germinate."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Most seeds do NOT need light to germinate. The essential conditions are WATER, OXYGEN, and a suitable TEMPERATURE. Some seeds are actually inhibited by light, while others are indifferent. Seeds can germinate in darkness.

---

### Statement 6
"The plumule develops into the root of the new plant."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: The plumule develops into the SHOOT (stem and leaves) of the new plant. The RADICLE develops into the root. Remember: Plumule = Plant above ground (shoot); Radicle = Root below ground.

---

### Statement 7
"Dandelion seeds are dispersed by wind."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Dandelion seeds have parachute-like structures (pappus) of fine hairs that catch the wind, allowing them to be carried long distances from the parent plant. This is an adaptation for wind dispersal.

---

### Statement 8
"Sticky pollen is an adaptation for wind pollination."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Sticky pollen is an adaptation for INSECT pollination - it adheres to insect bodies. Wind-pollinated plants produce LIGHT, DRY, SMOOTH pollen that can be carried easily by air currents.

---

### Statement 9
"Asexual reproduction produces clones."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Asexual reproduction involves only one parent and no fusion of gametes. All offspring are genetically identical to the parent and to each other - they are clones. This is because there is no mixing of genetic material.

---

### Statement 10
"Cotyledons store food for the developing embryo."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Cotyledons are seed leaves that store food reserves (starch, proteins, oils) for the developing embryo. During germination, these stored nutrients are broken down and used by the growing seedling until it can photosynthesise.

---

### Statement 11
"Wind-pollinated flowers produce nectar."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Wind-pollinated flowers do NOT produce nectar. Nectar is produced to attract insect pollinators. Wind-pollinated flowers rely on wind for pollen transfer and have no need to attract insects, so they don't produce nectar or scent.

---

### Statement 12
"Germination requires enzymes to break down stored food."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: During germination, enzymes are activated to break down stored food reserves. Amylase breaks down starch to sugars, proteases break down proteins to amino acids, and lipases break down fats. These products are used for respiration and building new cells.
