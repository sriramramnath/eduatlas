## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What is the genotype of a carrier for a recessive allele?

A) BB  
B) Bb  
C) bb  
D) BB or bb  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A carrier is heterozygous (Bb), meaning they carry one dominant allele and one recessive allele. They do not show the recessive trait because the dominant allele masks it, but they can pass the recessive allele to offspring.

---

### Question 2
In a cross between two heterozygous parents (Bb × Bb), what is the expected phenotypic ratio?

A) 1:1  
B) 2:1  
C) 3:1  
D) 1:2:1  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: A heterozygous cross (Bb × Bb) produces offspring in the ratio: 1 BB : 2 Bb : 1 bb. The phenotypic ratio is 3 dominant (BB + 2Bb) : 1 recessive (bb), giving 3:1.

---

### Question 3
A homozygous recessive individual has the genotype:

A) BB  
B) Bb  
C) bb  
D) BB or bb  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Homozygous means having two identical alleles. Recessive alleles are represented by lowercase letters. Therefore, homozygous recessive is bb. BB would be homozygous dominant, and Bb would be heterozygous.

---

### Question 4
What determines sex in humans?

A) The X chromosome only  
B) The Y chromosome only  
C) The combination of sex chromosomes  
D) The environment  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Sex in humans is determined by the combination of sex chromosomes. Females have XX, males have XY. The Y chromosome carries the gene that triggers male development.

---

### Question 5
In codominance:

A) One allele is dominant over the other  
B) Both alleles are equally expressed  
C) Neither allele is expressed  
D) Only the recessive allele is expressed  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: In codominance, both alleles are equally dominant and both are expressed in the phenotype. An example is blood group AB, where both A and B antigens are produced.

---

### Question 6
An X-linked recessive trait is more common in males because:

A) Males have two X chromosomes  
B) Males have only one X chromosome  
C) Males have a Y chromosome  
D) Males are genetically stronger  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Males have only one X chromosome, so they express X-linked recessive traits if they have one copy of the allele. Females need two copies to express the trait. Males cannot be carriers.

---

### Question 7
A male with blood group O marries a female with blood group AB. What blood groups could their children have?

A) A and B only  
B) A, B, and AB  
C) O only  
D) A, B, AB, and O  

**Answer: A**

**Answer**: The male is ii (group O), the female is IᴬIᴮ (group AB). All children will be Iᴬi or Iᴮi, giving blood groups A or B only.

---

### Question 8
What is the probability of having a male child?

A) 25%  
B) 50%  
C) 75%  
D) 100%  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: When XX (female) crosses with XY (male), the offspring can be XX (female) or XY (male). The probability is 50% male and 50% female.

---

### Question 9
If two parents are carriers for a recessive genetic disorder (Aa × Aa), what is the probability their child will have the disorder?

A) 0%  
B) 25%  
C) 50%  
D) 75%  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Aa × Aa produces offspring: 1 AA : 2 Aa : 1 aa. Only aa (25%) will have the disorder. 50% will be carriers (Aa), and 25% will be completely unaffected (AA).

---

### Question 10
A woman who is a carrier for haemophilia (XᴴXʰ) marries a normal man (XᴴY). What proportion of their sons will have haemophilia?

A) 0%  
B) 25%  
C) 50%  
D) 100%  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The cross XᴴXʰ × XᴴY produces sons: XᴴY (normal) and XʰY (affected). 50% of sons will have haemophilia. Daughters will be normal or carriers.

---

### Question 11
What does phenotype mean?

A) The genetic makeup  
B) The observable characteristics  
C) The alleles present  
D) The chromosomes present  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Phenotype refers to the observable physical characteristics of an organism - what you can see (e.g., brown eyes, tall height). Genotype is the genetic makeup (alleles present).

---

### Question 12
A heterozygous individual:

A) Has two identical alleles  
B) Has two different alleles  
C) Shows recessive phenotype  
D) Cannot reproduce  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Heterozygous means having two different alleles for a gene (e.g., Bb). The dominant allele is expressed in the phenotype. Homozygous individuals have two identical alleles.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"A dominant allele is always expressed in the phenotype when present."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: A dominant allele is expressed whenever it is present, whether in homozygous (BB) or heterozygous (Bb) form. Only homozygous recessive (bb) individuals show the recessive phenotype.

---

### Statement 2
"Homozygous means having two different alleles."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Homozygous means having TWO IDENTICAL alleles (e.g., BB or bb). HETEROZYGOUS means having two different alleles (e.g., Bb).

---

### Statement 3
"Two unaffected parents cannot have an affected child for a dominant trait."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: For an autosomal dominant trait, if both parents are unaffected (recessive homozygous), they cannot pass a dominant allele to their children. All children will be unaffected.

---

### Statement 4
"Fathers pass X-linked traits to their sons."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Fathers DO NOT pass X-linked traits to their sons. Fathers pass their Y chromosome to sons, not their X chromosome. X-linked traits pass from father to daughter (via X chromosome).

---

### Statement 5
"In codominance, the heterozygote shows a blend of both traits."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: In codominance, the heterozygote shows BOTH traits separately, not a blend. Blood group AB shows both A and B antigens, not a blend. Incomplete dominance shows a blend.

---

### Statement 6
"A 3:1 phenotypic ratio indicates both parents were heterozygous."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: A cross between two heterozygotes (Bb × Bb) produces offspring in the ratio 3 dominant phenotype : 1 recessive phenotype. This 3:1 ratio is characteristic of monohybrid heterozygous crosses.

---

### Statement 7
"Males can be carriers for X-linked recessive disorders."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Males CANNOT be carriers for X-linked recessive disorders. They have only one X chromosome, so they either have the disorder or they don't. Only females (with two X chromosomes) can be carriers.

---

### Statement 8
"The genotype determines the phenotype completely."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: The phenotype is influenced by BOTH genotype AND environment. For example, plant height depends on genes AND growing conditions (water, nutrients, light).

---

### Statement 9
"Blood group O is recessive."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Blood group O is caused by the recessive allele i. A person with blood group O has the genotype ii. This is the only homozygous recessive blood group.

---

### Statement 10
"A carrier has the genotype Bb for a recessive trait."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: A carrier is heterozygous (Bb), carrying one dominant and one recessive allele. They do not show the recessive trait but can pass the recessive allele to offspring.

---

### Statement 11
"Colour blindness is an X-linked recessive condition."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Red-green colour blindness is caused by a recessive allele on the X chromosome. It is more common in males because they only need one copy of the allele to be affected.

---

### Statement 12
"In a pedigree chart, a shaded symbol represents an unaffected individual."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: In a pedigree chart, a SHADED symbol represents an AFFECTED individual. Unaffected individuals are shown with unshaded (empty) symbols.

### Question 7 (3 marks)
A couple are both carriers for cystic fibrosis (Ff). What is the probability their child will be a carrier?

**Mark Scheme Answer:**

- Genotypes: 1 FF : 2 Ff : 1 ff [1]

What is a sex-linked trait?

Explain the difference between phenotype and genotype.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- Genotype: genetic makeup/alleles present [1]

- Example: Bb (genotype) gives brown eyes (phenotype) [1]

- Ratio: 1 female (XX) : 1 male (XY) [1]

Why can't males be carriers for X-linked disorders?

### Question 14 (5 marks)
Explain X-linked inheritance using haemophilia as an example.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- Gene on X chromosome [1]
- Recessive allele causes haemophilia [1]
- Males: XʰY = affected, XᴴY = normal [1]
- Females: XʰXʰ = affected, XᴴXʰ = carrier [1]
- More males affected than females [1]

### Question 15 (4 marks)
A woman with blood group A (Iᴬi) marries a man with blood group B (Iᴮi). Show the possible blood groups of their children.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- Punnett square: Iᴬi × Iᴮi [1]
- Possible genotypes: IᴬIᴮ, Iᴬi, Iᴮi, ii [1]
- Possible phenotypes: A, B, AB, O [2]
