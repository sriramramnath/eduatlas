# Chapter 5: Blood and Circulation - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Double Circulation

| Circuit | Pathway | Blood Type |
|---------|---------|------------|
| **Pulmonary** | Heart → Lungs → Heart | Deoxygenated → Oxygenated |
| **Systemic** | Heart → Body → Heart | Oxygenated → Deoxygenated |

**Advantages**: Higher pressure to body; oxygenated blood gets extra "boost"

---

## 2. Heart Structure

### Chambers and Functions

| Chamber | Receives blood from | Sends blood to | Wall thickness |
|---------|--------------------|----------------|----------------|
| Right atrium | Vena cava (body) | Right ventricle | Thin |
| Right ventricle | Right atrium | Pulmonary artery → Lungs | Medium |
| Left atrium | Pulmonary vein (lungs) | Left ventricle | Thin |
| Left ventricle | Left atrium | Aorta → Body | Thickest |

### Blood Vessels Connected to Heart

| Vessel | Carries | Direction |
|--------|---------|-----------|
| Vena cava | Deoxygenated | Body → Right atrium |
| Pulmonary artery | Deoxygenated | Right ventricle → Lungs |
| Pulmonary vein | Oxygenated | Lungs → Left atrium |
| Aorta | Oxygenated | Left ventricle → Body |

### Valves

| Valve | Location | Function |
|-------|----------|----------|
| Tricuspid | Right atrioventricular | Prevents backflow to right atrium |
| Bicuspid (mitral) | Left atrioventricular | Prevents backflow to left atrium |
| Semilunar | Pulmonary artery & Aorta | Prevents backflow to ventricles |

---

## 3. The Cardiac Cycle

| Phase | What Happens | Valves |
|-------|--------------|--------|
| **Atrial systole** | Atria contract | AV valves OPEN |
| **Ventricular systole** | Ventricles contract | AV valves CLOSE ("lub"), Semilunar OPEN |
| **Diastole** | Heart relaxes | Semilunar CLOSE ("dub"), AV valves OPEN |

---

## 4. Control of Heartbeat

- **Sinoatrial node (SAN)** = Pacemaker
- Heart is **myogenic** (beats without nerve signals)
- Impulse: SAN → Atria contract → AVN → Bundle of His → Purkyne fibres → Ventricles contract
- **Adrenaline** increases heart rate

---

## 5. Blood Vessels Comparison

| Feature | Artery | Vein | Capillary |
|---------|--------|------|-----------|
| Direction | Away from heart | Towards heart | Connects arteriole to venule |
| Wall | Thick, muscular, elastic | Thin | One cell thick |
| Lumen | Small | Large | Very small (one RBC) |
| Valves | No | Yes | No |
| Pressure | High | Low | Low |
| Pulse | Yes | No | No |
| Function | Carry blood under pressure | Return blood to heart | Exchange of materials |

---

## 6. Composition of Blood

| Component | Function | Adaptation |
|-----------|----------|------------|
| **Plasma** | Transport medium | Liquid; carries dissolved substances |
| **Red blood cells** | Carry oxygen | Biconcave; no nucleus; haemoglobin |
| **White blood cells** | Immune defence | Phagocytes engulf pathogens; lymphocytes make antibodies |
| **Platelets** | Blood clotting | Cell fragments; release clotting factors |

---

## 7. Haemoglobin and Oxygen

- **In lungs** (high O₂): Haemoglobin + O₂ → Oxyhaemoglobin
- **In tissues** (low O₂): Oxyhaemoglobin → Haemoglobin + O₂

---

## 8. Blood Clotting

**Sequence**: Damage → Platelets activate → Thrombin forms → Fibrinogen → Fibrin (mesh) → Clot

---

## 9. Coronary Heart Disease

### Risk Factors
- Diet high in saturated fats
- Smoking
- High blood pressure
- Lack of exercise
- Obesity
- Genetic factors

### Treatments
- Lifestyle changes
- Statins (lower cholesterol)
- Angioplasty (balloon + stent)
- Bypass surgery

---

## 10. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Double circulation** | Blood passes through heart twice per circuit |
| **Myogenic** | Heart beats without external nerve signals |
| **Systole** | Contraction phase |
| **Diastole** | Relaxation phase |
| **Haemoglobin** | Red pigment that carries oxygen |
| **Phagocytosis** | Engulfing pathogens by white blood cells |

---

## 11. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Pulmonary artery = DEoxygenated; Pulmonary vein = Oxygenated (opposite to rest!)
2. Left ventricle has thickest wall (pumps to whole body)
3. Arteries carry AWAY from heart; Veins TOWARDS heart
4. Heart is myogenic (beats by itself)
5. Coronary arteries supply heart muscle, not blood in chambers

### Common Mistakes
- Confusing pulmonary artery/vein
- Forgetting "away from" or "towards" heart in definitions
- Saying left ventricle pumps to left side only
- Tricuspid = Right; Bicuspid = Left
