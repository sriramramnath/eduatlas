# Chapter 9: Reproduction in Humans - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Male Reproductive System

| Structure | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **Testes** | Produce sperm and testosterone |
| **Scrotum** | Holds testes outside body (lower temperature) |
| **Epididymis** | Stores and matures sperm |
| **Sperm duct** | Carries sperm to urethra |
| **Prostate gland** | Produces alkaline fluid for semen |
| **Seminal vesicles** | Produce fluid rich in fructose |
| **Urethra** | Carries semen (and urine) |
| **Penis** | Delivers sperm into vagina |

---

## 2. Female Reproductive System

| Structure | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| **Ovaries** | Produce eggs and hormones (oestrogen, progesterone) |
| **Oviducts** | Carry egg to uterus; site of fertilisation |
| **Uterus** | Where embryo implants and develops |
| **Cervix** | Ring of muscle at uterus opening |
| **Vagina** | Receives penis; birth canal |

---

## 3. Gametes Comparison

| Feature | Sperm | Egg |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Size | Small | Large |
| Motility | Motile (tail) | Non-motile |
| Quantity | Millions | One per month |
| Nucleus | Haploid (23) | Haploid (23) |
| Cytoplasm | Very little | Large amount |
| Special features | Flagellum, acrosome, mitochondria | Nutrient reserves, protective layers |

---

## 4. Sperm Adaptations

| Feature | Adaptation |
|---------|------------|
| **Tail (flagellum)** | Swimming towards egg |
| **Mitochondria** | Energy for movement |
| **Acrosome** | Enzymes to penetrate egg |
| **Haploid nucleus** | 23 chromosomes to fuse with egg |
| **Streamlined shape** | Reduces resistance |

---

## 5. Egg Adaptations

| Feature | Adaptation |
|---------|------------|
| **Large cytoplasm** | Nutrients for embryo |
| **Haploid nucleus** | 23 chromosomes to fuse with sperm |
| **Zona pellucida** | Protective jelly coat |
| **Corona radiata** | Follicle cells for protection |

---

## 6. Fertilisation

**Definition**: Fusion of sperm and egg to form a zygote

**Process**:
1. Sperm swim to egg in oviduct
2. Acrosome releases enzymes
3. One sperm penetrates egg
4. Nuclei fuse
5. Zygote formed (diploid - 46 chromosomes)

**Location**: Oviduct (Fallopian tube)

---

## 7. Menstrual Cycle (28 days)

| Stage | Days | What Happens |
|-------|------|--------------|
| **Menstruation** | 1-5 | Uterine lining shed |
| **Follicular phase** | 1-13 | Follicle develops; oestrogen rises; lining thickens |
| **Ovulation** | 14 | Egg released (LH surge) |
| **Luteal phase** | 15-28 | Corpus luteum forms; progesterone maintains lining |

---

## 8. Hormones of Menstrual Cycle

| Hormone | Source | Function |
|---------|--------|----------|
| **FSH** | Pituitary | Stimulates follicle development |
| **Oestrogen** | Ovaries (follicle) | Thickens uterine lining; inhibits FSH |
| **LH** | Pituitary | Triggers ovulation; forms corpus luteum |
| **Progesterone** | Corpus luteum | Maintains uterine lining; inhibits FSH and LH |

---

## 9. The Placenta

**Functions**:
- Exchange of materials between mother and fetus
- Oxygen and nutrients → fetus
- Carbon dioxide and wastes → mother
- Produces hormones

**Key Point**: Maternal and fetal blood do NOT mix!

---

## 10. Birth Control Methods

| Method | Type | How it Works |
|--------|------|--------------|
| **Condom** | Barrier | Prevents sperm entering vagina |
| **Pill** | Hormonal | Prevents ovulation; thickens mucus |
| **IUD** | Device | Prevents implantation |
| **Vasectomy** | Sterilisation | Cuts sperm ducts |
| **Rhythm method** | Natural | Avoid fertile period |

---

## 11. IVF (In Vitro Fertilisation)

1. Fertility drugs → multiple eggs
2. Eggs collected from ovaries
3. Mixed with sperm in lab
4. Fertilisation occurs
5. Embryo develops 2-5 days
6. Embryo transferred to uterus

---

## 12. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Fertilisation** | Fusion of sperm and egg |
| **Zygote** | Cell formed by fertilisation |
| **Ovulation** | Release of egg from ovary |
| **Implantation** | Embryo embeds in uterus |
| **Gestation** | Pregnancy period |
| **Haploid** | Half chromosome number (23) |
| **Diploid** | Full chromosome number (46) |

---

## 13. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Fertilisation in oviduct; implantation in uterus
2. Sperm have tail, acrosome, mitochondria
3. LH triggers ovulation (day 14)
4. FSH stimulates follicle; LH triggers ovulation
5. Placenta: bloods don't mix; exchange by diffusion

### Common Mistakes
- Saying bloods mix in placenta (they don't!)
- Confusing fertilisation and implantation sites
- Confusing umbilical artery/vein
- Forgetting LH triggers ovulation
- Not explaining hormone interactions
