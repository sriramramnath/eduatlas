# Chapter 3: Breathing and Gas Exchange - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Respiratory System Structure

### Main Structures

| Structure | Description | Function |
|-----------|-------------|----------|
| **Nasal cavity** | Lined with mucus and cilia | Warms, moistens, filters air |
| **Trachea** | Tube with C-shaped cartilage rings | Carries air to bronchi |
| **Bronchi** | Two tubes branching from trachea | Carry air to each lung |
| **Bronchioles** | Smaller branches with smooth muscle | Carry air to alveoli |
| **Alveoli** | Tiny air sacs | Site of gas exchange |
| **Diaphragm** | Sheet of muscle below lungs | Changes thorax volume |
| **Intercostal muscles** | Muscles between ribs | Move ribcage |

### Airway Linings

| Structure | Lining | Function |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Trachea/Bronchi | Ciliated epithelium + goblet cells + cartilage | Mucus traps particles; cilia sweep mucus up; cartilage keeps airway open |
| Bronchioles | Smooth muscle (no cartilage) | Controls airway diameter |
| Alveoli | Squamous epithelium (one cell thick) | Short diffusion distance |

## 2. Mechanism of Breathing

### Inspiration vs Expiration

| Feature | Inspiration | Expiration |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| External intercostals | Contract | Relax |
| Diaphragm | Contracts and flattens | Relaxes and domes |
| Ribs | Move up and out | Move down and in |
| Thorax volume | Increases | Decreases |
| Lung pressure | Decreases (below atmospheric) | Increases (above atmospheric) |
| Air movement | Into lungs | Out of lungs |
| Energy needed? | Yes (active) | No (passive) |

### Key Equation
**Muscle contraction → Increased volume → Decreased pressure → Air enters**

## 3. Gas Exchange at the Alveoli

### Adaptations of Alveoli

| Adaptation | How it Helps Gas Exchange |
|------------|---------------------------|
| **Large surface area** | 300-500 million alveoli = more space for diffusion |
| **Thin walls** | One cell thick = short diffusion distance |
| **Rich blood supply** | Capillary network maintains concentration gradient |
| **Moist lining** | Gases dissolve before diffusing |
| **Good ventilation** | Breathing refreshes air, maintains gradient |

### Diffusion of Gases

| Gas | Direction | From | To |
|-----|-----------|------|-----|
| **Oxygen** | Into blood | Alveoli (high O₂) | Capillaries (low O₂) |
| **Carbon dioxide** | Into alveoli | Capillaries (high CO₂) | Alveoli (low CO₂) |

---

## 4. Composition of Inhaled vs Exhaled Air

| Component | Inhaled Air (%) | Exhaled Air (%) | Change |
|-----------|-----------------|-----------------|--------|
| Oxygen | 21 | 16-17 | Decreases (diffuses into blood) |
| Carbon dioxide | 0.04 | 4 | Increases (diffuses from blood) |
| Nitrogen | 78 | 78 | No change (inert) |
| Water vapour | Variable | Saturated | Increases (moist surfaces) |

## 5. Effects of Smoking

### Harmful Components

| Component | Effect on Body |
|-----------|----------------|
| **Tar** | Damages cilia; causes mucus build-up; contains carcinogens; causes lung cancer |
| **Nicotine** | Addictive; increases heart rate; constricts blood vessels; raises blood pressure |
| **Carbon monoxide** | Binds to haemoglobin; forms carboxyhaemoglobin; reduces oxygen transport |

### Smoking-Related Diseases

| Disease | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Lung cancer** | Carcinogens in tar cause mutations → uncontrolled cell division |
| **Chronic bronchitis** | Inflammation of bronchi; excess mucus; persistent cough |
| **Emphysema** | Alveoli walls break down → reduced surface area for gas exchange |
| **Heart disease** | Carbon monoxide damages blood vessels; increases clotting risk |

## 6. Gas Exchange in Other Organisms

### Comparison Table

| Feature | Humans (Mammals) | Fish | Insects |
|---------|------------------|------|---------|
| **Respiratory surface** | Alveoli | Gill lamellae | Tracheoles |
| **Medium** | Air | Water | Air |
| **Gas transport** | By blood | By blood | Direct to cells |
| **Structures** | Trachea → bronchi → bronchioles → alveoli | Gills with filaments and lamellae | Spiracles → tracheae → tracheoles |

### Fish Gills - Key Points
- **Counter-current flow**: Blood flows opposite to water direction
- **Maintains concentration gradient** along entire lamella length
- **Extracts ~80% oxygen** from water (vs ~50% with parallel flow)

## 7. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Ventilation/breathing** | Movement of air into and out of lungs |
| **Gas exchange** | Diffusion of O₂ into blood and CO₂ out of blood |
| **Respiration** | Chemical release of energy from glucose in cells |
| **Alveolus** | Tiny air sac where gas exchange occurs |
| **Diaphragm** | Muscle sheet separating thorax from abdomen |
| **Haemoglobin** | Red pigment that carries oxygen in red blood cells |
| **Carboxyhaemoglobin** | Haemoglobin bound to carbon monoxide |
| **Counter-current flow** | Blood and water flowing in opposite directions in gills |

## 8. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. **Breathing ≠ Respiration**: Breathing is mechanical; respiration is chemical
2. **Alveoli adaptations**: Large SA, thin walls, good blood supply, moist, ventilated
3. **Smoking damage**: Tar → cilia damage → mucus accumulation → infection
4. **Fish gills**: Counter-current flow maintains gradient → efficient extraction
5. **Muscles can only pull**: Intercostals pull ribs up/down; they never push

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Confusing breathing and respiration
- Saying muscles "push" the ribs
- Forgetting concentration gradient in gas exchange
- Not explaining HOW adaptations help
- Confusing bronchi (have cartilage) with bronchioles (no cartilage)

## 9. Practical Work Summary

### Measuring Breathing
- **Breathing rate**: Count breaths per minute
- **Lung volume**: Spirometer or balloon method
- **Peak flow**: Maximum exhalation speed

### Effect of Exercise
- Breathing rate increases during exercise
- Due to increased CO₂ in blood
- Recovery time: Time to return to normal rate
- Fitter people have shorter recovery time
