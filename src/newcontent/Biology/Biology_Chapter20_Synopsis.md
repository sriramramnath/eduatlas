# Chapter 20: Selective Breeding - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Definition

**Selective Breeding (Artificial Selection)**
Humans choose organisms with desirable traits to breed together.

---

## 2. Process

```
1. Identify desired trait
         ↓
2. Select parents showing trait
         ↓
3. Breed them together
         ↓
4. Select best offspring
         ↓
5. Repeat for many generations
         ↓
6. Improved variety produced
```

---

## 3. Natural vs Artificial Selection

| Feature | Natural Selection | Artificial Selection |
|---------|------------------|---------------------|
| Selector | Nature | Humans |
| Purpose | Survival | Human wants |
| Time | Very long | Shorter |

---

## 4. Examples

### Animals
| Animal | Selected For |
|--------|--------------|
| Dairy cows | High milk yield |
| Beef cattle | Muscle/meat |
| Sheep | Wool quality |
| Dogs | Various traits (size, temperament) |
| Horses | Speed, strength |

### Plants
| Plant | Selected For |
|-------|--------------|
| Wheat | High yield, disease resistance |
| Rice | Yield, nutrition |
| Tomatoes | Size, taste |
| Flowers | Colour, appearance |

---

## 5. Advantages

- Higher yields
- Better quality products
- Disease resistance
- Faster growth
- Adapted to human needs

---

## 6. Disadvantages

| Problem | Result |
|---------|--------|
| **Reduced genetic variation** | Less able to adapt |
| **Inbreeding** | Health problems |
| **Health issues** | Dogs with breathing problems |
| **Monocultures** | Vulnerable to disease |

---

## 7. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Selective breeding** | Humans breed organisms with desired traits |
| **Inbreeding** | Breeding closely related individuals |
| **Monoculture** | Single variety over large area |
| **Variety/Breed** | Type produced by selective breeding |

---

## 8. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Humans select, not nature
2. Takes many generations
3. Reduces genetic variation
4. Can cause health problems
5. NOT the same as genetic engineering

### Common Mistakes
- Confusing natural and artificial selection
- Forgetting "many generations"
- Not giving specific examples
- Confusing with genetic engineering
- Not discussing disadvantages
