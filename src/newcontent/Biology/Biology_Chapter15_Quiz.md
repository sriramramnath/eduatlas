## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What is the main cause of the enhanced greenhouse effect?

A) Increased oxygen in the atmosphere  
B) Increased carbon dioxide from human activities  
C) Decreased nitrogen in the atmosphere  
D) Natural climate cycles  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The enhanced greenhouse effect is primarily caused by increased concentrations of greenhouse gases, especially carbon dioxide from burning fossil fuels and deforestation. While the greenhouse effect is natural, human activities have enhanced it by adding more greenhouse gases to the atmosphere.

---

### Question 2
Which process is the first step in eutrophication?

A) Fish die from lack of oxygen  
B) Algae grow rapidly  
C) Nitrates enter the water  
D) Bacteria decompose dead matter  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Eutrophication begins when excess nutrients (nitrates and phosphates) enter a water body. This triggers the sequence: nutrient enrichment → algal bloom → sunlight blocked → underwater plants die → decomposition → oxygen depletion → fish death.

---

### Question 3
What is a consequence of deforestation?

A) Increased biodiversity  
B) Increased soil erosion  
C) Increased rainfall  
D) Increased oxygen production  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Deforestation leads to increased soil erosion because tree roots are no longer present to hold soil together. It actually decreases biodiversity, decreases local rainfall, and decreases oxygen production due to fewer trees photosynthesising.

---

### Question 4
Which greenhouse gas is released by cattle and rice paddies?

A) Carbon dioxide  
B) Methane  
C) Nitrous oxide  
D) CFCs  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Methane (CH₄) is released in significant quantities by cattle (through digestion) and rice paddies (through anaerobic decomposition in flooded fields). Methane is about 25 times more potent as a greenhouse gas than carbon dioxide.

---

### Question 5
What does "in-situ conservation" mean?

A) Conservation in zoos  
B) Conservation in seed banks  
C) Conservation in the natural habitat  
D) Conservation in laboratories  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: In-situ conservation means protecting species in their natural habitat. Examples include national parks, nature reserves, and protected areas. Zoos, seed banks, and laboratories are examples of ex-situ conservation (outside the natural habitat).

---

### Question 6
The melting of polar ice caps is a consequence of:

A) Ozone layer depletion  
B) Acid rain  
C) Global warming  
D) Eutrophication  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Global warming (caused by the enhanced greenhouse effect) is causing polar ice caps to melt. As average global temperatures rise, ice in polar regions melts, contributing to rising sea levels and habitat loss for species like polar bears.

---

### Question 7
Which gas is NOT a greenhouse gas?

A) Carbon dioxide  
B) Methane  
C) Nitrogen  
D) Water vapour  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Nitrogen (N₂) makes up about 78% of the atmosphere but is NOT a greenhouse gas. It does not absorb infrared radiation. Greenhouse gases include carbon dioxide, methane, water vapour, nitrous oxide, and CFCs.

---

### Question 8
What is the main reason for conserving endangered species?

A) To increase global warming  
B) To maintain biodiversity  
C) To reduce oxygen levels  
D) To increase pollution  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The main reason for conservation is to maintain biodiversity. This is important for ecosystem stability, potential medicinal resources, genetic diversity for future food crops, and ethical reasons about preserving species for future generations.

---

### Question 9
Algal bloom in eutrophication causes:

A) More oxygen in water  
B) Less sunlight reaching underwater plants  
C) Increased fish population  
D) Clearer water  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Algal bloom forms a thick layer on the water surface, blocking sunlight from reaching underwater plants. These plants then cannot photosynthesise and die, beginning the decomposition process that leads to oxygen depletion.

---

### Question 10
Which human activity contributes most to carbon dioxide emissions?

A) Recycling  
B) Burning fossil fuels  
C) Planting trees  
D) Using renewable energy  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, natural gas) for energy, transport, and industry is the largest source of anthropogenic carbon dioxide emissions. This includes electricity generation, vehicle emissions, and industrial processes.

---

### Question 11
An invasive species is:

A) A native species that increases in number  
B) A non-native species that causes harm  
C) An endangered species  
D) A species that is extinct  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: An invasive species is a non-native organism that has been introduced (intentionally or accidentally) to an area and causes harm to the environment, economy, or human health. Examples include grey squirrels and Japanese knotweed.

---

### Question 12
What is the purpose of a seed bank?

A) To grow new forests  
B) To store seeds as a genetic resource  
C) To sell seeds commercially  
D) To produce biofuels  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Seed banks store seeds from many plant species as a genetic resource for conservation. They preserve genetic diversity and provide backup in case species become extinct in the wild. The Svalbard Global Seed Vault is a famous example.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Deforestation increases biodiversity."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Deforestation DECREASES biodiversity. When forests are cut down, many species lose their habitat, leading to population declines and extinctions. Forests are among the most biodiverse ecosystems on Earth.

---

### Statement 2
"Carbon dioxide is a greenhouse gas."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Carbon dioxide (CO₂) is the main greenhouse gas responsible for the enhanced greenhouse effect. It absorbs infrared radiation emitted by the Earth, trapping heat in the atmosphere and contributing to global warming.

---

### Statement 3
"Eutrophication is caused by a lack of nutrients in water."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Eutrophication is caused by EXCESS nutrients (nitrates and phosphates) entering water bodies. Too many nutrients, not too few, trigger algal blooms that lead to oxygen depletion and ecosystem collapse.

---

### Statement 4
"The greenhouse effect is always harmful."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: The NATURAL greenhouse effect is essential for life on Earth, keeping our planet warm enough for life. It is the ENHANCED greenhouse effect (caused by human activities adding more greenhouse gases) that causes global warming and climate change.

---

### Statement 5
"Ex-situ conservation includes zoos and seed banks."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Ex-situ conservation means "off-site" conservation - protecting species outside their natural habitat. Zoos, botanical gardens, seed banks, and captive breeding programmes are all examples of ex-situ conservation.

---

### Statement 6
"Rising sea levels are caused by melting ice only."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Rising sea levels are caused by BOTH melting ice (adding water to oceans) AND thermal expansion of seawater (water expands as it warms). Both are consequences of global warming.

---

### Statement 7
"Methane is produced by cattle."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Cattle (and other ruminants) produce methane during digestion. Bacteria in their digestive system break down cellulose, releasing methane as a by-product. This is a significant source of greenhouse gas emissions.

---

### Statement 8
"Invasive species always benefit ecosystems."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Invasive species HARM ecosystems by outcompeting native species, introducing diseases, and disrupting food webs. They reduce biodiversity and can cause significant ecological and economic damage.

---

### Statement 9
"CITES regulates international trade in endangered species."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: CITES (Convention on International Trade in Endangered Species) is an international agreement that regulates trade in endangered species and their products to protect them from over-exploitation.

---

### Statement 10
"Sulphur dioxide causes acid rain."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Sulphur dioxide (SO₂), released from burning fossil fuels (especially coal), reacts with water vapour in the atmosphere to form sulphuric acid, which falls as acid rain. This damages forests, aquatic ecosystems, and buildings.

---

### Statement 11
"Planting trees helps reduce global warming."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Trees absorb carbon dioxide during photosynthesis, removing this greenhouse gas from the atmosphere. Afforestation (planting trees in new areas) and reforestation (replanting previously forested areas) can help mitigate climate change.

---

### Statement 12
"Eutrophication leads to increased oxygen in water."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Eutrophication leads to DECREASED oxygen in water. When bacteria decompose dead algae and plants, they use up dissolved oxygen, leading to hypoxic or anoxic conditions that kill fish and other aquatic organisms.
