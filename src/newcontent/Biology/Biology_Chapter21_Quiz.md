# Chapter 21: Genetic Modification (Biotechnology) - Quiz Section

## Exam-Style Questions for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What is genetic engineering?

A) Selective breeding of organisms  
B) Altering an organism's DNA to introduce desirable traits  
C) Natural evolution  
D) Cloning organisms  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Genetic engineering (genetic modification) involves directly altering an organism's DNA to introduce new characteristics. It differs from selective breeding, which uses traditional breeding methods.

---

### Question 2
Which enzyme is used to cut DNA at specific sequences?

A) DNA ligase  
B) Restriction enzyme  
C) Polymerase  
D) Helicase  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Restriction enzymes recognise specific DNA sequences and cut the DNA at these points. They are used to cut out desired genes from donor DNA.

---

### Question 3
What is a plasmid?

A) A type of enzyme  
B) Circular DNA found in bacteria  
C) A type of virus  
D) A plant cell  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Plasmids are small circular DNA molecules found in bacteria. They are commonly used as vectors to transfer genes into host cells in genetic engineering.

---

### Question 4
Genetically modified bacteria are used to produce:

A) Oxygen  
B) Human insulin  
C) Carbon dioxide  
D) Chlorophyll  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: GM bacteria are used to produce human insulin for diabetics. The human insulin gene is inserted into bacteria, which then produce insulin that can be harvested.

---

### Question 5
Golden Rice has been genetically modified to produce:

A) More protein  
B) Beta-carotene (Vitamin A precursor)  
C) Pest resistance  
D) Faster growth  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Golden Rice has been modified to produce beta-carotene, which the body converts to vitamin A. This could help prevent vitamin A deficiency in developing countries.

---

### Question 6
What is a vector in genetic engineering?

A) The organism receiving the gene  
B) A carrier that transfers a gene into a cell  
C) The gene being transferred  
D) An enzyme that cuts DNA  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A vector is a carrier (like a plasmid or virus) that transfers a gene into a host cell. Vectors are necessary to get the desired gene into the target organism.

---

### Question 7
Bt crops are genetically modified to:

A) Resist herbicides  
B) Grow faster  
C) Produce their own pesticide  
D) Have more nutrients  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Bt crops contain a gene from Bacillus thuringiensis bacteria that produces a protein toxic to certain insect pests. This reduces the need for pesticide spraying.

---

### Question 8
Which enzyme joins DNA pieces together?

A) Restriction enzyme  
B) DNA ligase  
C) DNA polymerase  
D) Helicase  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: DNA ligase joins pieces of DNA together. It is used to insert a gene into a plasmid vector, connecting the "sticky ends" produced by restriction enzymes.

---

### Question 9
A transgenic organism is one that:

A) Has no DNA  
B) Contains DNA from another species  
C) Is a clone  
D) Cannot reproduce  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A transgenic organism contains genetic material from another species, inserted through genetic engineering techniques.

---

### Question 10
Gene therapy involves:

A) Treating diseases with drugs  
B) Inserting healthy genes to treat genetic disorders  
C) Removing damaged genes  
D) Cloning humans  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Gene therapy involves inserting healthy copies of genes into cells to treat genetic disorders caused by faulty genes, such as cystic fibrosis.

---

### Question 11
A concern about GM crops is:

A) They always taste better  
B) Gene transfer to wild plants  
C) They grow too slowly  
D) They are always harmful  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A concern about GM crops is that genes could transfer to wild relatives through cross-breeding, potentially creating "super weeds" or affecting biodiversity.

---

### Question 12
GM insulin is preferred over animal insulin because:

A) It is cheaper to produce  
B) It is identical to human insulin  
C) Animals are not needed  
D) All of the above  

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: GM insulin has multiple advantages: it is identical to human insulin (no allergic reactions), doesn't require killing animals, and can be produced in large quantities.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Genetic engineering and selective breeding are the same thing."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Genetic engineering directly alters DNA and can transfer genes between species. Selective breeding uses traditional breeding methods over many generations and only works within a species.

---

### Statement 2
"Plasmids are used as vectors in genetic engineering."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Plasmids (circular DNA from bacteria) are commonly used as vectors to carry genes into host cells. They are useful because they can replicate independently and are easy to manipulate.

---

### Statement 3
"GM bacteria produce human insulin."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: The human insulin gene has been inserted into bacteria, which then produce human insulin. This has replaced the use of pig and cow insulin for treating diabetes.

---

### Statement 4
"GM foods are always clearly labelled."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Labelling of GM foods varies by country. In some countries, GM foods must be labelled; in others, labelling is not required. This is a matter of ongoing debate.

---

### Statement 5
"Golden Rice produces vitamin A directly."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Golden Rice produces beta-carotene, which the human body converts to vitamin A. Beta-carotene is the precursor to vitamin A, not vitamin A itself.

---

### Statement 6
"Bt crops reduce the need for pesticide spraying."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Bt crops produce their own insecticidal protein, so farmers need to spray less chemical pesticide. This is one of the claimed environmental benefits of GM crops.

---

### Statement 7
"Gene therapy is currently a routine treatment for all genetic diseases."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Gene therapy is still largely experimental. While there have been successful trials for some conditions, it is not yet a routine treatment for most genetic diseases.

---

### Statement 8
"Genetic engineering can transfer genes between different species."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Unlike selective breeding, genetic engineering can transfer genes between any species. For example, human genes can be inserted into bacteria, and bacterial genes can be inserted into plants.

---

### Statement 9
"Restriction enzymes cut DNA randomly."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Restriction enzymes cut DNA at SPECIFIC sequences, not randomly. Each restriction enzyme recognises a particular DNA sequence.

---

### Statement 10
"There are no concerns about genetic engineering."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: There are many concerns about genetic engineering, including environmental impacts, potential health effects, and ethical issues. It remains a controversial topic.

---

### Statement 11
"GM crops can potentially cross-breed with wild plants."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: GM crops can cross-breed with wild relatives if they are closely related. This could spread the modified genes to wild populations.

---

### Statement 12
"Insulin from GM bacteria causes fewer allergic reactions than animal insulin."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: GM insulin is identical to human insulin, whereas animal insulin has slight differences that can cause allergic reactions in some people.
