# Chapter 19: Natural Selection and Evolution - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Natural Selection Process

```
1. Variation in population
         ↓
2. Selection pressure (e.g., predators, climate)
         ↓
3. Advantageous traits help survival
         ↓
4. Survivors reproduce
         ↓
5. Advantageous alleles passed on
         ↓
6. Over generations → allele frequency changes
         ↓
7. Population EVOLVES
```

---

## 2. Key Principles

| Principle | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Variation** | Genetic differences exist in populations |
| **Inheritance** | Characteristics passed to offspring |
| **Competition** | More born than can survive |
| **Survival** | Best adapted survive and reproduce |

---

## 3. Sources of Variation

| Source | Type |
|--------|------|
| Mutations | Genetic |
| Meiosis (crossing over, independent assortment) | Genetic |
| Random fertilisation | Genetic |
| Environment (nutrition, sunlight) | Environmental |

---

## 4. Speciation

### Definition
Formation of new species

### Process
1. Isolation (geographical, reproductive)
2. Different selection pressures
3. Different adaptations evolve
4. Cannot interbreed
5. New species

---

## 5. Evidence for Evolution

| Evidence | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Fossils** | Show changes over time; simple → complex |
| **Comparative anatomy** | Homologous structures show common ancestry |
| **DNA/proteins** | Similar DNA = closely related |
| **Embryology** | Similar embryos = common ancestry |

---

## 6. Evolution Examples

| Example | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Peppered moths** | Light → Dark → Light (industrial revolution) |
| **Antibiotic resistance** | Bacteria evolve resistance quickly |
| **Darwin's finches** | Different beaks on different islands |
| **Pesticide resistance** | Insects evolve resistance |

---

## 7. Extinction

### Causes
- Climate change
- Habitat destruction
- Competition
- Predation
- Disease
- Human activities

---

## 8. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Evolution** | Change in heritable characteristics over time |
| **Natural selection** | Best adapted survive and reproduce |
| **Speciation** | Formation of new species |
| **Species** | Group producing fertile offspring |
| **Extinction** | Permanent loss of a species |
| **Mutation** | Change in DNA |

---

## 9. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Populations evolve, NOT individuals
2. Mutations create new alleles
3. Natural selection = nature selects
4. Homologous structures = common ancestry
5. Isolation leads to speciation

### Common Mistakes
- Saying individuals evolve
- Forgetting "heritable"
- Not explaining the sequence
- Confusing natural vs artificial selection
- Not mentioning mutations
