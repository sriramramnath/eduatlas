## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What is the correct order of stages in mitosis?

A) Anaphase, Metaphase, Prophase, Telophase  
B) Prophase, Metaphase, Anaphase, Telophase  
C) Telophase, Anaphase, Metaphase, Prophase  
D) Metaphase, Prophase, Telophase, Anaphase  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The correct order of mitosis stages is PMAT: Prophase (chromosomes become visible), Metaphase (chromosomes line up), Anaphase (chromatids separate), Telophase (new nuclei form).

---

### Question 2
How many chromosomes are in a human sperm cell?

A) 23  
B) 46  
C) 92  
D) 12  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: Sperm cells are gametes (sex cells), which are haploid. They contain half the normal chromosome number. Human body cells have 46 chromosomes, so gametes have 23 chromosomes.

---

### Question 3
The process of mitosis produces:

A) Four genetically different cells  
B) Two genetically identical cells  
C) Four genetically identical cells  
D) Two genetically different cells  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Mitosis produces two daughter cells that are genetically identical to each other and to the parent cell. They have the same chromosome number as the parent cell.

---

### Question 4
During which phase of mitosis do chromatids separate?

A) Prophase  
B) Metaphase  
C) Anaphase  
D) Telophase  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: During anaphase, spindle fibres contract and pull sister chromatids apart. Each chromatid moves to opposite poles of the cell.

---

### Question 5
What type of cell division produces gametes?

A) Mitosis  
B) Meiosis  
C) Binary fission  
D) Budding  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Meiosis is the type of cell division that produces gametes (sperm and egg cells). It involves two divisions and produces four haploid, genetically different cells.

---

### Question 6
A stem cell is:

A) A fully specialised cell  
B) An undifferentiated cell that can become specialised  
C) A cancer cell  
D) A gamete  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Stem cells are undifferentiated cells that have not yet specialised. They can divide and differentiate into different types of specialised cells.

---

### Question 7
Crossing over occurs during:

A) Mitosis  
B) Meiosis I  
C) Meiosis II  
D) Cytokinesis  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Crossing over occurs during Prophase I of meiosis. Homologous chromosomes pair up and exchange genetic material, creating new combinations of alleles.

---

### Question 8
Cancer is caused by:

A) Controlled cell division  
B) Uncontrolled cell division  
C) Meiosis  
D) Cell differentiation  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Cancer results from uncontrolled cell division. Mutations in genes that control cell division cause cells to divide rapidly and form tumours.

---

### Question 9
How many daughter cells are produced by meiosis?

A) 2  
B) 4  
C) 6  
D) 8  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Meiosis involves two divisions and produces four haploid daughter cells (gametes). Each cell is genetically different from the others.

---

### Question 10
DNA replication occurs during:

A) Prophase  
B) Metaphase  
C) Interphase (S phase)  
D) Telophase  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: DNA replication occurs during the S phase (Synthesis phase) of interphase, before mitosis begins. Each chromosome is duplicated to form two sister chromatids.

---

### Question 11
A benign tumour differs from a malignant tumour because it:

A) Spreads to other parts of the body  
B) Does not spread to other parts of the body  
C) Is always fatal  
D) Grows very rapidly  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Benign tumours do NOT spread to other parts of the body. They grow slowly, are enclosed, and are usually not life-threatening. Malignant tumours spread (metastasis) and can be fatal.

---

### Question 12
Independent assortment occurs during:

A) Prophase I  
B) Metaphase I  
C) Anaphase I  
D) Telophase I  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Independent assortment occurs during Metaphase I of meiosis when homologous chromosome pairs arrange randomly at the equator. This creates genetic variation.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Mitosis produces four daughter cells."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Mitosis produces TWO daughter cells. MEIOSIS produces four daughter cells. Mitosis involves one division; meiosis involves two divisions.

---

### Statement 2
"Human body cells contain 46 chromosomes."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Human body cells (somatic cells) are diploid and contain 46 chromosomes, arranged in 23 pairs. One chromosome in each pair comes from each parent.

---

### Statement 3
"Meiosis produces genetically identical cells."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Meiosis produces genetically DIFFERENT cells. Crossing over and independent assortment create genetic variation among the four gametes produced.

---

### Statement 4
"Cytokinesis is the division of the cytoplasm."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Cytokinesis is the division of the cytoplasm after mitosis or meiosis. It completes cell division, separating the two daughter cells.

---

### Statement 5
"Stem cells can differentiate into specialised cells."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Stem cells are undifferentiated cells that can divide and differentiate into various specialised cell types. This is their defining characteristic.

---

### Statement 6
"Gametes are diploid cells."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Gametes are HAPLOID cells, containing half the normal chromosome number. They are produced by meiosis. Body cells are diploid.

---

### Statement 7
"Crossing over creates genetic variation."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Crossing over exchanges genetic material between non-sister chromatids of homologous chromosomes, creating new combinations of alleles and increasing genetic variation.

---

### Statement 8
"Mitosis is used for growth and repair."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Mitosis produces new cells for growth of multicellular organisms and for repair and replacement of damaged or dead cells.

---

### Statement 9
"A zygote is haploid."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: A zygote is DIPLOID (2n). It is formed when two haploid gametes fuse during fertilisation, restoring the diploid chromosome number.

---

### Statement 10
"Carcinogens cause cancer."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Carcinogens are substances that can cause cancer by damaging DNA or causing mutations in genes that control cell division. Examples include tobacco smoke, UV radiation, and certain chemicals.

---

### Statement 11
"Embryonic stem cells can become any type of cell."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Embryonic stem cells are pluripotent, meaning they can differentiate into any type of specialised cell in the body. Adult stem cells are more limited in what they can become.

---

### Statement 12
"Anaphase is when chromosomes line up at the equator."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: METAPHASE is when chromosomes line up at the equator. ANAPHASE is when sister chromatids separate and move to opposite poles.
