## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
Which nutrient is essential for the production of haemoglobin?

A) Calcium  
B) Iron  
C) Vitamin C  
D) Protein  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Iron is an essential component of haemoglobin, the red pigment in red blood cells that carries oxygen. Without sufficient iron, the body cannot produce enough haemoglobin, leading to iron-deficiency anaemia. Calcium is needed for bones and teeth, vitamin C for collagen synthesis, and proteins for growth and repair.

### Question 2
Which of the following correctly describes the function of bile?

A) It digests proteins into amino acids  
B) It emulsifies fats and neutralises stomach acid  
C) It digests starch into maltose  
D) It absorbs water from the large intestine  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Bile has two main functions: emulsification of fats (breaking large fat droplets into smaller droplets to increase surface area for lipase) and neutralisation of acidic chyme from the stomach (bile is alkaline). Bile does NOT digest any food - it is not an enzyme. Lipase digests fats, amylase digests starch, and proteases digest proteins.

---

### Question 3
In which part of the digestive system does most absorption of digested food occur?

A) Stomach  
B) Small intestine  
C) Large intestine  
D) Oesophagus  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Most absorption of the products of digestion occurs in the small intestine (specifically the ileum). The small intestine is adapted for absorption with villi and microvilli providing a large surface area. The stomach mainly digests proteins, the large intestine absorbs water, and the oesophagus just transports food.

---

### Question 4
Which food test would you use to detect the presence of starch?

A) Benedict's test  
B) Biuret test  
C) Iodine test  
D) Emulsion test  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The iodine test is used to detect starch. When iodine solution (brown/orange) is added to a sample containing starch, it turns blue-black. Benedict's test detects reducing sugars, Biuret test detects proteins, and the emulsion test detects lipids.

---

### Question 5
What is the function of hydrochloric acid in the stomach?

A) To digest proteins  
B) To kill bacteria and provide the correct pH for pepsin  
C) To emulsify fats  
D) To absorb water  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Hydrochloric acid in gastric juice has two main functions: it kills bacteria in food (providing protection from pathogens) and creates the acidic pH (pH 2) that is the optimum for pepsin, the protease enzyme that digests proteins in the stomach. Pepsin itself digests proteins, not the acid.

---

### Question 6
Which enzyme digests starch in the mouth?

A) Pepsin  
B) Lipase  
C) Salivary amylase  
D) Trypsin  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Salivary amylase is produced by the salivary glands and begins the digestion of starch in the mouth, breaking it down into maltose. Pepsin and trypsin are proteases, and lipase digests fats. Salivary amylase works best at neutral pH and is denatured by stomach acid.

---

### Question 7
A person with a deficiency of vitamin C is likely to develop:

A) Rickets  
B) Scurvy  
C) Anaemia  
D) Night blindness  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Vitamin C deficiency causes scurvy, characterised by bleeding gums, poor wound healing, and joint pain. This occurs because vitamin C is needed for collagen synthesis. Rickets is caused by vitamin D/calcium deficiency, anaemia by iron deficiency, and night blindness by vitamin A deficiency.

---

### Question 8
The finger-like projections in the small intestine that increase surface area for absorption are called:

A) Villi  
B) Cilia  
C) Microvilli  
D) Papillae  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: Villi (singular: villus) are finger-like projections on the inner wall of the small intestine that greatly increase the surface area for absorption. Microvilli are even smaller projections on the surface of epithelial cells that further increase surface area. Cilia are found in the respiratory tract, and papillae are found on the tongue.

---

### Question 9
Which blood vessel carries absorbed nutrients from the small intestine to the liver?

A) Hepatic artery  
B) Hepatic vein  
C) Hepatic portal vein  
D) Renal vein  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The hepatic portal vein carries blood rich in absorbed nutrients from the small intestine directly to the liver for processing. This is a portal vein because it connects two capillary beds (intestine and liver). The hepatic artery supplies oxygen to the liver, and the hepatic vein carries blood away from the liver to the heart.

---

### Question 10
What happens to excess amino acids in the body?

A) They are stored as protein  
B) They are converted to glucose  
C) They are deaminated in the liver, with the nitrogen part converted to urea  
D) They are excreted directly in urine  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The body cannot store excess amino acids. In the liver, they undergo deamination - the nitrogen-containing amino group is removed and converted to urea (which is excreted by the kidneys), while the remaining carbon skeleton can be converted to carbohydrate or fat for energy or storage.

---

### Question 11
Which of the following is NOT a function of dietary fibre?

A) Providing energy  
B) Adding bulk to food  
C) Preventing constipation  
D) Stimulating peristalsis  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: Fibre cannot be digested by humans because we lack the enzyme cellulase. Therefore, it cannot provide energy. Its functions include adding bulk to food, absorbing water to soften faeces (preventing constipation), and stimulating peristalsis. These functions help maintain digestive health.

---

### Question 12
In the Benedict's test for reducing sugars, what colour change indicates a positive result?

A) Brown to blue-black  
B) Blue to brick red  
C) Blue to purple  
D) Clear to cloudy white  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: In the Benedict's test, a positive result for reducing sugars is indicated by a colour change from blue to green, yellow, orange, or brick red (depending on concentration). The more reducing sugar present, the more orange/red the colour. Brick red indicates a high concentration of reducing sugar.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Bile digests fats into fatty acids and glycerol."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Bile does NOT digest fats. Bile emulsifies fats (breaks large fat droplets into smaller droplets) to increase the surface area for lipase. It is lipase, not bile, that chemically digests fats into fatty acids and glycerol. Bile is not an enzyme and cannot break chemical bonds.

---

### Statement 2
"Proteins are digested into amino acids."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Proteins are large molecules made of amino acid chains. During digestion, protease enzymes (pepsin, trypsin, peptidases) break the peptide bonds between amino acids, producing amino acids that are small enough to be absorbed. Amino acids are the building blocks of proteins and can be used by cells for protein synthesis.

---

### Statement 3
"The stomach produces an alkaline environment for protein digestion."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: The stomach produces hydrochloric acid, creating an acidic environment (pH 2). This acidic environment is the optimum pH for pepsin, the protease enzyme that works in the stomach. The alkaline environment is found in the small intestine (pH 8), which is optimal for pancreatic enzymes like trypsin.

---

### Statement 4
"Villi contain blood capillaries and lacteals for absorption."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Each villus contains a network of blood capillaries and a lacteal (a lymph vessel). Blood capillaries absorb glucose and amino acids, while lacteals absorb the products of fat digestion (fatty acids and glycerol, which are reassembled into fats before entering the lacteal).

---

### Statement 5
"Kwashiorkor is caused by a deficiency of protein in the diet."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Kwashiorkor is a form of protein-energy malnutrition, commonly seen in developing countries where diets are high in carbohydrates but low in protein. Symptoms include a swollen abdomen (due to fluid accumulation), thin arms and legs, poor growth, and changes in skin and hair colour.

---

### Statement 6
"Digestion is the process of moving food along the alimentary canal."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Digestion is the breakdown of large insoluble molecules into small soluble molecules. The process of moving food along the alimentary canal is called peristalsis. Digestion involves both mechanical breakdown (chewing, churning) and chemical breakdown (by enzymes).

---

### Statement 7
"Peristalsis is an involuntary process."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Peristalsis is controlled by the autonomic nervous system and is therefore involuntary. We do not consciously control the wave-like muscular contractions that move food along the digestive tract. This is why we cannot prevent food from being moved through our digestive system even if we wanted to.

---

### Statement 8
"Lacteals absorb glucose and amino acids."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Lacteals absorb the products of fat digestion (fatty acids and glycerol), not glucose and amino acids. Glucose and amino acids are absorbed into the blood capillaries in the villi. The different absorption pathways reflect how these nutrients are transported in the body.

---

### Statement 9
"Pepsin works best at pH 2."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Pepsin is a protease enzyme produced in the stomach that works best at pH 2, which is acidic. This is because the stomach secretes hydrochloric acid, creating the acidic environment. Pepsin would be denatured at neutral or alkaline pH, which is why it stops working when food enters the small intestine.

---

### Statement 10
"The large intestine is the main site of water absorption."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: While the large intestine does absorb water, most water absorption occurs in the small intestine. The small intestine absorbs water along with nutrients from digested food. The large intestine's main role is to absorb remaining water and form faeces, but it is not the main site of water absorption overall.

---

### Statement 11
"Emulsification is a chemical process that breaks bonds in fat molecules."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Emulsification is a physical process, not a chemical one. It breaks large fat droplets into smaller droplets, but no chemical bonds are broken. This increases the surface area for lipase to act on. The actual digestion of fats (breaking chemical bonds) is done by lipase.

---

### Statement 12
"Children need more energy per kilogram of body weight than adults."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Children and teenagers need more energy per kilogram of body weight than adults because they are growing - they need energy not only for their daily activities but also for the synthesis of new tissues. Children also tend to be more physically active than adults, further increasing their energy requirements.

## Part D: Extended Response Questions (Higher Tier)

### Question 13 (6 marks)
Describe the digestion and absorption of proteins in the human digestive system.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**

**Digestion:**
- Proteins are large molecules made of amino acid chains [1]
- In the stomach, pepsin (protease) digests proteins into shorter peptide chains [1]
- Pepsin works at acidic pH (pH 2) [1]
- In the duodenum, trypsin (from pancreas) continues protein digestion [1]
- Trypsin works at alkaline pH (pH 8) [1]
- Peptidases on the intestinal wall break peptides into amino acids [1]

**Absorption:**
- Amino acids are small and soluble [1]
- Absorbed into blood capillaries of villi [1]
- By diffusion and active transport [1]
- Transported to liver via hepatic portal vein [1]

*Maximum 6 marks for comprehensive answer*

---

### Question 14 (5 marks)
Compare and contrast the digestion of carbohydrates and fats.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**

**Similarities:**
- Both involve enzymes (amylase for carbohydrates, lipase for fats) [1]
- Both produce small soluble molecules that can be absorbed [1]
- Both involve hydrolysis reactions [1]

**Differences:**

| Carbohydrates | Fats |
|---------------|------|
| Digested by amylase and maltase [1] | Digested by lipase only [1] |
| No emulsification required [1] | Bile emulsifies fats first [1] |
| Products: glucose [1] | Products: fatty acids and glycerol [1] |
| Absorbed into blood capillaries [1] | Absorbed into lacteals [1] |
| Starts in the mouth [1] | Starts in the small intestine [1] |

*Maximum 5 marks for comprehensive comparison*
