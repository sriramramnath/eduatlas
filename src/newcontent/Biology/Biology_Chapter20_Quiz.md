## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What is selective breeding?

A) Natural selection in the wild  
B) Humans choosing organisms with desired traits to breed  
C) Random mutations  
D) Genetic engineering  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Selective breeding (artificial selection) is when humans select organisms with desirable characteristics and breed them together to produce offspring with those traits.

---

### Question 2
Which of the following is an example of selective breeding?

A) Antibiotic resistance in bacteria  
B) Peppered moths changing colour  
C) Breeding dairy cows for high milk yield  
D) Finches evolving different beaks  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Breeding dairy cows for high milk yield is an example of selective breeding because humans select the cows that produce the most milk to breed. The other examples are natural selection.

---

### Question 3
A disadvantage of selective breeding is:

A) Increased genetic variation  
B) Reduced genetic variation  
C) Faster evolution  
D) No disadvantages  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Selective breeding reduces genetic variation because only individuals with the desired trait are bred. This can make populations vulnerable to diseases or environmental changes.

---

### Question 4
Modern dog breeds have been developed through:

A) Natural selection  
B) Genetic engineering  
C) Selective breeding  
D) Cloning  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: All modern dog breeds were developed through selective breeding over thousands of years. Humans selected dogs with desired traits to breed, eventually creating over 400 distinct breeds from wolf ancestors.

---

### Question 5
What is inbreeding?

A) Breeding unrelated individuals  
B) Breeding closely related individuals  
C) Breeding different species  
D) Natural selection  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Inbreeding occurs when closely related individuals are bred together. This is often done in selective breeding to maintain desired traits but can lead to health problems.

---

### Question 6
Which problem can result from inbreeding?

A) Increased fertility  
B) Reduced disease resistance  
C) Greater genetic variation  
D) Faster growth  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Inbreeding can lead to reduced disease resistance, reduced fertility, and increased expression of harmful recessive alleles because genetic variation is reduced.

---

### Question 7
Selective breeding differs from natural selection because:

A) It happens faster  
B) Humans make the selection decisions  
C) It only affects plants  
D) No variation is needed  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The key difference is that in selective breeding, HUMANS choose which individuals reproduce, while in natural selection, NATURE selects based on survival advantages.

---

### Question 8
Wheat has been selectively bred for:

A) Lower yield  
B) Susceptibility to disease  
C) High yield and disease resistance  
D) Shorter stems only  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Modern wheat varieties have been selectively bred for high grain yield, disease resistance, shorter stems, and other desirable traits.

---

### Question 9
What is a monoculture?

A) Breeding closely related individuals  
B) Growing one variety of crop over a large area  
C) Natural selection  
D) Genetic engineering  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Monoculture is the agricultural practice of growing a single crop variety over a large area. This can be a result of selective breeding but creates vulnerability to disease.

---

### Question 10
The Irish Potato Famine was worsened by:

A) Too much genetic variation  
B) Monoculture - all potatoes were the same variety  
C) Natural selection  
D) Too many varieties of potato  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: All potatoes grown in Ireland were the same variety (monoculture). When a fungus attacked, all plants were susceptible, causing massive crop failure and famine.

---

### Question 11
Selective breeding in dairy cows aims to produce:

A) Less milk  
B) More milk  
C) Smaller cows  
D) Wild-type cows  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Dairy cows are selectively bred to produce more milk. Modern dairy cows produce much more milk than their wild ancestors due to generations of selective breeding.

---

### Question 12
A problem with selectively bred dog breeds is:

A) They are too healthy  
B) Health problems like breathing difficulties  
C) Too much genetic variation  
D) They live too long  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Many selectively bred dog breeds have health problems. For example, pugs and bulldogs have breathing problems due to their flat faces, and German Shepherds often have hip problems.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Selective breeding is the same as natural selection."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Selective breeding (artificial selection) involves HUMANS selecting which organisms reproduce. Natural selection involves NATURE selecting based on survival advantages.

---

### Statement 2
"Selective breeding takes many generations."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Selective breeding takes many generations to produce significant changes in a population. Each generation must be selected and bred to develop the desired trait.

---

### Statement 3
"Selective breeding increases genetic variation."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Selective breeding DECREASES genetic variation because only individuals with desired traits are bred, reducing the gene pool.

---

### Statement 4
"Inbreeding can cause health problems."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Inbreeding (breeding closely related individuals) can cause health problems because harmful recessive alleles become more likely to be expressed. This leads to reduced fertility and disease resistance.

---

### Statement 5
"Modern crop varieties are the result of selective breeding."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Almost all modern crop varieties have been developed through selective breeding over thousands of years. They produce much higher yields than their wild ancestors.

---

### Statement 6
"Selective breeding can only be used on animals."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Selective breeding can be used on BOTH animals AND plants. Examples include high-yield crops, disease-resistant varieties, and ornamental flowers.

---

### Statement 7
"Monocultures are vulnerable to disease."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Monocultures (single crop variety over large area) are vulnerable because all plants have the same genetic makeup. A disease that affects one plant will likely affect all plants.

---

### Statement 8
"Selective breeding produces immediate results."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Selective breeding takes MANY GENERATIONS to produce significant changes. It is a slow process compared to genetic engineering.

---

### Statement 9
"Dairy cows produce more milk than their wild ancestors."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Modern dairy cows have been selectively bred for thousands of years to produce much more milk than their wild ancestors. This is a direct result of selective breeding.

---

### Statement 10
"Selective breeding and genetic engineering are the same process."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Selective breeding uses traditional breeding methods over many generations. Genetic engineering directly manipulates DNA and can produce changes much more quickly, sometimes across species barriers.

---

### Statement 11
"Traditional crop varieties are still important."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Traditional varieties contain genetic diversity that may be useful for future breeding programs. They may have disease resistance or other traits that could be valuable.

---

### Statement 12
"Dog breeds show great variety due to selective breeding."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: All modern dog breeds were created through selective breeding. From tiny Chihuahuas to massive Great Danes, all breeds descended from wolves and were shaped by human selection.
