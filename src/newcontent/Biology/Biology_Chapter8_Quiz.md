# Chapter 8: Homeostasis and Excretion - Quiz Section

## Exam-Style Questions for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What is homeostasis?

A) The removal of waste products from the body  
B) The maintenance of a constant internal environment  
C) The process of cell division  
D) The production of hormones  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Homeostasis is the maintenance of a constant internal environment despite changes in external conditions. It involves keeping factors such as temperature, blood glucose, and water content within narrow limits to ensure optimal conditions for enzyme activity and cell function.

---

### Question 2
Which part of the brain controls body temperature?

A) Cerebrum  
B) Cerebellum  
C) Hypothalamus  
D) Medulla  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The hypothalamus, located at the base of the brain, acts as the body's thermoregulatory centre. It contains thermoreceptors that detect blood temperature and coordinates responses to maintain body temperature around 37°C.

---

### Question 3
What happens to blood vessels in the skin when the body is too hot?

A) They constrict to reduce blood flow  
B) They dilate to increase blood flow  
C) They close completely  
D) They become thicker  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: When the body is too hot, blood vessels in the skin dilate (vasodilation), allowing more blood to flow near the skin surface. This increases heat loss through radiation, helping to cool the body down.

---

### Question 4
Which hormone controls water reabsorption in the kidneys?

A) Insulin  
B) Adrenaline  
C) ADH (antidiuretic hormone)  
D) Thyroxine  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: ADH (antidiuretic hormone), also known as vasopressin, controls water reabsorption in the kidneys. It makes the collecting ducts more permeable to water, allowing more water to be reabsorbed back into the blood, reducing urine volume.

---

### Question 5
Urea is produced in the:

A) Kidneys  
B) Liver  
C) Pancreas  
D) Stomach  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Urea is produced in the liver through the process of deamination. Excess amino acids cannot be stored, so they are deaminated - the amino group is removed and converted to urea, while the rest of the molecule can be used for energy.

---

### Question 6
When blood water content is too low:

A) Less ADH is released  
B) More ADH is released  
C) Insulin is released  
D) The kidneys stop working  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: When blood water content is too low (concentrated blood), osmoreceptors in the hypothalamus detect this and stimulate the release of more ADH from the pituitary gland. ADH increases water reabsorption in the kidneys, producing less but more concentrated urine.

---

### Question 7
What is the main excretory product removed by the lungs?

A) Urea  
B) Ammonia  
C) Carbon dioxide  
D) Uric acid  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Carbon dioxide, a waste product of cellular respiration, is excreted by the lungs. It diffuses from the blood into the alveoli and is exhaled. Urea and uric acid are excreted by the kidneys.

---

### Question 8
Shivering when cold helps to:

A) Increase heat loss  
B) Generate heat through muscle contraction  
C) Constrict blood vessels  
D) Produce sweat  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Shivering involves rapid, involuntary muscle contractions that generate heat as a by-product. This helps to warm the body when it is too cold. Shivering is one of the body's responses to lower than normal temperature.

---

### Question 9
Which process in the kidneys filters small molecules from blood into the nephron?

A) Selective reabsorption  
B) Ultrafiltration  
C) Osmosis  
D) Active transport  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Ultrafiltration is the process by which small molecules (water, glucose, urea, salts) are filtered from the blood in the glomerulus into the Bowman's capsule. Large molecules (proteins, blood cells) are too big to pass through.

---

### Question 10
When body temperature is too cold, hairs on the skin:

A) Lie flat to reduce insulation  
B) Stand erect to trap air  
C) Fall out  
D) Produce sweat  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: When cold, the erector pili muscles contract, causing hairs to stand erect. This traps a layer of air next to the skin, providing insulation and reducing heat loss. When hot, hairs lie flat to reduce insulation.

---

### Question 11
Dialysis is used to treat:

A) Diabetes  
B) Kidney failure  
C) Liver failure  
D) Heart disease  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Dialysis is a treatment for kidney failure. It artificially filters the blood to remove waste products like urea, excess salts, and water. It does not cure kidney failure but keeps patients alive while waiting for a transplant.

---

### Question 12
What is deamination?

A) The breakdown of proteins into amino acids  
B) The removal of the amino group from amino acids  
C) The production of glucose from amino acids  
D) The absorption of amino acids in the intestine  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Deamination is the removal of the amino group (NH₂) from amino acids. This occurs in the liver when there are excess amino acids. The amino group is converted to urea and excreted, while the remaining carbon skeleton can be used for energy.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Homeostasis involves positive feedback mechanisms."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Homeostasis primarily involves NEGATIVE feedback mechanisms, not positive feedback. Negative feedback reverses changes to bring conditions back to normal. Positive feedback amplifies changes and is rare in homeostasis (e.g., blood clotting, childbirth).

---

### Statement 2
"Vasodilation increases heat loss through the skin."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Vasodilation widens blood vessels in the skin, allowing more blood to flow near the surface. This increases heat loss through radiation, helping to cool the body when it is too hot.

---

### Statement 3
"Urea is produced in the kidneys."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Urea is produced in the LIVER through deamination of excess amino acids. The kidneys filter urea from the blood and excrete it in urine, but they do not produce it.

---

### Statement 4
"ADH makes the collecting duct more permeable to water."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: ADH increases the permeability of the collecting duct in the kidney nephron to water. This allows more water to be reabsorbed back into the blood, reducing urine volume and concentration.

---

### Statement 5
"Excretion is the removal of undigested food from the body."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Excretion is the removal of METABOLIC WASTE products (like urea, carbon dioxide) from the body. The removal of undigested food is called egestion or defecation, not excretion.

---

### Statement 6
"Sweating cools the body because sweat evaporates."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: When sweat evaporates from the skin surface, it requires energy (latent heat of vaporisation). This heat energy is taken from the body, cooling it down. This is why sweating is an effective cooling mechanism.

---

### Statement 7
"When blood is too concentrated, less ADH is released."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: When blood is too concentrated (low water content), MORE ADH is released, not less. This increases water reabsorption in the kidneys, diluting the blood back to normal. Less ADH is released when blood is too dilute.

---

### Statement 8
"Glucose is normally found in urine."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Glucose is NOT normally found in urine. All filtered glucose is normally reabsorbed in the kidney tubules. The presence of glucose in urine (glycosuria) indicates diabetes or kidney problems.

---

### Statement 9
"The hypothalamus controls both temperature and water balance."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: The hypothalamus acts as the body's control centre for both thermoregulation and osmoregulation. It contains thermoreceptors for temperature control and osmoreceptors for monitoring blood water concentration.

---

### Statement 10
"Ultrafiltration occurs in the glomerulus."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Ultrafiltration occurs in the glomerulus, a network of capillaries in the kidney. Blood pressure forces small molecules (water, glucose, urea, salts) through the capillary walls into Bowman's capsule, while large molecules and cells remain in the blood.

---

### Statement 11
"Vasoconstriction helps the body lose heat."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Vasoconstriction (narrowing of blood vessels) REDUCES heat loss by limiting blood flow to the skin surface. It helps the body retain heat when it is too cold. Vasodilation increases heat loss.

---

### Statement 12
"Kidney transplant can cure kidney failure."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: A successful kidney transplant can restore normal kidney function and cure kidney failure, unlike dialysis which only manages the condition. However, transplant recipients need immunosuppressant medication for life to prevent rejection.
