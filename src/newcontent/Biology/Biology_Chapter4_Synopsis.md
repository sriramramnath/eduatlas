## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

## 1. Nutrients Summary Table

| Nutrient | Main Sources | Main Function(s) | Deficiency Disease |
|----------|--------------|------------------|-------------------|
| **Carbohydrates** | Rice, bread, potatoes, pasta | Main energy source | - |
| **Proteins** | Meat, fish, eggs, beans | Growth, repair, enzymes, antibodies | Kwashiorkor |
| **Lipids (Fats)** | Butter, oils, dairy | Energy store, insulation, cell membranes | - |
| **Vitamin A** | Liver, carrots, dairy | Vision (rhodopsin), skin health | Night blindness |
| **Vitamin C** | Citrus fruits, peppers | Collagen synthesis, wound healing | Scurvy |
| **Vitamin D** | Sunlight, oily fish, eggs | Calcium absorption, bone health | Rickets |
| **Calcium** | Dairy, green vegetables | Bones, teeth, blood clotting | Rickets, osteoporosis |
| **Iron** | Red meat, liver, spinach | Haemoglobin component | Anaemia |
| **Fibre** | Whole grains, vegetables | Adds bulk, prevents constipation | Constipation |
| **Water** | Drinks, food | Solvent, transport, temperature regulation | Dehydration |

## 2. Food Tests

| Test | Reagent | Procedure | Positive Result |
|------|---------|-----------|-----------------|
| **Starch** | Iodine solution | Add iodine to food sample | Blue-black colour |
| **Reducing sugars** | Benedict's solution | Add Benedict's, heat in water bath | Brick red precipitate (from blue) |
| **Protein** | Biuret reagent | Add NaOH then CuSO₄ | Purple/violet colour |
| **Lipids** | Ethanol + water | Shake with ethanol, add water | Cloudy white emulsion |
| **Vitamin C** | DCPIP | Add food extract to DCPIP | Decolourisation (from blue) |

## 3. The Digestive System

### Structure and Function

| Organ | Function |
|-------|----------|
| **Mouth** | Mechanical digestion (teeth); starch digestion (salivary amylase) |
| **Oesophagus** | Transports food by peristalsis |
| **Stomach** | Protein digestion (pepsin); acidic conditions kill bacteria |
| **Liver** | Produces bile for fat emulsification |
| **Gall bladder** | Stores bile |
| **Pancreas** | Produces digestive enzymes (amylase, trypsin, lipase) |
| **Small intestine** | Main site of digestion and absorption |
| **Large intestine** | Absorbs water; forms faeces |

---

## 4. Digestive Enzymes

| Enzyme | Source | Substrate | Product | Site | Optimum pH |
|--------|--------|-----------|---------|------|------------|
| Salivary amylase | Salivary glands | Starch | Maltose | Mouth | pH 7 |
| Pepsin | Stomach wall | Proteins | Peptides | Stomach | pH 2 |
| Pancreatic amylase | Pancreas | Starch | Maltose | Duodenum | pH 8 |
| Trypsin | Pancreas | Proteins | Amino acids | Duodenum | pH 8 |
| Lipase | Pancreas | Fats | Fatty acids + glycerol | Duodenum | pH 8 |
| Maltase | Intestinal wall | Maltose | Glucose | Ileum | pH 8 |

---

## 5. Role of Bile

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Emulsification** | Breaks large fat droplets into smaller droplets → increases surface area for lipase |
| **Neutralisation** | Alkaline bile neutralises stomach acid → suitable pH for pancreatic enzymes |

*Note: Bile does NOT digest fats - only lipase does that!*

---

## 6. Adaptations of Small Intestine for Absorption

| Adaptation | How it Helps |
|------------|--------------|
| **Very long** (6m) | More time for absorption |
| **Villi** | Increase surface area |
| **Microvilli** | Further increase surface area |
| **Thin walls** (1 cell thick) | Short diffusion distance |
| **Blood capillaries** | Rapid removal of glucose and amino acids |
| **Lacteals** | Absorb fats (fatty acids and glycerol) |

---

## 7. Peristalsis

- Wave of muscular contraction
- Moves food along the gut
- Circular muscles contract behind food
- Longitudinal muscles relax ahead of food
- Involuntary (automatic) process

---

## 8. Digestion Summary

| Nutrient | Digestion Pathway |
|----------|-------------------|
| **Starch** | Starch → (amylase) → Maltose → (maltase) → Glucose |
| **Proteins** | Proteins → (protease/pepsin/trypsin) → Peptides → (peptidases) → Amino acids |
| **Fats** | Fats → (bile - emulsification) → Small droplets → (lipase) → Fatty acids + Glycerol |

---

## 9. Absorption and Transport

| Nutrient | Absorbed into | Transport |
|----------|--------------|-----------|
| Glucose | Blood capillaries | Hepatic portal vein → Liver |
| Amino acids | Blood capillaries | Hepatic portal vein → Liver |
| Fatty acids & glycerol | Lacteal | Lymph → Blood |

---

## 10. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Digestion** | Breakdown of large insoluble molecules into small soluble molecules |
| **Absorption** | Movement of digested food through gut wall into blood |
| **Assimilation** | Use of absorbed nutrients by cells |
| **Egestion** | Removal of undigested food as faeces |
| **Peristalsis** | Wave-like muscular contraction moving food along gut |
| **Villus** | Finger-like projection increasing surface area for absorption |
| **Lacteal** | Lymph vessel in villus for fat absorption |

---

## 11. Balanced Diet

### Energy Requirements Vary With:
- Age (young need more per kg for growth)
- Gender (males generally need more)
- Activity level (active need more)
- Pregnancy/breastfeeding (need extra)

### Malnutrition
- **Undernutrition**: Not enough nutrients (e.g., kwashiorkor, anaemia)
- **Overnutrition**: Too much food (obesity, type 2 diabetes)

---

## 12. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Digestion = insoluble → soluble
2. Bile emulsifies (physical) but does NOT digest (chemical)
3. Most absorption in small intestine, NOT stomach
4. Villi increase surface area for absorption
5. Fats absorbed into lacteals, not blood capillaries

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Saying bile digests fats (it only emulsifies)
- Confusing egestion with excretion
- Forgetting to mention "insoluble to soluble"
- Saying absorption occurs in stomach
- Confusing lacteal with capillary
