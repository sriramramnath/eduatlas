# Chapter 22: Cloning - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Definition

**Clone**: Genetically identical copy of an organism or cells

---

## 2. Natural Cloning

| Organism | Method |
|----------|--------|
| **Animals** | Identical twins |
| **Plants** | Runners, rhizomes, tubers, bulbs |

---

## 3. Plant Cloning Methods

### Tissue Culture (Micropropagation)

```
1. Take small piece of plant tissue
         ↓
2. Sterilise and place on agar
         ↓
3. Cells divide → callus
         ↓
4. Callus divided
         ↓
5. Hormones stimulate shoots and roots
         ↓
6. Plantlets transferred to soil
```

### Advantages
- Many plants quickly
- Disease-free
- Can clone rare plants
- Small space

### Disadvantages
- Expensive
- Risk of contamination
- All identical (disease risk)

---

## 4. Animal Cloning Methods

### Embryo Transplanting
- Divide embryo into pieces
- Each develops into complete embryo
- Implant into surrogate mothers

### Adult Cell Cloning (Somatic Cell Nuclear Transfer)

```
1. Remove nucleus from egg cell
         ↓
2. Insert nucleus from adult body cell
         ↓
3. Electric shock starts division
         ↓
4. Embryo implanted in surrogate
         ↓
5. Offspring is clone of adult donor
```

---

## 5. Dolly the Sheep

- First mammal cloned from adult cell (1996)
- Roslin Institute, Scotland
- Proof adult cells can create whole organism
- Shorter than normal lifespan

---

## 6. Advantages of Animal Cloning

- Clone desirable traits (milk yield)
- Medical research (identical test subjects)
- Medicine production
- Endangered species conservation

---

## 7. Concerns

### Ethical
- Animal welfare issues
- Playing God
- Human cloning fears

### Scientific
- Low success rate
- Health problems in clones
- Reduced genetic diversity

---

## 8. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Clone** | Genetically identical copy |
| **Tissue culture** | Growing cells in artificial medium |
| **Surrogate mother** | Carries cloned embryo |
| **Enucleation** | Removing nucleus from cell |

---

## 9. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Clones formed by mitosis
2. Tissue culture for plants
3. Nuclear transfer for animals
4. All clones genetically identical
5. Discuss both benefits AND concerns

### Common Mistakes
- Confusing cloning methods
- Forgetting mitosis
- Not explaining steps in order
- Only discussing advantages
- Not mentioning ethical issues
