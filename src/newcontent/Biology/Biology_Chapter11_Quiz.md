# Chapter 11: Transport in Plants - Quiz Section

## Exam-Style Questions for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
Which tissue transports water and minerals in plants?

A) Phloem  
B) Xylem  
C) Parenchyma  
D) Epidermis  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Xylem is the tissue specialised for transporting water and dissolved mineral ions from roots to leaves. It consists of dead cells with lignified walls forming continuous tubes. Phloem transports sugars, parenchyma stores food, and epidermis is a protective layer.

---

### Question 2
What is the function of root hair cells?

A) To anchor the plant in soil  
B) To increase surface area for water absorption  
C) To store starch  
D) To produce hormones  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Root hair cells are extensions of root epidermal cells that greatly increase the surface area for absorption of water and mineral ions from the soil. Their thin walls also facilitate rapid water uptake by osmosis.

---

### Question 3
Transpiration is defined as:

A) The movement of water through a plant  
B) The loss of water vapour from leaves  
C) The absorption of water by roots  
D) The transport of sugars in phloem  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Transpiration is specifically defined as the loss of water VAPOUR from the aerial parts of a plant, mainly through the stomata of leaves. It is NOT the same as water movement through the plant.

---

### Question 4
Which factor would DECREASE the rate of transpiration?

A) Increasing temperature  
B) Decreasing humidity  
C) Increasing humidity  
D) Increasing wind speed  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Increasing humidity means there is more water vapour in the air surrounding the leaf. This reduces the concentration gradient between the inside and outside of the leaf, slowing down the diffusion of water vapour out through stomata.

---

### Question 5
Xylem vessels are:

A) Living cells with nuclei  
B) Dead cells with no end walls  
C) Living cells with sieve plates  
D) Dead cells with companion cells  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Xylem vessels are formed from dead cells that have lost their nucleus, cytoplasm, and end walls. This creates continuous hollow tubes for water flow. The lack of end walls removes barriers to water movement.

---

### Question 6
Phloem transports:

A) Water and minerals  
B) Carbon dioxide  
C) Sucrose and amino acids  
D) Oxygen  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Phloem transports dissolved organic substances, mainly sucrose (the transport form of sugar in plants) and amino acids, from sources (where they are made) to sinks (where they are needed).

---

### Question 7
What creates the "pull" that draws water up through xylem?

A) Root pressure  
B) Capillary action  
C) Transpiration pull  
D) Gravity  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Transpiration pull is created when water evaporates from mesophyll cells and diffuses out through stomata. This creates tension (negative pressure) in the leaf that is transmitted down the xylem, pulling water upward from the roots.

---

### Question 8
Companion cells are associated with:

A) Xylem vessels  
B) Sieve tube elements  
C) Root hair cells  
D) Guard cells  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Companion cells are located alongside sieve tube elements in phloem. They contain a nucleus and many mitochondria, providing energy and proteins to support the sieve tube elements, which lack these organelles.

---

### Question 9
In translocation, a "sink" refers to:

A) The place where sugars are produced  
B) The place where sugars are used or stored  
C) The roots of the plant  
D) The xylem vessels  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A sink is any part of the plant where sugars are used for growth, respiration, or stored as starch. Examples include growing root tips, shoot tips, developing fruits, and storage organs like potato tubers.

---

### Question 10
What is the role of lignin in xylem?

A) To transport sugars  
B) To strengthen cell walls and make them waterproof  
C) To allow water to pass through  
D) To store starch  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Lignin is deposited in the walls of xylem vessels, making them thick, strong, and waterproof. This provides structural support and allows xylem to withstand the tension created by transpiration pull.

---

### Question 11
The Casparian strip is found in:

A) Root hair cells  
B) Xylem vessels  
C) Endodermal cells  
D) Phloem sieve tubes  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The Casparian strip is a waterproof band in the cell walls of endodermal cells in roots. It blocks the apoplast pathway, forcing water and minerals to pass through the cytoplasm of endodermal cells, allowing selective uptake.

---

### Question 12
A potometer measures:

A) Rate of photosynthesis  
B) Rate of water uptake  
C) Rate of translocation  
D) Rate of respiration  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A potometer measures the rate of water uptake by a plant. Since most water taken up is lost through transpiration, this gives an estimate of transpiration rate. The apparatus typically measures movement of an air bubble in a capillary tube.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Xylem transports sugars from leaves to roots."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Xylem transports WATER and MINERALS from roots to leaves. It is PHLOEM that transports sugars (sucrose) and other organic substances from source to sink, which can be in any direction.

---

### Statement 2
"Root hair cells increase the surface area for water absorption."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Root hair cells are elongated extensions of root epidermal cells. Their hair-like shape greatly increases the surface area of the root in contact with soil water, maximising water and mineral ion absorption.

---

### Statement 3
"Transpiration only occurs during the day."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Transpiration occurs whenever stomata are open. While stomata are typically open during the day for photosynthesis, some transpiration can occur at night when stomata are partially open. However, the rate is much lower at night.

---

### Statement 4
"Phloem consists of living cells."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Phloem tissue contains living cells, including sieve tube elements and companion cells. This is essential because translocation requires active transport of sugars, which can only occur in living cells with mitochondria to produce ATP.

---

### Statement 5
"Xylem vessels have sieve plates."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Xylem vessels have NO end walls at all - they form continuous tubes. It is PHLOEM sieve tubes that have sieve plates (perforated end walls). Xylem vessels are completely hollow with no barriers to water flow.

---

### Statement 6
"Increasing wind speed increases the rate of transpiration."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Wind removes water vapour from around the leaf surface, maintaining a steep concentration gradient between the inside and outside of the leaf. This increases the rate of diffusion of water vapour out through stomata.

---

### Statement 7
"Translocation requires energy from respiration."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Translocation involves active loading of sucrose into phloem sieve tubes at the source. This requires ATP from respiration. The companion cells, which have many mitochondria, provide the energy needed for this active transport.

---

### Statement 8
"Cohesion refers to the attraction between water molecules and xylem walls."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Cohesion refers to attraction BETWEEN water molecules (due to hydrogen bonds). ADHESION refers to attraction between water molecules and xylem walls. Both are important for water movement up xylem.

---

### Statement 9
"Leaves are examples of sources in translocation."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Leaves are the main sources in plants because they produce glucose through photosynthesis. This glucose is converted to sucrose and loaded into phloem for transport to sinks. Storage organs can also be sources when they break down stored starch.

---

### Statement 10
"The xylem transports water in both directions."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Xylem transports water in ONE direction only - upward from roots to leaves. The transpiration pull creates a one-way flow. It is PHLOEM that can transport in both directions (from source to sink, wherever they are).

---

### Statement 11
"Guard cells control the opening and closing of stomata."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Guard cells are pairs of specialised cells surrounding each stoma. When guard cells take up water and become turgid, they curve apart, opening the stomatal pore. When they lose water and become flaccid, the pore closes.

---

### Statement 12
"Translocation is a passive process."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Translocation is an ACTIVE process. It requires energy (ATP) for the active loading of sucrose into phloem sieve tubes at the source. This is why phloem consists of living cells with mitochondria.

### Question 14 (5 marks)
Describe the process of transpiration and explain its importance to plants.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**

**Process:**
- Water evaporates from mesophyll cell walls [1]
- Water vapour diffuses out through stomata [1]
- Creates tension in xylem (transpiration pull) [1]

**Importance:**
- Supplies water to leaves for photosynthesis [1]
- Transports minerals from roots [1]
- Cools leaves by evaporation [1]
- Maintains turgidity of cells [1]
