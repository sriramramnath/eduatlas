# Chapter 14: Ecosystems - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Ecosystem** | Community + physical (abiotic) environment |
| **Community** | All populations of different species in an area |
| **Population** | Organisms of same species in an area |
| **Habitat** | Place where an organism lives |
| **Niche** | Role of organism in ecosystem |

---

## 2. Biotic vs Abiotic Factors

| Biotic (Living) | Abiotic (Non-living) |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| Competition | Temperature |
| Predation | Light intensity |
| Disease | Water availability |
| Food availability | Soil pH |
| Parasitism | Oxygen concentration |
| Mutualism | CO₂ concentration |

---

## 3. Food Chains

### Structure
```
Producer → Primary Consumer → Secondary Consumer → Tertiary Consumer
   ↓              ↓                   ↓                    ↓
 (plant)      (herbivore)         (carnivore)         (top carnivore)
```

### Key Terms
| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Producer** | Makes food by photosynthesis |
| **Primary consumer** | Eats producers (herbivore) |
| **Secondary consumer** | Eats primary consumers |
| **Tertiary consumer** | Eats secondary consumers |
| **Herbivore** | Eats only plants |
| **Carnivore** | Eats only animals |
| **Omnivore** | Eats plants and animals |
| **Decomposer** | Breaks down dead organisms |

---

## 4. Food Webs

- Interconnected food chains
- Show complex feeding relationships
- Arrows show energy flow direction
- More stable than single food chains

---

## 5. Trophic Levels and Energy Transfer

| Trophic Level | Organisms | Energy |
|---------------|-----------|--------|
| 1st | Producers | Most energy |
| 2nd | Primary consumers | ~10% of level 1 |
| 3rd | Secondary consumers | ~10% of level 2 |
| 4th | Tertiary consumers | ~10% of level 3 |

### Why Energy is Lost
- Respiration (heat)
- Movement
- Body temperature maintenance
- Undigested food
- Not all organisms eaten
- Excretion

---

## 6. Carbon Cycle Summary

| Process | Description | CO₂ Effect |
|---------|-------------|------------|
| **Photosynthesis** | Plants make glucose | Removes CO₂ |
| **Respiration** | All organisms | Releases CO₂ |
| **Decomposition** | Decomposers | Releases CO₂ |
| **Combustion** | Burning | Releases CO₂ |
| **Fossilisation** | Forms fossil fuels | Stores carbon |

---

## 7. Nitrogen Cycle Summary

| Process | What Happens | Bacteria Involved |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Nitrogen fixation** | N₂ → NH₄⁺ | Rhizobium, Azotobacter |
| **Nitrification** | NH₄⁺ → NO₃⁻ | Nitrosomonas, Nitrobacter |
| **Denitrification** | NO₃⁻ → N₂ | Pseudomonas |
| **Ammonification** | Proteins → NH₄⁺ | Decomposers |

---

## 8. Population Growth

### Factors Affecting Population Size
- Births (↑)
- Deaths (↓)
- Immigration (↑)
- Emigration (↓)

### Sigmoid Curve Phases
1. **Lag phase**: Slow growth
2. **Log phase**: Rapid growth
3. **Stationary phase**: Carrying capacity reached
4. **Death phase**: Population declines

### Carrying Capacity
Maximum population size ecosystem can support

## 9. Ecological Pyramids

| Type | Shows | Shape |
|------|-------|-------|
| **Numbers** | Count of organisms | Can be inverted |
| **Biomass** | Mass at each level | Always pyramid |
| **Energy** | Energy at each level | Always pyramid |

---

## 10. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Food chain arrows show energy flow TO consumer
2. Only ~10% energy transferred between levels
3. Community = living; Ecosystem = living + non-living
4. Decomposers essential for nutrient cycles
5. CO₂ removed by photosynthesis; released by respiration

### Common Mistakes
- Arrows pointing wrong direction in food chains
- Confusing community with ecosystem
- Saying plants "breathe" CO₂
- Forgetting to name specific bacteria
- Not explaining energy loss mechanisms
