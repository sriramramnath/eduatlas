# Chapter 17: Cell Division - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. The Cell Cycle

```
INTERPHASE → MITOSIS → CYTOKINESIS
  (longest)            (division)
```

### Interphase Phases
- **G1**: Growth, organelles made
- **S**: DNA replication
- **G2**: Preparation for mitosis

---

## 2. Mitosis (PMAT)

| Stage | What Happens |
|-------|--------------|
| **Prophase** | Chromosomes condense, nucleus disappears, spindle forms |
| **Metaphase** | Chromosomes line up at equator |
| **Anaphase** | Chromatids separate, pulled to poles |
| **Telophase** | Two new nuclei form, chromosomes uncoil |

### Purpose of Mitosis
- Growth
- Repair
- Asexual reproduction

---

## 3. Mitosis vs Meiosis

| Feature | Mitosis | Meiosis |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Divisions | 1 | 2 |
| Daughter cells | 2 | 4 |
| Chromosome number | Same (diploid) | Half (haploid) |
| Genetic variation | None | Yes |
| Purpose | Growth, repair | Gametes |

---

## 4. Cytokinesis

| Animal Cells | Plant Cells |
|--------------|-------------|
| Membrane pinches in | Cell plate forms |
| Cleavage furrow | New cell wall |

---

## 5. Chromosome Numbers

| Cell Type | Human Number |
|-----------|--------------|
| Body cells | 46 (diploid, 2n) |
| Gametes | 23 (haploid, n) |
| Zygote | 46 (diploid, 2n) |

---

## 6. Stem Cells

| Type | Source | Potential |
|------|--------|-----------|
| **Embryonic** | Early embryo | Any cell type (pluripotent) |
| **Adult** | Bone marrow, tissues | Limited types (multipotent) |

### Uses
- Bone marrow transplants
- Treating leukaemia
- Research for disease treatment

---

## 7. Cancer

### Tumours

| Benign | Malignant |
|--------|-----------|
| Slow growth | Rapid growth |
| Doesn't spread | Spreads (metastasis) |
| Enclosed | Not enclosed |
| Not usually fatal | Can be fatal |

### Causes
- Carcinogens (chemicals, radiation)
- Viruses
- Genetic factors

---

## 8. Meiosis Stages

**Meiosis I** (Reduction division):
- Homologous chromosomes separate
- Crossing over occurs

**Meiosis II** (Like mitosis):
- Sister chromatids separate

### Sources of Variation
1. **Crossing over** - Prophase I
2. **Independent assortment** - Metaphase I
3. **Random fertilisation** - Any sperm + any egg

---

## 9. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Mitosis** | Division producing 2 identical cells |
| **Meiosis** | Division producing 4 gametes |
| **Diploid (2n)** | Full chromosome set |
| **Haploid (n)** | Half chromosome set |
| **Stem cell** | Undifferentiated cell |
| **Cancer** | Uncontrolled cell division |
| **Metastasis** | Spread of cancer |

---

## 10. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. PMAT: Prophase, Metaphase, Anaphase, Telophase
2. Mitosis = 2 identical cells; Meiosis = 4 different gametes
3. Body cells = 46 chromosomes; Gametes = 23
4. Stem cells can differentiate into specialised cells
5. Crossing over creates genetic variation

### Common Mistakes
- Confusing mitosis and meiosis
- Forgetting cytokinesis
- Wrong chromosome numbers
- Not explaining importance of mitosis
- Confusing chromatids and chromosomes
