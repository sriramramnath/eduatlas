# Chapter 1: Life Processes - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. The Seven Life Processes (MRS GREN)

| Process | Definition | Key Example |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| **M**ovement | Change in position of organism or body part | Animals moving; plants growing towards light |
| **R**espiration | Chemical reactions releasing energy from glucose | Glucose + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O + Energy |
| **S**ensitivity | Detecting and responding to stimuli | Reflex actions; tropisms |
| **G**rowth | Permanent increase in size and dry mass | Seedling to mature plant |
| **R**eproduction | Making more of the same kind of organism | Sexual or asexual reproduction |
| **E**xcretion | Removal of metabolic waste products | CO₂ from lungs; urea from kidneys |
| **N**utrition | Obtaining nutrients for energy and growth | Photosynthesis; ingestion and digestion |

## 2. Cell Structure

### Animal Cell Components
- **Cell membrane**: Controls entry/exit of substances
- **Cytoplasm**: Site of chemical reactions
- **Nucleus**: Contains genetic material, controls cell
- **Mitochondria**: Site of aerobic respiration
- **Ribosomes**: Site of protein synthesis

### Plant Cell Additional Components
- **Cell wall** (cellulose): Provides support, prevents bursting
- **Chloroplasts**: Site of photosynthesis
- **Permanent vacuole**: Contains cell sap, maintains turgidity

### Cell Comparisons

| Structure | Animal | Plant |
|-----------|--------|-------|
| Cell membrane | ✓ | ✓ |
| Nucleus | ✓ | ✓ |
| Mitochondria | ✓ | ✓ |
| Ribosomes | ✓ | ✓ |
| Cell wall | ✗ | ✓ |
| Chloroplasts | ✗ | ✓ (green parts) |
| Vacuole | Small/None | Large, central |

## 3. Specialised Cells

### Animal Cells

| Cell | Adaptation | Function |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Red blood cell** | Biconcave shape, no nucleus, haemoglobin | Oxygen transport |
| **Nerve cell** | Long axon, dendrites, myelin sheath | Transmit impulses |
| **Muscle cell** | Protein fibres, many mitochondria | Contraction |
| **Sperm cell** | Flagellum, mitochondria, acrosome | Fertilisation |

### Plant Cells

| Cell | Adaptation | Function |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Palisade cell** | Many chloroplasts, columnar shape | Photosynthesis |
| **Root hair cell** | Long extension, thin wall | Water/mineral absorption |
| **Guard cell** | Uneven wall thickening | Control stomatal opening |

## 4. Movement Across Membranes

### Transport Comparison Table

| Feature | Diffusion | Osmosis | Active Transport |
|---------|-----------|---------|------------------|
| **Definition** | Movement from high to low concentration | Water movement through partially permeable membrane | Movement against concentration gradient |
| **Energy needed?** | No | No | Yes (ATP) |
| **Membrane needed?** | No | Yes | Yes |
| **Carrier proteins?** | No | No | Yes |
| **Direction** | High → Low concentration | High → Low water potential | Low → High concentration |

### Osmosis Terminology

| Term | Definition | Effect on Animal Cell | Effect on Plant Cell |
|------|------------|----------------------|---------------------|
| **Hypotonic** | Lower solute concentration than cell | Swells/bursts (lysis) | Becomes turgid |
| **Hypertonic** | Higher solute concentration than cell | Shrinks (crenation) | Becomes plasmolysed |
| **Isotonic** | Same concentration as cell | No change | Flaccid |

### Factors Affecting Diffusion Rate
- **Concentration gradient** - steeper = faster
- **Temperature** - higher = faster
- **Surface area** - larger = faster
- **Distance** - shorter = faster

## 5. Enzymes

### Key Points
- **Definition**: Biological catalysts (proteins)
- **Function**: Speed up reactions without being used up
- **Specificity**: Each enzyme catalyses one reaction
- **Active site**: Where substrate binds (lock and key model)

### Factors Affecting Enzyme Activity

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Temperature** | Increases to optimum (usually 37-40°C), then denatures above |
| **pH** | Each enzyme has optimum pH; extreme pH causes denaturation |
| **Substrate concentration** | Increases until saturation point (Vmax) |
| **Enzyme concentration** | Increases reaction rate if substrate is in excess |

### Digestive Enzymes

| Enzyme | Substrate | Products | Location |
|--------|-----------|----------|----------|
| Amylase | Starch | Maltose | Mouth, small intestine |
| Maltase | Maltose | Glucose | Small intestine |
| Protease | Proteins | Amino acids | Stomach, small intestine |
| Lipase | Fats | Fatty acids + glycerol | Small intestine |

### Enzyme Optimum pH Values

| Enzyme | Optimum pH | Location |
|--------|------------|----------|
| Pepsin | pH 2 | Stomach |
| Salivary amylase | pH 7 | Mouth |
| Pancreatic enzymes | pH 8 | Small intestine |

## 6. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Respiration** | Chemical reactions releasing energy from glucose in cells |
| **Breathing** | Mechanical movement of air in/out of lungs |
| **Excretion** | Removal of metabolic waste (CO₂, urea) |
| **Egestion** | Removal of undigested food |
| **Diffusion** | Net movement from high to low concentration |
| **Osmosis** | Net water movement through partially permeable membrane from high to low water potential |
| **Active transport** | Movement against concentration gradient using ATP |
| **Denaturation** | Permanent loss of enzyme function due to shape change |

---

## 7. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. **Respiration ≠ Breathing**: Respiration is chemical, breathing is mechanical
2. **Excretion ≠ Egestion**: Metabolic waste vs undigested food
3. **Enzymes denature, not die**: Proteins change shape, they are not alive
4. **Osmosis requires a partially permeable membrane**: Essential for definition
5. **Active transport needs ATP**: Energy from respiration

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Saying water moves "from weak to strong solution"
- Forgetting to mention "partially permeable membrane" in osmosis
- Saying enzymes are "killed" or "destroyed"
- Confusing isotonic/hypotonic/hypertonic
- Drawing linear temperature-enzyme graphs (should be bell-shaped)

## 8. Practical Summary

### Potato Osmosis Experiment
- **Method**: Place potato cylinders in different sugar concentrations
- **Results**: 
  - Gains mass in dilute solution (water enters)
  - Loses mass in concentrated solution (water leaves)
  - No change at isotonic point

### Catalase Temperature Experiment
- **Method**: Test hydrogen peroxide breakdown at different temperatures
- **Results**: Highest foam/oxygen at optimum temperature
- **Graph**: Bell-shaped curve
