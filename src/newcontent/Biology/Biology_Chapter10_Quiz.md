## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What is the word equation for photosynthesis?

A) Glucose + Oxygen → Carbon dioxide + Water  
B) Carbon dioxide + Water → Glucose + Oxygen  
C) Carbon dioxide + Oxygen → Glucose + Water  
D) Water + Oxygen → Glucose + Carbon dioxide  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Photosynthesis uses carbon dioxide and water as raw materials, with light energy captured by chlorophyll, to produce glucose and oxygen. The equation is: Carbon dioxide + Water → (light + chlorophyll) → Glucose + Oxygen.

---

### Question 2
Where does photosynthesis mainly occur in a plant?

A) Roots  
B) Stem  
C) Chloroplasts  
D) Nucleus  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Photosynthesis occurs in chloroplasts, which are organelles found mainly in the mesophyll cells of leaves. Chloroplasts contain chlorophyll and the enzymes needed for photosynthesis. Roots and stems do not typically photosynthesise.

---

### Question 3
Which part of the leaf contains the most chloroplasts?

A) Upper epidermis  
B) Palisade mesophyll  
C) Spongy mesophyll  
D) Lower epidermis  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The palisade mesophyll cells, located just below the upper epidermis, contain the most chloroplasts. These cells are column-shaped and tightly packed to maximise light absorption. They are positioned near the top of the leaf to receive maximum light.

---

### Question 4
What is the function of stomata in leaves?

A) To absorb water from the air  
B) To allow gas exchange  
C) To store starch  
D) To produce chlorophyll  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Stomata are small pores on the leaf surface (mainly lower epidermis) that allow gas exchange. Carbon dioxide enters through stomata for photosynthesis, and oxygen exits as a waste product. Water vapour also leaves through stomata during transpiration.

---

### Question 5
A plant is grown in soil lacking nitrates. What symptom would you expect?

A) Yellow leaves  
B) Stunted growth  
C) Purple leaves  
D) Wilting  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Nitrates are needed to make amino acids and proteins, which are essential for growth. Nitrate deficiency causes stunted growth because the plant cannot make new proteins for cell growth. Yellowing of leaves is a symptom of magnesium deficiency (needed for chlorophyll).

---

### Question 6
What happens to the rate of photosynthesis when light intensity increases from low to moderate levels?

A) It decreases  
B) It stays the same  
C) It increases  
D) It stops completely  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: At low to moderate light intensities, light is the limiting factor. As light intensity increases, more energy is available for the light-dependent reactions, so the rate of photosynthesis increases. This continues until another factor becomes limiting.

---

### Question 7
Why do plants convert glucose to starch?

A) Because starch tastes better  
B) Because starch is insoluble and can be stored  
C) Because glucose is toxic  
D) Because starch is needed for respiration  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Glucose is soluble in water and would affect the water balance of cells if accumulated. Starch is insoluble, so it can be stored in large quantities without affecting the cell's water potential. Starch can be converted back to glucose when needed.

---

### Question 8
Which ion is needed to make chlorophyll?

A) Nitrate  
B) Phosphate  
C) Magnesium  
D) Potassium  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Magnesium ions are essential components of the chlorophyll molecule. Magnesium forms the central atom in the chlorophyll structure. Magnesium deficiency causes yellowing of leaves (chlorosis) because the plant cannot make enough chlorophyll.

---

### Question 9
What is the purpose of the iodine test on a leaf?

A) To test for glucose  
B) To test for starch  
C) To test for chlorophyll  
D) To test for protein  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The iodine test detects the presence of starch. When iodine solution is applied to a leaf that has been de-starched of chlorophyll, a blue-black colour indicates starch is present. This is used to demonstrate that photosynthesis has occurred.

---

### Question 10
At very high temperatures, the rate of photosynthesis decreases because:

A) There is not enough carbon dioxide  
B) The stomata close  
C) Enzymes become denatured  
D) There is too much light  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: At very high temperatures, the enzymes involved in photosynthesis become denatured (their shape changes permanently). This stops them from catalysing reactions, so photosynthesis stops. Temperature is a limiting factor that affects enzyme activity.

---

### Question 11
What is the role of chlorophyll in photosynthesis?

A) To absorb water  
B) To absorb light energy  
C) To absorb carbon dioxide  
D) To release oxygen  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Chlorophyll is a green pigment that absorbs light energy, particularly red and blue wavelengths. This light energy is used to split water molecules and drive the reactions that convert carbon dioxide to glucose.

---

### Question 12
In a variegated leaf, only the green parts test positive for starch because:

A) The green parts are bigger  
B) Only green parts have chlorophyll  
C) The white parts store glucose instead  
D) The white parts photosynthesise faster  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Variegated leaves have green areas (containing chlorophyll) and white/yellow areas (lacking chlorophyll). Only the green parts can carry out photosynthesis because only they contain chlorophyll to absorb light energy. The white parts cannot photosynthesise.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Plants respire only at night."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Plants respire ALL the time (24 hours a day). Respiration releases energy from glucose and is essential for life. During the day, the rate of photosynthesis may exceed respiration, but respiration still occurs. At night, only respiration occurs.

---

### Statement 2
"Carbon dioxide enters leaves through stomata."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Stomata are pores in the leaf epidermis that allow gas exchange. Carbon dioxide diffuses into the leaf through stomata, down a concentration gradient from the air (higher concentration) to inside the leaf (lower concentration, due to photosynthesis).

---

### Statement 3
"Glucose is stored in plants as glycogen."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Plants store glucose as STARCH, not glycogen. Glycogen is the storage carbohydrate in animals (stored in liver and muscles). Starch is an insoluble polysaccharide made of many glucose units joined together.

---

### Statement 4
"Magnesium deficiency causes yellowing of leaves."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Magnesium is needed to make chlorophyll. When magnesium is deficient, the plant cannot produce enough chlorophyll, causing leaves to turn yellow (chlorosis). This typically starts with older leaves as magnesium is mobile within the plant.

---

### Statement 5
"Increasing light intensity always increases the rate of photosynthesis."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Increasing light intensity increases the rate of photosynthesis ONLY up to a point. After this, the rate levels off (plateaus) because another factor (such as CO₂ concentration or temperature) becomes limiting. The relationship is not linear at all light intensities.

---

### Statement 6
"The spongy mesophyll contains air spaces for gas exchange."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: The spongy mesophyll consists of irregularly shaped cells with large air spaces between them. These air spaces allow gases (CO₂, O₂, water vapour) to diffuse throughout the leaf, facilitating gas exchange between the atmosphere and the photosynthetic cells.

---

### Statement 7
"Photosynthesis occurs in the nucleus of plant cells."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Photosynthesis occurs in CHLOROPLASTS, not the nucleus. Chloroplasts are organelles containing chlorophyll and the enzymes needed for photosynthesis. The nucleus contains genetic material and controls cell activities but is not involved in photosynthesis.

---

### Statement 8
"Starch is insoluble in water."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Starch is a polysaccharide made of many glucose molecules joined together. It is insoluble in water, which is why it is a good storage molecule. Being insoluble means it doesn't affect the water potential of cells and can be stored without drawing in water.

---

### Statement 9
"Nitrate ions are needed for making chlorophyll."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Nitrate ions are needed for making AMINO ACIDS and PROTEINS, not chlorophyll. MAGNESIUM ions are needed for making chlorophyll. Nitrogen is a component of the chlorophyll molecule, but the specific ion required for chlorophyll synthesis is magnesium.

---

### Statement 10
"The rate of photosynthesis is affected by temperature."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Temperature affects the rate of photosynthesis because photosynthesis is controlled by enzymes. Higher temperatures increase the rate up to an optimum (around 25-35°C for most plants). Above this, enzymes start to denature and the rate decreases.

---

### Statement 11
"Oxygen is a product of photosynthesis."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Oxygen is produced during photosynthesis as a by-product. It comes from the splitting of water molecules during the light-dependent reactions. The oxygen is released into the atmosphere through stomata.

---

### Statement 12
"Photosynthesis can occur without light."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Photosynthesis REQUIRES light energy to proceed. Light energy is absorbed by chlorophyll and used to split water molecules and produce ATP. Without light, the light-dependent reactions cannot occur, and photosynthesis stops.
