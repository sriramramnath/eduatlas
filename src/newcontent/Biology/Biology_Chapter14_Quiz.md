## Exam-Style Questions for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What is an ecosystem?

A) All the organisms of one species in an area  
B) All the living organisms in an area  
C) All the living and non-living components in an area  
D) All the plants in an area  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: An ecosystem includes both the living (biotic) components - all the organisms and their interactions - AND the non-living (abiotic) components - such as temperature, water, light, and soil. Option A describes a population, and option B describes a community.

---

### Question 2
In a food chain, the arrows show:

A) What eats what  
B) The direction of energy flow  
C) The direction of movement  
D) Which organisms are bigger  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: In food chains, arrows always show the direction of ENERGY FLOW from one organism to the next. Energy flows from the organism being eaten to the consumer. The arrow points TO the consumer, showing that energy is transferred to them.

---

### Question 3
Which process removes carbon dioxide from the atmosphere?

A) Respiration  
B) Photosynthesis  
C) Combustion  
D) Decomposition  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Photosynthesis removes carbon dioxide from the atmosphere as plants use CO₂ and water to make glucose. Respiration, combustion, and decomposition all RELEASE carbon dioxide into the atmosphere.

---

### Question 4
Approximately what percentage of energy is transferred between trophic levels?

A) 1%  
B) 10%  
C) 50%  
D) 90%  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Only about 10% of energy is transferred from one trophic level to the next. The remaining 90% is lost through respiration (heat), movement, body temperature maintenance, undigested food, and excretion.

---

### Question 5
Nitrogen-fixing bacteria convert:

A) Nitrates to nitrogen gas  
B) Nitrogen gas to ammonium compounds  
C) Ammonium to nitrates  
D) Proteins to ammonium  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Nitrogen-fixing bacteria (such as Rhizobium in root nodules) convert atmospheric nitrogen gas (N₂) into ammonium compounds (NH₄⁺) that plants can use. This process is called nitrogen fixation.

---

### Question 6
What type of organism is a mushroom in a food web?

A) Producer  
B) Consumer  
C) Decomposer  
D) Predator  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Mushrooms are fungi that act as decomposers. They break down dead organic matter and waste products, releasing nutrients back into the soil. They are not producers (don't photosynthesise) or consumers (don't eat other organisms).

---

### Question 7
The carrying capacity of an ecosystem refers to:

A) The total number of all species  
B) The maximum population size that can be supported  
C) The number of predators  
D) The amount of food available  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Carrying capacity is the maximum population size of a particular species that an ecosystem can support sustainably over a long period. It is determined by limiting factors such as food, water, space, and disease.

---

### Question 8
Which of the following is an abiotic factor?

A) Competition  
B) Predation  
C) Light intensity  
D) Disease  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Light intensity is an abiotic (non-living) factor. Competition, predation, and disease all involve interactions between living organisms and are therefore biotic factors. Abiotic factors are physical and chemical components of the environment.

---

### Question 9
Primary consumers are:

A) Organisms that make their own food  
B) Herbivores that eat producers  
C) Carnivores that eat herbivores  
D) Decomposers  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Primary consumers are herbivores that eat producers (plants). They are the second trophic level in a food chain. Organisms that make their own food are producers, carnivores are secondary or tertiary consumers.

---

### Question 10
In the carbon cycle, what does decomposition release?

A) Oxygen  
B) Nitrogen  
C) Carbon dioxide  
D) Water  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Decomposition releases carbon dioxide. Decomposers (bacteria and fungi) break down dead organic matter and respire, releasing CO₂ back into the atmosphere. This is how carbon is recycled from dead organisms.

---

### Question 11
Which bacteria convert ammonium compounds to nitrates?

A) Nitrogen-fixing bacteria  
B) Nitrifying bacteria  
C) Denitrifying bacteria  
D) Decomposer bacteria  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Nitrifying bacteria (such as Nitrosomonas and Nitrobacter) convert ammonium compounds (NH₄⁺) to nitrites (NO₂⁻) and then to nitrates (NO₃⁻). Nitrates are the form of nitrogen most easily absorbed by plant roots.

---

### Question 12
A pyramid of biomass shows:

A) The number of organisms at each level  
B) The mass of organisms at each level  
C) The energy at each level  
D) The size of organisms  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A pyramid of biomass shows the total mass of organisms at each trophic level. It is always pyramid-shaped because biomass is lost at each trophic level due to respiration, excretion, and waste.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"An ecosystem includes only the living organisms in an area."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: An ecosystem includes BOTH living (biotic) AND non-living (abiotic) components. It includes all organisms AND their physical environment (temperature, water, soil, light, etc.). Only the living organisms alone would be called a community.

---

### Statement 2
"Energy flows through ecosystems in one direction."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Energy flows through ecosystems in one direction only - from the sun to producers to consumers and eventually lost as heat. Unlike nutrients (which cycle), energy does not cycle back. It must be continually replenished by the sun.

---

### Statement 3
"Decomposers release carbon dioxide during respiration."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Decomposers (bacteria and fungi) are living organisms that respire. When they break down dead organic matter, they release carbon dioxide through respiration. This is an important part of the carbon cycle.

---

### Statement 4
"Nitrates are absorbed by plant roots for making proteins."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Plants absorb nitrates (NO₃⁻) from the soil through their roots. They use the nitrogen to make amino acids, which are joined together to make proteins. Nitrogen is essential for making proteins, DNA, and chlorophyll.

---

### Statement 5
"A pyramid of numbers is always pyramid-shaped."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: A pyramid of numbers can be INVERTED or have irregular shapes. For example, one large tree (producer) can support thousands of insects (primary consumers), giving an inverted pyramid. Pyramids of biomass and energy are always pyramid-shaped.

---

### Statement 6
"Herbivores are primary consumers."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Herbivores are animals that eat plants (producers). Since they eat producers, they are primary consumers - the first consumer level in a food chain. They form the second trophic level.

---

### Statement 7
"Combustion adds carbon dioxide to the atmosphere."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Combustion (burning) releases carbon dioxide into the atmosphere. When fossil fuels or wood are burned, the carbon stored in them combines with oxygen to form CO₂. This is a major cause of increasing atmospheric CO₂ levels.

---

### Statement 8
"Denitrifying bacteria convert nitrates to nitrogen gas."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Denitrifying bacteria convert nitrates (NO₃⁻) in the soil back into nitrogen gas (N₂), which returns to the atmosphere. This occurs in waterlogged, anaerobic soils and completes the nitrogen cycle.

---

### Statement 9
"All the energy in a producer is passed to the primary consumer."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Only about 10% of the energy in a producer is passed to the primary consumer. About 90% is lost through the producer's respiration, heat loss, and in parts not eaten. Energy transfer between trophic levels is inefficient.

---

### Statement 10
"Transpiration is part of the water cycle."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Transpiration (water loss from plant leaves through stomata) is an important part of the water cycle. It returns water vapour to the atmosphere, contributing to cloud formation and precipitation.

---

### Statement 11
"Predation is an abiotic factor."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Predation is a BIOTIC factor because it involves interactions between living organisms (predator and prey). Abiotic factors are non-living components like temperature, light, water, and soil pH.

---

### Statement 12
"Plants can absorb nitrogen gas directly from the air."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Plants CANNOT absorb nitrogen gas (N₂) directly from the air, even though it makes up 78% of the atmosphere. They can only absorb nitrogen in the form of ammonium ions (NH₄⁺) or nitrate ions (NO₃⁻) from the soil. Nitrogen must be "fixed" first.
