# Chapter 8: Homeostasis and Excretion - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. Homeostasis

### Definition
Maintenance of a **constant internal environment** despite changes in external conditions.

### Components of Homeostasis
- **Receptors**: Detect changes (stimuli)
- **Coordination centres**: Process information (brain, hypothalamus)
- **Effectors**: Produce responses (muscles, glands)

### Negative Feedback
Change triggers response that **reverses** the change.

---

## 2. Thermoregulation

### Too Hot (Response to Cooling)

| Effector | Response | Effect |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Sweat glands | Secrete more sweat | Evaporation cools skin |
| Blood vessels (skin) | Dilate (vasodilation) | More heat lost through skin |
| Hairs | Lie flat | Less insulation |
| Muscles | No shivering | No extra heat produced |

### Too Cold (Response to Warming)

| Effector | Response | Effect |
|----------|----------|--------|
| Sweat glands | Less sweat | Less heat lost |
| Blood vessels (skin) | Constrict (vasoconstriction) | Less heat lost through skin |
| Hairs | Stand erect | Traps insulating air |
| Muscles | Shiver | Heat from contraction |

### Control Centre
**Hypothalamus** - detects blood temperature and coordinates responses

---

## 3. Osmoregulation (Water Balance)

### Too Much Water (Dilute Blood)
1. Detected by osmoreceptors in hypothalamus
2. Less ADH released from pituitary
3. Kidney tubules less permeable to water
4. Less water reabsorbed
5. More dilute urine produced

### Too Little Water (Concentrated Blood)
1. Detected by osmoreceptors in hypothalamus
2. More ADH released from pituitary
3. Kidney tubules more permeable to water
4. More water reabsorbed
5. Less, more concentrated urine produced

---

## 4. Excretion

### Definition
**Removal of metabolic waste** products from the body.

### Main Excretory Products

| Product | Source | Excretory Organ |
|---------|--------|-----------------|
| Carbon dioxide | Respiration | Lungs |
| Urea | Protein breakdown | Kidneys |
| Excess water/salts | Various | Kidneys |

---

## 5. The Kidney

### Structure
- **Cortex**: Contains glomeruli and convoluted tubules
- **Medulla**: Contains loops of Henle
- **Ureter**: Carries urine to bladder
- **Renal artery/vein**: Blood vessels

### Nephron (Functional Unit)

| Part | Function |
|------|----------|
| Glomerulus | Ultrafiltration |
| Bowman's capsule | Collects filtrate |
| Proximal tubule | Reabsorption of useful substances |
| Loop of Henle | Water and ion balance |
| Distal tubule | Further adjustment |
| Collecting duct | Final water reabsorption (controlled by ADH) |

### Key Processes

| Process | What Happens |
|---------|--------------|
| **Ultrafiltration** | Small molecules filtered from blood into tubule |
| **Selective reabsorption** | Useful substances reabsorbed back into blood |

---

## 6. Kidney Failure and Treatment

### Treatments

| Treatment | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Dialysis** | Machine filters blood; waste removed; must be done regularly |
| **Transplant** | New kidney from donor; requires immunosuppressants; can cure |

---

## 7. Urine Composition

**Normal urine contains**: Water, urea, salts, uric acid
**NOT normally in urine**: Glucose, protein, blood cells

---

## 8. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Homeostasis** | Constant internal environment |
| **Negative feedback** | Response reverses the change |
| **Vasoconstriction** | Blood vessels narrow |
| **Vasodilation** | Blood vessels widen |
| **Osmoregulation** | Water balance control |
| **Excretion** | Removal of metabolic waste |
| **Deamination** | Removal of amino group from amino acids |

---

## 9. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Hypothalamus controls temperature AND water balance
2. ADH makes collecting duct MORE permeable to water
3. Vasoconstriction = less heat loss; Vasodilation = more heat loss
4. Excretion ≠ Egestion (metabolic waste vs undigested food)
5. Dialysis removes urea but keeps useful substances

### Common Mistakes
- Confusing excretion with egestion
- Saying ADH is produced by pituitary (produced by hypothalamus)
- Forgetting negative feedback explanation
- Confusing vasodilation/vasoconstriction
- Not linking response to effect
