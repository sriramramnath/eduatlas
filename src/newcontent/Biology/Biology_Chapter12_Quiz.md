## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
Which plant hormone is responsible for cell elongation in shoots?

A) Gibberellin  
B) Auxin  
C) Ethylene  
D) Cytokinin  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Auxin (specifically IAA - indole-3-acetic acid) is the main hormone responsible for cell elongation in shoots. It causes cells to elongate by loosening the cell wall structure. Gibberellins primarily affect stem elongation and seed germination, ethylene affects fruit ripening, and cytokinins promote cell division.

---

### Question 2
In phototropism, auxin moves to the:

A) Light side of the shoot  
B) Shaded side of the shoot  
C) Tip of the shoot  
D) Base of the shoot  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: When light shines on one side of a shoot, auxin redistributes to the shaded side. This causes more cell elongation on the shaded side, making the shoot bend towards the light. This is why shoots show positive phototropism.

---

### Question 3
What is the effect of high auxin concentration on root cells?

A) Promotes elongation  
B) Inhibits elongation  
C) No effect  
D) Promotes cell division  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Unlike shoot cells, root cells are inhibited by high auxin concentrations. This is why roots show positive gravitropism - auxin moves to the lower side, inhibiting growth there, so the upper side grows faster and the root bends downward.

---

### Question 4
Rooting powder contains which hormone?

A) Ethylene  
B) Gibberellin  
C) Auxin  
D) Abscisic acid  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Rooting powders contain auxins (often synthetic forms like IBA - indole-3-butyric acid). Auxins promote root formation in stem cuttings by stimulating cell division and differentiation at the cut surface.

---

### Question 5
Ethylene is used commercially to:

A) Promote root growth  
B) Ripen fruit  
C) Kill weeds  
D) Promote seed germination  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Ethylene is a gas hormone that promotes fruit ripening. It is used commercially to ripen fruits like bananas and tomatoes. Ethylene triggers colour changes, softening, and sugar production in ripening fruit.

---

### Question 6
Selective weedkillers work because:

A) They kill all plants equally  
B) Broad-leaved plants are more sensitive to synthetic auxins  
C) Grasses cannot absorb them  
D) They only work in darkness  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Synthetic auxins (like 2,4-D and MCPA) are used as selective weedkillers. Broad-leaved plants (dicots) are more sensitive to these compounds and die from uncontrolled growth, while narrow-leaved plants (grasses/monocots) are less affected.

---

### Question 7
In positive gravitropism, roots grow:

A) Upwards  
B) Downwards  
C) Towards light  
D) Sideways  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Positive gravitropism means growing TOWARDS the stimulus (gravity). Since gravity pulls downward, roots showing positive gravitropism grow downwards. Shoots show negative gravitropism and grow upwards.

---

### Question 8
What is apical dominance?

A) Growth of the shoot tip  
B) Suppression of side buds by the shoot tip  
C) Growth of roots  
D) Response to light  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Apical dominance is the suppression of lateral (side) bud growth by auxin produced in the apical bud (shoot tip). When the shoot tip is removed, the source of auxin is removed and side buds begin to grow, producing a bushier plant.

---

### Question 9
The hormone that closes stomata during water stress is:

A) Auxin  
B) Gibberellin  
C) Abscisic acid  
D) Cytokinin  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Abscisic acid (ABA) is produced in response to water stress. It causes stomata to close, reducing water loss through transpiration. This helps plants conserve water during drought conditions.

---

### Question 10
A clinostat is used to:

A) Measure growth rate  
B) Eliminate directional stimuli  
C) Apply hormones  
D) Measure photosynthesis  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A clinostat is a rotating device that slowly turns plants. This equalises the effect of gravity on all sides, effectively eliminating gravity as a directional stimulus. Plants on a clinostat grow straight rather than showing gravitropism.

---

### Question 11
In shoots, high auxin concentration causes:

A) Less cell elongation  
B) More cell elongation  
C) Cell death  
D) No change  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: In shoot cells, high auxin concentration promotes cell elongation by loosening the cell wall. This is opposite to the effect in root cells, where high auxin inhibits elongation.

---

### Question 12
Gibberellins are used to:

A) Kill weeds  
B) Ripen fruit  
C) Break seed dormancy  
D) Close stomata  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Gibberellins are used commercially to break seed dormancy and promote germination. They stimulate the production of enzymes that break down stored food in seeds, allowing germination to begin.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Plants use a nervous system to coordinate their responses."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Plants do NOT have a nervous system. They coordinate their responses using HORMONES (plant growth substances) that are produced in one part of the plant and transported to other parts to cause specific effects.

---

### Statement 2
"Auxin promotes cell elongation in both shoots and roots."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Auxin promotes cell elongation in SHOOTS but INHIBITS cell elongation in ROOTS. This difference in response is crucial for understanding how auxin causes different tropisms in different parts of the plant.

---

### Statement 3
"Phototropism is a growth response to light."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Phototropism is defined as growth movement in response to a light stimulus. Shoots show positive phototropism (grow towards light) while roots show negative phototropism (grow away from light).

---

### Statement 4
"Auxin is produced in the root tip."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Auxin is mainly produced in the SHOOT TIP (apical meristem), young leaves, and developing seeds. While roots do respond to auxin, the main production site is the shoot tip.

---

### Statement 5
"Removing the shoot tip removes apical dominance."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: The shoot tip (apex) produces auxin that suppresses the growth of side buds. When the shoot tip is removed, the source of auxin is removed, and the side buds begin to grow, resulting in a bushier plant.

---

### Statement 6
"Ethylene delays fruit ripening."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Ethylene PROMOTES fruit ripening. It triggers the breakdown of chlorophyll, softening of fruit, conversion of starch to sugars, and production of characteristic fruit aromas. Removing ethylene delays ripening.

---

### Statement 7
"Selective weedkillers kill grasses but not broad-leaved plants."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Selective weedkillers (synthetic auxins) kill BROAD-LEAVED plants but NOT grasses. This is because grasses (monocots) are less sensitive to these chemicals, while broad-leaved plants (dicots) are more sensitive and die from uncontrolled growth.

---

### Statement 8
"Roots show positive gravitropism."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Positive gravitropism means growing towards the gravitational stimulus (downward). Roots grow downward, showing positive gravitropism, while shoots grow upward, showing negative gravitropism.

---

### Statement 9
"Auxin causes the shoot to bend towards light by making cells on the lit side grow faster."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Auxin causes the shoot to bend towards light by making cells on the SHADED side grow faster (more cell elongation). Auxin moves to the shaded side, promoting elongation there, which causes bending towards the light.

---

### Statement 10
"Abscisic acid causes stomata to close."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Abscisic acid (ABA) is produced in response to water stress (drought conditions). It causes guard cells to lose water and become flaccid, closing the stomata and reducing water loss through transpiration.

---

### Statement 11
"Gibberellins can be used to produce seedless fruit."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Gibberellins can stimulate fruit development without fertilisation (parthenocarpy), resulting in seedless fruits. They are used commercially to produce seedless grapes, tomatoes, and other fruits.

---

### Statement 12
"Plant hormones only affect the part of the plant where they are produced."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Plant hormones are produced in one part of the plant but are transported to other parts where they cause their effects. For example, auxin produced in the shoot tip travels down to affect cell elongation in the stem and inhibit side bud growth.

### Question 13 (6 marks)
Describe the role of auxin in phototropism and gravitropism.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**

**Phototropism:**
- Auxin produced in shoot tip [1]
- Moves to shaded side of shoot [1]
- Promotes cell elongation on shaded side [1]
- Shoot bends towards light [1]

**Gravitropism:**
- In roots: auxin moves to lower side [1]
- Inhibits cell elongation in root cells [1]
- Root bends downward [1]
- In shoots: auxin promotes elongation on lower side [1]
- Shoot bends upward [1]
