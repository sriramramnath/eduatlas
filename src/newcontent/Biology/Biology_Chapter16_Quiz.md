## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What are the components of a DNA nucleotide?

A) Sugar, base, amino acid  
B) Phosphate, sugar, base  
C) Phosphate, base, protein  
D) Sugar, protein, phosphate  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Each DNA nucleotide consists of three components: a phosphate group, a deoxyribose sugar, and a nitrogenous base (A, T, C, or G). Amino acids are building blocks of proteins, not nucleotides.

---

### Question 2
Which base pairs with adenine in DNA?

A) Guanine  
B) Cytosine  
C) Thymine  
D) Uracil  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: In DNA, adenine (A) always pairs with thymine (T) through two hydrogen bonds. Uracil is found in RNA (not DNA) and pairs with adenine. Guanine pairs with cytosine.

---

### Question 3
How many chromosomes are found in a human body cell?

A) 23  
B) 46  
C) 92  
D) 44  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Human body (somatic) cells contain 46 chromosomes, arranged in 23 pairs. One chromosome in each pair comes from each parent. Gametes (sperm and egg) contain 23 chromosomes (haploid).

---

### Question 4
What is a gene?

A) A type of protein  
B) A section of DNA that codes for a protein  
C) A whole chromosome  
D) A type of cell  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A gene is a section of DNA that contains the code for a specific protein. Genes are located on chromosomes and are the basic units of heredity.

---

### Question 5
What is an allele?

A) The same as a gene  
B) A different version of the same gene  
C) A type of chromosome  
D) A protein  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Alleles are different versions of the same gene. For example, the gene for eye colour has alleles for brown eyes and blue eyes. Alleles arise through mutations.

---

### Question 6
In humans, what determines male sex?

A) Two X chromosomes  
B) One X and one Y chromosome  
C) Two Y chromosomes  
D) The environment  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: In humans, males have one X chromosome and one Y chromosome (XY). Females have two X chromosomes (XX). The Y chromosome carries genes that trigger male development.

---

### Question 7
Where does transcription occur?

A) In the cytoplasm  
B) At the ribosome  
C) In the nucleus  
D) In the mitochondria  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Transcription (copying DNA to mRNA) occurs in the nucleus where the DNA is located. Translation (making proteins from mRNA) occurs at ribosomes in the cytoplasm.

---

### Question 8
What does mRNA stand for?

A) Messenger ribonucleic acid  
B) Mutant ribonucleic acid  
C) Messenger ribosome acid  
D) Multiple RNA  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: mRNA stands for messenger ribonucleic acid. It carries the genetic message from DNA in the nucleus to ribosomes in the cytoplasm for protein synthesis.

---

### Question 9
A heterozygous organism has:

A) Two identical alleles  
B) Two different alleles  
C) Only one allele  
D) No alleles  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Heterozygous means having two different alleles for a gene (e.g., Bb). Homozygous means having two identical alleles (e.g., BB or bb).

---

### Question 10
What is the phenotype?

A) The genetic makeup  
B) The observable characteristics  
C) The type of cell  
D) The number of chromosomes  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Phenotype refers to the observable physical characteristics of an organism (what you can see), determined by the genotype (genetic makeup) and environment.

---

### Question 11
A recessive allele is expressed when:

A) It is present with a dominant allele  
B) It is homozygous (two copies present)  
C) The organism is heterozygous  
D) It is the only allele  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A recessive allele is only expressed in the phenotype when an organism has two copies (homozygous recessive). With a dominant allele present (heterozygous), the dominant allele is expressed instead.

---

### Question 12
How many bases code for one amino acid?

A) 1  
B) 2  
C) 3  
D) 4  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Three bases (a triplet or codon) code for one amino acid. This is the genetic code. For example, AUG codes for methionine, UUU codes for phenylalanine.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"DNA is a polymer of amino acids."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: DNA is a polymer of NUCLEOTIDES, not amino acids. Amino acids are the building blocks of proteins. Each nucleotide consists of a phosphate, sugar, and base.

---

### Statement 2
"Adenine pairs with thymine in DNA."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: In DNA, adenine (A) always pairs with thymine (T) through two hydrogen bonds. This is called complementary base pairing and is fundamental to DNA structure and replication.

---

### Statement 3
"Humans have 23 pairs of chromosomes."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Humans have 46 chromosomes total, arranged in 23 pairs. Each pair consists of one chromosome from each parent. This includes 22 pairs of autosomes and 1 pair of sex chromosomes.

---

### Statement 4
"A gene codes for a carbohydrate."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: A gene codes for a PROTEIN (or part of a protein). The sequence of bases in DNA determines the sequence of amino acids in a protein through the processes of transcription and translation.

---

### Statement 5
"Alleles are different versions of the same gene."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Alleles are different forms of the same gene that produce different variations of a trait. For example, different alleles of the eye colour gene produce brown or blue eyes.

---

### Statement 6
"Translation occurs in the nucleus."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: TRANSLATION occurs at RIBOSOMES in the cytoplasm. TRANSCRIPTION occurs in the nucleus. Translation uses mRNA to make proteins; transcription copies DNA to mRNA.

---

### Statement 7
"Females have two X chromosomes."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: In humans, females have two X chromosomes (XX). Males have one X and one Y chromosome (XY). The sex chromosomes determine biological sex.

---

### Statement 8
"A dominant allele is always expressed when present."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: A dominant allele is expressed in the phenotype whenever it is present. Whether homozygous (BB) or heterozygous (Bb), the dominant trait will be visible. Recessive alleles are only expressed when homozygous.

---

### Statement 9
"Homozygous means having two different alleles."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: HOMOZYGOUS means having TWO IDENTICAL alleles (e.g., BB or bb). HETEROZYGOUS means having two different alleles (e.g., Bb).

---

### Statement 10
"The genetic code is universal."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: The genetic code is universal, meaning the same codons code for the same amino acids in almost all living organisms. This provides evidence for common ancestry of all life.

---

### Statement 11
"tRNA carries amino acids to the ribosome."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Transfer RNA (tRNA) molecules carry specific amino acids to the ribosome during translation. Each tRNA has an anticodon that pairs with a codon on mRNA, ensuring the correct amino acid is added to the growing protein chain.

---

### Statement 12
"Genotype determines phenotype completely."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Phenotype is determined by BOTH genotype AND environmental factors. For example, plant height is affected by genes but also by nutrient availability, water, and light.
