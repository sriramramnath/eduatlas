## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
Which part of the brain controls involuntary activities such as heart rate and breathing?

A) Cerebrum  
B) Cerebellum  
C) Medulla oblongata  
D) Hypothalamus  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The medulla oblongata, located at the base of the brain, controls involuntary activities such as heart rate, breathing rate, and blood pressure. The cerebrum controls conscious activities, the cerebellum controls balance and coordination, and the hypothalamus regulates body temperature and water balance.

---

### Question 2
What is the function of a motor neuron?

A) To carry impulses from receptors to the CNS  
B) To carry impulses from the CNS to effectors  
C) To connect sensory neurons to motor neurons  
D) To detect stimuli in the environment  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Motor neurons carry impulses from the central nervous system (CNS) to effectors (muscles and glands). Sensory neurons carry impulses from receptors to the CNS, and relay neurons connect sensory and motor neurons within the CNS.

---

### Question 3
In a reflex arc, what is the correct sequence?

A) Receptor → Motor neuron → Sensory neuron → Effector  
B) Receptor → Sensory neuron → Relay neuron → Motor neuron → Effector  
C) Effector → Motor neuron → Relay neuron → Sensory neuron → Receptor  
D) Sensory neuron → Receptor → Motor neuron → Effector  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The correct sequence in a reflex arc is: Stimulus → Receptor → Sensory neuron → Relay neuron (in CNS) → Motor neuron → Effector → Response. Sensory neurons carry impulses TO the CNS, and motor neurons carry impulses FROM the CNS.

---

### Question 4
What happens at a synapse?

A) Electrical impulses pass directly between neurons  
B) Hormones are released into the bloodstream  
C) Neurotransmitters diffuse across a gap between neurons  
D) The two neurons fuse together  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: At a synapse, electrical impulses cause the release of neurotransmitters from the presynaptic neuron. These chemicals diffuse across the synaptic cleft (gap) and bind to receptors on the postsynaptic membrane, generating a new electrical impulse. The neurons do not touch, and no electrical signal crosses the gap directly.

---

### Question 5
When focusing on a distant object:

A) The ciliary muscles contract and the lens becomes rounder  
B) The ciliary muscles relax and the lens becomes flatter  
C) The suspensory ligaments become slack  
D) The pupil dilates  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: When focusing on distant objects, the ciliary muscles relax, allowing the suspensory ligaments to become taut. This pulls the lens into a flatter, thinner shape that refracts light less, focusing distant objects on the retina. For near objects, the ciliary muscles contract and the lens becomes rounder.

---

### Question 6
Which type of receptor detects light?

A) Thermoreceptor  
B) Chemoreceptor  
C) Photoreceptor  
D) Mechanoreceptor  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Photoreceptors detect light. In the human eye, photoreceptors are the rods and cones in the retina. Thermoreceptors detect temperature, chemoreceptors detect chemicals, and mechanoreceptors detect pressure and movement.

---

### Question 7
The pupil constricts in bright light because:

A) The circular muscles of the iris contract  
B) The radial muscles of the iris contract  
C) The ciliary muscles contract  
D) The suspensory ligaments tighten  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: In bright light, the circular muscles of the iris contract while the radial muscles relax. This causes the pupil to constrict (become smaller), reducing the amount of light entering the eye and protecting the retina from damage.

---

### Question 8
Which hormone is produced by the adrenal glands?

A) Insulin  
B) Adrenaline  
C) Thyroxine  
D) Testosterone  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The adrenal glands, located above the kidneys, produce adrenaline (epinephrine). This hormone prepares the body for "fight or flight" responses. Insulin is produced by the pancreas, thyroxine by the thyroid gland, and testosterone by the testes.

---

### Question 9
What is the function of the fovea in the eye?

A) To regulate the amount of light entering the eye  
B) To produce aqueous humour  
C) To provide the area of sharpest vision  
D) To connect the eye to the brain  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The fovea (or macula) is a small depression in the retina that contains the highest concentration of cone cells. It provides the area of sharpest vision and best colour perception. The iris regulates light entry, the ciliary body produces aqueous humour, and the optic nerve connects to the brain.

---

### Question 10
Which statement about reflex actions is correct?

A) They involve conscious thought  
B) They are slow responses  
C) They are automatic and involuntary  
D) They always involve the brain  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Reflex actions are rapid, automatic, and involuntary responses that do not involve conscious thought. They typically pass through the spinal cord rather than the brain, allowing faster responses. The brain may be informed of the stimulus after the reflex has occurred.

---

### Question 11
What is the function of rods in the retina?

A) To detect colour in bright light  
B) To detect light intensity in dim light  
C) To focus light on the retina  
D) To produce tears  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Rods are photoreceptors that detect light intensity (brightness) and work best in dim light. They produce black and white images. Cones detect colour and work best in bright light. The lens focuses light, and lacrimal glands produce tears.

---

### Question 12
The corpus callosum connects:

A) The eye to the brain  
B) The two cerebral hemispheres  
C) The brain to the spinal cord  
D) Sensory neurons to motor neurons  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The corpus callosum is a bundle of nerve fibres that connects the left and right cerebral hemispheres, allowing communication between the two sides of the brain. The optic nerve connects eye to brain, the brainstem connects brain to spinal cord, and synapses connect neurons.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Reflex actions are voluntary responses that involve conscious thought."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Reflex actions are involuntary responses that do NOT involve conscious thought. They are automatic, rapid responses that occur without the brain's involvement in the decision-making process. The spinal cord processes reflex arcs, and the brain is only informed after the reflex has occurred.

---

### Statement 2
"Motor neurons carry impulses from the CNS to effectors."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Motor neurons (also called efferent neurons) transmit electrical impulses from the central nervous system to effectors such as muscles and glands. When these impulses reach the effector, they cause a response such as muscle contraction or gland secretion.

---

### Statement 3
"Neurotransmitters are released from the postsynaptic membrane."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Neurotransmitters are released from the presynaptic membrane (the neuron sending the signal), not the postsynaptic membrane. They diffuse across the synaptic cleft and bind to receptors on the postsynaptic membrane of the receiving neuron.

---

### Statement 4
"The cerebellum controls balance and coordination of movement."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: The cerebellum, located at the back of the brain below the cerebrum, is responsible for coordinating voluntary movements, maintaining balance, and ensuring smooth, precise muscle actions. Damage to the cerebellum results in uncoordinated, jerky movements.

---

### Statement 5
"In dim light, the radial muscles of the iris relax."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: In dim light, the radial muscles CONTRACT while the circular muscles relax. This causes the pupil to dilate (become larger), allowing more light to enter the eye. In bright light, the circular muscles contract and the radial muscles relax, constricting the pupil.

---

### Statement 6
"Hormones are transported in the bloodstream."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Hormones are chemical messengers secreted by endocrine glands directly into the bloodstream. They are transported throughout the body but only affect target organs that have specific receptors for that hormone. This is different from nerves, which use electrical impulses.

---

### Statement 7
"The optic nerve carries impulses from the retina to the brain."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: The optic nerve is a cranial nerve that carries visual information from the photoreceptors in the retina to the visual cortex in the brain. The point where the optic nerve leaves the eye is called the blind spot because there are no photoreceptors there.

---

### Statement 8
"When focusing on near objects, the lens becomes thinner."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: When focusing on near objects, the ciliary muscles contract, the suspensory ligaments slacken, and the lens becomes thicker and more curved (rounder). This increases its refractive power. The lens becomes thinner when focusing on distant objects.

---

### Statement 9
"Synapses ensure one-way transmission of nerve impulses."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Synapses ensure one-way transmission because neurotransmitters are only released from the presynaptic membrane and receptors are only located on the postsynaptic membrane. This means impulses can only travel in one direction across the synapse.

---

### Statement 10
"The pituitary gland is known as the 'master gland'."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: The pituitary gland is called the "master gland" because it produces hormones that control many other endocrine glands. It releases hormones that stimulate the thyroid, adrenal glands, and gonads, as well as growth hormone and hormones involved in water balance.

---

### Statement 11
"Rods are responsible for colour vision."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Cones are responsible for colour vision, not rods. Rods detect light intensity and work in dim light, producing black and white images. There are three types of cones that detect different wavelengths of light (red, green, blue), enabling colour vision.

---

### Statement 12
"Adrenaline decreases heart rate."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Adrenaline INCREASES heart rate, along with breathing rate, blood pressure, and blood glucose levels. It prepares the body for "fight or flight" responses during stressful or dangerous situations. This is why people feel their heart racing when frightened or excited.
