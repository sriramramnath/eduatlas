## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
Which kingdom contains organisms that have cells with a nucleus but no cell wall?

A) Fungi  
B) Plants  
C) Animals  
D) Prokaryotes  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Animals are eukaryotic organisms (cells have a nucleus) but their cells do not have cell walls. Fungi have chitin cell walls, plants have cellulose cell walls, and prokaryotes (bacteria) have peptidoglycan cell walls.

---

### Question 2
What is the correct way to write the scientific name for humans?

A) Homo Sapiens  
B) homo sapiens  
C) Homo sapiens  
D) HOMO SAPIENS  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: In binomial nomenclature, the genus name (Homo) starts with a capital letter, the species name (sapiens) starts with a lowercase letter, and the complete name should be written in italics or underlined.

---

### Question 3
Which of the following is a characteristic of ALL mammals?

A) They lay eggs  
B) They have feathers  
C) They produce milk for their young  
D) They are cold-blooded  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: All mammals produce milk from mammary glands to feed their young. Only a few mammals (monotremes like platypus) lay eggs. Feathers are characteristic of birds, and mammals are warm-blooded (endothermic).

---

### Question 4
Viruses are NOT considered living organisms because:

A) They are too small  
B) They cannot carry out life processes independently  
C) They are made of protein only  
D) They cause diseases  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Viruses are not considered living because they cannot carry out any life processes (respiration, growth, nutrition, etc.) on their own. They can only reproduce inside a host cell using the host's cellular machinery. Being small, having protein, and causing disease are not reasons for not being living.

---

### Question 5
Which structure is found in plant cells but NOT in fungal cells?

A) Cell wall  
B) Nucleus  
C) Chloroplast  
D) Mitochondrion  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Chloroplasts are found in plant cells for photosynthesis but not in fungal cells. Fungi have cell walls (made of chitin), nuclei, and mitochondria, but they cannot photosynthesise and are saprotrophic.

---

### Question 6
A student observed an animal with eight legs and two body segments. This animal is most likely:

A) An insect  
B) An arachnid  
C) A crustacean  
D) A myriapod  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Arachnids (spiders, scorpions) have eight legs and two body segments (cephalothorax and abdomen). Insects have six legs, crustaceans have more than eight legs, and myriapods have many legs and many body segments.

---

### Question 7
Which of the following correctly describes bacteria?

A) They have a nucleus containing their DNA  
B) They reproduce by binary fission  
C) They are all harmful to humans  
D) They can only survive in oxygen-rich environments  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Bacteria reproduce asexually by binary fission, where one cell divides into two identical daughter cells. Bacteria are prokaryotes (no nucleus), many are beneficial (gut bacteria, decomposers), and they can be aerobic, anaerobic, or facultative.

---

### Question 8
Plasmodium, the organism that causes malaria, belongs to which kingdom?

A) Prokaryotes  
B) Protoctists  
C) Fungi  
D) Animals  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Plasmodium is a protoctist. It is a single-celled eukaryotic organism that is parasitic and causes malaria when transmitted to humans through mosquito bites. It is not a bacterium (prokaryote), fungus, or animal.

---

### Question 9
Which of the following is a dicotyledonous plant?

A) Wheat  
B) Rice  
C) Bean  
D) Maize  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Beans are dicots (two cotyledons, net-like leaf veins, vascular bundles in a ring). Wheat, rice, and maize are all monocots (one cotyledon, parallel leaf veins, scattered vascular bundles).

---

### Question 10
The body of a fungus is made up of thread-like structures called:

A) Hyphae  
B) Pili  
C) Flagella  
D) Rhizoids  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: Fungi are composed of thread-like structures called hyphae (singular: hypha). These hyphae form a network called mycelium. Pili and flagella are found on bacteria, and rhizoids are root-like structures in some plants and fungi.

---

### Question 11
Which of the following is a characteristic of arthropods?

A) They have an internal skeleton  
B) They have jointed legs  
C) They all live on land  
D) They are all vertebrates  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Arthropods (insects, arachnids, crustaceans, myriapods) are characterised by having jointed legs, a hard exoskeleton (external skeleton, not internal), and segmented bodies. They are invertebrates and can live in water (crustaceans) or on land.

---

### Question 12
In the classification hierarchy, which level contains organisms that are most closely related?

A) Kingdom  
B) Phylum  
C) Genus  
D) Species  

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Species is the most specific level in the classification hierarchy. Organisms in the same species can interbreed to produce fertile offspring and are most closely related. The hierarchy becomes more general as you move up from species to kingdom.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"All bacteria are harmful pathogens."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Many bacteria are beneficial and essential for life. Beneficial bacteria include those in our intestines that aid digestion, nitrogen-fixing bacteria that convert atmospheric nitrogen into compounds plants can use, and decomposer bacteria that recycle nutrients in ecosystems. Only a small percentage of bacteria cause disease.

---

### Statement 2
"Fungi are classified as plants because they have cell walls and cannot move."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Fungi are classified in their own kingdom, separate from plants. Key differences include: fungi cell walls are made of chitin (not cellulose), fungi cannot photosynthesise (they are saprotrophs), and fungi store glycogen (not starch) as their storage carbohydrate.

---

### Statement 3
"Viruses can only reproduce inside living host cells."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Viruses are obligate intracellular parasites. They lack the cellular machinery needed for reproduction and can only replicate by hijacking the metabolic processes of a living host cell. Outside a host, viruses are inert and cannot carry out any life processes.

---

### Statement 4
"Amphibians have dry, scaly skin."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Amphibians have moist, permeable skin without scales. This moist skin allows gas exchange (cutaneous respiration) and is why amphibians need to live in damp environments. Dry, scaly skin is characteristic of reptiles.

---

### Statement 5
"Monocotyledonous plants have leaves with parallel veins."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Monocots (monocotyledons) have several characteristic features including: one cotyledon in the seed, leaves with parallel veins, vascular bundles scattered throughout the stem, and flower parts in multiples of three. Examples include grasses, lilies, and cereals like wheat and rice.

---

### Statement 6
"Bacteria have membrane-bound organelles like mitochondria."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Bacteria are prokaryotes and do not have membrane-bound organelles. Their cells lack a true nucleus, mitochondria, endoplasmic reticulum, and Golgi apparatus. Respiration occurs on the cell membrane, and ribosomes are free in the cytoplasm (not attached to ER).

---

### Statement 7
"The genus name in binomial nomenclature is always written with a capital letter."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: In the binomial naming system developed by Linnaeus, the genus name (first word) always begins with a capital letter, while the species name (second word) begins with a lowercase letter. The complete name is written in italics when typed or underlined when handwritten.

---

### Statement 8
"Yeast is a single-celled fungus."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Yeasts are unicellular fungi, unlike most fungi which are multicellular. Saccharomyces cerevisiae (baker's yeast and brewer's yeast) is the most well-known yeast, used in baking to make bread rise and in brewing to produce alcohol through fermentation.

---

### Statement 9
"Birds are cold-blooded animals that lay eggs."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Birds are warm-blooded (endothermic) animals that maintain a constant body temperature independent of their environment. They do lay eggs with hard shells, but being warm-blooded is one of the key features that distinguishes them from reptiles, amphibians, and fish.

---

### Statement 10
"A dichotomous key always gives two choices at each step."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: "Dichotomous" means "divided into two parts." At each step in a dichotomous key, there are exactly two contrasting statements or questions. By choosing the correct option at each step, the user follows a path that eventually leads to the identification of the organism.

---

### Statement 11
"Protoctists are prokaryotic organisms."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Protoctists are eukaryotic organisms. They have cells with a true nucleus and membrane-bound organelles. Prokaryotes are bacteria - they lack a nucleus and membrane-bound organelles. This is a fundamental distinction between the two kingdoms.

---

### Statement 12
"Cold-blooded animals get their body heat from the environment."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Cold-blooded animals (ectotherms) cannot generate their own body heat internally. Their body temperature varies with the environmental temperature. This is why reptiles bask in the sun to warm up and seek shade to cool down. In contrast, warm-blooded animals (endotherms) maintain a constant internal body temperature.

### Question 15 (5 marks)
Explain how modern classification methods have improved our understanding of evolutionary relationships between organisms.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- DNA sequencing compares base sequences between organisms [1]
- More similar DNA = more closely related [1]
- Protein analysis compares amino acid sequences [1]
- Organisms previously thought unrelated may be reclassified based on molecular evidence [1]
- Provides objective, quantitative evidence rather than subjective morphological comparison [1]
- Has led to the development of the three-domain system [1]
- Shows evolutionary trees/phylogeny more accurately [1]

*Maximum 5 marks*
