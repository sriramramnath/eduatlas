# Chapter 2: The Variety of Living Organisms - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

---

## 1. The Five Kingdoms Overview

| Kingdom | Cell Type | Nutrition | Cell Wall | Key Features |
|---------|-----------|-----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Animals** | Eukaryotic, multicellular | Heterotrophic | None | Move, no chloroplasts |
| **Plants** | Eukaryotic, multicellular | Autotrophic (photosynthesis) | Cellulose | Chloroplasts, large vacuole |
| **Fungi** | Eukaryotic, uni/multicellular | Saprotrophic | Chitin | Hyphae, mycelium, spores |
| **Protoctists** | Eukaryotic, uni/multicellular | Varied | Varied | Diverse group (amoeba, algae) |
| **Prokaryotes** | Prokaryotic, unicellular | Varied | Peptidoglycan | No nucleus, plasmids |

## 2. Animal Classification

### Vertebrates (Animals with Backbone)

| Class | Body Covering | Reproduction | Temperature Regulation | Examples |
|-------|---------------|--------------|----------------------|----------|
| **Mammals** | Hair/fur | Live birth, produce milk | Warm-blooded | Human, whale, bat |
| **Birds** | Feathers | Lay eggs with shells | Warm-blooded | Eagle, penguin |
| **Reptiles** | Dry scales | Lay eggs with soft shells | Cold-blooded | Snake, crocodile |
| **Amphibians** | Moist skin | Lay eggs in water, metamorphosis | Cold-blooded | Frog, newt |
| **Fish** | Wet scales | Lay eggs in water, gills | Cold-blooded | Salmon, shark |

### Invertebrates (Animals without Backbone)

| Group | Key Features | Examples |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| **Arthropods** | Jointed legs, exoskeleton, segmented | Insects, spiders, crustaceans |
| **Molluscs** | Soft body, often with shell | Snail, octopus |
| **Annelids** | Segmented worms | Earthworm, leech |
| **Nematodes** | Roundworms, unsegmented | Threadworm |
| **Cnidarians** | Tentacles, stinging cells | Jellyfish, coral |

### Arthropod Subgroups

| Group | Legs | Antennae | Body Parts | Examples |
|-------|------|----------|------------|----------|
| **Insects** | 6 | 1 pair | 3 (head, thorax, abdomen) | Butterfly, beetle |
| **Arachnids** | 8 | None | 2 (cephalothorax, abdomen) | Spider, scorpion |
| **Crustaceans** | More than 8 | 2 pairs | Variable | Crab, lobster |
| **Myriapods** | Many pairs | 1 pair | Many segments | Centipede, millipede |

## 3. Plant Classification

### Flowering Plants (Angiosperms)

| Group | Cotyledons | Leaf Veins | Vascular Bundles | Flower Parts | Examples |
|-------|------------|------------|------------------|--------------|----------|
| **Monocots** | 1 | Parallel | Scattered | Multiples of 3 | Grass, wheat, lily |

| Group | Reproduction | Examples |
|-------|--------------|----------|
| **Ferns** | Spores on leaf undersides | Bracken |
| **Conifers** | Seeds in cones | Pine, spruce |
| **Mosses** | Spores in capsules | Sphagnum |

## 4. Fungi

### Key Characteristics
- Eukaryotic cells with chitin cell walls
- Multicellular (hyphae → mycelium) or unicellular (yeast)
- Saprotrophic nutrition (external digestion)
- Reproduce by spores

### Examples
| Type | Structure | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| Moulds | Hyphae form mycelium | Mucor, Penicillium |
| Yeasts | Single-celled | Saccharomyces (baking/brewing) |
| Mushrooms | Fruiting bodies | Agaricus |

---

## 5. Protoctists

| Type | Characteristics | Example |
|------|-----------------|---------|
| **Protozoa** (animal-like) | Heterotrophic, can move | Amoeba, Paramecium, Plasmodium |
| **Algae** (plant-like) | Photosynthetic, have chloroplasts | Chlorella, Spirogyra |

*Note: Plasmodium causes malaria*

---

## 6. Bacteria (Prokaryotes)

### Structure
- No nucleus (circular DNA in nucleoid)
- No membrane-bound organelles
- Cell wall made of peptidoglycan
- May have: plasmids, flagella, capsule, pili

### Shapes
| Shape | Name | Example |
|-------|------|---------|
| Spherical | Cocci | Streptococcus |
| Rod-shaped | Bacilli | E. coli |
| Spiral | Spirilla | Spirillum |
| Comma | Vibrios | Vibrio cholerae |

### Reproduction
- Binary fission (asexual)
- Can divide every 20 minutes under favourable conditions

### Beneficial vs Harmful
| Beneficial | Harmful (Pathogenic) |
|------------|---------------------|
| Gut bacteria (digestion) | Salmonella (food poisoning) |
| Nitrogen-fixing bacteria | Tuberculosis |
| Decomposers | Cholera |

---

## 7. Viruses

### Key Points
- **NOT living organisms**
- Cannot carry out life processes independently
- Only reproduce inside host cells
- Much smaller than bacteria (20-300 nm)

### Structure
- Genetic material (DNA or RNA)
- Protein coat (capsid)
- Some have outer envelope

### Viral Diseases
| Host | Disease | Virus |
|------|---------|-------|
| Humans | HIV/AIDS, flu, COVID-19, measles | Various |
| Plants | Tobacco mosaic virus | TMV |

## 8. Classification Systems

### Binomial Nomenclature
- Two-part Latin name: Genus species
- Genus = capital letter, species = lowercase
- Written in italics or underlined
- Example: *Homo sapiens*

### Classification Hierarchy
```
Kingdom → Phylum → Class → Order → Family → Genus → Species
   ↓          ↓        ↓        ↓        ↓        ↓        ↓
Broad ←—————————————————————————————→ Specific
```

### Three Domain System (Higher Tier)
| Domain | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Archaea** | Ancient bacteria-like, often extremophiles |
| **Bacteria** | True bacteria with peptidoglycan walls |
| **Eukarya** | All eukaryotes (protists, fungi, plants, animals) |

## 9. Dichotomous Keys

### Definition
A tool for identifying organisms using pairs of contrasting statements.

### How to Use
1. Start at statement 1
2. Choose the option that matches your organism
3. Follow to next statement or identification
4. Continue until organism is identified

### How to Construct
1. Observe characteristics of all organisms
2. Choose a feature that divides organisms into two groups
3. Continue dividing until each organism is identified

## 10. Pathogens

### Definition
An organism or virus that causes disease.

### Types and Examples
| Type | Disease Example | Transmission |
|------|-----------------|--------------|
| Bacteria | Cholera, TB, Salmonella | Water, air, food |
| Virus | HIV, flu, COVID-19 | Body fluids, airborne |
| Fungus | Athlete's foot | Direct contact |
| Protoctist | Malaria | Vector (mosquito) |

## 11. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Autotroph** | Organism that makes its own food |
| **Heterotroph** | Organism that cannot make its own food |
| **Saprotroph** | Organism feeding on dead matter by external digestion |
| **Pathogen** | Disease-causing organism or virus |
| **Hypha** | Thread-like structure in fungi |
| **Mycelium** | Network of hyphae |
| **Plasmid** | Small circular DNA in bacteria |
| **Vertebrate** | Animal with backbone |
| **Invertebrate** | Animal without backbone |

## 12. Exam Focus Points

### High-Yield Facts
1. Viruses are NOT living - they need host cells to reproduce
2. Fungi cell walls are made of chitin (NOT cellulose)
3. Bacteria are prokaryotes (no nucleus); protoctists are eukaryotes (have nucleus)
4. Scientific names: Genus (capital) species (lowercase), italics
5. Monocots: 1 cotyledon, parallel veins; Dicots: 2 cotyledons, net veins

### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Writing scientific names incorrectly (wrong capitals, no italics)
- Saying viruses are living
- Confusing prokaryotes and protists
- Forgetting fungi have chitin cell walls, not cellulose
- Mixing up arthropod groups (count legs!)

## 13. Quick Comparison Tables

### Living vs Non-Living vs Viruses

| Characteristic | Living Organisms | Viruses |
|----------------|------------------|---------|
| Cellular structure | Yes | No |
| Respiration | Yes | No |
| Reproduction (independent) | Yes | No (need host) |
| Growth | Yes | No |
| Response to stimuli | Yes | Limited |
| Evolution | Yes | Yes |

### Prokaryote vs Eukaryote

| Feature | Prokaryote | Eukaryote |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| Nucleus | No | Yes |
| Membrane-bound organelles | No | Yes |
| DNA structure | Circular, naked | Linear, with proteins |
| Size | Small (1-5 μm) | Larger (10-100 μm) |
| Ribosomes | Smaller (70S) | Larger (80S) |
| Examples | Bacteria | Animals, plants, fungi, protists |
