## Detailed Notes for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Biology

## 22.1 What is Cloning?

### Definition

A clone is a group of genetically identical organisms, or a group of cells derived from a single parent cell by mitosis. Cloning produces genetically identical copies of an organism.

### Natural Cloning

**In animals:**
- Identical twins form when a fertilised egg divides into two embryos
- Each twin is genetically identical to the other
- Some animals reproduce asexually (e.g., aphids, some lizards)

**In plants:**
- Natural vegetative reproduction produces clones
- Runners (strawberries)
- Rhizomes (iris)
- Tubers (potatoes)
- Bulbs (onions)

## 22.2 Plant Cloning Methods

### Tissue Culture (Micropropagation)

Tissue culture is a modern method of cloning plants using small pieces of plant tissue grown in a sterile nutrient medium.

**Process:**
1. Small piece of plant tissue (explant) taken from meristem or other tissue
2. Tissue sterilised to remove microorganisms
3. Explant placed on sterile nutrient agar containing nutrients and plant hormones
4. Cells divide by mitosis to form a mass of cells called a callus
5. Callus divided into smaller pieces
6. Each piece placed on growing medium with hormones to stimulate shoot growth
7. Shoots transferred to medium with hormones to stimulate root growth
8. Plantlets transferred to soil and hardened off

**Advantages:**
- Large numbers of plants produced quickly
- Disease-free plants
- Can clone rare or endangered species
- Can produce plants year-round
- Plants are genetically identical (useful for crops with desirable traits)
- Small space required

**Disadvantages:**
- Expensive equipment and skilled workers needed
- Risk of contamination
- All plants genetically identical (vulnerable to disease)
- May not be true to type if mutations occur

### Cuttings

Traditional method of cloning plants:
1. Cut piece of stem with leaves
2. Dip cut end in rooting hormone
3. Plant in moist compost
4. New roots grow from cut end
5. New plant is clone of parent

## 22.3 Animal Cloning

### Embryo Transplanting

**Process:**
1. Cow with desirable traits treated with hormones to produce many eggs
2. Eggs fertilised by sperm from bull with desirable traits
3. Fertilised eggs develop into embryos
4. Embryos divided into several pieces
5. Each piece develops into a complete embryo
6. Embryos implanted into surrogate mothers
7. Calves born are genetically identical clones

### Adult Cell Cloning (Somatic Cell Nuclear Transfer)

This is the method used to create Dolly the sheep.

**Process:**
1. Nucleus removed from an egg cell (enucleation)
2. Nucleus from body cell (somatic cell) of donor animal inserted into egg cell
3. Egg given electric shock to start dividing
4. Embryo develops
5. Embryo implanted into surrogate mother
6. Offspring is genetic clone of donor

**Dolly the Sheep (1996):**
- First mammal cloned from adult cell
- Created at Roslin Institute, Scotland
- Proved that adult cells could be used to create a whole organism
- Lived for 6.5 years (shorter than normal)

## 22.4 Advantages of Animal Cloning

### Agriculture
- Clone animals with desirable traits
- High milk yield, disease resistance
- Consistent quality of livestock

### Medicine
- Clone animals for medical research
- Produce genetically identical animals for testing
- Clone animals that produce medicines in milk

### Conservation
- Clone endangered species
- Preserve genetic material
- Potentially restore extinct species

---

## 22.5 Concerns About Animal Cloning

### Ethical Concerns

**Welfare of cloned animals**
- Higher rates of abnormalities
- Shorter lifespans
- Health problems
- Low success rate

**Playing God**
- Interfering with nature
- Religious objections

**Human cloning**
- Fears about human cloning
- Ethical implications

### Scientific Concerns

**Low success rate**
- Many embryos fail to develop
- High rate of miscarriage

**Health problems in clones**
- Organ defects
- Immune system problems
- Premature aging

**Genetic diversity**
- Reduced genetic variation
- Vulnerability to disease

## Key Definitions Summary

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Clone** | Genetically identical copy |
| **Tissue culture** | Growing cells/tissues in artificial medium |
| **Micropropagation** | Cloning plants using tissue culture |
| **Adult cell cloning** | Creating clone using adult body cell nucleus |
| **Surrogate mother** | Animal that carries cloned embryo |
| **Enucleation** | Removal of nucleus from cell |

## Common Exam Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Confusing different cloning methods**: Tissue culture for plants, nuclear transfer for animals

2. **Forgetting to mention mitosis**: All cloning involves mitosis (genetically identical)

3. **Not explaining the steps in order**: Sequence matters in procedures

4. **Only giving advantages**: Always discuss concerns too

5. **Confusing embryo splitting with adult cell cloning**: Different processes
