## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
Who proposed the theory of evolution by natural selection?

A) Gregor Mendel  
B) Charles Darwin  
C) Louis Pasteur  
D) Rosalind Franklin  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Charles Darwin proposed the theory of evolution by natural selection in his book "On the Origin of Species" (1859). Alfred Russel Wallace independently developed similar ideas.

---

### Question 2
What is natural selection?

A) Humans choosing which organisms reproduce  
B) Organisms with advantageous traits survive and reproduce more  
C) Random changes in populations  
D) The formation of new species  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Natural selection is the process where organisms with characteristics that give them a survival advantage are more likely to survive and reproduce, passing those advantageous traits to their offspring.

---

### Question 3
Which of the following is a source of genetic variation?

A) Learning  
B) Mutation  
C) Exercise  
D) Diet  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Mutations create new alleles and are a source of genetic variation. Learning, exercise, and diet are environmental factors that do not change DNA and therefore do not contribute to genetic variation.

---

### Question 4
What is speciation?

A) The extinction of a species  
B) The formation of new species  
C) The movement of species  
D) The selection of species  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Speciation is the process by which new species form from existing species. This typically occurs when populations become isolated and evolve differently.

---

### Question 5
Which of the following provides evidence for evolution?

A) Fossil record  
B) DNA comparisons  
C) Comparative anatomy  
D) All of the above  

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Multiple lines of evidence support evolution: the fossil record shows changes over time, DNA comparisons show relationships between species, and comparative anatomy reveals homologous structures indicating common ancestry.

---

### Question 6
In the peppered moth example, dark moths became more common during the Industrial Revolution because:

A) They were more attractive  
B) They were better camouflaged on soot-covered trees  
C) They produced more offspring  
D) They could fly faster  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: During the Industrial Revolution, trees were darkened by soot. Dark moths were better camouflaged against dark bark and were less likely to be eaten by birds. This selective advantage meant more dark moths survived to reproduce.

---

### Question 7
What is a selection pressure?

A) The weight of an organism  
B) An environmental factor that affects survival  
C) The pressure inside cells  
D) The number of offspring  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A selection pressure is any environmental factor that affects an organism's chance of survival and reproduction. Examples include predators, food availability, disease, and climate.

---

### Question 8
Antibiotic resistance in bacteria is an example of:

A) Artificial selection  
B) Natural selection  
C) Genetic engineering  
D) Cloning  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Antibiotic resistance is a clear example of natural selection. When antibiotics are used, bacteria with resistance genes survive and reproduce, while non-resistant bacteria die. The population evolves to become resistant.

---

### Question 9
What is extinction?

A) Formation of new species  
B) Permanent loss of a species  
C) Movement of species  
D) Evolution of species  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Extinction is the permanent loss of a species. Once a species is extinct, it cannot return. Extinction can occur due to natural causes or human activities.

---

### Question 10
Homologous structures provide evidence for evolution because they:

A) Have the same function but different structure  
B) Have the same basic structure but different functions  
C) Are found only in humans  
D) Have no purpose  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Homologous structures (like the pentadactyl limb) have the same basic bone structure but different functions (human hand, bat wing, whale flipper). This suggests descent from a common ancestor with modification for different purposes.

---

### Question 11
Two populations that are geographically separated may evolve into different species through:

A) Artificial selection  
B) Allopatric speciation  
C) Convergent evolution  
D) Genetic drift  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Allopatric speciation occurs when populations are geographically isolated. Each population faces different selection pressures and evolves differently, eventually becoming separate species.

---

### Question 12
What role do mutations play in evolution?

A) They have no role  
B) They create new alleles for selection to act upon  
C) They always cause extinction  
D) They only occur in humans  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Mutations are random changes in DNA that create new alleles. These new alleles provide the genetic variation upon which natural selection acts. Without mutations, there would be no new variation for evolution.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Individual organisms evolve during their lifetime."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: POPULATIONS evolve, not individuals. Evolution occurs over generations as allele frequencies change in a population. An individual cannot change its genes during its lifetime in response to the environment.

---

### Statement 2
"Natural selection acts on heritable characteristics."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Natural selection can only act on characteristics that can be passed from parents to offspring (heritable characteristics). Acquired characteristics (like muscles from exercise) cannot be inherited.

---

### Statement 3
"Fossils provide evidence for evolution."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: The fossil record shows a sequence of organisms over time, with simple organisms in older rocks and more complex organisms in younger rocks. Transitional fossils show intermediate stages in evolution.

---

### Statement 4
"Mutations are always harmful."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Mutations can be harmful, neutral, OR beneficial. Beneficial mutations provide new variations that may give a selective advantage. These are the mutations that natural selection can act upon to drive evolution.

---

### Statement 5
"Competition leads to natural selection."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Organisms produce more offspring than can survive. This leads to competition for resources. Those with advantageous characteristics are more likely to survive and reproduce, leading to natural selection.

---

### Statement 6
"Speciation occurs when populations can interbreed."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Speciation occurs when populations can NO LONGER interbreed to produce fertile offspring. Reproductive isolation is a key feature of separate species.

---

### Statement 7
"DNA comparisons can show how closely related species are."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Organisms that share more similar DNA sequences are more closely related. For example, humans and chimpanzees share about 98% of their DNA, indicating recent common ancestry.

---

### Statement 8
"Extinction can only be caused by humans."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Extinction can be caused by natural factors (climate change, volcanic eruptions, asteroid impacts) as well as human activities (habitat destruction, hunting, pollution). Mass extinctions occurred before humans evolved.

---

### Statement 9
"Antibiotic resistance evolves because bacteria try to survive."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Bacteria do NOT "try" to develop resistance. Mutations occur randomly. When antibiotics are present, bacteria that happen to have resistance genes survive. Evolution has no goal or purpose - it is not directed.

---

### Statement 10
"Isolated populations may evolve differently."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: When populations are isolated, they face different environmental conditions and selection pressures. Over time, they accumulate different genetic changes and may evolve into different species.

---

### Statement 11
"The fossil record is complete."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: The fossil record is INCOMPLETE. Fossilisation is rare, and many organisms that lived have not left fossils. However, the fossils we do have provide strong evidence for evolution.

---

### Statement 12
"Darwin's finches evolved different beak shapes due to different food sources."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: On different Galápagos Islands, finches faced different food sources. Natural selection favoured different beak shapes: large beaks for cracking hard seeds, thin beaks for catching insects, etc. This adaptive radiation produced multiple species.
