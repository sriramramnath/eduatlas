## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What is a clone?

A) An organism that looks similar to another  
B) A genetically identical copy of an organism  
C) An organism with half the chromosomes  
D) A hybrid organism  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A clone is a genetically identical copy of an organism or cell. Clones are produced by mitosis and have exactly the same DNA as the parent.

---

### Question 2
Which process produces clones naturally in animals?

A) Sexual reproduction  
B) Identical twins  
C) Fertilisation  
D) Meiosis  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Identical twins are natural clones formed when a fertilised egg divides into two separate embryos. Both twins have identical DNA.

---

### Question 3
Tissue culture in plants involves:

A) Growing plants from seeds  
B) Growing small pieces of plant tissue in a nutrient medium  
C) Cross-breeding plants  
D) Genetic engineering  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Tissue culture (micropropagation) involves growing small pieces of plant tissue on sterile nutrient agar containing hormones. The cells divide by mitosis to produce new plants.

---

### Question 4
In adult cell cloning, the nucleus is taken from:

A) An egg cell  
B) A sperm cell  
C) A body (somatic) cell  
D) A plant cell  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: In adult cell cloning (somatic cell nuclear transfer), the nucleus is taken from a body cell of the animal to be cloned. This nucleus is inserted into an egg cell that has had its nucleus removed.

---

### Question 5
Dolly the sheep was created by:

A) Embryo transplanting  
B) Adult cell cloning  
C) Natural reproduction  
D) Genetic engineering  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Dolly the sheep (1996) was the first mammal cloned from an adult cell using somatic cell nuclear transfer. The nucleus from an adult sheep's udder cell was inserted into an egg cell.

---

### Question 6
Which of the following is an advantage of plant tissue culture?

A) Produces genetically different plants  
B) Can produce many plants in a small space  
C) Is very cheap  
D) Takes many years  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Tissue culture can produce large numbers of plants in a relatively small space. It's efficient for mass-producing plants with desirable traits.

---

### Question 7
A surrogate mother in cloning is:

A) The genetic mother  
B) An animal that carries the cloned embryo  
C) The animal being cloned  
D) A cloned animal  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A surrogate mother is the animal that carries and gives birth to the cloned embryo. She is not genetically related to the clone.

---

### Question 8
Which natural plant structure is an example of cloning?

A) Seeds  
B) Flowers  
C) Potato tubers  
D) Pollen  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Potato tubers are examples of natural cloning in plants. New potato plants grow from tubers and are genetically identical to the parent plant.

---

### Question 9
A concern about cloning animals is:

A) Too much genetic variation  
B) Low success rate and health problems  
C) Animals become too healthy  
D) No ethical concerns  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Cloning animals has a low success rate, and cloned animals often have health problems. There are also ethical concerns about animal welfare.

---

### Question 10
In tissue culture, plant hormones are used to:

A) Kill bacteria  
B) Stimulate root and shoot growth  
C) Produce flowers  
D) Make plants grow taller  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: In tissue culture, plant hormones (auxins and cytokinins) are used to stimulate the growth of roots and shoots from the callus.

---

### Question 11
Embryo transplanting involves:

A) Removing the nucleus from an egg  
B) Dividing an embryo into pieces that each develop into a complete embryo  
C) Cloning adult animals  
D) Producing genetically different offspring  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Embryo transplanting involves dividing an early embryo into pieces. Each piece develops into a complete embryo, producing genetically identical clones.

---

### Question 12
Cloned animals are genetically identical because:

A) They are produced by meiosis  
B) They are produced by mitosis  
C) They have different parents  
D) They are genetically engineered  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Cloned organisms are produced by mitosis, which produces genetically identical daughter cells. No genetic mixing occurs as in sexual reproduction.

## Part B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
"Cloned animals are genetically identical to each other."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Clones are produced by mitosis, which produces genetically identical cells. All cloned animals from the same parent have the same DNA.

---

### Statement 2
"Tissue culture is the only way to clone plants."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Plants can also be cloned naturally through runners, rhizomes, tubers, and bulbs. They can be cloned artificially through cuttings as well as tissue culture.

---

### Statement 3
"Dolly the sheep lived longer than normal sheep."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Dolly the sheep had a SHORTER than normal lifespan (6.5 years vs. 10-12 years). She developed health problems, raising concerns about cloning.

---

### Statement 4
"In adult cell cloning, the egg cell provides the genetic material."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: In adult cell cloning, the egg cell has its nucleus REMOVED. The genetic material comes from the adult body cell nucleus that is inserted into the enucleated egg.

---

### Statement 5
"Tissue culture can produce disease-free plants."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Tissue culture uses sterile techniques and meristem tissue that is often virus-free, so the resulting plants are typically disease-free.

---

### Statement 6
"Cloning always involves genetic engineering."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Cloning does NOT require genetic engineering. Cloning simply produces copies of an organism's existing DNA. Genetic engineering modifies DNA before cloning.

---

### Statement 7
"Identical twins are natural clones."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Identical twins are formed when one fertilised egg splits into two embryos. Both twins have identical DNA and are therefore natural clones.

---

### Statement 8
"All cloned animals have health problems."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Not ALL cloned animals have health problems, but there is a HIGHER RATE of health issues compared to naturally conceived animals. Some clones are healthy.

---

### Statement 9
"Tissue culture requires sterile conditions."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Tissue culture requires strict sterile conditions to prevent contamination by bacteria and fungi, which would ruin the culture.

---

### Statement 10
"Cloning can help conserve endangered species."

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Cloning could potentially help conserve endangered species by producing more individuals. However, it doesn't address the underlying causes of extinction.

---

### Statement 11
"Embryo transplanting produces fewer clones than adult cell cloning."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Embryo transplanting can produce MORE clones than adult cell cloning in some cases because an embryo can be divided into multiple pieces.

---

### Statement 12
"Cloning produces genetic variation."

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Cloning produces genetically IDENTICAL copies, reducing genetic variation. This is one of the concerns about cloning.
