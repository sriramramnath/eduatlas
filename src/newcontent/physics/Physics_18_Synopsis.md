# Chapter 18: Density and Pressure
## Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Physics - Concise Synopsis

---

## Essential Equations

```
Density:              ρ = m ÷ V
Pressure:             p = F ÷ A
Liquid pressure:      p = ρ × g × h
Hydraulic force:      F₂ = F₁ × (A₂ ÷ A₁)
```

---

## Density

### Definition
Mass per unit volume.

### Equation
```
ρ = m ÷ V
```

### Units
- kg/m³ (SI)
- g/cm³ (common)

### Conversion
```
1 g/cm³ = 1000 kg/m³
```

### Floating/Sinking
- Less dense → floats
- More dense → sinks

### Example
```
m = 500 g, V = 250 cm³
ρ = 500 ÷ 250 = 2 g/cm³
```

---

## Pressure

### Definition
Force per unit area.

### Equation
```
p = F ÷ A
```

### Unit
Pascal (Pa) = N/m²

### Example
```
F = 500 N, A = 2 m²
p = 500 ÷ 2 = 250 Pa
```

---

## Pressure in Liquids

### Equation
```
p = ρ × g × h
```

### Key Points
- Increases with depth
- Same in all directions
- Depends on depth and density

### Example
```
Water (ρ=1000), h=10 m
p = 1000 × 10 × 10 = 100,000 Pa
```

---

## Atmospheric Pressure

- Sea level: ~101,000 Pa (101 kPa)
- Decreases with altitude
- Measured with barometer

---

## Hydraulic Systems

### Principle
Pressure transmitted equally throughout liquid.

### Force Multiplication
```
F₂ = F₁ × (A₂ ÷ A₁)
```

### Example
```
F₁ = 100 N, A₁ = 0.01 m², A₂ = 0.5 m²
F₂ = 100 × (0.5 ÷ 0.01) = 5000 N
```

### Applications
- Hydraulic brakes
- Hydraulic press
- Car jacks

---

## Key Exam Points

1. **ρ = m/V** - density formula
2. **p = F/A** - pressure formula
3. **p = ρgh** - liquid pressure
4. **Hydraulics** - pressure same, force varies
5. **Units** - kg/m³, Pa
6. **Floating** - less dense floats
