## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
Sound waves are:

A) Transverse waves  
B) Longitudinal waves  
C) Electromagnetic waves  
D) Stationary waves

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Sound waves are longitudinal - particles vibrate parallel to the direction of travel.

---

### Question 2
Sound cannot travel through:

A) Water  
B) Steel  
C) Air  
D) A vacuum

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Sound is a mechanical wave and needs a medium to travel. It cannot travel through a vacuum.

---

### Question 3
The pitch of a sound is determined by its:

A) Amplitude  
B) Frequency  
C) Speed  
D) Wavelength

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Higher frequency = higher pitch. Amplitude determines loudness.

---

### Question 4
Approximately what is the speed of sound in air?

A) 30 m/s  
B) 330 m/s  
C) 3000 m/s  
D) 30,000 m/s

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The speed of sound in air is approximately 330-340 m/s (at room temperature).

---

### Question 5
In which medium does sound travel fastest?

A) Air  
B) Water  
C) Steel  
D) Sound travels at same speed in all

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Sound travels fastest in solids, then liquids, then gases. Steel is a solid.

---

### Question 6
The normal range of human hearing is:

A) 2 Hz - 200 Hz  
B) 20 Hz - 200 Hz  
C) 20 Hz - 20,000 Hz  
D) 200 Hz - 200,000 Hz

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Humans can typically hear sounds between 20 Hz and 20,000 Hz (20 kHz).

---

### Question 7
Ultrasound has a frequency:

A) Below 20 Hz  
B) Between 20 Hz and 20,000 Hz  
C) Above 20,000 Hz  
D) Above 200,000 Hz

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Ultrasound is sound with frequency above the upper limit of human hearing (20,000 Hz).

---

### Question 8
An echo is heard 4 seconds after a sound is made. If the speed of sound is 330 m/s, how far away is the reflecting surface?

A) 82.5 m  
B) 330 m  
C) 660 m  
D) 1320 m

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Distance = (speed × time) ÷ 2 = (330 × 4) ÷ 2 = 660 m. Divide by 2 because sound travels to the surface and back.

---

### Question 9
Which of the following is a use of ultrasound?

A) Radio broadcasting  
B) Medical imaging  
C) Sun tanning  
D) Cooking food

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Ultrasound is used for medical imaging (e.g., scanning fetuses) because it is non-ionizing and safe.

---

### Question 10
The loudness of a sound is determined by its:

A) Frequency  
B) Amplitude  
C) Speed  
D) Wavelength

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Larger amplitude = louder sound. Frequency determines pitch.

---

### Question 11
Sound level is measured in:

A) Hertz

**Explanation**: Sound intensity level is measured in decibels (dB).

### Question 12
Bats use echoes to:

A) Communicate with other bats

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
Sound travels faster in air than in water.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Sound travels faster in water (~1500 m/s) than in air (~330 m/s). Sound travels faster in denser media.*

---

### Statement 2
The amplitude of a sound wave determines its pitch.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Amplitude determines loudness. Frequency determines pitch.*

---

### Statement 3
Ultrasound can be used for medical imaging during pregnancy.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Ultrasound is safe for fetuses (non-ionizing) and is commonly used for prenatal scans.

---

### Statement 4
Infrasound has a frequency above 20,000 Hz.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Infrasound has frequency BELOW 20 Hz. Ultrasound has frequency above 20,000 Hz.*

---

### Statement 5
Sound waves can travel through a vacuum.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Sound is a mechanical wave and needs particles to vibrate. It cannot travel through a vacuum.*

---

### Statement 6
The human ear converts sound vibrations into electrical signals.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: The cochlea in the inner ear converts mechanical vibrations into electrical signals sent to the brain.

---

### Statement 7
Higher frequency sound waves have higher pitch.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Pitch is directly related to frequency. Higher frequency = higher pitch.

---

### Statement 8
SONAR uses radio waves to detect objects underwater.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: SONAR uses ultrasound (sound waves), not radio waves, to detect objects underwater.*

---

### Statement 9
Prolonged exposure to loud noise can damage hearing.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Loud noise (typically above 85 dB) can cause permanent hearing damage with prolonged exposure.

---

### Statement 10
Sound travels at the same speed in all materials.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Speed of sound varies with medium. Fastest in solids, slower in liquids, slowest in gases.*

---

### Statement 11
The eardrum vibrates when sound waves reach it.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Sound waves cause the eardrum to vibrate, which is then transmitted through the middle ear bones.

---

### Statement 12
Echoes are caused by the refraction of sound waves.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Echoes are caused by the REFLECTION of sound waves from a surface, not refraction.*

### Question 4 (3 marks)
An echo is heard 2.5 seconds after a shout. Calculate the distance to the reflecting surface. (Speed of sound = 340 m/s)

**Mark Scheme:**
- Formula: distance = (speed × time) ÷ 2 (1 mark)
- Distance = (340 × 2.5) ÷ 2 (1 mark)
- Distance = 425 m (1 mark)

## Extended Answer Question (6 marks)

### Question 11 (6 marks)
(a) Describe an experiment to compare the speed of sound in air and in a solid. (3 marks)
(b) A student hears thunder 5 seconds after seeing lightning. Calculate how far away the lightning strike was. (Speed of sound in air = 330 m/s) (2 marks)
(c) Explain why you see lightning before you hear thunder. (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme:**

**(a) Experiment to compare speeds**
- Method: Tap one end of a long metal rod / use two microphones (1 mark)
- Measure time for sound to travel known distance in air and in solid (1 mark)
- Compare times / calculate speeds for each medium (1 mark)
- Or: listen through air and through solid simultaneously, sound arrives faster through solid

**(b) Distance calculation**
- Distance = speed × time = 330 × 5 (1 mark)
- Distance = 1650 m (1 mark)

**(c) Lightning before thunder**
- Light travels much faster than sound (1 mark)
- So light arrives almost instantly while sound takes noticeable time (1 mark)
