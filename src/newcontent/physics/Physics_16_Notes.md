# Chapter 16: Work and Power
## Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Physics Detailed Notes

---

## 16.1 Work

### Definition

**Work** is done when a force causes an object to move.

### Conditions for Work

1. A **force** must act on the object
2. The object must **move** in the direction of the force

### Equation

```
work done = force × distance moved in direction of force

W = F × d

Where:
W = work done (J)
F = force (N)
d = distance (m)
```

### Unit

The unit of work is the **joule (J)**.

1 joule = 1 newton-metre (1 J = 1 Nm)

### When is Work NOT Done?

| Situation | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Pushing a wall that doesn't move | No distance moved |
| Carrying a box horizontally | Force is vertical, movement is horizontal |
| Holding an object stationary | No distance moved |

### Worked Examples

**Example 1:** A 50 N force moves an object 8 m. Calculate the work done.
```
W = F × d = 50 × 8 = 400 J
```

**Example 2:** Calculate the force needed to do 600 J of work over 12 m.
```
F = W ÷ d = 600 ÷ 12 = 50 N
```

## 16.2 Work and Energy

### Relationship

Work done = Energy transferred

When work is done on an object, energy is transferred to that object.

### Examples

| Situation | Work Done | Energy Transfer |
|-----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Lifting an object | Against gravity | GPE gained |
| Accelerating a car | Against inertia | KE gained |
| Stretching a spring | Against spring | Elastic PE gained |
| Pushing against friction | Against friction | Thermal energy |

## 16.3 Work Done Against Gravity

### Lifting Objects

When lifting an object at constant velocity:
```
Force needed = weight of object = m × g

Work done = force × height = m × g × h
```

This equals the **gravitational potential energy gained**.

### Worked Example

A 5 kg box is lifted 3 m. Calculate the work done. (g = 10 N/kg)

```
Weight = m × g = 5 × 10 = 50 N
Work done = F × d = 50 × 3 = 150 J

Or directly:
Work done = m × g × h = 5 × 10 × 3 = 150 J
```

## 16.4 Power

### Definition

**Power** is the rate at which work is done or energy is transferred.

### Equation

```
power = work done ÷ time taken

P = W ÷ t

Or:

power = energy transferred ÷ time taken

P = E ÷ t

Where:
P = power (W)
W = work done (J)
E = energy transferred (J)
t = time (s)
```

### Unit

The unit of power is the **watt (W)**.

1 watt = 1 joule per second (1 W = 1 J/s)

### Larger Units

| Unit | Equivalent |
|------|------------|
| 1 kilowatt (kW) | 1000 W |
| 1 megawatt (MW) | 1,000,000 W |
| 1 gigawatt (GW) | 1,000,000,000 W |

### Worked Examples

**Example 1:** Calculate the power if 1200 J of work is done in 20 s.
```
P = W ÷ t = 1200 ÷ 20 = 60 W
```

**Example 2:** Calculate the energy transferred by a 2 kW heater in 30 s.
```
E = P × t = 2000 × 30 = 60,000 J = 60 kJ
```

---

## 16.5 Power and Velocity

### Alternative Equation

For an object moving at constant velocity against a force:

```
power = force × velocity

P = F × v

Where:
P = power (W)
F = force (N)
v = velocity (m/s)
```

### Derivation

```
P = W ÷ t = (F × d) ÷ t = F × (d ÷ t) = F × v
```

### Worked Example

A car travels at 20 m/s against a resistive force of 500 N. Calculate the power required.

```
P = F × v = 500 × 20 = 10,000 W = 10 kW
```

## 16.6 Human Power

### Typical Values

| Activity | Power Output |
|----------|--------------|
| Sleeping | ~70 W |
| Walking | ~150 W |
| Cycling (leisurely) | ~200 W |
| Running | ~400 W |
| Sprinting | ~1000 W |

### Measuring Human Power

A person can climb stairs and measure:
- Their mass (m)
- Height climbed (h)
- Time taken (t)

```
Power = (m × g × h) ÷ t
```

---

## 16.7 Efficiency and Power

### Efficiency Equation (using power)

```
efficiency = useful power output ÷ total power input

Or as percentage:
percentage efficiency = (useful power output ÷ total power input) × 100%
```

### Worked Example

A motor has a power input of 500 W and produces 350 W of useful mechanical power. Calculate its efficiency.

```
Efficiency = 350 ÷ 500 = 0.7 = 70%
```

## Exam Tips for Chapter 16

1. **Work needs movement** - No movement = no work
2. **Direction matters** - Force and distance in same direction
3. **Power is rate** - Work or energy per unit time
4. **Units** - Work in J, Power in W
5. **P = Fv** - Useful for moving vehicles
6. **Efficiency** - Always less than 100%

---

## Common Student Errors

| Error | Correction |
|-------|------------|
| Thinking holding something is work | No distance = no work |
| Confusing work and power | Work is total, power is rate |
| Wrong units | Work in J, not N |
| Using wrong formula | P = W/t, not P = W × t |
| Forgetting direction | Force and distance must be parallel |
