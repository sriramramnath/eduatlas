## 17.1 Renewable Energy Resources

### Solar Power

**Photovoltaic (PV) cells:**
- Convert sunlight directly into electricity
- Made from semiconductor materials (usually silicon)
- No moving parts, low maintenance

**Solar thermal:**
- Uses sunlight to heat water
- Heated water used directly or to generate electricity

**Advantages:**
- Renewable and free fuel (sunlight)
- No pollution during operation
- Low running costs

**Disadvantages:**
- Only works during daylight
- Weather dependent
- High initial cost
- Requires large areas for significant power

---

### Wind Power

**How it works:**
- Wind turns turbine blades
- Blades spin generator
- Generator produces electricity

**Advantages:**
- Renewable
- No pollution during operation
- Low running costs

**Disadvantages:**
- Weather dependent (needs wind)
- Visual impact
- Noise
- Can harm birds
- Large land area needed

---

### Hydroelectric Power

**How it works:**
- Water stored in reservoir behind dam
- Water released through turbines
- Turbines drive generators

**Advantages:**
- Renewable
- Reliable (can control output)
- No pollution
- Can respond quickly to demand

**Disadvantages:**
- High initial cost
- Floods large areas
- Affects wildlife and local communities
- Limited suitable sites

---

### Tidal Power

**How it works:**
- Uses rise and fall of tides
- Water flows through turbines
- Turbines drive generators

**Advantages:**
- Renewable
- Predictable (tides are known)
- No pollution

**Disadvantages:**
- High initial cost
- Limited suitable sites
- Affects marine life
- Only generates power at certain times

---

### Wave Power

**How it works:**
- Uses motion of waves
- Various designs (oscillating water column, floating devices)
- Movement drives generators

**Advantages:**
- Renewable
- No pollution

**Disadvantages:**
- Weather dependent
- High maintenance (harsh sea conditions)
- Limited suitable sites
- Can affect shipping

---

### Geothermal Power

**How it works:**
- Uses heat from Earth's interior
- Hot water/steam from underground drives turbines
- Turbines drive generators

**Advantages:**
- Renewable
- Reliable
- No pollution
- Small land area needed

**Disadvantages:**
- Very limited suitable sites
- High initial cost
- Can release harmful gases

---

### Biomass

**How it works:**
- Organic material (wood, crops, waste) burned
- Heat used to generate electricity
- Or: biological processes produce biogas

**Advantages:**
- Renewable (if managed sustainably)
- Can use waste materials
- Carbon neutral (if replanted)

**Disadvantages:**
- Produces CO₂ when burned
- Requires large areas for crops
- Can compete with food production

---

## 17.2 Non-Renewable Energy Resources

### Fossil Fuels (Coal, Oil, Natural Gas)

**How they work:**
- Fuel burned to release heat
- Heat boils water to produce steam
- Steam drives turbines
- Turbines drive generators

**Advantages:**
- Reliable
- High energy density
- Existing infrastructure
- Relatively cheap

**Disadvantages:**
- Produces CO₂ (greenhouse gas)
- Produces other pollutants (SO₂, NOx)
- Finite resource (will run out)
- Mining/extraction damages environment

---

### Nuclear Power

**How it works:**
- Nuclear fission releases heat
- Heat boils water to produce steam
- Steam drives turbines
- Turbines drive generators

**Advantages:**
- No CO₂ during operation
- High energy density
- Reliable
- Fuel readily available

**Disadvantages:**
- Radioactive waste (difficult to dispose of)
- Risk of accidents (Chernobyl, Fukushima)
- High initial cost
- Decommissioning costs
- Public concern

## 17.4 Electricity Generation

### Basic Principle

Most power stations use the same basic process:

```
Energy source → Heat → Steam → Turbine → Generator → Electricity
```

### Generator Principle

Generators work on **electromagnetic induction**:
- Coil rotates in magnetic field
- Changing magnetic field induces voltage
- Voltage drives current

### Transformers

Used to change voltage for transmission:
- **Step-up transformer:** Increases voltage for transmission
- **Step-down transformer:** Decreases voltage for use

**Why high voltage transmission?**
- Higher voltage = lower current for same power
- Lower current = less energy lost as heat in cables
- More efficient transmission

## 17.5 Energy and the Environment

### Global Warming

- Burning fossil fuels releases CO₂
- CO₂ is a greenhouse gas
- Traps heat in atmosphere
- Causes global temperature rise

### Acid Rain

- Burning fossil fuels releases SO₂ and NOx
- These dissolve in rainwater
- Forms acid rain
- Damages buildings, forests, lakes

### Solutions

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Global warming | Use renewable energy, reduce fossil fuel use |
| Acid rain | Use low-sulfur fuels, scrubbers on power stations |
| Nuclear waste | Safe storage, reprocessing |

## Common Student Errors

| Error | Correction |
|-------|------------|
| Thinking nuclear is renewable | Nuclear uses finite uranium fuel |
| Thinking biomass is carbon-free | Burning biomass releases CO₂ |
| Forgetting environmental impacts | Consider all impacts for each type |
| Confusing advantages/disadvantages | Learn both for each resource |
| Thinking all renewables are unreliable | Hydro and geothermal are reliable |
