## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
What is the unit of work?

A) Newton  
B) Watt  
C) Joule  
D) Pascal

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Work is measured in joules (J). 1 J = 1 Nm.*

---

### Question 2
A 40 N force moves an object 5 m in the direction of the force. How much work is done?

A) 8 J  
B) 35 J  
C) 200 J  
D) 2000 J

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: W = F × d = 40 × 5 = 200 J.*

---

### Question 3
What is the unit of power?

A) Newton  
B) Watt  
C) Joule  
D) Ampere

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Power is measured in watts (W). 1 W = 1 J/s.*

---

### Question 4
A machine does 600 J of work in 15 s. What is its power output?

A) 40 W  
B) 400 W  
C) 9000 W  
D) 40,000 W

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: P = W ÷ t = 600 ÷ 15 = 40 W.*

---

### Question 5
A car engine produces 8000 W of power. How much work does it do in 20 s?

A) 400 J  
B) 4000 J  
C) 160,000 J  
D) 400,000 J

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: W = P × t = 8000 × 20 = 160,000 J.*

---

### Question 6
A force of 200 N is applied to a car moving at 15 m/s. What is the power?

A) 13.3 W  
B) 300 W  
C) 3000 W  
D) 30,000 W

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: P = F × v = 200 × 15 = 3000 W.*

---

### Question 7
No work is done when:

A) A force acts and the object moves  
B) A force acts but the object doesn't move  
C) An object moves with no force  
D) Both B and C

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Work requires both force AND movement in the direction of the force.*

---

### Question 8
A 3 kg object is lifted 4 m. How much work is done? (g = 10 N/kg)

A) 7.5 J  
B) 12 J  
C) 120 J  
D) 1200 J

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Weight = 3 × 10 = 30 N. W = F × d = 30 × 4 = 120 J.*

---

### Question 9
Which equation correctly relates power, force, and velocity?

A) P = F ÷ v  
B) P = F × v  
C) P = F + v  
D) P = F - v

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Power = force × velocity (when force and velocity are in the same direction).*

---

### Question 10
A student climbs a rope, doing 800 J of work in 10 s. What is her power output?

A) 8 W  
B) 80 W  
C) 800 W  
D) 8000 W

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: P = W ÷ t = 800 ÷ 10 = 80 W.*

---

### Question 11
A motor has an input power of 500 W and an output power of 350 W. What is its efficiency?

A) 70%  
B) 140%  
C) 150%  
D) 850%

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: Efficiency = (350 ÷ 500) × 100% = 70%.*

---

### Question 12
A man holds a 20 kg box at chest height for 30 seconds. How much work does he do on the box?

A) 0 J  
B) 200 J  
C) 600 J  
D) 6000 J

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: No work is done because there is no movement. Holding requires force but no distance.*

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
Work is done whenever a force acts on an object.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Work is done only when a force causes movement in the direction of the force.*

---

### Statement 2
Power is the total amount of work done.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Power is the RATE at which work is done (work per unit time).*

---

### Statement 3
1 watt is equal to 1 joule per second.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: 1 W = 1 J/s by definition.*

---

### Statement 4
A force acting perpendicular to the direction of motion does no work.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Work = F × d × cos(θ). When θ = 90°, cos(90°) = 0, so no work is done.*

---

### Statement 5
Lifting an object increases its gravitational potential energy.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Work done against gravity = mgh = GPE gained.*

---

### Statement 6
A more powerful machine always does more work than a less powerful one.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Power is rate of work. A less powerful machine could do more total work if it runs for longer.*

---

### Statement 7
The work done by a force is independent of the time taken.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Work = F × d. Time affects power, not work.*

---

### Statement 8
When a car travels at constant speed, the engine power equals the power dissipated by resistive forces.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: At constant speed, net force is zero, so engine force equals resistive force. P = Fv for both.*

---

### Statement 9
1 kilowatt = 1000 watts.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: 1 kW = 1000 W.*

---

### Statement 10
Walking upstairs slowly requires less work than running up quickly.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: The work done (mgh) is the same. Running requires more POWER because the same work is done in less time.*

---

### Statement 11
A machine can be 100% efficient.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: All real machines have some energy losses (friction, heat), so efficiency is always less than 100%.*

---

### Statement 12
Power can be calculated as force multiplied by velocity.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: P = F × v is a valid equation for power when force and velocity are parallel.*

## Short Answer Questions

### Question 1 (2 marks)
Define work done.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Work done = force × distance moved in direction of force (1 mark)
- Unit: joule (J) (1 mark)

---

### Question 2 (3 marks)
A 60 N force pulls a box 10 m along the floor. Calculate the work done.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Formula: W = F × d (1 mark)
- W = 60 × 10 (1 mark)
- W = 600 J (1 mark)

---

### Question 3 (4 marks)
Explain why a person holding a heavy weight stationary does no work on the weight.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Work requires both force and movement (1 mark)
- The person applies an upward force (1 mark)
- But the weight does not move (1 mark)
- Therefore no work is done (1 mark)

---

### Question 4 (3 marks)
A motor does 2400 J of work in 30 s. Calculate its power output.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Formula: P = W ÷ t (1 mark)
- P = 2400 ÷ 30 (1 mark)
- P = 80 W (1 mark)

---

### Question 5 (4 marks)
A crane lifts a 500 kg load 12 m in 20 s. Calculate:
(a) The work done by the crane. (2 marks)
(b) The power output of the crane. (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:***
(a) Force = weight = 500 × 10 = 5000 N (1 mark)
    W = 5000 × 12 = 60,000 J (1 mark)
(b) P = W ÷ t = 60,000 ÷ 20 (1 mark)
    P = 3000 W (or 3 kW) (1 mark)

---

### Question 6 (3 marks)
A car travels at a constant speed of 25 m/s against resistive forces of 800 N. Calculate the power output of the engine.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Formula: P = F × v (1 mark)
- P = 800 × 25 (1 mark)
- P = 20,000 W (or 20 kW) (1 mark)

---

### Question 7 (4 marks)
Explain the difference between work and power.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Work is force × distance (energy transferred) (1 mark)
- Unit: joule (J) (1 mark)
- Power is work ÷ time (rate of doing work) (1 mark)
- Unit: watt (W) (1 mark)

---

### Question 8 (2 marks)
A machine has an input power of 800 W and an output power of 600 W. Calculate its efficiency.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Efficiency = 600 ÷ 800 (1 mark)
- Efficiency = 0.75 = 75% (1 mark)

---

### Question 9 (3 marks)
A student of mass 50 kg runs up a flight of stairs, gaining 8 m of height in 6 s. Calculate her power output. (g = 10 N/kg)

**Mark Scheme:***
- Work done = mgh = 50 × 10 × 8 = 4000 J (1 mark)
- P = W ÷ t = 4000 ÷ 6 (1 mark)
- P = 667 W (accept 660-670 W) (1 mark)

---

### Question 10 (4 marks)
A cyclist travels at a constant speed of 8 m/s against total resistive forces of 120 N.
(a) Calculate the power output of the cyclist. (2 marks)
(b) Explain why the cyclist must keep pedalling to maintain constant speed. (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:***
(a) P = F × v = 120 × 8 (1 mark)
    P = 960 W (1 mark)
(b) Resistive forces oppose motion (1 mark)
    Cyclist must apply equal force to maintain constant speed (1 mark)

## Extended Answer Question (6 marks)

### Question
A crane lifts a 2000 kg load from the ground to a height of 15 m in 25 seconds.
(a) Calculate the work done by the crane. (g = 10 N/kg) (2 marks)
(b) Calculate the power output of the crane. (2 marks)
(c) The crane motor has an efficiency of 75%. Calculate the electrical power input to the motor. (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:***

**(a) Work done***
- Force = weight = 2000 × 10 = 20,000 N (1 mark)
- W = F × d = 20,000 × 15 = 300,000 J (1 mark)

**(b) Power output***
- P = W ÷ t = 300,000 ÷ 25 (1 mark)
- P = 12,000 W (or 12 kW) (1 mark)

**(c) Power input***
- Efficiency = useful output ÷ total input (1 mark)
- 0.75 = 12,000 ÷ input → input = 12,000 ÷ 0.75 = 16,000 W (1 mark)
