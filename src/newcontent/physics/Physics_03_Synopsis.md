## Newton's Three Laws of Motion

### First Law (Inertia)
> An object remains at rest or moves with constant velocity unless acted on by a resultant force.

- **Resultant force = 0** → Constant velocity (or stationary)
- **Inertia** = resistance to change in motion
- Greater mass = greater inertia

### Second Law
> Resultant force = mass × acceleration

```
F = m × a
```

- F in newtons (N)
- m in kilograms (kg)
- a in m/s²

**Key relationships:**
- F ∝ a (at constant mass)
- a ∝ 1/m (at constant force)

### Third Law (Action-Reaction)
> When two objects interact, they exert equal and opposite forces on each other.

**Characteristics:**
- Equal magnitude
- Opposite direction
- Same type of force
- Act on **different objects**
- Simultaneous

## Resultant Force and Motion

| Resultant Force | Motion |
|----------------|--------|
| Zero | Stationary OR constant velocity |
| Non-zero | Acceleration in direction of resultant force |

### Finding Resultant Force
- Same direction: **Add**
- Opposite directions: **Subtract**
- Use F = ma to find acceleration

## Terminal Velocity (Newton's Laws Applied)

| Stage | Forces | Resultant | Acceleration |
|-------|--------|-----------|--------------|
| Start | Weight > Air resistance | Downward | Maximum |
| Falling | Weight > Air resistance (decreasing) | Downward (decreasing) | Decreasing |
| Terminal | Weight = Air resistance | Zero | Zero (constant velocity) |

## Stopping Distance

```
Stopping distance = Thinking distance + Braking distance
```

### Thinking Distance
```
thinking distance = speed × reaction time
```

**Factors increasing thinking distance:**
- Higher speed
- Tiredness, alcohol, drugs
- Distractions
- Poor visibility

### Braking Distance

**Factors increasing braking distance:**
- Higher speed (proportional to v²)
- Greater mass
- Worn brakes/tyres
- Wet/icy roads
- Poor tyre pressure

### Typical Stopping Distances (dry road)

| Speed (mph) | Thinking (m) | Braking (m) | Total (m) |
|-------------|--------------|-------------|-----------|
| 30 | 9 | 14 | 23 |
| 50 | 15 | 38 | 53 |
| 70 | 21 | 75 | 96 |

## Safety Features

| Feature | How it Reduces Injury |
|---------|----------------------|
| **Crumple zones** | Increase stopping time, reduce force |
| **Seat belts** | Increase stopping time |
| **Air bags** | Increase stopping time, spread force |
| **Helmets** | Increase stopping time |

**Physics:** F = m(v-u)/t → Increase t → Decrease F

## Quick Calculation Guide

### Using F = ma

**Example 1:** Find force
```
m = 800 kg, a = 2.5 m/s²
F = 800 × 2.5 = 2000 N
```

**Example 2:** Find acceleration
```
m = 1200 kg, F = 3600 N
a = 3600 ÷ 1200 = 3 m/s²
```

**Example 3:** Find mass
```
F = 500 N, a = 2 m/s²
m = 500 ÷ 2 = 250 kg
```
