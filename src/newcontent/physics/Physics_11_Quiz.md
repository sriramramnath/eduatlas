## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
Which type of electromagnetic wave has the longest wavelength?

A) Gamma rays  
B) X-rays  
C) Microwaves  
D) Radio waves

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Radio waves have the longest wavelength in the electromagnetic spectrum.

---

### Question 2
What is the speed of all electromagnetic waves in a vacuum?

A) 3 × 10⁶ m/s  
B) 3 × 10⁷ m/s  
C) 3 × 10⁸ m/s  
D) 3 × 10⁹ m/s

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: All electromagnetic waves travel at 3 × 10⁸ m/s in a vacuum.

---

### Question 3
Which electromagnetic wave is used in microwave ovens?

A) Radio waves  
B) Microwaves  
C) Infrared  
D) X-rays

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Microwave ovens use microwaves, which are absorbed by water molecules causing heating.

---

### Question 4
X-rays are used in medicine primarily because they:

A) Are visible to the eye  
B) Can pass through soft tissue but are absorbed by bone  
C) Have very low energy  
D) Are harmless to humans

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: X-rays penetrate soft tissue but are absorbed by denser materials like bone, creating images.

---

### Question 5
Which type of electromagnetic radiation is ionizing?

A) Radio waves  
B) Microwaves  
C) Visible light  
D) Gamma rays

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Gamma rays, X-rays, and UV are ionizing. Radio, microwaves, infrared, and visible light are non-ionizing.

---

### Question 6
Infrared radiation is used in:

A) X-ray imaging  
B) Remote controls  
C) Cancer treatment  
D) Sterilization

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Remote controls use infrared signals to communicate with devices.

---

### Question 7
Which colour of visible light has the shortest wavelength?

A) Red  
B) Green  
C) Blue  
D) Violet

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Violet light has the shortest wavelength (about 400 nm) in the visible spectrum.

---

### Question 8
Gamma rays are produced by:

A) Light bulbs  
B) Radio transmitters  
C) Radioactive decay  
D) Microwave ovens

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Gamma rays are emitted during radioactive decay of unstable atomic nuclei.

---

### Question 9
Which electromagnetic wave is used for mobile phone communication?

A) X-rays  
B) Gamma rays  
C) Microwaves  
D) Ultraviolet

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Mobile phones use microwaves to transmit signals.

---

### Question 10
Sunscreen is used to protect against:

A) Infrared  
B) Visible light  
C) Ultraviolet  
D) X-rays

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Sunscreen absorbs ultraviolet radiation, protecting skin from damage.

---

### Question 11
Which type of electromagnetic wave has the highest frequency?

A) Radio waves  
B) Microwaves  
C) X-rays  
D) Gamma rays

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Gamma rays have the highest frequency and highest energy in the EM spectrum.

---

### Question 12
Optical fibres use which type of electromagnetic radiation to transmit data?

A) Radio waves  
B) Microwaves  
C) Infrared/Visible light

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Optical fibres use infrared or visible light to carry data as pulses.

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
All electromagnetic waves are transverse.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: All electromagnetic waves are transverse waves.

---

### Statement 2
Sound waves are part of the electromagnetic spectrum.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Sound waves are mechanical waves, not electromagnetic waves.*

---

### Statement 3
Ultraviolet radiation has longer wavelength than visible light.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Ultraviolet has shorter wavelength than visible light. The order is: visible → UV → X-rays → Gamma.*

---

### Statement 4
Gamma rays are used in cancer treatment.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Gamma rays are used in radiotherapy to kill cancer cells.

---

### Statement 5
Infrared radiation can be felt as heat.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Infrared radiation is thermal radiation and can be felt as heat.

---

### Statement 6
X-rays have lower energy than visible light.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: X-rays have much higher energy than visible light. Energy increases from radio to gamma.*

---

### Statement 7
Radio waves can diffract around obstacles.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Radio waves have long wavelengths and can diffract (bend) around obstacles.

---

### Statement 8
Microwaves are absorbed by metal objects.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Microwaves are reflected by metal. This is why metal objects should not be put in microwave ovens.*

---

### Statement 9
The ozone layer absorbs ultraviolet radiation.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: The ozone layer in the atmosphere absorbs much of the harmful UV radiation from the Sun.

---

### Statement 10
All electromagnetic waves travel at the same speed in air as in a vacuum.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: EM waves travel slightly slower in air than in a vacuum (though the difference is small).*

---

### Statement 11
Red light has a higher frequency than blue light.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Red light has lower frequency and longer wavelength than blue light. Blue/violet has the highest frequency in visible light.*

---

### Statement 12
Lead is used as shielding against X-rays and gamma rays.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Lead is dense and absorbs X-rays and gamma rays, making it ideal for shielding.

Compare the properties of gamma rays and radio waves.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Gamma: short wavelength, high frequency, high energy (1 mark)
- Radio: long wavelength, low frequency, low energy (1 mark)
- Both: transverse, travel at 3 × 10⁸ m/s in vacuum (1 mark)
- Gamma is ionizing, radio is not (1 mark)

Explain why gamma rays are dangerous to living organisms.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Gamma rays are highly ionizing (1 mark)
- They can damage DNA in cells (1 mark)
- This can cause mutations or cancer (1 mark)

---

### Question 10 (4 marks)

- Radio, microwaves, infrared, and visible light are generally safe (1 mark)
- We are exposed to visible light and infrared constantly without harm (1 mark)
- Radio waves are used safely for communication (1 mark)
