## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
What is the unit of force?

A) Kilogram  
B) Newton  
C) Joule  
D) Watt

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Force is measured in newtons (N), named after Isaac Newton.*

---

### Question 2
A mass of 5 kg is placed on Earth where g = 10 N/kg. What is its weight?

A) 0.5 N  
B) 5 N  
C) 50 N  
D) 500 N

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: W = m × g = 5 × 10 = 50 N.*

---

### Question 3
Which statement about mass and weight is correct?

A) Mass and weight are the same thing  
B) Mass is measured in newtons  
C) Weight depends on location  
D) Mass changes when you go to the Moon

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Weight = m × g, and g varies with location. Mass is constant.*

---

### Question 4
A spring extends by 4 cm when a 2 N weight is hung from it. What is the spring constant?

A) 0.5 N/m  
B) 2 N/m  
C) 50 N/m  
D) 200 N/m

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: k = F ÷ e = 2 N ÷ 0.04 m = 50 N/m. Note: extension must be in metres.*

---

### Question 5
Hooke's Law states that:

A) Force is inversely proportional to extension  
B) Force is directly proportional to length  
C) Force is directly proportional to extension  
D) Extension is inversely proportional to force

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: F = k × e, so F ∝ e (within limit of proportionality).*

---

### Question 6
What does the gradient of a force-extension graph represent in the linear region?

A) Energy stored  
B) Spring constant  
C) Work done  
D) Limit of proportionality

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Gradient = F ÷ e = k (spring constant). 

---

### Question 7
A force of 10 N acts to the right and 6 N acts to the left. What is the resultant force?

A) 16 N to the right  
B) 4 N to the right  
C) 4 N to the left  
D) 16 N to the left

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Resultant = 10 - 6 = 4 N in direction of larger force (right).*

---

### Question 8
The energy stored in a stretched spring is given by:

A) E = F × e  
B) E = ½ × F × e  
C) E = F ÷ e  
D) E = k × e

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Energy stored = area under force-extension graph = ½ × base × height = ½ × e × F.*

---

### Question 9
Which of the following is a non-contact force?

A) Friction  
B) Tension  
C) Gravitational force  
D) Air resistance

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Gravitational, magnetic, and electrostatic forces act without physical contact.*

---

### Question 10
A spring is stretched beyond its limit of proportionality. What happens?

A) It breaks immediately  
B) It no longer obeys Hooke's Law  
C) It stores more energy  
D) It becomes stiffer

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Beyond the limit, the force-extension graph is no longer a straight line through the origin.*

---

### Question 11
An object has a mass of 60 kg. What would be its mass on the Moon?

A) 10 kg  
B) 60 kg  
C) 360 kg  
D) 600 kg

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Mass is constant everywhere. Only weight changes with g.*

---

### Question 12
A spring with k = 100 N/m is stretched by 0.1 m. What energy is stored?

A) 0.5 J  
B) 1 J  
C) 5 J  
D) 10 J

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: E = ½ × k × e² = ½ × 100 × (0.1)² = 50 × 0.01 = 0.5 J.*

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
Weight is a scalar quantity.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Weight is a vector quantity (it has magnitude and direction - towards the centre of the Earth).*

---

### Statement 2
A stiffer spring has a higher spring constant.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Spring constant k measures stiffness. Higher k means more force needed for same extension.*

---

### Statement 3
Friction always acts in the same direction as motion.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Friction always acts in the opposite direction to motion (or attempted motion).*

---

### Statement 4
The extension of a spring is measured in newtons.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Extension is a length, measured in metres (or cm). Force is measured in newtons.*

---

### Statement 5
On a force-extension graph, the area under the graph represents the work done.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Area under force-extension graph = energy stored = work done in stretching the spring.*

---

### Statement 6
Mass is measured in newtons.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Mass is measured in kilograms (kg). Weight (force) is measured in newtons (N).*

---

### Statement 7
Air resistance is a type of friction.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Air resistance is the friction force that opposes motion through air.*

---

### Statement 8
When the resultant force on an object is zero, the object must be stationary.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: When resultant force is zero, the object is either stationary OR moving at constant velocity (Newton's First Law).*

---

### Statement 9
Lubrication increases friction between surfaces.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Lubrication reduces friction by separating the surfaces with a fluid layer.*

---

### Statement 10
A spring that stretches 2 cm with 4 N force will stretch 4 cm with 8 N force (within limit).

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Hooke's Law: F ∝ e, so doubling force doubles extension.*

---

### Statement 11
Tension is a pushing force.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Tension is a pulling force in a string, rope, or cable.*

---

### Statement 12
The value of g is the same on the Moon as on Earth.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: g on Moon ≈ 1.6 N/kg, while g on Earth ≈ 10 N/kg. The Moon has weaker gravity.*

## Short Answer Questions

### Question 1 (2 marks)
State the difference between mass and weight.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Mass is the amount of matter in an object (measured in kg) (1 mark)
- Weight is the force of gravity acting on an object (measured in N) (1 mark)

---

### Question 2 (3 marks)
A spring has an original length of 8 cm. When a 6 N weight is attached, it stretches to 14 cm. Calculate:
(a) The extension of the spring (1 mark)
(b) The spring constant (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:***
(a) Extension = 14 - 8 = 6 cm = 0.06 m (1 mark)
(b) Formula: k = F ÷ e (1 mark)
    k = 6 ÷ 0.06 = 100 N/m (1 mark)

---

### Question 3 (3 marks)
Explain what is meant by the limit of proportionality for a spring.

**Mark Scheme:***
- The point beyond which Hooke's Law is no longer obeyed (1 mark)
- Beyond this point, force is no longer proportional to extension (1 mark)
- The spring may be permanently stretched/deformed (1 mark)

---

### Question 4 (4 marks)
Describe an experiment to determine the spring constant of a spring.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Suspend spring from clamp stand and measure original length (1 mark)
- Add known masses/weights and measure new length each time (1 mark)
- Calculate extension for each weight (1 mark)
- Plot force-extension graph; gradient = spring constant (1 mark)

---

### Question 5 (3 marks)
A 2 kg mass is taken from Earth (g = 10 N/kg) to the Moon (g = 1.6 N/kg).
Calculate:
(a) Its weight on Earth (1 mark)
(b) Its weight on the Moon (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:***
(a) W = m × g = 2 × 10 = 20 N (1 mark)
(b) W = m × g = 2 × 1.6 (1 mark)
    W = 3.2 N (1 mark)

---

### Question 6 (2 marks)
State two ways to reduce friction between moving surfaces.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Any two from:
  - Use lubricants/oil/grease (1 mark)
  - Use ball bearings (1 mark)
  - Make surfaces smoother (1 mark)
  - Streamline the shape (1 mark)

---

### Question 7 (4 marks)
A spring with spring constant 80 N/m is stretched by 0.15 m.
(a) Calculate the force required. (2 marks)
(b) Calculate the energy stored in the spring. (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:***
(a) F = k × e = 80 × 0.15 (1 mark)
    F = 12 N (1 mark)
(b) E = ½ × k × e² = ½ × 80 × (0.15)² (1 mark)
    E = 0.9 J (1 mark)

---

### Question 8 (3 marks)
Explain why a person weighs less on the Moon than on Earth.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Weight = mass × gravitational field strength (1 mark)
- Mass is constant (same on Moon and Earth) (1 mark)
- g on Moon (1.6 N/kg) is less than g on Earth (10 N/kg) (1 mark)

---

### Question 9 (2 marks)
Forces of 8 N north and 6 N east act on an object. Calculate the magnitude of the resultant force.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Use Pythagoras: F² = 8² + 6² = 64 + 36 = 100 (1 mark)
- F = √100 = 10 N (1 mark)

---

### Question 10 (4 marks)
Explain the difference between elastic and inelastic deformation.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Elastic: object returns to original shape when force removed (1 mark)
- Inelastic (plastic): object does not return to original shape (1 mark)
- Elastic occurs within limit of proportionality (1 mark)
- Inelastic occurs when material is stretched too far (1 mark)

## Extended Answer Question (6 marks)

### Question
A student investigates how a spring stretches when different masses are hung from it.

| Mass (g) | 0 | 100 | 200 | 300 | 400 | 500 |
|----------|---|-----|-----|-----|-----|-----|
| Length (cm) | 10.0 | 12.0 | 14.0 | 16.0 | 18.5 | 21.5 |

(a) Calculate the extension for each mass and complete the table. (2 marks)
(b) Plot a graph of force against extension. (2 marks)
(c) Determine the spring constant from your graph. (1 mark)
(d) Identify the limit of proportionality and explain your answer. (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme:***

**(a) Extensions***
- 0, 2.0, 4.0, 6.0, 8.5, 11.5 cm (all correct = 2 marks, 4-5 correct = 1 mark)
- Force values: 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 N (using W = mg, g = 10 N/kg)

**(b) Graph***
- Correct axes labelled with units (1 mark)
- Points plotted correctly and suitable line (1 mark)

**(c) Spring constant***
- Gradient from linear region: k = ΔF ÷ Δe (e.g., 3 ÷ 0.06 = 50 N/m) (1 mark)
- Accept 48-52 N/m

**(d) Limit of proportionality***
- At 300 g / 3 N / 6 cm extension (1 mark)
- Beyond this, graph is not straight/line doesn't go through origin if extended (1 mark)
