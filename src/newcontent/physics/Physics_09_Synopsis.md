# Chapter 9: Electric Charge
## Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Physics - Concise Synopsis (Physics only)

---

## Charging by Friction

### Process
- Two insulating materials rubbed together
- Electrons transfer from one to the other
- One becomes negative (gains electrons)
- One becomes positive (loses electrons)

### Common Examples

| Materials | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| Polythene + cloth | Polythene negative, cloth positive |
| Perspex + cloth | Perspex positive, cloth negative |
| Ebonite + fur | Ebonite negative, fur positive |

---

## Electric Fields

### Definition
Region where a charged object experiences a force.

### Field Lines
- **Direction**: Positive → Negative
- **Density**: Shows field strength
- **Never cross**

### Field Patterns

| Arrangement | Pattern |
|-------------|---------|
| Single + | Lines radiate outward |
| Single - | Lines point inward |
| + and - | Lines from + to - |
| + and + | Lines repel |
| Parallel plates | Uniform, parallel lines |

---

## Forces Between Charges

| Combination | Force |
|-------------|-------|
| Like charges (+/+ or -/-) | **Repel** |
| Opposite charges (+/-) | **Attract** |

### Induction
- Charged object near neutral object
- Charges separate in neutral object
- Attraction occurs (opposite charges closer)

## Electrostatic Hazards

| Situation | Risk |
|-----------|------|
| Fuel tankers | Sparks ignite fuel |
| Operating theatres | Sparks ignite gases |
| Electronics | Damage to components |
| Grain silos | Explosions |

### Safety Measures

| Measure | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Earthing** | Safe discharge to ground |
| **Conducting straps** | Prevent charge build-up |
| **Anti-static mats** | Conduct charge away |
| **Humidity control** | Moist air conducts charge |

---

## Electrostatic Applications

| Application | Principle |
|-------------|-----------|
| **Photocopiers** | Toner attracted to charged drum |
| **Inkjet printers** | Charged droplets deflected |
| **Precipitators** | Charged dust attracted to plates |
| **Spray painting** | Charged paint attracted to object |

---

## Gold Leaf Electroscope

### Function
Detects presence of electric charge.

### Operation
- Charged object touches cap
- Charge flows to gold leaf
- Like charges repel, leaf rises

---

## Key Points

1. **Only electrons move** - protons stay in nucleus
2. **Field lines** - from + to -
3. **Like charges repel** - opposite charges attract
4. **Induction** - explains attraction of neutral objects
5. **Earthing** - essential safety measure
6. **Charge conservation** - total charge remains constant
