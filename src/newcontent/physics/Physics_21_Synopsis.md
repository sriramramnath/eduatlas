# Chapter 21: Electric Motors and Electromagnetic Induction
## Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Physics - Concise Synopsis

---

## Electromagnetic Induction

### Definition
Generating voltage by changing magnetic field.

### Methods
- Move magnet through coil
- Move wire through field
- Change current in nearby coil

### Factors Affecting Voltage
- Stronger magnet → more voltage
- More turns → more voltage
- Faster movement → more voltage

---

## Generators

### AC Generator
- Coil rotates in magnetic field
- Slip rings maintain contact
- Produces AC

### DC Generator
- Commutator reverses connections
- Produces DC

---

## Fleming's Right-Hand Rule

For generators (induction):
- **Th**umb = **Th**rust (Motion)
- **F**irst finger = **F**ield (N→S)
- **S**econd finger = Current

---

## Transformers

### Structure
- Primary coil
- Secondary coil
- Iron core

### How They Work
1. AC in primary → changing magnetic field
2. Iron core channels field
3. Field induces voltage in secondary

### Equation
```
Vp/Vs = Np/Ns
```

### Types

| Type | Turns | Effect |
|------|-------|--------|
| **Step-up** | Ns > Np | Vs > Vp |
| **Step-down** | Ns < Np | Vs < Vp |

### Example
```
Np = 100, Ns = 500, Vp = 20 V
Vp/Vs = Np/Ns
20/Vs = 100/500 = 1/5
Vs = 100 V (step-up)
```

---

## Power Transmission

### Why High Voltage?
- P = IV
- High V → Low I
- Low I → Less heat loss (P = I²R)
- More efficient

### National Grid
```
Power station → Step-up → Transmission → Step-down → Homes
   ~25 kV        400 kV      400 kV       11-33 kV    230 V
```

## Key Exam Points

1. **Induction** - changing field induces voltage
2. **Right-hand rule** - for generators
3. **Transformers** - AC only
4. **Vp/Vs = Np/Ns** - transformer equation
5. **Step-up/down** - know difference
6. **High voltage transmission** - less loss
