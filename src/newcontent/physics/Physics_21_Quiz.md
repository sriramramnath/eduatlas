## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
Electromagnetic induction was discovered by:

A) Isaac Newton  
B) Michael Faraday  
C) Albert Einstein  
D) James Watt

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Michael Faraday discovered electromagnetic induction in 1831.*

---

### Question 2
A voltage is induced in a coil when:

A) A steady current flows through it  
B) The magnetic field through it changes  
C) It is heated  
D) It is placed in water

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Electromagnetic induction requires a changing magnetic field.*

---

### Question 3
A transformer has 200 primary turns and 800 secondary turns. If the primary voltage is 50 V, what is the secondary voltage?

A) 12.5 V  
B) 50 V  
C) 100 V  
D) 200 V

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Vp/Vs = Np/Ns → 50/Vs = 200/800 → Vs = 50 × 4 = 200 V.*

---

### Question 4
Transformers only work with:

A) DC current  
B) AC current  
C) Both AC and DC  
D) Neither AC nor DC

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Transformers need a changing current to create a changing magnetic field, so they only work with AC.*

---

### Question 5
Electricity is transmitted at high voltage because:

A) It is safer  
B) It reduces energy loss in cables  
C) It increases the current  
D) It makes the electricity cleaner

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: High voltage means lower current for the same power, which reduces heat loss (I²R) in transmission cables.*

---

### Question 6
Fleming's right-hand rule is used for:

A) Finding the force on a wire  
B) Finding the direction of induced current  
C) Finding the magnetic field direction  
D) Finding the resistance of a wire

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Fleming's right-hand rule (Thumb = Motion, First = Field, Second = Current) is used for generators/induction.*

---

### Question 7
A step-down transformer has:

A) More turns on the secondary than primary  
B) Fewer turns on the secondary than primary  
C) Equal turns on both coils  
D) No iron core

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Step-down transformers have Ns < Np, so Vs < Vp.*

---

### Question 8
In an ideal transformer:

A) Power in > Power out  
B) Power in = Power out  
C) Power in < Power out  
D) Power out = 0

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: In an ideal transformer, no energy is lost, so power in equals power out (Vp × Ip = Vs × Is).*

---

### Question 9
Which of the following increases the induced voltage in a generator?

A) Using a weaker magnet  
B) Rotating the coil more slowly  
C) Using more turns on the coil  
D) Using a smaller coil

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: More turns, stronger magnet, or faster rotation all increase induced voltage.*

---

### Question 10
The National Grid transmits electricity at approximately:

A) 230 V  
B) 1000 V  
C) 11,000 V  
D) 275,000 - 400,000 V

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: The National Grid transmits at very high voltages (275-400 kV) to minimize energy loss.*

---

### Question 11
A commutator in a DC generator:

A) Increases the voltage  
B) Reverses the connections every half turn  
C) Reduces the current  
D) Stores energy

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The commutator reverses connections to keep current flowing in the same direction (DC).*

---

### Question 12
Slip rings are used in:

A) DC generators  
B) AC generators  
C) Both AC and DC generators  
D) Neither type of generator

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: AC generators use slip rings for continuous contact. DC generators use a commutator.*

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
Electromagnetic induction occurs when a conductor moves through a magnetic field.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Moving a conductor through a magnetic field (or changing the field) induces a voltage.*

---

### Statement 2
A transformer can increase voltage without any energy loss.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Real transformers have some energy loss (heat). Only ideal transformers have no loss.*

---

### Statement 3
Fleming's left-hand rule is used for generators.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Fleming's left-hand rule is for the motor effect. Right-hand rule is for generators/induction.*

---

### Statement 4
Step-up transformers increase voltage and decrease current.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Step-up transformers increase voltage (Vs > Vp) and decrease current (Is < Ip) to conserve power.*

---

### Statement 5
The iron core in a transformer is used to reduce energy loss.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: The iron core channels the magnetic field efficiently from primary to secondary coil.*

---

### Statement 6
DC generators use slip rings.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: DC generators use a commutator. AC generators use slip rings.*

---

### Statement 7
Higher transmission voltages mean higher currents for the same power.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: P = IV, so higher voltage means LOWER current for the same power.*

---

### Statement 8
A changing magnetic field is required for electromagnetic induction.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Induction requires a changing magnetic field - either by moving the magnet, moving the coil, or changing the current.*

---

### Statement 9
The primary and secondary coils in a transformer are electrically connected.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: The coils are magnetically coupled through the iron core but are electrically isolated.*

---

### Statement 10
Eddy currents in a transformer core reduce efficiency.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Eddy currents in the core cause heating, which wastes energy and reduces efficiency.*

---

### Statement 11
AC is used for power transmission because it can be easily transformed.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: AC can be stepped up or down easily using transformers, making it ideal for power transmission.*

---

### Statement 12
The faster a coil rotates in a generator, the lower the induced voltage.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Faster rotation means the coil cuts magnetic field lines more quickly, inducing a HIGHER voltage.*

### Question 3 (4 marks)
A transformer has 400 primary turns and 100 secondary turns. The primary voltage is 240 V.
(a) Calculate the secondary voltage. (2 marks)
(b) State whether this is a step-up or step-down transformer. (1 mark)
(c) Calculate the current in the secondary coil if the primary current is 0.5 A (assume 100% efficiency). (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme:***
(a) Vp/Vs = Np/Ns → 240/Vs = 400/100 (1 mark)
    Vs = 60 V (1 mark)
(b) Step-down (1 mark)
(c) Vp × Ip = Vs × Is → 240 × 0.5 = 60 × Is → Is = 2 A (1 mark)

## Extended Answer Question (6 marks)

### Question
(a) Describe the construction of a simple AC generator. (3 marks)
(b) Explain how the generator produces an alternating current. (3 marks)

**Mark Scheme:***

**(a) AC generator construction***
- Coil of wire that can rotate (1 mark)
- Magnetic field (permanent magnets or electromagnets) (1 mark)
- Slip rings and brushes for electrical contact (1 mark)

**(b) How AC is produced***
- Coil rotates, cutting magnetic field lines (1 mark)
- This induces a voltage in the coil (1 mark)
- Direction of induced current reverses every half turn, producing AC (1 mark)
