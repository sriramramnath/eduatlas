# Chapter 6: Mains Electricity
## Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Physics - Concise Synopsis

---

## Essential Equations

```
Current:           I = Q ÷ t
Voltage:           V = E ÷ Q
Power:             P = I × V
Energy:            E = P × t
```

---

## Electric Current

### Definition
**Current** = rate of flow of charge

```
I = Q ÷ t
```

- I in amperes (A)
- Q in coulombs (C)
- t in seconds (s)

### Direction
- **Conventional current**: Positive to negative
- **Electron flow**: Negative to positive (opposite)

---

## AC vs DC

| Feature | DC (Direct Current) | AC (Alternating Current) |
|---------|--------------------|--------------------------|
| Direction | One direction only | Changes periodically |
| Source | Batteries, cells | Mains electricity |
| Graph | Horizontal line | Sine wave |

### UK Mains Supply
- **Voltage**: 230 V
- **Frequency**: 50 Hz
- **Type**: AC

---

## Mains Wiring

| Wire | Colour | Function | Voltage |
|------|--------|----------|---------|
| **Live** | Brown | Supplies current | 230 V AC |
| **Neutral** | Blue | Completes circuit | ~0 V |
| **Earth** | Green/Yellow | Safety | 0 V |

### Safety Features

| Feature | Purpose |
|---------|---------|
| **Fuse** | Melts if current too high |
| **Circuit breaker** | Switches off if current too high (resettable) |
| **RCD** | Detects current leakage to earth |
| **Earth wire** | Provides safe path for fault current |
| **Double insulation** | Plastic casing, no earth needed |

### Fuse Selection
- Choose next standard value above normal operating current
- Standard values: 3 A, 5 A, 13 A

---

## Electrical Power

```
P = I × V
```

- P in watts (W)
- I in amperes (A)
- V in volts (V)

### Alternative Forms
```
P = I² × R
P = V² ÷ R
```

---

## Electrical Energy

```
E = P × t
```

| Power | Time | Energy |
|-------|------|--------|
| W | s | J (joules) |
| kW | h | kWh (kilowatt-hours) |

### Kilowatt-hour (kWh)
- Unit on electricity bills
- 1 kWh = 3.6 MJ = 3 600 000 J

### Cost Calculation
```
cost = energy (kWh) × price per kWh
```

---

## Quick Calculation Guide

### Finding Current
```
Q = 60 C, t = 15 s
I = 60 ÷ 15 = 4 A
```

### Finding Power
```
I = 5 A, V = 230 V
P = 5 × 230 = 1150 W
```

### Finding Energy (in kWh)
```
P = 2 kW, t = 4 h
E = 2 × 4 = 8 kWh
```

### Finding Cost
```
Energy = 10 kWh, price = 14p/kWh
cost = 10 × 14 = 140p = £1.40
```

---

## Key Exam Points

1. **AC changes direction**, DC flows one way
2. **Mains frequency** = 50 Hz (UK)
3. **Wire colours** - Brown L, Blue N, Green/Yellow E
4. **Fuse in live wire** - breaks circuit completely
5. **Earth wire** - safety, 0 V
6. **Power** = IV (watts)
7. **Energy bills** use kWh

---

## Safety Summary

| Situation | Protection |
|-----------|------------|
| Current too high | Fuse melts / Circuit breaker trips |
| Current leaks to earth | RCD trips |
| Metal casing becomes live | Earth wire provides safe path |
| Plastic appliance | Double insulation, no earth needed |
