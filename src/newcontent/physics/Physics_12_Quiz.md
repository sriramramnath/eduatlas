## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
According to the law of reflection:

A) Angle of incidence is greater than angle of reflection  
B) Angle of incidence equals angle of reflection  
C) Angle of incidence is less than angle of reflection  
D) Angles are unrelated

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The law of reflection states that the angle of incidence equals the angle of reflection.*

---

### Question 2
When light passes from air into glass, it:

A) Speeds up and bends away from the normal  
B) Slows down and bends towards the normal  
C) Speeds up and bends towards the normal  
D) Slows down and bends away from the normal

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Light slows down in denser media and bends towards the normal when entering a denser medium.*

---

### Question 3
Total internal reflection can occur when light travels from:

A) Air to glass  
B) Glass to air  
C) Air to water  
D) Any medium to any other

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: TIR occurs when light travels from a denser to a less dense medium and the angle of incidence exceeds the critical angle.*

---

### Question 4
The critical angle for a medium with refractive index 2 is:

A) 30°  
B) 45°  
C) 60°  
D) 90°

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: sin(c) = 1/n = 1/2 = 0.5, so c = 30°.*

---

### Question 5
Optical fibres work by:

A) Refraction  
B) Reflection  
C) Total internal reflection  
D) Diffraction

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Light undergoes repeated total internal reflection along the core of the optical fibre.*

---

### Question 6
A converging lens is:

A) Thinner in the middle  
B) Thicker in the middle  
C) Flat  
D) Curved on one side only

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Converging (convex) lenses are thicker in the middle and converge light to a focal point.*

---

### Question 7
When an object is placed inside the focal length of a converging lens, the image is:

A) Real and inverted  
B) Virtual and upright  
C) Real and upright  
D) Virtual and inverted

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: When the object is inside F, a converging lens produces a virtual, upright, magnified image (like a magnifying glass).*

---

### Question 8
Which colour of light is deviated most when passing through a prism?

A) Red  
B) Green  
C) Blue  
D) Violet

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Violet light has the shortest wavelength and is deviated most during dispersion.*

---

### Question 9
The image in a plane mirror is:

A) Real and inverted  
B) Virtual and upright  
C) Real and upright  
D) Virtual and inverted

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Plane mirrors produce virtual, upright images that are laterally inverted.*

---

### Question 10
A red object appears red because it:

A) Absorbs red light  
B) Reflects red light  
C) Transmits red light  
D) Refracts red light

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A red object reflects red light and absorbs all other colours of visible light.*

---

### Question 11
The refractive index of a medium is defined as:

A) Speed of light in medium ÷ speed in vacuum  
B) Speed of light in vacuum ÷ speed in medium  
C) Wavelength in vacuum ÷ wavelength in medium  
D) Frequency in vacuum ÷ frequency in medium

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: n = c/v, where c is speed in vacuum and v is speed in the medium.*

### Question 12
Which of the following is NOT an application of total internal reflection?

A) Optical fibres

**Explanation**: A magnifying glass uses refraction through a lens, not total internal reflection.*

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
The angle of incidence is measured from the surface.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: The angle of incidence is measured from the normal (perpendicular to the surface), not from the surface itself.*

---

### Statement 2
Light travels faster in glass than in air.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Light travels slower in denser media. It travels fastest in a vacuum, slower in air, and even slower in glass.*

---

### Statement 3
Total internal reflection can only occur when light travels from a less dense to a denser medium.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: TIR occurs when light travels from a denser to a less dense medium (e.g., glass to air).*

---

### Statement 4
A diverging lens always produces a virtual image.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Diverging (concave) lenses always produce virtual, upright, smaller images regardless of object position.*

---

### Statement 5
White light is a mixture of all colours of the visible spectrum.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: White light can be split into the colours of the spectrum (ROYGBIV) by a prism.*

---

### Statement 6
The critical angle depends on the colour of light.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Different colours have slightly different refractive indices, so they have slightly different critical angles (dispersion).*

---

### Statement 7
Optical fibres can only carry signals over short distances.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Optical fibres can carry signals over very long distances with minimal signal loss, making them ideal for telecommunications.*

---

### Statement 8
A convex lens is also called a converging lens.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Convex lenses converge parallel rays to a focal point, so they are called converging lenses.*

---

### Statement 9
The image formed by a plane mirror is laterally inverted.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Images in plane mirrors are left-right reversed (laterally inverted).*

---

### Statement 10
Diamond has a high refractive index, which contributes to its sparkle.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Diamond's high refractive index (2.42) means light undergoes total internal reflection easily, creating brilliant sparkle.*

---

### Statement 11
When light passes from water to air, it bends towards the normal.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: When light passes from water (denser) to air (less dense), it speeds up and bends away from the normal.*

---

### Statement 12
The primary colours of light are red, yellow, and blue.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: The primary colours of light are red, green, and blue (RGB).*

- Light undergoes total internal reflection at the core-cladding boundary (1 mark)
- This happens repeatedly, guiding light along the fibre (1 mark)

### Question
(a) Explain the difference between specular and diffuse reflection. (2 marks)
(b) Describe an experiment to demonstrate total internal reflection. (2 marks)
(c) Explain why diamond sparkles more than glass. (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:***

**(a) Specular vs diffuse reflection***
- Specular: smooth surface, parallel rays reflected parallel, clear image formed (1 mark)
- Diffuse: rough surface, light scattered in different directions, no clear image (1 mark)

**(b) TIR experiment***
- Shine light through semi-circular glass block (1 mark)
- Increase angle of incidence until light no longer emerges / reflects internally (1 mark)
- Or: demonstrate with optical fibre or water jet

**(c) Diamond sparkle***
- Diamond has higher refractive index than glass (1 mark)
- This gives a smaller critical angle / more TIR occurs (1 mark)
- Light is trapped and reflected many times, creating sparkle (1 mark)
