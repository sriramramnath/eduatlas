## 15.1 Temperature and Heat

### Definitions

| Term | Definition | Unit |
|------|------------|------|
| **Temperature** | Measure of how hot or cold something is | °C or K |
| **Heat** | Energy transferred due to temperature difference | Joules (J) |
| **Thermal energy** | Total kinetic energy of particles | Joules (J) |

### Temperature Scales

| Scale | Freezing point of water | Boiling point of water |
|-------|------------------------|------------------------|
| **Celsius (°C)** | 0°C | 100°C |
| **Kelvin (K)** | 273 K | 373 K |

### Converting Temperatures

```
K = °C + 273
°C = K - 273
```

### Absolute Zero

- **0 K** = -273°C
- Theoretical temperature where all particle motion stops
- Lowest possible temperature

## 15.2 Methods of Heat Transfer

### Conduction

**Definition:** Heat transfer through a material without the material moving.

**How it works:**
1. Particles at hot end vibrate more
2. Vibrations passed to neighboring particles
3. Energy transferred along material

**Good conductors:** Metals (copper, aluminum)
**Good insulators:** Wood, plastic, air, wool

```
Hot end → Cold end
▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓░░░░░░░░
(vibrations passed along)
```

### Convection

**Definition:** Heat transfer through the movement of fluids (liquids and gases).

**How it works:**
1. Fluid heated → expands → becomes less dense
2. Less dense fluid rises
3. Cooler, denser fluid sinks
4. Creates convection current

```
    ↑↑↑ Heated fluid rises
    ┌─────────┐
    │  Heat   │
    │ source  │
    └─────────┘
    ↓↓↓ Cool fluid sinks
```

**Examples:**
- Hot air rising
- Sea breezes
- Heating a room with radiator

### Radiation

**Definition:** Heat transfer by electromagnetic waves (infrared).

**Key facts:**
- Does not require a medium
- Can travel through vacuum
- All objects emit infrared radiation
- Hotter objects emit more radiation
- Dark, matte surfaces are best emitters and absorbers
- Light, shiny surfaces are poor emitters and absorbers

```
    Sun →→→→→→→→→→→→→ Earth
    (infrared radiation travels through space)
```

A cube with different surfaces shows that:
- **Matte black** surface emits most infrared
- **Shiny silver** surface emits least infrared

## 15.4 Specific Heat Capacity

### Definition

**Specific heat capacity (c)** is the amount of energy required to raise the temperature of 1 kg of a substance by 1°C.

### Equation

```
energy = mass × specific heat capacity × temperature change

E = m × c × Δθ

Where:
E = energy transferred (J)
m = mass (kg)
c = specific heat capacity (J/kg°C)
Δθ = temperature change (°C)
```

### Specific Heat Capacity Values

| Substance | Specific Heat Capacity (J/kg°C) |
|-----------|--------------------------------|
| Water | 4200 |
| Aluminum | 900 |
| Iron | 450 |
| Copper | 390 |
| Lead | 130 |

Water has a very high specific heat capacity - it takes a lot of energy to heat it up.

### Worked Example

Calculate the energy needed to heat 2 kg of water from 20°C to 80°C.

```
E = m × c × Δθ
E = 2 × 4200 × (80 - 20)
E = 2 × 4200 × 60
E = 504,000 J = 504 kJ
```

## 15.5 Specific Latent Heat

### Changes of State

```
    SOLID →→→ LIQUID →→→ GAS
         melt        boil
         freeze      condense
    ←←←←←←←   ←←←←←←←
```

During a change of state:
- Temperature stays constant
- Energy is used to break/form bonds between particles

### Specific Latent Heat of Fusion

**Definition:** Energy required to melt 1 kg of solid at its melting point.

```
E = m × Lf

Where:
E = energy (J)
m = mass (kg)
Lf = specific latent heat of fusion (J/kg)
```

For water: Lf = 334,000 J/kg

### Specific Latent Heat of Vaporization

**Definition:** Energy required to boil 1 kg of liquid at its boiling point.

```
E = m × Lv

Where:
E = energy (J)
m = mass (kg)
Lv = specific latent heat of vaporization (J/kg)
```

For water: Lv = 2,260,000 J/kg

### Worked Example

Calculate the energy needed to melt 0.5 kg of ice at 0°C.

```
E = m × Lf
E = 0.5 × 334,000
E = 167,000 J = 167 kJ
```

## 15.6 Insulation and Reducing Heat Loss

### Methods to Reduce Heat Loss

| Method | How it Works |
|--------|--------------|
| **Cavity wall insulation** | Traps air (poor conductor) in wall cavity |
| **Loft insulation** | Thick layer of fibreglass traps air |
| **Double glazing** | Air gap between panes reduces conduction |
| **Draught excluders** | Prevents convection currents |
| **Carpets and curtains** | Trap air, reduce conduction |

### U-Values

**U-value** measures how easily heat passes through a material.

- **Lower U-value** = better insulator
- **Higher U-value** = poorer insulator

## Common Student Errors

| Error | Correction |
|-------|------------|
| Confusing heat and temperature | Heat is energy, temperature is a measure |
| Forgetting Δθ | Temperature change, not final temperature |
| Thinking temperature changes during state change | Temperature stays constant |
| Confusing Lf and Lv | Fusion = melting, Vaporization = boiling |
| Wrong units | Mass in kg, c in J/kg°C |
