## Nuclear Fission

### Definition
Splitting of heavy nucleus → two smaller nuclei + energy + neutrons

### Chain Reaction
```tex
\begin{tikzpicture}[x=1cm,y=1cm,>=stealth,font=\small]
  \node (f1) at (0,0) {Fission};
  \node (n) at (3.3,0) {Neutrons};
  \node (f2) at (6.6,0) {More fission};
  \draw[->] (f1) -- (n);
  \draw[->] (n) -- (f2);
  \draw[->] (f2.south) .. controls (5.2,-1.0) and (1.4,-1.0) .. (f1.south);
\end{tikzpicture}
```

**Controlled:** Nuclear reactor
**Uncontrolled:** Nuclear weapon

## Nuclear Reactor Components

| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| Fuel rods | U-235 undergoes fission |
| Moderator | Slows neutrons (graphite/water) |
| Control rods | Absorb neutrons, control rate |
| Coolant | Removes heat |
| Shielding | Contains radiation |

---

## Nuclear Fusion

### Definition
Joining of two light nuclei → heavier nucleus + energy

### Example
```
²H + ³H → ⁴He + ¹n + Energy
```

### Conditions Required
- **High temperature** (~10⁸ °C)
- **High pressure/density**
- **Confinement**

---

## Fission vs Fusion

| | Fission | Fusion |
|--|---------|--------|
| Process | Split heavy | Join light |
| Fuel | U, Pu | H isotopes |
| Conditions | Normal T | Extreme T |
| Waste | Radioactive | Non-radioactive |
| Control | Achieved | Not yet for power |

## Mass-Energy Equivalence

```
E = mc²
```
- Mass can convert to energy
- Mass defect = energy released
