## Nature of Sound

### Properties
- **Type:** Longitudinal wave
- **Medium:** Requires material medium
- **Production:** Vibrating objects
- **Cannot travel through vacuum**

## Speed of Sound

### In Different Media

| Medium | Speed (approx) |
|--------|----------------|
| Air (20°C) | 343 m/s |
| Water | 1500 m/s |
| Steel | 5000 m/s |

### Key Facts
- Fastest in **solids**
- Slowest in **gases**
- Increases with **temperature** in gases
- Air: ~330-340 m/s

---

## Properties of Sound

### Pitch
- Determined by **frequency**
- Higher f = higher pitch
- Lower f = lower pitch

### Loudness
- Determined by **amplitude**
- Larger amplitude = louder
- Smaller amplitude = quieter

---

## Range of Hearing

### Human Hearing
- **Lower limit:** 20 Hz
- **Upper limit:** 20,000 Hz (20 kHz)

### Types of Sound

| Type | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| **Infrasound** | < 20 Hz |
| **Audible** | 20 Hz - 20 kHz |
| **Ultrasound** | > 20 kHz |

---

## Ultrasound

### Definition
Sound with frequency above 20,000 Hz.

### Uses
- Medical imaging (fetus, organs)
- SONAR (underwater detection)
- Cleaning (vibrations remove dirt)
- Distance measurement
- Industrial testing

### Advantages over X-rays
- Non-ionizing (safe)
- Can be used during pregnancy
- Real-time imaging

---

## Echoes

### Formula
```
distance = (speed × time) ÷ 2
```
(Divide by 2: sound travels to object AND back)

### Example
```
v = 330 m/s, t = 4 s
d = (330 × 4) ÷ 2 = 660 m
```

### Applications
- SONAR (submarines, seabed mapping)
- Bats navigation
- Distance measurement

## The Ear

### Structure
1. **Outer ear (pinna)** - collects sound
2. **Ear canal** - directs sound
3. **Eardrum** - vibrates
4. **Ossicles** - amplify vibrations
5. **Cochlea** - converts to electrical signals
6. **Auditory nerve** - sends to brain
