## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
A car travels 100 m north, then 60 m south. What is the displacement of the car?

A) 160 m  
B) 40 m north  
C) 40 m south  
D) 100 m

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Displacement = final position - initial position = (100 - 60) = 40 m in the north direction.

---

### Question 2
What does the gradient of a distance-time graph represent?

A) Acceleration  
B) Velocity  
C) Speed  
D) Displacement

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Gradient of distance-time graph = change in distance ÷ change in time = speed.

---

### Question 3
A car accelerates from rest to 20 m/s in 5 seconds. What is its acceleration?

A) 100 m/s²  
B) 4 m/s²  
C) 0.25 m/s²  
D) 25 m/s²

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: a = (v - u) ÷ t = (20 - 0) ÷ 5 = 4 m/s²

---

### Question 4
What does the area under a velocity-time graph represent?

A) Acceleration  
B) Speed  
C) Displacement  
D) Time

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Area under v-t graph = velocity × time = displacement (when velocity is constant). 

---

### Question 5
An object is thrown vertically upwards. At its maximum height:

A) Its velocity is maximum  
B) Its acceleration is zero  
C) Its velocity is zero  
D) Its acceleration changes direction

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: At maximum height, the object momentarily stops before falling back down, so velocity = 0. Acceleration remains g = 10 m/s² downwards.

---

### Question 6
A ticker timer produces 50 dots per second. What is the time interval between consecutive dots?

A) 0.5 s  
B) 0.05 s  
C) 0.02 s  
D) 0.01 s

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Time interval = 1 ÷ frequency = 1 ÷ 50 = 0.02 s.

---

### Question 7
Which of the following is a vector quantity?

A) Distance  
B) Speed  
C) Mass  
D) Velocity

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Velocity has both magnitude and direction, making it a vector. Distance, speed, and mass are scalars.

---

### Question 8
A skydiver reaches terminal velocity when:

A) Air resistance is zero  
B) Weight equals air resistance  
C) Weight is greater than air resistance  
D) The parachute is opened

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: At terminal velocity, forces are balanced (weight = air resistance), so resultant force = 0 and velocity is constant.

---

### Question 9
On a velocity-time graph, a horizontal line indicates:

A) The object is stationary  
B) Constant acceleration  
C) Constant velocity  
D) Deceleration

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Horizontal line means velocity is not changing (constant), so acceleration = 0.

---

### Question 10
A ball is dropped from rest. What is its velocity after 2 seconds? (g = 10 m/s²)

A) 5 m/s  
B) 10 m/s  
C) 20 m/s  
D) 40 m/s

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: v = u + at = 0 + (10 × 2) = 20 m/s.

---

### Question 11
An object moves with constant speed in a circular path. Which statement is correct?

A) Its velocity is constant  
B) Its acceleration is zero  
C) Its velocity is changing  
D) Its displacement is constant

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: In circular motion, direction is constantly changing, so velocity (a vector) is changing even if speed is constant.

---

### Question 12
The gradient of a velocity-time graph gives:

A) Speed  
B) Distance  
C) Acceleration  
D) Displacement

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Gradient = Δvelocity ÷ Δtime = acceleration.

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
Distance and displacement always have the same value.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Distance is the total path length (scalar), while displacement is the straight-line distance with direction (vector). They only have the same value when motion is in a straight line without changing direction.*

---

### Statement 2
Speed is a vector quantity.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Speed is a scalar quantity (magnitude only). Velocity is the vector equivalent that includes direction.*

---

### Statement 3
The unit of acceleration is m/s².

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Acceleration = change in velocity ÷ time = (m/s) ÷ s = m/s².

---

### Statement 4
A horizontal line on a distance-time graph means the object is moving at constant speed.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: A horizontal line on a distance-time graph means the object is stationary (not moving). The distance is not changing with time.*

---

### Statement 5
At terminal velocity, the acceleration of a falling object is zero.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: At terminal velocity, weight = air resistance, so resultant force = 0, and acceleration = 0. The object falls at constant velocity.

---

### Statement 6
The area under a velocity-time graph represents acceleration.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: The area under a velocity-time graph represents displacement (or distance if all positive). The gradient represents acceleration.*

---

### Statement 7
A car braking has negative acceleration.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Negative acceleration (deceleration) occurs when an object slows down - its velocity is decreasing.

---

### Statement 8
An object can have constant speed but changing velocity.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: In circular motion, speed can be constant while velocity changes because the direction is constantly changing.

---

### Statement 9
The value of g on Earth is approximately 10 m/s².

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: g = 9.8 m/s², but 10 m/s² is commonly used for calculations in IGCSE Physics.

---

### Statement 10
On a velocity-time graph, a curved line indicates changing acceleration.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: A curved line means the gradient is changing, which means acceleration is changing (non-uniform acceleration).

---

### Statement 11
Displacement can be zero even when distance travelled is not zero.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: If you walk 10 m north and then 10 m south, distance = 20 m but displacement = 0 (you're back at the start). 

---

### Statement 12
A ticker timer with frequency 50 Hz produces dots every 0.05 seconds.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Time interval = 1 ÷ frequency = 1 ÷ 50 = 0.02 seconds between dots.*

## Short Answer Questions

### Question 1 (2 marks)
Define acceleration and state its unit.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity (1 mark)
- Unit: m/s² (1 mark)

---

### Question 2 (3 marks)
A car travels 300 m in 20 s. Calculate its average speed.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Formula: speed = distance ÷ time (1 mark)
- Substitution: speed = 300 ÷ 20 (1 mark)
- Answer: 15 m/s (1 mark)

---

### Question 3 (4 marks)
Explain what is meant by terminal velocity and why a skydiver reaches it.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Terminal velocity is the maximum (constant) velocity reached by a falling object (1 mark)
- As the skydiver falls, air resistance increases with speed (1 mark)
- Eventually air resistance equals weight (1 mark)
- Resultant force becomes zero, so acceleration stops (1 mark)

---

### Question 4 (3 marks)
A train accelerates from 10 m/s to 30 m/s in 5 seconds. Calculate its acceleration.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Formula: a = (v - u) ÷ t (1 mark)
- Substitution: a = (30 - 10) ÷ 5 (1 mark)
- Answer: 4 m/s² (1 mark)

---

### Question 5 (4 marks)
Describe how you would use a ticker timer to investigate the motion of a trolley rolling down a ramp.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Attach ticker tape to trolley and thread through ticker timer (1 mark)
- Release trolley and start timer (1 mark)
- Timer makes dots on tape at regular intervals (0.02 s for 50 Hz) (1 mark)
- Measure distances between dots to calculate speeds/acceleration (1 mark)

---

### Question 6 (3 marks)
Distinguish between distance and displacement.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Distance is the total path length travelled (scalar) (1 mark)
- Displacement is the straight-line distance from start to finish (1 mark)
- Displacement includes direction (vector) / can be positive, negative, or zero (1 mark)

State two factors that would increase the terminal velocity of a falling object.

**Mark Scheme:**

  - More streamlined shape (1 mark)

### Question 9 (3 marks)
A stone is dropped from a cliff. Calculate its velocity after falling for 2.5 seconds. (g = 10 m/s²)

**Mark Scheme:**
- Formula: v = u + at or v = gt (1 mark)
- Substitution: v = 0 + (10 × 2.5) (1 mark)
- Answer: 25 m/s (downwards) (1 mark)

---

### Question 10 (4 marks)
Explain why the acceleration of a skydiver decreases as she falls, before reaching terminal velocity.

**Mark Scheme:**
- As speed increases, air resistance increases (1 mark)
- Resultant force = weight - air resistance (1 mark)
- As air resistance increases, resultant force decreases (1 mark)
- Since F = ma, acceleration decreases as resultant force decreases (1 mark)

## Extended Answer Question (6 marks)

### Question 11 (6 marks)
A car accelerates uniformly from rest at 3 m/s² for 8 seconds, then travels at constant velocity for 12 seconds, before decelerating to rest in 6 seconds.

(a) Calculate the maximum velocity reached by the car. (2 marks)
(b) Sketch the velocity-time graph for this journey. (2 marks)
(c) Calculate the total distance travelled by the car. (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:**

**(a) Maximum velocity**
- v = u + at = 0 + (3 × 8) (1 mark)
- v = 24 m/s (1 mark)

**(b) Velocity-time graph**
- Correct shape: increasing straight line, horizontal, decreasing straight line (1 mark)
- Correct values: reaches 24 m/s at 8 s, constant until 20 s, zero at 26 s (1 mark)

**(c) Total distance**
- Area 1 (triangle): ½ × 8 × 24 = 96 m (0.5 mark)
- Area 2 (rectangle): 12 × 24 = 288 m (0.5 mark)
- Area 3 (triangle): ½ × 6 × 24 = 72 m (0.5 mark)
- Total: 96 + 288 + 72 = 456 m (0.5 mark)
