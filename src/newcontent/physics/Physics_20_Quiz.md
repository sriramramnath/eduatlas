## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
Which of the following materials is magnetic?

A) Copper  
B) Aluminum  
C) Iron  
D) Plastic

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Iron is a ferromagnetic material. Copper, aluminum, and plastic are not magnetic.

---

### Question 2
The direction of a magnetic field around a straight wire is found using:

A) Fleming's left-hand rule  
B) The right-hand grip rule  
C) Ohm's Law  
D) The motor effect

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The right-hand grip rule gives the direction of the magnetic field around a current-carrying wire.

---

### Question 3
Like magnetic poles:

A) Attract  
B) Repel  
C) Cancel out  
D) Have no effect on each other

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Like poles repel, opposite poles attract.

---

### Question 4
Which material is best for the core of an electromagnet?

A) Steel  
B) Soft iron  
C) Copper  
D) Aluminum

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Soft iron is easy to magnetize and demagnetize, making it ideal for electromagnets.

---

### Question 5
Fleming's left-hand rule is used for:

A) Finding the direction of a magnetic field  
B) Finding the direction of force on a current-carrying wire  
C) Finding the resistance of a wire  
D) Finding the voltage in a circuit

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Fleming's left-hand rule (First = Field, Second = Current, Thumb = Force) is used for the motor effect.

---

### Question 6
In an electric motor, the commutator:

A) Increases the current  
B) Reverses the current every half turn  
C) Measures the voltage  
D) Reduces friction

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The commutator reverses the current direction to keep the motor rotating in the same direction.

---

### Question 7
Which of the following increases the strength of an electromagnet?

A) Using fewer turns of wire  
B) Using a copper core  
C) Increasing the current  
D) Using a steel core

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: More current, more turns, and a soft iron core all increase electromagnet strength.

---

### Question 8
The Earth's magnetic field is similar to that of:

A) A straight wire  
B) A solenoid  
C) A bar magnet  
D) A coil with no core

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Earth behaves like a giant bar magnet with magnetic poles near the geographic poles.

---

### Question 9
A current-carrying wire experiences a force when placed:

A) In a vacuum  
B) In a magnetic field  
C) In water  
D) In any material

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The motor effect - a current-carrying wire in a magnetic field experiences a force.

---

### Question 10
Which of the following is an advantage of an electromagnet over a permanent magnet?

A) It is always magnetic  
B) It can be switched on and off  
C) It is stronger  
D) It is cheaper

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Electromagnets can be switched on/off, their strength varied, and pole direction changed.

---

### Question 11
The magnetic field lines around a bar magnet go from:

A) South to North  
B) North to South  
C) East to West  
D) Random directions

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Magnetic field lines go from North pole to South pole outside the magnet.

---

### Question 12
To increase the speed of a DC motor, you could:

A) Decrease the current  
B) Use weaker magnets  
C) Increase the current  
D) Reduce the number of turns

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: More current, stronger magnets, or more turns all increase motor speed.

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
Copper is a magnetic material.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Copper is not magnetic. Only iron, nickel, cobalt, and some alloys are magnetic.*

---

### Statement 2
Magnetic field lines go from North to South.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Outside a magnet, field lines go from North pole to South pole.

---

### Statement 3
Steel is better than soft iron for making electromagnets.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Soft iron is better for electromagnets because it magnetizes and demagnetizes easily. Steel retains magnetism.*

---

### Statement 4
A compass needle points toward the Earth's magnetic North Pole.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: A compass needle (which is a small magnet with North seeking pole) points toward Earth's magnetic North Pole.

---

### Statement 5
The force on a current-carrying wire in a magnetic field is greatest when the wire is parallel to the field.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: The force is greatest when the wire is perpendicular to the field. No force when parallel.*

---

### Statement 6
Increasing the number of turns on a coil increases the strength of an electromagnet.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: More turns means more magnetic field, making a stronger electromagnet.

---

### Statement 7
A solenoid with a current flowing through it behaves like a bar magnet.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: A current-carrying solenoid produces a magnetic field similar to a bar magnet.

---

### Statement 8
The motor effect is used in generators to produce electricity.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: The motor effect produces force/ motion from current. Generators use electromagnetic induction (the reverse process).*

---

### Statement 9
Hard magnetic materials are used for permanent magnets.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Hard magnetic materials (like steel) retain their magnetism, making them suitable for permanent magnets.

---

### Statement 10
Magnetic monopoles (single North or South poles) exist.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Magnetic poles always come in pairs. You cannot have a single North or South pole.*

---

### Statement 11
The right-hand grip rule is used to find the direction of force on a wire.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: The right-hand grip rule finds the direction of magnetic field around a wire. Fleming's left-hand rule finds force direction.*

---

### Statement 12
Loudspeakers use the motor effect to produce sound.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Loudspeakers use a varying current in a coil in a magnetic field to move a cone and produce sound.
