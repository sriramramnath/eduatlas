# Chapter 17: Energy Resources and Electricity Generation
## Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Physics - Concise Synopsis (Physics only)

---

## Renewable Energy Resources

### Solar Power
- PV cells convert light → electricity
- **Pros:** Free fuel, no pollution, low running cost
- **Cons:** Daylight only, weather dependent, high initial cost

### Wind Power
- Wind turns turbines → generators
- **Pros:** Renewable, no pollution, low running cost
- **Cons:** Weather dependent, visual/noise impact, land area

### Hydroelectric
- Flowing water turns turbines
- **Pros:** Reliable, no pollution, fast response
- **Cons:** High cost, floods land, limited sites

### Tidal Power
- Tides turn turbines
- **Pros:** Predictable, no pollution
- **Cons:** Limited sites, affects marine life

### Wave Power
- Wave motion drives generators
- **Pros:** Renewable, no pollution
- **Cons:** Weather dependent, high maintenance

### Geothermal
- Earth's heat drives turbines
- **Pros:** Reliable, no pollution, small area
- **Cons:** Very limited sites

### Biomass
- Burning organic material
- **Pros:** Renewable, uses waste
- **Cons:** Produces CO₂, needs land

---

## Non-Renewable Energy Resources

### Fossil Fuels (Coal, Oil, Gas)
- Burn fuel → heat → steam → turbine → generator
- **Pros:** Reliable, high energy, cheap
- **Cons:** CO₂, pollution, will run out

### Nuclear
- Fission → heat → steam → turbine → generator
- **Pros:** No CO₂, reliable, high energy
- **Cons:** Radioactive waste, accidents, high cost

---

## Comparison

| Feature | Renewable | Non-Renewable |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| Runs out? | No | Yes |
| CO₂ | Low/Zero | High |
| Reliable | Some weather | Yes |

## Electricity Generation

### Basic Process
```
Energy → Heat → Steam → Turbine → Generator → Electricity
```

### Transmission
- **Step-up transformer:** High voltage for transmission
- **Step-down transformer:** Lower voltage for use

**Why high voltage?**
- Lower current for same power
- Less energy lost as heat
- More efficient

---

## Environmental Issues

### Global Warming
- Fossil fuels → CO₂ → greenhouse effect → temperature rise

### Acid Rain
- Fossil fuels → SO₂, NOx → acid rain → damage

### Solutions
- Use renewable energy
- Reduce fossil fuel use
- Carbon capture

---

## Key Exam Points

1. **Renewable** - won't run out
2. **Non-renewable** - will run out
3. **CO₂ emissions** - fossil fuels produce CO₂
4. **Generation process** - heat → steam → turbine → generator
5. **High voltage transmission** - reduces losses
6. **Environmental impact** - know for each type
