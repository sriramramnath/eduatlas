# Chapter 17: Energy Resources and Electricity Generation
## Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Physics - Quiz Section (Physics only)

## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
Which of the following is a renewable energy resource?

A) Coal  
B) Natural gas  
C) Wind  
D) Nuclear fuel

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Wind is renewable - it won't run out. Coal, natural gas, and nuclear fuel are finite resources.

---

### Question 2
Which energy resource produces CO₂ when used to generate electricity?

A) Solar  
B) Wind  
C) Hydroelectric  
D) Coal

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Burning coal releases CO₂. Solar, wind, and hydroelectric do not produce CO₂ during operation.

---

### Question 3
In most power stations, what is the correct order of energy transfer?

A) Heat → kinetic → electrical  
B) Kinetic → heat → electrical  
C) Electrical → kinetic → heat  
D) Heat → electrical → kinetic

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: Heat produces steam, which drives turbines (kinetic), which drive generators (electrical).

---

### Question 4
Which renewable energy resource is most reliable (not weather dependent)?

A) Solar  
B) Wind  
C) Hydroelectric  
D) Wave

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Hydroelectric power can be controlled and is not dependent on weather like solar, wind, and wave.

---

### Question 5
Nuclear power stations produce electricity using:

A) Nuclear fusion  
B) Nuclear fission  
C) Burning uranium  
D) Solar energy

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Nuclear power stations use nuclear fission (splitting of atomic nuclei) to release heat.

---

### Question 6
Why is electricity transmitted at high voltage?

A) To make it safer  
B) To reduce energy loss in cables  
C) To increase the current  
D) To make it cheaper to produce

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Higher voltage means lower current for the same power, which reduces heat loss in transmission cables.

---

### Question 7
Which of the following is a disadvantage of wind power?

A) It produces CO₂  
B) It is weather dependent  
C) It is very expensive to build  
D) It produces radioactive waste

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Wind power requires wind to generate electricity, so it is weather dependent.

---

### Question 8
Burning fossil fuels contributes to global warming because:

A) It releases oxygen  
B) It releases CO₂  
C) It uses up water  
D) It produces acid rain

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Burning fossil fuels releases CO₂, a greenhouse gas that traps heat in the atmosphere.

---

### Question 9
Which energy resource uses the rise and fall of sea levels?

A) Wave  
B) Tidal  
C) Hydroelectric  
D) Geothermal

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Tidal power uses the rise and fall of tides (sea levels) to generate electricity.

---

### Question 10
Geothermal power uses:

A) Heat from the Sun  
B) Heat from the Earth's interior  
C) Heat from burning fuel  
D) Heat from nuclear reactions

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Geothermal power uses natural heat from within the Earth.

---

### Question 11
Which of the following produces radioactive waste?

A) Coal power station  
B) Wind farm  
C) Nuclear power station  
D) Solar farm

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Nuclear power stations produce radioactive waste that must be carefully stored and disposed of.

---

### Question 12
Biomass is considered renewable because:

A) It produces no CO₂  
B) Plants can be regrown  
C) It is very efficient  
D) It is cheap

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Biomass is renewable because the organic material (plants) can be regrown, replacing what is used.

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
Solar panels work by converting heat from the Sun into electricity.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Solar panels (photovoltaic cells) convert LIGHT from the Sun into electricity, not heat.*

---

### Statement 2
Nuclear power stations produce CO₂ during operation.

**Answer: False***

**Explanation**: Nuclear power stations do not produce CO₂ during operation (though some CO₂ is produced during construction and fuel processing).

---

### Statement 3
Hydroelectric power stations can respond quickly to changes in electricity demand.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Hydroelectric power can be increased or decreased quickly by controlling water flow through turbines.

---

### Statement 4
All renewable energy resources are weather dependent.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Some renewable resources like hydroelectric and geothermal are reliable and not weather dependent.*

---

### Statement 5
Fossil fuels are finite resources that will eventually run out.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Coal, oil, and natural gas are finite resources formed over millions of years and will eventually be depleted.

---

### Statement 6
Wave power is more predictable than tidal power.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Tidal power is more predictable because tides follow regular patterns, while waves depend on weather.*

---

### Statement 7
High voltage transmission reduces energy losses in power lines.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Higher voltage means lower current for the same power, which reduces heat loss (I²R losses) in cables.

---

### Statement 8
Acid rain is caused by releasing CO₂ into the atmosphere.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Acid rain is caused by releasing SO₂ and NOx, not CO₂. CO₂ causes global warming.*

---

### Statement 9
Wind turbines convert kinetic energy into electrical energy.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Wind (kinetic energy) turns turbines, which drive generators to produce electrical energy.

---

### Statement 10
Geothermal power can be used anywhere in the world.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Geothermal power is limited to areas with accessible underground heat (e.g., near tectonic plate boundaries).*

---

### Statement 11
Biomass is carbon neutral if the plants are replaced.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: If plants are regrown, they absorb the same amount of CO₂ that was released when the previous plants were burned.

---

### Statement 12
Nuclear fusion is used in current nuclear power stations.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Current nuclear power stations use nuclear fission (splitting nuclei), not fusion (joining nuclei).*

### Question 3 (4 marks)
Describe how a coal-fired power station generates electricity.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Coal is burned to release heat (1 mark)
- Heat boils water to produce steam (1 mark)
- Steam drives turbines (1 mark)
- Turbines drive generators to produce electricity (1 mark)

---

### Question 4 (3 marks)
Explain why wind power is considered renewable but unreliable.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Renewable: wind will not run out / is naturally replenished (1 mark)
- Unreliable: only generates when wind blows (1 mark)
- Wind speed varies / cannot guarantee output (1 mark)

---

### Question 5 (4 marks)
Compare nuclear power and coal power as methods of electricity generation.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Nuclear: no CO₂ during operation, produces radioactive waste (2 marks)
- Coal: produces CO₂ and other pollutants, no radioactive waste (2 marks)
- Both: reliable, use heat → steam → turbine → generator process

---
