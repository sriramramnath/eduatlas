## 9.1 Static Electricity

### Charging by Friction

When two insulating materials are rubbed together:
- Electrons are transferred from one material to the other
- One material gains electrons (becomes negatively charged)
- One material loses electrons (becomes positively charged)

### Common Charging Examples

| Materials Rubbed | Result |
|-----------------|--------|
| Polythene + cloth | Polythene negative, cloth positive |
| Perspex + cloth | Perspex positive, cloth negative |
| Ebonite + fur | Ebonite negative, fur positive |
| Comb + hair | Comb negative, hair positive |

### The Electron Transfer Model

- Electrons are negatively charged
- Protons are positively charged (fixed in nucleus)
- Only electrons can move between materials
- **Gaining electrons** в†’ negative charge
- **Losing electrons** в†’ positive charge

## 9.2 Electric Fields

### Definition

An **electric field** is a region where a charged object experiences a force.

### Representing Electric Fields

Electric fields are shown by **field lines**:
- Direction: from positive to negative
- Density: shows field strength

## 9.3 Forces Between Charges

### The Rule

| Combination | Force |
|-------------|-------|
| Positive + Positive | Repel |
| Negative + Negative | Repel |
| Positive + Negative | Attract |

**Like charges repel, opposite charges attract.**

### Induced Charge

A charged object can attract a neutral object by **induction**:
1. Charged object brought near neutral object
2. Charges in neutral object separate (polarization)
3. Opposite charges are closer в†’ attraction

## 9.4 Electrostatic Phenomena

### Everyday Examples

| Phenomenon | Explanation |
|------------|-------------|
| **Clothes sticking together** | Friction causes charge separation |
| **Hair standing up** | Like charges repel |
| **Sparks when touching door handle** | Charge builds up then discharges |
| **Lightning** | Huge discharge between clouds and ground |
| **Dust attracted to TV screen** | Charged screen attracts neutral dust |

### Lightning

1. Charge separation in storm clouds (negative at bottom)
2. Induces positive charge on ground
3. When potential difference is large enough в†’ discharge
4. Rapid heating of air causes shock wave (thunder)

## 9.5 Electrostatic Hazards and Safety

### Hazards

| Situation | Risk |
|-----------|------|
| **Fuel tankers** | Sparks can ignite fuel vapour |
| **Hospital operating theatres** | Sparks can ignite anaesthetic gases |
| **Electronic components** | Static can damage sensitive circuits |
| **Grain silos** | Sparks can cause explosions |

### Safety Measures

| Measure | How it Works |
|---------|--------------|
| **Earthing** | Provides path for charge to flow to ground |
| **Conducting straps** | On fuel tankers, prevent charge build-up |
| **Anti-static mats** | Conduct charge away from workers |
| **Humidity control** | Moist air conducts charge away |
| **Ionisers** | Neutralise static charges |

## 9.6 Electrostatic Applications

### Photocopiers

1. Drum charged uniformly
2. Light reflects off page, discharges areas corresponding to white parts
3. Toner (charged ink) attracted to charged (dark) areas
4. Toner transferred to paper
5. Paper heated to fix toner

### Inkjet Printers

1. Ink droplets given precise charge
2. Charged plates deflect droplets
3. Droplets hit exact position on paper

### Electrostatic Precipitators

1. Wires in chimney given high voltage
2. Dust particles become charged
3. Charged particles attracted to collecting plates
4. Clean gas released

### Spray Painting

1. Paint droplets charged
2. Object to be painted earthed (opposite charge)
3. Paint attracted to object
4. Even coating, less waste

## 9.7 Gold Leaf Electroscope

### Structure

- Metal cap connected to metal rod
- Thin gold leaf attached to rod
- All enclosed in glass case (for protection)

### Detecting Charge

| Charge on cap | Effect on leaf |
|---------------|----------------|
| Negative | Leaf rises (repelled by rod) |
| Positive | Leaf rises (repelled by rod) |
| Neutral | Leaf hangs down |

### Testing Materials

- Charged rod brought near cap
- If leaf rises в†’ material is conductor (charge flows)
- If no effect в†’ material is insulator

## 9.8 Van de Graaff Generator

### Principle

- Belt carries charge to metal dome
- Charge builds up on dome
- Can produce very high voltages (hundreds of thousands of volts)

### Demonstrations

| Demonstration | Explanation |
|---------------|-------------|
| Hair standing up | Like charges on hair repel |
| Sparks | Charge discharges to earth |
| Small fluorescent tubes lighting | High voltage causes excitation |
