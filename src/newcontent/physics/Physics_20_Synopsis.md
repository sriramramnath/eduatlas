# Chapter 20: Magnetism and Electromagnetism
## Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Physics - Concise Synopsis

---

## Magnetic Fields

### Properties
- Magnets have **North** and **South** poles
- Like poles **repel**
- Opposite poles **attract**

### Field Lines
- Go from **North to South**
- Closer lines = stronger field

---

## Magnetic Materials

### Ferromagnetic
- Iron, nickel, cobalt, steel

### Types

| Type | Properties | Example |
|------|------------|---------|
| **Soft** | Easy to magnetize/demagnetize | Iron |
| **Hard** | Hard to magnetize/demagnetize | Steel |

---

## Electromagnetism

### Current Produces Magnetic Field

### Right-Hand Grip Rule
- Thumb = current direction
- Fingers = field direction

### Solenoid (Coil)
- Like a bar magnet
- North at one end, South at other

---

## Electromagnets

### Features
- Coil with soft iron core
- Magnetic when current flows

### Advantages
- Can switch on/off
- Can vary strength
- Can change pole direction

### Factors Affecting Strength
- More turns → stronger
- More current → stronger
- Soft iron core → stronger

---

## Motor Effect

### Force on Current-Carrying Wire in Magnetic Field

### Fleming's Left-Hand Rule
- **F**irst finger = **F**ield (N→S)
- **S**econd finger = Current
- **Th**umb = **Th**rust (Force)

### Equation
```
F = B × I × L
```

---

## Electric Motor

### Parts
- Coil in magnetic field
- Commutator (reverses current)
- Brushes (make contact)

### How It Works
1. Current in coil → force → rotation
2. Commutator reverses current every half turn
3. Continuous rotation

### Increasing Speed
- More current
- Stronger magnets
- More coil turns

| Rule | Use |
