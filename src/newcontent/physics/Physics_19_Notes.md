## 19.1 States of Matter

### The Three States

| State | Shape | Volume | Particle Arrangement |
|-------|-------|--------|---------------------|
| **Solid** | Fixed | Fixed | Regular, close together, vibrate in fixed positions |
| **Liquid** | Takes shape of container | Fixed | Close together, can move past each other |
| **Gas** | Fills container | Fills container | Far apart, move freely in all directions |

### Particle Model

```

│ │ │ │ │                ╲│╱   ╱                   ●    ●
●─●─●─●─●                 ●  ●─●                       ●
│ │ │ │ │                ╱    ╲                    ●      ●
●─●─●─●─●               ●   ●  ●                         ●

Fixed pattern          Particles flow            Particles spread
Vibrate in place       Past each other           Out freely
```

## 19.2 Changes of State

### Processes

| Process | Change | Energy |
|---------|--------|--------|
| **Melting** | Solid → Liquid | Absorbed |
| **Freezing** | Liquid → Solid | Released |
| **Boiling/Evaporation** | Liquid → Gas | Absorbed |
| **Condensation** | Gas → Liquid | Released |
| **Sublimation** | Solid → Gas | Absorbed |
| **Deposition** | Gas → Solid | Released |

### Heating/Cooling Curve

```
Temperature
    │
100 ┤        ╭─────── Boiling (liquid + gas)
    │       ╱
    │      ╱
    │     ╱
  0 ┤────╱──────── Melting (solid + liquid)
    │   ╱
    │  ╱
    │ ╱
    │╱
    └──────────────────→ Time/Heat added
    
    Plateaus = change of state (temperature constant)
```

### During Changes of State

- **Temperature stays constant**
- Energy goes into breaking/forming bonds between particles
- Called **latent heat**

## 19.3 Gas Pressure

### Cause of Gas Pressure

Gas pressure is caused by gas particles colliding with the walls of their container.

### Factors Affecting Gas Pressure

| Factor | Effect on Pressure |
|--------|-------------------|
| **Temperature increase** | Pressure increases (particles move faster) |
| **Volume decrease** | Pressure increases (more frequent collisions) |
| **More particles** | Pressure increases (more collisions) |

### Boyle's Law (Constant Temperature)

```
pressure × volume = constant

p₁ × V₁ = p₂ × V₂

(At constant temperature)
```

### Pressure-Temperature Law (Constant Volume)

```
pressure ÷ temperature = constant

p₁/T₁ = p₂/T₂

(Temperature in Kelvin)
```

### Worked Example (Boyle's Law)

A gas at 100,000 Pa occupies 2 m³. What is the pressure when compressed to 0.5 m³ at constant temperature?

```
p₁ × V₁ = p₂ × V₂
100,000 × 2 = p₂ × 0.5
200,000 = p₂ × 0.5
p₂ = 400,000 Pa
```

## 19.4 Brownian Motion

### Observation

- Smoke particles in air move randomly
- This is evidence for the kinetic theory of matter

### Explanation

- Smoke particles are bombarded by invisible air molecules
- Air molecules move randomly
- This causes the smoke particles to move randomly

### Conclusion

Brownian motion provides evidence that:
- Matter is made of particles
- Particles are in constant random motion

## 19.5 Evaporation and Boiling

### Differences

| Feature | Evaporation | Boiling |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Temperature** | Any temperature | At boiling point only |
| **Location** | At surface only | Throughout liquid |
| **Bubbles** | No bubbles | Bubbles form |
| **Rate** | Slow | Rapid |

### Factors Affecting Evaporation Rate

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Higher temperature** | Faster evaporation |
| **Larger surface area** | Faster evaporation |
| **Higher air flow** | Faster evaporation |
| **Lower humidity** | Faster evaporation |

### Cooling by Evaporation

- Fastest particles escape from surface
- Average kinetic energy of remaining particles decreases
- Temperature of liquid decreases
- This is why sweating cools you down

## 19.6 Absolute Zero

### Definition

**Absolute zero** (0 K or -273°C) is the temperature at which particles have minimum kinetic energy.

### Kelvin Scale

| °C | K |
|----|---|
| -273 | 0 (absolute zero) |
| 0 | 273 |
| 100 | 373 |

### Conversion

```
K = °C + 273
°C = K - 273
```

## Common Student Errors

| Error | Correction |
|-------|------------|
| Thinking temperature changes during state change | Temperature is constant |
| Confusing evaporation and boiling | Evaporation at surface, any temp; boiling throughout, at boiling point |
| Forgetting Kelvin conversion | K = °C + 273 |
| Thinking gas pressure is just from weight | Gas pressure from particle collisions |
| Forgetting cooling effect of evaporation | Evaporation causes cooling |
