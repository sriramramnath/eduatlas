## 11.1 Properties of Electromagnetic Waves

### Common Properties

All electromagnetic waves:
1. Are **transverse** waves
2. Can travel through a **vacuum**
3. Travel at **3 × 10⁸ m/s** in a vacuum
4. Transfer **energy**
5. Obey the **wave equation** (v = fλ)
6. Can be **reflected, refracted, diffracted**

### Speed in Different Media

| Medium | Speed (approx) |
|--------|----------------|
| Vacuum | 3 × 10⁸ m/s |
| Air | Slightly less than 3 × 10⁸ m/s |
| Water | 2.25 × 10⁸ m/s |
| Glass | 2 × 10⁸ m/s |

## 11.2 The Electromagnetic Spectrum

### Order by Wavelength and Frequency

```
Long λ ──────────────────────────────────────→ Short λ
Low f  ──────────────────────────────────────→ High f
Low energy ───────────────────────────────────→ High energy

Radio → Micro → Infrared → Visible → UV → X-rays → Gamma
        waves                              rays      rays
```

### Detailed Table

| Type | Wavelength | Frequency | Energy | Uses |
|------|------------|-----------|--------|------|
| **Radio** | > 1 m | < 300 MHz | Lowest | Communication, TV, radio |
| **Microwaves** | 1 mm - 1 m | 300 MHz - 300 GHz | Low | Mobile phones, cooking, radar, satellites |
| **Infrared** | 700 nm - 1 mm | 300 GHz - 430 THz | Low-Med | Heating, remote controls, thermal imaging, fibre optics |
| **Visible** | 400-700 nm | 430-750 THz | Medium | Vision, photography, optical fibres |
| **Ultraviolet** | 10-400 nm | 750 THz - 30 PHz | High | Sun tanning, sterilization, security marking |
| **X-rays** | 0.01-10 nm | 30 PHz - 30 EHz | Very high | Medical imaging, security scanning, crystallography |
| **Gamma rays** | < 0.01 nm | > 30 EHz | Highest | Cancer treatment, sterilization, food preservation |

### Visible Light Spectrum

```
Red (700 nm) → Orange → Yellow → Green → Blue → Indigo → Violet (400 nm)
```

- **Red** has the longest wavelength in visible light
- **Violet** has the shortest wavelength in visible light

## 11.3 Radio Waves

### Properties
- Longest wavelength in EM spectrum
- Can diffract around obstacles
- Can travel long distances

### Uses

| Use | Explanation |
|-----|-------------|
| **Radio/TV broadcasting** | Carry signals over long distances |
| **Mobile phone communication** | Transmit voice and data |
| **Radar** | Detect objects using reflection |
| **Satellite communication** | Global communication links |

### AM and FM

| Type | Full Name | Characteristics |
|------|-----------|-----------------|
| **AM** | Amplitude Modulation | Varies amplitude, affected by interference |
| **FM** | Frequency Modulation | Varies frequency, better quality |

---

## 11.4 Microwaves

### Properties
- Shorter wavelength than radio waves
- Can penetrate atmosphere
- Absorbed by water molecules

### Uses

| Use | Explanation |
|-----|-------------|
| **Mobile phones** | Communication |
| **Microwave ovens** | Water molecules absorb microwaves and heat up |
| **Satellite communication** | Pass through atmosphere |
| **Radar** | Reflection from objects |

### Microwave Ovens

- Microwaves at 2.45 GHz are absorbed by water molecules
- Water molecules vibrate faster → heating
- Metal reflects microwaves (don't put metal in!)
- Plastic/glass are transparent to microwaves

---

## 11.5 Infrared Radiation

### Properties
- Emitted by all objects (thermal radiation)
- Hotter objects emit more infrared
- Can be felt as heat

### Uses

| Use | Explanation |
|-----|-------------|
| **Thermal imaging** | Detect heat patterns |
| **Remote controls** | Send signals to devices |
| **Heating** | Electric heaters, grills |
| **Fibre optic communication** | Carry data as infrared pulses |
| **Night vision equipment** | Detect infrared emitted by warm objects |

### Detecting Infrared

- **Thermometers** (non-contact)
- **Infrared cameras**
- **Photographic film** (special)
- **Skin** (we can feel it as heat)

---

## 11.6 Visible Light

### Properties
- Only part of EM spectrum visible to human eye
- Detected by eyes and photographic film

### Uses

| Use | Explanation |
|-----|-------------|
| **Vision** | Humans and animals see using visible light |
| **Photography** | Captures images |
| **Optical fibres** | Carry data as light pulses |
| **Photosynthesis** | Plants convert light to chemical energy |

### Colours and Wavelengths

| Colour | Wavelength (nm) |
|--------|-----------------|
| Red | 620-700 |
| Orange | 590-620 |
| Yellow | 570-590 |
| Green | 495-570 |
| Blue | 450-495 |
| Violet | 400-450 |

---

## 11.7 Ultraviolet Radiation

### Properties
- Higher energy than visible light
- Can cause chemical reactions
- Can damage living cells

### Uses

| Use | Explanation |
|-----|-------------|
| **Sun tanning** | Stimulates melanin production |
| **Sterilization** | Kills bacteria and viruses |
| **Security marking** | Invisible ink glows under UV |
| **Fluorescent lamps** | UV makes phosphor coating glow |

### Hazards

| Hazard | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Skin damage** | Sunburn, premature aging |
| **Eye damage** | Cataracts, corneal damage |
| **Skin cancer** | Excessive exposure increases risk |

### Protection

- Sunscreen (absorbs UV)
- Sunglasses (block UV)
- Stay in shade
- Limit exposure time

---

## 11.8 X-rays

### Properties
- High energy
- Can pass through soft tissue
- Absorbed by bones and metal
- Can damage living cells

### Uses

| Use | Explanation |
|-----|-------------|
| **Medical imaging** | Pass through flesh, absorbed by bones |
| **Airport security** | Reveal objects inside luggage |
| **Industrial inspection** | Check for cracks in metal |
| **Crystallography** | Study atomic structure |

### Medical X-rays

- **Radiographs** (standard X-rays): 2D images
- **CT scans**: Multiple X-rays create 3D images
- **Contrast agents**: Improve visibility of soft tissues

### Hazards

- Ionizing radiation
- Can damage DNA
- Increased cancer risk

### Protection

- **Lead aprons** absorb X-rays
- **Minimize exposure time**
- **Maximize distance** from source
- **Shielding** with lead or concrete

---

## 11.9 Gamma Rays

### Properties
- Highest energy EM radiation
- Most penetrating
- Produced by radioactive decay

### Uses

| Use | Explanation |
|-----|-------------|
| **Cancer treatment (radiotherapy)** | Kill cancer cells |
| **Sterilization** | Kill bacteria on medical equipment |
| **Food preservation** | Kill bacteria and pests |
| **Medical tracers** | Diagnose diseases |
| **Industrial inspection** | Detect cracks and flaws |

### Hazards

- Highly ionizing
- Can cause severe cell damage
- High cancer risk

### Protection

- **Thick lead shielding**
- **Concrete barriers**
- **Minimize exposure time**
- **Maximize distance**
- **Never touch source directly**

---

## 11.10 Ionizing Radiation

### What is Ionization?

Ionization is the process of removing electrons from atoms, creating ions.

### Ionizing EM Radiation

| Type | Ionizing Ability |
|------|------------------|
| Ultraviolet | Weak (can ionize some molecules) |
| X-rays | Strong |
| Gamma rays | Very strong |

### Effects of Ionization

- Can damage DNA
- Can cause mutations
- Can kill cells
- Can cause cancer

## Exam Tips for Chapter 11

1. **Know the order** - Radio, Micro, IR, Visible, UV, X, Gamma
2. **Common properties** - All transverse, travel at 3 × 10⁸ m/s in vacuum
3. **Uses and hazards** - Know key uses and dangers of each type
4. **Ionizing radiation** - UV, X-rays, Gamma rays
5. **Protection methods** - Know how to protect from harmful radiation
6. **Wavelength trends** - Radio (long) to Gamma (short)

## Common Student Errors

| Error | Correction |
|-------|------------|
| Wrong spectrum order | Learn: Radio, Micro, IR, Visible, UV, X, Gamma |
| Thinking all EM waves are harmful | Only high frequency (UV, X, Gamma) are harmful |
| Confusing uses | Learn specific uses for each type |
| Forgetting speed in vacuum | All travel at 3 × 10⁸ m/s |
| Thinking sound is EM | Sound is mechanical, not EM |
