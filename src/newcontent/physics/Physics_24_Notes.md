## 24.1 Medical Applications

### Tracers

**Principle:**
- Radioactive isotope attached to chemical
- Chemical absorbed by target organ
- Radiation detected outside body

**Requirements for tracers:**
| Property | Reason |
|----------|--------|
| Gamma emitter | Can exit body for detection |
| Short half-life | Minimizes patient dose |
| Non-toxic | Safe for patient |

**Common tracer: Technetium-99m**
- Half-life: 6 hours
- Gamma emitter
- Used for bone, heart, kidney scans

**Process:**
```
1. Inject tracer into patient
2. Tracer accumulates in target organ
3. Gamma camera detects radiation
4. Computer creates image
```

### Radiotherapy (Cancer Treatment)

**Principle:**
- High doses of radiation kill cancer cells
- Cancer cells more susceptible to radiation damage
- Healthy cells can repair damage

**Methods:**

**External Beam Therapy:**
- Gamma rays from cobalt-60
- Precisely targeted at tumor
- Rotating source minimizes healthy tissue damage

**Internal Radiotherapy (Brachytherapy):**
- Radioactive source placed inside body
- Near or inside tumor
- Short-range radiation (beta or low-energy gamma)

**Requirements for radiotherapy:**
| Property | Reason |
|----------|--------|
| High activity | Kill cancer cells |
| Gamma or beta | Penetrates to tumor |
| Controlled dose | Minimize healthy tissue damage |

## 24.2 Industrial Applications

### Thickness Gauging

**Principle:**
- Radiation passes through material
- Amount transmitted depends on thickness
- Detector measures transmitted radiation

```
Source ──► ┌─────────────┐ ──► Detector
           │   Material  │
           │   (sheet)   │
           └─────────────┘
```

**Radiation choice:**
| Material | Radiation |
|----------|-----------|
| Thin paper/plastic | Beta |
| Thick metal | Gamma |

**Advantages:**
- Non-contact measurement
- Real-time monitoring
- Automatic control possible

### Tracers in Industry

**Applications:**
1. **Pipeline leak detection** - Add tracer, detect where it emerges
2. **Flow rate measurement** - Time between injection and detection
3. **Mixing efficiency** - Check uniform distribution
4. **Sediment movement** - Track in rivers/harbors

### Sterilization

**Principle:**
- Gamma radiation kills bacteria and viruses
- Used for medical equipment
- Used for food preservation

**Advantages:**
- Cold process (no heat damage)
- Penetrates packaging
- No chemical residues

## 24.3 Carbon Dating

### Principle

- Living organisms absorb carbon-14 from atmosphere
- Ratio of C-14 to C-12 stays constant while alive
- After death, C-14 decays (half-life 5730 years)
- Measure remaining C-14 to estimate age

### Calculation

```
Age = -8033 × ln(N/N₀)

Where:
N = current C-14 activity
N₀ = original C-14 activity (from living sample)
```

**Example:**
If ancient wood has 25% of the C-14 activity of living wood:
- 100% → 50% → 25% = 2 half-lives
- Age = 2 × 5730 = 11,460 years

### Limitations

| Limitation | Explanation |
|------------|-------------|
| Limited range | Only effective to ~50,000 years |
| Contamination | Modern carbon affects accuracy |
| Assumption | Constant C-14 production rate |
| Sample size | Need sufficient carbon |

## 24.4 Radioactive Dating of Rocks

### Principle

- Measure ratio of parent to daughter isotopes
- Long half-life isotopes used for ancient rocks

**Uranium-Lead Dating:**
```
²³⁸U → ²⁰⁶Pb (half-life = 4.5 billion years)
```

**Potassium-Argon Dating:**
```
⁴⁰K → ⁴⁰Ar (half-life = 1.3 billion years)
```

### Calculation

```
Age = (t½ / ln 2) × ln(1 + D/P)

Where:
D = number of daughter atoms
P = number of parent atoms
```

## 24.5 Smoke Detectors

### Ionization Smoke Detectors

**Components:**
- Americium-241 source (alpha emitter)
- Ionization chamber
- Battery
- Alarm circuit

**How it works:**
```
1. Alpha particles ionize air in chamber
2. Ions allow small current to flow
3. Smoke particles attach to ions
4. Current decreases
5. Alarm triggered
```

**Why alpha?**
- High ionization
- Short range (safe in detector)
- Long half-life (458 years)

## 24.6 Safety Considerations

### Radiation Hazards

| Effect | Cause |
|--------|-------|
| Ionization | Damages DNA and cells |
| Burns | High doses damage tissue |
| Cancer | Mutations in cells |
| Genetic damage | Mutations in reproductive cells |

### Safety Measures

**ALARA Principle:** As Low As Reasonably Achievable

**Time:** Minimize exposure time
**Distance:** Maximize distance from source
**Shielding:** Use appropriate barriers

| Radiation | Shielding |
|-----------|-----------|
| Alpha | Paper (not needed) |
| Beta | Aluminum, plastic |
| Gamma | Lead, concrete |

### Monitoring

- Film badges for workers
- Regular health checks
- Environmental monitoring
