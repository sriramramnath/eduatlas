## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
What is the unit of frequency?

A) Metre  
B) Second  
C) Hertz  
D) Watt

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Frequency is measured in hertz (Hz), which means cycles per second.*

---

### Question 2
The wave equation is:

A) v = f + λ  
B) v = f × λ  
C) v = f ÷ λ  
D) v = λ ÷ f

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Wave speed = frequency × wavelength.*

---

### Question 3
In a transverse wave, particles vibrate:

A) Parallel to the direction of travel  
B) Perpendicular to the direction of travel  
C) In circles  
D) Not at all

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: In transverse waves, particle vibration is perpendicular to wave direction.*

---

### Question 4
Sound waves are:

A) Transverse  
B) Longitudinal  
C) Electromagnetic  
D) Stationary

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Sound waves are longitudinal - particles vibrate parallel to the direction of travel.*

---

### Question 5

A) 3 × 10⁶ m/s  
B) 3 × 10⁷ m/s  
C) 3 × 10⁸ m/s  
D) 3 × 10⁹ m/s

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: All electromagnetic waves travel at 3 × 10⁸ m/s in a vacuum.*

### Question 6
A wave has frequency 10 Hz and wavelength 0.5 m. What is its speed?

A) 5 m/s  
B) 10 m/s  
C) 20 m/s

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: v = f × λ = 10 × 0.5 = 5 m/s.*

The amplitude of a wave is:

A) The distance between two crests

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
All electromagnetic waves are transverse.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: All electromagnetic waves (radio, light, X-rays, etc.) are transverse waves.*

---

### Statement 2
Sound can travel through a vacuum.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Sound is a mechanical wave and needs a medium to travel. It cannot travel through a vacuum.*

---

### Statement 3
Wavelength is the distance between two consecutive crests.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Wavelength is the distance between any two consecutive corresponding points on a wave.*

---

### Statement 4
The period of a wave is the number of waves per second.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: The period is the time for one complete wave. Frequency is the number of waves per second.*

---

### Statement 5
In a longitudinal wave, particles vibrate perpendicular to the direction of travel.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: In longitudinal waves, particles vibrate parallel to the direction of travel. In transverse waves, they vibrate perpendicular.*

---

### Statement 6
Radio waves have longer wavelengths than X-rays.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Radio waves have the longest wavelengths in the EM spectrum; gamma rays have the shortest.*

---

### Statement 7
The loudness of a sound is determined by its frequency.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Loudness is determined by amplitude. Pitch is determined by frequency.*

---

### Statement 8
Diffraction is the spreading of waves when they pass through a gap.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Diffraction occurs when waves encounter obstacles or pass through gaps, causing them to spread out.*

---

### Statement 9
All electromagnetic waves travel at the same speed in a vacuum.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: All EM waves travel at 3 × 10⁸ m/s in a vacuum, regardless of frequency or wavelength.*

---

### Statement 10
The speed of sound is faster in air than in water.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Sound travels faster in denser media. Speed in water (~1500 m/s) is faster than in air (~340 m/s).*

---

### Statement 11
Visible light has wavelengths between 400-700 nm.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Visible light ranges from about 700 nm (red) to 400 nm (violet).*

---

### Statement 12
Reflection is when waves change direction entering a new medium.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Reflection is when waves bounce off a surface. Refraction is when waves change direction entering a new medium.*

### Question 2 (3 marks)
A wave has frequency 20 Hz and wavelength 0.25 m. Calculate the wave speed.

**Mark Scheme:***

Distinguish between transverse and longitudinal waves.

**Mark Scheme:***

- Longitudinal: particles vibrate parallel to direction of travel (1 mark)
- Transverse example: light/water waves (1 mark)
- Longitudinal example: sound waves (1 mark)

### Question 4 (3 marks)
A sound wave has frequency 500 Hz and speed 340 m/s. Calculate its wavelength.

- Formula: λ = v ÷ f (1 mark)
- Substitution: λ = 340 ÷ 500 (1 mark)
- Answer: 0.68 m (1 mark)

## Extended Answer Question (6 marks)

### Question
A student is investigating waves on a string.

(a) The student observes that the wave has frequency 5 Hz and wavelength 0.4 m. Calculate the speed of the wave. (2 marks)
(b) The student doubles the frequency while keeping the wavelength constant. State and explain what happens to the wave speed. (2 marks)
(c) Explain why the wave on the string is a transverse wave. (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:***

**(a) Wave speed calculation***
- v = f × λ = 5 × 0.4 (1 mark)
- v = 2 m/s (1 mark)

**(b) Effect of doubling frequency***
- Wave speed doubles (1 mark)
- Because v = fλ, and λ is constant, so v ∝ f (1 mark)

**(c) Transverse wave explanation***
- Particles of the string vibrate perpendicular to the direction of wave travel (1 mark)
- This is the definition of a transverse wave (1 mark)
