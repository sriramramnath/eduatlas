## 14.1 Forms of Energy

### Types of Energy

| Energy Type | Description | Example |
|-------------|-------------|---------|
| **Kinetic** | Energy of movement | Moving car, running person |
| **Gravitational Potential** | Energy due to height | Object raised above ground |
| **Elastic Potential** | Energy stored in stretched/compressed springs | Wound-up clock, stretched elastic |
| **Chemical** | Energy stored in chemical bonds | Food, fuel, batteries |
| **Nuclear** | Energy stored in atomic nuclei | Nuclear fuel, Sun |
| **Thermal (Heat)** | Energy due to temperature | Hot water, warm room |
| **Electrical** | Energy of moving charges | Current in a circuit |
| **Light** | Energy carried by electromagnetic waves | Sunlight, lamp light |
| **Sound** | Energy carried by sound waves | Music, speech |

## 14.2 Conservation of Energy

### Principle

**Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred from one form to another.**

```
Total energy before = Total energy after
```

### Energy Transfers

| Situation | Energy Transfer |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Object falling | Gravitational potential → Kinetic |
| Car braking | Kinetic → Thermal (brakes heat up) |
| Battery powering lamp | Chemical → Electrical → Light + Thermal |
| Stretching a spring | Kinetic → Elastic potential |
| Burning fuel | Chemical → Thermal + Light |

## 14.3 Kinetic Energy

### Definition

**Kinetic energy** is the energy an object has due to its motion.

### Equation

```
kinetic energy = ½ × mass × velocity²

KE = ½ × m × v²

Where:
KE = kinetic energy (J)
m = mass (kg)
v = velocity (m/s)
```

### Worked Examples

**Example 1:** A 500 kg car travels at 20 m/s. Calculate its kinetic energy.
```
KE = ½ × 500 × 20²
KE = ½ × 500 × 400
KE = 100,000 J = 100 kJ
```

**Example 2:** An object has 2000 J of kinetic energy and mass 4 kg. Calculate its velocity.
```
2000 = ½ × 4 × v²
2000 = 2 × v²
v² = 1000
v = 31.6 m/s
```

### Key Points

- KE depends on velocity squared (doubling speed = 4× KE)
- Stationary objects have zero KE
- KE is always positive

## 14.4 Gravitational Potential Energy

### Definition

**Gravitational potential energy (GPE)** is the energy an object has due to its position in a gravitational field (height).

### Equation

```
gravitational potential energy = mass × g × height

GPE = m × g × h

Where:
GPE = gravitational potential energy (J)
m = mass (kg)
g = gravitational field strength (10 N/kg)
h = height (m)
```

### Worked Examples

**Example 1:** A 2 kg book is on a shelf 1.5 m high. Calculate its GPE.
```
GPE = 2 × 10 × 1.5
GPE = 30 J
```

**Example 2:** An object has 500 J of GPE at a height of 10 m. Calculate its mass.
```
500 = m × 10 × 10
500 = 100 × m
m = 5 kg
```

### Key Points

- GPE increases with height
- GPE is relative to a reference level (usually ground)
- When object falls, GPE converts to KE

## 14.5 Energy Transfer: Falling Objects

### Energy Conservation

For a falling object (ignoring air resistance):
```
Loss in GPE = Gain in KE
m × g × h = ½ × m × v²
```

### Worked Example

A 1 kg ball is dropped from 20 m. Calculate its speed just before hitting the ground.

```
Loss in GPE = Gain in KE
m × g × h = ½ × m × v²

1 × 10 × 20 = ½ × 1 × v²
200 = 0.5 × v²
v² = 400
v = 20 m/s
```

### With Air Resistance

In reality, some energy is transferred to thermal energy (heating the air):
```
Loss in GPE = Gain in KE + Energy transferred to surroundings
```

## 14.6 Efficiency

### Definition

**Efficiency** is the proportion of input energy that is usefully transferred.

### Equation

```
efficiency = useful energy output ÷ total energy input

Or:

efficiency = useful power output ÷ total power input
```

### Percentage Efficiency

```
Percentage efficiency = efficiency × 100%
```

### Worked Example

A motor uses 500 J of electrical energy and produces 350 J of useful kinetic energy. Calculate its efficiency.

```
Efficiency = 350 ÷ 500 = 0.7
Percentage efficiency = 0.7 × 100% = 70%
```

### Improving Efficiency

| Method | How it Works |
|--------|--------------|
| **Lubrication** | Reduces friction |
| **Insulation** | Reduces heat loss |
| **Streamlining** | Reduces air resistance |
| **Using efficient designs** | Better energy transfer |

## 14.7 Energy Resources

### Renewable Resources

| Resource | How Energy is Obtained |
|----------|------------------------|
| **Solar** | Solar panels convert light to electricity |
| **Wind** | Wind turbines convert kinetic energy to electricity |
| **Hydroelectric** | Flowing water turns turbines |
| **Tidal** | Rising and falling tides turn turbines |
| **Wave** | Wave motion drives generators |
| **Geothermal** | Heat from Earth's interior |
| **Biomass** | Burning organic material |

### Non-Renewable Resources

| Resource | How Energy is Obtained |
|----------|------------------------|
| **Coal** | Burning releases thermal energy |
| **Oil** | Burning releases thermal energy |
| **Natural gas** | Burning releases thermal energy |
| **Nuclear** | Nuclear fission releases energy |

### Comparison

| Feature | Renewable | Non-Renewable |
|---------|-----------|---------------|
| **Will run out?** | No | Yes |
| **CO₂ emissions** | Low/zero | High |
| **Reliability** | Weather dependent | Reliable |
| **Initial cost** | Often high | Varies |
| **Running cost** | Low | Fuel costs |

## Exam Tips for Chapter 14

1. **Know the energy types** - kinetic, potential, thermal, etc.
2. **Conservation of energy** - total energy stays constant
3. **KE equation** - KE = ½mv², v is squared
4. **GPE equation** - GPE = mgh
5. **Efficiency** - always < 1 (or < 100%)
6. **Falling objects** - GPE loss = KE gain (without air resistance)
7. **Renewable vs non-renewable** - know the differences

## Common Student Errors

| Error | Correction |
|-------|------------|
| Forgetting v² in KE | KE = ½mv², not ½mv |
| Using wrong units | Mass in kg, velocity in m/s, height in m |
| Efficiency > 100% | Efficiency is always less than 100% |
| Confusing renewable definitions | Renewable won't run out |
| Forgetting g = 10 N/kg | Use g = 10 unless told otherwise |
