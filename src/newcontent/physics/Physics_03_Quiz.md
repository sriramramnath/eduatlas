## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
Newton's First Law states that an object will:

A) Accelerate when a force acts on it  
B) Remain at rest or constant velocity unless acted on by a resultant force  
C) Experience equal and opposite forces  
D) Move in a circular path

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Newton's First Law (Law of Inertia) describes what happens when resultant force is zero.*

---

### Question 2
A 500 kg car accelerates at 4 m/s². What is the resultant force?

A) 125 N  
B) 504 N  
C) 2000 N  
D) 8000 N

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: F = m × a = 500 × 4 = 2000 N.*

---

### Question 3
According to Newton's Third Law, action and reaction forces:

A) Cancel each other out  
B) Act on the same object  
C) Are equal in magnitude and opposite in direction  
D) Are always contact forces

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Newton's Third Law states that forces are equal and opposite, but act on different objects.*

---

### Question 4
When a car doubles its speed, its braking distance:

A) Doubles  
B) Triples  
C) Quadruples  
D) Stays the same

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Braking distance is proportional to speed squared (v²). Double speed = 4× braking distance.*

---

### Question 5
A skydiver reaches terminal velocity when:

A) Air resistance is zero  
B) Weight equals air resistance  
C) The parachute opens  
D) Acceleration is maximum

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: At terminal velocity, forces are balanced (resultant force = 0), so acceleration = 0.*

---

### Question 6
What is the unit of force?

A) Kilogram  
B) Metre per second  
C) Newton  
D) Joule

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Force is measured in newtons (N), named after Isaac Newton.*

---

### Question 7
A 60 kg person is in a lift accelerating upward at 2 m/s². What is the resultant force on the person?

A) 30 N upward  
B) 120 N upward  
C) 600 N upward  
D) 720 N upward

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: F = m × a = 60 × 2 = 120 N in direction of acceleration (upward).*

---

### Question 8
Which factor does NOT affect thinking distance?

A) Speed of vehicle  
B) Condition of brakes  
C) Driver's tiredness  
D) Driver's reaction time

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Brake condition affects braking distance, not thinking distance. Thinking distance depends on speed and reaction time.*

---

### Question 9
When you walk forward, your foot pushes backward on the ground. The ground:

A) Exerts no force on you  
B) Pushes you forward with an equal force  
C) Pushes you backward  
D) Exerts a smaller force on you

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Newton's Third Law - ground pushes forward on you with equal and opposite force.*

---

### Question 10
A car of mass 1000 kg has a driving force of 3000 N and experiences 1000 N of air resistance. What is its acceleration?

A) 1 m/s²  
B) 2 m/s²  
C) 3 m/s²  
D) 4 m/s²

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Resultant force = 3000 - 1000 = 2000 N. a = F/m = 2000/1000 = 2 m/s².*

---

### Question 11
Inertia is:

A) A force that opposes motion  
B) The tendency to resist changes in motion  
C) The same as momentum  
D) A type of energy

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Inertia is the resistance of an object to changes in its state of motion. Greater mass = greater inertia.*

---

### Question 12
Crumple zones in cars work by:

A) Making the car stronger  
B) Increasing the stopping time of collision  
C) Reducing the car's mass  
D) Preventing collisions

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: F = m(v-u)/t. Increasing stopping time (t) reduces force (F) on passengers.*

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
According to Newton's First Law, a moving object will eventually come to rest.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Newton's First Law states a moving object continues at constant velocity unless acted on by a resultant force. It doesn't slow down on its own.*

---

### Statement 2
If the resultant force on an object is zero, it must be stationary.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: If resultant force is zero, the object is either stationary OR moving at constant velocity.*

---

### Statement 3
Action and reaction forces cancel each other out.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Action and reaction forces act on different objects, so they cannot cancel each other out.*

---

### Statement 4
Acceleration is directly proportional to resultant force.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Newton's Second Law: F = ma, so a = F/m. At constant mass, a ∝ F.*

---

### Statement 5
A more massive object has more inertia than a less massive object.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Inertia depends on mass. More mass = harder to change motion = more inertia.*

---

### Statement 6
Wet roads decrease thinking distance.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Wet roads increase braking distance (less friction). Thinking distance is unaffected by road conditions.*

---

### Statement 7
When a rocket launches, the rocket pushes exhaust gases downward and the gases push the rocket upward.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: This is Newton's Third Law in action - equal and opposite forces.*

---

### Statement 8
Seat belts work by reducing the driver's mass during a collision.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Seat belts work by increasing stopping time and preventing the driver from hitting the windscreen. Mass cannot be changed.*

---

### Statement 9
At terminal velocity, a falling object has zero acceleration.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: At terminal velocity, weight = air resistance, so resultant force = 0, and acceleration = 0.*

---

### Statement 10
The braking distance of a car is directly proportional to its speed.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Braking distance is proportional to speed squared (v²), not directly proportional to speed.*

---

### Statement 11
A 2000 kg truck has more inertia than a 500 kg car.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Inertia depends on mass. The truck has 4× the mass, so it has 4× the inertia.*

---

### Statement 12
When you push against a wall, the wall pushes back with a smaller force.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: According to Newton's Third Law, the wall pushes back with an equal force. You don't move because other forces (friction with ground) act on you.*

## Short Answer Questions

### Question 1 (2 marks)
State Newton's Second Law of Motion.

**Mark Scheme:***
- The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the resultant force (1 mark)
- And inversely proportional to its mass / F = ma (1 mark)

---

### Question 2 (3 marks)
A car of mass 800 kg accelerates from rest to 20 m/s in 10 seconds. Calculate:
(a) The acceleration of the car (1 mark)
(b) The resultant force acting on the car (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:***
(a) a = (v-u)/t = (20-0)/10 = 2 m/s² (1 mark)
(b) F = m × a = 800 × 2 (1 mark)
    F = 1600 N (1 mark)

---

### Question 3 (4 marks)
Explain, using Newton's Third Law, how a swimmer moves forward through the water.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Swimmer pushes water backward with hands/feet (1 mark)
- Water pushes swimmer forward with equal force (1 mark)
- These are action-reaction forces (1 mark)
- Forces are equal and opposite but act on different objects (1 mark)

---

### Question 4 (3 marks)
State three factors that increase the braking distance of a vehicle.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Any three from:
  - Higher speed (1 mark)
  - Greater mass (1 mark)
  - Worn brakes/tyres (1 mark)
  - Wet/icy road surface (1 mark)
  - Poor tyre pressure (1 mark)

---

### Question 5 (4 marks)
Explain why a driver who has been drinking alcohol has an increased stopping distance.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Alcohol increases reaction time (1 mark)
- Thinking distance = speed × reaction time (1 mark)
- Increased reaction time means increased thinking distance (1 mark)
- Braking distance is unchanged, but total stopping distance increases (1 mark)

---

### Question 6 (3 marks)
A 50 kg crate is pulled across a floor with a 200 N force. Friction is 150 N.
(a) Calculate the resultant force. (1 mark)
(b) Calculate the acceleration. (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:***
(a) Resultant force = 200 - 150 = 50 N (1 mark)
(b) F = ma, so a = F/m = 50/50 (1 mark)
    a = 1 m/s² (1 mark)

---

### Question 7 (4 marks)
Explain, in terms of forces, why a skydiver reaches a terminal velocity.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Initially, weight > air resistance, so acceleration downward (1 mark)
- As speed increases, air resistance increases (1 mark)
- Eventually, air resistance = weight (1 mark)
- Resultant force = 0, so acceleration = 0 (constant velocity) (1 mark)

---

### Question 8 (2 marks)
State the difference between thinking distance and braking distance.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Thinking distance: distance travelled during driver's reaction time (1 mark)
- Braking distance: distance travelled while brakes are applied (1 mark)

---

### Question 9 (3 marks)
Explain how airbags reduce injury during a car collision.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Airbag increases stopping time for passenger (1 mark)
- F = m(v-u)/t, so increased time means decreased force (1 mark)
- Force is also spread over larger area, reducing pressure (1 mark)

---

### Question 10 (4 marks)
A rocket of mass 5000 kg is launched vertically upward. The engines provide an upward thrust of 80000 N.
(a) Calculate the weight of the rocket. (2 marks)
(b) Calculate the initial acceleration. (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:***
(a) W = m × g = 5000 × 10 = 50000 N (2 marks)
(b) Resultant force = 80000 - 50000 = 30000 N upward (1 mark)
    a = F/m = 30000/5000 = 6 m/s² (1 mark)

## Extended Answer Question (6 marks)

### Question
A car of mass 1200 kg is travelling at 30 m/s on a dry road.

(a) Calculate the thinking distance if the driver's reaction time is 0.4 s. (2 marks)
(b) The braking distance at 30 m/s is 14 m. Calculate the total stopping distance. (1 mark)
(c) Explain why the braking distance would be longer on a wet road. (2 marks)
(d) The driver doubles his speed to 60 m/s. State the new braking distance. (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme:***

**(a) Thinking distance***
- Formula: thinking distance = speed × reaction time (1 mark)
- Thinking distance = 30 × 0.4 = 12 m (1 mark)

**(b) Total stopping distance***
- Stopping distance = 12 + 14 = 26 m (1 mark)

**(c) Wet road explanation***
- Wet road reduces friction between tyres and road (1 mark)
- Less friction means less braking force / tyres can't grip as well (1 mark)
- So car travels further while slowing down (1 mark)
(Any 2 points)

**(d) New braking distance at 60 m/s***
- Braking distance ∝ v², so double speed = 4× braking distance (1 mark)
- New braking distance = 14 × 4 = 56 m (1 mark)
