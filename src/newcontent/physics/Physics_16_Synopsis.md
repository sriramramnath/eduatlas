# Chapter 16: Work and Power
## Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Physics - Concise Synopsis

---

## Essential Equations

```
Work done:     W = F × d
Power:         P = W ÷ t
Power:         P = E ÷ t
Power:         P = F × v
```

---

## Work

### Definition
Work is done when a force causes movement.

### Conditions
1. Force must act
2. Object must move in direction of force

### Equation
```
W = F × d
```

- **W** in joules (J)
- **F** in newtons (N)
- **d** in metres (m)

### No Work When
- No movement
- Force perpendicular to movement

---

## Power

### Definition
Rate of doing work.

### Equations
```
P = W ÷ t
P = E ÷ t
```

- **P** in watts (W)
- **W** or **E** in joules (J)
- **t** in seconds (s)

### Alternative
```
P = F × v
```

- **F** in newtons (N)
- **v** in m/s

---

## Work and Energy

Work done = Energy transferred

| Situation | Energy Transfer |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Lifting | GPE gained |
| Accelerating | KE gained |
| Stretching spring | Elastic PE |
| Against friction | Thermal |

---

## Work Against Gravity

```
W = m × g × h
```

This equals GPE gained.

### Example
```
Lift 10 kg by 2 m:
W = 10 × 10 × 2 = 200 J
```

---

## Quick Calculations

### Work
```
F = 50 N, d = 8 m
W = 50 × 8 = 400 J
```

### Power
```
W = 1200 J, t = 20 s
P = 1200 ÷ 20 = 60 W
```

### Power (F × v)
```
F = 500 N, v = 20 m/s
P = 500 × 20 = 10,000 W = 10 kW
```

---

## Key Exam Points

1. **Work needs movement**
2. **Force and distance parallel**
3. **Power = rate of work**
4. **Units: J for work, W for power**
5. **P = Fv for vehicles**
