## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
What is the unit of momentum?

A) kg/m/s  
B) kg m/s  
C) N/m  
D) J/s

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Momentum = mass × velocity, so units = kg × m/s = kg m/s (also equal to Ns).*

---

### Question 2
A 1000 kg car travels at 20 m/s. What is its momentum?

A) 50 kg m/s  
B) 500 kg m/s  
C) 20000 kg m/s  
D) 200000 kg m/s

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: p = m × v = 1000 × 20 = 20000 kg m/s.*

---

### Question 3
In a closed system, total momentum is:

A) Always zero  
B) Always increasing  
C) Conserved  
D) Always decreasing

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The principle of conservation of momentum states that total momentum before equals total momentum after in a closed system.*

---

### Question 4
A 2 kg trolley moving at 3 m/s collides with a stationary 1 kg trolley. They stick together. What is their velocity?

A) 1 m/s  
B) 2 m/s  
C) 3 m/s  
D) 6 m/s

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: (2×3) + (1×0) = (2+1)×v → 6 = 3v → v = 2 m/s.*

---

### Question 5
Which equation correctly relates force to momentum?

A) F = p × t  
B) F = p ÷ t  
C) F = p + t  
D) F = p - t

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Force = change in momentum ÷ time taken, or F = Δp ÷ t.*

---

### Question 6
In an elastic collision:

A) Momentum is not conserved  
B) Kinetic energy is conserved  
C) Objects stick together  
D) Total momentum is zero

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: In elastic collisions, both momentum and kinetic energy are conserved. Objects bounce apart.*

---

### Question 7
A 0.05 kg bullet is fired at 400 m/s from a 4 kg gun. What is the gun's recoil velocity?

A) 0.5 m/s  
B) 5 m/s  
C) 20 m/s  
D) 80 m/s

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: 0 = (4×v) + (0.05×400) → 4v = -20 → v = -5 m/s (5 m/s in opposite direction).*

---

### Question 8
A force of 100 N acts for 0.5 s. What is the impulse?

A) 50 Ns  
B) 200 Ns  
C) 50 N  
D) 200 N

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: Impulse = F × t = 100 × 0.5 = 50 Ns (also equals change in momentum).*

---

### Question 9
A ball of mass 0.2 kg hits a wall at 10 m/s and rebounds at 8 m/s. What is the change in momentum?

A) 0.4 kg m/s  
B) 1.6 kg m/s  
C) 3.6 kg m/s  
D) 36 kg m/s

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Δp = mv - mu = (0.2×-8) - (0.2×10) = -1.6 - 2 = -3.6 kg m/s. Magnitude = 3.6 kg m/s.*

---

### Question 10
Crumple zones in cars reduce injury by:

A) Reducing the change in momentum  
B) Increasing the collision time  
C) Reducing the car's mass  
D) Increasing the impact force

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: F = Δp ÷ t. Increasing time reduces force for same momentum change.*

---

### Question 11
Two objects collide elastically. Which statement is true?

A) They stick together  
B) Kinetic energy is lost  
C) Both momentum and kinetic energy are conserved  
D) Total momentum is zero

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Elastic collisions conserve both momentum and kinetic energy. Inelastic collisions only conserve momentum.*

---

### Question 12
A 1500 kg car moving at 10 m/s collides with a stationary 1000 kg car. They lock together. What is their velocity?

A) 4 m/s  
B) 6 m/s  
C) 10 m/s  
D) 15 m/s

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: (1500×10) + (1000×0) = (1500+1000)×v → 15000 = 2500v → v = 6 m/s.*

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
Momentum is a scalar quantity.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Momentum is a vector quantity - it has both magnitude and direction (same direction as velocity).*

---

### Statement 2
In an inelastic collision, kinetic energy is conserved.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: In inelastic collisions, kinetic energy is not conserved (some is converted to heat/sound). Only momentum is conserved.*

---

### Statement 3
The total momentum of a system is always conserved.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Momentum is conserved only in a closed system with no external forces acting.*

---

### Statement 4
Impulse equals force multiplied by time.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Impulse = F × t = change in momentum.*

---

### Statement 5
Air bags reduce the force on passengers by increasing the stopping time.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: F = Δp ÷ t. For the same momentum change, increasing time decreases force.*

---

### Statement 6
When a gun fires a bullet, the momentum of the gun equals the momentum of the bullet.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Total momentum before = 0, so after firing, momentum of gun + momentum of bullet = 0. They are equal and opposite.*

---

### Statement 7
In an explosion, total momentum before equals total momentum after.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Conservation of momentum applies to explosions. Usually total momentum before = 0, so pieces move in opposite directions.*

---

### Statement 8
A heavier object always has more momentum than a lighter object.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Momentum depends on both mass AND velocity. A light object moving fast can have more momentum than a heavy object moving slowly.*

---

### Statement 9
The unit Ns is equivalent to kg m/s.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Ns = (kg m/s²) × s = kg m/s. Both are correct units for momentum.*

---

### Statement 10
In a collision between two objects, the force on object A is greater than the force on object B.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: By Newton's Third Law, the forces are equal and opposite. FA = -FB.*

---

### Statement 11
Seat belts work by reducing the change in momentum of passengers.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Seat belts don't change the momentum change (that's determined by initial speed). They increase stopping time, which reduces force.*

---

### Statement 12
If two objects collide and stick together, the collision is elastic.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: When objects stick together, the collision is inelastic. Elastic collisions involve objects bouncing apart.*

## Short Answer Questions

### Question 1 (2 marks)
Define momentum and state its unit.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Momentum = mass × velocity (1 mark)
- Unit: kg m/s or Ns (1 mark)

---

### Question 2 (3 marks)
A 500 kg car travels at 30 m/s. Calculate its momentum.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Formula: p = m × v (1 mark)
- Substitution: p = 500 × 30 (1 mark)
- Answer: 15000 kg m/s (1 mark)

---

### Question 3 (4 marks)
A 3 kg ball moving at 4 m/s collides with a stationary 2 kg ball. After the collision, the 3 kg ball moves at 1 m/s in the same direction. Calculate the velocity of the 2 kg ball.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Before: (3×4) + (2×0) = 12 kg m/s (1 mark)
- After: (3×1) + (2×v) = 3 + 2v (1 mark)
- Conservation: 12 = 3 + 2v (1 mark)
- v = 4.5 m/s (1 mark)

---

### Question 4 (4 marks)
Explain how crumple zones reduce injury to passengers during a collision.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Crumple zones collapse/compact during collision (1 mark)
- This increases the time taken to stop (1 mark)
- F = Δp ÷ t, so increased time means decreased force (1 mark)
- Reduced force means less injury to passengers (1 mark)

---

### Question 5 (3 marks)
A 0.1 kg ball hits a wall at 12 m/s and rebounds at 8 m/s. The collision lasts 0.02 s. Calculate the average force on the ball.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Change in momentum = (0.1×-8) - (0.1×12) = -2 kg m/s (1 mark)
- F = Δp ÷ t = 2 ÷ 0.02 (1 mark)
- F = 100 N (1 mark)

---

### Question 6 (3 marks)
Distinguish between an elastic and an inelastic collision.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Elastic: objects bounce apart, kinetic energy conserved (1 mark)
- Inelastic: objects stick together (1 mark)
- In inelastic collision, kinetic energy is not conserved (converted to heat/sound) (1 mark)

---

### Question 7 (4 marks)
A 5 kg cannon fires a 0.02 kg cannonball at 250 m/s. Calculate the recoil velocity of the cannon.

**Mark Scheme:***
- Total momentum before = 0 (1 mark)
- After: (5×v) + (0.02×250) = 0 (1 mark)
- 5v + 5 = 0 (1 mark)
- v = -1 m/s (1 m/s in opposite direction) (1 mark)

---

### Question 8 (2 marks)
State the principle of conservation of momentum.

**Mark Scheme:***
- In a closed system with no external forces (1 mark)
- Total momentum before collision = total momentum after collision (1 mark)

---

### Question 9 (3 marks)
Explain why momentum is conserved in a collision between two objects.

**Mark Scheme:***
- During collision, forces on the two objects are equal and opposite (Newton's Third Law) (1 mark)
- Forces act for the same time (1 mark)
- Therefore changes in momentum are equal and opposite, so total momentum unchanged (1 mark)

---

### Question 10 (4 marks)
A 2000 kg truck moving at 15 m/s collides with a 1000 kg stationary car. They lock together and move as one.
(a) Calculate their velocity immediately after the collision. (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:***

    30000 = 3000v, so v = 10 m/s (1 mark)

    Objects stick together / kinetic energy is not conserved (1 mark)

## Extended Answer Question (6 marks)

### Question
A 0.5 kg trolley moving at 3 m/s collides with a 1.5 kg trolley moving at 1 m/s in the opposite direction. After the collision, the 0.5 kg trolley rebounds at 1 m/s.

(a) Calculate the total momentum before the collision. (2 marks)
(b) Calculate the velocity of the 1.5 kg trolley after the collision. (3 marks)
(c) State whether the collision is elastic or inelastic, giving a reason. (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme:***

**(a) Total momentum before***
- Taking initial direction of 0.5 kg trolley as positive:
- p = (0.5×3) + (1.5×-1) = 1.5 - 1.5 = 0 kg m/s (2 marks)

**(b) Velocity after collision***
- Total momentum after = 0 (conservation) (1 mark)
- After: (0.5×-1) + (1.5×v) = 0 (1 mark)
- -0.5 + 1.5v = 0, so v = 0.33 m/s (in original direction) (1 mark)

**(c) Type of collision***
- KE before = ½(0.5)(3)² + ½(1.5)(1)² = 2.25 + 0.75 = 3 J
- KE after = ½(0.5)(1)² + ½(1.5)(0.33)² = 0.25 + 0.08 = 0.33 J
- KE not conserved, so inelastic (1 mark)
- Or: objects don't stick together but KE not conserved, so inelastic (1 mark)
