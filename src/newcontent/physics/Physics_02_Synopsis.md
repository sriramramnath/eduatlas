# Chapter 2: Forces and Shape
## Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Physics - Concise Synopsis

---

## Essential Equations

```
Weight:           W = m × g
Hooke's Law:      F = k × e
Energy stored:    E = ½ × F × e  or  E = ½ × k × e²
```

---

## Mass vs Weight

| Mass | Weight |
|------|--------|
| Amount of matter (kg) | Force of gravity (N) |
| Scalar | Vector |
| Constant everywhere | Changes with g |
| Measured with balance | Measured with newton meter |

**g values:**
- Earth: 10 N/kg
- Moon: 1.6 N/kg
- Jupiter: 25 N/kg

---

## Types of Forces

### Contact Forces
- **Friction** - opposes motion
- **Air resistance** - opposes motion through air
- **Tension** - pulling force in ropes
- **Normal contact force** - perpendicular to surface
- **Upthrust** - upward force in fluids

### Non-Contact Forces
- **Gravitational (weight)**
- **Magnetic**
- **Electrostatic**

---

## Hooke's Law

```
F = k × e
```

- **F** = force (N)
- **k** = spring constant (N/m) - measures stiffness
- **e** = extension (m) - NOT total length

### Key Points

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Valid range | Up to limit of proportionality |
| Graph shape | Straight line through origin |
| Gradient | = spring constant k |
| Beyond limit | Permanent deformation |

---

## Force-Extension Graph

```
Force
  │      ╱ (non-linear)
  │     ╱
  │    ● (limit of proportionality)
  │   ╱
  │  ╱ (linear - Hooke's Law)
  │ ╱
  │╱
  └──────────→ Extension
```

---

## Energy in Springs

```
E = ½ × k × e²
```

- Area under force-extension graph = work done = energy stored
- Unit: joules (J)

---

## Resultant Forces

| Situation | Calculation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Same direction | Add: F₁ + F₂ |
| Opposite directions | Subtract: F₁ - F₂ |
| Perpendicular | Pythagoras: √(F₁² + F₂²) |

### Balanced vs Unbalanced

| Resultant | Motion |
|-----------|--------|
| Zero | Stationary or constant velocity |
| Non-zero | Acceleration |

---

## Friction

### Types
- **Static** - on stationary objects
- **Dynamic** - on moving objects
- **Rolling** - on rolling objects

### Factors Affecting Friction
- Surface roughness (↑ roughness = ↑ friction)
- Normal force (↑ force = ↑ friction)
- Lubrication (reduces friction)

### Reducing Friction
- Lubricants (oil, grease)
- Ball bearings
- Streamlining
- Smooth surfaces

---

## Elastic vs Inelastic Deformation

| Elastic | Inelastic (Plastic) |
|---------|---------------------|
| Returns to original shape | Permanent change |
| Within limit of proportionality | Beyond limit |
| Energy stored can be recovered | Energy dissipated as heat |

---

## Quick Calculation Guide

### Finding Weight
```
Mass = 5 kg, g = 10 N/kg
Weight = 5 × 10 = 50 N
```

### Finding Spring Constant
```
Force = 8 N, extension = 0.04 m
k = 8 ÷ 0.04 = 200 N/m
```

### Finding Energy Stored
```
k = 100 N/m, e = 0.05 m
E = ½ × 100 × (0.05)² = 0.125 J
```

---

## Key Exam Points

1. **Extension** = final length - original length
2. **Hooke's Law** only valid up to limit of proportionality
3. **Higher k** = stiffer spring
4. **Weight** changes with location; **mass** doesn't
5. **Resultant force** considers direction (vectors)
6. **Area under F-e graph** = energy stored
