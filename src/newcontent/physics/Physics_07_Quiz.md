## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
In a series circuit, the current is:

A) Different at different points  
B) The same at all points  
C) Zero  
D) Shared between components

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: In a series circuit, there is only one path, so current is the same everywhere.

---

### Question 2
In a parallel circuit, the voltage across each branch is:

A) Different for each branch  
B) The same for all branches  
C) Zero  
D) Shared between branches

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: In parallel circuits, all branches are connected across the same two points, so voltage is the same.

---

### Question 3
Three resistors (2 Ω, 3 Ω, 5 Ω) are connected in series. What is the total resistance?

A) 0.97 Ω  
B) 3 Ω  
C) 7.5 Ω  
D) 10 Ω

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: In series, R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ = 2 + 3 + 5 = 10 Ω.

---

### Question 4
Two resistors (4 Ω and 12 Ω) are connected in parallel. What is the total resistance?

A) 3 Ω  
B) 4 Ω  
C) 8 Ω  
D) 16 Ω

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: 1/R = 1/4 + 1/12 = 3/12 + 1/12 = 4/12 = 1/3, so R = 3 Ω.

---

### Question 5
An ammeter should be connected:

A) In parallel with the component  
B) In series with the component  
C) Anywhere in the circuit  
D) Only across the battery

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Ammeters measure current and must be connected in series so all current flows through them.

---

### Question 6
A voltmeter should be connected:

A) In series with the component  
B) In parallel across the component  
C) Anywhere in the circuit  
D) Only across the battery

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Voltmeters measure potential difference and must be connected in parallel across the component.

---

### Question 7
In a potential divider circuit with R₁ = 2 kΩ and R₂ = 4 kΩ, and Vin = 12 V, what is Vout?

A) 4 V  
B) 6 V  
C) 8 V  
D) 12 V

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Vout = Vin × R₂/(R₁+R₂) = 12 × 4/(2+4) = 12 × 4/6 = 8 V.

---

### Question 8
An LDR has:

A) High resistance in bright light  
B) Low resistance in bright light  
C) Constant resistance regardless of light  
D) High resistance at all times

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Light-Dependent Resistors have low resistance in bright light and high resistance in darkness.

---

### Question 9
A thermistor (NTC) has:

A) High resistance when hot  
B) Low resistance when hot  
C) Constant resistance regardless of temperature  
D) High resistance at all temperatures

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Negative Temperature Coefficient (NTC) thermistors have lower resistance at higher temperatures.

---

### Question 10
Two identical resistors (R) are connected in parallel. The total resistance is:

A) 2R  
B) R  
C) R/2  
D) R²

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: For n identical resistors in parallel, R_total = R/n. With 2 resistors, R_total = R/2.

---

### Question 11
In a parallel circuit, if one branch is disconnected:

A) All branches stop working  
B) Only that branch stops working  
C) The current increases in other branches  
D) The voltage increases

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A major advantage of parallel circuits is that components operate independently.

---

### Question 12
The total resistance of resistors in parallel is:

A) Greater than the largest resistor  
B) Less than the smallest resistor  
C) The average of all resistors  
D) Equal to the sum of all resistors

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Parallel resistances provide multiple paths, so total resistance is always less than the smallest individual resistance.

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
In a series circuit, the voltage is the same across all components.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: In a series circuit, voltage is shared between components. Current is the same everywhere.*

---

### Statement 2
In a parallel circuit, the current is the same through all branches.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: In a parallel circuit, current splits between branches. Voltage is the same across all branches.*

---

### Statement 3
Adding more resistors in series increases the total resistance.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: R_total = R₁ + R₂ + R₃ + ... Each added resistor increases total resistance.

---

### Statement 4
Adding more resistors in parallel decreases the total resistance.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Parallel connections provide more paths for current, reducing overall resistance.

---

### Statement 5
An ammeter should have very high resistance.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: An ammeter should have very low resistance so it doesn't significantly affect the circuit current.*

---

### Statement 6
A voltmeter should have very high resistance.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: A voltmeter needs high resistance so it draws minimal current and doesn't affect the circuit.

---

### Statement 7
In a potential divider, the output voltage is always less than the input voltage.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Vout = Vin × R₂/(R₁+R₂). Since R₂/(R₁+R₂) is always less than 1, Vout < Vin.

---

### Statement 8
Christmas tree lights are usually connected in parallel.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Christmas tree lights are typically connected in series (older types) so they all share the same current. If one fails, all go out.*

---

### Statement 9
Household appliances are connected in parallel.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Household appliances are connected in parallel so they all receive the same voltage (230 V) and work independently.

---

### Statement 10
The resistance of an LDR increases when light intensity increases.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: LDR resistance decreases when light intensity increases (more light = lower resistance).*

---

### Statement 11
For two resistors in parallel, R_total = (R₁ × R₂)/(R₁ + R₂).

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: This is the correct formula for two resistors in parallel, derived from 1/R = 1/R₁ + 1/R₂.

---

### Statement 12
In a series circuit, the component with the largest resistance has the largest voltage across it.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: V = I × R. With the same current through all components, the largest R has the largest V.

### Question 2 (3 marks)
Three resistors (4 Ω, 6 Ω, and 8 Ω) are connected in series to a 9 V battery. Calculate the current in the circuit.

**Mark Scheme:**

- I = V/R = 9/18 (1 mark)
- I = 0.5 A (1 mark)

### Question 3 (4 marks)
Two resistors (10 Ω and 15 Ω) are connected in parallel to a 12 V battery.
(a) Calculate the total resistance. (2 marks)
(b) Calculate the total current from the battery. (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:**
(a) 1/R = 1/10 + 1/15 = 3/30 + 2/30 = 5/30 = 1/6 (1 mark)
    R = 6 Ω (1 mark)
(b) I = V/R = 12/6 (1 mark)
    I = 2 A (1 mark)

(a) Calculate the total resistance of the circuit. (2 marks)

## Extended Answer Question (6 marks)

### Question 11 (6 marks)
The circuit diagram shows three resistors connected to a 12 V battery.
*(Assume: R₁ = 4 Ω and R₂ = 6 Ω in parallel, then in series with R₃ = 3 Ω)**

(a) Calculate the total resistance of the parallel combination of R₁ and R₂. (2 marks)
(b) Calculate the total resistance of the circuit. (1 mark)
(c) Calculate the current drawn from the battery. (1 mark)
(d) Calculate the voltage across R₃. (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:**

**(a) Parallel combination**
- 1/R = 1/4 + 1/6 = 3/12 + 2/12 = 5/12 (1 mark)
- R = 12/5 = 2.4 Ω (1 mark)

**(b) Total resistance**
- R_total = 2.4 + 3 = 5.4 Ω (1 mark)

**(c) Current from battery**
- I = V/R = 12/5.4 (1 mark)
- I = 2.22 A (accept 2.2 A) (1 mark)

**(d) Voltage across R₃**
- V = I × R = 2.22 × 3 (1 mark)
- V = 6.67 V (accept 6.6-6.7 V) (1 mark)
- Or using ratio: V₃ = 12 × 3/(2.4+3) = 12 × 3/5.4 = 6.67 V
