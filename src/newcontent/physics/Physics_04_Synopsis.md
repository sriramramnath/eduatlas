## Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Physics - Concise Synopsis (Physics only)

## Essential Equations

```
Momentum:              p = m × v
Conservation:          m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = m₁v₁ + m₂v₂
Force and momentum:    F = Δp ÷ t  or  F × t = Δp
```

---

## Momentum Basics

| Property | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Definition** | Mass × velocity |
| **Unit** | kg m/s or Ns |
| **Type** | Vector (has direction) |
| **Symbol** | p |

### Example Calculations

```
Mass = 5 kg, velocity = 4 m/s
p = 5 × 4 = 20 kg m/s
```

---

## Conservation of Momentum

> **Total momentum before = Total momentum after**
> (provided no external forces act)

### Collision Types

| Type | Description | Kinetic Energy |
|------|-------------|----------------|
| **Elastic** | Objects bounce apart | Conserved |
| **Inelastic** | Objects stick together | Not conserved |

### Problem Solving Approach

1. Write "Before" momentum for each object
2. Write "After" momentum for each object
3. Set total before = total after
4. Solve for unknown

### Inelastic Collision (stick together)

```
m₁u₁ + m₂u₂ = (m₁ + m₂) × v

Example: 3 kg at 4 m/s hits 2 kg at rest
3 × 4 + 2 × 0 = (3 + 2) × v
12 = 5v
v = 2.4 m/s
```

### Explosion/Recoil

```
Total momentum before = 0
So: m₁v₁ + m₂v₂ = 0

Example: Gun (2 kg) fires bullet (0.01 kg) at 500 m/s
2 × v + 0.01 × 500 = 0
2v = -5
v = -2.5 m/s (recoil)
```

---

## Force and Momentum

```
F = Δp ÷ t = (mv - mu) ÷ t

or

Impulse: F × t = Δp
```

### Safety Application

| Feature | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Crumple zones | Increase t → Decrease F |
| Air bags | Increase t → Decrease F |
| Seat belts | Increase t → Decrease F |

**Key idea:** For same change in momentum, increasing time reduces force.

## Quick Calculation Guide

### Finding Momentum
```
m = 800 kg, v = 15 m/s
p = 800 × 15 = 12000 kg m/s
```

### Finding Force from Momentum Change
```
Δp = 500 kg m/s, t = 0.1 s
F = 500 ÷ 0.1 = 5000 N
```

### Collision Problem
```
Before: 4 kg at 5 m/s + 1 kg at rest
After: 4 kg at 2 m/s + 1 kg at v

(4×5) + (1×0) = (4×2) + (1×v)
20 = 8 + v
v = 12 m/s
```

---

## Key Points to Remember

1. **Momentum is a vector** - use + and - for directions
2. **Conservation applies** to closed systems (no external forces)
3. **Inelastic collisions** - objects stick together
4. **Elastic collisions** - objects bounce, KE conserved
5. **Impulse** = F × t = change in momentum
6. **Safety features** work by increasing impact time
