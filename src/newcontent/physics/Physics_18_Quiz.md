## Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
What is the unit of density?

A) kg/m²  
B) kg/m³  
C) N/m²  
D) N/m³

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Density = mass/volume, so units are kg/m³ (or g/cm³).

---

### Question 2
A block has mass 240 g and volume 80 cm³. What is its density?

A) 0.33 g/cm³  
B) 3 g/cm³  
C) 30 g/cm³  
D) 300 g/cm³

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: ρ = m/V = 240/80 = 3 g/cm³.

---

### Question 3
What is the unit of pressure?

A) Newton  
B) Pascal  
C) Joule  
D) Watt

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Pressure is measured in pascals (Pa), where 1 Pa = 1 N/m².

---

### Question 4
A force of 400 N acts on an area of 2 m². What is the pressure?

A) 100 Pa  
B) 200 Pa  
C) 800 Pa  
D) 1600 Pa

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: p = F/A = 400/2 = 200 Pa.

---

### Question 5
The pressure at a depth in a liquid depends on:

A) The shape of the container  
B) The depth and density of the liquid  
C) The volume of the liquid  
D) The colour of the liquid

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: p = ρgh, so pressure depends on density (ρ), g, and depth (h).

---

### Question 6
Calculate the pressure at 5 m depth in water (ρ = 1000 kg/m³, g = 10 N/kg).

A) 500 Pa  
B) 5000 Pa  
C) 50,000 Pa  
D) 500,000 Pa

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: p = ρgh = 1000 × 10 × 5 = 50,000 Pa.

---

### Question 7
An object will float in water if its density is:

A) Greater than water  
B) Equal to water  
C) Less than water  
D) The same as air

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Objects less dense than water float; more dense objects sink.

---

### Question 8
In a hydraulic system, the pressure is:

A) Greater on the large piston  
B) Greater on the small piston  
C) The same throughout  
D) Zero

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Pressure is transmitted equally throughout a liquid (Pascal's principle).

---

### Question 9
A hydraulic jack has a small piston of area 0.02 m² and a large piston of area 0.4 m². If 50 N is applied to the small piston, what force is exerted on the large piston?

A) 2.5 N  
B) 25 N  
C) 100 N  
D) 1000 N

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: F₂ = F₁ × (A₂/A₁) = 50 × (0.4/0.02) = 50 × 20 = 1000 N.

---

### Question 10
Atmospheric pressure at sea level is approximately:

A) 10 Pa  
B) 1000 Pa  
C) 10,000 Pa  
D) 100,000 Pa

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Atmospheric pressure at sea level is about 101,000 Pa (101 kPa).

---

### Question 11
Ice floats on water because:

A) Ice is warmer than water  
B) Ice is less dense than water  
C) Ice is more dense than water  
D) Ice is heavier than water

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Ice (920 kg/m³) is less dense than water (1000 kg/m³), so it floats.

---

### Question 12
As altitude increases, atmospheric pressure:

A) Increases  
B) Stays the same  
C) Decreases  
D) Becomes zero

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: There is less air above at higher altitudes, so atmospheric pressure decreases.

## True or False Statements

### Statement 1
Density is defined as mass divided by volume.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: ρ = m/V is the definition of density.

---

### Statement 2
Pressure in a liquid depends on the shape of the container.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Liquid pressure depends only on depth and density (p = ρgh), not on container shape.*

---

### Statement 3
1 g/cm³ is equal to 1000 kg/m³.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: 1 g/cm³ = 1000 kg/m³ (conversion factor). 

---

### Statement 4
In a hydraulic system, the force is the same on both pistons.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Pressure is the same throughout, but force = pressure × area, so the larger piston has greater force.*

---

### Statement 5
An object with density 800 kg/m³ will sink in water.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: An object with density 800 kg/m³ is LESS dense than water (1000 kg/m³), so it will FLOAT.*

---

### Statement 6
Pressure acts equally in all directions in a liquid.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: At any point in a liquid, pressure acts equally in all directions.

---

### Statement 7
The pressure at the bottom of a 10 m deep swimming pool is greater than at the bottom of a 10 m deep lake.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Pressure depends only on depth and density (p = ρgh), not on the amount of water or container shape.*

---

### Statement 8
Hydraulic systems can multiply force.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Hydraulic systems use different piston areas to multiply force (F₂ = F₁ × A₂/A₁).

---

### Statement 9
A pascal is equal to one newton per square metre.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: 1 Pa = 1 N/m² by definition.

---

### Statement 10
The density of a substance changes with its shape.

**Answer: False***

**Correction**: Density is a property of the material and does not change with shape (for the same material at same temperature).*

---

### Statement 11
Atmospheric pressure is caused by the weight of air above us.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: Atmospheric pressure is due to the weight of the column of air above a point.

---

### Statement 12
Pressure increases with depth in a liquid because there is more liquid above pushing down.

**Answer: True***

**Explanation**: The weight of the liquid above increases with depth, increasing pressure.

### Question 3 (4 marks)
Explain why an object floats in water.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Object is less dense than water (1 mark)
- Weight of object = weight of water displaced (1 mark)
- Upthrust equals weight of object (1 mark)
- Object remains at surface (1 mark)

### Question 4 (3 marks)
Calculate the pressure exerted by a 600 N person standing on an area of 0.03 m².

**Mark Scheme:**
- Formula: p = F/A (1 mark)
- p = 600/0.03 (1 mark)
- p = 20,000 Pa (1 mark)

---

### Question 5 (4 marks)
Explain how a hydraulic car jack works.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Small force applied to small piston (1 mark)
- Pressure transmitted equally through liquid (1 mark)
- Large piston has larger area (1 mark)
- Larger force produced on large piston (1 mark)

---

### Question 6 (3 marks)
Calculate the pressure at 8 m depth in seawater (ρ = 1030 kg/m³, g = 10 N/kg).

**Mark Scheme:**
- Formula: p = ρgh (1 mark)
- p = 1030 × 10 × 8 (1 mark)
- p = 82,400 Pa (1 mark)

---

Describe an experiment to measure the density of an irregular solid.

**Mark Scheme:**

- Calculate volume from displacement (1 mark)
- Calculate density: ρ = m/V (1 mark)

---

### Question 8 (2 marks)
Explain why atmospheric pressure decreases with altitude.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Less air above at higher altitudes (1 mark)
- So less weight of air / less mass of air above (1 mark)

---

### Question 9 (3 marks)
A hydraulic brake system has a master piston of area 0.005 m² and a slave piston of area 0.05 m². If the driver applies a force of 100 N to the brake pedal, calculate the force applied to the brake pads.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Pressure = F/A = 100/0.005 = 20,000 Pa (1 mark)
- F = p × A = 20,000 × 0.05 (1 mark)
- F = 1000 N (1 mark)

## Extended Answer Question (6 marks)

### Question 11 (6 marks)
(a) Calculate the pressure at the bottom of a 3 m deep swimming pool. (Density of water = 1000 kg/m³, g = 10 N/kg) (2 marks)
(b) Explain why a dam is built thicker at the bottom than at the top. (2 marks)
(c) A hydraulic lift has a small piston of area 0.01 m² and a large piston of area 0.5 m². Calculate the force needed on the small piston to lift a 2000 kg car. (g = 10 N/kg) (2 marks)

**Mark Scheme:**

**(a) Pressure calculation**
- p = ρgh = 1000 × 10 × 3 (1 mark)
- p = 30,000 Pa (1 mark)

**(b) Dam thickness**
- Pressure increases with depth (1 mark)
- So greater pressure at bottom requires thicker wall to withstand force (1 mark)

**(c) Hydraulic force**
- Weight of car = 2000 × 10 = 20,000 N (1 mark)
- F₁ = F₂ × (A₁/A₂) = 20,000 × (0.01/0.5) = 400 N (1 mark)
