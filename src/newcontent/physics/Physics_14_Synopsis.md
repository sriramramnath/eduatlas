# Chapter 14: Energy Transfers
## Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Physics - Concise Synopsis

---

## Essential Equations

```
Kinetic energy:       KE = ½ × m × v²
Gravitational PE:     GPE = m × g × h
Efficiency:           Efficiency = useful output ÷ total input
```

---

| Type | Description |

| **Elastic Potential** | Energy in stretched/compressed springs |
| **Chemical** | Energy in chemical bonds |
| **Nuclear** | Energy in atomic nuclei |
| **Thermal** | Energy due to temperature |

## Conservation of Energy

> **Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred.**

```
Total energy before = Total energy after
```

### Common Energy Transfers

| Situation | Transfer |
|-----------|----------|
| Object falling | GPE → KE |
| Car braking | KE → Thermal |
| Stretching spring | KE → Elastic |
| Burning fuel | Chemical → Thermal |
| Battery | Chemical → Electrical |

---

## Kinetic Energy

```
KE = ½ × m × v²
```

- **m** in kg
- **v** in m/s
- **KE** in joules (J)

### Key Point
- v is **squared**
- Double speed = 4× KE

---

## Gravitational Potential Energy

```
GPE = m × g × h
```

- **m** in kg
- **g** = 10 N/kg
- **h** in m
- **GPE** in joules (J)

---

## Falling Objects

Without air resistance:
```
Loss in GPE = Gain in KE
mgh = ½mv²
```

### Example
```
Object falls from 20 m:
v² = 2gh = 2 × 10 × 20 = 400
v = 20 m/s
```

---

## Efficiency

```
Efficiency = useful energy output ÷ total energy input

Or:

Efficiency = useful power output ÷ total power input
```

### Percentage
```
% Efficiency = Efficiency × 100
```

### Example
```
Useful output = 300 J
Total input = 500 J
Efficiency = 300/500 = 0.6 = 60%
```

### Improving Efficiency
- Lubrication (reduce friction)
- Insulation (reduce heat loss)
- Streamlining (reduce air resistance)

## Key Exam Points

1. **KE = ½mv²** - v is squared
2. **GPE = mgh** - h is height
3. **Conservation** - energy not created/destroyed
4. **Efficiency** - always < 100%
5. **Falling objects** - GPE → KE
6. **Renewable** - won't run out
