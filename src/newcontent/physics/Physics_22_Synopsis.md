## Atomic Structure

### Particles

| Particle | Charge | Mass | Location |
|----------|--------|------|----------|
| **Proton** | +1 | 1 | Nucleus |
| **Neutron** | 0 | 1 | Nucleus |
| **Electron** | -1 | 1/1840 | Shells |

### Key Numbers
- **Atomic number (Z)** = protons
- **Mass number (A)** = protons + neutrons
- **Neutrons** = A - Z

## Isotopes

- Same element (same Z)
- Different number of neutrons
- Same chemical properties
- Different physical properties

---

## Types of Radiation

| Type | Symbol | Nature | Charge | Mass |
|------|--------|--------|--------|------|
| **Alpha (α)** | ⁴He | Helium nucleus | +2 | 4 |
| **Beta (β)** | ⁰e | Electron | -1 | ~0 |
| **Gamma (γ)** | γ | EM wave | 0 | 0 |

---

## Properties Comparison

| Property | Alpha | Beta | Gamma |
|----------|-------|------|-------|
| **Range** | Few cm | ~1 m | km |
| **Stopped by** | Paper | Aluminum | Lead |
| **Ionization** | Strong | Moderate | Weak |
| **Deflected by fields** | Yes | Yes | No |

## Decay Equations

### Alpha Decay
```
ᴬX → ᴬ⁻⁴Y + ⁴He
 Z     Z-2     2
```

### Beta Decay
```
ᴬX → ᴬY + ⁰e
 Z     Z+1    -1
```

### Gamma Decay
```
ᴬX* → ᴬX + γ
 Z      Z
```

---

## Key Points

1. **Alpha** - helium nucleus, +2, stopped by paper
2. **Beta** - electron, -1, stopped by aluminum
3. **Gamma** - EM wave, 0, needs lead/concrete
4. **Conservation** - mass and charge balanced
5. **Ionization** - alpha strongest, gamma weakest
