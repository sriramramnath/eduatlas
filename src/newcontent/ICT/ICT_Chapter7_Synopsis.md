## Quick Reference Summary

### 1. E-Commerce Types

| Type | Full Name | Example |
|------|-----------|---------|
| **B2C** | Business to Consumer | Amazon, ASOS |
| **B2B** | Business to Business | Alibaba |
| **C2C** | Consumer to Consumer | eBay, Facebook Marketplace |
| **C2B** | Consumer to Business | Freelancers, Upwork |

---

### 2. E-Commerce Advantages and Disadvantages

| | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|---|------------|---------------|
| **Consumer** | 24/7 shopping, price comparison, wide selection, home delivery | Cannot examine products, delivery waits, security concerns |
| **Business** | Global reach, lower costs, data for marketing | Competition, returns handling, security required |

---

### 3. Communication Changes

| Method | Key Feature | Impact |
|--------|-------------|--------|
| Email | Asynchronous | Formal/business communication |
| Instant Messaging | Real-time | Personal communication |
| Video Calls | Face-to-face | Remote work, relationships |
| Social Media | Broadcast | News, communities, activism |

---

### 4. Social Media Impact

| Positive | Negative |
|----------|----------|
| Global connection | Cyberbullying |
| Community building | Addiction/screen time |
| Platform for voices | Privacy concerns |
| Business marketing | Misinformation spread |
| Educational content | Mental health effects |

---

### 5. Online Learning

**Types:**
- MOOCs (Coursera, edX)
- Virtual Classrooms (Zoom, Teams)
- Educational Videos (Khan Academy, YouTube)
- Interactive Learning (Duolingo)

**Advantages:** Flexible, accessible, lower cost, self-paced
**Disadvantages:** Requires self-discipline, limited interaction, quality varies

---

### 6. Digital Divide

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Access Divide** | Physical access to technology/internet |
| **Skills Divide** | Ability to use technology effectively |
| **Usage Divide** | How technology is used |
| **Quality Divide** | Quality of connection and devices |

**Factors:** Geography, economics, age, education

---

### 7. Impact Areas Summary

| Area | Positive Impacts | Negative Impacts |
|------|------------------|------------------|
| **Economic** | New jobs, e-commerce, global markets | Job losses, competition, gig economy insecurity |
| **Social** | Global connection, communities | Isolation, cyberbullying, privacy loss |
| **Educational** | Information access, online learning | Information overload, misinformation, divide |
| **Cultural** | Cultural exchange, global media | Cultural homogenisation, Western dominance |
| **Health** | Telemedicine, health apps | Sedentary lifestyle, mental health issues |
| **Environmental** | Less travel, less paper | Energy use, e-waste |

### 9. High-Yield Exam Points

**Must-Know Facts:**

- **E-commerce = buying/selling online** — B2C, B2B, C2C, C2B
- **Digital divide creates inequality** — access, skills, usage gaps
- **Social media has dual impact** — connection vs mental health concerns
- **Online learning offers flexibility** — but needs self-discipline
- **Jobs created AND lost** — new roles vs traditional decline
- **Information access democratised** — but misinformation spreads
- **Remote work enabled** — work-life balance challenges

**Key Statistics to Remember:**
- Digital divide affects rural areas, elderly, low-income groups
- E-commerce growing rapidly vs high street decline
- Online learning expanded significantly since 2020

| **Remote Work** | Working from location other than office |

---

### 11. Exam Tips

1. **Balance positive and negative** — every impact has both sides
2. **Use specific examples** — name platforms, services, situations
3. **Consider different groups** — elderly, young, rural, urban
4. **Address the digital divide** — not everyone has equal access
5. **Think broadly** — economic, social, educational, cultural impacts
6. **Support with evidence** — examples make answers stronger
