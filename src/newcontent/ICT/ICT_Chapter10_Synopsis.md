### 2. Search Operators

| Operator | Function | Example |
|----------|----------|---------|
| `"quotes"` | Exact phrase | `"climate change"` |
| AND | Both terms | cats AND dogs |
| OR | Either term | football OR soccer |
| NOT | Excludes term | apple NOT fruit |
| `*` | Wildcard | comput* |
| site: | Specific site | site:bbc.com |
| filetype: | Specific format | filetype:pdf |

---

### 3. CRAAP Test for Evaluation

| Letter | Factor | Questions |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| **C** | Currency | When published/updated? |
| **R** | Relevance | Does it answer your question? |
| **A** | Authority | Who wrote it? Credentials? |
| **A** | Accuracy | Evidence? Verifiable? |
| **P** | Purpose | Why does it exist? Bias? |

---

### 4. Domain Extensions

| Domain | Type | Reliability |
|--------|------|-------------|
| .edu | Educational | Generally reliable |
| .gov | Government | Official, reliable |
| .org | Organisation | Varies—check source |
| .com | Commercial | May have bias |
| .ac.uk | UK academic | Generally reliable |

---

### 5. Types of False Information

| Type | Intent | Example |
|------|--------|---------|
| **Misinformation** | No intent to harm | Sharing wrong info by mistake |
| **Disinformation** | Deliberately deceive | Fake news to mislead |
| **Malinformation** | Harm with true info | Leaking private data |

---

### 6. Signs of Unreliable Sources

```
 WARNING SIGNS:
• Sensational/emotional headlines
• No author or credentials
• No citations or references
• Outdated information
• Extreme bias
• Spelling/grammar errors
• Only on one website
• Contradicts reliable sources
```

---

### 7. Plagiarism and Citation

**Plagiarism = Using others' work without attribution**

**Avoid by:**
- Citing all sources
- Using quotation marks for quotes
- Paraphrasing with credit
- Tracking sources during research

**Citation Elements:**
- Author
- Title
- Date
- Publisher/URL
- Date accessed

### 9. High-Yield Exam Points

**Must-Know Facts:**

- **Search engines crawl, index, rank** — how results are found
- **Operators improve search** — quotes, AND, OR, NOT
- **Evaluate with CRAAP** — currency, relevance, authority, accuracy, purpose
- **.edu and .gov generally reliable** — check domain
- **Cross-reference sources** — verify in multiple places
- **Always cite sources** — avoid plagiarism
- **Misinformation vs disinformation** — intent differs
- **Fact-check before sharing** — use verification sites

**Quick Evaluation Checklist:**
1. Who wrote it? (Authority)
2. When was it published? (Currency)
3. Why was it written? (Purpose)
4. Is it supported by evidence? (Accuracy)
5. Can it be verified elsewhere? (Accuracy)

### 11. Exam Tips

1. **Always evaluate sources** — use CRAAP framework
2. **Use specific search techniques** — operators, filters
3. **Cross-reference information** — don't rely on single source
4. **Distinguish information types** — misinformation vs disinformation
5. **Cite properly** — avoid plagiarism in all work
6. **Consider purpose and bias** — why was this created?
