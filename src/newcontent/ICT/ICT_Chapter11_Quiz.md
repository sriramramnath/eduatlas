## SECTION A: Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
Which security measure requires both a password and a code sent to your phone?

A) Strong password  
B) Two-factor authentication (2FA)  
C) Encryption  
D) Firewall  

**Answer: B) Two-factor authentication (2FA)**

**Explanation:** Two-factor authentication requires two different types of verification—typically something you know (password) and something you have (phone for receiving codes). This significantly improves security beyond just a password.

---

### Question 2
What does MOOC stand for?

A) Massive Open Online Course  
B) Multiple Online Option Course  
C) Major Online Open Class  
D) Mobile Open Online Certificate  

**Answer: A) Massive Open Online Course**

**Explanation:** MOOC stands for Massive Open Online Course. These are online courses designed for unlimited participation, offered by platforms like Coursera, edX, and FutureLearn.

---

### Question 3
Which is a disadvantage of online shopping compared to physical stores?

A) Wider product selection  
B) Cannot physically examine products before purchase  
C) Price comparison is easier  
D) 24/7 availability  

**Answer: B) Cannot physically examine products before purchase**

**Explanation:** A key disadvantage of online shopping is that customers cannot touch, try on, or closely examine products before buying. This can lead to disappointment when products don't meet expectations.

---

### Question 4
What is telemedicine?

A) Medical equipment sold online  
B) Medical care delivered remotely via technology  
C) Online medical insurance  
D) Social media for doctors  

**Answer: B) Medical care delivered remotely via technology**

**Explanation:** Telemedicine involves providing healthcare services remotely using telecommunications technology, such as video consultations with doctors, online diagnosis, and remote monitoring.

---

### Question 5
Which of these is a digital wallet service?

A) Visa  
B) PayPal  
C) Cash  
D) Cheque  

**Answer: B) PayPal**

**Explanation:** PayPal is a digital wallet that stores payment information and enables online transactions. Other digital wallets include Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Samsung Pay.

---

### Question 6
What consumer right do UK online shoppers have?

A) Right to keep goods without paying  
B) 14-day right to cancel online orders  
C) Right to half price on all products  
D) No rights for online purchases  

**Answer: B) 14-day right to cancel online orders**

**Explanation:** UK consumers have the right to cancel most online orders within 14 days of delivery for a full refund. This is part of consumer protection legislation for distance selling.

---

### Question 7
What is the main advantage of online banking?

A) Higher interest rates are guaranteed  
B) Available 24/7 without visiting branches  
C) No security needed  
D) Banks give free money  

**Answer: B) Available 24/7 without visiting branches**

**Explanation:** Online banking is available around the clock, allowing customers to check balances, transfer money, and pay bills at any time without needing to visit a physical branch during opening hours.

---

### Question 8
What is phishing in the context of online banking?

A) A type of online game  
B) Fraudulent attempt to steal login credentials  
C) A banking feature  
D) A secure login method  

**Answer: B) Fraudulent attempt to steal login credentials**

**Explanation:** Phishing is a fraudulent technique where attackers impersonate legitimate organisations (like banks) to trick people into revealing sensitive information such as usernames, passwords, or credit card details.

---

### Question 9
Which service would you use to stream video content on demand?

A) Spotify  
B) Netflix  
C) PayPal  
D) Online banking  

**Answer: B) Netflix**

**Explanation:** Netflix is a video streaming service offering on-demand access to films and TV series. Spotify streams music, PayPal is for payments, and online banking is for financial services.

---

### Question 10
What is a key challenge of online learning?

A) Too much face-to-face interaction  
B) Requires self-discipline and motivation  
C) Always more expensive than traditional learning  
D) Too many teachers available  

**Answer: B) Requires self-discipline and motivation**

**Explanation:** Online learning typically requires greater self-discipline than traditional classroom learning because students must manage their own time, stay focused without direct supervision, and maintain motivation independently.

---

### Question 11
What does "click and collect" mean in online shopping?

A) Clicking items randomly  
B) Ordering online and picking up in store  
C) Collecting coupons online  
D) Returning unwanted items  

**Answer: B) Ordering online and picking up in store**

**Explanation:** Click and collect allows customers to order products online and collect them from a physical store. This combines online convenience with avoiding delivery fees and waiting for delivery.

---

### Question 12
Who is most likely to be excluded from online services due to the digital divide?

A) Young people  
B) IT professionals  
C) Elderly people without internet access  
D) University students  

**Answer: C) Elderly people without internet access**

**Explanation:** The digital divide particularly affects elderly people who may lack internet access, devices, or digital skills. This exclusion prevents them from accessing increasingly digital services.

## SECTION B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
**Online banking is only available during bank opening hours.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Online banking is available 24/7, 365 days a year. This is one of its main advantages over traditional branch banking, which is limited to opening hours.

---

### Statement 2
**Two-factor authentication makes accounts more secure.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Two-factor authentication adds an extra layer of security by requiring two different types of verification. Even if someone obtains your password, they cannot access your account without the second factor (like a code sent to your phone).

---

### Statement 3
**Online shoppers cannot return faulty goods.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Online shoppers have legal rights to return faulty goods for a refund. In the UK, the Consumer Rights Act protects buyers, and online purchases also have a 14-day cancellation right for most items.

---

### Statement 4
**MOOCs are free online courses available to anyone.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** MOOCs (Massive Open Online Courses) are designed to be open and accessible to unlimited participants. Many are free to access, though certification may require payment.

---

### Statement 5
**Digital wallets like PayPal are less secure than credit cards.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Digital wallets can be more secure than credit cards because they don't share your actual card details with merchants. They use encryption and tokenisation for security.

---

### Statement 6
**Online learning requires the same level of self-discipline as classroom learning.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Online learning typically requires more self-discipline than classroom learning. Without scheduled classes, teachers present, and peer pressure, students must motivate themselves and manage their own time.

---

### Statement 7
**Phishing emails often mimic legitimate organisations like banks.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Phishing emails typically impersonate trusted organisations such as banks, online services, or government agencies to trick recipients into revealing sensitive information or clicking malicious links.

---

### Statement 8
**Streaming services require physical media like DVDs.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Streaming services deliver content directly over the internet without requiring physical media. Users can watch or listen instantly without owning DVDs, CDs, or other physical formats.

---

### Statement 9
**Government services are increasingly available online.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Governments are increasingly moving services online, including tax filing, passport applications, benefit claims, and local services. This "digital by default" approach offers convenience but creates challenges for those without internet access.

---

### Statement 10
**Online health services can replace all doctor visits.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Online health services (telemedicine) are not suitable for all conditions. They cannot perform physical examinations, and some health issues require in-person assessment. They complement but don't replace all traditional healthcare.

---

### Statement 11
**Customer reviews are always reliable when shopping online.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Customer reviews can be helpful but aren't always reliable. Some reviews may be fake (either positive reviews paid for by sellers or negative reviews from competitors). Consider the overall pattern and look for verified purchases.

---

### Statement 12
**The digital divide affects only people in developing countries.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The digital divide affects people in all countries, including developed nations. Factors include age, income, location (rural vs urban), disability, and education level. Many people in developed countries also lack adequate internet access or digital skills.

### Question 5 (3 marks)
**Explain how phishing attacks target online banking users and how to protect against them.**

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
**How phishing works:**
- Attackers send emails pretending to be from the bank [1 mark]
- Emails contain links to fake websites that look like the real bank site [1 mark]
- Users enter login details which are captured by attackers [1 mark]

**Protection:**
- Never click links in suspicious emails [1 mark]
- Type bank URL directly into browser [1 mark]
- Check sender email address carefully [1 mark]
- Contact bank directly if uncertain [1 mark]

*Award up to 3 marks for explanation covering attack method and protection.*

### Question 7 (4 marks)
**A student wants to learn a new programming language online. Describe four factors they should consider when choosing an online learning platform.**

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Any four factors:

1. **Course content and quality** — Does it cover what they need to learn? Is the content current? [1 mark]

2. **Cost** — Is the course free or paid? Are there subscription options? Hidden fees? [1 mark]

3. **Credential value** — Will completion be recognised? Is there a certificate? [1 mark]

4. **Learning style fit** — Videos, text, interactive? Self-paced or scheduled? [1 mark]

5. **Reviews and reputation** — What do other learners say about the platform/course? [1 mark]

6. **Support available** — Forums, tutors, community support for questions? [1 mark]

7. **Time commitment** — How long does the course take? Flexible schedule? [1 mark]

8. **Technical requirements** — What software or equipment is needed? [1 mark]

### Question 8 (4 marks)
**Explain the security risks of using online services and describe two measures to protect yourself.**

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
**Security risks:**
- Phishing attacks tricking users into revealing credentials [1 mark]
- Malware capturing login information [1 mark]
- Unsecured Wi-Fi allowing data interception [1 mark]
- Data breaches at service providers [1 mark]
- Identity theft from stolen information [1 mark]

**Protection measures:**

1. **Two-factor authentication** — Even if password is stolen, attacker cannot access without second factor [1 mark]

2. **Strong, unique passwords** — Different password for each service prevents multiple breaches [1 mark]

3. **Use secure connections** — Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities [1 mark]

4. **Keep software updated** — Patches security vulnerabilities [1 mark]

5. **Monitor accounts regularly** — Detect unauthorised activity quickly [1 mark]

*Award up to 4 marks for covering risks and protection measures.*
