# IGCSE ICT — Chapter 9: Implications of Digital Technologies

## SECTION A: Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
What does GDPR stand for?

A) General Data Privacy Rules  
B) General Data Protection Regulation  
C) Global Data Protection Rights  
D) Government Data Privacy Regulation  

**Answer: B) General Data Protection Regulation**

**Explanation:** GDPR (General Data Protection Regulation) is an EU regulation that sets standards for collecting, storing, and processing personal data, giving individuals rights over their data.

---

### Question 2
Which law criminalises unauthorised access to computer systems?

A) GDPR  
B) Copyright Act  
C) Computer Misuse Act  
D) Data Protection Act  

**Answer: C) Computer Misuse Act**

**Explanation:** The Computer Misuse Act (UK) criminalises unauthorised access to computer systems, hacking, and creating/distributing malware. GDPR deals with data protection, while copyright law protects creative works.

---

### Question 3
What type of intellectual property protects brand logos and names?

A) Copyright  
B) Patents  
C) Trademarks  
D) Design rights  

**Answer: C) Trademarks**

**Explanation:** Trademarks protect brand identity elements like logos, names, and slogans. Copyright protects creative works, patents protect inventions, and design rights protect visual appearance of products.

---

### Question 4
What is a key ethical concern regarding artificial intelligence?

A) AI is too expensive  
B) AI can reflect and amplify biases from training data  
C) AI cannot connect to the internet  
D) AI is always accurate  

**Answer: B) AI can reflect and amplify biases from training data**

**Explanation:** AI systems trained on historical data may inherit and amplify existing biases, leading to discriminatory outcomes. This is a significant ethical concern in AI development and deployment.

---

### Question 5
Which group is most affected by the skills dimension of the digital divide?

A) Urban teenagers  
B) University students  
C) Elderly people  
D) IT professionals  

**Answer: C) Elderly people**

**Explanation:** Elderly people are often most affected by the skills aspect of the digital divide because they may have had less exposure to digital technologies and face greater challenges learning new digital skills.

---

### Question 6
What is e-waste?

A) Energy wasted by computers  
B) Electronic waste from discarded devices  
C) Excess bandwidth usage  
D) Email spam  

**Answer: B) Electronic waste from discarded devices**

**Explanation:** E-waste refers to discarded electronic devices such as old smartphones, computers, and tablets. It's a growing environmental problem due to toxic materials and improper disposal.

---

### Question 7
What right does GDPR give individuals regarding their personal data?

A) Right to sell their data  
B) Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")  
C) Right to copy anyone's data  
D) Right to delete others' data  

**Answer: B) Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")**

**Explanation:** GDPR gives individuals the right to request deletion of their personal data under certain circumstances. This is often called the "right to be forgotten."

---

### Question 8
What is DRM (Digital Rights Management)?

A) A type of computer virus  
B) Technology that restricts how digital content can be used  
C) A social media platform  
D) A type of internet connection  

**Answer: B) Technology that restricts how digital content can be used**

**Explanation:** DRM (Digital Rights Management) refers to technologies that restrict how digital content can be copied, shared, or used, often to protect copyright.

---

### Question 9
Which is an environmental benefit of digital technologies?

A) Increased e-waste  
B) Higher energy consumption  
C) Reduced travel through video conferencing  
D) More paper usage  

**Answer: C) Reduced travel through video conferencing**

**Explanation:** Video conferencing and remote work reduce the need for travel, decreasing carbon emissions from transportation. This is a positive environmental impact of digital technologies.

---

### Question 10
What economic impact has digital technology had on traditional retail?

A) Created more physical stores  
B) Increased demand for cashiers  
C) Disrupted traditional retail through e-commerce  
D) Eliminated all shopping  

**Answer: C) Disrupted traditional retail through e-commerce**

**Explanation:** E-commerce has significantly disrupted traditional retail, with many physical stores closing as consumers shift to online shopping. This has reduced demand for some retail positions while creating new e-commerce roles.

---

### Question 11
What is the "gig economy"?

A) Economy based on music streaming  
B) Labour market with flexible, freelance work arrangements  
C) Gaming industry economy  
D) Government internet regulation  

**Answer: B) Labour market with flexible, freelance work arrangements**

**Explanation:** The gig economy refers to a labour market characterised by short-term contracts and freelance work rather than permanent employment. Examples include Uber drivers and freelance designers.

---

### Question 12
Which job role has been created by digital technology?

A) Travel agent  
B) Social media manager  
C) Bank teller  
D) Video store clerk  

**Answer: B) Social media manager**

**Explanation:** Social media manager is a job created by digital technology that didn't exist before social media platforms. Travel agents, bank tellers, and video store clerks are roles that have declined due to digital technology.

## SECTION B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
**GDPR only applies to organisations based in the European Union.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** GDPR applies to any organisation processing personal data of EU residents, regardless of where the organisation is based. This means companies worldwide must comply if they serve EU customers.

---

### Statement 2
**Copyright automatically protects creative works when they are created.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Copyright protection is automatic when a creative work is fixed in tangible form. Registration is not required (though it can help in legal disputes). The work is protected from the moment of creation.

---

### Statement 3
**AI systems cannot be biased because they are based on mathematics.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** AI systems can be biased because they learn from training data that may contain historical biases. If the data reflects discrimination, the AI may perpetuate or amplify those biases.

---

### Statement 4
**The digital divide affects both developed and developing countries.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** The digital divide exists in all countries, affecting rural communities, low-income households, elderly people, and those with limited education. It's not limited to developing nations.

---

### Statement 5
**Digital technologies have only positive effects on mental health.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Digital technologies have both positive and negative effects on mental health. Positives include access to resources and support; negatives include social media anxiety, addiction, and cyberbullying.

---

### Statement 6
**Organisations must report data breaches under GDPR.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Under GDPR, organisations must report certain types of data breaches to the relevant supervisory authority within 72 hours of becoming aware of the breach.

---

### Statement 7
**Automation has eliminated all traditional jobs.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Automation has transformed the job market, eliminating some jobs while creating others. Many traditional jobs still exist but may have changed to include digital elements.

---

### Statement 8
**Data centres consume significant amounts of energy.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Data centres running 24/7 to power cloud services, websites, and applications consume enormous amounts of electricity, both for computing and for cooling systems.

---

### Statement 9
**Online harassment and cyberbullying have no legal consequences.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Online harassment and cyberbullying can have serious legal consequences. They may constitute criminal offences under harassment laws, and victims can pursue civil action for damages.

---

### Statement 10
**E-waste contains toxic materials that can harm the environment.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Electronic devices contain toxic materials including lead, mercury, and cadmium. Improper disposal can contaminate soil and water, harming the environment and human health.

---

### Statement 11
**Digital technologies have made work-life balance easier for everyone.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** While digital technologies enable flexible work, they've also created an "always-on" culture where employees feel pressured to be constantly available. Work-life balance impacts vary.

---

### Statement 12
**Children face both benefits and risks from digital technology use.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Children benefit from educational resources, creative platforms, and social connections. However, they also face risks including screen time concerns, inappropriate content, and online predators.

### Question 6 (4 marks)
**Explain how organisations should protect personal data under GDPR.**

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Any four points:

1. **Obtain consent** — Get clear permission before collecting data [1 mark]

2. **Inform individuals** — Explain how data will be used [1 mark]

3. **Implement security measures** — Protect data from unauthorised access [1 mark]

4. **Data minimisation** — Only collect necessary data [1 mark]

5. **Report breaches** — Notify authorities of data breaches within 72 hours [1 mark]

6. **Appoint DPO** — Large organisations need Data Protection Officer [1 mark]

7. **Allow data access** — Let individuals see their data on request [1 mark]

8. **Delete when requested** — Honour right to erasure requests [1 mark]

---

- Reducing travel through remote work [1 mark]

### Question 8 (4 marks)
**A school wants to use AI software to monitor students' online activity and identify potential safeguarding concerns. Discuss the legal and ethical implications.**

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
**Legal implications:**
- Must comply with GDPR for student data [1 mark]
- Need parental consent for minors [1 mark]
- Data must be stored securely [1 mark]
- Clear purpose limitation required [1 mark]

**Ethical implications:**
- Privacy concerns with constant monitoring [1 mark]
- Student trust and autonomy issues [1 mark]
- Potential bias in AI flagging certain students [1 mark]
- Balance between safety and privacy rights [1 mark]
- Transparency about what is monitored [1 mark]
- Accuracy of AI in identifying genuine concerns [1 mark]

*Award up to 4 marks for discussion covering both legal and ethical aspects.*
