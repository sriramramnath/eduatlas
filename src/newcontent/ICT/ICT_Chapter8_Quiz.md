# IGCSE ICT — Chapter 8: Online Communities
## Quiz Section (Pearson Edexcel Specification)

## SECTION A: Multiple Choice Questions

### Question 1
What type of online community is LinkedIn primarily used for?

A) Gaming  
B) Professional networking  
C) Dating  
D) Photo sharing  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** LinkedIn is designed for professional networking, allowing users to create professional profiles, connect with colleagues, find job opportunities, and engage with industry content.

---

### Question 2
What does "netiquette" refer to?

A) Internet safety software  
B) Etiquette guidelines for online communication  
C) Social media privacy settings  
D) Online gaming rules  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Netiquette (network etiquette) refers to the conventions of polite and appropriate behaviour when communicating online, such as being respectful, clear, and protecting privacy.

---

### Question 3
Which of the following is a benefit of online support communities?

A) They replace professional medical advice  
B) They provide emotional support and shared experiences  
C) They guarantee solutions to problems  
D) They are always moderated by professionals  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Online support communities allow people facing similar challenges to share experiences, offer emotional support, and exchange practical advice. However, they don't replace professional help and may not be professionally moderated.

---

### Question 4
What is a "digital footprint"?

A) The physical device used to access the internet  
B) The trail of data left by online activities  
C) A type of computer virus  
D) An online security feature  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** A digital footprint is the record of your online activities, including posts, searches, purchases, and interactions. This data can persist and may be visible to others.

---

### Question 5
Which is a significant risk of online communities for young people?

A) Improved digital literacy  
B) Access to educational resources  
C) Online predators posing as peers  
D) Connection with distant family  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Online predators may create fake profiles pretending to be young people to gain trust and exploit children. This is a serious safety risk in online communities.

---

### Question 6
What is an "echo chamber" in online communities?

A) A platform for voice messages  
B) An environment reinforcing existing beliefs without challenge  
C) A music sharing community  
D) A type of discussion forum  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Echo chambers occur when people are only exposed to viewpoints that agree with their existing beliefs. This can reinforce biases and prevent exposure to diverse perspectives.

---

### Question 7
What is the role of a moderator in an online community?

A) Creating content for the community  
B) Enforcing community rules and managing content  
C) Selling products to community members  
D) Designing the platform interface  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Moderators are responsible for enforcing community rules, removing inappropriate content, warning or banning violators, and maintaining a safe and positive community environment.

---

### Question 8
Which platform is specifically designed for short-form video content?

A) LinkedIn  
B) Twitter/X  
C) TikTok  
D) Reddit  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** TikTok is a social media platform focused on short-form video content, where users create and share creative videos, often following trends and using music or filters.

---

### Question 9
What should you do if you encounter cyberbullying in an online community?

A) Respond aggressively to defend yourself  
B) Delete your account immediately  
C) Report it to moderators and avoid engaging  
D) Share it publicly to expose the bully  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** The best response to cyberbullying is to report it to community moderators and avoid engaging with the bully. Responding aggressively often escalates the situation.

---

### Question 10
What type of community would be most suitable for a fan of a particular music band?

A) Professional networking site  
B) Interest-based community  
C) Educational forum  
D) Dating platform  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Interest-based communities form around shared hobbies, passions, or topics. A music fan would find like-minded people in fan communities, music forums, or dedicated social media groups.

---

### Question 11
Why should you be cautious about sharing personal information in online communities?

A) It makes your profile less attractive  
B) It could be used for identity theft or fraud  
C) Moderators don't allow it  
D) It uses too much storage space  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Personal information shared online can be collected by criminals for identity theft, fraud, or social engineering attacks. Limiting shared information protects your safety and privacy.

---

### Question 12
Which is an example of good netiquette?

A) Using ALL CAPITALS for emphasis  
B) Respecting others' opinions even when disagreeing  
C) Sharing private messages publicly  
D) Responding immediately to every message  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Good netiquette includes treating others with respect, even during disagreements. This helps maintain positive community interactions and constructive discussions.

## SECTION B: True/False Questions

### Statement 1
**Online communities can help people with rare conditions find support from others with similar experiences.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** One of the significant benefits of online communities is connecting people who might never meet locally. Those with rare conditions can find others worldwide who understand their experiences.

---

### Statement 2
**Information shared in online communities is always accurate and trustworthy.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Information in online communities is not always accurate. Anyone can post information regardless of expertise. Users should verify important information from reliable sources and not rely solely on community advice.

---

### Statement 3
**A digital footprint can affect future job prospects.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Employers often check candidates' online presence. Inappropriate posts, photos, or comments can negatively impact job applications. Your digital footprint creates a lasting impression.

---

### Statement 4
**Moderators in online communities are always paid professionals.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Many online community moderators are volunteers who care about the community. While some large platforms employ professional moderators, many communities rely on volunteer members for moderation.

---

### Statement 5
**Cyberbullying can have serious psychological effects on victims.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Cyberbullying can cause significant emotional distress, anxiety, depression, and even self-harm. The 24/7 nature of online access means victims cannot easily escape the bullying.

---

### Statement 6
**You should accept all friend requests to expand your network.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** You should only accept friend requests from people you know or have good reason to trust. Accepting requests from strangers exposes you to potential scams, predators, and privacy risks.

---

### Statement 7
**Professional networking sites can help with career development.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Platforms like LinkedIn allow professionals to build networks, find job opportunities, showcase skills, and connect with industry peers, all of which support career development.

---

### Statement 8
**Posts deleted from social media are permanently removed from the internet.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Deleted posts may still exist in various forms—screenshots, archives, cached copies, or on others' devices. Once something is posted online, complete removal is difficult or impossible.

---

### Statement 9
**Online communities can provide support during difficult times when local support isn't available.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Online communities offer 24/7 access to support from people who understand specific challenges. This can be especially valuable for those without local support networks or facing stigmatised issues.

---

### Statement 10
**Everyone in online communities uses their real identity.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Many people use pseudonyms or fake identities online. Some for privacy, others for deception. You should never assume someone is who they claim to be without verification.

---

### Statement 11
**Reporting inappropriate content helps keep online communities safe.**

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** User reports help moderators identify and remove harmful content, bullying, scams, and other violations. Reporting is an important part of community self-regulation.

---

### Statement 12
**All online communities have the same rules and expectations.**

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Each online community has its own rules, culture, and expectations. What's acceptable in one community may be inappropriate in another. Users should read and understand each community's guidelines.

### Question 8 (4 marks)
**A student is considering joining several online communities. Explain four pieces of advice you would give them to participate safely.**

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Any four pieces of advice:

1. **Check privacy settings** — Ensure personal information is protected and limit who can see your content [1 mark]

2. **Read community rules** — Understand what behaviour is expected and prohibited [1 mark]

3. **Limit personal information** — Don't share address, phone number, school, or identifying details [1 mark]

4. **Be cautious with strangers** — Don't trust people immediately; verify identities before sharing more [1 mark]

5. **Report problems** — Alert moderators to bullying, inappropriate content, or suspicious behaviour [1 mark]

6. **Think before posting** — Consider whether content could be harmful or have negative consequences [1 mark]

7. **Protect your accounts** — Use strong, unique passwords and enable two-factor authentication [1 mark]

8. **Balance online time** — Don't let online communities replace real-world activities and relationships [1 mark]

*Award up to 4 marks for four distinct pieces of advice with explanations.*
