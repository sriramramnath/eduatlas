### 7. Security Measures for Online Services

```
ESSENTIAL SECURITY:
✓ Strong, unique passwords
✓ Two-factor authentication (2FA)
✓ Secure connections (HTTPS)
✓ Official apps only
✓ Regular account monitoring
✓ Avoid public Wi-Fi for sensitive activities
```

### 9. High-Yield Exam Points

**Must-Know Facts:**

- **Online banking = 24/7 access** — convenience but security essential
- **Two-factor authentication adds security** — something you know + have
- **E-commerce advantages** — choice, comparison, convenience
- **Online learning requires self-discipline** — flexibility is double-edged
- **Consumer rights protect online shoppers** — 14-day cancellation
- **Digital divide excludes some users** — not everyone has access
- **Phishing targets banking users** — verify before clicking

**Security Hierarchy:**

| Risk Level | Example | Security Needed |
|------------|---------|-----------------|
| High | Banking | 2FA, strong password, monitoring |
| Medium | Shopping | Secure payment, reputable sites |
| Lower | Streaming | Password protection |

### 11. Exam Tips

1. **Always mention security** — especially for banking and payments
2. **Balance advantages and disadvantages** — every service has both
3. **Use specific examples** — name real services and platforms
4. **Consider different users** — accessibility and digital divide
5. **Include consumer protection** — rights when things go wrong
6. **Think about authentication** — passwords, 2FA, biometrics
