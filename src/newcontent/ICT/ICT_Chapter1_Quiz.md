# Chapter 1: Digital Devices - Quiz Section

## Exam-Style Questions for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE ICT

---

### Question 1
Which storage type is volatile and loses data when power is removed?

A) Solid State Drive (SSD)  
B) Random Access Memory (RAM)  
C) Hard Disk Drive (HDD)  
D) USB Flash Drive  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: RAM is volatile memory, meaning it requires constant power to maintain data. When power is removed, all data in RAM is lost. SSDs, HDDs, and USB flash drives are non-volatile storage devices that retain data without power.

---

### Question 2
What is the primary advantage of a capacitive touchscreen over a resistive touchscreen?

A) It works with gloves  
B) It supports multi-touch gestures  
C) It is cheaper to manufacture  
D) It uses less power  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Capacitive touchscreens detect the electrical properties of the human touch and support multi-touch gestures such as pinch-to-zoom. Resistive touchscreens detect pressure and only support single-touch input, though they can work with gloves or styluses.

---

### Question 3
Which device would be most suitable for a professional video editor working with 4K footage?

A) Tablet with 64GB storage  
B) Budget laptop with integrated graphics  
C) High-performance desktop with dedicated GPU  
D) Smartphone with cloud storage  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Video editing, especially 4K footage, requires substantial processing power, dedicated graphics for rendering, large fast storage, and adequate RAM. A high-performance desktop meets these requirements and can be upgraded as needed.

### Question 4
What type of printer uses toner powder and is most suitable for high-volume office printing?

A) Inkjet printer  
B) Dot matrix printer  
C) Laser printer  
D) Thermal printer  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Laser printers use toner powder fused onto paper with heat. They are faster, more cost-effective for high-volume printing, and produce sharper text than inkjet printers. They are ideal for office environments with high print volumes.

### Question 5
Which wireless technology has the shortest range but is commonly used for contactless payments?

A) Wi-Fi  
B) Bluetooth  
C) NFC (Near Field Communication)  
D) 5G Cellular  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: NFC has a very short range (less than 10cm) which makes it ideal for secure applications like contactless payments. The short range ensures the devices must be deliberately placed close together, adding security.

---

### Question 6
What is the main difference between an SSD and an HDD?

A) SSDs have moving parts; HDDs do not  
B) SSDs are faster with no moving parts; HDDs use spinning platters  
C) HDDs are more expensive than SSDs  
D) SSDs have larger storage capacities than HDDs  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: SSDs use flash memory with no moving parts, making them faster, quieter, and more durable. HDDs store data on spinning magnetic platters, making them slower but typically offering larger capacities at lower costs.

### Question 7
A student needs a device for taking notes in lectures and writing essays. Which would be the BEST choice?

A) Desktop computer  
B) Laptop computer  
C) Games console  
D) Smartwatch  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A laptop provides the portability needed for taking notes in different locations, a physical keyboard suitable for writing essays, and sufficient processing power for word processing and research. Desktops aren't portable; games consoles and smartwatches aren't designed for document creation.

### Question 8
Which display technology produces the deepest blacks and is commonly used in high-end smartphones?

A) LCD  
B) LED-backlit LCD  
C) OLED  
D) Plasma  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: OLED (Organic Light-Emitting Diode) displays have pixels that emit their own light. When a pixel is off, it produces true black because no light is emitted. This results in superior contrast compared to LCD displays which require a backlight.

### Question 9
What does USB stand for?

A) Universal Serial Bus  
B) Unified System Buffer  
C) Universal Storage Block  
D) Unified Serial Buffer  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: USB stands for Universal Serial Bus. It is an industry standard for connecting peripherals to computers, providing both data transfer and power supply capabilities.

### Question 10
Which device would be most appropriate for a travelling salesperson who needs to give presentations and access company systems remotely?

A) Desktop computer  
B) Laptop computer  
C) Games console  
D) Smart TV  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: A laptop provides portability for travel, the ability to connect to projectors for presentations, sufficient processing power for remote access to company systems, and network connectivity options. Desktop computers aren't portable, while games consoles and smart TVs aren't designed for business applications.

### Question 11
Which type of memory card is most commonly used in smartphones?

A) CompactFlash  
B) SD Card  
C) microSD  
D) Memory Stick  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: microSD cards are the smallest form factor of SD cards and are commonly used in smartphones to expand storage capacity. Full-size SD cards are used in cameras, and CompactFlash and Memory Stick are older formats.

### Question 12
What is the function of an Analogue-to-Digital Converter (ADC)?

A) Converts digital signals to analogue  
B) Converts analogue signals to digital format  
C) Converts AC power to DC power  
D) Converts text to speech  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: An ADC (Analogue-to-Digital Converter) converts continuous analogue signals from the real world (like sound, light, temperature) into digital binary data that computers can process and store.

### Statement 13
RAM retains data even when the computer is turned off.

**Answer: False**

**Explanation**: RAM (Random Access Memory) is volatile memory. It requires constant power to retain data. When the computer is turned off, all data in RAM is lost. Only non-volatile storage like HDDs, SSDs, and USB drives retain data without power.

---

### Statement 14
A tablet computer is more portable than a desktop computer but typically has less processing power.

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Tablets are designed for portability with lightweight, slim designs and built-in batteries. However, this portability comes with trade-offs including less powerful processors, less RAM, and limited storage compared to desktop computers.

---

### Statement 15
Bluetooth has a longer range than Wi-Fi.

**Answer: False**

**Explanation**: Wi-Fi typically has a range of 30-100 metres indoors, while Bluetooth has a shorter range of approximately 10-100 metres depending on the class. Wi-Fi is designed for networking over larger areas, while Bluetooth is intended for short-range peripheral connections.

---

### Statement 16
A scanner is an output device.

**Answer: False**

**Explanation**: A scanner is an INPUT device. It captures physical documents or images and converts them into digital format that can be stored and edited on a computer. Output devices include monitors, printers, and speakers.

---

### Statement 17
Solid State Drives (SSDs) are more durable than Hard Disk Drives (HDDs) because they have no moving parts.

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: SSDs use flash memory with no moving mechanical components, making them more resistant to physical shock, drops, and vibration. HDDs have spinning platters and read/write heads that can be damaged by movement during operation.

---

### Statement 18
A laser printer is the best choice for printing high-quality photographs.

**Answer: False**

**Explanation**: While laser printers excel at printing sharp text and are cost-effective for high-volume office printing, inkjet printers are generally better for high-quality photograph printing. Inkjet printers can produce smoother colour gradients and better colour accuracy for images.

---

### Statement 19
Smartwatches can receive notifications from a paired smartphone.

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Smartwatches connect to smartphones via Bluetooth and can display notifications for calls, messages, emails, and app alerts. This allows users to check important information without removing their phone from their pocket or bag.

---

### Statement 20
A desktop computer with a 500GB SSD will load applications faster than one with a 500GB HDD.

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: SSDs have significantly faster read/write speeds than HDDs because they use flash memory with no moving parts. This results in faster boot times, quicker application loading, and more responsive system performance.

---

### Statement 21
All tablets use a resistive touchscreen.

**Answer: False**

**Explanation**: Most modern tablets use capacitive touchscreens, which support multi-touch gestures and provide better clarity. Resistive touchscreens are older technology that requires pressure and only supports single-touch. Some specialised industrial tablets still use resistive screens.

---

### Statement 22
A 3D printer creates objects by adding material layer by layer.

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: 3D printers use additive manufacturing, building objects layer by layer from the bottom up. This is different from traditional subtractive manufacturing (like CNC machining) which removes material from a larger block.

---

### Statement 23
E-ink displays are commonly used in e-readers because they consume very little power.

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: E-ink displays only use power when the image changes, not to maintain a static display. This makes them ideal for e-readers where users spend extended periods reading text, resulting in battery life measured in weeks rather than hours.

---

### Statement 24
A joystick is primarily used as an input device for word processing.

**Answer: False**

**Explanation**: A joystick is primarily used as an input device for gaming and simulation applications. It is not suited for word processing, which typically uses a keyboard for text input. Joysticks are designed for controlling movement in games and flight simulators.
