### Question 13
Alcohols have the general formula CnH2nOH.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Alcohols have the general formula CnH2n+1OH. The formula CnH2nOH would have one fewer hydrogen atom than correct.

---

### Question 14
Ethanol burns with a clean blue flame.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Unlike alkenes which burn with smoky flames due to incomplete combustion, alcohols like ethanol burn completely with a clean blue flame, producing only carbon dioxide and water.

---

### Question 15
Fermentation produces pure ethanol.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Fermentation produces dilute ethanol (approximately 10-15% concentration). Pure ethanol requires distillation of the fermentation mixture. The yeast enzymes are inhibited/killed when ethanol concentration reaches about 15%.

---

### Question 16
The hydration of ethene is a reversible reaction.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: The reaction C2H4 + H2O ⇌ C2H5OH is reversible, which is why the reversible arrow (⇌) is used. Not all ethene is converted, and unreacted starting materials are recycled.

---

### Question 17
Dehydration of ethanol requires concentrated sulfuric acid.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Dehydration of alcohols to form alkenes requires concentrated sulfuric acid (or concentrated phosphoric acid) as a catalyst, with heating. The acid helps remove the water molecule.

---

### Question 18
Oxidation of ethanol produces ethane.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Oxidation of ethanol produces ethanoic acid, not ethane. Ethane is an alkane produced by hydrogenation of ethene. The oxidation equation is: C2H5OH + 2[O] → CH3COOH + H2O.

---

### Question 19
Fermentation requires aerobic conditions.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Fermentation requires anaerobic conditions (no oxygen). In the presence of oxygen, the yeast would respire aerobically, producing carbon dioxide and water instead of ethanol.

---

### Question 20
Small alcohols are soluble in water.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Small alcohols like methanol and ethanol are completely miscible with water. The -OH group can form hydrogen bonds with water molecules, allowing the alcohols to dissolve.

---

### Question 21
The optimum temperature for fermentation is 100°C.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: The optimum temperature for fermentation is 25-35°C. At 100°C, the yeast enzymes would be denatured and would not work. High temperatures destroy the enzymes.

---

### Question 22
Hydration of ethene produces a higher atom economy than fermentation.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Hydration of ethene has 100% atom economy (all atoms in reactants become product). Fermentation has lower atom economy because carbon dioxide is produced as a by-product.

---

### Question 23
Propanol can exist as positional isomers.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Propanol has two positional isomers: propan-1-ol (OH on the first carbon) and propan-2-ol (OH on the middle carbon). Both have the molecular formula C3H7OH.

---

### Question 24
Ethanol produced by fermentation is called bioethanol.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: When ethanol is produced by fermentation using plant materials (sugar cane, corn, etc.), it is called bioethanol. It is considered a renewable fuel because the carbon released was recently absorbed by plants.
