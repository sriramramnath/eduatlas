# Chapter 20: Rates of Reaction - Quiz Section

## Exam-Style Questions for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
According to collision theory, what must happen for a reaction to occur?

A) Particles must collide with each other
B) Particles must collide with sufficient energy
C) Particles must collide with sufficient energy and correct orientation
D) Particles must be heated to a high temperature

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** For a successful collision, particles must: (1) collide, (2) have sufficient energy (at least the activation energy), AND (3) have the correct orientation. Simply colliding is not enough - the collision must be successful.

---

### Question 2
Which of the following will NOT increase the rate of a reaction?

A) Increasing the temperature
B) Increasing the concentration
C) Adding a catalyst
D) Increasing the volume of the container (at constant pressure)

**Answer: D**

**Explanation:** Increasing the volume at constant pressure actually decreases concentration, which would DECREASE the rate. Temperature increase, concentration increase, and catalyst addition all increase rate.

---

### Question 3
Why does increasing temperature increase the rate of reaction?

A) Particles collide less frequently
B) More particles have energy greater than or equal to the activation energy
C) The activation energy decreases
D) The reaction becomes more exothermic

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Increasing temperature gives particles more kinetic energy. This means more particles have energy ≥ Ea, resulting in more successful collisions. Temperature does NOT change activation energy (only a catalyst does that).

---

### Question 4
Which statement about catalysts is correct?

A) Catalysts are used up during the reaction
B) Catalysts lower the activation energy of a reaction
C) Catalysts increase the ΔH of a reaction
D) Catalysts only work with gases

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Catalysts work by providing an alternative reaction pathway with lower activation energy. Catalysts are NOT used up, they do NOT change ΔH, and they work with solids, liquids, and gases.

---

### Question 5
A reaction is carried out using 5 g of powdered calcium carbonate and 50 cm³ of acid. Which would produce the fastest rate?

A) 5 g of large marble chips + 50 cm³ of acid
B) 5 g of small marble chips + 50 cm³ of acid
C) 5 g of powdered CaCO₃ + 50 cm³ of acid
D) All would have the same rate

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Powdered calcium carbonate has the largest surface area. With more particles exposed, there are more frequent collisions and therefore a faster rate. The mass is the same in all cases.

---

### Question 6
In the disappearing cross experiment (sodium thiosulfate + HCl), what is measured?

A) The volume of gas produced
B) The mass loss during the reaction
C) The time for the cross to disappear
D) The temperature change

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** The disappearing cross experiment measures the time taken for sufficient sulfur precipitate to form such that the cross underneath the flask is no longer visible. Rate is calculated as 1/time.

---

### Question 7
What effect does increasing the concentration of a reactant have on collision theory?

A) Particles have more kinetic energy
B) Particles collide with more energy
C) There are more particles in the same volume, leading to more frequent collisions
D) The activation energy is lowered

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Higher concentration means more particles per unit volume. This leads to more frequent collisions between reactant particles. Concentration does not affect particle energy or activation energy.

---

### Question 8
Which catalyst is used in the Haber process for making ammonia?

A) Vanadium(V) oxide
B) Iron
C) Platinum
D) Manganese dioxide

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Iron (Fe) is the catalyst used in the Haber process (N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃). Vanadium(V) oxide is used in the Contact process, platinum in catalytic converters, and manganese dioxide in hydrogen peroxide decomposition.

---

### Question 9
The graph below shows the volume of gas produced over time for a reaction. What does the gradient represent?

A) The total volume of gas produced
B) The time taken for the reaction
C) The rate of reaction at that point
D) The activation energy

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** On a graph of quantity (volume, mass, concentration) vs time, the gradient represents the rate of reaction. A steeper gradient means a faster rate.

---

### Question 10
Why do enzymes have an optimum temperature?

A) Higher temperatures always increase rate
B) At high temperatures, enzymes are denatured and lose their shape
C) Enzymes work faster at lower temperatures
D) Temperature does not affect enzymes

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Enzymes are proteins with a specific shape. At high temperatures (above their optimum, usually around 37°C for human enzymes), the enzyme's structure is destroyed (denatured), and the active site no longer fits the substrate.

---

### Question 11
A reaction produces 60 cm³ of gas in 30 seconds. What is the average rate of reaction?

A) 0.5 cm³/s
B) 2 cm³/s
C) 30 cm³/s
D) 1800 cm³/s

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Rate = Change in volume / Time = 60 cm³ / 30 s = 2 cm³/s

---

### Question 12
What happens to the rate of reaction as the reaction progresses?

A) It stays constant throughout
B) It increases as reactants are used up
C) It decreases as reactants are used up
D) It decreases, then increases

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** As the reaction proceeds, reactant concentration decreases. This means fewer particles available to collide, leading to fewer successful collisions per second. Therefore, the rate decreases. This is why rate graphs curve and flatten.

## Part B: True/False Questions

**For each statement, state whether it is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.**

---

### Question 1
**Statement:** Increasing the temperature decreases the activation energy of a reaction.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Increasing temperature does **NOT** change the activation energy. It increases the proportion of particles that have energy equal to or greater than the activation energy.

---

### Question 2
**Statement:** A catalyst provides an alternative reaction pathway with lower activation energy.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** This is exactly how catalysts work - they provide a different mechanism for the reaction that requires less energy to initiate, thereby lowering the activation energy.

---

### Question 3
**Statement:** Powdered marble reacts faster with acid than marble chips because powder has greater mass.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Powdered marble reacts faster because it has **greater surface area** (not greater mass). The mass is the same, but more particles are exposed for collisions.

---

### Question 4
**Statement:** In collision theory, all collisions between reactant particles result in a reaction.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Not all collisions result in a reaction. Only **successful collisions** (where particles have sufficient energy and correct orientation) lead to a reaction.

---

### Question 5
**Statement:** Catalysts are chemically unchanged at the end of a reaction and can be reused.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** By definition, a catalyst is not used up in the reaction. It participates in the reaction mechanism but is regenerated, so it can be used repeatedly.

---

### Question 6
**Statement:** Doubling the concentration of a reactant will always double the rate of reaction.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Increasing concentration increases the rate, but the relationship is not always directly proportional. For some reactions, doubling concentration doubles rate, but for others the relationship is more complex. However, for many simple reactions at IGCSE level, rate is proportional to concentration.

---

### Question 7
**Statement:** Enzymes are biological catalysts that are specific to particular reactions.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Enzymes are highly specific - each enzyme catalyzes only one type of reaction or acts on only one specific substrate. This is due to the specific shape of the active site.

---

### Question 8
**Statement:** The rate of a reaction can be measured by timing how long it takes for the reaction to finish.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** While timing how long a reaction takes gives an average rate, the rate changes throughout the reaction. True rate measurement involves measuring quantity vs time, giving instantaneous rate at different points.

---

### Question 9
**Statement:** Increasing the pressure of a gaseous reaction mixture increases the rate because particles collide more frequently.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Higher pressure forces gas particles closer together, increasing their concentration. This leads to more frequent collisions and therefore a faster rate.

---

### Question 10
**Statement:** A catalyst changes both the rate of reaction and the enthalpy change (ΔH).

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A catalyst changes the **rate** but does **NOT** change the enthalpy change (ΔH). The products and reactants have the same energy levels; only the pathway to get there is different.

---

### Question 11
**Statement:** The steeper the gradient on a rate graph, the faster the reaction.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** On a graph of quantity vs time, the gradient represents the rate. A steeper gradient means a larger quantity change per unit time, indicating a faster rate.

---

### Question 12
**Statement:** In the sodium thiosulfate experiment, the independent variable is the time for the cross to disappear.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The independent variable is the **concentration of sodium thiosulfate** (what we change). The time for the cross to disappear is the **dependent variable** (what we measure).

## Extended Response Practice (For Higher Level)

### Extended Question (6 marks)
**Explain, in terms of particles and collisions, how increasing temperature affects the rate of a chemical reaction.**

**Mark Scheme (6 marks):**

| Point | Mark |
|-------|------|
| Particles have more kinetic energy at higher temperature | 1 |
| Particles move faster | 1 |
| More frequent collisions between particles | 1 |
| More particles have energy equal to or greater than activation energy | 1 |
| Higher proportion of successful collisions | 1 |
| QWC: Clear, logical explanation using appropriate terminology | 1 |

**Sample Answer:**
"When temperature is increased, particles gain more kinetic energy and move faster. This means particles collide more frequently. However, more importantly, at higher temperatures a greater proportion of particles have energy equal to or greater than the activation energy. This means a higher proportion of collisions are successful and result in a reaction. More successful collisions per second means the rate of reaction increases. Overall, rate approximately doubles for every 10°C increase in temperature."
