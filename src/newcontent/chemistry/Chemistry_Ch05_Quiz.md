### Question 1
What is the formula of calcium chloride?

A) CaCl  
B) CaCl₂  
C) Ca₂Cl  
D) Ca₂Cl₂  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Calcium forms Ca²⁺ ions and chlorine forms Cl⁻ ions. To balance the charges, two chloride ions are needed for every calcium ion: CaCl₂.

---

### Question 2
What is the formula of aluminium oxide?

A) AlO  
B) AlO₂  
C) Al₂O  
D) Al₂O₃  

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Aluminium forms Al³⁺ ions, oxygen forms O²⁻ ions. Crossing charges: Al₂O₃ (charges balance: 2×+3 = 3×-2).

---

### Question 3
What is the relative formula mass (Mᵣ) of NaOH?

A) 23  
B) 40  
C) 39  
D) 24  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Mᵣ(NaOH) = Ar(Na) + Ar(O) + Ar(H) = 23 + 16 + 1 = 40.

---

### Question 4
What is the relative formula mass (Mᵣ) of CaCO₃?

A) 68  
B) 84  
C) 100  
D) 116  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Mᵣ(CaCO₃) = Ar(Ca) + Ar(C) + (3 × Ar(O)) = 40 + 12 + (3 × 16) = 40 + 12 + 48 = 100.

---

### Question 5
How many moles are there in 36 g of water (H₂O)?

A) 1 mol  
B) 2 mol  
C) 3 mol  
D) 18 mol  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Mᵣ(H₂O) = (2 × 1) + 16 = 18. Number of moles = mass / molar mass = 36 / 18 = 2 mol.

---

### Question 6
What is Avogadro's constant?

A) 6.02 × 10²²  
B) 6.02 × 10²³  
C) 6.02 × 10²⁴  
D) 6.02 × 10²⁵  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Avogadro's constant (Nₐ) is approximately 6.02 × 10²³ mol⁻¹, representing the number of particles in one mole of a substance.

---

### Question 7
What is the mass of 2 moles of carbon dioxide (CO₂)?

A) 22 g  
B) 44 g  
C) 66 g  
D) 88 g  

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Mᵣ(CO₂) = 12 + (2 × 16) = 44. Mass = number of moles × molar mass = 2 × 44 = 88 g.

---

### Question 8
Which is the correct formula for ammonium sulfate?

A) NH₄SO₄  
B) (NH₄)₂SO₄  
C) NH₄(SO₄)₂  
D) N₂H₈SO₄  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Ammonium is NH₄⁺ and Sulfate is SO₄²⁻. Crossing the charges gives (NH₄)₂SO₄. Brackets are necessary around the polyatomic ammonium ion.

---

### Question 9
A compound has the empirical formula CH₂ and Mᵣ = 56. What is the molecular formula?

A) CH₂  
B) C₂H₄  
C) C₃H₆  
D) C₄H₈  

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Empirical unit mass (CH₂) = 12 + 2 = 14. Factor = Mᵣ / unit mass = 56 / 14 = 4. Molecular formula = (CH₂)₄ = C₄H₈.

---

### Question 10
What is the formula of aluminium sulfate?

A) AlSO₄  
B) Al₂SO₄  
C) Al(SO₄)₃  
D) Al₂(SO₄)₃  

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Aluminium is Al³⁺ and Sulfate is SO₄²⁻. Crossing charges: Al₂(SO₄)₃. Brackets are needed around the sulfate ion.

---

### Question 11
How many molecules are in 0.5 mol of water?

A) 3.01 × 10²³  
B) 6.02 × 10²³  
C) 1.20 × 10²⁴  
D) 9.03 × 10²³  

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: Number of molecules = moles × Avogadro's constant = 0.5 × 6.02 × 10²³ = 3.01 × 10²³.

---

### Question 12
A compound contains 23% sodium, 32% sulfur and 45% oxygen by mass. What is its empirical formula?

A) NaSO₄  
B) Na₂SO₄  
C) Na₂SO₃  
D) NaSO₃  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Using common atomic masses, the molar ratio of atoms matches sodium sulfate (Na₂SO₄). (Note: percentages are often rounded in exam questions).

### Question 13
The empirical formula is the simplest whole-number ratio of atoms in a compound.

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: By definition, the empirical formula gives the simplest ratio, while the molecular formula gives the actual number of atoms.
