# Chapter 11: The Alkali Metals - Quiz Section

## Exam-Style Questions for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

---

## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
Which statement about alkali metals is correct?

A) They are all gases at room temperature
B) They are stored under water
C) They have one electron in their outer shell
D) Their reactivity decreases down the group

**Answer: C**

**Explanation** Alkali metals are in Group 1 and all have one electron in their outer shell. They are solids at room temperature, stored under oil (not water), and their reactivity increases (not decreases) down the group.

---

### Question 2
What is observed when potassium is added to water?

A) The metal sinks and dissolves quietly
B) The metal melts and moves slowly on the surface
C) The metal catches fire with a lilac flame
D) The metal burns with a bright white flame

**Answer: C**

**Explanation** Potassium is highly reactive. When added to water, it floats, melts, moves rapidly, and catches fire with a characteristic lilac (purple) flame. The reaction is vigorous and produces hydrogen gas.

---

### Question 3
Why are alkali metals stored under oil?

A) To prevent them from sinking
B) To keep them cool
C) To prevent reaction with air and moisture
D) To make them easier to cut

**Answer: C**

**Explanation** Alkali metals are highly reactive and would react with oxygen and moisture in the air. Storing them under oil prevents these reactions and keeps the metals safe.

---

### Question 4
Which of the following correctly shows the trend in melting points down Group 1?

A) Melting points increase
B) Melting points decrease
C) Melting points stay the same
D) Melting points first increase then decrease

**Answer: B**

**Explanation** Melting points decrease down Group 1 because the metallic bonding becomes weaker as the atomic radius increases. The outer electron is further from the nucleus, resulting in weaker attraction.

---

### Question 5
What is the product formed when sodium reacts with water?

A) Sodium oxide and hydrogen
B) Sodium hydroxide and oxygen
C) Sodium hydroxide and hydrogen
D) Sodium oxide and oxygen

**Answer: C**

**Explanation** The equation is: 2Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂. Sodium reacts with water to form sodium hydroxide (an alkaline solution) and hydrogen gas.

---

### Question 6
Which alkali metal would be expected to react most vigorously with water?

A) Lithium
B) Sodium
C) Potassium
D) Rubidium

**Answer: D**

**Explanation** Reactivity increases down Group 1. Rubidium is lower in the group than lithium, sodium, and potassium, so it would react most vigorously (explosively).

---

### Question 7
What colour flame does lithium produce when burned in air?

A) Yellow
B) Lilac
C) Crimson-red
D) Green

**Answer: C**

**Explanation** Lithium burns with a characteristic crimson-red flame. Sodium produces a yellow-orange flame, and potassium produces a lilac flame.

---

### Question 8
What is the formula of lithium oxide?

A) LiO
B) Li₂O
C) LiO₂
D) Li₂O₂

**Answer: B**

**Explanation** Lithium forms Li⁺ ions (Group 1, loses one electron). Oxygen forms O²⁻ ions (Group 6, gains two electrons). To balance charges: 2Li⁺ + O²⁻ → Li₂O.

---

### Question 9
When sodium burns in chlorine gas, what type of compound is formed?

A) Covalent molecule
B) Ionic compound
C) Metallic compound
D) Acidic compound

**Answer: B**

**Explanation** Sodium (metal) transfers its outer electron to chlorine (non-metal) to form an ionic compound, sodium chloride (NaCl). This is an ionic bond between Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions.

---

### Question 10
Which statement explains why reactivity increases down Group 1?

A) The atomic radius decreases
B) The number of outer electrons increases
C) The outer electron is further from the nucleus
D) The nuclear charge decreases

**Answer: C**

**Explanation** As we go down the group, the atomic radius increases and there are more inner electron shells. This means the outer electron is further from the nucleus and experiences more shielding, making it easier to lose and increasing reactivity.

---

### Question 11
What gas is produced when an alkali metal reacts with water?

A) Oxygen
B) Carbon dioxide
C) Hydrogen
D) Chlorine

**Answer: C**

**Explanation** The general equation is: 2M + 2H₂O → 2MOH + H₂. Hydrogen gas is always produced when alkali metals react with water.

---

### Question 12
Which property is NOT typical of alkali metals?
**Answer: B**
**Explanation** Alkali metals have LOW density (lithium, sodium, and potassium all float on water). High density is NOT a property of alkali metals - it is typical of transition metals.

## Part B: True/False Questions
### Statement 1
Alkali metals are good conductors of electricity.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Like all metals, alkali metals have delocalised electrons that can move freely and carry electrical charge, making them good conductors.
---
### Statement 2
Lithium is the most reactive alkali metal.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Lithium is the **least** reactive alkali metal.
**Explanation**: Reactivity increases down Group 1. Lithium is at the top of the group, so it is the least reactive. Caesium is the most reactive.
---
### Statement 3
Alkali metals are stored under water to prevent them reacting with air.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Alkali metals are stored under **oil** to prevent them reacting with air and moisture.
**Explanation**: Water would react violently with alkali metals. Oil is used because it prevents contact with both air (oxygen) and moisture without reacting with the metals.
---
### Statement 4
Sodium burns with a yellow-orange flame.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Each alkali metal produces a characteristic flame colour. Sodium produces a yellow-orange flame, lithium produces crimson-red, and potassium produces lilac.
---
### Statement 5
The melting points of alkali metals increase down the group.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: The melting points of alkali metals **decrease** down the group.
**Explanation**: Metallic bonding becomes weaker as atomic radius increases down the group. The outer electron is further from the nucleus, resulting in weaker attraction and lower melting points.
---
### Statement 6
Alkali metals react with water to form alkaline solutions.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: When alkali metals react with water, they form metal hydroxides (MOH), which are alkaline solutions with pH greater than 7. This is why they are called "alkali metals."
---
### Statement 7
All alkali metals sink in water.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Lithium, sodium, and potassium **float** on water.
**Explanation**: Alkali metals have low density. Lithium, sodium, and potassium all have densities less than water, so they float. Only rubidium and caesium have higher densities and would sink.
---
### Statement 8
Alkali metals form +1 ions when they react.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: All alkali metals have one electron in their outer shell. When they react, they lose this single electron to form M⁺ ions with a stable noble gas electronic configuration.
---
### Statement 9
Potassium reacts more vigorously with water than sodium does.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Reactivity increases down Group 1. Potassium is below sodium in the group, so it reacts more vigorously. Potassium catches fire with a lilac flame, while sodium only melts and moves rapidly.
---
### Statement 10
When sodium reacts with chlorine, a covalent bond is formed.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: When sodium reacts with chlorine, an **ionic** bond is formed.
**Explanation**: Sodium (a metal) transfers its outer electron to chlorine (a non-metal). This creates Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions, which are held together by ionic bonding (electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions).
---
### Statement 11
The softness of alkali metals decreases down the group.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: The softness of alkali metals **increases** down the group.
**Explanation**: Metallic bonding becomes weaker down the group as the outer electron is further from the nucleus. This makes the metals softer - lithium can be cut with a knife, while caesium is as soft as butter.
---
### Statement 12
Alkali metals react with water to produce hydrogen gas.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: The general equation for the reaction is: 2M + 2H₂O → 2MOH + H₂. Hydrogen gas is always produced, which can be detected by the "squeaky pop" test with a lighted splint.
---

### Question 1
Explain why alkali metals are stored under oil. [2 marks]

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- To prevent reaction with air/oxygen (1 mark)
- To prevent reaction with moisture/water in the air (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** Alkali metals are stored under oil because they are highly reactive. The oil prevents them from coming into contact with oxygen in the air (which would cause oxidation) and moisture in the air (which would cause a violent reaction with water).

---

### Question 2
Describe what you would observe when a small piece of sodium is added to water. [4 marks]

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- Floats on water (1 mark)
- Melts into a ball/dome shape (1 mark)
- Moves rapidly across the surface (1 mark)
- Fizzes/effervesces (due to hydrogen gas) (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** When sodium is added to water, it floats on the surface (due to low density), melts into a ball or dome shape (the reaction is exothermic), moves rapidly across the surface of the water, and fizzes vigorously as hydrogen gas is produced.

---

### Question 3
Write a balanced equation for the reaction of potassium with water. [2 marks]

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- Correct formulae (K, H₂O, KOH, H₂) (1 mark)
- Correctly balanced equation (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** 2K + 2H₂O → 2KOH + H₂

---

### Question 4
Explain why reactivity increases down Group 1. [3 marks]

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- Atomic radius increases down the group (1 mark)
- Outer electron is further from the nucleus (1 mark)
- Outer electron is more easily lost / lower ionization energy (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** Reactivity increases down Group 1 because the atomic radius increases as extra electron shells are added. This means the outer electron is further from the nucleus and experiences more shielding from inner electron shells. As a result, the outer electron is less strongly attracted to the nucleus and is more easily lost, making the metal more reactive.

### Question 7
Explain why alkali metals are good conductors of electricity. [2 marks]

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- Presence of delocalised electrons (1 mark)
- Electrons can move freely and carry charge (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** Alkali metals are good conductors of electricity because they have delocalised electrons that are free to move throughout the metal structure. When a potential difference is applied, these electrons can move and carry electrical charge through the metal.

---

### Question 8
Describe the trend in melting points down Group 1 and explain this trend. [3 marks]

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- Melting points decrease down the group (1 mark)
- Metallic bonding gets weaker (1 mark)
- Because atomic radius increases / outer electron further from nucleus (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** The melting points decrease down Group 1. This is because metallic bonding becomes weaker as the atomic radius increases down the group. The outer electron is further from the nucleus, so the attraction between the positive ions and delocalised electrons is weaker, requiring less energy to break the bonds and melt the metal.

---

### Question 9
Give two safety precautions that should be taken when handling alkali metals in the laboratory. [2 marks]

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Any two from:
- Wear safety goggles/eye protection
- Wear gloves / use forceps to handle
- Use small pieces of metal
- Have sand ready to extinguish fires
- Work in a fume cupboard
- Never touch with bare hands

**Acceptable Answer:** Two safety precautions when handling alkali metals: (1) Wear safety goggles to protect eyes from any splashes or reactions, and (2) Use forceps or wear gloves to handle the metals, never touching them with bare hands as moisture on skin could cause a reaction.

### Question 11
Explain why lithium, sodium and potassium float on water. [2 marks]

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- They have low density (1 mark)
- Density is less than that of water (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** Lithium, sodium and potassium float on water because they have very low density for metals. Their densities are all less than 1 g/cm³ (the density of water), so they float on the surface rather than sinking.

---

### Question 12
Describe the difference in reactivity between lithium and potassium with water, and explain why this difference occurs. [4 marks]

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- Potassium reacts more vigorously than lithium (1 mark)
- Potassium catches fire / lithium does not catch fire (1 mark)
- Potassium is lower in the group / has more electron shells (1 mark)
- Outer electron further from nucleus / more shielding / easier to lose electron (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** Potassium reacts much more vigorously with water than lithium. Potassium catches fire immediately with a lilac flame, while lithium only fizzes gently and does not catch fire. This is because potassium is lower in Group 1, so it has more electron shells. The outer electron is further from the nucleus and experiences more shielding, making it easier to lose and therefore making potassium more reactive.
