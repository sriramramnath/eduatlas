# Chapter 9: Metallic Bonding - Quiz Section

## Exam-Style Questions for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

---

## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Select the correct answer for each question.

---

### Question 1
What particles are present in a metallic lattice?

A) Atoms only

B) Positive ions and delocalised electrons

C) Positive ions and negative ions

D) Atoms and molecules

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Metallic lattice contains positive metal ions and delocalised electrons

---

### Question 2
What holds metal ions together in a metallic lattice?

A) Covalent bonds

B) Ionic bonds

C) Electrostatic attraction between ions and delocalised electrons

D) Intermolecular forces

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Metallic bonding is the electrostatic attraction between positive ions and delocalised electrons

---

### Question 3
Why do metals conduct electricity?

A) The ions are free to move through the structure

B) The delocalised electrons can move throughout the structure

C) The atoms are free to move through the structure

D) The protons can move through the structure

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Delocalised electrons move and carry charge through the metal

---

### Question 4
Which statement explains why metals are malleable?

A) The metallic bonds are weak and easy to break

B) The ions are arranged randomly so can move easily

C) Layers of ions can slide over each other while maintaining metallic bonding

D) The delocalised electrons prevent the ions from moving

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Layers can slide while maintaining metallic bonding, not breaking it

---

### Question 5
What is an alloy?

A) A compound of two metals

B) A mixture of a metal with other elements

C) A pure metal with a special structure

D) A type of ionic compound

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Alloys are mixtures, not compounds

---

### Question 6
Why are alloys often harder than pure metals?

A) Alloys have stronger metallic bonds

B) Alloys have more delocalised electrons

C) Different-sized atoms disrupt the regular arrangement, preventing layers from sliding

D) Alloys are compounds which are always harder than mixtures

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Different-sized atoms disrupt the regular lattice arrangement

---

### Question 7
Which of the following is an alloy?

A) Copper

B) Bronze

C) Graphite

D) Sodium chloride

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Bronze is an alloy of copper and tin

---

### Question 8
Why does sodium chloride NOT conduct electricity when solid?

A) It does not contain any charged particles

B) The ions are held in fixed positions and cannot move

C) The electrons are held in fixed positions

D) The bonding is covalent

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Ions are held in fixed positions in the ionic lattice

---

### Question 9
Which property is typical of most metals?

A) Low melting point

B) Brittle

C) Poor conductor of heat

D) Ductile

**Answer: D**
**Explanation:** Metals are ductile - can be drawn into wires

---

### Question 10
What type of bonding exists in pure iron?

A) Ionic bonding

B) Covalent bonding

C) Metallic bonding

D) Hydrogen bonding

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Pure iron contains metal ions and delocalised electrons - metallic bonding

---

### Question 11
Why is stainless steel used for making cutlery?

A) It is very light

B) It is resistant to corrosion and is hard

C) It has a low melting point

D) It is the cheapest metal available

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Stainless steel is hard and resistant to corrosion

---

### Question 12
Which statement about metallic bonding is correct?

A) It involves sharing electrons between atoms

B) It involves transfer of electrons from one atom to another

C) It involves the attraction between positive ions and delocalised electrons

D) It involves the attraction between positive and negative ions

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Metallic bonding involves attraction between positive ions and delocalised electrons

## Part B: True/False Questions (12 marks)

For each statement, indicate whether it is True or False. If False, provide the correct statement.

---

### Statement 1
Metals conduct electricity because their ions are free to move through the structure.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Metals conduct electricity because delocalised electrons are free to move through the structure

---

### Statement 2
In a metallic lattice, the delocalised electrons come from the outer shell of the metal atoms.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Delocalised electrons come from the outer (valence) shell

---

### Statement 3
Alloys are compounds formed between two or more metals.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Alloys are mixtures, not compounds

---

### Statement 4
Metals are malleable because the metallic bonds are weak.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Metals are malleable because layers can slide while maintaining metallic bonding

---

### Statement 5
Pure metals have a regular arrangement of ions that allows layers to slide easily.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Pure metals have regular arrangements that allow layers to slide

---

### Statement 6
Sodium chloride conducts electricity when molten because the ions become free to move.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** When molten, ions are free to move and carry charge

---

### Statement 7
All metals are solid at room temperature.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Mercury is a liquid at room temperature

---

### Statement 8
Steel is an alloy of iron and carbon.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Steel contains iron with carbon

---

### Statement 9
Metallic bonding only acts in one direction between atoms.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Metallic bonding acts in all directions throughout the lattice

---

### Statement 10
Adding larger or smaller atoms to a metal makes it harder for layers to slide.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Different-sized atoms prevent layers from sliding easily

---

### Statement 11
Metals are good conductors of heat because the ions vibrate faster when heated.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Metals conduct heat because delocalised electrons transfer energy through the metal

---

### Statement 12
Copper is used for electrical wires because it is ductile and a good conductor.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Copper is ductile (can be drawn into wires) and conducts well

## Part C: Short Answer Questions (12 marks)

Answer the following questions in the spaces provided.

---

### Question 1
Describe the structure and bonding in a metallic lattice.

*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Regular arrangement/lattice of positive metal ions (1)
- M2: Surrounded by delocalised electrons / sea of electrons (1)
- M3: Held together by strong electrostatic attraction between ions and electrons (1)

---

### Question 2
Explain why metals are good conductors of electricity.

*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Metals have delocalised electrons (1)
- M2: These electrons are free to move throughout the structure and carry charge (1)

---

### Question 3
Explain why metals are malleable but ionic compounds are brittle.

*(4 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
**Metals:**
- M1: Layers of ions can slide over each other (1)
- M2: Metallic bonding is maintained when layers slide (1)

**Ionic compounds:**
- M3: When layers are displaced, like-charged ions come together (1)
- M4: The repulsion between like charges causes the crystal to shatter (1)

---

### Question 4
What is an alloy? Explain why alloys are often harder than pure metals.

*(4 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: An alloy is a mixture of a metal with one or more other elements (1)
- M2: Different-sized atoms are added to the metal (1)
- M3: These disrupt the regular arrangement of ions (1)
- M4: Making it harder for layers to slide over each other (1)

---

### Question 5
Steel is used in the construction of buildings. State the main metal in steel and explain one property that makes steel suitable for this use.

*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Main metal is iron (1)
- M2: Property: hard/strong/malleable (1)
- M3: Explanation: can support heavy loads / can be shaped into girders (1)

---

### Question 6
Compare the electrical conductivity of magnesium and magnesium oxide when solid. Explain your answer.

*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Magnesium conducts, magnesium oxide does not conduct when solid (1)
- M2: Magnesium has delocalised electrons that can move (1)
- M3: In MgO, ions are held in fixed positions and cannot move (1)

---

### Question 7
Brass is an alloy used for making musical instruments. It is made from copper and zinc. Explain why brass is harder than pure copper.

*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Zinc atoms are a different size to copper atoms (1)
- M2: This disrupts the regular arrangement of ions (1)
- M3: Making it harder for layers to slide (1)

---

### Question 8
Explain why most metals have high melting points.

*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Strong metallic bonds (1)
- M2: Large amounts of energy needed to overcome these bonds (1)

---

### Question 9
State two differences between metallic bonding and ionic bonding.

*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Any two from:
- Metallic involves ions and delocalised electrons; ionic involves oppositely charged ions (1)
- Metallic conducts when solid; ionic does not conduct when solid (1)
- Metallic is malleable; ionic is brittle (1)
- Metallic bonding is non-directional; ionic bonds act in specific directions (1)

---

### Question 10
The diagram shows the structure of a pure metal and an alloy.

```
Pure Metal:         Alloy:
⊕ ⊕ ⊕ ⊕            ⊕ ⊕ ⊕ ⊕
⊕ ⊕ ⊕ ⊕            ⊕ ⊕ ◉ ⊕
⊕ ⊕ ⊕ ⊕            ⊕ ⊕ ⊕ ⊕
⊕ ⊕ ⊕ ⊕            ⊕ ⊕ ⊕ ◉
```

Explain why the alloy is harder than the pure metal.

*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: The alloy contains atoms of different sizes (shown by ◉) (1)
- M2: These disrupt the regular arrangement of ions (1)
- M3: Preventing layers from sliding easily, making the alloy harder (1)

---

### Question 11
Mercury is a metal that is liquid at room temperature. Suggest why mercury has a lower melting point than most other metals.

*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Metallic bonds in mercury are weaker than in other metals (1)
- M2: Due to larger atomic size / fewer delocalised electrons per atom / weaker attraction (1)

---

### Question 12
Describe how delocalised electrons enable metals to conduct heat.

*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: When heated, delocalised electrons gain kinetic energy and move faster (1)
- M2: These fast-moving electrons collide with ions and other electrons (1)
- M3: Transferring energy throughout the metal quickly (1)
