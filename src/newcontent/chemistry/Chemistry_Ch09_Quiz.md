## Section C: Short Answer Mark Schemes

### Question 1: Structure and bonding in metallic lattice (3 marks)

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Regular arrangement/lattice of positive metal ions (1)
- M2: Surrounded by delocalised electrons / sea of electrons (1)
- M3: Held together by strong electrostatic attraction between ions and electrons (1)

---

### Question 2: Why metals conduct electricity (2 marks)

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Metals have delocalised electrons (1)
- M2: These electrons are free to move throughout the structure and carry charge (1)

---

### Question 3: Malleability of metals vs brittleness of ionic compounds (4 marks)

**Mark scheme:**
**Metals:**
- M1: Layers of ions can slide over each other (1)
- M2: Metallic bonding is maintained when layers slide (1)

**Ionic compounds:**
- M3: When layers are displaced, like-charged ions come together (1)
- M4: The repulsion between like charges causes the crystal to shatter (1)

---

### Question 4: Alloys and why they are harder (4 marks)

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: An alloy is a mixture of a metal with one or more other elements (1)
- M2: Different-sized atoms are added to the metal (1)
- M3: These disrupt the regular arrangement of ions (1)
- M4: Making it harder for layers to slide over each other (1)

---

### Question 5: Steel in construction (3 marks)

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Main metal is iron (1)
- M2: Property: hard/strong/malleable (1)
- M3: Explanation: can support heavy loads / can be shaped into girders (1)

---

### Question 6: Comparing conductivity of Mg and MgO (3 marks)

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Magnesium conducts, magnesium oxide does not conduct when solid (1)
- M2: Magnesium has delocalised electrons that can move (1)
- M3: In MgO, ions are held in fixed positions and cannot move (1)

---

### Question 7: Brass harder than copper (3 marks)

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Zinc atoms are a different size to copper atoms (1)
- M2: This disrupts the regular arrangement of ions (1)
- M3: Making it harder for layers to slide (1)

---

### Question 8: Why metals have high melting points (2 marks)

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Strong metallic bonds (1)
- M2: Large amounts of energy needed to overcome these bonds (1)

---

### Question 9: Two differences between metallic and ionic bonding (2 marks)

**Mark scheme:**
Any two from:
- Metallic involves ions and delocalised electrons; ionic involves oppositely charged ions (1)
- Metallic conducts when solid; ionic does not conduct when solid (1)
- Metallic is malleable; ionic is brittle (1)
- Metallic bonding is non-directional; ionic bonds act in specific directions (1)

---

### Question 10: Alloy hardness explanation (3 marks)

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: The alloy contains atoms of different sizes (shown by ◉) (1)
- M2: These disrupt the regular arrangement of ions (1)
- M3: Preventing layers from sliding easily, making the alloy harder (1)

---

### Question 11: Mercury's low melting point (2 marks)

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: Metallic bonds in mercury are weaker than in other metals (1)
- M2: Due to larger atomic size / fewer delocalised electrons per atom / weaker attraction (1)

---

### Question 12: How metals conduct heat (3 marks)

**Mark scheme:**
- M1: When heated, delocalised electrons gain kinetic energy and move faster (1)
- M2: These fast-moving electrons collide with ions and other electrons (1)
- M3: Transferring energy throughout the metal quickly (1)
