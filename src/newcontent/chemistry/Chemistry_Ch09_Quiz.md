## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What particles are present in a metallic lattice?

A) Atoms only  
B) Positive ions and delocalised electrons  
C) Positive ions and negative ions  
D) Atoms and molecules  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Metallic lattice contains positive metal ions and delocalised electrons

---

### Question 2
What holds metal ions together in a metallic lattice?

A) Covalent bonds  
B) Ionic bonds  
C) Electrostatic attraction between ions and delocalised electrons  
D) Intermolecular forces  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Metallic bonding is the electrostatic attraction between positive ions and delocalised electrons

---

### Question 3
Why do metals conduct electricity?

A) The ions are free to move through the structure  
B) The delocalised electrons can move throughout the structure  
C) The atoms are free to move through the structure  
D) The protons can move through the structure  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Delocalised electrons move and carry charge through the metal

---

### Question 4
Which statement explains why metals are malleable?

A) The metallic bonds are weak and easy to break  
B) The ions are arranged randomly so can move easily  
C) Layers of ions can slide over each other while maintaining metallic bonding  
D) The delocalised electrons prevent the ions from moving  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Layers can slide while maintaining metallic bonding, not breaking it

---

### Question 5
What is an alloy?

A) A compound of two metals  
B) A mixture of a metal with other elements  
C) A pure metal with a special structure  
D) A type of ionic compound  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Alloys are mixtures, not compounds

---

### Question 6
Why are alloys often harder than pure metals?

A) Alloys have stronger metallic bonds  
B) Alloys have more delocalised electrons  
C) Different-sized atoms disrupt the regular arrangement, preventing layers from sliding  
D) Alloys are compounds which are always harder than mixtures  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Different-sized atoms disrupt the regular lattice arrangement

### Question 25
Describe the structure and bonding in a metallic lattice.

**Answer**: A regular lattice of positive metal ions surrounded by delocalised electrons (sea of electrons), held together by strong electrostatic attraction between the ions and electrons.

---

### Question 26
Explain why metals are good conductors of electricity.

**Answer**: Metals have delocalised electrons that are free to move throughout the structure and carry charge.

---

### Question 27
Explain why metals are malleable but ionic compounds are brittle.

**Answer**: Metals: Layers of ions can slide over each other while maintaining metallic bonding. Ionic compounds: When layers are displaced, like-charged ions come together, causing repulsion that shatters the crystal.

---

### Question 28
What is an alloy? Explain why alloys are often harder than pure metals.

**Answer**: An alloy is a mixture of a metal with one or more other elements. Different-sized atoms disrupt the regular arrangement of ions, making it harder for layers to slide over each other.

---

### Question 29
State two differences between metallic bonding and ionic bonding.

**Answer**: 
- Metallic involves ions and delocalised electrons; ionic involves oppositely charged ions
- Metallic conducts when solid; ionic does not conduct when solid
- Metallic is malleable; ionic is brittle
