# Chapter 7: Ionic Bonding - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

---

## 7.1 Formation of Ions

**Ions form to achieve stable electron configurations (full outer shells).**

| Type | Process | Charge |
|------|---------|--------|
| **Cations** | Metal loses electrons | Positive (+) |
| **Anions** | Non-metal gains electrons | Negative (-) |

**Ion Charges by Group:**
- Group 1 → +1
- Group 2 → +2
- Group 3 → +3
- Group 5 → -3
- Group 6 → -2
- Group 7 → -1

**Key Polyatomic Ions:**
- NH₄⁺ (ammonium)
- OH⁻ (hydroxide)
- NO₃⁻ (nitrate)
- CO₃²⁻ (carbonate)
- SO₄²⁻ (sulfate)

---

## 7.2 Ionic Bonding

**Definition:** Strong electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions.

**Formation:**
1. Metal loses electrons → cation
2. Non-metal gains electrons → anion
3. Electrostatic attraction forms ionic bond

**Writing Formulae:**
- Swap charges (ignore signs)
- Balance for zero overall charge
- Use brackets for multiple polyatomic ions

---

## 7.3 Structure

**Giant Ionic Lattice:**
- Regular 3D arrangement
- Alternating positive and negative ions
- Each ion surrounded by ions of opposite charge
- Coordination number = number of oppositely charged neighbours

---

## 7.4 Properties

| Property | Explanation |
|----------|-------------|
| **High MP/BP** | Strong electrostatic forces need much energy |
| **Solid: doesn't conduct** | Ions fixed in lattice, cannot move |
| **Molten/aqueous: conducts** | Ions free to move and carry charge |
| **Often water-soluble** | Water molecules separate ions |
| **Brittle** | Layers slide → like charges repel → breaks |

---

## 7.5 Limitations of Model

The simple model assumes:
- Perfect spherical ions
- 100% ionic character
- No electron sharing

**Reality:**
- Ions can be polarised
- Bonds can have covalent character
- Some compounds behave unexpectedly (e.g., AlCl₃)

## Key Phrases for Exam Answers

- "Electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions"
- "Ions are free to move and carry charge"
- "Strong forces require large amounts of energy to overcome"
- "Layers slide bringing like charges together, causing repulsion"

---

## Common Exam Errors

❌ Saying ionic solids conduct electricity
❌ Saying electrons move in ionic conduction
❌ Forgetting brackets around polyatomic ions
❌ Not explaining brittleness with repulsion
❌ Thinking the ionic model has no limitations
