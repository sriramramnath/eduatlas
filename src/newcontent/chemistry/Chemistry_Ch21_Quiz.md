## Part C: Short Answer Mark Schemes

---

### Question 1 (3 marks)
**Define "dynamic equilibrium" and state two conditions required.**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Definition: Dynamic equilibrium is when the rate of the forward reaction equals the rate of the reverse reaction AND the concentrations of reactants and products remain constant (1 mark)
- Condition 1: Closed system (1 mark)
- Condition 2: Reversible reaction (1 mark)

---

### Question 2 (3 marks)
**Effect of increasing temperature on N₂O₄(g) ⇌ 2NO₂(g) (ΔH = +58 kJ/mol)**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Forward reaction is endothermic (absorbs heat) (1 mark)
- According to Le Chatelier's principle, equilibrium shifts to oppose the change (1 mark)
- Equilibrium shifts to the right (endothermic direction) producing more NO₂ (brown gas) (1 mark)

---

### Question 3 (3 marks)
**Haber process equation and conditions**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Correct equation: N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) ⇌ 2NH₃(g) (1 mark)
- Temperature: 450°C (1 mark)
- Pressure: 200 atm with iron catalyst (1 mark)

---

### Question 4 (3 marks)
**Why use 450°C instead of lower temperature?**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Lower temperature would give higher yield (exothermic forward reaction) (1 mark)
- But at lower temperatures, reaction rate is too slow (1 mark)
- 450°C is a compromise between yield and rate/economic viability (1 mark)

---

### Question 5 (3 marks)
**Observations for CuSO₄·5H₂O(s) ⇌ CuSO₄(s) + 5H₂O(g)**

**Mark Scheme:**
- a) Adding water vapour shifts equilibrium left, blue colour intensifies (or blue colour remains) (1.5 marks)
- b) Heating shifts equilibrium right (endothermic), white colour appears as CuSO₄ forms (1.5 marks)

---

### Question 6 (3 marks)
**Le Chatelier's principle and effect of decreasing pressure on 2SO₃(g) ⇌ 2SO₂(g) + O₂(g)**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Le Chatelier's principle: If a system at equilibrium is disturbed, it shifts to oppose the change (1 mark)
- Decreasing pressure causes equilibrium to shift to side with more moles of gas (1 mark)
- Shifts left (towards SO₃) OR shift towards fewer moles - needs correction: 2 moles → 3 moles, so shifts RIGHT towards SO₂ and O₂ (1 mark)

**Correct answer for final mark**: Shifts to the RIGHT (towards 3 moles of gas)

---

### Question 7 (3 marks)
**Two fertilizers from ammonia and equations**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Naming two fertilizers: e.g., ammonium nitrate, ammonium sulfate, urea (1 mark)
- Equation 1: NH₃ + HNO₃ → NH₄NO₃ (1 mark)
- Equation 2: 2NH₃ + H₂SO₄ → (NH₄)₂SO₄ (1 mark)

---

### Question 8 (3 marks)
**Why use a catalyst in Haber process?**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Catalyst speeds up the rate of reaction (1 mark)
- Allows equilibrium to be reached more quickly (1 mark)
- Makes the process economically viable at lower temperatures (1 mark)

---

### Question 9 (3 marks)
**Contact Process conditions**

**Mark Scheme:**
- a) Increasing pressure shifts equilibrium right (fewer moles: 3 → 2) (1.5 marks)
- b) Increasing temperature shifts equilibrium left (exothermic forward reaction) (1.5 marks)

---

### Question 10 (3 marks)
**Equilibrium position meaning**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Equilibrium position describes the relative amounts of reactants and products at equilibrium (1 mark)
- Position to the right means more products than reactants (1 mark)
- Position to the left means more reactants than products (1 mark)

---

### Question 11 (3 marks)
**Difference between reversible and irreversible reactions**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Reversible: Products can react to reform reactants (1 mark)
- Irreversible: Products cannot react to reform reactants; reaction goes to completion (1 mark)
- Example reversible: NH₄Cl ⇌ NH₃ + HCl; Example irreversible: combustion of methane (1 mark)

---

### Question 12 (3 marks)
**Why not use very low temperatures in Haber process?**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Lower temperatures would give higher yield (exothermic reaction favoured) (1 mark)
- But reaction rate would be too slow (1 mark)
- Process would not be economically viable; takes too long to reach equilibrium (1 mark)
