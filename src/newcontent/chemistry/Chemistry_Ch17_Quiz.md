## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)



### Question 1
What are the products when zinc reacts with hydrochloric acid?

A) Zinc chloride and hydrogen  
B) Zinc chloride and water  
C) Zinc hydroxide and hydrogen  
D) Zinc oxide and water

**Answer: A**  
**Explanation:** Metal + acid → salt + hydrogen. Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂

---

### Question 2
Which salt is produced when sulfuric acid reacts with copper(II) oxide?

A) Copper(II) chloride  
B) Copper(II) sulfate  
C) Copper(II) nitrate  
D) Copper(I) sulfate

**Answer: B**  
**Explanation:** Sulfuric acid produces sulfates. CuO + H₂SO₄ → CuSO₄ + H₂O

---

### Question 3
What gas is produced when an acid reacts with a metal carbonate?

A) Hydrogen  
B) Oxygen  
C) Carbon dioxide  
D) Nitrogen

**Answer: C**  
**Explanation:** Acid + carbonate → salt + water + carbon dioxide

---

### Question 4
Which of the following salts is INSOLUBLE in water?

A) Sodium chloride  
B) Potassium nitrate  
C) Barium sulfate  
D) Ammonium carbonate

**Answer: C**  
**Explanation:** Barium sulfate (BaSO₄) is an insoluble sulfate. All sodium, potassium, and ammonium salts are soluble.

---

### Question 5
The ionic equation for the reaction between hydrochloric acid and sodium hydroxide is:

A) Na⁺ + Cl⁻ → NaCl  
B) H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O  
C) HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O  
D) 2H⁺ + O²⁻ → H₂O

**Answer: B**  
**Explanation:** H⁺(aq) + OH⁻(aq) → H₂O(l) is the ionic equation for neutralisation

---

### Question 6
Which method is most suitable for preparing copper(II) sulfate crystals?

A) Copper + sulfuric acid  
B) Copper(II) oxide + sulfuric acid  
C) Copper(II) chloride + sodium sulfate  
D) Copper hydroxide + hydrochloric acid

**Answer: B**  
**Explanation:** Copper is below hydrogen in reactivity series, so use acid + insoluble base method

---

### Question 7
What is observed when calcium carbonate is added to dilute hydrochloric acid?

A) The solid dissolves with no visible change  
B) Effervescence and the solid dissolves  
C) The solution turns blue  
D) A white precipitate forms

**Answer: B**  
**Explanation:** Effervescence (CO₂ gas) and solid dissolves (CaCO₃ + 2HCl → CaCl₂ + H₂O + CO₂)

---

### Question 8
Which compound forms a white precipitate with silver nitrate solution?

A) Sodium nitrate  
B) Potassium sulfate  
C) Sodium chloride  
D) Calcium nitrate

**Answer: C**  
**Explanation:** Silver chloride (AgCl) is an insoluble chloride. Ag⁺ + Cl⁻ → AgCl(s)

---

### Question 9
What is the correct name for the salt formed when iron reacts with hydrochloric acid?

A) Iron chloride  
B) Iron(II) chloride  
C) Iron(III) chloride  
D) Iron hydrochloride

**Answer: B**  
**Explanation:** Iron forms Fe²⁺ ions with acids (iron below hydrogen but not very reactive). Iron(II) chloride.

---

### Question 10
In the preparation of a soluble salt from an acid and an insoluble base, why is excess base used?

A) To speed up the reaction  
B) To ensure all the acid is neutralised  
C) To produce more crystals  
D) To make the solution more concentrated

**Answer: B**  
**Explanation:** Excess base ensures all acid is used up, giving a neutral salt solution

---

### Question 11
Which pair of solutions would produce a precipitate when mixed?

A) Sodium chloride and potassium nitrate  
B) Sodium sulfate and potassium nitrate  
C) Barium chloride and sodium sulfate  
D) Sodium nitrate and potassium chloride

**Answer: C**  
**Explanation:** BaCl₂ + Na₂SO₄ → BaSO₄(s) + 2NaCl. BaSO₄ is insoluble

---

### Question 12
What happens to the solubility of most solid salts as the solution is cooled?

A) It increases  
B) It decreases  
C) It stays the same  
D) It depends on the salt only

**Answer: B**  
**Explanation:** Solubility of most solids decreases with cooling, causing crystallisation

## Part B: True/False Questions

**For each statement, state whether it is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.**

---

### Statement 1
All chlorides are soluble in water.

**Answer: False**  
**Correction:** Most chlorides are soluble, but **silver chloride (AgCl) and lead(II) chloride (PbCl₂)** are insoluble.

---

### Statement 2
When an acid reacts with a metal, hydrogen gas is always produced.

**Answer: False**  
**Correction:** Only metals **above hydrogen** in the reactivity series react with acids to produce hydrogen. Copper, silver, and gold do not.

---

### Statement 3
Copper reacts with dilute hydrochloric acid to produce copper(II) chloride and hydrogen.

**Answer: False**  
**Correction:** Copper does NOT react with dilute hydrochloric acid as it is **below hydrogen** in the reactivity series.

---

### Statement 4
The name of the salt formed from nitric acid ends in "-nitrate".

**Answer: True**

---

### Statement 5
In a precipitation reaction, two soluble solutions react to form an insoluble solid.

**Answer: True**

---

### Statement 6
Crystallisation involves evaporating the solution to dryness.

**Answer: False**  
**Correction:** Crystallisation involves concentrating the solution and allowing it to cool. Evaporating to dryness gives a powder, not crystals.

---

### Statement 7
Sodium carbonate is insoluble in water.

**Answer: False**  
**Correction:** **Sodium carbonate is soluble** - all sodium compounds are soluble.

---

### Statement 8
The ionic equation for the reaction between a carbonate and an acid is: CO₃²⁻ + 2H⁺ → H₂O + CO₂

**Answer: True**

---

### Statement 9
When making a soluble salt, the solution should be filtered before crystallisation.

**Answer: True**

---

### Statement 10
Barium sulfate is used in medicine ("barium meal") because it is soluble.

**Answer: False**  
**Correction:** Barium sulfate is used because it is **insoluble** and will not be absorbed by the body.

---

### Statement 11
Iron can form salts with different names: iron(II) sulfate and iron(III) sulfate.

**Answer: True**

---

### Statement 12
The salt formed from ethanoic acid ends in "-ethanoate".

**Answer: True**

### Question 1
Write balanced chemical equations for the following reactions:
(a) Magnesium oxide with hydrochloric acid
(b) Sodium carbonate with sulfuric acid
(c) Zinc with nitric acid

**(3 marks)**

**Mark Scheme Answer:** (a) MgO + 2HCl → MgCl₂ + H₂O (1 mark for correct products and balanced equation) (b) Na₂CO₃ + H₂SO₄ → Na₂SO₄ + H₂O + CO₂ (All three products required) (c) Zn + 2HNO₃ → Zn(NO₃)₂ + H₂ (Note: zinc nitrate formula must be correct)

---

### Question 2
Describe how you would prepare a pure, dry sample of copper(II) sulfate crystals starting from copper(II) oxide and dilute sulfuric acid. Include a balanced equation for the reaction.

**(3 marks)**

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Balanced equation: CuO(s) + H₂SO₄(aq) → CuSO₄(aq) + H₂O(l) (1 mark). Method points (2 marks total): Award 1 mark for each of the following (max 2 marks): Add copper(II) oxide to warm sulfuric acid **in excess** until no more reacts. Filter to remove excess copper(II) oxide. Heat the filtrate to concentrate / evaporate some water. Allow to cool and crystallise. Filter crystals, wash with distilled water and dry. Minimum expected: mention excess base, filtration, and crystallisation

---

### Question 3
(a) What is a precipitate?
(b) Write an ionic equation for the formation of silver chloride when silver nitrate solution is added to sodium chloride solution.
(c) State what you would observe in this reaction.

**(3 marks)**

**Mark Scheme Answer:** (a) Definition of precipitate (1 mark): An **insoluble solid** formed when two solutions react together OR: A solid that forms from mixing two solutions (b) Ionic equation (1 mark): Ag⁺(aq) + Cl⁻(aq) → AgCl(s) (Must show correct state symbols and no spectator ions) (c) Observation (1 mark): A **white precipitate** forms OR: White solid appears

---

### Question 4
Use the solubility rules to answer the following:
(a) Name one insoluble sulfate.
(b) Name one soluble hydroxide.
(c) Explain why sodium carbonate is soluble but calcium carbonate is insoluble.

**(3 marks)**

**Mark Scheme Answer:** (a) One insoluble sulfate (1 mark): **Barium sulfate / BaSO₄** OR **Lead(II) sulfate / PbSO₄** OR **Calcium sulfate / CaSO₄** (slightly soluble) (b) One soluble hydroxide (1 mark): **Sodium hydroxide / NaOH** OR **Potassium hydroxide / KOH** OR **Ammonium hydroxide / NH₄OH** (c) Explanation (1 mark): Sodium compounds are **always soluble** / all sodium salts are soluble. Calcium carbonate is a **carbonate of a metal other than Na, K, or NH₄** / most carbonates are insoluble. Need both parts for full mark
