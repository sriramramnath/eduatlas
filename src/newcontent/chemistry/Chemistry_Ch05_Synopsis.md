## 5.1 Writing Formulae for Ionic Compounds

### Key Principle:
**Total positive charge = Total negative charge**

### Common Ions to Learn:

**Cations (Positive):**
| Ion | Formula | Ion | Formula |
|-----|---------|-----|---------|
| Sodium | Na⁺ | Magnesium | Mg²⁺ |
| Potassium | K⁺ | Calcium | Ca²⁺ |
| Ammonium | NH₄⁺ | Aluminium | Al³⁺ |
| Silver | Ag⁺ | Zinc | Zn²⁺ |
| Copper(II) | Cu²⁺ | Iron(II) | Fe²⁺ |
| Iron(III) | Fe³⁺ | Lead | Pb²⁺ |

**Anions (Negative):**
| Ion | Formula | Ion | Formula |
|-----|---------|-----|---------|
| Chloride | Cl⁻ | Oxide | O²⁻ |
| Bromide | Br⁻ | Sulfide | S²⁻ |
| Iodide | I⁻ | Nitrate | NO₃⁻ |
| Hydroxide | OH⁻ | Sulfate | SO₄²⁻ |
| Carbonate | CO₃²⁻ | Phosphate | PO₄³⁻ |

### Crossing-Over Method:
1. Write ions with charges
2. Cross over charges (become subscripts)
3. Simplify if needed
4. **Use brackets for polyatomic ions when subscript > 1**

### Examples:
| Compound | Ions | Formula |
|----------|------|---------|
| Sodium chloride | Na⁺ Cl⁻ | NaCl |
| Magnesium oxide | Mg²⁺ O²⁻ | MgO |
| Calcium chloride | Ca²⁺ Cl⁻ | CaCl₂ |
| Aluminium oxide | Al³⁺ O²⁻ | Al₂O₃ |
| Sodium sulfate | Na⁺ SO₄²⁻ | Na₂SO₄ |
| Calcium hydroxide | Ca²⁺ OH⁻ | Ca(OH)₂ |
| Aluminium sulfate | Al³⁺ SO₄²⁻ | Al₂(SO₄)₃ |

## 5.2 Relative Formula Mass (Mᵣ)

**Definition:** Sum of relative atomic masses of all atoms in a formula.

### Method:
1. List all atoms
2. Multiply Aᵣ by number of atoms
3. Add together

### Common Aᵣ Values:
| Element | Aᵣ | Element | Aᵣ |
|---------|-----|---------|-----|
| H | 1 | Na | 23 |
| C | 12 | Mg | 24 |
| N | 14 | S | 32 |
| O | 16 | Cl | 35.5 |
| Ca | 40 | Fe | 56 |

### Examples:
| Compound | Calculation | Mᵣ |
|----------|-------------|-----|
| H₂O | (2×1) + 16 | 18 |
| CO₂ | 12 + (2×16) | 44 |
| CaCO₃ | 40 + 12 + (3×16) | 100 |
| H₂SO₄ | (2×1) + 32 + (4×16) | 98 |

## 5.3 The Mole and Avogadro's Constant

### Key Definitions:

| Term | Definition | Value |
|------|------------|-------|
| **Mole** | Amount of substance | SI unit (mol) |
| **Avogadro's constant (Nₐ)** | Particles in 1 mole | 6.02 × 10²³ mol⁻¹ |
| **Molar mass (M)** | Mass of 1 mole | g/mol (same as Mᵣ) |

### Key Points:
- 1 mol = 6.02 × 10²³ particles
- Molar mass (g/mol) = Mᵣ (no units)

## 5.4 Moles Calculations

### The Triangle:

```
         m (mass in g)
              ↓
        ┌───────────┐
        │           │
        │     n     │
        │   (mol)   │
        │           │
        └───────────┘
              ↑
         M (g/mol)
```

### Equations:
$$n = \frac{m}{M}$$
$$m = n \times M$$
$$M = \frac{m}{n}$$

### Number of Particles:
$$\text{Particles} = n \times N_A$$

### Worked Examples:

| Question | Working | Answer |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Moles in 24g C? | n = 24/12 | 2 mol |
| Moles in 36g H₂O? | n = 36/18 | 2 mol |
| Mass of 0.5 mol CaCO₃? | m = 0.5 × 100 | 50 g |
| Mass of 2 mol NaCl? | m = 2 × 58.5 | 117 g |
| Molecules in 9g H₂O? | n = 0.5, particles = 0.5 × 6.02×10²³ | 3.01×10²³ |

## 5.5 Empirical and Molecular Formulae

### Definitions:

| Type | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Empirical formula** | Simplest whole number ratio of atoms |
| **Molecular formula** | Actual number of atoms in a molecule |

### Calculating Empirical Formula:

**Step-by-step:**
1. Write masses (or percentages)
2. Divide by Aᵣ to get moles
3. Divide all by smallest value
4. Multiply if needed for whole numbers

### Example:
A compound contains 40% Ca, 12% C, 48% O:

| Element | Ca | C | O |
|---------|-----|-----|-----|
| Mass | 40 | 12 | 48 |
| Aᵣ | 40 | 12 | 16 |
| Moles | 1 | 1 | 3 |
| Ratio | 1 | 1 | 3 |

**Empirical formula = CaCO₃**

### Molecular Formula:

$$n = \frac{M_r(\text{molecular})}{M_r(\text{empirical})}$$

**Example:** Empirical = CH₂, Mᵣ(molecular) = 56

$$M_r(CH_2) = 14$$
$$n = 56/14 = 4$$
$$\text{Molecular formula} = C_4H_8$$

## Quick Memory Aids

### For Formula Writing:
**"Cross charges, simplify, use brackets"**

### For Mole Calculations:
**"Mass divided by Mᵣ equals moles"**
$$\frac{m}{M_r} = n$$

### For Empirical Formula:
**"Mass → Moles → Ratio → Formula"**

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. ❌ CaCl for calcium chloride → ✅ CaCl₂
2. ❌ AlSO₄ for aluminium sulfate → ✅ Al₂(SO₄)₃
3. ❌ NH₄₂SO₄ → ✅ (NH₄)₂SO₄ (brackets needed!)
4. ❌ Leaving decimal ratios (1:1.5) → ✅ Multiply (2:3)
5. ❌ Using Aᵣ instead of Mᵣ for compounds
6. ❌ Forgetting to multiply atoms in Mᵣ calculations
