### Question 1
What happens when a sodium atom forms a sodium ion?

A) It gains one electron

B) It loses one electron

C) It gains one proton

D) It loses one proton

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Sodium (Group 1) loses 1 electron to form Na⁺

---

### Question 2
Which ion has the same electron configuration as argon?

A) Na⁺

B) Mg²⁺

C) Cl⁻

D) All of the above

**Answer: D**
**Explanation:** Na⁺ (2,8), Mg²⁺ (2,8), Cl⁻ (2,8,8) all have same config as Ar (2,8,8) or Ne (2,8) - Cl⁻ matches Ar exactly

---

### Question 3
What is the formula of calcium phosphate?

A) CaPO₄

B) Ca₂PO₄

C) Ca₃(PO₄)₂

D) Ca(PO₄)₂

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Ca²⁺ + PO₄³⁻ = Ca₃(PO₄)₂ (swap and balance: 3×2 and 2×3)

---

### Question 4
In an ionic lattice, each ion is surrounded by:

A) Ions of the same charge

B) Ions of the opposite charge

C) Neutral atoms

D) Molecules

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Each ion is surrounded by ions of opposite charge

---

### Question 5
Why do ionic compounds have high melting points?

A) They have weak forces between ions

B) Ions are held together by strong covalent bonds

C) Strong electrostatic forces need large amounts of energy to overcome

D) Ions are very heavy

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Strong electrostatic attraction requires large amounts of energy to overcome

---

### Question 6
Which statement about ionic compounds is correct?

A) They conduct electricity when solid

B) They conduct electricity when molten

C) They have low melting points

D) They are always insoluble in water

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Ions are free to move when molten, allowing conduction

---

### Question 7
What is the charge on the sulfate ion?

A) -1

B) -2

C) +2

D) +4

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Sulfate = SO₄²⁻

---

### Question 8
Why are ionic crystals brittle?

A) The ions are weakly bonded

B) Layers slide and like charges repel

C) The ions are too heavy

D) They contain water molecules

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** When hit, layers slide, bringing like charges together causing repulsion

---

### Question 9
Which compound would you expect to have the highest melting point?

A) NaCl

B) KCl

C) MgO

D) NaBr

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** MgO has ions with higher charges (Mg²⁺, O²⁻) so stronger attraction than NaCl (Na⁺, Cl⁻)

---

### Question 10
When sodium chloride dissolves in water:

A) NaCl molecules are formed

B) The lattice breaks up and ions become free to move

C) Covalent bonds are broken

D) The solution cannot conduct electricity

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Water molecules separate the ions, making them free to move

---

### Question 11
Which of the following is NOT a limitation of the ionic model?

A) Ions are not perfect spheres

B) Some bonds have covalent character

C) Ionic compounds cannot exist

D) Some compounds show intermediate behaviour

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Ionic compounds DO exist - this is not a limitation

---

### Question 12
What is the coordination number in sodium chloride?

A) 4

B) 6

C) 8

D) 12

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Each Na⁺ is surrounded by 6 Cl⁻ ions (and vice versa)

## Part B: True or False (12 marks)

**For each statement, write TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, write the correct statement.**

---

### Statement 1
Metals form negative ions by gaining electrons.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Metals form positive ions (cations) by losing electrons

---

### Statement 2
The ionic bond is the electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Correct definition of ionic bonding

---

### Statement 3
Solid ionic compounds conduct electricity because electrons can move through the lattice.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Solid ionic compounds do NOT conduct electricity because ions cannot move

---

### Statement 4
The formula of magnesium oxide is MgO.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Correct formula for magnesium oxide

---

### Statement 5
In a sodium chloride lattice, each sodium ion is surrounded by 4 chloride ions.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Each sodium ion is surrounded by 6 chloride ions

---

### Statement 6
Ionic compounds are always soluble in water.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Many ionic compounds are soluble, but not all (e.g., AgCl, BaSO₄ are insoluble)

---

### Statement 7
A chloride ion (Cl⁻) has the same electron configuration as argon.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Cl⁻ (2,8,8) has the same electron configuration as argon

---

### Statement 8
Transition metals can form ions with different charges.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Transition metals exhibit variable oxidation states

---

### Statement 9
When writing formulae, the charge on one ion becomes the subscript of the other ion.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Correct ionic formula convention

---

### Statement 10
Ionic compounds exist as discrete molecules.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Ionic compounds exist as giant lattice structures, not molecules

---

### Statement 11
The ionic model explains all properties of ionic compounds perfectly.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** The ionic model has limitations - ions aren't perfect spheres and bonds can have covalent character

---

### Statement 12
Ionic compounds with higher charges on their ions generally have higher melting points.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Higher ionic charges lead to stronger electrostatic forces

### Question 1
Explain how the following atoms form ions: (4 marks)

a) Lithium (Li)

b) Oxygen (O)

c) Aluminium (Al)

d) Fluorine (F)

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
a) Lithium: Loses 1 electron to form Li⁺ (1 mark for electron loss, positive ion)
b) Oxygen: Gains 2 electrons to form O²⁻ (1 mark for electron gain, -2 charge)
c) Aluminium: Loses 3 electrons to form Al³⁺ (1 mark)
d) Fluorine: Gains 1 electron to form F⁻ (1 mark)

---

### Question 2
Write the formulae for the following ionic compounds: (6 marks)

a) Sodium oxide

b) Magnesium nitrate

c) Aluminium sulfate

d) Iron(III) chloride

e) Ammonium carbonate

f) Copper(II) hydroxide

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
a) Na₂O (1 mark)
b) Mg(NO₃)₂ (1 mark - brackets needed)
c) Al₂(SO₄)₃ (1 mark - brackets needed)
d) FeCl₃ (1 mark)
e) (NH₄)₂CO₃ (1 mark - brackets needed)
f) Cu(OH)₂ (1 mark - brackets needed)

---

### Question 3
Draw dot and cross diagrams to show the formation of: (4 marks)

a) Sodium chloride from sodium and chlorine atoms

b) Magnesium oxide from magnesium and oxygen atoms

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
a) NaCl formation: Na atom with one outer electron (dot or cross) (1 mark); Cl atom with seven outer electrons (1 mark); arrow showing electron transfer; Na⁺ with no outer electrons, Cl⁻ with eight outer electrons (different symbols)
b) MgO formation: Mg atom with two outer electrons (1 mark); O atom with six outer electrons (1 mark); two electrons transferred; Mg²⁺ with no outer electrons, O²⁻ with eight outer electrons

---

### Question 4
Explain, in terms of structure and bonding, why: (6 marks)

a) Ionic compounds have high melting points.

b) Ionic compounds conduct electricity when molten but not when solid.

c) Ionic crystals are brittle.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
a) High melting points: Strong electrostatic forces between oppositely charged ions (1 mark); large amounts of energy needed to overcome these forces (1 mark)
b) Conductivity: Solid - ions are fixed in the lattice and cannot move (1 mark); Molten - ions are free to move and carry charge (1 mark)
c) Brittle: When hit, layers of ions slide past each other (1 mark); this brings like charges together, causing repulsion and the crystal breaks (1 mark)

---

### Question 5
Compare the melting points of sodium chloride (NaCl) and magnesium oxide (MgO). Explain your answer in terms of the ions involved. (3 marks)

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
MgO has a higher melting point than NaCl (1 mark); Mg²⁺ and O²⁻ have charges of +2 and -2 (higher charges) compared to Na⁺ and Cl⁻ (+1 and -1) (1 mark); stronger electrostatic attraction in MgO requires more energy to overcome (1 mark)

---

### Question 6
The table shows some properties of four substances: (4 marks)

| Substance | Melting point (°C) | Conducts when solid? | Conducts when molten? |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| A | 801 | No | Yes |
| B | 1610 | Yes | Yes |
| C | -78 | No | No |
| D | 98 | Yes | Yes |

a) Which substance is ionic? Explain your answer.

b) Which substance could be a metal? Explain your answer.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
a) Substance A is ionic (1 mark); Reason: High melting point (801°C), does not conduct when solid but does conduct when molten (1 mark)
b) Substance B or D could be a metal (1 mark); Reason: Conducts electricity when solid - metals have delocalised electrons that can move (1 mark)

### Question 7
Describe the structure of a sodium chloride crystal. Include the following terms in your answer: (4 marks)
- Lattice
- Electrostatic attraction
- Coordination number
- Regular arrangement

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Sodium chloride forms a giant ionic lattice (1 mark); Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions are held together by strong electrostatic attraction (1 mark); ions are arranged in a regular arrangement with alternating charges (1 mark); each ion has a coordination number of 6 (surrounded by 6 ions of opposite charge) (1 mark)

---

### Question 8
State two limitations of the simple ionic model and give an example for each. (4 marks)

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
1. Ions are not perfect spheres: Example - Large anions can be polarised by small cations (2 marks)
2. Bonds can have covalent character: Example - AgCl shows some covalent character due to polarisation (2 marks)
(2 marks per limitation with example)

---

### Question 9
Aluminium chloride (AlCl₃) sublimes at 178°C and behaves more like a covalent compound than an ionic compound. Explain why this is unexpected and what it tells us about the limitations of the ionic model. (3 marks)

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
AlCl₃ is expected to be ionic (Al³⁺ and Cl⁻) (1 mark); however, it sublimes at a relatively low temperature and behaves like a covalent compound (1 mark); this shows that the ionic model has limitations - some ionic compounds have covalent character (1 mark)

### Question 12
A student investigates the electrical conductivity of sodium chloride. They set up a circuit with solid NaCl between two electrodes and observe that the bulb does not light. They then heat the NaCl until it melts and the bulb lights up. (3 marks)

a) Explain why the bulb does not light with solid NaCl.

b) Explain why the bulb lights up when the NaCl melts.

c) What particles carry the charge in molten NaCl?

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
a) Solid NaCl does not conduct because: ions are held fixed in the lattice and cannot move (1 mark)
b) Molten NaCl conducts because: ions are free to move and carry charge (1 mark)
c) Ions (Na⁺ and Cl⁻) carry the charge (1 mark)
