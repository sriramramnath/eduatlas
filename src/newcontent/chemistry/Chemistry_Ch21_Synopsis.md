## 🔑 Key Concepts at a Glance

### Reversible Reactions
- Reactions that can proceed in **both directions**
- Notation: **⇌** (reversible arrow)
- Example: NH₄Cl(s) ⇌ NH₃(g) + HCl(g)

### Chemical Equilibrium
- **Definition**: Rate of forward reaction = Rate of reverse reaction
- **Dynamic**: Both reactions continue to occur
- **Requires**: Closed system
- **Concentrations**: Remain constant (NOT necessarily equal)

---

## ⚖️ Le Chatelier's Principle

**"If a system at equilibrium is disturbed, it shifts to oppose the change"**

---

## 📊 Quick Reference: Effect of Changes

### Concentration Changes
| Change | Shift |
|--------|-------|
| ↑ Reactant | Right (→) |
| ↓ Reactant | Left (←) |
| ↑ Product | Left (←) |
| ↓ Product | Right (→) |

### Temperature Changes
| Forward Reaction | ↑ Temperature | ↓ Temperature |
|-----------------|---------------|---------------|
| **Exothermic** (ΔH -ve) | ← Left | → Right |
| **Endothermic** (ΔH +ve) | → Right | ← Left |

**Memory trick**: System shifts in the **endothermic direction** when temperature increases.

### Pressure Changes (Gases Only)
| Change | Shift Direction |
|--------|-----------------|
| ↑ Pressure | Towards **fewer** gas moles |
| ↓ Pressure | Towards **more** gas moles |

**Same gas moles both sides → No effect**

### Catalysts
- ⚡ **Speeds up** reaching equilibrium
- ❌ **NO effect** on equilibrium position
- ❌ **NO effect** on product yield

## 🏭 The Haber Process

**Reaction**: N₂ + 3H₂ ⇌ 2NH₃ (ΔH = -92 kJ/mol, exothermic)

### Conditions - COMPROMISE Approach
| Condition | Used | Why? |
|-----------|------|------|
| **Temperature** | 450°C | Lower temp → better yield but too slow |
| **Pressure** | 200 atm | Higher pressure → better yield but expensive/dangerous |
| **Catalyst** | Iron (Fe) | Speeds up reaction, reduces costs |

### Raw Materials
- **Nitrogen**: From air (fractional distillation)
- **Hydrogen**: From natural gas/methane

### Uses of Ammonia
- Fertilizers (ammonium nitrate, ammonium sulfate)
- Nitric acid production
- Nylon production

## 📝 Essential Equations to Know

### Haber Process
```
N₂(g) + 3H₂(g) ⇌ 2NH₃(g)
```

### Reversible Reaction Examples
```
NH₄Cl(s) ⇌ NH₃(g) + HCl(g)
CuSO₄(s) + 5H₂O(l) ⇌ CuSO₄·5H₂O(s)
2NO₂(g) ⇌ N₂O₄(g)
```

### Contact Process (Stage 2)
```
2SO₂(g) + O₂(g) ⇌ 2SO₃(g)
```

##  Common Exam Traps

1. **Equilibrium is NOT static** - reactions continue (dynamic)
2. **Concentrations are constant, NOT equal**
3. **Equilibrium requires CLOSED system**
4. **Catalyst does NOT change equilibrium position**
5. **Pressure only affects equilibria with gases AND different mole numbers**
6. **Haber conditions are COMPROMISE, not "best"**

## 🎯 Memory Aids

**Le Chatelier's Principle Summary:**
- More reactant → shifts right
- More product → shifts left  
- Hotter → shifts endothermic direction
- Higher pressure → shifts to fewer gas moles

**Haber Process (4-2-450-200-Fe):**
- 4 moles → 2 moles
- 450°C temperature
- 200 atm pressure
- Iron catalyst
