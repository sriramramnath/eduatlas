# IGCSE Chemistry Chapter 23: Crude Oil

## Chapter Synopsis – Quick Revision Summary
**Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry**

---

## Overview

Crude oil is a **finite fossil fuel** formed from ancient marine organisms over millions of years. It is a mixture of **hydrocarbons** (mostly alkanes) that must be separated through **fractional distillation** to produce useful fractions. **Cracking** converts less useful long-chain hydrocarbons into more valuable short-chain products.

---

## Key Points at a Glance

### 1. Formation of Crude Oil

| Aspect | Details |
|--------|---------|
| **Source** | Ancient marine organisms (plankton, algae) |
| **Conditions** | High pressure, high temperature, no oxygen |
| **Timeframe** | Millions of years |
| **Nature** | Finite, non-renewable resource |

### 2. Composition

- **Hydrocarbons** – compounds containing only C and H
- Mostly **alkanes** (saturated hydrocarbons)
- Range from C₁ to C₇₀+ chain lengths

### 3. Fractional Distillation

```
TOP OF COLUMN (cooler)     →  Small molecules, low boiling point
                                    ↓
         Refinery Gas        →  C₁-C₄   (LPG)
         Gasoline/Petrol     →  C₅-C₁₀  (car fuel)
         Naphtha             →  C₆-C₁₂  (chemicals)
         Kerosene            →  C₁₀-C₁₆ (jet fuel)
         Diesel              →  C₁₅-C₂₀ (heavy vehicles)
         Fuel Oil            →  C₂₀-C₃₀ (ships, power stations)
         Lubricating Oil     →  C₂₀-C₄₀ (engine oil)
         Bitumen             →  C₄₀+    (roads, roofing)
                                    ↓
BOTTOM OF COLUMN (hotter)   →  Large molecules, high boiling point
```

### 4. Properties Trends

As chain length **increases**:

| Property | Trend |
|----------|-------|
| Boiling Point | ↑ Increases |
| Viscosity | ↑ Increases |
| Volatility | ↓ Decreases |
| Flammability | ↓ Decreases |

**Reason:** More electrons → stronger van der Waals forces between molecules

### 5. Cracking

**Definition:** Breaking long-chain hydrocarbons into shorter chains

| Type | Conditions | Products |
|------|------------|----------|
| **Thermal** | High temp (450-750°C), high pressure | Alkenes |
| **Catalytic** | Moderate temp (450-550°C), zeolite catalyst | Branched alkanes, alkenes |

**General Equation:**
```
Long alkane → Shorter alkane + Alkene
C₁₄H₃₀     → C₈H₁₈          + C₆H₁₂
```

### 6. Combustion

| Type | Oxygen | Products | Flame |
|------|--------|----------|-------|
| **Complete** | Plentiful | CO₂ + H₂O | Blue |
| **Incomplete** | Limited | CO + H₂O or C + H₂O | Yellow/orange, sooty |

---

## Essential Equations

**Complete Combustion (Methane):**
```
CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O
```

**Incomplete Combustion:**
```
2CH₄ + 3O₂ → 2CO + 4H₂O
CH₄ + O₂ → C + 2H₂O
```

**Cracking Example:**
```
C₁₀H₂₂ → C₈H₁₈ + C₂H₄
```

## Must-Know Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Hydrocarbon** | Compound containing only carbon and hydrogen |
| **Fraction** | Group of hydrocarbons with similar boiling points |
| **Cracking** | Breaking long-chain hydrocarbons into shorter chains |
| **Viscosity** | Resistance to flow |
| **Finite resource** | Cannot be replaced once used |

---

## Exam Alert – Common Mistakes

❌ Crude oil formed from **dinosaurs** → ✅ From **marine plankton/algae**
❌ Fractions have **fixed** boiling points → ✅ Boiling point **ranges**
❌ Higher BP due to **stronger covalent bonds** → ✅ Stronger **intermolecular forces**
❌ Cracking produces **only alkanes** → ✅ **Alkanes AND alkenes**
❌ Column is **hotter at top** → ✅ **Hotter at bottom**
