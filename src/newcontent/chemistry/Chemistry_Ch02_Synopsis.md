# Chapter 2: Elements, Compounds and Mixtures
## Concise Synopsis - Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

---

## 1. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Element** | Pure substance containing only one type of atom; cannot be broken down chemically |
| **Compound** | Two or more elements chemically combined in fixed proportions |
| **Mixture** | Two or more substances physically mixed together; no chemical bonding |
| **Molecule** | Two or more atoms covalently bonded together |
| **Lattice** | Regular 3D arrangement of particles in a solid |

---

## 2. Elements: Classification

| Property | Metals | Non-metals |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Appearance | Shiny (lustrous) | Dull |
| State at room temp | Mostly solids (Hg = liquid) | Solids, liquids, gases |
| Conductivity | Good conductors | Poor conductors (except graphite) |
| Malleability | Malleable (hammered into shape) | Brittle |
| Ductility | Ductile (drawn into wires) | Non-ductile |
| Melting point | Generally high | Generally low |
| Density | High | Low |
| Examples | Fe, Cu, Au, Al | C, O, N, S, Cl |

**Metalloids**: Elements with intermediate properties (Si, Ge, B, As) - used in semiconductors

---

## 3. Compounds vs Mixtures

| Feature | Compound | Mixture |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Composition | Fixed proportions | Variable proportions |
| Bonding | Chemical bonds present | No chemical bonds |
| Properties | Different from constituent elements | Components retain original properties |
| Separation | Chemical methods only | Physical methods |
| Melting/Boiling point | Fixed | Variable (range) |
| Energy change | Released when formed | No energy change |

---

## 4. Examples of Property Changes

| Compound | Elements | Element Properties | Compound Properties |
|----------|----------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Water (H₂O) | H₂ + O₂ | H₂: flammable gas; O₂: supports combustion | Liquid, extinguishes fire |
| Sodium chloride (NaCl) | Na + Cl₂ | Na: reactive metal; Cl₂: toxic green gas | White solid, edible salt |
| Carbon dioxide (CO₂) | C + O₂ | C: black solid; O₂: colourless gas | Colourless gas, fire extinguisher |
| Iron sulfide (FeS) | Fe + S | Fe: magnetic metal; S: yellow powder | Black solid, non-magnetic |

---

## 5. Types of Mixtures

| Type | Description | Examples |
|------|-------------|----------|
| **Homogeneous** | Uniform throughout | Salt solution, air, alloys |
| **Heterogeneous** | Non-uniform, visible regions | Sand + water, oil + water, iron + sulfur |

---

## 6. Separation Techniques Summary

| Technique | Principle | Separates | Key Terms |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| **Filtration** | Particle size/solubility | Insoluble solid from liquid | Residue, filtrate |
| **Crystallisation** | Solubility differences | Solid from solution | Saturated solution |
| **Simple distillation** | Boiling point differences | Liquid from solution | Distillate |
| **Fractional distillation** | Similar boiling points | Liquid mixtures | Fractionating column |
| **Chromatography** | Solubility & adsorption | Mixtures of solutes | Rf value, mobile/stationary phase |
| **Evaporation** | Volatility | Dissolved solid from solution | None |
| **Magnetic separation** | Magnetic properties | Magnetic from non-magnetic | None |
| **Separating funnel** | Density/immiscibility | Immiscible liquids | Layers |

---

## 7. Chromatography: Key Points

### Setup
- Baseline drawn in **pencil** (ink would dissolve)
- Spot of mixture on baseline
- Solvent level below baseline
- Solvent rises by capillary action

### Rf Value
$$R_f = \frac{\text{distance moved by substance}}{\text{distance moved by solvent}}$$

- Always between 0 and 1
- Used to identify substances by comparison
- Same substance = same Rf value (in same solvent)

---

## 8. Distillation Comparison

| Feature | Simple Distillation | Fractional Distillation |
|---------|---------------------|------------------------|
| Purpose | Separate liquid from dissolved solid | Separate miscible liquids |
| Apparatus | Condenser only | Fractionating column + condenser |
| Boiling points | Large difference needed | Can separate similar boiling points |
| Example | Water from salt water | Crude oil into fractions |

---

## 9. Atom Economy (Chemistry Only)

$$\text{Atom economy} = \frac{\text{M_r of desired product}}{\text{Sum of M_r of all reactants}} \times 100\%$$

**Importance:**
- High atom economy = less waste
- More sustainable and economical
- Important for industrial processes

## 10. Practical Tips

### Filtration
1. Fold filter paper into cone
2. Wet paper to stick to funnel
3. Pour slowly, don't overfill

### Crystallisation
1. Heat to concentrate (NOT to dryness)
2. Cool slowly for larger crystals
3. Filter and wash with cold water
4. Dry between filter papers

### Distillation
1. Add anti-bumping granules
2. Thermometer bulb at side arm level
3. Water in at bottom, out at top

### Chromatography
1. Pencil baseline, not pen
2. Small, concentrated spots
3. Solvent below baseline
4. Remove before solvent reaches top

## 11. Exam Tips

✅ **DO:**
- Use "chemically combined" for compounds
- Use "physically mixed" for mixtures
- Specify the separation method based on physical properties
- Calculate Rf values from chromatograms
- Explain why compounds have different properties

❌ **DON'T:**
- Say elements in compounds are "mixed"
- Confuse homogeneous and heterogeneous
- Heat to dryness in crystallisation
- Use pen for chromatography baseline
- Forget labels on apparatus diagrams

## 12. Quick Reference: What Technique to Use?

| Mixture Type | Separation Technique |
|--------------|---------------------|
| Insoluble solid + liquid | Filtration |
| Soluble solid + liquid (pure crystals) | Crystallisation |
| Soluble solid + liquid (just recover solid) | Evaporation |
| Liquid + dissolved solid | Simple distillation |
| Miscible liquids with different BP | Fractional distillation |
| Immiscible liquids | Separating funnel |
| Magnetic + non-magnetic | Magnetic separation |
| Coloured substances/solutes | Chromatography |
