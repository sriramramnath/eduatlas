### Question 3
Why are carboxylic acids described as weak acids?
**Answer: B**
**Explanation**: Carboxylic acids are weak acids because they only partially dissociate in water, meaning only a small fraction of the molecules release H⁺ ions. This is different from strong acids like HCl which fully dissociate.
---
A) They have low molecular mass  
B) They partially dissociate in water  
C) They contain few hydrogen atoms  
D) They react slowly with all substances

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Carboxylic acids are weak acids because they only partially dissociate in water, meaning only a small fraction of the molecules release H⁺ ions. This is different from strong acids like HCl which fully dissociate.

---

---
A) Sodium ethanoate and hydrogen  
B) Sodium ethanoate, water and carbon dioxide  
C) Sodium ethanoate and water only

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Carboxylic acids react with carbonates to produce a salt, water, and carbon dioxide: 2CH₃COOH + Na₂CO₃ → 2CH₃COONa + H₂O + CO₂

### Question 7
Carboxylic acids have higher boiling points than alcohols of similar molecular mass because they:
**Answer: C**
**Explanation**: Carboxylic acids form dimers (pairs of molecules) through extensive hydrogen bonding between the -COOH groups. This requires more energy to break, resulting in higher boiling points.
---
A) Are more reactive  
B) Have more carbon atoms  
C) Can form dimers through hydrogen bonding  
D) Are stronger acids

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Carboxylic acids form dimers (pairs of molecules) through extensive hydrogen bonding between the -COOH groups. This requires more energy to break, resulting in higher boiling points.

### Question 11
What happens to the solubility of carboxylic acids in water as the carbon chain length increases?
**Answer: B**
**Explanation**: As the carbon chain length increases, the non-polar hydrocarbon portion becomes larger relative to the polar carboxyl group. This reduces the ability to form hydrogen bonds with water, decreasing solubility.
---
A) Increases  
B) Decreases  
C) Remains the same  
D) First increases then decreases

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: As the carbon chain length increases, the non-polar hydrocarbon portion becomes larger relative to the polar carboxyl group. This reduces the ability to form hydrogen bonds with water, decreasing solubility.

### Question 12
Methanoic acid has the formula:
**Answer: B**
**Explanation**: Methanoic acid (formic acid) is the simplest carboxylic acid with only 1 carbon atom. Its formula is HCOOH - it has a hydrogen atom directly attached to the carboxyl group (no alkyl chain).

### Question 13
**Statement**: Carboxylic acids completely dissociate in aqueous solution.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Carboxylic acids **partially dissociate** in aqueous solution, which is why they are classified as weak acids.

---

### Question 14
**Statement**: The functional group of carboxylic acids is -OH.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: The functional group of carboxylic acids is **-COOH** (the carboxyl group). The -OH alone is the functional group for alcohols.

---

### Question 15
**Statement**: Ethanoic acid is found in vinegar.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Vinegar contains approximately 5-8% ethanoic acid (acetic acid), which gives it its characteristic smell and taste.

---

### Question 16
**Statement**: Carboxylic acids react with sodium carbonate to produce hydrogen gas.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Carboxylic acids react with sodium carbonate to produce **carbon dioxide gas**, not hydrogen. Hydrogen is produced when carboxylic acids react with metals like magnesium or sodium.

---

### Question 17
**Statement**: Propanoic acid has 3 carbon atoms in its molecule.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Propanoic acid (C₂H₅COOH) has a total of 3 carbon atoms: 2 in the ethyl group and 1 in the carboxyl group.

---

### Question 18
**Statement**: The salt formed from ethanoic acid and sodium hydroxide is called sodium ethanoic.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: The salt is called **sodium ethanoate**. The naming rule is to change "-oic acid" to "-oate" when naming salts of carboxylic acids.

---

### Question 19
**Statement**: Carboxylic acids are stronger acids than hydrochloric acid.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Carboxylic acids are **weaker acids** than hydrochloric acid. HCl is a strong acid that fully dissociates, whereas carboxylic acids only partially dissociate.

---

### Question 20
**Statement**: Butanoic acid has an unpleasant smell often described as "rancid butter."

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Butanoic acid is indeed responsible for the characteristic smell of rancid butter and is found naturally in butter that has gone off.

---

### Question 21
**Statement**: The reaction between a carboxylic acid and a base is called neutralization.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: The reaction between an acid (including carboxylic acids) and a base produces a salt and water, which is the definition of a neutralization reaction.

---

### Question 22
**Statement**: Methanoic acid is also known as acetic acid.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Methanoic acid is also known as **formic acid**. **Ethanoic acid** is known as acetic acid.

---

### Question 23
**Statement**: Carboxylic acids can form hydrogen bonds with water molecules.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: The -COOH group is polar and can form hydrogen bonds with water, which is why smaller carboxylic acids are soluble in water.

---

### Question 24
**Statement**: When naming carboxylic acids, the carbon in the -COOH group is not counted as part of the carbon chain.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: The carbon in the -COOH group **is counted** as part of the carbon chain. For example, ethanoic acid has 2 carbons total: 1 in the methyl group and 1 in the carboxyl group.
