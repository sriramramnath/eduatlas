# Chapter 8: Covalent Bonding - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

---

## 8.1 Formation of Covalent Bonds

**Definition:** A covalent bond is a shared pair of electrons between two atoms.

**Forms between:** Non-metal atoms

**Types:**
| Bond | Shared pairs | Example |
|------|--------------|---------|
| Single | 1 | H₂, HCl, CH₄ |
| Double | 2 | O₂, CO₂ |
| Triple | 3 | N₂ |

---

## 8.2 Simple Molecular Substances

**Structure:** Small molecules with weak intermolecular forces between them

**Properties:**
| Property | Explanation |
|----------|-------------|
| Low MP/BP | Weak intermolecular forces need little energy to overcome |
| Don't conduct | No free electrons or ions |
| Often insoluble in water | Most are non-polar |

**Key Point:** When melting/boiling - only intermolecular forces break, NOT covalent bonds!

**Boiling point trend in halogens:**
F₂ < Cl₂ < Br₂ < I₂
(Larger molecules = more electrons = stronger Van der Waals)

---

## 8.3 Giant Covalent Structures

### Diamond
- Each C bonded to 4 other C atoms (tetrahedral)
- **Properties:** Very hard, high MP, doesn't conduct
- **Uses:** Cutting tools, jewellery

### Graphite
- Each C bonded to 3 other C atoms (layers)
- Delocalised electrons between layers
- **Properties:** Soft/slippery, high MP, conducts electricity
- **Uses:** Pencils, lubricant, electrodes

### Silicon Dioxide (SiO₂)
- Giant 3D network of Si and O atoms
- **Properties:** Hard, high MP, doesn't conduct
- **Uses:** Glass, sand

---

## Diamond vs Graphite Comparison

| Property | Diamond | Graphite |
|----------|---------|----------|
| Bonds per C | 4 | 3 |
| Structure | 3D network | Layers |
| Hardness | Very hard | Soft |
| Conducts? | No | Yes |
| Why different? | No free electrons | Delocalised electrons |

## 8.4 Properties Summary

| Type | Simple Molecular | Giant Covalent |
|------|------------------|----------------|
| MP/BP | Low | Very high |
| Conductivity | None | Graphite only |
| Strength | Weak | Strong |
| Forces | Intermolecular (weak) | Covalent (strong) |

## 8.5 Polymers (Chemistry Only)

**Key Terms:**
- **Monomer** = small repeating unit
- **Polymer** = large molecule of many monomers

**Addition Polymerisation:**
- C=C double bond opens
- Monomers join in a chain
- No other products

**Drawing polymers:**
1. Open double bond
2. Draw repeating unit in brackets
3. Add subscript 'n'

**Example:**
```
Ethene: CH₂=CH₂
Poly(ethene): -[-CH₂-CH₂-]-ₙ-
```

## Thermosoftening vs Thermosetting

| Type | Structure | Heated |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Thermosoftening | Linear chains | Softens, can reshape |
| Thermosetting | Cross-linked | Does not soften |

## Key Exam Phrases

- "Shared pair of electrons between atoms"
- "Weak intermolecular forces between molecules"
- "Strong covalent bonds throughout the structure"
- "Delocalised electrons can move through the structure"
- "Layers can slide over each other"

---

## Common Exam Errors

❌ Confusing intermolecular forces with covalent bonds
❌ Saying graphite has "free" electrons (say "delocalised")
❌ Saying giant structures have intermolecular forces
❌ Forgetting 'n' in polymer notation
❌ Drawing monomer instead of repeating unit
