## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

**Choose the correct answer for each question.**

---

### Question 1
Which method is used to extract aluminium from its ore?

A) Reduction with carbon

B) Electrolysis

C) Heating in air

D) Displacement with iron

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Aluminium is very reactive (high in reactivity series) and forms very stable compounds. Carbon cannot reduce aluminium oxide, so electrolysis is required.

---

### Question 2
In the blast furnace, what is the role of limestone?

A) Acts as a fuel

B) Reduces iron(III) oxide

C) Removes acidic impurities

D) Produces carbon monoxide

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Limestone (CaCO₃) decomposes to CaO, which reacts with silica (SiO₂) to form slag (CaSiO₃).

---

### Question 3
Why is cryolite used in the extraction of aluminium?

A) To increase the melting point of aluminium oxide

B) To act as a reducing agent

C) To lower the melting point of aluminium oxide

D) To prevent corrosion of the anodes

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Cryolite lowers the melting point from ~2050°C to ~950°C, saving energy and costs.

---

### Question 4
Which equation represents the reduction of iron(III) oxide in the blast furnace?

A) Fe₂O₃ + 3C → 2Fe + 3CO

B) Fe₂O₃ + 3CO → 2Fe + 3CO₂

C) Fe₂O₃ + 3CO₂ → 2Fe + 3CO

D) 2Fe₂O₃ + 3C → 4Fe + 3CO₂

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Carbon monoxide is the reducing agent in the blast furnace, reducing iron(III) oxide to iron.

---

### Question 5
During the electrolysis of aluminium oxide, aluminium is produced at the:

A) Anode, which is positively charged

B) Anode, which is negatively charged

C) Cathode, which is positively charged

D) Cathode, which is negatively charged

**Answer: D**
**Explanation:** During electrolysis, Al³⁺ ions are attracted to the negatively charged cathode where they gain electrons.

---

### Question 6
What is the main advantage of recycling aluminium compared to extracting it from its ore?

A) Recycling produces higher purity aluminium

B) Recycling uses only about 5% of the energy required for extraction

C) Recycling does not require any energy

D) Recycling is the only way to obtain aluminium

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Electrolysis requires huge amounts of energy; recycling avoids this energy-intensive process.

---

### Question 7
Brass is an alloy of:

A) Copper and tin

B) Copper and zinc

C) Copper and iron

D) Zinc and tin

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Brass is an alloy of copper (Cu) and zinc (Zn).

---

### Question 8
Which conditions are necessary for iron to rust?

A) Oxygen only

B) Water only

C) Both oxygen and water

D) Neither oxygen nor water

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Both must be present for iron to rust: Iron + Oxygen + Water → Rust.

---

### Question 9
Galvanizing protects iron from rusting by:

A) Painting the surface

B) Coating with a layer of tin

C) Coating with a layer of zinc

D) Covering with plastic

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Galvanizing involves coating iron with zinc, which provides both barrier and sacrificial protection.

---

### Question 10
In phytomining, copper is extracted using:

A) Bacteria

B) Plants

C) Electrolysis directly on the ore

D) Carbon reduction

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Phytomining uses plants to absorb copper compounds from low-grade ores through their roots.

---

### Question 11
The anodes in aluminium extraction need to be replaced regularly because:

A) They dissolve in the electrolyte

B) They react with oxygen produced to form carbon dioxide

C) They melt at high temperature

D) Aluminium deposits on them

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** At the anode, oxygen is produced which reacts with carbon: C + O₂ → CO₂.

---

### Question 12
Stainless steel contains iron mixed with:

A) Carbon only

B) Zinc and tin

C) Chromium and nickel

D) Copper and aluminium

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Stainless steel contains iron with chromium and nickel to provide corrosion resistance.

## Part B: True/False Questions

**For each statement, state whether it is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.**

---

### Statement 1
Iron is extracted from haematite by reduction with carbon monoxide in the blast furnace.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Iron(III) oxide is reduced by carbon monoxide to produce iron.

---

### Statement 2
Aluminium is extracted by heating its ore with carbon in a blast furnace.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Aluminium is extracted by electrolysis of molten aluminium oxide, not by reduction with carbon.

---

### Statement 3
Slag is formed when limestone reacts with silica impurities.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** CaO from limestone reacts with SiO₂ impurities: CaO + SiO₂ → CaSiO₃ (slag).

---

### Statement 4
Copper is always extracted from its ores by electrolysis.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Copper can be extracted by smelting or from low-grade ores by phytomining/bioleaching. Electrolysis is used for purification.

---

### Statement 5
Alloys are harder than pure metals because the different-sized atoms disrupt the regular structure.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Different-sized atoms disrupt the regular metallic structure, preventing layers from sliding.

---

### Statement 6
Rust is hydrated iron(II) oxide.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Rust is hydrated iron(III) oxide (Fe₂O₃·xH₂O), not iron(II) oxide.

---

### Statement 7
Sacrificial protection uses a less reactive metal to protect iron.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Sacrificial protection uses a more reactive metal (e.g., zinc) to protect iron.

---

### Statement 8
Bioleaching uses bacteria to produce a solution containing copper ions.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Bacteria break down ores and produce a leachate containing copper ions.

---

### Statement 9
The cathode is the positive electrode in electrolysis.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** The cathode is the negative electrode (cations are attracted to it).

---

### Statement 10
Aluminium resists corrosion because it forms a protective oxide layer.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Aluminium forms a thin, protective layer of aluminium oxide (Al₂O₃).

---

### Statement 11
Mild steel contains less than 1% carbon and is used for car bodies.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Mild steel contains less than 1% carbon and is malleable, making it suitable for car bodies.

---

### Statement 12
Solder is an alloy of tin and lead used for joining electrical components.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Solder is traditionally an alloy of tin and lead (lead-free versions use tin with silver or other metals).

### Question 1
(a) Name the ore from which aluminium is extracted.

(b) Why must aluminium be extracted by electrolysis rather than by reduction with carbon?

(c) Explain why cryolite is added to aluminium oxide during electrolysis.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
(a) Ore name: Bauxite (or aluminium oxide/Al₂O₃) (1 mark)
(b) Why electrolysis: Aluminium is very reactive (1 mark); Carbon is not reactive enough to reduce aluminium oxide (1 mark)
(c) Role of cryolite: Lowers the melting point (1 mark); From ~2050°C to ~950°C, saving energy/cost (1 mark)

---

### Question 2
The extraction of iron uses a blast furnace.

(a) Name the four raw materials added to the blast furnace.

(b) Write a balanced equation for the reduction of iron(III) oxide by carbon monoxide.

(c) Explain the role of limestone in the blast furnace. Include an equation.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
(a) Raw materials (all four): Haematite (iron ore)/Iron(III) oxide; Coke (carbon); Limestone (calcium carbonate); Hot air (oxygen). Award 1 mark for 2 correct, 2 marks for all 4 correct.
(b) Reduction equation: Fe₂O₃ + 3CO → 2Fe + 3CO₂. 1 mark for correct reactants and products, 1 mark for balancing.
(c) Role of limestone: Decomposes to form calcium oxide: CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂ (1 mark); Calcium oxide reacts with silica (acidic impurity): CaO + SiO₂ → CaSiO₃ (1 mark); Forms slag which removes impurities (1 mark)

---

### Question 3
(a) What is meant by the term "alloy"?

(b) Explain why alloys are generally harder than pure metals.

(c) Give one example of an alloy and state its use.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
(a) Definition: An alloy is a mixture of a metal with one or more other elements (2 marks)
(b) Why harder: Different-sized atoms disrupt the regular structure (1 mark); Layers cannot slide over each other easily (1 mark)
(c) Example and use: Brass → musical instruments / door fittings; Bronze → statues / medals; Stainless steel → cutlery / surgical instruments. 1 mark for correct alloy, 1 mark for appropriate use.

---

### Question 4
(a) Write a word equation for the rusting of iron.

(b) State two conditions necessary for rusting to occur.

(c) Describe two different methods of preventing rusting and explain how each method works.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
(a) Word equation: Iron + Oxygen + Water → Hydrated iron(III) oxide. Award 1 mark for reactants, 1 mark for product.
(b) Two conditions: Oxygen (from air) (1 mark); Water (or moisture) (1 mark)
(c) Two prevention methods: Painting - barrier method, prevents oxygen and water reaching iron (2 marks); Oiling/greasing - barrier method, used for moving parts (2 marks); Galvanizing - coating with zinc, provides barrier and sacrificial protection (2 marks); Sacrificial anodes - attaching more reactive metal (zinc/magnesium) that corrodes instead of iron (2 marks). 1 mark for naming method, 1 mark for explanation.

---

### Question 5
Describe how bioleaching is used to extract copper from low-grade ores.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Bacteria are used to break down low-grade copper ores (1 mark); Bacteria produce a solution (leachate) containing copper ions (1 mark); Copper is extracted from the solution by displacement with scrap iron or electrolysis (1 mark)

### Question 6
The diagram shows the extraction of aluminium by electrolysis.

(a) What is the cathode made from?

(b) Write the half-equation for the reaction at the cathode.

(c) Why must the aluminium oxide be molten for electrolysis to occur?

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
(a) Cathode material: Carbon (or carbon-lined steel) (1 mark)
(b) Half-equation: Al³⁺ + 3e⁻ → Al. 1 mark for Al³⁺ and Al, 1 mark for 3 electrons. (2 marks)
(c) Why molten: Ions must be free to move (1 mark); In solid state, ions are fixed in position and cannot carry current (1 mark)

---

### Question 7
(a) What is sacrificial protection?

(b) Explain why zinc can be used for sacrificial protection of iron.

(c) Give one example of where sacrificial protection is used.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
(a) Definition: A method of preventing rusting where a more reactive metal (1 mark) is attached to iron and corrodes instead of the iron (1 mark)
(b) Why zinc works: Zinc is more reactive than iron (1 mark); Zinc loses electrons more readily and corrodes preferentially (1 mark)
(c) Example: Ship hulls; Underground pipelines; Oil rigs; Galvanized roofs/buckets. (1 mark)

---

### Question 8
Aluminium is used for overhead power cables. State two properties of aluminium that make it suitable for this use.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Any two from: Good conductor of electricity (1 mark); Low density (lightweight) (1 mark); Resistant to corrosion (1 mark); Ductile (can be drawn into wires) (1 mark)

### Question 9
A student investigates the extraction of copper using scrap iron.

(a) Write an ionic equation for the reaction between copper(II) ions and iron.

(b) Why can iron displace copper from a solution of copper(II) sulfate?

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
(a) Ionic equation: Cu²⁺(aq) + Fe(s) → Cu(s) + Fe²⁺(aq). 1 mark for correct species, 1 mark for balanced equation. (2 marks)
(b) Why iron displaces copper: Iron is more reactive than copper (1 mark); More reactive metals can displace less reactive metals from their compounds (1 mark)

### Question 10
Compare the extraction of iron with the extraction of aluminium. Include:
- The name of the ore used
- The extraction method
- One similarity and one difference

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
| Aspect | Iron | Aluminium |
|--------|------|-----------|
| Ore | Haematite (Fe₂O₃) | Bauxite (Al₂O₃) |
| Method | Blast furnace (reduction with CO) | Electrolysis |

Similarity: Both involve extraction from oxide ores; Both require high temperatures; Both involve reduction of the metal. (1 mark)
Difference: Iron uses chemical reduction (with CO), aluminium uses electrolysis; Iron extraction is continuous, aluminium extraction is batch process; Different energy requirements. (1 mark)

### Question 11
Explain why the carbon anodes in aluminium extraction need to be replaced regularly. Include an equation.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Oxygen is produced at the anode (1 mark); Oxygen reacts with the carbon anode (1 mark); Equation: C + O₂ → CO₂ (1 mark)

### Question 12
Stainless steel is used for cutlery. Explain:
(a) What stainless steel is made from
(b) Why it is more suitable than pure iron for cutlery
(c) Why it does not rust like ordinary iron

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
(a) Composition: Iron (1 mark); Chromium and nickel (1 mark)
(b) Why better than pure iron: Harder and stronger than pure iron (1 mark); Resistant to corrosion/rusting (1 mark)
(c) Why doesn't rust: Chromium forms a protective oxide layer (1 mark); This layer prevents further oxidation/corrosion (1 mark)
