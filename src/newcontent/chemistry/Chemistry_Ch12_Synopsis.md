# Chapter 12: The Halogens - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

---

## 📌 Key Facts at a Glance

| Element | Symbol | State at RTP | Colour | Reactivity |
|---------|--------|--------------|--------|------------|
| Fluorine | F | Gas | Pale yellow | Most reactive |
| Chlorine | Cl | Gas | Greenish-yellow | ↓ |
| Bromine | Br | Liquid | Red-brown | ↓ |
| Iodine | I | Solid | Dark grey | Least reactive |

---

## Essential Points

### Electronic Structure
- **All have SEVEN electrons in outer shell**
- Form **X⁻ ions** (gain one electron)
- Exist as **diatomic molecules** (F₂, Cl₂, Br₂, I₂)

### Physical Properties
- **Melting/boiling points INCREASE** down the group
- Colours get **darker** down the group
- State changes: Gas → Liquid → Solid

---

## 📉 Trends Down Group 7

| Property | Trend | Reason |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Reactivity | **DECREASES** ↓ | Harder to attract electron (more shielding, larger radius) |
| Melting point | **INCREASES** ↓ | Stronger van der Waals' forces |
| Boiling point | **INCREASES** ↓ | Stronger van der Waals' forces |
| Atomic radius | **INCREASES** ↓ | More electron shells |

---

## ⚗️ Key Reactions

### With Metals
```
2Na + Cl₂ → 2NaCl
2Fe + 3Cl₂ → 2FeCl₃
```
Form **ionic halides** (metal + halogen)

### With Hydrogen
```
H₂ + Cl₂ → 2HCl
```
Form **hydrogen halides** (covalent gases)

### Displacement Reactions
**More reactive halogen displaces less reactive halogen**

| Halogen added | NaCl | NaBr | NaI |
|---------------|------|------|-----|
| Cl₂ | ✗ No reaction | ✓ Displaces Br₂ | ✓ Displaces I₂ |
| Br₂ | ✗ No reaction | ✗ No reaction | ✓ Displaces I₂ |
| I₂ | ✗ No reaction | ✗ No reaction | ✗ No reaction |

---

## 🧪 Tests for Halide Ions

**Method:** Add AgNO₃ (after acidifying with HNO₃)

| Ion | Precipitate | Colour | Solubility in NH₃ |
|-----|-------------|--------|-------------------|
| Cl⁻ | AgCl | **White** | Soluble in dilute NH₃ |
| Br⁻ | AgBr | **Cream** | Soluble in conc. NH₃ only |
| I⁻ | AgI | **Yellow** | Insoluble in NH₃ |

## 🛡️ Uses of Halogens

| Halogen | Uses |
|---------|------|
| Chlorine | Water treatment, bleach, PVC |
| Fluorine | Toothpaste, Teflon (PTFE) |
| Bromine | Flame retardants, photography |
| Iodine | Antiseptics, iodised salt |

---

##  Quick Memory Aids

**Reactivity Order:** "First Class Boys In" (F > Cl > Br > I)

**States:** "Gas Gas Liquid Solid" (F, Cl = gas; Br = liquid; I = solid)

**Precipitate Colours:** 
- Chloride = **White** (Cl = Clean/White)
- Bromide = **Cream** (Br = Brownish/Cream)
- Iodide = **Yellow** (I = Iodine = Yellow)

**Displacement:** "Higher displaces lower"

---

##  Common Exam Pitfalls

1. ❌ "Reactivity increases down Group 7" → ✅ **Decreases**
2. ❌ "Halogens lose electrons" → ✅ **Gain** electrons
3. ❌ Writing Cl instead of **Cl₂** (diatomic!)
4. ❌ AgCl is yellow → ✅ **White**
5. ❌ Forgetting to acidify before AgNO₃ test
6. ❌ Br₂ cannot displace Cl⁻ (chlorine is MORE reactive)

---

## 📊 Comparison: Group 1 vs Group 7

| Aspect | Group 1 (Alkali Metals) | Group 7 (Halogens) |
|--------|------------------------|-------------------|
| Outer electrons | 1 | 7 |
| Ion formed | +1 | -1 |
| Trend in reactivity | Increases down | Decreases down |
| Type of element | Metals | Non-metals |
| State at RTP | All solids | Gases → Liquid → Solid |
