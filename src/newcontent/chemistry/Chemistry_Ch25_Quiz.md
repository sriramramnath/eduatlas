### Question 1
What is the general formula of alkenes?
**Answer: B**
**Explanation**: Alkenes are unsaturated hydrocarbons with the general formula CnH2n. This reflects the presence of one carbon-carbon double bond, which means they have two fewer hydrogen atoms than the corresponding alkane (CnH2n+2).
---
A) CnH2n+2  
B) CnH2n  
C) CnH2n-2  
D) CnHn

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Alkenes are unsaturated hydrocarbons with the general formula CnH2n. This reflects the presence of one carbon-carbon double bond, which means they have two fewer hydrogen atoms than the corresponding alkane (CnH2n+2).

### Question 13
Alkenes have the general formula CnH2n+2.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Alkenes have the general formula CnH2n. The formula CnH2n+2 is for alkanes, which are saturated hydrocarbons.

---

### Question 14
Ethene is the simplest alkene.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Ethene (C2H4) is the simplest alkene because alkenes must have at least 2 carbon atoms to form a C=C double bond. There is no "methene" because a single carbon cannot form a double bond.

---

### Question 15
When bromine water is added to an alkane, the solution turns from orange to colorless.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: When bromine water is added to an alkane, the solution stays orange. Only alkenes (unsaturated compounds) decolorize bromine water because they have a C=C double bond that can react with bromine.

---

### Question 16
Hydrogenation of ethene produces ethanol.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Hydrogenation of ethene produces ethane. The reaction is: C2H4 + H2 → C2H6. Ethanol is produced by hydration (reaction with steam/water), not hydrogenation.

---

### Question 17
A nickel catalyst is used in the hydrogenation of alkenes.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Nickel (Ni) is commonly used as a catalyst for hydrogenation reactions at around 150°C. Platinum (Pt) can also be used as a catalyst.

---

### Question 18
Alkenes are saturated hydrocarbons.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Alkenes are unsaturated hydrocarbons. "Saturated" means containing only single bonds, while "unsaturated" means containing at least one double bond. Alkenes contain a C=C double bond.

---

### Question 19
The product of the reaction between ethene and steam is ethane.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: The product of the reaction between ethene and steam is ethanol. This is a hydration reaction: C2H4 + H2O → C2H5OH. The product is an alcohol, not an alkane.

---

### Question 20
Addition reactions produce only one product.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: In an addition reaction, two molecules combine to form one larger molecule. The C=C double bond "opens up" and the atoms add on, resulting in a single product. This is different from substitution reactions which produce two products.

---

### Question 21
Propene can exist as two positional isomers.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Propene can only exist as one isomer. The double bond must be between carbons 1 and 2 because there are only 3 carbon atoms. Positional isomerism is only possible for alkenes with 4 or more carbon atoms.

---

### Question 22
When chlorine adds to ethene, 1,2-dichloroethane is formed.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Halogens (like chlorine and bromine) add across the C=C double bond. Each carbon of the double bond gains one chlorine atom, producing 1,2-dichloroethane: C2H4 + Cl2 → C2H4Cl2.

---

### Question 23
The bromine water test can distinguish between alkenes and alkanes.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: This is the standard test for unsaturation. Alkenes decolorize orange/brown bromine water because the bromine adds across the C=C double bond. Alkanes have no double bond, so there is no reaction and the color remains.

---

### Question 24
Vegetable oils are hydrogenated to decrease their melting point.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Vegetable oils are hydrogenated to increase their melting point. The addition of hydrogen across C=C bonds makes the oil more saturated, which increases the melting point and converts liquid oil into semi-solid margarine.
