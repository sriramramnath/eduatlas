## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)



### Question 1
Which test is used to identify oxygen gas?

A) Lighted splint - produces a "pop"  
B) Glowing splint - relights  
C) Damp red litmus - turns blue  
D) Limewater - turns milky

**Answer: B**  
**Explanation:** Oxygen relights a glowing splint. A lighted splint is used for hydrogen.

---

### Question 2
A gas turns limewater milky. What is the gas?

A) Oxygen  
B) Hydrogen  
C) Carbon dioxide  
D) Ammonia

**Answer: C**  
**Explanation:** Carbon dioxide turns limewater milky due to formation of calcium carbonate precipitate.

---

### Question 3
Which metal ion produces a lilac flame in a flame test?

A) Lithium  
B) Sodium  
C) Potassium  
D) Calcium

**Answer: C**  
**Explanation:** Potassium ions (K‚Āļ) produce a lilac (pale purple) flame. Lithium is crimson, sodium is yellow, calcium is orange-red.

### Question 7
What colour precipitate is formed when silver nitrate solution is added to a solution containing chloride ions?

A) White  
B) Cream  
C) Yellow  
D) Brown

**Answer: A**  
**Explanation:** Chloride ions (Cl‚ĀĽ) form white AgCl precipitate. Bromide gives cream, iodide gives yellow.

### Question 8
A gas turns damp red litmus paper blue. What is the gas?

A) Chlorine  
B) Ammonia  
C) Carbon dioxide  
D) Hydrogen

**Answer: B**  
**Explanation:** Ammonia is alkaline and turns damp red litmus blue.

### Question 9
Which metal ion gives a brick red (orange-red) flame colour?

A) Lithium  
B) Sodium  
C) Potassium  
D) Calcium

**Answer: D**

### Question 10
When testing for halide ions, why is dilute nitric acid added before silver nitrate?

A) To acidify the solution  
B) To remove carbonate ions  
C) To speed up the reaction  
D) To make the precipitate more visible

**Answer: B**  
**Explanation:** HNO‚āÉ removes carbonate ions which would otherwise form white precipitate with Ag‚Āļ and give a false positive.

---

### Question 11
Which of the following statements about flame emission spectroscopy is correct?

A) It can only identify one element at a time  
B) It is less accurate than a simple flame test  
C) It can measure the concentration of metal ions  
D) It does not require the sample to be in solution

**Answer: C**  
**Explanation:** Flame emission spectroscopy is quantitative - it can measure concentration of metal ions. It can identify multiple elements simultaneously.

## Part B: True/False Questions

**For each statement, state whether it is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.**

---

### Statement 1
A glowing splint is used to test for hydrogen gas.

**Answer: False**  
**Correction:** A glowing splint is used to test for **oxygen**. A **lighted** splint is used to test for hydrogen.

---

### Statement 2
Chlorine gas turns damp blue litmus paper red, then bleaches it white.

**Answer: True**

---

### Statement 3
Sodium ions produce a crimson red flame in a flame test.

**Answer: False**  
**Correction:** Sodium ions produce a **yellow** flame. **Lithium** ions produce a crimson red flame.

---

### Statement 4
Iron(III) ions (Fe¬≥‚Āļ) give a brown precipitate with sodium hydroxide solution.

**Answer: True**

---

### Statement 5
Calcium hydroxide precipitate dissolves in excess sodium hydroxide.

**Answer: False**  
**Correction:** Calcium hydroxide precipitate does **NOT** dissolve in excess sodium hydroxide. (Only Al(OH)‚āÉ and Zn(OH)‚āā dissolve in excess NaOH)

---

### Statement 6
When testing for sulfates, hydrochloric acid is added before barium chloride to remove any carbonate ions.

**Answer: True**

---

### Statement 7
Silver iodide is a cream coloured precipitate.

**Answer: False**  
**Correction:** Silver iodide is a **yellow** precipitate. Silver **bromide** is cream coloured.

---

### Statement 8
Flame emission spectroscopy can be used to determine the concentration of metal ions in a solution.

**Answer: True**

---

### Statement 9
Ammonia gas turns damp blue litmus paper red.

**Answer: False**  
**Correction:** Ammonia gas turns damp **red** litmus paper **blue** (it is alkaline, not acidic).

---

### Statement 10
Copper(II) ions give a blue precipitate with sodium hydroxide that dissolves in excess.

**Answer: False**  
**Correction:** Copper(II) ions give a blue precipitate with sodium hydroxide that does **NOT** dissolve in excess.

---

### Statement 11
Limewater is a solution of calcium hydroxide used to test for carbon dioxide.

**Answer: True**

---

### Statement 12
Potassium ions give a yellow flame in a flame test.

**Answer: False**  
**Correction:** Potassium ions give a **lilac** flame. **Sodium** ions give a yellow flame.
