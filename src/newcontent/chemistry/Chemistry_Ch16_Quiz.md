## Section C: Short Answer Mark Schemes

### Q1. Definitions (3 marks)

**(a) An acid (1 mark):**
- A substance that **donates protons (H⁺ ions)** when dissolved in water
- OR: A substance that releases H⁺ ions in aqueous solution

**(b) An alkali (1 mark):**
- A **soluble base** that dissolves in water to produce OH⁻ ions
- OR: A base that is soluble in water

**(c) A neutralisation reaction (1 mark):**
- A reaction between an acid and a base to form a **salt and water**
- OR: H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O

---

### Q2. Strong vs Weak Acids (3 marks)

**Explanation (2 marks):**
- A strong acid **completely dissociates** in water, releasing all its H⁺ ions (1 mark)
- A weak acid **partially dissociates** in water, releasing only some H⁺ ions (1 mark)

**Examples (1 mark):**
- Strong acid example: HCl / H₂SO₄ / HNO₃ (any one)
- Weak acid example: Ethanoic acid / CH₃COOH / Citric acid / Carbonic acid (any one)

*(Award 1 mark total for both correct examples)*

---

### Q3. Titration Calculation (3 marks)

**Working:**

1. Calculate moles of HCl (1 mark):
```
Moles HCl = Concentration × Volume
Moles HCl = 0.150 × (20.0/1000) = 0.00300 mol
```

2. Use stoichiometric ratio (1 mark):
```
From equation: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O
Ratio is 1:1
Therefore: Moles NaOH = Moles HCl = 0.00300 mol
```

3. Calculate concentration of NaOH (1 mark):
```
[NaOH] = Moles ÷ Volume
[NaOH] = 0.00300 ÷ (25.0/1000)
[NaOH] = 0.120 mol/dm³
```

**Answer: 0.120 mol/dm³** (accept 0.12 mol/dm³)

---

### Q4. Titration Method (3 marks)

**Award marks for key points:**

1. **Equipment preparation (1 mark):**
   - Rinse burette with HCl solution / Fill burette with known concentration HCl
   - Use pipette to measure known volume of NaOH into conical flask

2. **Indicator and titration (1 mark):**
   - Add phenolphthalein (or other suitable indicator) to the flask
   - Add acid slowly from burette while swirling the flask
   - Add dropwise near endpoint

3. **Endpoint and recording (1 mark):**
   - Stop when indicator changes colour permanently
   - Record volume of acid used
   - Repeat to obtain concordant results (within 0.1 cm³)

*Alternative indicators: methyl orange for strong acid-strong alkali*
*Key practical details: white tile, reading meniscus at eye level*
