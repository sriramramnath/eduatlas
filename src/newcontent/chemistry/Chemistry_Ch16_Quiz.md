## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)



### Question 1
What is the definition of an acid?

A) A substance that accepts protons

B) A substance that donates protons

C) A substance that produces OH⁻ ions in water

D) A substance with pH greater than 7

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** An acid is a proton (H⁺) donor. Option A describes a base, C describes an alkali, D describes an alkali.

---

### Question 2
Which of the following is NOT an alkali?

A) Sodium hydroxide

B) Potassium hydroxide

C) Copper(II) oxide

D) Ammonia solution

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Copper(II) oxide is a base but is insoluble in water, so it is NOT an alkali. The others are soluble bases.

---

### Question 3
What is the colour of phenolphthalein in an alkaline solution?

A) Red

B) Blue

C) Pink

D) Yellow

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Phenolphthalein turns pink in alkaline solutions and colourless in acidic/neutral solutions.

---

### Question 4
Which statement about strong acids is correct?

A) They have a high concentration of acid molecules

B) They partially dissociate in water

C) They completely dissociate in water

D) They always have a pH below 1

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Strong acids completely dissociate in water. Concentration and strength are different concepts.

---

### Question 5
The ionic equation for neutralisation is:

A) H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O₂

B) H⁺ + O²⁻ → H₂O

C) H⁺ + OH⁻ → H₂O

D) 2H⁺ + O²⁻ → H₂O

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** H⁺(aq) + OH⁻(aq) → H₂O(l) is the ionic equation for neutralisation.

---

### Question 6
25.0 cm³ of 0.100 mol/dm³ NaOH is neutralised by 25.0 cm³ of HCl. What is the concentration of the HCl?

A) 0.050 mol/dm³

B) 0.100 mol/dm³

C) 0.200 mol/dm³

D) 0.400 mol/dm³

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Moles NaOH = 0.100 × 0.025 = 0.00250 mol. Same moles HCl needed. [HCl] = 0.00250 ÷ 0.025 = 0.100 mol/dm³

---

### Question 7
Which indicator is most suitable for titrating a weak acid against a strong alkali?

A) Litmus

B) Methyl orange

C) Phenolphthalein

D) Universal indicator

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Phenolphthalein is best for weak acid-strong alkali titrations as it changes colour in the correct pH range.

---

### Question 8
What happens to the pH of a solution when the concentration of H⁺ ions increases?

A) pH increases

B) pH decreases

C) pH stays the same

D) pH becomes 7

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Higher H⁺ concentration means lower pH (more acidic). pH = -log[H⁺].

---

### Question 9
Ethanoic acid is a weak acid. This means it:

A) Does not contain hydrogen

B) Is not corrosive

C) Partially dissociates in water

D) Has a low concentration

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Weak acids partially dissociate in water, shown by a reversible arrow (⇌).

---

### Question 10
In a titration, why is a white tile used?

A) To protect the bench

B) To see the colour change more clearly

C) To keep the flask clean

D) To measure the temperature

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** The white tile provides a white background to see the colour change clearly.

---

### Question 11
Which equation shows the dissociation of a weak acid?

A) HCl → H⁺ + Cl⁻

B) H₂SO₄ → 2H⁺ + SO₄²⁻

C) CH₃COOH ⇌ H⁺ + CH₃COO⁻

D) NaOH → Na⁺ + OH⁻

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** The reversible arrow (⇌) shows partial dissociation characteristic of weak acids.

---

### Question 12
A solution has a pH of 3. How many times more H⁺ ions does it have compared to a solution of pH 5?

A) 2 times

B) 10 times

C) 100 times

D) 1000 times

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Each pH unit is a 10-fold change. pH 3 to pH 5 is a difference of 2 units, so 10² = 100 times more H⁺ ions.

## Part B: True/False Questions

**For each statement, state whether it is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.**

---

### Statement 1
All bases are alkalis.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Not all bases are alkalis. Only soluble bases are alkalis.

---

### Statement 2
The pH scale ranges from 1 to 14.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** The pH scale ranges from 0 to 14 (not 1 to 14).

---

### Statement 3
In a neutralisation reaction, an acid reacts with a base to form a salt and water.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** This is the correct definition of neutralisation.

---

### Statement 4
A concentrated acid is always a strong acid.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Concentration and strength are different. A concentrated acid can be weak (like concentrated ethanoic acid).

---

### Statement 5
Methyl orange turns yellow in acidic solutions.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Methyl orange turns red in acidic solutions and yellow in alkaline solutions.

---

### Statement 6
Sulfuric acid is an example of a strong acid.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Sulfuric acid completely dissociates and is a strong acid.

---

### Statement 7
The endpoint of a titration is when the indicator changes colour permanently.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** The endpoint is when the indicator shows the neutralisation is complete.

---

### Statement 8
Weak acids have higher electrical conductivity than strong acids of the same concentration.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Strong acids have higher electrical conductivity than weak acids due to higher ion concentration.

---

### Statement 9
In a titration calculation, volumes must be converted from cm³ to dm³ by dividing by 1000.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** To convert cm³ to dm³, divide by 1000.

---

### Statement 10
Phenolphthalein is colourless in neutral solutions.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Phenolphthalein is colourless in acidic and neutral solutions, pink only in alkaline.

---

### Statement 11
A strong acid has a higher pH than a weak acid of the same concentration.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** A strong acid has a lower pH than a weak acid of the same concentration because it releases more H⁺ ions.

---

### Statement 12
The burette should be rinsed with water before filling with the acid solution for a titration.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** The burette should be rinsed with the solution to be used (not water) to avoid dilution.

### Question 1
Define what is meant by:
(a) An acid
(b) An alkali
(c) A neutralisation reaction

**(3 marks)**

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
(a) An acid: A substance that donates protons (H⁺ ions) when dissolved in water (1 mark)
(b) An alkali: A soluble base that dissolves in water to produce OH⁻ ions (1 mark)
(c) A neutralisation reaction: A reaction between an acid and a base to form a salt and water (1 mark)

---

### Question 2
Explain the difference between a strong acid and a weak acid. Give one example of each.

**(3 marks)**

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
A strong acid completely dissociates in water, releasing all its H⁺ ions (1 mark); A weak acid partially dissociates in water, releasing only some H⁺ ions (1 mark); Strong acid example: HCl / H₂SO₄ / HNO₃ (any one); Weak acid example: Ethanoic acid / CH₃COOH / Citric acid / Carbonic acid (any one) (1 mark)

---

### Question 3
A student carries out a titration between hydrochloric acid and sodium hydroxide solution.

The balanced equation is:
```
HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O
```

The student uses:
- 25.0 cm³ of sodium hydroxide solution
- 0.150 mol/dm³ hydrochloric acid
- Mean titre volume = 20.0 cm³

Calculate the concentration of the sodium hydroxide solution.

**(3 marks)**

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
1. Calculate moles of HCl: Moles HCl = 0.150 × (20.0/1000) = 0.00300 mol (1 mark)
2. Use stoichiometric ratio: From equation: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O, ratio is 1:1, so Moles NaOH = 0.00300 mol (1 mark)
3. Calculate concentration of NaOH: [NaOH] = 0.00300 ÷ (25.0/1000) = 0.120 mol/dm³ (1 mark)

---

### Question 4
Describe how you would carry out a titration to find the concentration of a solution of sodium hydroxide using a standard solution of hydrochloric acid. Include the use of an appropriate indicator.

**(3 marks)**

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Equipment preparation: Rinse burette with HCl solution / Fill burette with known concentration HCl; Use pipette to measure known volume of NaOH into conical flask (1 mark)
Indicator and titration: Add phenolphthalein (or other suitable indicator) to the flask; Add acid slowly from burette while swirling the flask; Add dropwise near endpoint (1 mark)
Endpoint and recording: Stop when indicator changes colour permanently; Record volume of acid used; Repeat to obtain concordant results (within 0.1 cm³) (1 mark)
