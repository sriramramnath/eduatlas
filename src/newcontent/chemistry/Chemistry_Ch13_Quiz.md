# Chapter 13: Gases in the Atmosphere - Quiz Section

## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What is the approximate percentage of nitrogen in clean, dry air?

A) 21%  
B) 78%  
C) 0.93%  
D) 0.04%  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Nitrogen (N₂) is the most abundant gas in the atmosphere, making up approximately 78% of clean, dry air. Oxygen is about 21%, argon about 0.93%, and carbon dioxide about 0.04%.

---

### Question 2
Which gas is collected first during the fractional distillation of liquid air?

A) Oxygen  
B) Argon  
C) Nitrogen  
D) Carbon dioxide  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Nitrogen has the lowest boiling point (-196°C), so it boils first and is collected at the top of the fractionating column. Oxygen (-183°C) and argon (-186°C) have higher boiling points and are collected later.

---

### Question 3
Which test is used to identify oxygen gas?

A) Lighted splint - squeaky pop  
B) Glowing splint - relights  
C) Limewater - turns milky  
D) Damp red litmus - turns blue  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** A glowing splint (not burning) will relight (burst into flame) when placed in oxygen. The lighted splint test with a squeaky pop is for hydrogen, limewater is for CO₂, and damp red litmus turning blue is for ammonia.

---

### Question 4
What happens when carbon dioxide is bubbled through limewater?

A) The solution turns blue  
B) The solution turns milky  
C) The solution becomes colourless  
D) Nothing happens  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Limewater (calcium hydroxide solution) turns milky when CO₂ is bubbled through it because insoluble calcium carbonate is formed: Ca(OH)₂ + CO₂ → CaCO₃ + H₂O. With excess CO₂, the solution would then clear.

---

### Question 5
Which gas is responsible for acid rain when released from burning fossil fuels containing sulfur?

A) Carbon monoxide  
B) Sulfur dioxide  
C) Nitrogen dioxide  
D) Methane  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Sulfur dioxide (SO₂) is produced when sulfur-containing fossil fuels (like coal) are burned. SO₂ reacts with water and oxygen in the atmosphere to form sulfuric acid, contributing to acid rain.

---

### Question 6
Which pollutant is produced by incomplete combustion of fuels?

A) Carbon dioxide  
B) Sulfur dioxide  
C) Carbon monoxide  
D) Nitrogen dioxide  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Incomplete combustion occurs when there is insufficient oxygen, producing carbon monoxide (CO) and/or carbon (soot) instead of carbon dioxide. CO is toxic because it binds to haemoglobin more strongly than oxygen.

---

### Question 7
What is the boiling point of oxygen?

A) -196°C  
B) -186°C  
C) -183°C  
D) -200°C  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Oxygen has a boiling point of -183°C. This is higher than nitrogen (-196°C) and argon (-186°C), so oxygen is collected last during fractional distillation of liquid air.

---

### Question 8
Which gas is an alkaline gas that turns damp red litmus paper blue?

A) Chlorine  
B) Ammonia  
C) Hydrogen  
D) Carbon dioxide  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Ammonia (NH₃) is the only common alkaline gas. When it dissolves in water on damp litmus paper, it forms an alkaline solution that turns red litmus blue.

---

### Question 9
Which greenhouse gas has the highest global warming potential?

A) Carbon dioxide  
B) Methane  
C) CFCs  
D) Water vapour  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** CFCs (chlorofluorocarbons) have a global warming potential approximately 10,000 times that of CO₂. Although they are now banned, they remain in the atmosphere for a long time.

---

### Question 10
What is removed from air before fractional distillation to prevent pipes from blocking?

A) Nitrogen and oxygen  
B) Carbon dioxide and water vapour  
C) Argon and helium  
D) Methane and hydrogen  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Carbon dioxide and water vapour are removed because they would freeze and block the pipes during the cooling process. CO₂ freezes at -78°C and water freezes at 0°C, both higher than the temperatures needed to liquefy air.

---

### Question 11
Which equation correctly represents the complete combustion of methane?

A) CH₄ + O₂ → C + 2H₂O  
B) CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O  
C) 2CH₄ + 3O₂ → 2CO + 4H₂O  
D) CH₄ + O₂ → CO + H₂O  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Complete combustion requires sufficient oxygen and produces carbon dioxide and water. The balanced equation is: CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O. Options A and C show incomplete combustion products.

---

### Question 12
Nitrogen oxides (NOₓ) are formed in car engines because:
**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** At the high temperatures in car engines, nitrogen and oxygen from the air react to form nitrogen oxides: N₂ + O₂ → 2NO. This is why NOₓ emissions are associated with vehicles, not because fuels contain nitrogen impurities.

### Statement 2
Nitrogen has the lowest boiling point of the main atmospheric gases.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Nitrogen boils at -196°C, which is lower than argon (-186°C) and oxygen (-183°C). This is why nitrogen is collected first during fractional distillation of liquid air.

### Statement 3
A lighted splint is used to test for oxygen.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: A **glowing** splint (not lighted) is used to test for oxygen. The glowing splint will relight (burst into flame) in the presence of oxygen. A **lighted** splint is used to test for hydrogen, which produces a "squeaky pop."
**Explanation**: A **glowing** splint (not lighted) is used to test for oxygen. The glowing splint will relight (burst into flame) in the presence of oxygen. A **lighted** splint is used to test for hydrogen, which produces a "squeaky pop."

### Statement 4
Carbon monoxide is produced by complete combustion of hydrocarbons.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Carbon monoxide is produced by **incomplete** combustion of hydrocarbons (insufficient oxygen). Complete combustion produces carbon dioxide and water. CO is dangerous because it is colourless, odourless, and binds to haemoglobin.
**Explanation**: Carbon monoxide is produced by **incomplete** combustion of hydrocarbons (insufficient oxygen). Complete combustion produces carbon dioxide and water. CO is dangerous because it is colourless, odourless, and binds to haemoglobin.

### Statement 5
Sulfur dioxide contributes to acid rain.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: SO₂ dissolves in atmospheric water to form sulfuric acid (H₂SO₄), which falls as acid rain. This damages buildings, harms aquatic life, and affects plant health. SO₂ comes mainly from burning fossil fuels containing sulfur.
---
### Statement 6
Chlorine gas turns damp blue litmus paper red.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Chlorine is an acidic gas and also a bleaching agent. It first turns blue litmus red (showing acidity), then bleaches it white. This two-step colour change is characteristic of chlorine.
---
### Statement 7
Carbon dioxide is removed from air before fractional distillation because it would otherwise be collected as a product.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Carbon dioxide is removed because it would **freeze and block the pipes** during the cooling process. CO₂ freezes at -78°C, which is much higher than the temperatures needed to liquefy air (below -200°C).
**Explanation**: Carbon dioxide is removed because it would **freeze and block the pipes** during the cooling process. CO₂ freezes at -78°C, which is much higher than the temperatures needed to liquefy air (below -200°C).
---
### Statement 8
The greenhouse effect is entirely caused by human activities.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: There is a **natural greenhouse effect** that keeps Earth warm enough for life. Human activities have **enhanced** the greenhouse effect by increasing greenhouse gas concentrations, leading to global warming.
**Explanation**: There is a **natural greenhouse effect** that keeps Earth warm enough for life. Human activities have **enhanced** the greenhouse effect by increasing greenhouse gas concentrations, leading to global warming.
---
### Statement 9
Methane is a more potent greenhouse gas than carbon dioxide.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Methane (CH₄) has a global warming potential approximately 28 times that of CO₂ over 100 years. However, CO₂ is still the main concern because it is released in much larger quantities.
---
### Statement 10
Limewater turns milky because calcium carbonate is formed as a soluble product.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Limewater turns milky because calcium carbonate is formed as an **insoluble** (white precipitate) product: Ca(OH)₂ + CO₂ → CaCO₃↓ + H₂O. The insoluble particles scatter light, creating the milky appearance.
**Explanation**: Limewater turns milky because calcium carbonate is formed as an **insoluble** (white precipitate) product: Ca(OH)₂ + CO₂ → CaCO₃↓ + H₂O. The insoluble particles scatter light, creating the milky appearance.
---
### Statement 11
Argon is collected from the middle of the fractional distillation column.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Argon has a boiling point of -186°C, which is between nitrogen (-196°C) and oxygen (-183°C). Therefore, argon is collected from the middle of the column, nitrogen from the top, and oxygen from the bottom.
---
### Statement 12
Electric vehicles help reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Electric vehicles produce no exhaust emissions during operation. However, the overall environmental benefit depends on how the electricity is generated - it's greatest when electricity comes from renewable or low-carbon sources.

### Question 2 (3 marks)
Describe how oxygen is obtained from liquid air by fractional distillation.

**Answer:**
- Liquid air is passed into a fractionating column which is colder at the top than at the bottom (1 mark)
- Nitrogen (lowest boiling point, -196°C) boils first and is collected at the top (1 mark)
- Oxygen (highest boiling point, -183°C) remains liquid longer and is collected at the bottom (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: describing the fractionating column (1 mark); explaining nitrogen boils first due to lowest BP (1 mark); explaining oxygen collected last due to highest BP (1 mark).

**Answer:**

- A "squeaky pop" sound indicates hydrogen is present (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: correct method (lighted splint) (1 mark); correct observation (squeaky pop) (1 mark).

### Question 4 (3 marks)
Write the equation for the reaction between carbon dioxide and limewater, and explain why the limewater turns milky.

**Answer:**

The limewater turns milky because calcium carbonate (CaCO₃) is formed (1 mark), which is an insoluble white precipitate that makes the solution appear cloudy (1 mark).

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: correct balanced equation (1 mark); identifying CaCO₃ as product (1 mark); explaining precipitate causes cloudiness (1 mark).

### Question 5 (3 marks)
Explain why carbon monoxide is dangerous to humans.

**Answer:**
- Carbon monoxide is colourless and odourless, so it cannot be detected (1 mark)
- It binds to haemoglobin in red blood cells much more strongly than oxygen (about 200 times) (1 mark)
- This reduces the blood's ability to carry oxygen, leading to oxygen deprivation, which can cause headaches, unconsciousness, and death (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: cannot be detected (1 mark); binds to haemoglobin more strongly than O₂ (1 mark); reduces oxygen transport (1 mark).

### Question 6 (4 marks)
Compare complete and incomplete combustion of a hydrocarbon fuel.

**Answer:**
Complete combustion:
- Occurs with sufficient oxygen supply (1 mark)
- Produces carbon dioxide (CO₂) and water (H₂O) (1 mark)

Incomplete combustion:
- Occurs with insufficient oxygen supply (1 mark)
- Produces carbon monoxide (CO) and/or carbon (C/soot) and water (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: complete combustion has sufficient O₂ (1 mark); produces CO₂ and H₂O (1 mark); incomplete combustion has insufficient O₂ (1 mark); produces CO and/or C (1 mark).

### Question 8 (4 marks)
Explain the greenhouse effect and how it leads to global warming.

**Answer:**
- The Sun emits short-wave radiation (visible light, UV) which passes through the atmosphere and warms the Earth (1 mark)
- The Earth emits long-wave infrared radiation (1 mark)
- Greenhouse gases (CO₂, CH₄, H₂O) absorb some of this infrared radiation, trapping heat in the atmosphere (1 mark)
- Increased greenhouse gas concentrations from human activities trap more heat, causing the Earth's average temperature to rise - this is global warming (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: incoming solar radiation (1 mark); Earth emits infrared (1 mark); greenhouse gases absorb infrared (1 mark); enhanced effect causes warming (1 mark).

### Question 9 (3 marks)
Suggest two ways of reducing carbon dioxide emissions and explain how each method works.

**Answer:**
Any two from:
- **Renewable energy sources** (solar, wind, hydro): Generate electricity without burning fossil fuels, so no CO₂ is released (1 mark + 1 mark)
- **Energy efficiency** (insulation, efficient appliances): Reduces the amount of energy needed, so less fossil fuel is burned (1 mark + 1 mark)
- **Electric vehicles**: Don't burn fuel directly, so no exhaust CO₂ emissions (1 mark + 1 mark)
- **Carbon capture and storage**: CO₂ from power stations is captured and stored underground, preventing release (1 mark + 1 mark)
- **Reforestation**: Trees absorb CO₂ through photosynthesis, acting as carbon sinks (1 mark + 1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award 1 mark for naming a method and 1 mark for explaining how it reduces CO₂. Maximum 3 marks total.

### Question 10 (3 marks)
Why must water vapour and carbon dioxide be removed from air before it is liquefied for fractional distillation?

**Answer:**
- Water vapour would freeze at 0°C and carbon dioxide would freeze at -78°C (1 mark)
- Air must be cooled below -200°C to become liquid (1 mark)
- The frozen water and CO₂ would block the pipes and damage equipment during the cooling process (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: stating they would freeze at relatively high temperatures (1 mark); air cooled below -200°C (1 mark); would block pipes/damage equipment (1 mark).

### Question 12 (3 marks)
Explain why nitrogen oxides are formed in car engines and describe one problem they cause.

**Answer:**
Formation:
- At high temperatures in car engines, nitrogen and oxygen from the air react (1 mark)
- N₂ + O₂ → 2NO, and further oxidation produces NO₂ (1 mark)

Problem:
- Acid rain: NOₓ dissolves in rainwater to form nitric acid, damaging buildings, plants, and aquatic life (1 mark)
OR
- Photochemical smog: NOₓ reacts with sunlight and other pollutants to form smog, causing respiratory problems (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: high temperature reaction (1 mark); correct equation or explanation (1 mark); one correct problem with explanation (1 mark).
