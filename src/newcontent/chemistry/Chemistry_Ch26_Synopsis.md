# Chapter 26: Alcohols - Synopsis

## Quick Revision Summary for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry (Chemistry Only)

---

## The Essentials at a Glance

### What are Alcohols?
- Organic compounds with the **hydroxyl functional group (-OH)**
- **General formula**: CnH2n+1OH
- Important in fuels, solvents, and alcoholic drinks

---

## Key Facts Table

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **General Formula** | CnH2n+1OH |
| **Functional Group** | -OH (hydroxyl group) |
| **Naming Suffix** | -ol |
| **First Member** | Methanol (CH3OH) |
| **Most Important** | Ethanol (C2H5OH) |

## Reactions of Alcohols Summary

| Reaction | Reactants | Products | Conditions |
|----------|-----------|----------|------------|
| **Combustion** | Alcohol + O2 | CO2 + H2O | Heat/ignition |
| **Oxidation** | Alcohol + [O] | Carboxylic acid + H2O | Acidified K2Cr2O7 |
| **Dehydration** | Alcohol | Alkene + H2O | Conc. H2SO4, heat |

---

## Reaction Equations

### Combustion
```
Ethanol + Oxygen → Carbon dioxide + Water
C2H5OH + 3O2 → 2CO2 + 3H2O
```

### Oxidation
```
Ethanol → Ethanoic acid + Water
C2H5OH + 2[O] → CH3COOH + H2O
Conditions: Acidified potassium dichromate
Observation: Orange → Green
```

### Dehydration
```
Ethanol → Ethene + Water
C2H5OH → C2H4 + H2O
Conditions: Concentrated sulfuric acid, heat
```

---

## Production of Ethanol

### Method 1: Fermentation

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Equation** | C6H12O6 → 2C2H5OH + 2CO2 |
| **Raw Material** | Sugar (glucose) |
| **Catalyst** | Yeast (enzymes) |
| **Temperature** | 25-35°C |
| **Conditions** | Anaerobic (no oxygen) |
| **Product** | Dilute ethanol (~15%) |

**Key Points:**
- **Renewable** resource
- **Low energy** needed
- **Slow** process
- Needs **distillation** to purify

---

### Method 2: Hydration of Ethene

| Feature | Detail |
|---------|--------|
| **Equation** | C2H4 + H2O ⇌ C2H5OH |
| **Raw Material** | Ethene (from crude oil) |
| **Catalyst** | Phosphoric acid (H3PO4) |
| **Temperature** | 300°C |
| **Pressure** | 60-70 atmospheres |
| **Product** | Pure ethanol |

**Key Points:**
- **Non-renewable** resource
- **High energy** needed
- **Fast** process
- **Reversible** reaction

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Alcohol** | Organic compound with -OH group |
| **Hydroxyl group** | -OH functional group |
| **Fermentation** | Sugar → Ethanol + CO2 by yeast, anaerobic |

---

## Exam Tips

1. **Fermentation temperature**: Must be 25-35°C - explain why (enzymes denature above 35°C)

2. **Hydration conditions**: State ALL - catalyst, temperature AND pressure

3. **Oxidation test**: Orange dichromate turns green

4. **Dehydration**: Forms ALKENE (opposite of hydration which forms alcohol)

5. **Comparisons**: Always compare both methods for advantages AND disadvantages

6. **Equations**: Balance them! Combustion of ethanol needs 3O2

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ CnH2nOH → ✓ CnH2n+1OH

❌ Fermentation at high temperature → ✓ 25-35°C

❌ Hydration uses high temperature without pressure → ✓ State both 300°C AND 60-70 atm

❌ Hydration makes ethene from ethanol → ✓ Hydration makes ETHANOL from ETHENE

❌ Dehydration makes alcohol → ✓ Dehydration makes ALKENE from alcohol

❌ Using → for hydration → ✓ Use ⇌ (reversible arrow)

## Quick Memory Aids

**"Alcohols have -OH at the end of the formula"**

**"Fermentation: YEAST makes ethanol from sugar at body temperature"**

**"Hydration: ADD water to ethene; Dehydration: REMOVE water from ethanol"**

**"Oxidation: Orange goes Green (dichromate test)"**

**"Combustion: Always CO2 + H2O with O2"**

## Reaction Pathways Summary

```
FERMENTATION PATHWAY:
Glucose --(yeast, 25-35°C, anaerobic)--> Ethanol + CO2

HYDRATION PATHWAY:
Ethene + Steam --(H3PO4, 300°C, 60-70 atm)--> Ethanol

DEHYDRATION PATHWAY:
Ethanol --(conc. H2SO4, heat)--> Ethene + Water

OXIDATION PATHWAY:
Ethanol + [O] --(acidified K2Cr2O7)--> Ethanoic acid + Water

COMBUSTION:
Ethanol + O2 --(ignite)--> CO2 + H2O
```
