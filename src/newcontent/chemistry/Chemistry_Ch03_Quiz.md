# Chapter 3: Atomic Structure - Quiz Section

## Exam-Style Questions for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

---

## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What is the relative charge of a proton?

A) -1

B) 0

C) +1

D) +2

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Protons have a relative charge of +1. Electrons are -1 and neutrons are 0.

---

### Question 2
Which subatomic particle has negligible mass?

A) Proton

B) Neutron

C) Electron

D) Nucleus

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Electrons have a relative mass of 1/1836, which is considered negligible compared to protons and neutrons (both mass = 1).

---

### Question 3
What does the atomic number represent?

A) Number of neutrons

B) Number of protons

C) Number of electrons and neutrons

D) Mass of the atom

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The atomic number (Z) represents the number of protons in the nucleus. This determines the element's identity.

---

### Question 4
An atom has 17 protons, 18 neutrons and 17 electrons. What is its mass number?

A) 17

B) 34

C) 35

D) 52

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Mass number = protons + neutrons = 17 + 18 = 35.

---

### Question 5
Which statement about isotopes is correct?

A) They have different numbers of protons

B) They have different chemical properties

C) They have the same number of protons but different neutrons

D) They have the same mass number

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Isotopes are atoms of the same element (same atomic number) with different mass numbers (different neutrons). They have identical chemical properties.

---

### Question 6
What is the electron configuration of an atom with atomic number 15?

A) 2,8,5

B) 2,8,4

C) 2,8,6

D) 2,7,6

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: Atomic number 15 = phosphorus. Following the 2,8,8 rule: 15 = 2 + 8 + 5, so configuration is 2,8,5.

---

### Question 7
Which particle is found in the nucleus of an atom?

A) Electron only

B) Proton only

C) Neutron only

D) Proton and neutron

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: The nucleus contains both protons and neutrons. Electrons orbit outside the nucleus in shells.

---

### Question 8
An element has the notation $_{20}^{40}Ca$. How many neutrons does it contain?

A) 20

B) 40

C) 60

D) 20/40

**Answer: A**

**Explanation**: Neutrons = Mass number - Atomic number = 40 - 20 = 20.

---

### Question 9
What is the electron configuration of potassium (atomic number 19)?

A) 2,8,9

B) 2,8,8,1

C) 2,9,8

D) 2,8,7,2

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Potassium has 19 electrons. Following the 2,8,8 rule: 19 = 2 + 8 + 8 + 1. The 4th shell starts after the 3rd has 8 electrons.

---

### Question 10
Chlorine has two isotopes: $^{35}Cl$ (75%) and $^{37}Cl$ (25%). What is the relative atomic mass?

A) 35.0

B) 35.5

C) 36.0

D) 37.0

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Aᵣ = [(35 × 75) + (37 × 25)] ÷ 100 = (2625 + 925) ÷ 100 = 35.5.

---

### Question 11
Which of the following is a compound?

A) Oxygen gas (O₂)

B) Water (H₂O)

C) Neon gas (Ne)

D) Chlorine gas (Cl₂)

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Water is a compound because it contains two different elements (hydrogen and oxygen) chemically combined. O₂, Ne, and Cl₂ are all elements.

---

### Question 12
How many electrons are in the outer shell of an element in Group 7?

A) 1

B) 5

C) 7

D) 8

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Group number = number of outer shell electrons. Group 7 elements have 7 electrons in their outer shell.

## Part B: True/False Questions

State whether each statement is True or False. If False, provide the correct statement.

---

### Question 13
Protons and electrons have the same mass.

**Answer: False**

**Explanation**: Protons have a relative mass of 1, whereas electrons have a negligible mass of 1/1836.

---

### Question 14
The nucleus of an atom is positively charged.

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: The nucleus contains protons (+1 charge) and neutrons (0 charge), giving it an overall positive charge.

---

### Question 15
Isotopes of the same element have different chemical properties.

**Answer: False**

**Explanation**: Isotopes have the same number of electrons and thus identical chemical properties. They only differ in physical properties due to different masses.
