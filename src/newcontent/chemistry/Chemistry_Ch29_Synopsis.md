## Key Concepts at a Glance

### What is a Polymer?
- **Polymer** = Large molecule made from many small repeating units
- **Monomer** = Small molecule that joins to form polymer
- **Polymerisation** = Reaction forming polymers from monomers

### Types of Polymers
- **Natural**: Proteins, starch, cellulose, DNA
- **Synthetic**: Poly(ethene), PVC, nylon, polyester

---

## ⚗️ Addition Polymerisation

### Definition
Unsaturated monomers (with C=C) join together **without losing any atoms**.

### Key Points
- Requires **C=C double bond** in monomer
- Double bond **opens** to form polymer
- **No by-products** formed
- 100% atom economy

### General Equation
```
n CH₂=CHR  →  -[-CH₂-CHR-]-n-
```

---

## 🧪 Common Addition Polymers

| Polymer | Monomer | Key Property | Main Uses |
|---------|---------|--------------|-----------|
| **Poly(ethene)** | CH₂=CH₂ | Flexible, waterproof | Bags, bottles, pipes |
| **Poly(propene)** | CH₂=CHCH₃ | Strong, heat-resistant | Ropes, carpets, containers |
| **PVC** | CH₂=CHCl | Rigid, flame-resistant | Windows, pipes, cables |
| **PTFE** | CF₂=CF₂ | Non-stick, low friction | Non-stick pans, bearings |

---

## 📊 Polymer Details

### Poly(ethene) - PE
- **LDPE** (Low Density): Flexible → Bags, cling film
- **HDPE** (High Density): Stronger → Bottles, buckets, pipes

### Poly(propene) - PP
- Higher melting point (~160°C)
- Can be made into fibres
- Used for: Ropes, carpets, microwave containers

### PVC (Polyvinyl Chloride)
- **Rigid PVC**: Window frames, pipes
- **Flexible PVC** (with plasticiser): Cable insulation, flooring
- Flame-resistant due to chlorine

### PTFE (Teflon)
- **Most slippery solid** known
- Chemically inert
- Heat resistant (~327°C melting point)
- Non-stick coating for cookware

## 🔬 Condensation Polymerisation (Chemistry Only)

### Definition
Monomers join together with the **loss of a small molecule** (usually H₂O or HCl).

### Key Points
- Requires **two functional groups** (e.g., -OH and -COOH)
- **Small molecule eliminated**
- Often involves **two different monomers**

### Addition vs Condensation

| Feature | Addition | Condensation |
|---------|----------|--------------|
| Monomer | Unsaturated (C=C) | Two functional groups |
| By-product | None | H₂O or HCl |
| Atom economy | 100% | <100% |

---

## 🧵 Polyesters

### Formation
From **diol** (two -OH groups) + **dicarboxylic acid** (two -COOH groups)

### Ester Linkage
```
-COOH + HO-  →  -COO- + H₂O
                (ester linkage)
```

### Terylene (PET)
- Monomers: Ethane-1,2-diol + Benzene-1,4-dicarboxylic acid
- Uses: Clothing fibres, drink bottles, packaging

## 🏗️ Structure & Properties

### Factors Affecting Properties

| Factor | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| **Chain length** | Longer = stronger |
| **Branching** | More branches = lower density, lower melting point |
| **Cross-linking** | Makes polymer rigid, infusible |
| **Intermolecular forces** | Stronger = higher melting point |

### Thermoplastic vs Thermosetting

| Property | Thermoplastic | Thermosetting |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| Chains | Linear/branched | Cross-linked |
| On heating | Softens, melts | Decomposes |
| Reshaping | Can be reshaped | Cannot reshape |
| Examples | PE, PP, PVC, PET | Bakelite, epoxy |

## 🌍 Environmental Issues

### Problems with Polymer Waste
1. **Non-biodegradable** - persist for hundreds of years
2. **Landfill space** - takes up land
3. **Marine pollution** - harms wildlife
4. **Resource waste** - made from crude oil (finite)

### Disposal Methods

| Method | Advantages | Disadvantages |
|--------|------------|---------------|
| **Landfill** | Simple, cheap | Takes space, persists |
| **Incineration** | Reduces volume, energy | CO₂, toxic gases |
| **Recycling** | Saves resources, reduces waste | Must sort by type |
| **Feedstock** | Handles mixed plastics | Expensive, high energy |

### Recycling Codes
- **1** = PET (bottles)
- **2** = HDPE (milk bottles)
- **3** = PVC (pipes)
- **4** = LDPE (bags)
- **5** = PP (yogurt pots)

## ♻️ Biodegradable vs Non-biodegradable

### Non-biodegradable
- Most synthetic polymers (PE, PP, PVC, PTFE)
- Strong C-C bonds not broken by microorganisms
- Persist for 500+ years

### Biodegradable
- Can be broken down by **microorganisms**
- Products: CO₂, H₂O, biomass
- Types: Starch-based, PLA, some synthetic

**Biodegradation Time**:
- Biodegradable bag: 3-6 months
- Paper: 2-5 months
- Plastic bag: 500+ years

## 📝 Drawing Polymer Equations

### Addition Polymerisation

**From Monomer to Polymer:**
```
Monomer:    CH₂=CH₂
            ↓ (double bond opens)
Polymer:    -[-CH₂-CH₂-]-n-
```

### Examples:
```
Ethene:     n CH₂=CH₂  →  -[-CH₂-CH₂-]-n-

Propene:    n CH₂=CHCH₃  →  -[-CH₂-CH(CH₃)-]-n-

Chloroethene: n CH₂=CHCl  →  -[-CH₂-CHCl-]-n-
```

### Tips for Drawing:
1. ✅ **Open the C=C double bond**
2. ✅ Draw as repeating unit in brackets
3. ✅ Add subscript 'n' outside brackets
4. ✅ Include side groups (CH₃, Cl, F) on correct carbon

---

##  Common Exam Traps

1. **Polymer bonds**: Polymer has C-C single bonds, NOT C=C
2. **Always use brackets**: poly(ethene) not polyethene
3. **Include subscript n**: -[-unit-]-n-
4. **Addition = no by-product**; Condensation = H₂O/HCl lost
5. **Different polymers cannot be recycled together**
6. **Biodegradable ≠ disappears instantly** - needs right conditions
7. **PTFE is expensive** - used for unique properties, not cost
