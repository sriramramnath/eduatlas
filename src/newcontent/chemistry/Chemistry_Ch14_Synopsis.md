## Key Reaction Patterns

### 1. Metals + Cold Water → Metal Hydroxide + Hydrogen

| Metal | Reaction | Speed |
|-------|----------|-------|
| K, Na, Li | React vigorously | Explosive to rapid |
| Ca | Reacts steadily | Moderate |
| Mg | Very slow | Barely noticeable |

**Equations:**
- 2Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂
- Ca + 2H₂O → Ca(OH)₂ + H₂

---

### 2. Metals + Steam → Metal Oxide + Hydrogen

| Metal | Reaction | Product |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Mg | Burns brightly | MgO (white) |
| Zn | Reacts when hot | ZnO (yellow→white) |
| Fe | Reacts when hot | Fe₃O₄ (black) |
| Cu and below | No reaction | — |

**Equations:**
- Mg + H₂O(g) → MgO + H₂
- 3Fe + 4H₂O(g) → Fe₃O₄ + 4H₂

---

### 3. Metals + Dilute Acids → Salt + Hydrogen

| Metal | With HCl | With H₂SO₄ | Speed |
|-------|----------|------------|-------|
| Mg | MgCl₂ + H₂ | MgSO₄ + H₂ | Rapid |
| Zn | ZnCl₂ + H₂ | ZnSO₄ + H₂ | Moderate |
| Fe | FeCl₂ + H₂ | FeSO₄ + H₂ | Slow |
| Cu, Ag, Au | No reaction | No reaction | — |

**Note:** Metals below hydrogen do NOT react with dilute acids.

---

### 4. Displacement Reactions

**Rule:** A MORE reactive metal displaces a LESS reactive metal from its compound.

**Example:**
```
Fe + CuSO₄ → FeSO₄ + Cu
(Iron displaces copper because iron is more reactive)
```

**No Reaction:**
```
Cu + FeSO₄ → No reaction
(Copper is less reactive than iron)
```

---

## The Thermit Reaction

```
2Al + Fe₂O₃ → Al₂O₃ + 2Fe
```

- **Type:** Displacement reaction (also redox)
- **Why it works:** Aluminium is more reactive than iron
- **Observation:** Bright white light, molten iron produced (~2500°C)
- **Use:** Welding railway tracks

## Using the Reactivity Series

### Predicting Reactions:

| What? | Prediction |
|-------|------------|
| With water/steam | Higher = more vigorous |
| With acids | Above H = reacts; Below H = no reaction |
| Displacement | Higher metal displaces lower metal |
| Extraction | High = electrolysis; Medium = carbon reduction; Low = found native |

## Oxidation & Reduction (Higher Tier)

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Oxidation** | Loss of electrons (OIL: Oxidation Is Loss) |
| **Reduction** | Gain of electrons (RIG: Reduction Is Gain) |

**In displacement:**
- The displacing metal is oxidized (loses electrons)
- The displaced metal ion is reduced (gains electrons)

---

## Quick Facts to Remember

1. **K, Na, Li** are stored under oil (react with air and water)
2. **Na + water** = orange flame, floats, melts into ball
3. **K + water** = lilac flame, explosive
4. **Mg + steam** = bright white flame, white MgO formed
5. **Metal + steam** = oxide (not hydroxide!)
6. **Metal + cold water** = hydroxide + H₂
7. **Below hydrogen** = no reaction with acids
8. **Thermit** = Al + Fe₂O₃ (welding rails)
9. **Displacement test** = Fe + CuSO₄ (blue → pale green + Cu deposit)

---

## Common Exam Errors to Avoid

| ❌ Wrong | ✓ Correct |
|---------|----------|
| Mg + steam → Mg(OH)₂ | Mg + steam → MgO |
| Metal + H₂SO₄ → chloride | Metal + H₂SO₄ → sulfate |
| Cu displaces Fe | Fe displaces Cu |
| "Higher in series" alone | "More reactive than..." |
