# Chapter 4: The Periodic Table - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

---

## 4.1 Development of the Periodic Table

### Mendeleev's Contribution (1869)
- Arranged elements by **atomic mass**
- Left **gaps** for undiscovered elements
- **Predicted** properties of unknown elements (later confirmed)
- Elements with similar properties placed in same **groups**

**Key Point:** Modern table arranged by **atomic number**, not atomic mass.

---

## 4.2 Structure of the Periodic Table

| Feature | Definition | Indicates |
|---------|------------|-----------|
| **Groups** | Vertical columns | Number of outer shell electrons |
| **Periods** | Horizontal rows | Number of electron shells |

### Layout:
```
         Group →  1   2   3   4   5   6   7   0
                  ↓   ↓   ↓   ↓   ↓   ↓   ↓   ↓
Period 1          H                       He
Period 2          Li  Be  B   C   N   O   F   Ne
Period 3          Na  Mg  Al  Si  P   S   Cl  Ar
                  ↑                           ↑
            Alkali metals              Noble gases
```

---

## 4.3 Group 1: Alkali Metals

**Elements:** Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs

### Properties:
- **Soft** (can be cut with knife)
- **Low density** (Li, Na, K float on water)
- **Low melting points** (for metals)
- **Very reactive** - stored in oil

### Reaction with Water:
$$2M + 2H_2O \rightarrow 2MOH + H_2$$

| Metal | Observation |
|-------|-------------|
| Lithium | Fizzes, moves slowly |
| Sodium | Melts, fizzes rapidly |
| Potassium | Catches fire (lilac flame) |

### Trend:
- **Reactivity INCREASES down group**
- Atoms get larger → outer electron further from nucleus → easier to lose

---

## 4.4 Group 7: Halogens

**Elements:** F, Cl, Br, I

### Properties:
| Element | State | Colour |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Fluorine | Gas | Pale yellow |
| Chlorine | Gas | Green-yellow |
| Bromine | Liquid | Red-brown |
| Iodine | Solid | Grey-black |

### Trends:
- **Melting/boiling points INCREASE down group**
- **Reactivity DECREASES down group**
- Atoms get larger → harder to attract an extra electron

### Displacement Reactions:
**More reactive halogen displaces less reactive halogen from its compound.**

$$Cl_2 + 2NaBr \rightarrow 2NaCl + Br_2$$

**Reactivity order:** F > Cl > Br > I

---

## 4.5 Group 0: Noble Gases

**Elements:** He, Ne, Ar, Kr, Xe

### Properties:
- **Unreactive (inert)** - full outer shell
- **Monatomic** - exist as single atoms
- **Colourless gases**

### Trend:
- **Boiling points INCREASE down group** (larger atoms)

### Uses:
| Gas | Use |
|-----|-----|
| Helium | Balloons, deep-sea diving |
| Neon | Advertising signs |
| Argon | Light bulbs, welding |

---

## 4.6 Metals vs Non-Metals

| Property | Metals | Non-Metals |
|----------|--------|------------|
| Appearance | Shiny | Dull |
| State | Solid (except Hg) | Solid/liquid/gas |
| Melting point | High | Low |
| Density | High | Low |
| Conductivity | Good | Poor |
| Malleable/Ductile | Yes | No (brittle) |
| Ions formed | Positive (+) | Negative (-) |
| Oxides | Basic | Acidic |

---

## 4.7 Transition Metals (Chemistry Only)

**Position:** Center of periodic table, between Groups 2 and 3

### Key Properties:
1. **High melting points** and **high density**
2. **Variable oxidation states** (Fe²⁺ and Fe³⁺, Cu⁺ and Cu²⁺)
3. **Coloured compounds**
4. **Catalytic activity**

### Coloured Compounds:
| Compound | Colour |
|----------|--------|
| CuSO₄ | Blue |
| FeSO₄ | Pale green |
| FeCl₃ | Yellow/brown |
| KMnO₄ | Purple |
| K₂Cr₂O₇ | Orange |

### Catalysts:
| Catalyst | Use |
|----------|-----|
| Iron (Fe) | Haber process |
| V₂O₅ | Contact process |
| Platinum | Catalytic converters |

## Quick Memory Aids

### Group 1 Reactivity:
**"Larger atom = easier to lose electron = more reactive"**

### Group 7 Reactivity:
**"Larger atom = harder to gain electron = less reactive"**

### Noble Gases:
**"Full shell = stable = unreactive"**

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. ❌ Group 7 reactivity increases down → ✅ Decreases down
2. ❌ Period = outer shell electrons → ✅ Period = number of shells
3. ❌ Noble gases have 8 outer electrons → ✅ He has 2, others have 8
4. ❌ "Nucleus gets bigger" → ✅ "Atomic radius increases"
