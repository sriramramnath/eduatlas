## 13.1 Composition of the Atmosphere

### The Earth's Atmosphere

The atmosphere is a mixture of gases surrounding the Earth. The composition of **clean, dry air** is:

| Gas | Formula | Percentage by Volume |
|-----|---------|---------------------|
| Nitrogen | N₂ | ~78% |
| Oxygen | O₂ | ~21% |
| Argon | Ar | ~0.93% |
| Carbon dioxide | CO₂ | ~0.04% |
| Other noble gases | - | Trace amounts |
| Water vapour | H₂O | Variable (0-4%) |

### Key Points About Atmospheric Composition

1. **Nitrogen** is the most abundant gas (approximately 78%)
2. **Oxygen** is the second most abundant (approximately 21%)
3. **Noble gases** (mainly argon) make up about 1%
4. **Carbon dioxide** is present in small but important amounts (~0.04%)
5. **Water vapour** varies with location and weather conditions

### Evidence for Atmospheric Composition

The composition can be determined by:
- **Fractional distillation of liquid air**
- **Gas chromatography**
- **Mass spectrometry**

## 13.2 Obtaining Gases from Air

### Fractional Distillation of Liquid Air

Air can be separated into its component gases by **fractional distillation**.

#### Process Steps

**Step 1: Filtration**
- Air is filtered to remove dust particles

**Step 2: Cooling and Compression**
- Air is compressed to high pressure (200 atm)
- This warms the air, so it is then cooled
- Process repeated to get very cold air

**Step 3: Removal of Carbon Dioxide and Water**
- CO₂ freezes and is removed (would block pipes)
- Water vapour freezes and is removed

**Step 4: Further Cooling**
- Air is cooled to below -200°C
- At this temperature, air becomes liquid

**Step 5: Fractional Distillation**
- Liquid air enters a fractionating column
- Column is colder at top, warmer at bottom
- Gases separate based on their **boiling points**

#### Boiling Points of Atmospheric Gases

| Gas | Boiling Point (°C) |
|-----|-------------------|
| Nitrogen (N₂) | -196 |
| Oxygen (O₂) | -183 |
| Argon (Ar) | -186 |

**Separation Process:**
1. **Nitrogen** has the lowest boiling point (-196°C)
   - Boils first and is collected at the top of the column
2. **Argon** boils at -186°C
   - Collected from the middle of the column
3. **Oxygen** has the highest boiling point (-183°C)
   - Collected from the bottom of the column

### Why Does Fractional Distillation Work?

- Each gas has a different boiling point
- The gas with the **lowest boiling point** boils first
- Gases condense at different heights in the column
- This allows them to be collected separately

## 13.3 Tests for Gases

### Test for Oxygen (O₂)

**Method:**
1. Collect the gas in a test tube
2. Insert a glowing splint (not burning) into the tube

**Positive Result:**
- The glowing splint **relights** (bursts into flame)

**Equation for Combustion:**
$$C + O_2 \rightarrow CO_2 \text{ (carbon burns)}$$

---

### Test for Hydrogen (H₂)

**Method:**
1. Collect the gas in a test tube
2. Apply a lighted splint to the mouth of the tube

**Positive Result:**
- A **"squeaky pop"** sound is heard
- Hydrogen burns rapidly with a characteristic pop

**Equation:**
$$2H_2 + O_2 \rightarrow 2H_2O$$

---

### Test for Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)

**Method 1: Limewater Test**
1. Bubble the gas through limewater (calcium hydroxide solution)

**Positive Result:**
- Limewater turns **milky/cloudy white**

**Equation:**
$$Ca(OH)_2 + CO_2 \rightarrow CaCO_3 + H_2O$$

Calcium carbonate (CaCO₃) is the white precipitate that causes cloudiness.

**Method 2: With Excess CO₂**
If excess CO₂ is bubbled through, the solution clears:

$$CaCO_3 + CO_2 + H_2O \rightarrow Ca(HCO_3)_2$$

Calcium hydrogencarbonate is soluble, so the solution clears.

---

### Test for Ammonia (NH₃)

**Method:**
1. Hold damp red litmus paper in the gas

**Positive Result:**
- Red litmus paper turns **blue**
- Ammonia is the only common alkaline gas

---

### Test for Chlorine (Cl₂)

**Method:**
1. Hold damp blue litmus paper in the gas

**Positive Result:**
- Blue litmus paper turns **red**, then **white** (bleached)
- Chlorine is acidic and a bleaching agent

## 13.4 Combustion and Pollutants

### Combustion Reactions

#### Complete Combustion
When fuels burn with sufficient oxygen:

**Hydrocarbons (e.g., methane):**
$$CH_4 + 2O_2 \rightarrow CO_2 + 2H_2O$$

**General equation for complete combustion:**
$$Fuel + Oxygen \rightarrow Carbon\ dioxide + Water$$

#### Incomplete Combustion
When there is insufficient oxygen:

**Produces carbon monoxide (CO):**
$$2CH_4 + 3O_2 \rightarrow 2CO + 4H_2O$$

**Produces carbon (C/soot):**
$$CH_4 + O_2 \rightarrow C + 2H_2O$$

---

### Atmospheric Pollutants

| Pollutant | Source | Effect on Health/Environment |
|-----------|--------|------------------------------|
| Carbon monoxide (CO) | Incomplete combustion of fuels | Toxic - reduces blood's ability to carry oxygen |
| Carbon dioxide (CO₂) | Complete combustion, respiration | Greenhouse gas - contributes to climate change |
| Sulfur dioxide (SO₂) | Burning fossil fuels containing sulfur | Acid rain, respiratory problems |
| Nitrogen oxides (NOₓ) | High-temperature combustion in engines | Acid rain, photochemical smog, respiratory irritation |
| Particulates (PM) | Incomplete combustion, dust | Respiratory problems, reduced visibility |
| Methane (CH₄) | Agriculture, landfill, fossil fuel extraction | Greenhouse gas - contributes to climate change |

---

### Sources of Pollutants

#### Carbon Monoxide (CO)
- Incomplete combustion in car engines
- Faulty gas appliances
- Coal fires with poor ventilation

**Why is CO dangerous?**
- Colourless and odourless (can't detect it)
- Binds to haemoglobin 200× more strongly than oxygen
- Reduces oxygen transport in blood
- Causes headaches, dizziness, death

#### Sulfur Dioxide (SO₂)
- Burning coal and oil (contain sulfur impurities)
- Volcanic eruptions
- Industrial processes

**Effects:**
- Dissolves in rainwater to form acid rain
- Damages buildings (limestone and marble)
- Harms aquatic life
- Causes respiratory problems (asthma)

#### Nitrogen Oxides (NOₓ)
- Car engines (high temperatures cause N₂ + O₂ to react)
- Power stations
- Lightning

**Formation at high temperature:**
$$N_2 + O_2 \rightarrow 2NO$$

---

### Acid Rain

**Formation:**
1. SO₂ and NOₓ released into atmosphere
2. React with water and oxygen
3. Form sulfuric acid and nitric acid

**Equations:**
$$SO_2 + H_2O \rightarrow H_2SO_3 \text{ (sulfurous acid)}$$
$$2SO_2 + O_2 + 2H_2O \rightarrow 2H_2SO_4 \text{ (sulfuric acid)}$$
$$4NO_2 + 2H_2O + O_2 \rightarrow 4HNO_3 \text{ (nitric acid)}$$

**Effects of Acid Rain:**
| Effect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Building damage | Erodes limestone and marble buildings |
| Plant damage | Damages leaves, leaches nutrients from soil |
| Aquatic life | Lowers pH of lakes, kills fish |
| Soil | Leaches minerals, harms microorganisms |

## 13.5 Greenhouse Gases and Climate Change

### The Greenhouse Effect

#### Natural Greenhouse Effect
1. Sun emits short-wave radiation (visible light, UV)
2. This passes through the atmosphere
3. Earth absorbs radiation and warms up
4. Earth emits long-wave radiation (infrared)
5. Greenhouse gases absorb some infrared radiation
6. This keeps Earth warm enough for life (~15°C average)

#### Enhanced Greenhouse Effect
1. Increased greenhouse gases from human activities
2. More infrared radiation absorbed
3. Earth's temperature rises above natural levels
4. This is **global warming**

### Greenhouse Gases

| Gas | Source | Global Warming Potential |
|-----|--------|-------------------------|
| Carbon dioxide (CO₂) | Burning fossil fuels, deforestation | 1 (reference) |
| Methane (CH₄) | Agriculture (cows), landfill, rice paddies | ~28× CO₂ |
| Water vapour (H₂O) | Natural evaporation | Variable |
| Nitrous oxide (N₂O) | Fertilizers, industrial processes | ~265× CO₂ |
| CFCs | Old refrigerants, aerosols (now banned) | ~10,000× CO₂ |

### Evidence for Climate Change

| Evidence | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Temperature records | Average global temperature increased by ~1°C since pre-industrial times |
| Ice cores | Show historical CO₂ levels and temperatures |
| Glacial retreat | Glaciers and ice caps melting |
| Sea level rise | Due to melting ice and thermal expansion |
| Ocean acidification | CO₂ dissolving in oceans |
| Extreme weather | Increased frequency of heatwaves, floods, storms |

---

### Effects of Climate Change

#### Environmental Effects
- **Rising sea levels:** Coastal flooding, loss of habitat
- **Melting ice caps:** Loss of polar habitats
- **Ocean acidification:** Coral bleaching, harm to marine life
- **Biodiversity loss:** Species extinction, migration changes

#### Human Effects
- **Food security:** Crop failures, changing growing seasons
- **Water scarcity:** Changing rainfall patterns
- **Health impacts:** Heat stress, spread of diseases
- **Economic damage:** Property damage, insurance costs

---

### Reducing Climate Change

#### Reducing CO₂ Emissions
| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Renewable energy | Solar, wind, hydroelectric power |
| Nuclear power | Low carbon emissions |
| Energy efficiency | Better insulation, efficient appliances |
| Electric vehicles | Replace petrol/diesel vehicles |
| Carbon capture | Capture CO₂ from power stations |

#### Carbon Sinks
| Sink | How It Works |
|------|-------------|
| Forests | Trees absorb CO₂ through photosynthesis |
| Oceans | Dissolve CO₂, marine organisms use it |
| Soil | Stores carbon in organic matter |

#### International Agreements
- **Kyoto Protocol (1997):** First agreement to reduce emissions
- **Paris Agreement (2015):** Limit warming to 1.5-2°C above pre-industrial levels

## 13.6 Common Exam Mistakes

### ❌ Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Wrong atmospheric percentages**
   - Mistake: Oxygen is 78%
   - Correct: **Nitrogen** is 78%, oxygen is 21%

2. **Confusing gas tests**
   - Oxygen: Glowing splint **relights**
   - Hydrogen: Lighted splint gives **"squeaky pop"**

3. **Incorrect limewater equation**
   - Mistake: CO₂ + limewater → turns milky (no equation)
   - Correct: Ca(OH)₂ + CO₂ → CaCO₃ + H₂O

4. **Wrong boiling point order in fractional distillation**
   - Mistake: Oxygen boils first
   - Correct: **Nitrogen** has lowest boiling point, boils first

5. **Confusing complete and incomplete combustion**
   - Complete: Produces CO₂ + H₂O
   - Incomplete: Produces CO and/or C (soot)

6. **Forgetting state symbols**
   - Mistake: Air is separated at room temperature
   - Correct: Air must be **liquefied** first (below -200°C)

7. **Wrong pollutant sources**
   - Mistake: SO₂ comes from car engines
   - Correct: SO₂ comes from burning **sulfur-containing fuels** (coal, oil)
   - NOₓ comes from car engines (high temperature reaction)

8. **Confusing greenhouse effect with ozone depletion**
   - Greenhouse effect: CO₂, CH₄ trap heat
   - Ozone depletion: CFCs break down ozone layer

## 13.8 Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Atmosphere | The layer of gases surrounding the Earth |
| Fractional distillation | A process that separates liquids (or liquefied gases) based on their different boiling points |
| Combustion | A chemical reaction between a fuel and oxygen that releases energy |
| Complete combustion | Combustion with sufficient oxygen, producing CO₂ and H₂O |
| Incomplete combustion | Combustion with insufficient oxygen, producing CO and/or C |
| Greenhouse effect | The trapping of heat by gases in the atmosphere |
| Global warming | The gradual increase in Earth's average temperature |
| Acid rain | Rain with a lower pH than normal, caused by SO₂ and NOₓ |
