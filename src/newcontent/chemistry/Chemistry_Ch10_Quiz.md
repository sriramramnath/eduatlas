# Chapter 10: Electrolysis - Quiz Section

## Exam-Style Questions for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Select the correct answer for each question.

---

### Question 1
What is an electrolyte?

A) A metal that conducts electricity

B) An ionic compound that conducts electricity when molten or aqueous

C) A covalent compound that conducts electricity

D) A solution that does not conduct electricity

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Electrolytes are ionic compounds that conduct when molten or aqueous due to mobile ions

---

### Question 2
Which particles move towards the cathode during electrolysis?

A) Electrons

B) Anions

C) Cations

D) Atoms

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Cations (positive ions) are attracted to the negative cathode

---

### Question 3
In the electrolysis of molten sodium chloride, what is formed at the cathode?

A) Chlorine gas

B) Sodium metal

C) Hydrogen gas

D) Oxygen gas

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** In molten NaCl, Na⁺ ions are reduced to sodium metal at the cathode

---

### Question 4
Why must the electrolyte be molten or in solution for electrolysis to occur?

A) The ions need to be free to move

B) The compound needs to be heated

C) The electrodes need to be wet

D) Water must be present for the reaction

**Answer: A**
**Explanation:** Ions must be free to move to carry charge through the electrolyte

---

### Question 5
In the electrolysis of aqueous sodium chloride, which product is formed at the cathode?

A) Sodium metal

B) Chlorine gas

C) Hydrogen gas

D) Oxygen gas

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Na is more reactive than H, so H⁺ ions are discharged, producing hydrogen

---

### Question 6
What is the product at the anode when aqueous copper(II) sulfate is electrolysed using inert electrodes?

A) Copper

B) Oxygen

C) Hydrogen

D) Chlorine

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** No halide present, so OH⁻ ions are discharged, producing oxygen

---

### Question 7
Why is cryolite used in the extraction of aluminium?

A) To increase the melting point of aluminium oxide

B) To lower the melting point of aluminium oxide

C) To react with aluminium oxide

D) To produce the carbon anodes

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Cryolite lowers the melting point from ~2050°C to ~900°C, saving energy

---

### Question 8
During the extraction of aluminium, why must the carbon anodes be replaced regularly?

A) They melt at high temperatures

B) They react with oxygen produced to form carbon dioxide

C) They dissolve in the electrolyte

D) They become coated with aluminium

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Oxygen produced reacts with carbon anodes: C + O₂ → CO₂

---

### Question 9
In the purification of copper by electrolysis, what is used as the anode?

A) Pure copper

B) Impure copper

C) Carbon

D) Platinum

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Impure copper anode dissolves; pure copper deposits on cathode

---

### Question 10
When electroplating a spoon with silver, what should be used as the cathode?

A) Pure silver

B) The spoon

C) Carbon rod

D) Silver nitrate solution

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** The object to be plated is always the cathode (negative electrode)

---

### Question 11
Which half-equation represents oxidation?

A) Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu

B) 2H⁺ + 2e⁻ → H₂

C) 2Cl⁻ → Cl₂ + 2e⁻

D) Al³⁺ + 3e⁻ → Al

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Oxidation is loss of electrons - Cl⁻ loses electrons to form Cl₂

---

### Question 12
Which statement about electrolysis is correct?

A) Reduction occurs at the anode

B) Oxidation occurs at the cathode

C) Cations are attracted to the cathode

D) Anions are attracted to the cathode

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Cations are positively charged and attracted to the negatively charged cathode

## Part B: True/False Questions (12 marks)

For each statement, indicate whether it is True or False. If False, provide the correct statement.

---

### Statement 1
Electrolysis involves the decomposition of an ionic compound using electricity.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Correct definition of electrolysis

---

### Statement 2
In electrolysis, the cathode is the positive electrode.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** The cathode is the negative electrode

---

### Statement 3
During electrolysis, cations move towards the anode.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Cations move towards the cathode (negative electrode)

---

### Statement 4
When aqueous sodium chloride is electrolysed, chlorine gas is produced at the anode.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Cl⁻ ions (halide) are discharged at the anode to form Cl₂

---

### Statement 5
In the electrolysis of aqueous copper(II) sulfate, copper is produced at the cathode because copper is less reactive than hydrogen.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Cu is below H in reactivity series, so Cu²⁺ is discharged

---

### Statement 6
The electrolysis of aqueous sodium sulfate produces hydrogen at the cathode and oxygen at the anode.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Na is more reactive than H (H₂ at cathode); no halide present (O₂ at anode)

---

### Statement 7
Aluminium can be extracted from its ore by heating with carbon.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Aluminium is too reactive - it must be extracted by electrolysis

---

### Statement 8
During the extraction of aluminium, aluminium ions are reduced at the anode.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Aluminium ions are reduced at the cathode

---

### Statement 9
In the purification of copper, pure copper is used as the anode and impure copper as the cathode.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Impure copper is the anode and pure copper is the cathode

---

### Statement 10
Electroplating is used to coat objects with a thin layer of metal.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Correct definition of electroplating

---

### Statement 11
When electroplating with nickel, the object to be plated is made the anode.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** The object to be plated is the cathode

---

### Statement 12
The concentration of copper(II) ions remains constant during the purification of copper by electrolysis.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Cu²⁺ ions are replaced from the anode at the same rate they are deposited

## Part C: Short Answer Questions (12 marks)

Answer the following questions in the spaces provided.

---

### Question 1
Explain why ionic compounds conduct electricity when molten but not when solid.

*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: In solid ionic compounds, ions are held in fixed positions and cannot move (1)
- M2: When molten, ions are free to move and can carry charge through the electrolyte (1)

---

### Question 2
Write the half-equations for the reactions at the cathode and anode during the electrolysis of molten lead(II) bromide, PbBr₂.

*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Cathode: Pb²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Pb (1)
- M2: Anode: 2Br⁻ → Br₂ + 2e⁻ (1)

---

### Question 3
Predict the products at the cathode and anode when aqueous potassium iodide is electrolysed. Explain your answers.

*(4 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Cathode product: Hydrogen gas (1)
- M2: Explanation: Potassium is more reactive than hydrogen, so H⁺ ions are discharged (1)
- M3: Anode product: Iodine (1)
- M4: Explanation: Iodide (I⁻) is a halide ion, so it is discharged at the anode (1)

---

### Question 4
Describe the extraction of aluminium from aluminium oxide. Include:
- Why cryolite is used
- The reactions at each electrode
- Why the carbon anodes need regular replacement

*(5 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Cryolite is used to lower the melting point of aluminium oxide (from ~2050°C to ~900°C), reducing energy costs (1)
- M2: Cathode reaction: Al³⁺ + 3e⁻ → Al (aluminium ions gain electrons/are reduced) (1)
- M3: Anode reaction: 2O²⁻ → O₂ + 4e⁻ (oxide ions lose electrons/are oxidised) (1)
- M4: Carbon anodes react with oxygen produced: C + O₂ → CO₂ (1)
- M5: The anodes burn away and need regular replacement (1)

---

### Question 5
Explain how copper is purified by electrolysis. Include the materials used for the electrodes and the electrolyte.

*(4 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Anode: Impure copper (1)
- M2: Cathode: Pure copper (1)
- M3: Electrolyte: Copper(II) sulfate solution (1)
- M4: Copper dissolves from impure anode and deposits on pure cathode (1)

---

### Question 6
A student wants to electroplate a copper coin with silver. Describe how they should set up the apparatus.

*(4 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Cathode: The copper coin (object to be plated) (1)
- M2: Anode: Pure silver (1)
- M3: Electrolyte: Silver nitrate solution (1)
- M4: Silver dissolves at anode and deposits on coin at cathode (1)

---

Write the half-equation for the formation of oxygen at the anode from hydroxide ions.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**

- OR M1: 4OH⁻ → O₂ + ... (1 mark for partially correct)

### Question 8
Explain why hydrogen gas is produced at the cathode when aqueous sodium sulfate is electrolysed, rather than sodium metal.

*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Sodium is more reactive than hydrogen (1)
- M2: The more reactive the metal, the more it tends to stay as ions in solution (1)
- M3: Therefore H⁺ ions from water are discharged (reduced) instead of Na⁺ ions, producing hydrogen gas (1)

---

### Question 9
State two differences between the electrolysis of molten sodium chloride and aqueous sodium chloride.

*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Any two valid differences:
- Molten: produces sodium at cathode; Aqueous: produces hydrogen at cathode (1)
- Molten: products are elements only; Aqueous: products include hydrogen (1)
- Molten: no water involved; Aqueous: water provides H⁺ and OH⁻ ions (1)

---

### Question 10
In the electrolysis of aqueous copper(II) sulfate using inert electrodes:
- What would be observed at the cathode?
- What happens to the colour of the solution?
- Explain why this colour change occurs.

*(4 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Cathode observation: Brown/pink deposit of copper (1)
- M2: Solution colour: Blue colour fades/becomes paler (1)
- M3: Explanation: Cu²⁺ ions are removed from solution (1)
- M4: Cu²⁺ ions give the blue colour (1)

---

### Question 11
Why is the purification of copper by electrolysis important for its use in electrical wiring?

*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
- M1: Impurities reduce electrical conductivity (1)
- M2: Pure copper has maximum conductivity for efficient electrical wiring (1)
