### Question 1
Which of the following is an element?

A) Water  
B) Air  
C) Copper  
D) Carbon dioxide  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Copper (Cu) is an element - it contains only one type of atom. Water is a compound (H₂O), air is a mixture of gases, and carbon dioxide is a compound (CO₂).

---

### Question 2
Which statement about compounds is correct?

A) They can be separated by filtration  
B) They have variable composition  
C) They contain elements chemically combined in fixed proportions  
D) They retain the properties of their constituent elements  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Compounds consist of two or more elements chemically combined in fixed proportions by mass. They cannot be separated by physical means and have properties different from their constituent elements.

---

### Question 3
Which method is used to separate sand from water?

A) Distillation  
B) Filtration  
C) Chromatography  
D) Crystallisation  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Filtration is used to separate an insoluble solid (sand) from a liquid (water). The sand is the residue on the filter paper, and water is the filtrate.

---

### Question 4
What is the Rf value if a substance moves 4.0 cm and the solvent moves 8.0 cm?

A) 0.20  
B) 0.50  
C) 2.0  
D) 32  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Rf = distance moved by substance ÷ distance moved by solvent = 4.0 ÷ 8.0 = 0.50. Rf values are always between 0 and 1.

---

### Question 5
Which of the following is a mixture?

A) Sodium chloride  
B) Water  
C) Air  
D) Copper sulfate  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Air is a mixture of gases (nitrogen, oxygen, argon, carbon dioxide, and others) that are not chemically combined. Sodium chloride, water, and copper sulfate are all compounds.

---

### Question 6
Which property is characteristic of metals?

A) Poor conductor of electricity  
B) Low melting point  
C) Brittle when solid  
D) Ductile  

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Metals are ductile - they can be drawn into wires. Metals are also good conductors of electricity and heat, have high melting points, and are malleable.

---

### Question 7
In fractional distillation, what is the purpose of the fractionating column?

A) To heat the mixture  
B) To provide surface area for repeated condensation and vaporisation  
C) To cool the vapour  
D) To filter impurities  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: The fractionating column provides a large surface area for repeated condensation and vaporisation, which helps separate liquids with similar boiling points.

---

### Question 8
Which separation technique would be most suitable for obtaining pure water from seawater?

A) Filtration  
B) Chromatography  
C) Simple distillation  
D) Crystallisation  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Simple distillation separates a liquid (water) from dissolved solids (salt). The water evaporates and is then condensed back into liquid form.

---

### Question 9
Which statement correctly describes a heterogeneous mixture?

A) It has uniform composition throughout  
B) The components are not visible  
C) It has a single phase  
D) The composition varies from one region to another  

**Answer: D**

**Explanation**: Heterogeneous mixtures have non-uniform composition, where the components are often visible and the composition varies in different regions.

---

### Question 10
A compound contains 40% calcium, 12% carbon, and 48% oxygen by mass. What type of compound is this?

A) An element  
B) A mixture  
C) A compound with fixed composition  
D) A solution  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The substance has a fixed percentage composition by mass, which is a key characteristic of a chemical compound (this one is CaCO₃).

---

### Question 11
Which technique separates substances based on their different solubilities in a solvent?

A) Filtration  
B) Magnetic separation  
C) Chromatography  
D) Distillation  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Chromatography separates substances based on their different solubilities in the moving solvent and their relative attraction to the stationary phase.

---

### Question 12
Why does water have different properties from hydrogen and oxygen?

A) Water is a mixture of hydrogen and oxygen  
B) Hydrogen and oxygen are physically combined in water  
C) Hydrogen and oxygen are chemically bonded in water, forming a new substance  
D) Water contains more oxygen than hydrogen  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: In water (H₂O), hydrogen and oxygen atoms are chemically bonded. This chemical combination creates a new substance with completely different properties from the original elements.

### Question 13
An element can be broken down into simpler substances by chemical reactions.

**Answer: False**

**Correction**: Elements are the simplest form of matter and cannot be decomposed chemically into simpler substances.

### Question 14
Mixtures can be separated by physical methods.

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Since components in a mixture are physically combined but not chemically bonded, they can be separated by physical methods like filtration or distillation.
