## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
Which of the following is an element?

A) Water  
B) Air  
C) Copper  
D) Carbon dioxide  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Copper (Cu) is an element - it contains only one type of atom. Water is a compound (H₂O), air is a mixture of gases, and carbon dioxide is a compound (CO₂).

---

### Question 2
Which statement about compounds is correct?

A) They can be separated by filtration  
B) They have variable composition  
C) They contain elements chemically combined in fixed proportions  
D) They retain the properties of their constituent elements  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Compounds consist of two or more elements chemically combined in fixed proportions by mass (Law of Constant Composition). They cannot be separated by physical means, have fixed composition, and have properties different from their constituent elements.

---

### Question 3
Which method is used to separate sand from water?

A) Distillation  
B) Filtration  
C) Chromatography  
D) Crystallisation  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Filtration is used to separate an insoluble solid (sand) from a liquid (water). The sand is the residue on the filter paper, and water is the filtrate.

---

### Question 4
What is the Rf value if a substance moves 4.0 cm and the solvent moves 8.0 cm?

A) 0.20  
B) 0.50  
C) 2.0  
D) 32  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Rf = distance moved by substance ÷ distance moved by solvent = 4.0 ÷ 8.0 = 0.50. Rf values are always between 0 and 1.

---

### Question 5
Which of the following is a mixture?

A) Sodium chloride  
B) Water  
C) Air  
D) Copper sulfate  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Air is a mixture of gases (nitrogen, oxygen, argon, carbon dioxide, and others) that are not chemically combined. Sodium chloride, water, and copper sulfate are all compounds.

---

### Question 6
Which property is characteristic of metals?

A) Poor conductor of electricity  
B) Low melting point  
C) Brittle when solid  
D) Ductile  

**Answer: D**

**Explanation:** Metals are ductile - they can be drawn into wires. Metals are also good conductors of electricity and heat, have high melting points, and are malleable (not brittle).

---

### Question 7
In fractional distillation, what is the purpose of the fractionating column?

A) To heat the mixture  
B) To provide surface area for repeated condensation and vaporisation  
C) To cool the vapour  
D) To filter impurities  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The fractionating column contains glass beads or plates that provide a large surface area for repeated condensation and vaporisation. This enriches the vapour in the component with the lower boiling point, allowing better separation of liquids with similar boiling points.

---

### Question 8
Which separation technique would be most suitable for obtaining pure water from seawater?

A) Filtration  
B) Chromatography  
C) Simple distillation  
D) Crystallisation  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Simple distillation separates a liquid (water) from dissolved solids (salt and other minerals in seawater). The water boils, vapour passes through the condenser, and pure water is collected as the distillate.

---

### Question 9
Which statement correctly describes a heterogeneous mixture?

A) It has uniform composition throughout  
B) The components are not visible  
C) It has a single phase  
D) The composition varies from one region to another  

**Answer: D**

**Explanation:** Heterogeneous mixtures have non-uniform composition - the components are often visible and the composition varies in different regions. Examples include sand in water and oil mixed with water.

---

### Question 10
A compound contains 40% calcium, 12% carbon, and 48% oxygen by mass. What type of compound is this?

A) An element  
B) A mixture  
C) A compound with fixed composition  
D) A solution  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** The substance has a fixed percentage composition (40:12:48 for Ca:C:O), which is characteristic of a compound. This compound is calcium carbonate (CaCO₃). Fixed composition is evidence of chemical combination.

---

### Question 11
Which technique separates substances based on their different solubilities in a solvent?

A) Filtration  
B) Magnetic separation  
C) Chromatography  
D) Distillation  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Chromatography separates substances based on their different solubilities in the mobile phase (solvent) and their different attractions to the stationary phase. Components that are more soluble in the solvent move further up the paper.

---

### Question 12
Why does water have different properties from hydrogen and oxygen?
**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** In water (H₂O), hydrogen and oxygen atoms are chemically bonded through covalent bonds. This chemical combination creates a new substance with completely different properties from the constituent elements. Hydrogen is flammable and oxygen supports combustion, but water extinguishes fire.

## Part B: True/False Questions
### Statement 1
An element can be broken down into simpler substances by chemical reactions.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Elements CANNOT be broken down into simpler substances by chemical reactions. This is the definition of an element - it is the simplest form of matter that cannot be decomposed chemically.
**Explanation**: Elements CANNOT be broken down into simpler substances by chemical reactions. This is the definition of an element - it is the simplest form of matter that cannot be decomposed chemically.
---
### Statement 2
Compounds have properties that are different from their constituent elements.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: When elements combine chemically to form compounds, the new bonds create substances with completely different properties. For example, sodium (a reactive metal) and chlorine (a toxic gas) combine to form sodium chloride (table salt), which is safe to eat.
---
### Statement 3
Mixtures can be separated by physical methods.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Mixtures contain substances that are physically combined but not chemically bonded. They can be separated using physical methods such as filtration, distillation, chromatography, or magnetic separation, based on differences in physical properties.
---
### Statement 4
Air is an example of a compound.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Air is a MIXTURE, not a compound. It contains nitrogen, oxygen, argon, carbon dioxide, and other gases that are physically mixed but not chemically combined. The composition of air can vary, which is characteristic of mixtures, not compounds.
**Explanation**: Air is a MIXTURE, not a compound. It contains nitrogen, oxygen, argon, carbon dioxide, and other gases that are physically mixed but not chemically combined. The composition of air can vary, which is characteristic of mixtures, not compounds.
---
### Statement 5
In crystallisation, the solution should be evaporated to complete dryness.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: In crystallisation, the solution should NOT be evaporated to complete dryness. The solution is heated until it is concentrated (some solvent evaporated), then allowed to cool so crystals form. Heating to dryness would produce a powder rather than pure crystals, and some compounds might decompose.
**Explanation**: In crystallisation, the solution should NOT be evaporated to complete dryness. The solution is heated until it is concentrated (some solvent evaporated), then allowed to cool so crystals form. Heating to dryness would produce a powder rather than pure crystals, and some compounds might decompose.
---
### Statement 6
Rf values can be used to identify substances in chromatography.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Each substance has a characteristic Rf value in a particular solvent. By comparing the Rf value of an unknown substance with known values, the substance can be identified. However, the same Rf value should be confirmed using different solvents for reliable identification.
---
### Statement 7
Metals are generally poor conductors of heat.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Metals are generally GOOD conductors of heat. The delocalised electrons in metals can transfer thermal energy quickly throughout the metal structure. This is why metal pots are used for cooking and why metals feel cold to touch (they conduct heat away from your skin).
**Explanation**: Metals are generally GOOD conductors of heat. The delocalised electrons in metals can transfer thermal energy quickly throughout the metal structure. This is why metal pots are used for cooking and why metals feel cold to touch (they conduct heat away from your skin).
---
### Statement 8
A separating funnel is used to separate immiscible liquids.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: A separating funnel is used to separate two immiscible liquids (liquids that do not mix, like oil and water). The denser liquid forms the lower layer and can be drained off through the tap, leaving the less dense liquid behind.
---
### Statement 9
Homogeneous mixtures have visible boundaries between their components.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Heterogeneous mixtures have visible boundaries between components. Homogeneous mixtures have uniform composition throughout with no visible boundaries - the components are evenly distributed and cannot be seen separately, like salt dissolved in water.
**Explanation**: Heterogeneous mixtures have visible boundaries between components. Homogeneous mixtures have uniform composition throughout with no visible boundaries - the components are evenly distributed and cannot be seen separately, like salt dissolved in water.
---
### Statement 10
The elements in a compound are present in fixed proportions by mass.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: This is the Law of Constant Composition (or Law of Definite Proportions). A given compound always contains the same elements in the same proportions by mass. For example, water (H₂O) always contains hydrogen and oxygen in a 1:8 ratio by mass, regardless of its source.
---
### Statement 11
Fractional distillation is used to separate miscible liquids with similar boiling points.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Fractional distillation is specifically designed to separate miscible liquids (liquids that mix completely) that have similar boiling points. The fractionating column allows for repeated vaporisation and condensation, achieving better separation than simple distillation.
---
### Statement 12
Non-metals are always solids at room temperature.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Non-metals can be solids, liquids, or gases at room temperature. Examples: carbon and sulfur are solids, bromine is a liquid, and oxygen, nitrogen, and chlorine are gases. This contrasts with metals, which are almost all solids at room temperature (mercury is the exception).
**Explanation**: Non-metals can be solids, liquids, or gases at room temperature. Examples: carbon and sulfur are solids, bromine is a liquid, and oxygen, nitrogen, and chlorine are gases. This contrasts with metals, which are almost all solids at room temperature (mercury is the exception).

Describe how you would obtain pure, dry crystals of copper(II) sulfate from copper(II) sulfate solution.

**Answer:**
- Heat the solution gently to evaporate some water and concentrate the solution (1 mark)
- Allow the concentrated solution to cool so that crystals form (1 mark)
- Filter to collect the crystals, wash with a little cold distilled water, and dry between filter papers (1 mark)

### Question 5 (3 marks)
A student wants to separate a mixture of ethanol (boiling point 78°C) and water (boiling point 100°C). Name and describe the separation technique they should use.

**Answer:**
Technique: Fractional distillation (1 mark)
- The mixture is heated in a flask with a fractionating column (1 mark)
- Ethanol (lower boiling point) vaporises first, passes up the column and into the condenser where it condenses and is collected as the first fraction (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: naming fractional distillation (1 mark); describing heating with fractionating column (1 mark); explaining collection of lower BP substance first (1 mark).

### Question 6 (4 marks)
In a chromatography experiment, a substance moves 6.0 cm while the solvent moves 10.0 cm. Calculate the Rf value and explain what the Rf value can be used for.

**Answer:**
Calculation: Rf = 6.0 ÷ 10.0 = 0.60 (2 marks for correct calculation)

The Rf value can be used to identify the substance by comparing it with known Rf values in reference tables (1 mark). The same substance will have the same Rf value when run in the same solvent under the same conditions (1 mark).

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award 2 marks for correct Rf calculation (0.60); award 1 mark for identification use; award 1 mark for same substance same Rf explanation.

### Question 9 (3 marks)
Explain why sodium chloride (table salt) has very different properties from sodium and chlorine.

**Answer:**
- Sodium chloride is a compound formed when sodium and chlorine react and form ionic bonds (1 mark)
- The sodium atoms lose electrons to become sodium ions (Na⁺) and chlorine atoms gain electrons to become chloride ions (Cl⁻) (1 mark)
- The new ionic structure has completely different properties from the original elements - sodium ions and chloride ions are stable and non-reactive, unlike the highly reactive elements (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: compound formed by chemical reaction/bonding (1 mark); electron transfer described (1 mark); new ionic structure with different properties (1 mark).

### Question 11 (3 marks) - Chemistry Only
Calculate the atom economy for the reaction: Fe₂O₃ + 3CO → 2Fe + 3CO₂, where iron is the desired product.
(A_r values: Fe = 56, O = 16, C = 12)

**Answer:**
M_r of desired product (2Fe) = 2 × 56 = 112 (1 mark)
M_r of all reactants = Fe₂O₃ + 3CO = (2 × 56 + 3 × 16) + 3 × (12 + 16) = 160 + 84 = 244 (1 mark)

Atom economy = (112/244) × 100% = 45.9% or 46% (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: calculating M_r of product (1 mark); calculating M_r of reactants (1 mark); correct calculation with units (1 mark).

### Question 12 (4 marks)
Compare and contrast simple distillation and fractional distillation.

**Answer:**
Similarities:
- Both use evaporation followed by condensation (1 mark)
- Both separate mixtures based on differences in boiling points (1 mark)

Differences:
- Simple distillation uses only a condenser; fractional distillation uses a fractionating column (1 mark)
- Simple distillation is used for separating a liquid from a dissolved solid or liquids with very different boiling points; fractional distillation is used for miscible liquids with similar boiling points (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: similarity about process (1 mark); similarity about principle (1 mark); difference in apparatus (1 mark); difference in application (1 mark).
