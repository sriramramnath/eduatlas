# IGCSE Chemistry Chapter 20: Rates of Reaction
## Synopsis (Quick Revision Summary)

---

## Key Definitions at a Glance

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Rate of reaction** | Measure of how fast reactants are converted into products |
| **Collision theory** | Reactions occur when particles collide with sufficient energy and correct orientation |
| **Activation energy (Ea)** | Minimum energy particles must have to react upon collision |
| **Successful collision** | A collision that results in a reaction |
| **Catalyst** | Substance that increases rate without being chemically changed |
| **Enzyme** | Biological catalyst made of protein |

---

## The Four Factors Affecting Rate

| Factor | Effect on Rate | Collision Theory Explanation |
|--------|---------------|------------------------------|
| **Temperature ↑** | Increases | More KE → more frequent collisions + MORE particles have energy ≥ Ea |
| **Concentration ↑** | Increases | More particles per volume → more frequent collisions |
| **Surface area ↑** | Increases | More exposed particles → more frequent collisions |
| **Catalyst added** | Increases | Lowers Ea → more particles have sufficient energy |

---

## Collision Theory Essentials

**For a successful collision:**
1. Particles must **collide**
2. Must have **sufficient energy** (≥ activation energy)
3. Must have **correct orientation**

**Rate = Number of successful collisions per unit time**

---

## Temperature Effect - Two Parts!

When explaining temperature effects, **always mention BOTH:**

1. **More frequent collisions** - particles move faster
2. **More energetic collisions** - MORE particles have energy ≥ Ea

**Key fact:** Rate roughly **doubles for every 10°C increase**

---

## Surface Area Quick Guide

| Form | Surface Area | Rate |
|------|--------------|------|
| Large lump | Small | Slow |
| Small pieces | Medium | Medium |
| Powder | Large | Fast |

**Remember:** Same mass, different surface area!

---

## Catalysts - Key Points

| Feature | Effect |
|---------|--------|
| Rate | **Increases** |
| Activation energy | **Decreases** |
| ΔH (enthalpy change) | **Unchanged** |
| Catalyst itself | **Not used up** |
| Reaction pathway | Provides **alternative** pathway |

### Important Catalysts

| Catalyst | Reaction |
|----------|----------|
| Iron (Fe) | Haber process |
| Vanadium(V) oxide | Contact process |
| Platinum/Palladium | Catalytic converters |
| Enzymes | Biological reactions |

## Measuring Rate - Three Methods

### 1. Gas Volume (Gas Syringe)
- Record volume at regular intervals
- Plot volume vs time graph
- Suitable for reactions producing gas

### 2. Mass Loss (Balance)
- Record mass at regular intervals
- Gas escapes (cotton wool plug)
- Plot mass vs time graph

### 3. Precipitation (Disappearing Cross)
- Time for cross to disappear
- Rate = 1/time
- Suitable for reactions forming precipitate

## Interpreting Rate Graphs

```
Quantity
    ↑
    |           ●●●●●● ← Reaction complete (plateau)
    |         ●
    |       ●
    |     ●  ← Steep gradient = fast rate
    |   ●
    | ●
    |●
    |____________________________→
           Time
```

**Key features:**
- **Steep initial slope** = fast initial rate
- **Curve flattens** = rate decreases (reactants used up)
- **Plateau** = reaction finished
- **Gradient = Rate** at that point

## Required Practical Summary

### Sodium Thiosulfate + HCl (Concentration Effect)

**Equation:** Na₂S₂O₃ + 2HCl → 2NaCl + S + SO₂ + H₂O

**Method:**
1. Place cross under flask
2. Add thiosulfate to flask
3. Add HCl, start timer
4. Stop when cross disappears
5. Repeat with different concentrations
6. Calculate rate = 1/time

**Variables:**
- **Independent:** Concentration of thiosulfate
- **Dependent:** Time/rate
- **Control:** Volume, temperature, same cross

**Result:** Higher concentration → faster rate

## Top 6 Exam Mistakes

1. ❌ Forgetting to mention **activation energy** for temperature effects
   → ✅ Always say: "More particles have energy ≥ Ea"

2. ❌ Saying catalysts are "used up"
   → ✅ Catalysts are **NOT** used up

3. ❌ Confusing surface area with mass
   → ✅ Same mass, different surface area

4. ❌ "More collisions = faster rate"
   → ✅ More **successful** collisions = faster rate

5. ❌ Forgetting units in calculations
   → ✅ Include units: s⁻¹, g/s, cm³/s

6. ❌ Not mentioning control variables
   → ✅ Always list control variables in practical questions

## Quick Memory Aids

**FACTS** (Factors Affecting Collision Theory Success):
- **F**requency of collisions (concentration, surface area)
- **A**ctivation energy (temperature, catalysts)
- **C**ollisions must occur
- **T**emperature affects both frequency AND energy
- **S**uccessful collisions need energy AND orientation

**Catalyst Mnemonic:**
- **C**hanges rate
- **A**lternative pathway
- **T**emperature not changed
- **A**ctivation energy lowered
- **L**owers Ea
- **Y**ields same products
- **S**ame ΔH
- **T**he catalyst is unchanged

## Enzyme Facts

- **Biological catalysts** made of protein
- **Specific** to one reaction
- **Optimum temperature** (usually 37°C for human enzymes)
- **Optimum pH** (varies by enzyme)
- **Denatured** by high temperature or extreme pH
- Denaturation changes shape → active site no longer fits substrate

---

## Rate Equation (Simple)

**Rate = Change in quantity / Time**

**Units:**
- g/s (grams per second)
- cm³/s (volume per second)
- mol/dm³/s (concentration per second)

**For precipitation:** Rate = 1/time (units: s⁻¹)
