## Part C: Short Answer Mark Schemes

---

### Question 1 (3 marks)
**Define "homologous series" and state three characteristics.**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Definition: A family of organic compounds with the same general formula and similar chemical properties (1 mark)
- Characteristic 1: Same functional group (1 mark)
- Characteristic 2: Each member differs by CH₂ from the next (1 mark)
- Characteristic 3: Gradual change in physical properties (accept as alternative)

---

### Question 2 (3 marks)
**Molecular formulas and names**

**Mark Scheme:**
- a) C₅H₁₂, pentane (1 mark)
- b) C₄H₈, butene (1 mark)
- c) C₃H₇OH, propanol (1 mark)

---

### Question 3 (3 marks)
**Describe fractional distillation of crude oil**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Crude oil heated to vaporise it (350-400°C) (1 mark)
- Vapour enters fractionating column with temperature gradient (hot at bottom, cool at top) (1 mark)
- Different fractions condense at different heights based on their boiling points; smaller molecules condense higher up (lower BP), larger molecules condense lower down (higher BP) (1 mark)

---

### Question 4 (3 marks)
**Distinguish hexane and hexene**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Add bromine water to each sample (1 mark)
- Hexene (unsaturated) decolourises bromine water; hexane (saturated) does not (1 mark)
- Equation: C₆H₁₂ + Br₂ → C₆H₁₂Br₂ (1 mark)

---

### Question 5 (3 marks)
**Combustion equations**

**Mark Scheme:**
- a) CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O (1.5 marks)
- b) C₂H₅OH + 3O₂ → 2CO₂ + 3H₂O (1.5 marks)

---

### Question 6 (3 marks)
**Name compounds and series**

**Mark Scheme:**
- a) C₂H₆: Ethane, alkane (1 mark)
- b) C₂H₄: Ethene, alkene (1 mark)
- c) CH₃COOH: Methanoic acid, carboxylic acid (1 mark)

---

### Question 7 (3 marks)
**Explain properties of smaller molecules**

**Mark Scheme:**
- a) Lower BP because weaker intermolecular forces between molecules (1 mark)
- b) More flammable because more volatile, vaporises more easily (1 mark)
- c) Condense higher up because they have lower boiling points and need to cool more before condensing (1 mark)

---

### Question 8 (3 marks)
**Saturated hydrocarbon definition**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Saturated hydrocarbon: Contains only single C-C bonds (all C atoms have maximum H atoms) (1 mark)
- No double or triple bonds between carbon atoms (1 mark)
- Example: Ethane (C₂H₆) or any correct alkane (1 mark)

---

### Question 9 (3 marks)
**Three fractions and their uses**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Any three correct pairs (1 mark each):
  - Refinery gases: Bottled gas, camping gas
  - Petrol (gasoline): Car fuel
  - Naphtha: Chemical feedstock
  - Kerosene: Jet fuel, paraffin heaters
  - Diesel: Lorries, buses, trains
  - Fuel oil: Ships, power stations
  - Lubricating oil: Engine oil
  - Bitumen: Road surfacing, roofing

---

### Question 10 (3 marks)
**Ethanol structural formula and oxidation**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Structural formula: CH₃CH₂OH or C₂H₅OH (1 mark)
- Oxidation description: Ethanol reacts with oxygen to form ethanoic acid (1 mark)
- Equation: C₂H₅OH + [O] → CH₃COOH + H₂O (1 mark)

---

### Question 11 (3 marks)
**Saturated vs unsaturated and test**

**Mark Scheme:**
- Saturated: Contains only single C-C bonds (1 mark)
- Unsaturated: Contains at least one C=C double bond (1 mark)
- Test: Add bromine water; unsaturated compounds decolourise it (orange → colourless) (1 mark)

---

### Question 12 (3 marks)
**Balanced equations**

**Mark Scheme:**
- a) 2CH₃COOH + Na₂CO₃ → 2CH₃COONa + H₂O + CO₂ (1.5 marks)
- b) C₂H₅OH + O₂ → CH₃COOH + H₂O OR C₂H₅OH + 2[O] → CH₃COOH + H₂O (1.5 marks)
