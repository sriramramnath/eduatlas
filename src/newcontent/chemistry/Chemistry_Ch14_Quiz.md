# ANSWERS AND MARK SCHEMES

## Part A: Multiple Choice Answers

| Question | Answer | Explanation |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Q1 | **C) Sodium** | Sodium is more reactive than magnesium and zinc. Potassium would be more vigorous, but it's not an option. Sodium reacts vigorously with cold water, melting into a ball and producing hydrogen. |
| Q2 | **B) Magnesium oxide and hydrogen** | With steam, metals form oxides (not hydroxides) due to the higher temperature. Mg + H₂O(g) → MgO + H₂ |
| Q3 | **C) Copper** | Copper is below hydrogen in the reactivity series, so it does not react with dilute acids. Zinc, iron, and magnesium are all above hydrogen. |
| Q4 | **B) Iron is more reactive than copper** | In displacement reactions, a more reactive metal displaces a less reactive metal from its compound. |
| Q5 | **B) Sodium** | Sodium is very reactive and would react with air and moisture. It must be stored under oil. |
| Q6 | **B) Weld railway tracks together** | The Thermit reaction produces molten iron at high temperatures, which is used to weld railway tracks. |
| Q7 | **B) The solution becomes colorless and copper is deposited** | Zinc displaces copper: Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu. The blue Cu²⁺ ions are removed, leaving colorless Zn²⁺ ions. |
| Q8 | **C) 3Fe + 4H₂O → Fe₃O₄ + 4H₂** | Iron reacts with steam to form triiron tetroxide (Fe₃O₄), not simple FeO or Fe₂O₃. |
| Q9 | **C) Gold** | Gold is very unreactive (low in the reactivity series) and is found native (uncombined) in nature. |
| Q10 | **B) Calcium hydroxide and hydrogen** | Ca + 2H₂O → Ca(OH)₂ + H₂. Metals form hydroxides with cold water. |
| Q11 | **B) Copper ions (Cu²⁺)** | Reduction is gain of electrons. Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu. Copper ions gain electrons. |
| Q12 | **B) Iron** | The order is: Al > Zn > Fe > Sn > Pb > Cu. Iron is between zinc and lead. |

## Part B: True/False Answers

| Question | Answer | Correction if False |
|----------|--------|---------------------|
| Q1 | **TRUE** | Potassium is more reactive than sodium and reacts more violently with water. |
| Q2 | **FALSE** | Magnesium reacts with cold water to produce **magnesium hydroxide** and hydrogen. (Oxide is produced with steam.) |
| Q3 | **TRUE** | Metals above hydrogen react with dilute acids to produce hydrogen gas. |
| Q4 | **FALSE** | Copper **cannot** displace iron because copper is less reactive than iron. |
| Q5 | **TRUE** | Aluminium loses electrons (oxidized), and iron(III) ions gain electrons (reduced). |
| Q6 | **FALSE** | Silver is below hydrogen in the reactivity series and does **not** react with dilute acids. |
| Q7 | **FALSE** | When iron reacts with steam, **triiron tetroxide (Fe₃O₄)** is formed. |
| Q8 | **TRUE** | Zinc is more reactive than copper, so it can displace copper from its compounds. |
| Q9 | **FALSE** | Lithium is **less** reactive than potassium. Potassium is the most reactive of the three alkali metals. |
| Q10 | **TRUE** | Metals below hydrogen cannot displace hydrogen from acids. |
| Q11 | **TRUE** | 2Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂ |
| Q12 | **TRUE** | The displacing metal is oxidized (loses electrons) while the displaced ion is reduced (gains electrons). |

## Part C: Short Answer Mark Schemes

### Q1. Magnesium and Hydrochloric Acid

**(a) Balanced equation:** [2 marks]

```
Mg(s) + 2HCl(aq) → MgCl₂(aq) + H₂(g)
```

Award 1 mark for correct products, 1 mark for balanced equation with state symbols.

**(b) Observations:** [2 marks]
- Rapid fizzing/effervescence (1 mark)
- Magnesium dissolves/disappears (1 mark)

---

### Q2. Electron Transfer Explanation [3 marks]

- Iron atoms **lose electrons** (Fe → Fe²⁺ + 2e⁻) (1 mark)
- Iron is **oxidized** (1 mark)
- Copper ions **gain electrons** (Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu) (1 mark)
- Copper ions are **reduced** (1 mark)

*Maximum 3 marks*

---

### Q3. Determining Reactivity Order [3 marks]

- Add each metal to solutions containing ions of the other two metals (1 mark)
- Observe which metals displace others (1 mark)
- The metal that displaces both others is most reactive; the metal displaced by both is least reactive (1 mark)

---

### Q4. Thermit Reaction

**(a) Equation:** [2 marks]
```
2Al(s) + Fe₂O₃(s) → Al₂O₃(s) + 2Fe(l)
```
Award 1 mark for correct products, 1 mark for balanced equation.

**(b) Why aluminium:** [2 marks]
- Aluminium is more reactive than iron (1 mark)
- Aluminium has a stronger tendency to displace iron from its oxide (1 mark)
- OR: The reaction is more exothermic with aluminium (1 mark)

---

### Q5. Position of Metal M [4 marks]

- M is between magnesium and iron in the reactivity series (1 mark)
- Reacts with water: above lead and copper (1 mark)
- Does not displace iron: below iron (1 mark)
- Displaces copper: above copper (1 mark)
- Most likely position: between zinc and iron, or could be zinc itself (1 mark)

---

### Q6. Storage Under Oil [2 marks]

- Sodium and potassium are very reactive (1 mark)
- They react with oxygen and moisture in the air (1 mark)
- Oil prevents contact with air and water (1 mark)

*Maximum 2 marks*

---

### Q7. Equations

**(a) Calcium + water:** [2 marks]
```
Ca(s) + 2H₂O(l) → Ca(OH)₂(aq) + H₂(g)
```

**(b) Zinc + steam:** [2 marks]
```
Zn(s) + H₂O(g) → ZnO(s) + H₂(g)
```

---

### Q8. Reactivity Order [3 marks]

**Order:** Magnesium > Zinc > Iron > Copper (1 mark)

**Evidence (any one):** [2 marks]
- Mg displaces Zn, Fe, and Cu from their salts
- Zn displaces Fe and Cu from their salts
- Fe displaces Cu from its salts
- Cu does not displace any of the others

---

### Q9. Displacement Reaction

**(a) Definition:** [2 marks]
A reaction where a **more reactive element** displaces a **less reactive element** from its compound/solution.

**(b) Example with equation:** [2 marks]
```
Fe(s) + CuSO₄(aq) → FeSO₄(aq) + Cu(s)
```
1 mark for correct reaction, 1 mark for balanced equation.

---

### Q10. Position in Reactivity Series [3 marks]

- The metal is between magnesium and hydrogen (1 mark)
- It reacts with acid (above hydrogen) (1 mark)
- It doesn't react with water (below calcium/magnesium) (1 mark)

*Accept: Between lead and magnesium OR Between zinc and magnesium*

---

### Q11. Thermit as Redox Reaction [3 marks]

- **Redox** means both oxidation and reduction occur (1 mark)
- Aluminium is **oxidized** (loses electrons): Al → Al³⁺ + 3e⁻ (1 mark)
- Iron(III) ions are **reduced** (gain electrons): Fe³⁺ + 3e⁻ → Fe (1 mark)

---

### Q12. Identifying Metals A, B, C [6 marks]

**Metal A = Sodium** [2 marks]
- Reacts vigorously with cold water (matches sodium's behavior)
- Could also be potassium or lithium (accept alternatives)

**Metal B = Zinc** [2 marks]
- Reacts with steam but not cold water (matches zinc/iron)
- Zinc is correct, iron also acceptable

**Metal C = Copper** [2 marks]
- No reaction with water, steam, or dilute acid (matches copper)
- Below hydrogen, so no acid reaction
- Silver and gold also acceptable
