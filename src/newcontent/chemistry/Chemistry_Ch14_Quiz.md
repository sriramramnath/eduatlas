# Chapter 14: Reactivity Series - Quiz Section

## Exam-Style Questions for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)



### Question 1
Which metal reacts most vigorously with cold water?

A) Copper

B) Magnesium

C) Sodium

D) Zinc

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Sodium is more reactive than magnesium and zinc. Potassium would be more vigorous, but it's not an option. Sodium reacts vigorously with cold water, melting into a ball and producing hydrogen.

---

### Question 2
When magnesium reacts with steam, the product formed is:

A) Magnesium hydroxide and hydrogen

B) Magnesium oxide and hydrogen

C) Magnesium oxide and oxygen

D) Magnesium and water

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** With steam, metals form oxides (not hydroxides) due to the higher temperature. Mg + H₂O(g) → MgO + H₂

---

### Question 3
Which of the following metals will NOT react with dilute hydrochloric acid?

A) Zinc

B) Iron

C) Copper

D) Magnesium

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Copper is below hydrogen in the reactivity series, so it does not react with dilute acids. Zinc, iron, and magnesium are all above hydrogen.

---

### Question 4
Iron displaces copper from copper(II) sulfate solution. This is because:

A) Iron is less reactive than copper

B) Iron is more reactive than copper

C) Iron has a higher density than copper

D) Iron is heavier than copper

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** In displacement reactions, a more reactive metal displaces a less reactive metal from its compound.

---

### Question 5
Which metal is stored under oil to prevent reaction with air and moisture?

A) Iron

B) Sodium

C) Copper

D) Zinc

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Sodium is very reactive and would react with air and moisture. It must be stored under oil.

---

### Question 6
The Thermit reaction is used to:

A) Extract sodium from its ore

B) Weld railway tracks together

C) Produce aluminium metal

D) Prevent iron from rusting

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** The Thermit reaction produces molten iron at high temperatures, which is used to weld railway tracks.

---

### Question 7
What is observed when a piece of zinc is added to copper(II) sulfate solution?

A) The solution turns blue and copper is deposited

B) The solution becomes colorless and copper is deposited

C) No reaction occurs

D) Zinc dissolves and no solid is formed

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Zinc displaces copper: Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu. The blue Cu²⁺ ions are removed, leaving colorless Zn²⁺ ions.

---

### Question 8
Which equation correctly represents the reaction of iron with steam?

A) Fe + H₂O → FeO + H₂

B) 2Fe + 3H₂O → Fe₂O₃ + 3H₂

C) 3Fe + 4H₂O → Fe₃O₄ + 4H₂

D) Fe + 2H₂O → Fe(OH)₂ + H₂

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Iron reacts with steam to form triiron tetroxide (Fe₃O₄), not simple FeO or Fe₂O₃.

---

### Question 9
Which metal is found native (uncombined) in the Earth's crust?

A) Sodium

B) Iron

C) Gold

D) Aluminium

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Gold is very unreactive (low in the reactivity series) and is found native (uncombined) in nature.

---

### Question 10
When calcium reacts with cold water, the products are:

A) Calcium oxide and hydrogen

B) Calcium hydroxide and hydrogen

C) Calcium oxide and oxygen

D) Calcium and water vapor

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Ca + 2H₂O → Ca(OH)₂ + H₂. Metals form hydroxides with cold water.

---

### Question 11
In the displacement reaction: Zn + CuSO₄ → ZnSO₄ + Cu, which species is reduced?

A) Zinc

B) Copper ions (Cu²⁺)

C) Zinc ions (Zn²⁺)

D) Sulfate ions

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Reduction is gain of electrons. Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu. Copper ions gain electrons.

---

### Question 12
Which metal is between zinc and lead in the reactivity series?

A) Copper

B) Iron

C) Aluminium

D) Magnesium

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** The order is: Al > Zn > Fe > Sn > Pb > Cu. Iron is between zinc and lead.

## Part B: True/False Questions

**For each statement, state whether it is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.**

---

### Statement 1
Potassium reacts more vigorously with water than sodium.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Potassium is more reactive than sodium and reacts more violently with water.

---

### Statement 2
Magnesium reacts with cold water to produce magnesium oxide.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Magnesium reacts with cold water to produce magnesium hydroxide and hydrogen. (Oxide is produced with steam.)

---

### Statement 3
All metals above hydrogen in the reactivity series react with dilute acids.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Metals above hydrogen react with dilute acids to produce hydrogen gas.

---

### Statement 4
Copper can displace iron from iron(II) sulfate solution.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Copper cannot displace iron because copper is less reactive than iron.

---

### Statement 5
In the Thermit reaction, aluminium is oxidized and iron is reduced.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Aluminium loses electrons (oxidized), and iron(III) ions gain electrons (reduced).

---

### Statement 6
Silver reacts vigorously with dilute hydrochloric acid.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Silver is below hydrogen in the reactivity series and does not react with dilute acids.

---

### Statement 7
When iron reacts with steam, iron(II) oxide is formed.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** When iron reacts with steam, triiron tetroxide (Fe₃O₄) is formed.

---

### Statement 8
Zinc can displace copper from copper(II) sulfate solution.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Zinc is more reactive than copper, so it can displace copper from its compounds.

---

### Statement 9
Lithium is more reactive than potassium.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Lithium is less reactive than potassium. Potassium is the most reactive of the three alkali metals.

---

### Statement 10
Metals below hydrogen in the reactivity series do not react with dilute acids.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Metals below hydrogen cannot displace hydrogen from acids.

---

### Statement 11
The products of sodium reacting with water are sodium hydroxide and hydrogen gas.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** 2Na + 2H₂O → 2NaOH + H₂

---

### Statement 12
Displacement reactions involve oxidation and reduction occurring simultaneously.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** The displacing metal is oxidized (loses electrons) while the displaced ion is reduced (gains electrons).

### Question 1
(a) Write a balanced symbol equation for the reaction between magnesium and dilute hydrochloric acid.

(b) What two observations would you make during this reaction?

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
(a) Balanced equation: Mg(s) + 2HCl(aq) → MgCl₂(aq) + H₂(g). Award 1 mark for correct products, 1 mark for balanced equation with state symbols.
(b) Observations: Rapid fizzing/effervescence (1 mark); Magnesium dissolves/disappears (1 mark)

---

### Question 2
Explain, in terms of electrons, what happens to iron and copper ions in the displacement reaction between iron and copper(II) sulfate.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Iron atoms lose electrons (Fe → Fe²⁺ + 2e⁻) (1 mark); Iron is oxidized (1 mark); Copper ions gain electrons (Cu²⁺ + 2e⁻ → Cu) (1 mark); Copper ions are reduced (1 mark). Maximum 3 marks.

---

### Question 3
Describe how you would use displacement reactions to determine the order of reactivity of three metals: X, Y, and Z.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Add each metal to solutions containing ions of the other two metals (1 mark); Observe which metals displace others (1 mark); The metal that displaces both others is most reactive; the metal displaced by both is least reactive (1 mark)

---

### Question 4
(a) Write a balanced symbol equation for the Thermit reaction.

(b) Why is aluminium used in this reaction rather than zinc?

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
(a) Equation: 2Al(s) + Fe₂O₃(s) → Al₂O₃(s) + 2Fe(l). Award 1 mark for correct products, 1 mark for balanced equation.
(b) Why aluminium: Aluminium is more reactive than iron (1 mark); Aluminium has a stronger tendency to displace iron from its oxide (1 mark)

---

### Question 5
A student adds a piece of metal M to water and observes slow fizzing. When M is added to iron(II) sulfate solution, no reaction occurs. When M is added to copper(II) sulfate solution, a reddish-brown deposit forms. Predict the position of metal M in the reactivity series and explain your reasoning.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
M is between magnesium and iron in the reactivity series (1 mark); Reacts with water: above lead and copper (1 mark); Does not displace iron: below iron (1 mark); Displaces copper: above copper (1 mark); Most likely position: between zinc and iron, or could be zinc itself (1 mark)

---

### Question 6
Explain why sodium and potassium are stored under oil.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Sodium and potassium are very reactive (1 mark); They react with oxygen and moisture in the air (1 mark); Oil prevents contact with air and water (1 mark). Maximum 2 marks.

---

### Question 7
Write balanced symbol equations for:
(a) Calcium reacting with water
(b) Zinc reacting with steam

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
(a) Calcium + water: Ca(s) + 2H₂O(l) → Ca(OH)₂(aq) + H₂(g) (2 marks)
(b) Zinc + steam: Zn(s) + H₂O(g) → ZnO(s) + H₂(g) (2 marks)

---

### Question 8
State the order of reactivity (most reactive first) for the following metals: copper, magnesium, zinc, iron. Give one piece of evidence for this order.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Order: Magnesium > Zinc > Iron > Copper (1 mark); Evidence (any one): Mg displaces Zn, Fe, and Cu from their salts; Zn displaces Fe and Cu; Fe displaces Cu; Cu does not displace any of the others (2 marks)

---

### Question 9
(a) What is meant by the term "displacement reaction"?

(b) Give one example of a displacement reaction with a balanced equation.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
(a) Definition: A reaction where a more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound/solution (2 marks)
(b) Example with equation: Fe(s) + CuSO₄(aq) → FeSO₄(aq) + Cu(s) (2 marks); 1 mark for correct reaction, 1 mark for balanced equation

---

### Question 10
A metal does not react with cold water but reacts slowly with dilute acid. Where might this metal be placed in the reactivity series? Give reasoning.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
The metal is between magnesium and hydrogen (1 mark); It reacts with acid (above hydrogen) (1 mark); It doesn't react with water (below calcium/magnesium) (1 mark). Accept: Between lead and magnesium OR Between zinc and magnesium

---

### Question 11
Explain why the Thermit reaction can be described as a redox reaction.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Redox means both oxidation and reduction occur (1 mark); Aluminium is oxidized (loses electrons): Al → Al³⁺ + 3e⁻ (1 mark); Iron(III) ions are reduced (gain electrons): Fe³⁺ + 3e⁻ → Fe (1 mark)

---

### Question 12
Metal A reacts vigorously with cold water. Metal B reacts with steam but not cold water. Metal C does not react with water, steam, or dilute acid. Identify metals A, B, and C from the list: sodium, zinc, copper, magnesium. Justify your choices.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
Metal A = Sodium: Reacts vigorously with cold water (matches sodium's behavior) (2 marks)
Metal B = Zinc: Reacts with steam but not cold water (matches zinc/iron) (2 marks)
Metal C = Copper: No reaction with water, steam, or dilute acid (below hydrogen, so no acid reaction) (2 marks)
