# IGCSE Chemistry Chapter 23: Crude Oil

## Assessment Quiz
**Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry**

---

## Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (12 marks)

Select the correct answer for each question.

---

### Question 1
Crude oil was formed from:
A) Dinosaurs and land plants
B) Ancient marine organisms
C) Volcanic rocks
D) Coal deposits

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Crude oil formed from ancient marine organisms (plankton and algae) that died and sank to the ocean floor. It did NOT form from dinosaurs.

---

### Question 2
Which statement about fractional distillation is correct?
A) The column is hotter at the top
B) Long-chain hydrocarbons condense near the top
C) Fractions are separated by different boiling points
D) All fractions have the same chain length

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Fractional distillation separates crude oil into fractions based on their different boiling points. The column is hotter at the bottom, and longer chains condense near the bottom where it is hotter.

---

### Question 3
As the chain length of hydrocarbons increases:
A) Boiling point decreases
B) Viscosity decreases
C) Flammability decreases
D) Volatility increases

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** As chain length increases, flammability decreases because longer chains vaporise less easily and don't mix as well with oxygen. Boiling point and viscosity increase, volatility decreases.

---

### Question 4
Which fraction has the highest boiling point?
A) Petrol
B) Kerosene
C) Diesel
D) Bitumen

**Answer: D**

**Explanation:** Bitumen has the longest chain length (C₄₀+) and therefore the highest boiling point (above 400°C). It is collected at the bottom of the fractionating column.

---

### Question 5
The main products of cracking are:
A) Only alkanes
B) Only alkenes
C) Shorter alkanes and alkenes
D) Carbon dioxide and water

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Cracking breaks long-chain alkanes into shorter-chain alkanes AND alkenes. For example: C₁₄H₃₀ → C₈H₁₈ + C₆H₁₂.

---

### Question 6
Which equation correctly represents cracking?
A) C₈H₁₈ → C₄H₁₀ + C₄H₈
B) C₈H₁₈ + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O
C) C₈H₁₈ → C₈ + H₁₈
D) C₄H₁₀ + C₄H₈ → C₈H₁₈

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Cracking breaks one alkane into a shorter alkane and an alkene. The equation C₈H₁₈ → C₄H₁₀ + C₄H₈ is balanced and shows the correct products.

---

### Question 7
Complete combustion of methane produces:
A) Carbon and water
B) Carbon monoxide and water
C) Carbon dioxide and water
D) Carbon dioxide and hydrogen

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Complete combustion with plentiful oxygen produces carbon dioxide and water: CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O.

---

### Question 8
Incomplete combustion of hydrocarbons occurs when:
A) There is excess oxygen
B) Oxygen supply is limited
C) Temperature is very high
D) A catalyst is used

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Incomplete combustion occurs when there is insufficient (limited) oxygen. This produces carbon monoxide (CO) and/or carbon (soot) instead of carbon dioxide.

---

### Question 9
Why do longer chain hydrocarbons have higher boiling points?
A) They have stronger covalent bonds
B) They have stronger intermolecular forces
C) They have more atoms in the molecule
D) They are more flammable

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Longer chain hydrocarbons have more electrons and greater surface area, leading to stronger van der Waals (intermolecular) forces that require more energy to overcome.

---

### Question 10
Catalytic cracking uses:
A) Very high pressure
B) A zeolite catalyst
C) Low temperature
D) An iron catalyst

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Catalytic cracking uses a zeolite catalyst (aluminium silicate) at moderate temperature (450-550°C) and slightly above atmospheric pressure.

---

### Question 11
Which is a use of the naphtha fraction?
A) Road surfacing
B) Jet fuel
C) Chemical feedstock for making plastics
D) Ship fuel

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Naphtha (C₆-C₁₂) is mainly used as a chemical feedstock for making plastics and other chemicals.

---

### Question 12
Which product of incomplete combustion is toxic?
A) Carbon dioxide
B) Carbon monoxide
C) Water vapour
D) Oxygen

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Carbon monoxide (CO) is a toxic, colourless, odourless gas that binds to haemoglobin in blood, preventing oxygen transport.

---

## Section B: True/False Questions (12 marks)

State whether each statement is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.

---

### Question 13
Crude oil is a renewable resource.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Crude oil is a **finite (non-renewable) resource** because it takes millions of years to form and cannot be replaced on a human timescale.

---

### Question 14
Crude oil consists mainly of alkanes.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Crude oil is a mixture of hydrocarbons, predominantly alkanes (saturated hydrocarbons with single bonds only).

---

### Question 15
Short-chain hydrocarbons are more viscous than long-chain hydrocarbons.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Short-chain hydrocarbons are **less viscous** (runnier) than long-chain hydrocarbons. Long chains are more viscous because they tangle together.

---

### Question 16
In a fractionating column, the temperature decreases from bottom to top.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** The column is hottest at the bottom (where crude oil vapour enters) and gets progressively cooler towards the top.

---

### Question 17
Cracking converts short-chain alkanes into long-chain alkanes.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Cracking converts **long-chain alkanes into shorter-chain alkanes** (and alkenes). This is because short-chain hydrocarbons are in higher demand.

---

### Question 18
The petrol fraction has a lower boiling point than the diesel fraction.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Petrol (C₅-C₁₀) has a boiling point range of 40-70°C, while diesel (C₁₅-C₂₀) has a boiling point range of 200-300°C.

---

### Question 19
Complete combustion produces a yellow, sooty flame.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Complete combustion produces a **blue flame**. A yellow, sooty flame is characteristic of **incomplete combustion**.

---

### Question 20
Bitumen is used for road surfacing.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Bitumen, the fraction with the longest chains and highest boiling point, is used for road surfacing and roofing.

---

### Question 21
Alkenes are produced during cracking.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Cracking produces both shorter alkanes and alkenes (unsaturated hydrocarbons). Alkenes are useful for making polymers.

---

### Question 22
Thermal cracking uses a catalyst.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Thermal cracking uses **high temperature and high pressure without a catalyst**. **Catalytic cracking** uses a zeolite catalyst.

---

### Question 23
Carbon monoxide is produced during complete combustion.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Carbon monoxide is produced during **incomplete combustion**. Complete combustion produces **carbon dioxide** and water.

---

### Question 24
Hydrocarbons with longer chains are more flammable.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Hydrocarbons with **shorter chains** are more flammable. Short chains vaporise more easily and mix better with oxygen in the air.

---

## Section C: Short Answer Questions (12 marks)

Answer each question clearly. Show your working where appropriate.

---

### Question 25
Explain how crude oil was formed.
*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Marine organisms (plankton/algae) died and sank to ocean floor (1 mark)
- Buried under layers of sediment/sand/mud (1 mark)
- High pressure and temperature over millions of years with no oxygen (1 mark)

---

### Question 26
Describe how fractional distillation separates crude oil into fractions.
*(4 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Crude oil is heated/vaporised (1 mark)
- Vapour enters fractionating column (which is hotter at bottom, cooler at top) (1 mark)
- Vapours rise and cool (1 mark)
- Different fractions condense at different levels based on their boiling points (1 mark)

---

### Question 27
Write a balanced equation for the complete combustion of propane (C₃H₈).
*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Correct equation: C₃H₈ + 5O₂ → 3CO₂ + 4H₂O (2 marks)
- If unbalanced or products incorrect: 0-1 mark depending on partial credit

---

### Question 28
Explain why longer chain hydrocarbons have higher boiling points than shorter chain hydrocarbons.
*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Longer chains have more electrons/greater surface area (1 mark)
- Stronger van der Waals/intermolecular forces between molecules (1 mark)
- More energy required to overcome these forces (1 mark)

*Do NOT award marks for mentioning stronger covalent bonds*

---

### Question 29
State two reasons why cracking is important.
*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
Any two from:
- Matches supply with demand (1 mark)
- Converts less useful long-chain hydrocarbons into useful short-chain hydrocarbons (1 mark)
- Produces alkenes needed for polymer/plastic production (1 mark)

---

### Question 30
Write an equation for the cracking of C₁₀H₂₂ to produce ethene (C₂H₄) and one other product.
*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Correct balanced equation: C₁₀H₂₂ → C₂H₄ + C₈H₁₈ (2 marks)
- Or C₁₀H₂₂ → C₂H₄ + C₈H₁₈ can have different products if balanced
- Understanding that products are alkane + alkene (1 mark if unbalanced)

---

### Question 31
Compare complete and incomplete combustion of hydrocarbons. Include the conditions, products, and type of flame for each.
*(4 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
**Complete combustion:**
- Plentiful/excess oxygen (1 mark)
- Products: CO₂ and H₂O; blue flame (1 mark)

**Incomplete combustion:**
- Limited/insufficient oxygen (1 mark)
- Products: CO and/or C + H₂O; yellow/orange sooty flame (1 mark)

---

### Question 32
Describe the trend in volatility as hydrocarbon chain length increases. Explain this trend.
*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Volatility decreases as chain length increases (1 mark)
- Longer chains have stronger intermolecular forces/van der Waals forces (1 mark)
- More energy needed to vaporise/more difficult to evaporate (1 mark)

---

### Question 33
State the main use of each of the following fractions:
a) Kerosene
b) Bitumen
*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
a) Kerosene: jet fuel / aviation fuel / paraffin heaters (1 mark)
b) Bitumen: road surfacing / roofing / waterproofing (1 mark)

---

### Question 34
Explain why carbon monoxide is dangerous.
*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Binds to haemoglobin in red blood cells (1 mark)
- Prevents oxygen transport in blood / reduces oxygen-carrying capacity of blood (1 mark)

---

### Question 35
A hydrocarbon C₁₄H₃₀ is cracked to produce two molecules of ethene (C₂H₄) and one other product. Write a balanced equation for this reaction and name the other product.
*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Working: 14 C atoms total, 2 × 2 = 4 C in ethenes, so product has 10 C atoms (1 mark)
- 30 H atoms total, 2 × 4 = 8 H in ethenes, so product has 22 H atoms (1 mark)
- Equation: C₁₄H₃₀ → 2C₂H₄ + C₁₀H₂₂ (1 mark)
- Product is decane (optional, bonus if named correctly)

---

### Question 36
Give one advantage of catalytic cracking compared to thermal cracking.
*(1 mark)*

**Mark Scheme:**
Any one from:
- Lower pressure required (safer/cheaper) (1 mark)
- Produces more useful branched alkanes (better for petrol) (1 mark)
- More efficient/faster reaction (1 mark)
- Lower operating costs (1 mark)
