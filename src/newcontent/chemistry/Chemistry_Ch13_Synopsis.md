# Chapter 13: Gases in the Atmosphere
## Concise Synopsis - Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

---

## 1. Composition of Clean, Dry Air

| Gas | Formula | Percentage by Volume |
|-----|---------|---------------------|
| Nitrogen | N₂ | ~78% |
| Oxygen | O₂ | ~21% |
| Argon | Ar | ~0.93% |
| Carbon dioxide | CO₂ | ~0.04% |

**Key Point:** Nitrogen is the most abundant gas, followed by oxygen.

---

## 2. Fractional Distillation of Air

### Process Steps
1. **Filter** - Remove dust
2. **Compress and cool** - To ~200 atm, then cool
3. **Remove CO₂ and H₂O** - They freeze and block pipes
4. **Cool to below -200°C** - Air becomes liquid
5. **Fractional distillation** - Separate by boiling points

### Boiling Points and Collection Order

| Gas | Boiling Point | Collected |
|-----|---------------|-----------|
| Nitrogen | -196°C | 1st (top, lowest BP) |
| Argon | -186°C | 2nd (middle) |
| Oxygen | -183°C | 3rd (bottom, highest BP) |

---

## 3. Gas Tests Summary

| Gas | Test Method | Positive Result |
|-----|-------------|-----------------|
| **Oxygen (O₂)** | Glowing splint | Relights (bursts into flame) |
| **Hydrogen (H₂)** | Lighted splint | "Squeaky pop" sound |
| **Carbon dioxide (CO₂)** | Bubble through limewater | Turns milky/cloudy |
| **Ammonia (NH₃)** | Damp red litmus paper | Turns blue (alkaline) |
| **Chlorine (Cl₂)** | Damp blue litmus paper | Turns red, then white (bleached) |

---

## 4. Limewater Test Equations

**Normal test:**
$$Ca(OH)_2 + CO_2 \rightarrow CaCO_3 + H_2O$$
(Calcium carbonate = white precipitate = milky)

**With excess CO₂:**
$$CaCO_3 + CO_2 + H_2O \rightarrow Ca(HCO_3)_2$$
(Solution clears - soluble calcium hydrogencarbonate)

---

## 5. Combustion

| Type | Condition | Products |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **Complete** | Sufficient O₂ | CO₂ + H₂O |
| **Incomplete** | Insufficient O₂ | CO and/or C (soot) + H₂O |

**Complete combustion (methane):**
$$CH_4 + 2O_2 \rightarrow CO_2 + 2H_2O$$

**Incomplete combustion:**
$$2CH_4 + 3O_2 \rightarrow 2CO + 4H_2O$$
$$CH_4 + O_2 \rightarrow C + 2H_2O$$

---

## 6. Atmospheric Pollutants

| Pollutant | Source | Effect |
|-----------|--------|--------|
| **Carbon monoxide (CO)** | Incomplete combustion | Toxic, reduces blood O₂ capacity |
| **Sulfur dioxide (SO₂)** | Burning sulfur-containing fuels | Acid rain, respiratory problems |
| **Nitrogen oxides (NOₓ)** | High temp engines | Acid rain, smog |
| **Particulates** | Incomplete combustion | Respiratory problems |
| **Carbon dioxide (CO₂)** | Combustion, respiration | Greenhouse effect |

---

## 7. Acid Rain Formation

**Sources:** SO₂ and NOₓ from combustion

**Formation:**
$$SO_2 + H_2O \rightarrow H_2SO_3$$
$$2SO_2 + O_2 + 2H_2O \rightarrow 2H_2SO_4$$
$$4NO_2 + 2H_2O + O_2 \rightarrow 4HNO_3$$

**Effects:**
- Damages limestone buildings
- Harms plants and aquatic life
- Leaches minerals from soil

---

## 8. Greenhouse Effect

### Process
1. Sun emits short-wave radiation → passes through atmosphere
2. Earth absorbs radiation and warms
3. Earth emits long-wave infrared radiation
4. Greenhouse gases absorb infrared → trap heat

### Greenhouse Gases

| Gas | Source | Warming Potential |
|-----|--------|-------------------|
| CO₂ | Fossil fuels, deforestation | 1 (reference) |
| CH₄ | Agriculture, landfill | ~28× CO₂ |
| N₂O | Fertilizers | ~265× CO₂ |
| CFCs | Old refrigerants | ~10,000× CO₂ |

---

## 9. Climate Change Effects

| Category | Effects |
|----------|---------|
| **Environmental** | Rising sea levels, melting ice caps, ocean acidification, biodiversity loss |
| **Human** | Food insecurity, water scarcity, health impacts, economic damage |

---

## 10. Reducing Climate Change

### Reducing Emissions
- Renewable energy (solar, wind, hydro)
- Nuclear power
- Energy efficiency
- Electric vehicles
- Carbon capture and storage

### Carbon Sinks
- **Forests** - Trees absorb CO₂ via photosynthesis
- **Oceans** - Dissolve CO₂
- **Soil** - Stores carbon in organic matter

---

## 11. Exam Tips

✅ **DO:**
- Remember: N₂ = 78%, O₂ = 21%
- Learn gas tests and their specific results
- Know boiling point order: N₂ < Ar < O₂
- Include equations for limewater test

❌ **DON'T:**
- Confuse oxygen test (glowing splint) with hydrogen test (lighted splint)
- Forget that air must be liquefied before fractional distillation
- Mix up SO₂ sources (coal/oil) with NOₓ sources (car engines)
- Confuse greenhouse effect with ozone depletion
