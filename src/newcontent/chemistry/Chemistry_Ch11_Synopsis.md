## 🔑 Essential Points

### Physical Properties
- **Soft metals** - can be cut with a knife
- **Low density** - Li, Na, K float on water
- **Low melting points** - decrease down the group
- **Good conductors** of heat and electricity
- **Silvery appearance** - tarnish quickly in air

### Electronic Structure
- **All have ONE electron in outer shell**
- Form **M⁺ ions** (lose one electron)
- Electronic structure ends in **...s¹**

---

## 📈 Trends Down Group 1

| Property | Trend | Reason |
|----------|-------|--------|
| Reactivity | **INCREASES** ↓ | Outer electron further from nucleus, easier to lose |
| Melting point | **DECREASES** ↓ | Weaker metallic bonding |
| Density | **INCREASES** ↓ | Atomic mass increases more than size |
| Softness | **INCREASES** ↓ | Weaker metallic bonding |

---

## ⚗️ Key Reactions

### With Water
```
2M + 2H₂O → 2MOH + H₂
```
- **All** alkali metals react with water
- Produce **metal hydroxide** (alkaline) + **hydrogen**
- Reactivity increases down group: Li < Na < K < Rb < Cs

| Metal | Observation |
|-------|-------------|
| Li | Fizzes gently, moves slowly |
| Na | Melts into ball, moves rapidly |
| K | Catches fire (lilac flame), explosive |
| Rb, Cs | Explosive/violent explosion |

### With Oxygen
```
4Li + O₂ → 2Li₂O (oxide)
4Na + O₂ → 2Na₂O (oxide)
```
- Form oxides, peroxides, or superoxides
- Burn with characteristic flame colours

### With Chlorine
```
2M + Cl₂ → 2MCl
```
- Form white ionic chlorides
- Vigorous reaction with bright white flame

---

## 🛡️ Safety & Storage

- **Stored under oil** - prevents reaction with air and moisture
- **Never touch with bare hands**
- **Never use water** to extinguish fires - use **dry sand**
- **Wear eye protection** and gloves

##  Common Exam Pitfalls

1. ❌ "Reactivity decreases down group" → ✅ **Increases**
2. ❌ "Melting points increase down group" → ✅ **Decrease**
3. ❌ Forgetting H₂ in water reaction equation
4. ❌ Wrong formula: LiO → ✅ **Li₂O**
5. ❌ Confusing flame colours between Li and K
