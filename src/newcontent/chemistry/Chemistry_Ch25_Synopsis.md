# Chapter 25: Alkenes - Synopsis

## Quick Revision Summary for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

---

## The Essentials at a Glance

### What are Alkenes?
- **Unsaturated hydrocarbons** with a C=C double bond
- **General formula**: CnH2n
- **More reactive** than alkanes due to the double bond

---

## Key Facts Table

| Property | Details |
|----------|---------|
| **General Formula** | CnH2n (n ≥ 2) |
| **Functional Group** | C=C (carbon-carbon double bond) |
| **Type** | Unsaturated hydrocarbon |
| **First Member** | Ethene (C2H4) |
| **Main Reaction Type** | Addition reactions |

---

## First Three Alkenes

| Name | Molecular Formula | Displayed Formula |

| Propene | C3H6 | CH2=CH-CH3 |
| But-1-ene | C4H8 | CH2=CH-CH2-CH3 |
| But-2-ene | C4H8 | CH3-CH=CH-CH3 |

---

## Addition Reactions Summary

| Reaction | Reagent | Product Type | Conditions |
|----------|---------|--------------|------------|
| **Hydrogenation** | H2 | Alkane | Ni catalyst, 150°C |
| **Hydration** | H2O (steam) | Alcohol | H3PO4 catalyst, 300°C, 60-70 atm |
| **Halogenation** | Br2 or Cl2 | Dihaloalkane | Room temperature |

---

## Reaction Equations

### Hydrogenation
```
Ethene + Hydrogen → Ethane
C2H4 + H2 → C2H6
Conditions: Nickel catalyst, 150°C
```

### Hydration
```
Ethene + Steam ⇌ Ethanol
C2H4 + H2O → C2H5OH
Conditions: Phosphoric acid catalyst, 300°C, 60-70 atm
```

### Halogenation
```
Ethene + Bromine → 1,2-dibromoethane
C2H4 + Br2 → C2H4Br2
Conditions: Room temperature
```

## Bromine Water Test

| Test | Alkene Result | Alkane Result |
|------|---------------|---------------|
| Add bromine water | Orange → Colorless | Stays orange |
| Explanation | Addition reaction occurs | No reaction |

**Key Phrase**: "The bromine water decolorizes because it adds across the C=C double bond"

---

## Uses of Alkenes

### Ethene
- Making **ethanol** (hydration)
- Making **poly(ethene)** (polymerization)
- Ripening fruit

### Propene
- Making **poly(propene)**
- Making **propanone** (acetone solvent)

## Alkenes vs Alkanes Comparison

| Feature | Alkenes | Alkanes |
|---------|---------|---------|
| Formula | CnH2n | CnH2n+2 |
| Bonds | C=C double bond | Only C-C single bonds |
| Type | Unsaturated | Saturated |
| Reactivity | More reactive | Less reactive |
| Main reaction | Addition | Substitution |
| Bromine water | Decolorizes | No change |

---

## Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Alkene** | Unsaturated hydrocarbon with C=C double bond |
| **Unsaturated** | Contains double bond; can undergo addition reactions |
| **Addition reaction** | Two molecules combine to form one product |
| **Hydrogenation** | Addition of hydrogen across C=C |
| **Hydration** | Addition of water across C=C |

---

## Exam Tips

1. **Always state conditions**: Catalyst, temperature, pressure where required
2. **Bromine water**: Write "orange/brown to colorless" not just "clear"
3. **Naming**: Use lowest number for double bond position (but-1-ene, not but-3-ene)
4. **Addition reactions**: Remember - only ONE product forms
5. **Drawing**: Each carbon in C=C has two other bonds

---

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ CnH2n+2 for alkenes → ✓ CnH2n
❌ "Turns clear" for bromine test → ✓ "Orange/brown to colorless"
❌ Forgetting catalysts → ✓ State all conditions
❌ but-3-ene → ✓ but-1-ene (use lowest number)

---

## Quick Memory Aid

**"Alkenes are UNsaturated with a double bond, CnH2n"**

**"ADDition reactions ADD atoms across the double bond"**

**"Bromine water loses its ORANGE color when it ADDS to the double bond"**
