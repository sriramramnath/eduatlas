## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)



### Question 1
Which statement about exothermic reactions is correct?

A) ΔH is positive and the temperature of the surroundings decreases
B) ΔH is negative and the temperature of the surroundings increases
C) ΔH is positive and the temperature of the surroundings increases
D) ΔH is negative and the temperature of the surroundings decreases

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Exothermic reactions release energy to the surroundings, causing the temperature to increase. The enthalpy change (ΔH) is negative because energy is lost from the system to the surroundings.

---

### Question 2
Which of the following is an endothermic reaction?

A) Combustion of methane
B) Neutralisation of hydrochloric acid with sodium hydroxide
C) Photosynthesis
D) Oxidation of magnesium

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Photosynthesis (6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂) requires energy input from sunlight, making it endothermic. Combustion, neutralisation, and oxidation are all exothermic processes.

---

### Question 3
In a chemical reaction, what happens when bonds are broken?

A) Energy is released and the process is exothermic
B) Energy is absorbed and the process is endothermic
C) Energy is released and the process is endothermic
D) Energy is absorbed and the process is exothermic

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Breaking bonds always requires energy input. This energy is absorbed from the surroundings, making bond breaking an endothermic process.

---

### Question 4
What is activation energy?

A) The energy released when products form
B) The total energy change of a reaction
C) The minimum energy particles must have to react successfully
D) The energy absorbed when bonds break

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Activation energy (Ea) is defined as the minimum amount of energy that colliding particles must have for a reaction to occur. It represents the energy barrier that must be overcome.

---

### Question 5
In an energy level diagram for an exothermic reaction, the products are:

A) Higher than the reactants
B) At the same level as the reactants
C) Lower than the reactants
D) At the activation energy level

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** In exothermic reactions, energy is released, so the products have less energy than the reactants. Therefore, on an energy level diagram, the products are at a lower level than the reactants.

---

### Question 6
Which reaction is used in disposable hand warmers?

A) Dissolving ammonium nitrate in water
B) Crystallisation of sodium ethanoate
C) Oxidation of iron powder
D) Combustion of methane

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Disposable hand warmers contain iron powder that oxidises (rusts) when exposed to air. This exothermic reaction (Fe + O₂ → Fe₂O₃) releases heat. Ammonium nitrate dissolving is endothermic and used in cold packs.

---

### Question 7
Calculate ΔH for a reaction where:
- Total energy to break bonds = 1500 kJ/mol
- Total energy released when bonds form = 2000 kJ/mol

A) +3500 kJ/mol
B) −3500 kJ/mol
C) +500 kJ/mol
D) −500 kJ/mol

**Answer: D**

**Explanation:** ΔH = Energy in − Energy out = 1500 − 2000 = −500 kJ/mol. The negative sign indicates an exothermic reaction.

---

### Question 8
Which statement about activation energy is correct?

A) Only endothermic reactions need activation energy
B) Only exothermic reactions need activation energy
C) Both exothermic and endothermic reactions need activation energy
D) Neither exothermic nor endothermic reactions need activation energy

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** ALL chemical reactions require activation energy to begin, regardless of whether they are exothermic or endothermic. This is the energy needed to break initial bonds and reach the activated complex.

---

### Question 9
The bond energy of H–H is 436 kJ/mol. What does this value represent?

A) Energy released when one H–H bond forms
B) Energy required to break one H–H bond
C) Energy required to break one mole of H–H bonds
D) Total energy in one mole of hydrogen gas

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Bond energy is defined as the energy required to break one mole of a specific covalent bond. Therefore, 436 kJ is needed to break one mole of H–H bonds (not just one bond).

---

### Question 10
Which process occurs in a self-heating can of coffee?

A) NH₄NO₃ dissolving in water (endothermic)
B) CaO reacting with H₂O (exothermic)
C) Photosynthesis (endothermic)
D) Thermal decomposition of CaCO₃ (endothermic)

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Self-heating cans use the exothermic reaction between calcium oxide (quicklime) and water: CaO + H₂O → Ca(OH)₂. This releases heat to warm the food or drink.

---

### Question 11
When calculating energy changes using bond energies, which formula should be used?

A) ΔH = Bonds formed − Bonds broken
B) ΔH = Bonds broken + Bonds formed
C) ΔH = Bonds broken − Bonds formed
D) ΔH = Bonds formed × Bonds broken

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** The formula is ΔH = Σ(bonds broken) − Σ(bonds formed). This accounts for energy absorbed to break bonds (positive) minus energy released when bonds form (which is subtracted).

---

### Question 12
What is the sign of ΔH for the reaction: NH₄NO₃(s) → NH₄⁺(aq) + NO₃⁻(aq)?

A) Zero
B) Positive (+)
C) Negative (−)
D) Cannot be determined

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** This is the dissolving of ammonium nitrate used in cold packs. It absorbs energy from the surroundings (feels cold), making it endothermic with a positive ΔH.

## Part B: True/False Questions

**For each statement, state whether it is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.**

---

### Question 1
**Statement:** Breaking chemical bonds is an exothermic process.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Breaking chemical bonds is an **endothermic** process. Energy must be absorbed to overcome the attractive forces between atoms in a bond.

---

### Question 2
**Statement:** The enthalpy change (ΔH) for an exothermic reaction is negative.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Exothermic reactions release energy to the surroundings, so energy is lost from the system. This is represented by a negative ΔH value.

---

### Question 3
**Statement:** Activation energy is only needed for endothermic reactions.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Activation energy is needed for **ALL** chemical reactions, both exothermic and endothermic. It is the energy barrier that must be overcome for any reaction to begin.

---

### Question 4
**Statement:** In an endothermic reaction, the products have less energy than the reactants.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** In an endothermic reaction, the products have **more energy** than the reactants because energy has been absorbed from the surroundings.

---

### Question 5
**Statement:** Combustion reactions are exothermic.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Combustion is the reaction of a substance with oxygen, releasing large amounts of energy in the form of heat and light. All combustion reactions are exothermic.

---

### Question 6
**Statement:** Bond energy is measured in joules per bond.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Bond energy is measured in **kilojoules per mole (kJ/mol)**. It represents the energy required to break one mole of a particular bond, not a single bond.

---

### Question 7
**Statement:** Making new bonds releases energy.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** When new bonds form, atoms come together and release the energy that was previously required to keep them apart. Bond formation is always exothermic.

---

### Question 8
**Statement:** The activated complex exists at the highest energy point on a reaction profile.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** The activated complex (or transition state) is the temporary, unstable arrangement of atoms that exists at the peak of the activation energy barrier.

---

### Question 9
**Statement:** Photosynthesis is an exothermic reaction.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Photosynthesis is an **endothermic** reaction. It requires energy input from sunlight to convert carbon dioxide and water into glucose and oxygen.

---

### Question 10
**Statement:** A reaction with ΔH = −500 kJ/mol releases 500 kJ of energy per mole of reactant.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** The negative sign indicates energy is released (exothermic), and the value tells us 500 kJ of energy is released per mole of reaction.

---

### Question 11
**Statement:** The units for enthalpy change are kJ.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The units for enthalpy change are **kJ/mol** (kilojoules per mole). The "per mole" is essential as it indicates the energy change per mole of reaction.

---

### Question 12
**Statement:** Neutralisation reactions between acids and alkalis are exothermic.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** When an acid reacts with an alkali, H⁺ ions react with OH⁻ ions to form water. This reaction releases energy, making all neutralisation reactions exothermic.

### Question 12 (1 mark)
For the reaction: H₂ + Cl₂ → 2HCl

Given: H–H bond energy = 436 kJ/mol, Cl–Cl bond energy = 243 kJ/mol, H–Cl bond energy = 432 kJ/mol

Calculate the enthalpy change for the reaction.

**Mark Scheme:**
- Bonds broken: H–H (436) + Cl–Cl (243) = 679 kJ/mol (1 mark for working)
- Bonds formed: 2 × H–Cl = 2 × 432 = 864 kJ/mol (shown)
- ΔH = 679 − 864 = −185 kJ/mol (final answer with correct sign and unit)
