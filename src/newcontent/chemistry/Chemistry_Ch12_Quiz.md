## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
What is the physical state of bromine at room temperature?

A) Solid
B) Liquid
C) Gas
D) Plasma

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** At room temperature (20°C), bromine is a red-brown liquid. Fluorine and chlorine are gases, while iodine is a solid. Bromine is the only liquid halogen at room temperature.

---

### Question 2
Which statement correctly describes the trend in reactivity in Group 7?

A) Reactivity increases down the group
B) Reactivity decreases down the group
C) Reactivity stays the same down the group
D) Reactivity increases then decreases down the group

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Reactivity decreases down Group 7. Fluorine is the most reactive, and iodine is the least reactive. This is because atomic radius increases and shielding increases, making it harder to attract an extra electron.

---

### Question 3
What is the colour of the precipitate formed when silver nitrate solution is added to a solution containing iodide ions?

A) White
B) Cream
C) Yellow
D) Blue

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** When silver nitrate (AgNO₃) is added to a solution containing iodide ions (I⁻), a yellow precipitate of silver iodide (AgI) is formed. Chloride gives a white precipitate, and bromide gives a cream precipitate.

---

### Question 4
Which halogen can displace bromine from sodium bromide solution?

A) Iodine
B) Astatine
C) Chlorine
D) None of the above

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** A more reactive halogen can displace a less reactive halogen from its halide solution. Chlorine is more reactive than bromine (higher in Group 7), so it can displace bromine from sodium bromide. Iodine and astatine are less reactive than bromine.

---

### Question 5
What type of bond is formed when chlorine reacts with sodium?

A) Covalent bond
B) Ionic bond
C) Metallic bond
D) Hydrogen bond

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** When chlorine (a non-metal) reacts with sodium (a metal), sodium transfers its outer electron to chlorine. This creates Na⁺ and Cl⁻ ions held together by ionic bonding - the electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions.

---

### Question 6
Why do boiling points increase down Group 7?

A) Covalent bonds get stronger
B) Ionic bonds get stronger
C) Van der Waals' forces get stronger
D) Metallic bonds get stronger

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Halogens exist as diatomic molecules (F₂, Cl₂, Br₂, I₂). Between these molecules are van der Waals' forces (London dispersion forces). As molecules get larger down the group, these forces become stronger, requiring more energy to overcome, so boiling points increase.

---

### Question 7
What observation would be made when chlorine is added to sodium iodide solution?

A) The solution remains colourless
B) The solution turns orange-brown
C) The solution turns brown
D) A white precipitate forms

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Chlorine displaces iodine from sodium iodide because chlorine is more reactive. The equation is: Cl₂ + 2NaI → 2NaCl + I₂. Iodine (I₂) is produced, which turns the solution brown.

---

### Question 8
Which of the following correctly shows the electronic configuration of a chloride ion (Cl⁻)?

A) 2.7
B) 2.8
C) 2.8.7
D) 2.8.8

**Answer: D**

**Explanation:** A chlorine atom has electronic configuration 2.8.7. When it gains one electron to form Cl⁻, it achieves a stable noble gas configuration (same as argon) with electronic configuration 2.8.8.

---

### Question 9
What is the formula of hydrogen bromide?

A) HBr
B) H₂Br
C) HBr₂
D) H₂Br₂

**Answer: A**

**Explanation:** Hydrogen bromide has the formula HBr. Hydrogen forms one bond, and bromine can form one bond by sharing an electron pair. It is a covalent molecule.

---

### Question 10
Which halogen is used in water treatment to kill bacteria?

A) Fluorine
B) Chlorine
C) Bromine
D) Iodine

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Chlorine is widely used in water treatment plants and swimming pools to kill bacteria and make water safe to drink. It is effective as a disinfectant and is used in relatively small, safe quantities.

---

### Question 11
Which silver halide precipitate is soluble in dilute ammonia solution?

A) Silver fluoride
B) Silver chloride
C) Silver bromide
D) Silver iodide

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Silver chloride (AgCl) is the only silver halide precipitate that is soluble in dilute ammonia solution. Silver bromide requires concentrated ammonia, and silver iodide is insoluble in ammonia.

---

### Question 12
What happens when bromine is added to sodium chloride solution?

A) Chlorine is displaced
B) Sodium bromide is formed
C) No reaction occurs
D) A white precipitate forms

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** No reaction occurs because bromine is less reactive than chlorine. A halogen can only displace halides of less reactive halogens (those below it in Group 7). Bromine cannot displace chlorine.

## Section B: True or False Questions (12 Questions)

### Question 1
Halogens exist as diatomic molecules.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Correction if FALSE:** N/A - Statement is correct.

**Explanation:** All halogens exist as diatomic molecules: F₂, Cl₂, Br₂, and I₂. This is because each halogen atom needs one more electron to complete its outer shell, so two atoms share a pair of electrons.

---

### Question 2
Fluorine is the least reactive halogen.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Fluorine is the **most** reactive halogen.

**Explanation:** Reactivity decreases down Group 7. Fluorine, being at the top of the group, is the most reactive halogen. It has the smallest atomic radius and least shielding, making it easiest to attract an extra electron.

---

### Question 3
Chlorine is a greenish-yellow gas at room temperature.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Correction if FALSE:** N/A - Statement is correct.

**Explanation:** Chlorine is a greenish-yellow gas with a characteristic pungent smell. It is denser than air and is toxic, so it must be handled carefully in a fume cupboard.

---

### Question 4
Iodine turns from a solid directly to a purple vapour when heated, without passing through a liquid phase.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Correction if FALSE:** N/A - Statement is correct.

**Explanation:** Iodine undergoes sublimation when heated - it changes directly from a solid to a purple vapour without becoming a liquid first. This is a characteristic physical property of iodine.

---

### Question 5
A more reactive halogen can displace a more reactive halogen from its halide solution.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** A more reactive halogen can displace a **less** reactive halogen from its halide solution.

**Explanation:** In displacement reactions, a more reactive halogen (higher in Group 7) can displace a less reactive halogen (lower in Group 7) from its compounds. For example, chlorine can displace bromine or iodine, but iodine cannot displace chlorine.

---

### Question 6
The melting points of halogens decrease down the group.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The melting points of halogens **increase** down the group.

**Explanation:** Melting points increase down Group 7 because van der Waals' forces between molecules become stronger as the molecules get larger. More energy is needed to overcome these forces, resulting in higher melting points.

---

### Question 7
Silver bromide precipitate is yellow in colour.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Silver bromide precipitate is **cream** (pale yellow) in colour.

**Explanation:** Silver chloride is white, silver bromide is cream, and silver iodide is yellow. These different colours help identify which halide ion is present in a solution.

---

### Question 8
Hydrogen chloride gas dissolves in water to form hydrochloric acid.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Correction if FALSE:** N/A - Statement is correct.

**Explanation:** When hydrogen chloride gas (HCl) dissolves in water, it ionizes to form H⁺ and Cl⁻ ions, creating hydrochloric acid (HCl(aq)). This is why hydrogen chloride is acidic only in aqueous solution.

---

### Question 9
Chlorine can displace both bromide and iodide ions from their solutions.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Correction if FALSE:** N/A - Statement is correct.

**Explanation:** Chlorine is more reactive than both bromine and iodine. Therefore, it can displace both Br⁻ and I⁻ from their halide solutions: Cl₂ + 2Br⁻ → 2Cl⁻ + Br₂ and Cl₂ + 2I⁻ → 2Cl⁻ + I₂.

---

### Question 10
Bromine can displace chloride ions from sodium chloride solution.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Bromine **cannot** displace chloride ions from sodium chloride solution.

**Explanation:** Bromine is less reactive than chlorine. A halogen can only displace halides of less reactive halogens (those below it in Group 7). Since chlorine is above bromine, bromine cannot displace it.

---

### Question 11
Iodine is used as an antiseptic to clean wounds.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Correction if FALSE:** N/A - Statement is correct.

**Explanation:** Iodine solution (often in the form of povidone-iodine) is commonly used as an antiseptic to clean wounds and prevent infection. It kills bacteria and other microorganisms.

---

### Question 12
When a halogen atom gains an electron, it forms a positive ion.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** When a halogen atom gains an electron, it forms a **negative** ion (anion).

**Explanation:** Halogens have 7 electrons in their outer shell. When they gain one electron, they achieve a stable octet but now have one more electron than protons, giving them a -1 charge (e.g., F⁻, Cl⁻, Br⁻, I⁻).

## Section C: Short Answer Questions (12 Questions)

### Question 1
Describe the trend in boiling points of the halogens and explain why this trend occurs. [4 marks]

**Mark Scheme:**
- Boiling points increase down the group (1 mark)
- Van der Waals' forces between molecules (1 mark)
- Molecules get larger down the group (1 mark)
- Forces become stronger, more energy needed to overcome them (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** The boiling points of the halogens increase down the group from fluorine to iodine. This is because halogens exist as diatomic molecules, and between these molecules are van der Waals' forces. As you go down the group, the molecules become larger, and the van der Waals' forces become stronger. More energy is required to overcome these stronger forces, resulting in higher boiling points.

---

### Question 2
Write a balanced equation for the reaction between chlorine and sodium bromide solution. State one observation you would make. [3 marks]

**Mark Scheme:**
- Correct equation: Cl₂ + 2NaBr → 2NaCl + Br₂ (2 marks)
- Observation: Solution turns orange-brown (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** Cl₂ + 2NaBr → 2NaCl + Br₂. The solution would turn orange-brown in colour because bromine (Br₂) is produced.

---

### Question 3
Explain why fluorine is more reactive than chlorine. [3 marks]

**Mark Scheme:**
- Fluorine has smaller atomic radius (1 mark)
- Less shielding (1 mark)
- Greater attraction for an extra electron / easier to gain an electron (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** Fluorine is more reactive than chlorine because it has a smaller atomic radius with fewer inner electron shells. This means there is less shielding of the nucleus, and the outer shell is closer to the nucleus. Therefore, fluorine has a stronger attraction for an extra electron and can gain an electron more easily than chlorine.

---

### Question 4
Describe how you would test for the presence of chloride ions in a solution. Include the expected observation. [3 marks]

**Mark Scheme:**
- Add dilute nitric acid (to remove interfering ions) (1 mark)
- Add silver nitrate solution (1 mark)
- White precipitate formed indicates chloride ions (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** To test for chloride ions, first add dilute nitric acid to the solution to remove any ions that might interfere with the test. Then add silver nitrate solution. If chloride ions are present, a white precipitate of silver chloride will form.

---

**Acceptable Answer:** No reaction would occur. Iodine is less reactive than bromine (it is lower in Group 7), so it cannot displace bromide ions from potassium bromide. Only a more reactive halogen can displace a less reactive halogen from its compounds.

### Question 7
Explain why hydrogen chloride is acidic in water but not in methylbenzene. [3 marks]

**Mark Scheme:**
- In water, HCl dissociates/ionizes (1 mark)
- Forms H⁺ ions / H₃O⁺ ions (1 mark)
- In methylbenzene, HCl remains as covalent molecules / no H⁺ ions (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** In water, hydrogen chloride dissolves and dissociates into H⁺ and Cl⁻ ions. The presence of H⁺ ions makes the solution acidic. In methylbenzene (an organic solvent), hydrogen chloride does not dissociate and remains as covalent HCl molecules. Without H⁺ ions, the solution is not acidic.

### Question 11
Explain why the silver nitrate test for halide ions should be carried out in the presence of dilute nitric acid. [2 marks]

**Mark Scheme:**
- To remove/remove effect of other ions (1 mark)
- That would also form precipitates with silver nitrate / give false positives (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:** Dilute nitric acid is added before the silver nitrate test to remove any interfering ions, such as carbonate ions (CO₃²⁻) or hydroxide ions (OH⁻). These ions would also form precipitates with silver nitrate and could give false positive results for halide ions.

### Question 12
Describe the displacement reactions that would occur (if any) when:
(a) Chlorine is added to sodium bromide solution
(b) Bromine is added to sodium chloride solution
(c) Iodine is added to sodium bromide solution
[4 marks]

**Mark Scheme:**
- (a) Reaction occurs, bromine displaced (1 mark)
- (b) No reaction, bromine less reactive than chlorine (1 mark)
- (c) No reaction, iodine less reactive than bromine (1 mark)
- Correct explanation of reactivity trend (1 mark)

**Acceptable Answer:**
(a) When chlorine is added to sodium bromide, a displacement reaction occurs. Chlorine is more reactive than bromine, so it displaces bromine from the solution: Cl₂ + 2NaBr → 2NaCl + Br₂.
(b) When bromine is added to sodium chloride, no reaction occurs because bromine is less reactive than chlorine and cannot displace chloride ions.
(c) When iodine is added to sodium bromide, no reaction occurs because iodine is less reactive than bromine and cannot displace bromide ions.
