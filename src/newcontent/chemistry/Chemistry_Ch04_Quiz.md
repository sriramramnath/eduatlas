## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
Who developed the first widely accepted periodic table?
A) John Dalton
B) Dmitri Mendeleev
C) John Newlands
D) Ernest Rutherford

### Question 2
**Answer: C**
**Explanation**: By atomic number
- The modern periodic table is arranged by atomic number (number of protons), not atomic mass.How are elements arranged in the modern periodic table?
A) By atomic mass
B) By number of neutrons
C) By atomic number
D) By number of electrons

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: By atomic number

---

### Question 3
**Answer: C**
**Explanation**: Same number of outer shell electrons
- Elements in the same group have the same number of outer shell electrons, which gives them similar chemical properties.What do elements in the same group have in common?
A) Same number of protons
B) Same number of electron shells
C) Same number of outer shell electrons
D) Same atomic mass

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Same number of outer shell electrons

---

### Question 4
**Answer: C**
**Explanation**: They react vigorously with water
- Alkali metals are soft, have low melting points, and react vigorously with water to produce hydrogen gas and metal hydroxides.Which statement about alkali metals is correct?
A) They are hard and dense
B) They have high melting points
C) They react vigorously with water
D) They form +2 ions

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: They react vigorously with water

---

### Question 5
**Answer: C**
**Explanation**: Bromine
- Fluorine and chlorine are gases, bromine is a liquid, and iodine is a solid at room temperature.Which halogen is a liquid at room temperature?
A) Fluorine
B) Chlorine
C) Bromine
D) Iodine

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Bromine

---

### Question 6
**Answer: B**
**Explanation**: Sodium chloride and bromine are formed
- Chlorine is more reactive than bromine, so it displaces bromine from sodium bromide: Cl₂ + 2NaBr → 2NaCl + Br₂What happens when chlorine is added to sodium bromide solution?
A) No reaction occurs
B) Sodium chloride and bromine are formed
C) Sodium chlorate is formed
D) Chlorine gas is released

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Sodium chloride and bromine are formed

---

### Question 7
**Answer: B**
**Explanation**: They have full outer electron shells
- Noble gases have full outer shells (8 electrons, except helium with 2), making them stable and unreactive.Why are noble gases unreactive?
A) They have low atomic mass
B) They have full outer electron shells
C) They have no electrons
D) They are all metals

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: They have full outer electron shells

---

### Question 8
**Answer: C**
**Explanation**: Reactivity
- Reactivity increases down Group 1 because the outer electron is further from the nucleus and easier to lose.Which property increases down Group 1?
A) Melting point
B) Density
C) Reactivity
D) Electronegativity

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Reactivity

---

### Question 9
**Answer: C**
**Explanation**: Reactivity
- Reactivity decreases down Group 7 because atoms get larger and it becomes harder to attract an extra electron.Which property decreases down Group 7?
A) Atomic radius
B) Melting point
C) Reactivity
D) Boiling point

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Reactivity

---

### Question 10

A) They have low melting points

D) They are found in Group 1
