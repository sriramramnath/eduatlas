## Exam-Style Questions for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

Select the correct answer for each question.

---

### Question 1
Who developed the first widely accepted periodic table?

A) John Dalton  
B) Dmitri Mendeleev  
C) John Newlands  
D) Ernest Rutherford  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Dmitri Mendeleev developed the first widely accepted periodic table, leaving gaps for undiscovered elements.

---

### Question 2
How are elements arranged in the modern periodic table?

A) By atomic mass  
B) By number of neutrons  
C) By atomic number  
D) By number of electrons  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The modern periodic table is arranged by atomic number (number of protons), not atomic mass.

---

### Question 3
What do elements in the same group have in common?

A) Same number of protons  
B) Same number of electron shells  
C) Same number of outer shell electrons  
D) Same atomic mass  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Elements in the same group have the same number of outer shell electrons, which gives them similar chemical properties.

---

### Question 4
Which statement about alkali metals is correct?

A) They are hard and dense  
B) They have high melting points  
C) They react vigorously with water  
D) They form +2 ions  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Alkali metals are soft, have low melting points, and react vigorously with water to produce hydrogen gas and metal hydroxides.

---

### Question 5
Which halogen is a liquid at room temperature?

A) Fluorine  
B) Chlorine  
C) Bromine  
D) Iodine  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Fluorine and chlorine are gases, bromine is a liquid, and iodine is a solid at room temperature.

---

### Question 6
What happens when chlorine is added to sodium bromide solution?

A) No reaction occurs  
B) Sodium chloride and bromine are formed  
C) Sodium chlorate is formed  
D) Chlorine gas is released  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Chlorine is more reactive than bromine, so it displaces bromine from sodium bromide: Cl₂ + 2NaBr → 2NaCl + Br₂.

---

### Question 7
Why are noble gases unreactive?

A) They have low atomic mass  
B) They have full outer electron shells  
C) They have no electrons  
D) They are all metals  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Noble gases have full outer shells (8 electrons, except helium with 2), making them stable and unreactive.

---

### Question 8
Which property increases down Group 1?

A) Melting point  
B) Density  
C) Reactivity  
D) Electronegativity  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Reactivity increases down Group 1 because the outer electron is further from the nucleus and easier to lose.

---

### Question 9
Which property decreases down Group 7?

A) Atomic radius  
B) Melting point  
C) Reactivity  
D) Boiling point  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Reactivity decreases down Group 7 because atoms get larger and it becomes harder to attract an extra electron.

---

### Question 10
Which statement about transition metals is correct?

A) They have low melting points  
B) They are all very reactive  
C) They can form ions with different charges  
D) They are found in Group 1  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Transition metals can form ions with different charges (variable oxidation states), such as Fe²⁺ and Fe³⁺.

---

### Question 11
What colour is copper(II) sulfate solution?

A) Green  
B) Blue  
C) Yellow  
D) Purple  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Copper(II) sulfate solution is blue. This is a characteristic colour of copper compounds.

---

### Question 12
Which element is used in balloons because it is less dense than air and non-flammable?

A) Neon  
B) Argon  
C) Helium  
D) Xenon  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Helium is less dense than air and non-flammable, making it safe for use in balloons.

## Part B: True/False Questions

---

### Question 13
Groups are vertical columns and periods are horizontal rows in the periodic table.

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: Groups represent elements with similar properties in vertical columns, while periods represent the number of electron shells in horizontal rows.

---

### Question 14
The periodic table is divided into metals and non-metals by a zig-zag line.

**Answer: True**

**Explanation**: The zig-zag line separates metals (left) from non-metals (right), with metalloids like Boron and Silicon along the line.

---
