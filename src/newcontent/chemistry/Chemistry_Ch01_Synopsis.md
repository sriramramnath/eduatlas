# Chapter 1: States of Matter
## Concise Synopsis - Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

---

## 1. Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Matter** | Anything that has mass and occupies space |
| **Kinetic Theory** | Theory explaining states of matter in terms of particle movement |
| **Melting** | Change from solid to liquid at melting point |
| **Freezing** | Change from liquid to solid at freezing point |
| **Boiling** | Change from liquid to gas throughout the liquid at boiling point |
| **Evaporation** | Change from liquid to gas at the surface, below boiling point |
| **Condensation** | Change from gas to liquid |
| **Sublimation** | Change from solid directly to gas |
| **Deposition** | Change from gas directly to solid |
| **Diffusion** | Random movement of particles from high to low concentration |
| **Brownian Motion** | Random movement of particles suspended in a fluid |

---

## 2. States of Matter Summary

| State | Particle Arrangement | Particle Movement | Shape | Volume | Compressibility |
|-------|---------------------|-------------------|-------|--------|-----------------|
| **Solid** | Regular, close, lattice | Vibrate in fixed positions | Fixed | Fixed | Incompressible |
| **Liquid** | Irregular, close | Move past each other | Container shape | Fixed | Nearly incompressible |
| **Gas** | Random, far apart | Rapid, random movement | Fills container | Variable | Easily compressed |

---

## 3. Particle Theory - Key Points

### Assumptions
- All matter made of tiny particles (atoms, molecules, ions)
- Particles in constant motion
- Particles have kinetic energy (increases with temperature)
- Forces of attraction exist between particles
- Spaces between particles vary between states

### Limitations
- Particles are not solid spheres
- Intermolecular forces are more complex than the model suggests
- Particles have internal structure

---

## 4. State Changes

| Change | Process | Energy Change | Temperature During Change |
|--------|---------|---------------|--------------------------|
| Melting | Solid → Liquid | Absorbs energy | Constant (melting point) |
| Freezing | Liquid → Solid | Releases energy | Constant (freezing point) |
| Boiling | Liquid → Gas | Absorbs energy | Constant (boiling point) |
| Evaporation | Liquid → Gas (surface) | Absorbs energy | Any temperature below BP |
| Condensation | Gas → Liquid | Releases energy | Constant |
| Sublimation | Solid → Gas | Absorbs energy | Variable |
| Deposition | Gas → Solid | Releases energy | Variable |

---

## 5. Boiling vs Evaporation

| Feature | Boiling | Evaporation |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Location | Throughout liquid | Surface only |
| Temperature | At boiling point only | Below boiling point |
| Rate | Rapid | Slow |
| Bubbles | Yes | No |
| Cooling effect | No | Yes |

---

## 6. Heating Curve Features

```
Temperature
    ↑
    |          ___________ Gas
    |         /
    |    ____/  Boiling (constant T)
    |   /
    |  /__________ Liquid
    | /
    |/  Melting (constant T)
    |/
    |_____________ Solid
    |___________________________→ Time
```

- **Sloped sections**: Single state, temperature increases
- **Flat sections**: State change, temperature constant (energy used to overcome forces)

---

## 7. Factors Affecting Melting/Boiling Points

| Factor | Effect on Melting Point | Effect on Boiling Point |
|--------|------------------------|------------------------|
| Strong intermolecular forces | Higher | Higher |
| Increased pressure | Slight increase (water: decrease) | Increase |
| Impurities | Decrease | Increase |

---

## 8. Diffusion

### Key Points
- Random movement from high to low concentration
- Evidence for particle movement
- Occurs in gases and liquids only

### Factors Affecting Rate
- **Temperature** ↑ → Rate ↑
- **Particle mass** ↑ → Rate ↓
- **Concentration gradient** ↑ → Rate ↑

### Graham's Law
Lighter particles diffuse faster than heavier particles.
- Example: NH₃ (M_r = 17) diffuses faster than HCl (M_r = 36.5)

---

## 9. Gas Laws (Higher Tier)

| Law | Relationship | Equation |
|-----|--------------|----------|
| Boyle's Law | p ∝ 1/V (constant T) | p₁V₁ = p₂V₂ |
| Charles's Law | V ∝ T (constant p) | V₁/T₁ = V₂/T₂ |
| Pressure-Temperature | p ∝ T (constant V) | p₁/T₁ = p₂/T₂ |

**Kelvin Scale:** T(K) = T(°C) + 273

---

## 10. Exam Tips

✅ **DO:**
- Explain properties using particle arrangement AND movement
- State that energy is used to "overcome forces of attraction"
- Remember temperature stays constant during state changes
- Use correct terminology (particles vibrate, not "wiggle")

❌ **DON'T:**
- Say particles stop moving in solids
- Confuse melting with dissolving
- Confuse evaporation with boiling
- Say bonds are broken during state changes (forces are overcome)
- Forget flat sections on heating curves

## 11. Quick Reference Table

| Observation | Explanation |
|-------------|-------------|
| Gases are compressible | Large spaces between particles |
| Solids have fixed shape | Particles held in fixed positions |
| Liquids flow | Particles can move past each other |
| Boiling point constant | Energy used to overcome forces, not increase KE |
| Evaporation causes cooling | Higher energy particles escape, leaving cooler ones |
| Diffusion occurs | Particles in constant random motion |
| Gases exert pressure | Particles collide with container walls |

## 12. Required Practical Knowledge

### Demonstrating Diffusion
- **Bromine and air**: Brown gas spreads through both jars
- **Ammonia and HCl**: White ring forms closer to HCl (NH₃ lighter, diffuses faster)

### Heating Curve Analysis
- Identify melting and boiling points from flat sections
- Explain why temperature remains constant during state changes
- Relate curve shape to energy changes
