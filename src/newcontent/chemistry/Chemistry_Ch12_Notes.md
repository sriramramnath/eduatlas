## 12.1 Introduction to Group 7 Elements

### Definition
**Halogens** are the elements in Group 7 (also called Group 17) of the Periodic Table. The name "halogen" means "salt-former" because these elements react with metals to form salts called halides.

### Elements in Group 7

| Element | Symbol | Atomic Number | Electronic Structure | State at RTP |
|---------|--------|---------------|----------------------|--------------|
| Fluorine | F | 9 | 2.7 | Pale yellow gas |
| Chlorine | Cl | 17 | 2.8.7 | Greenish-yellow gas |
| Bromine | Br | 35 | 2.8.18.7 | Red-brown liquid |
| Iodine | I | 53 | 2.8.18.18.7 | Dark grey solid |
| Astatine | At | 85 | 2.8.18.32.18.7 | Black solid (radioactive) |

**Note:** RTP = Room Temperature and Pressure (20°C, 1 atm)

---

## 12.2 Physical Properties of Halogens

### General Physical Properties

| Property | Fluorine | Chlorine | Bromine | Iodine |
|----------|----------|----------|---------|--------|
| Appearance | Pale yellow gas | Greenish-yellow gas | Red-brown liquid | Dark grey solid |
| Melting Point (°C) | -220 | -101 | -7 | 114 |
| Boiling Point (°C) | -188 | -34 | 59 | 184 |
| Density | Very low | Low | Higher | Highest |
| Colour of vapour | Pale yellow | Greenish-yellow | Orange-brown | Purple |

### Trends in Physical Properties

#### State at Room Temperature
- **Gas**: Fluorine, Chlorine
- **Liquid**: Bromine
- **Solid**: Iodine, Astatine

**Trend:** Going down the group, elements change from gases to liquid to solid.

#### Melting and Boiling Points
- **Trend:** Melting and boiling points **increase down the group**

**Explanation:**
- Molecules are diatomic (F₂, Cl₂, Br₂, I₂)
- Forces between molecules are **van der Waals' forces** (also called London forces)
- As molecules get larger down the group, van der Waals' forces become stronger
- More energy needed to overcome these forces → higher melting/boiling points

#### Colour and Appearance
- **Trend:** Colours become **darker** down the group
- Fluorine: Pale yellow
- Chlorine: Greenish-yellow
- Bromine: Red-brown liquid (gives off orange-brown vapour)
- Iodine: Dark grey solid (gives off purple vapour when heated)

## 12.3 Electronic Configuration and Reactivity

### Electronic Structure
- All halogens have **seven electrons in their outer shell**
- Electronic configuration ends in ...p⁵
- They need to **gain one electron** to achieve stable noble gas configuration

```
F: 2.7        → F⁻: 2.8
Cl: 2.8.7     → Cl⁻: 2.8.8
Br: 2.8.18.7  → Br⁻: 2.8.18.8
I: 2.8.18.18.7 → I⁻: 2.8.18.18.8
```

### Why Do Halogens Form -1 Ions?
- Halogens have **one electron short** of a full outer shell
- Gaining one electron gives them a stable noble gas configuration
- Form **halide ions** (F⁻, Cl⁻, Br⁻, I⁻) with a **-1 charge**

### Trend in Reactivity Down the Group

**Reactivity DECREASES down the group:** F > Cl > Br > I > At

#### Explanation

| Factor | Effect Down Group | Impact on Reactivity |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------|
| Atomic radius | Increases | Outer shell further from nucleus |
| Shielding effect | Increases | More inner shells block nuclear attraction |
| Nuclear charge | Increases | But outweighed by shielding |
| Net attraction for electrons | Decreases | Harder to attract an extra electron |
| Electron affinity | Decreases | Less energy released when gaining electron |

**Key Point:** Although nuclear charge increases down the group, the increased atomic radius and shielding mean the nucleus has less attraction for the incoming electron. Therefore, it becomes harder to gain an electron and reactivity decreases.

## 12.4 Chemical Reactions

### 12.4.1 Reactions with Metals

Halogens react with metals to form **ionic halides** (metal halides).

#### General Equation
$$2M + X_2 \rightarrow 2MX$$

Where M = metal, X = halogen

#### Examples

**Reaction with sodium:**
$$2Na + Cl_2 \rightarrow 2NaCl \text{ (sodium chloride)}$$
$$2Na + Br_2 \rightarrow 2NaBr \text{ (sodium bromide)}$$
$$2Na + I_2 \rightarrow 2NaI \text{ (sodium iodide)}$$

**Reaction with iron:**
$$2Fe + 3Cl_2 \rightarrow 2FeCl_3 \text{ (iron(III) chloride)}$$

#### Ionic Bond Formation
- Metal atoms **lose electrons** to form cations
- Halogen atoms **gain electrons** to form anions
- Electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions = ionic bond

#### Reactivity Trend with Metals
- Fluorine reacts most vigorously (most reactive)
- Iodine reacts least vigorously (least reactive)

---

### 12.4.2 Reactions with Non-Metals

#### Reaction with Hydrogen
Halogens react with hydrogen to form **hydrogen halides**.

$$H_2 + X_2 \rightarrow 2HX$$

| Halogen | Reaction Conditions | Product | Observations |
|---------|--------------------|---------|--------------|
| Fluorine | Explosive, even in dark | HF | Violent explosion |
| Chlorine | Burns in H₂ with pale flame | HCl | Burns with pale green flame |
| Bromine | Requires heat | HBr | Burns only when heated |
| Iodine | Partial reaction, reversible | HI | Equilibrium reached |

**Trend:** Reactivity with hydrogen decreases down the group.

---

### 12.4.3 Displacement Reactions

A **more reactive halogen** will displace a **less reactive halogen** from its halide solution.

#### Order of Reactivity
**Fluorine > Chlorine > Bromine > Iodine**

#### Displacement Reactions Explained

A halogen higher in the group (more reactive) can:
- Take electrons from halide ions of halogens lower in the group
- Displace the less reactive halogen from its compounds

#### Examples

**Chlorine with sodium bromide:**
$$Cl_2 + 2NaBr \rightarrow 2NaCl + Br_2$$

Ionic equation:
$$Cl_2 + 2Br^- \rightarrow 2Cl^- + Br_2$$

**Observation:** Colourless solution turns orange-brown (Br₂ is produced)

**Chlorine with sodium iodide:**
$$Cl_2 + 2NaI \rightarrow 2NaCl + I_2$$

**Observation:** Colourless solution turns brown (I₂ is produced)

**Bromine with sodium iodide:**
$$Br_2 + 2NaI \rightarrow 2NaBr + I_2$$

**Observation:** Orange-brown solution turns darker brown (I₂ is produced)

#### Summary Table of Displacement Reactions

| Halogen added | NaCl (aq) | NaBr (aq) | NaI (aq) |
|---------------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Chlorine (Cl₂) | No reaction | Orange-brown (Br₂) | Brown (I₂) |
| Bromine (Br₂) | No reaction | No reaction | Brown (I₂) |
| Iodine (I₂) | No reaction | No reaction | No reaction |

**Key Point:** A halogen can only displace halides of less reactive halogens (those below it in Group 7).

## 12.5 Hydrogen Halides and Halide Ions

### 12.5.1 Hydrogen Halides

#### Properties
| Compound | Formula | State at RTP | Appearance |
|----------|---------|--------------|------------|
| Hydrogen fluoride | HF | Gas | Colourless |
| Hydrogen chloride | HCl | Gas | Colourless |
| Hydrogen bromide | HBr | Gas | Colourless |
| Hydrogen iodide | HI | Gas | Colourless |

#### Hydrogen Chloride (HCl)
- **Physical properties:** Colourless gas, pungent smell, dissolves in water
- **In dry conditions:** Exists as covalent molecules (gas)
- **In water:** Dissolves and ionizes to form hydrochloric acid

$$HCl(g) \xrightarrow{water} H^+(aq) + Cl^-(aq)$$

#### Reactions of Hydrogen Halides
- All hydrogen halides are **acidic** when dissolved in water
- They dissolve in water to form **hydrohalic acids**
- HF → Hydrofluoric acid (weak acid)
- HCl → Hydrochloric acid (strong acid)
- HBr → Hydrobromic acid (strong acid)
- HI → Hydroiodic acid (strong acid)

---

### 12.5.2 Tests for Halide Ions

#### Silver Nitrate Test

**Method:**
1. Add dilute nitric acid (HNO₃) to remove interfering ions
2. Add silver nitrate solution (AgNO₃)
3. Observe the precipitate formed

**Results:**

| Halide Ion | Precipitate Formed | Colour of Precipitate |
|------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Cl⁻ | AgCl | White |
| Br⁻ | AgBr | Cream (pale yellow) |
| I⁻ | AgI | Yellow |

**Equations:**
$$Ag^+(aq) + Cl^-(aq) \rightarrow AgCl(s)$$
$$Ag^+(aq) + Br^-(aq) \rightarrow AgBr(s)$$
$$Ag^+(aq) + I^-(aq) \rightarrow AgI(s)$$

#### Effect of Light
- Silver chloride (AgCl) turns grey in sunlight
- Silver bromide (AgBr) also turns grey in sunlight
- This is used in photography (traditional film)

#### Solubility in Ammonia

| Precipitate | Dilute NH₃ | Concentrated NH₃ |
|-------------|------------|------------------|
| AgCl (white) | Soluble | Soluble |
| AgBr (cream) | Insoluble | Soluble |
| AgI (yellow) | Insoluble | Insoluble |

## 12.6 Uses of Halogens and Their Compounds

| Hydrochloric acid | Manufacture of HCl |

| Use | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| Toothpaste | Sodium fluoride added to prevent tooth decay |
| Water fluoridation | Added to water supplies in small amounts |

### Bromine (Br₂)

| Use | Description |
|-----|-------------|
| Flame retardants | Added to plastics and textiles |

- Radioactive and rare
- No significant commercial uses

## 12.7 Hazards and Safety

### Hazards of Halogens

| Halogen | Hazard Level | Effects |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| Fluorine | Extremely hazardous | Severe burns, very toxic |
| Chlorine | Hazardous | Irritates respiratory system, toxic |
| Bromine | Hazardous | Liquid causes burns, vapour toxic |
| Iodine | Less hazardous | Can cause skin irritation |

### Safety Precautions
1. **Use in fume cupboard** - all halogens are toxic
2. **Wear eye protection** - all halogens can damage eyes
3. **Wear gloves** - especially when handling bromine liquid
4. **Avoid inhalation** - halogen vapours irritate lungs
5. **Work with small quantities** - minimizes risk
6. **Do not mix with metals directly** - can be violent reactions

## 12.8 Common Exam Mistakes

### ❌ Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Wrong reactivity trend direction**
   - Mistake: Reactivity increases down Group 7
   - Correct: Reactivity **decreases** down Group 7

2. **Confusing Group 1 and Group 7 trends**
   - Group 1: Reactivity **increases** down the group
   - Group 7: Reactivity **decreases** down the group

3. **Wrong explanation for reactivity**
   - Mistake: "Halogens lose electrons more easily"
   - Correct: Halogens **gain** electrons. Reactivity decreases because it's harder to gain an electron down the group.

4. **Incorrect precipitate colours**
   - AgCl = White (NOT cream)
   - AgBr = Cream (NOT white or yellow)
   - AgI = Yellow

5. **Forgetting the diatomic nature**
   - Mistake: Writing Cl instead of Cl₂
   - Correct: Halogens exist as diatomic molecules (F₂, Cl₂, Br₂, I₂)

6. **Wrong displacement predictions**
   - Chlorine can displace Br⁻ and I⁻
   - Bromine can only displace I⁻
   - Iodine cannot displace any halide

7. **Confusing hydrogen halides with acids**
   - HCl(g) = hydrogen chloride (gas, covalent)
   - HCl(aq) = hydrochloric acid (solution, ionic)

8. **Incorrect state symbols**
   - At room temperature: Cl₂ is a gas, Br₂ is a liquid, I₂ is a solid

## 12.10 Key Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| Halogens | Elements in Group 7 of the Periodic Table that have seven electrons in their outer shell |
| Halide ion | A negatively charged ion formed when a halogen gains one electron (F⁻, Cl⁻, Br⁻, I⁻) |
| Displacement reaction | A reaction in which a more reactive element displaces a less reactive element from its compound |
| Hydrogen halide | A covalent compound formed when a halogen reacts with hydrogen (HF, HCl, HBr, HI) |
| Van der Waals' forces | Weak intermolecular forces between molecules that increase with molecular size |
