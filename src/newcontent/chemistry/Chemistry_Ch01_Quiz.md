## Part A: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)

### Question 1
Which statement correctly describes the arrangement of particles in a solid?

A) Particles are far apart and move randomly in all directions  
B) Particles are close together in a regular pattern and vibrate in fixed positions  
C) Particles are close together in an irregular pattern and move past each other  
D) Particles are arranged randomly with large spaces between them  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** In solids, particles are arranged in a regular, repeating pattern (lattice) and are close together. They vibrate about fixed positions but cannot move past each other. This arrangement gives solids their fixed shape and volume.

---

### Question 2
Which process is represented by the equation: H₂O(s) → H₂O(l)?

A) Boiling  
B) Evaporation  
C) Melting  
D) Condensation  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** The equation shows solid water (ice) changing to liquid water. This is melting, which occurs at 0°C for pure water. Boiling would show liquid changing to gas, and condensation would show gas changing to liquid.

---

### Question 3
What happens to the temperature of a substance while it is melting?

A) It increases steadily  
B) It decreases steadily  
C) It remains constant  
D) It fluctuates up and down  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** During melting, the temperature remains constant at the melting point. The energy being supplied is used to overcome the forces of attraction between particles rather than increase their kinetic energy. This is shown as a flat section on a heating curve.

---

### Question 4
Which of the following is a difference between boiling and evaporation?

A) Boiling occurs at any temperature, evaporation at a specific temperature  
B) Boiling occurs throughout the liquid, evaporation only at the surface  
C) Boiling is a slow process, evaporation is rapid  
D) Boiling forms no bubbles, evaporation forms bubbles  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Boiling occurs throughout the liquid at a specific temperature (boiling point) and involves bubble formation. Evaporation occurs only at the surface and can happen at any temperature below the boiling point. Boiling is rapid; evaporation is slow.

---

### Question 5
Which state of matter has particles with the highest kinetic energy?

A) Solid  
B) Liquid  
C) Gas  
D) All have the same kinetic energy  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Gas particles have the highest average kinetic energy because they move most rapidly. Solid particles have the lowest kinetic energy (only vibrating), and liquid particles have intermediate kinetic energy. This is why gases have no fixed shape or volume.

---

### Question 6
Why are gases easily compressed?

A) Particles are soft and can be squashed  
B) There are large spaces between particles  
C) The particles have low mass  
D) The forces between particles are strong  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Gases are easily compressed because there are large empty spaces between particles. When pressure is applied, particles can be pushed closer together into these spaces. In solids and liquids, particles are already close together, making compression difficult.

---

### Question 7
Which substance undergoes sublimation?

A) Water  
B) Iodine  
C) Sodium chloride  
D) Copper  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Iodine sublimes - it changes directly from solid to gas without passing through the liquid state. When heated, solid iodine produces purple vapour. Water melts then boils; sodium chloride and copper have very high melting points but do not sublime.

---

### Question 8
In an experiment, ammonia gas and hydrogen chloride gas are released at opposite ends of a tube. The white ring of ammonium chloride forms closer to the hydrogen chloride end. What does this show?

A) Ammonia is heavier than hydrogen chloride  
B) Hydrogen chloride diffuses faster than ammonia  
C) Ammonia diffuses faster than hydrogen chloride  
D) The two gases react immediately  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** The white ring forms closer to the hydrogen chloride because ammonia (M_r = 17) is lighter than hydrogen chloride (M_r = 36.5). Lighter particles diffuse faster, so ammonia travels further in the same time. This demonstrates Graham's law of diffusion.

---

### Question 9
What causes the cooling effect of evaporation?

A) The liquid absorbs heat from surroundings  
B) The most energetic particles escape, leaving cooler particles behind  
C) New bonds are formed releasing energy  
D) The liquid changes colour  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** During evaporation, particles with the highest kinetic energy escape from the liquid surface first. This leaves behind particles with lower average kinetic energy, meaning the remaining liquid has a lower temperature. This is why sweating cools the body.

---

### Question 10
According to Boyle's Law, what happens to the pressure of a gas if its volume is halved at constant temperature?

A) Pressure is halved  
B) Pressure stays the same  
C) Pressure doubles  
D) Pressure becomes zero  

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Boyle's Law states that pV = constant at constant temperature. If volume is halved, pressure must double to keep pV constant (p₁V₁ = p₂V₂, so p₂ = p₁V₁/V₂ = p₁V₁/(V₁/2) = 2p₁). Particles collide with walls twice as often in the smaller volume.

### Question 11
At what temperature do particles theoretically have no kinetic energy?

A) 0°C  
B) -273°C  
C) 0°F  
D) 100°C  

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Absolute zero is -273°C (or 0 K). At this temperature, particles would have minimum kinetic energy and would stop moving. This is the lowest possible temperature and is the zero point on the Kelvin scale.

### Question 12
Which row correctly describes the properties of a liquid?
| Row | Shape | Volume | Compressibility |
|-----|-------|--------|-----------------|
| A | Fixed | Fixed | Incompressible |
| B | Container shape | Fixed | Nearly incompressible |
| C | Container shape | Variable | Easily compressed |
| D | Fills container | Fixed | Incompressible |
**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Liquids take the shape of their container (particles can move past each other), have a fixed volume (particles are close together), and are nearly incompressible (little space between particles). This distinguishes them from solids (fixed shape) and gases (variable volume and shape, easily compressed).

## Part B: True/False Questions
### Statement 1
Particles in a solid are stationary and do not move.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Particles in a solid vibrate about fixed positions. They are NOT stationary. Only at absolute zero (0 K, -273°C) would motion theoretically cease. The particles have kinetic energy due to their vibration.
**Explanation**: Particles in a solid vibrate about fixed positions. They are NOT stationary. Only at absolute zero (0 K, -273°C) would motion theoretically cease. The particles have kinetic energy due to their vibration.
---
### Statement 2
Melting and freezing occur at the same temperature for a pure substance.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: For a pure substance, the melting point and freezing point are identical. At this temperature, solid and liquid can coexist in equilibrium. Melting absorbs energy; freezing releases the same amount of energy.
---
### Statement 3
Evaporation only occurs at the boiling point of a liquid.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Evaporation occurs at any temperature below the boiling point. Unlike boiling, which occurs at a specific temperature throughout the liquid, evaporation occurs only at the surface and can happen at any temperature.
**Explanation**: Evaporation occurs at any temperature below the boiling point. Unlike boiling, which occurs at a specific temperature throughout the liquid, evaporation occurs only at the surface and can happen at any temperature.
---
### Statement 4
The temperature of a substance increases while it is boiling.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: During boiling, the temperature remains constant at the boiling point. The energy supplied is used to overcome the forces of attraction between particles completely, not to increase kinetic energy. This is shown as a flat section on a heating curve.
**Explanation**: During boiling, the temperature remains constant at the boiling point. The energy supplied is used to overcome the forces of attraction between particles completely, not to increase kinetic energy. This is shown as a flat section on a heating curve.
---
### Statement 5
Gases have lower density than solids because their particles have less mass.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Gases have lower density because particles are far apart with large empty spaces between them. The same type of particles have the same mass regardless of state. Density depends on how closely particles are packed, not particle mass.
**Explanation**: Gases have lower density because particles are far apart with large empty spaces between them. The same type of particles have the same mass regardless of state. Density depends on how closely particles are packed, not particle mass.
---
### Statement 6
Diffusion provides evidence that particles are in constant motion.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Diffusion occurs because particles move randomly from areas of high concentration to low concentration. Experiments like bromine gas diffusing into air or potassium manganate(VII) spreading through water demonstrate particle movement.
---
### Statement 7
Adding impurities to water raises its freezing point.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Adding impurities to water LOWERS its freezing point (freezing point depression). This is why salt is spread on icy roads - the salt lowers the freezing point of water, causing ice to melt even at temperatures below 0°C.
**Explanation**: Adding impurities to water LOWERS its freezing point (freezing point depression). This is why salt is spread on icy roads - the salt lowers the freezing point of water, causing ice to melt even at temperatures below 0°C.
---
### Statement 8
Brownian motion is caused by particles being bombarded by smaller, invisible particles.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Brownian motion is the random, jerky movement of visible particles (like pollen grains or smoke particles) caused by collisions with invisible molecules of the surrounding fluid. This provided evidence for the existence of molecules.
---
### Statement 9
Increasing pressure on a gas decreases its boiling point.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Increasing pressure on a liquid RAISES its boiling point. Higher pressure makes it harder for particles to escape the liquid surface. This is why pressure cookers cook food faster - water boils at a higher temperature under pressure.
**Explanation**: Increasing pressure on a liquid RAISES its boiling point. Higher pressure makes it harder for particles to escape the liquid surface. This is why pressure cookers cook food faster - water boils at a higher temperature under pressure.
---
### Statement 10
In a heating curve, flat sections represent state changes where temperature is constant.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: During state changes (melting, boiling), the temperature remains constant despite continued heating. The energy supplied is used to overcome forces of attraction between particles rather than increase kinetic energy. These appear as flat horizontal sections on heating curves.
---
### Statement 11
Lighter gas particles diffuse more slowly than heavier gas particles at the same temperature.
**Answer: False**
**Correction**: Lighter gas particles diffuse FASTER than heavier particles at the same temperature. At the same temperature, all particles have the same average kinetic energy, but lighter particles must move faster to have the same kinetic energy (KE = ½mv²).
**Explanation**: Lighter gas particles diffuse FASTER than heavier particles at the same temperature. At the same temperature, all particles have the same average kinetic energy, but lighter particles must move faster to have the same kinetic energy (KE = ½mv²).
---
### Statement 12
Sublimation is the process of a solid changing directly to a gas.
**Answer: True**
**Explanation**: Sublimation is a state change where a solid turns directly into a gas without passing through the liquid phase. Examples include iodine (produces purple vapour), dry ice (solid CO₂), and ammonium chloride.

### Question 6 (4 marks)
A student places a drop of red ink at the bottom of a beaker of water. After some time, all the water becomes uniformly pale red. Use the kinetic particle theory to explain this observation.

**Answer:**
- The ink particles and water particles are in constant random motion (1 mark)
- The ink particles move from the region of higher concentration (the drop) to regions of lower concentration (the rest of the water) by diffusion (1 mark)
- Water particles also move, spreading out the ink particles further (1 mark)
- Eventually, the ink particles are evenly distributed throughout the water, giving uniform colour (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: particles in constant random motion (1 mark); diffusion from high to low concentration (1 mark); role of water particle movement (1 mark); even distribution achieved (1 mark).

Explain how evaporation causes cooling.

**Answer:**
- During evaporation, particles with the highest kinetic energy escape from the liquid surface first (1 mark)
- The remaining particles have lower average kinetic energy (1 mark)
- Since temperature is related to average kinetic energy, the remaining liquid is cooler (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: most energetic particles escape (1 mark); remaining particles have lower average KE (1 mark); lower KE means lower temperature (1 mark).

### Question 10 (4 marks)
Describe the limitations of the simple particle model in explaining the behaviour of matter.

**Answer:**
- The model represents particles as solid inelastic spheres, but real atoms/molecules are not solid spheres and contain mostly empty space (1 mark)
- The model suggests particles are static in solids, but real particles in solids vibrate (1 mark)
- The model does not show that there are different types of intermolecular forces (hydrogen bonding, dipole-dipole, van der Waals) (1 mark)
- The model does not show that atoms have internal structure (protons, neutrons, electrons) or that molecules have specific shapes (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award 1 mark for each valid limitation explained, up to 4 marks.

### Question 11 (3 marks) - Higher Tier
A gas has a volume of 200 cm³ at a pressure of 100 kPa. Calculate the new volume if the pressure is increased to 250 kPa at constant temperature.

**Answer:**
Using Boyle's Law: p₁V₁ = p₂V₂ (1 mark for correct equation)
100 × 200 = 250 × V₂ (1 mark for correct substitution)
V₂ = (100 × 200) / 250 = 80 cm³ (1 mark for correct answer with units)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: stating/using p₁V₁ = p₂V₂ (1 mark); correct substitution (1 mark); correct answer 80 cm³ with units (1 mark).

### Question 12 (4 marks)
Explain, using the kinetic particle theory, why a liquid has a fixed volume but takes the shape of its container.

**Answer:**
A liquid has a fixed volume because:
- The particles are close together with little empty space between them (1 mark)
- The forces of attraction between particles are strong enough to keep them close together (1 mark)

A liquid takes the shape of its container because:
- The particles are arranged irregularly and can move past each other (1 mark)
- The forces of attraction are not strong enough to hold particles in fixed positions, so the liquid can flow and adapt to the container shape (1 mark)

**Mark Scheme Answer:** Award marks for: fixed volume - particles close together (1 mark); fixed volume - forces keep particles together (1 mark); container shape - particles move past each other (1 mark); container shape - particles not in fixed positions (1 mark).
