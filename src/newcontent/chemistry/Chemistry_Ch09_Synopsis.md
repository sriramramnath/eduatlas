# Chapter 9: Metallic Bonding - Concise Synopsis

## Quick Revision Guide for Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry

---

## Chapter Overview

This chapter covers metallic bonding, the structure and properties of metals, and how alloys are designed to improve metallic properties. Understanding metallic bonding completes your knowledge of the three main types of chemical bonding.

---

## Key Points Summary

### 1. Metallic Structure

- Metals consist of a **giant lattice of positive metal ions**
- Surrounded by a **sea of delocalised electrons**
- Delocalised electrons = electrons that are **free to move** throughout the structure

### 2. Metallic Bonding Definition

> **Metallic bonding** is the electrostatic attraction between positive metal ions and delocalised electrons.

### 3. Properties of Metals

| Property | Explanation (Learn These!) |
|----------|---------------------------|
| Conduct electricity | Delocalised electrons move and carry charge |
| Conduct heat | Electrons transfer energy through the metal |
| Malleable | Layers of ions can slide while maintaining bonds |
| Ductile | Layers can slide past each other without breaking bonds |
| High MP/BP | Strong metallic bonds require lots of energy to break |
| Shiny | Delocalised electrons reflect light |

### 4. Alloys

> **Alloy** = A mixture of a metal with other elements

- Alloys are **harder** than pure metals
- **Reason:** Different-sized atoms disrupt the regular arrangement, preventing layers from sliding easily

**Common alloys:**
- Steel (Fe + C) - construction, tools
- Brass (Cu + Zn) - musical instruments
- Bronze (Cu + Sn) - statues, medals
- Stainless steel (Fe + Cr + Ni) - cutlery

### 5. Comparing Bonding Types - Quick Reference

| Type | Particles | Conducts when solid? | Melting point |
|------|-----------|---------------------|---------------|
| Ionic | Ions | No | High |
| Simple covalent | Molecules | No | Low |
| Giant covalent | Atoms | No (except graphite) | Very high |
| Metallic | Ions + delocalised e⁻ | Yes | High |

## Essential Definitions to Learn

1. **Metallic bond** - The electrostatic attraction between positive metal ions and delocalised electrons

2. **Delocalised electrons** - Electrons free to move throughout a metallic structure

3. **Malleable** - Can be hammered into shape without breaking

4. **Ductile** - Can be drawn into wires

5. **Alloy** - A mixture of a metal with other elements

---

## Exam Tips

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ "Metals conduct because ions move" 
✓ Metals conduct because **delocalised electrons** move

❌ "Alloys are compounds"
✓ Alloys are **mixtures**

❌ "Bonds break when metals are bent"
✓ Bonds are **maintained** when layers slide

❌ "Metals have free electrons"
✓ Metals have **delocalised** electrons

### Key Phrases to Use

- "Electrostatic attraction between positive metal ions and delocalised electrons"
- "Delocalised electrons are free to move throughout the structure"
- "Layers of ions can slide over each other while maintaining metallic bonding"
- "Different-sized atoms disrupt the regular arrangement"

## Quick Comparison: Why Do These Differences Exist?

| Why does... | ...this happen? |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Metals conduct but ionic solids don't? | Metals have delocalised electrons that can move; ionic solids have fixed ions |
| Alloys are harder than pure metals? | Different-sized atoms prevent layers from sliding easily |
| Metals are malleable but ionic compounds are brittle? | Metallic bonds are maintained when layers slide; ionic bonds break due to repulsion |
| Metals have high melting points? | Strong metallic bonds need lots of energy to break |
