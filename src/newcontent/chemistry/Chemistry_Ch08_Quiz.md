## Section C: Short Answer Mark Schemes

### Q1. Dot and Cross Diagrams (4 marks)

a) **H₂:** Two H nuclei with one dot and one cross between them (shared pair)
   - Each H should show one electron in total (1 mark)

b) **H₂O:** O with 6 electrons (dots), each of 2 H atoms shares one electron (crosses)
   - O should have 8 electrons total (2 bonds + 4 lone pairs) (1 mark)

c) **NH₃:** N with 5 electrons, shares with 3 H atoms
   - N should have 8 electrons total (3 bonds + 1 lone pair) (1 mark)

d) **CO₂:** C with 4 electrons, double bonded to 2 O atoms
   - Each O has 8 electrons, C has 8 electrons (1 mark)

---

### Q2. Explanations (6 marks)

a) **Cl₂ gas, Br₂ liquid:**
- Both are simple molecular substances with weak intermolecular forces (1 mark)
- Br₂ has larger molecules with more electrons → stronger Van der Waals forces → higher boiling point (1 mark)

b) **H₂O vs H₂S:**
- H₂O has hydrogen bonding (strong intermolecular force) (1 mark)
- H₂S only has weaker Van der Waals/dipole-dipole forces
- Hydrogen bonding requires more energy to overcome → higher boiling point (1 mark)

c) **CH₄ doesn't conduct:**
- No free ions or delocalised electrons (1 mark)
- Electrons are held in covalent bonds within molecules (1 mark)

---

### Q3. Diamond vs Graphite (6 marks)

a) **Diamond structure:**
- Each carbon bonded to 4 other carbons (1 mark)
- Tetrahedral arrangement, giant 3D network (1 mark)

b) **Graphite structure:**
- Each carbon bonded to 3 other carbons (1 mark)
- Layers of hexagonal rings, delocalised electrons between layers (1 mark)

c) **Soft vs hard:**
- Graphite: weak forces between layers allow them to slide (1 mark)
- Diamond: strong covalent bonds in all directions make it rigid (1 mark)

d) **Conductivity:**
- Graphite: each C has 1 delocalised electron that can move (1 mark)
- Diamond: all 4 outer electrons per C used in bonding, none free to move (1 mark)

e) **Use of diamond:**
- Cutting tools/drill bits (1 mark) - hardness allows it to cut other materials (1 mark)

f) **Use of graphite:**
- Pencils OR lubricant OR electrodes (1 mark) - slippery layers rub off / conduct electricity (1 mark)

---

### Q4. Silicon Dioxide (4 marks)

a) **Structure:**
- Each Si bonded to 4 O atoms, each O bonded to 2 Si atoms (1 mark)
- Giant 3D covalent network (1 mark)

b) **High melting point:**
- Strong covalent bonds throughout structure (1 mark)
- Large amounts of energy needed to break many bonds (1 mark)

c) **No conductivity:**
- No free ions or delocalised electrons (1 mark)
- All electrons held in covalent bonds (1 mark)

d) **Use:**
- Glass (1 mark) - transparent, hard, high melting point (1 mark)
- OR Sand - abundant, hard

### Q6. Identifying Substances (4 marks)

a) **Substance A - simple molecular:**
- Very low melting point (-183°C) indicates weak intermolecular forces (1 mark)

b) **Substance B - diamond:**
- Very high melting point + doesn't conduct = giant covalent (1 mark)

c) **Substance C - ionic:**
- High melting point + doesn't conduct when solid = ionic compound (1 mark)

d) **Substance D - metal:**
- Conducts when solid = metallic bonding (1 mark)

---

### Q7. Differences (4 marks)

a) **Intermolecular forces vs covalent bonds:**
- Intermolecular forces: BETWEEN molecules, weak (1 mark)
- Covalent bonds: WITHIN molecules, strong (1 mark)

b) **Thermosoftening vs thermosetting:**
- Thermosoftening: linear chains, soften when heated, can be reshaped (1 mark)
- Thermosetting: cross-linked 3D network, do not soften when heated (1 mark)

---

### Q8. Nitrogen and Oxygen (3 marks)

a) **N₂ with triple bond:**
- Correct drawing showing N≡N with three shared pairs of electrons (1 mark)

b) **O₂ with double bond:**
- Correct drawing showing O=O with two shared pairs of electrons (1 mark)

c) **Low boiling points:**
- Weak intermolecular forces between molecules (1 mark)
- Little energy needed to separate molecules

---

### Q9. PTFE (3 marks)

a) **PTFE repeating unit:**
- -[-CF₂-CF₂-]-ₙ- (1 mark)

b) **Property for non-stick:**
- Low friction / inert / heat resistant / doesn't react with food (1 mark)

c) **No conductivity:**
- No free electrons or ions (1 mark)
- Electrons held in covalent bonds

### Q11. Halogen Trend (2 marks)

- Larger molecules have more electrons, stronger Van der Waals forces between molecules (1 mark)
