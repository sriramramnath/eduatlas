### Question 1
What is a covalent bond?

A) The transfer of electrons from one atom to another

B) The electrostatic attraction between oppositely charged ions

C) A shared pair of electrons between two atoms

D) The attraction between a nucleus and its own electrons

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Covalent bond = shared pair of electrons between atoms

---

### Question 2
How many electrons are shared in a double bond?

A) 1

B) 2

C) 4

D) 6

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Double bond = 2 shared pairs = 4 electrons total

---

### Question 3
Why do simple molecular substances have low boiling points?

A) They have weak covalent bonds

B) They have weak intermolecular forces between molecules

C) They are always gases

D) They have no bonding

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Weak intermolecular forces between molecules need little energy to overcome

---

### Question 4
Which statement about diamond is correct?

A) It conducts electricity

B) Each carbon is bonded to 3 other carbon atoms

C) It has a layered structure

D) It has a giant covalent structure

**Answer: D**
**Explanation:** Diamond has giant covalent structure; each C bonded to 4 others

---

### Question 5
Why does graphite conduct electricity?

A) It has free protons

B) It has delocalised electrons between its layers

C) It contains metal atoms

D) It has ionic bonds

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Each C only uses 3 electrons for bonding; 1 electron per C is delocalised

---

### Question 6
Which has the highest boiling point?

A) CH₄

B) Cl₂

C) Br₂

D) I₂

**Answer: D**
**Explanation:** I₂ has largest Mr (254), most electrons, strongest Van der Waals forces

### Question 7
In methane (CH₄), how many covalent bonds does carbon form?

A) 1

C) 3

D) 4

**Answer: D**
**Explanation:** Carbon forms 4 bonds in methane (single bonds to 4 H atoms)

### Question 8
What holds the layers together in graphite?

A) Strong covalent bonds

B) Ionic bonds

C) Weak intermolecular forces

D) Metallic bonds

**Answer: C**
**Explanation:** Layers held by weak intermolecular forces (Van der Waals)

### Question 9
Silicon dioxide (SiO₂) is used to make glass because:

A) It is soft and flexible

B) It conducts electricity

C) It has a high melting point and is transparent

### Question 10
In addition polymerisation:

A) A small molecule is eliminated

B) Monomers with double bonds join together

C) Two different monomers are always needed

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Addition polymerisation uses monomers with C=C bonds that open and join

### Question 11
Which type of polymer can be softened and reshaped by heating?

A) Thermosetting

B) Thermosoftening

C) Both types

**Answer: B**
**Explanation:** Thermosoftening polymers have linear chains that can slide when heated

## Part B: True or False (12 marks)

**For each statement, write TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, write the correct statement.**

---

### Statement 1
Covalent bonds are formed by the transfer of electrons from one atom to another.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Covalent bonds are formed by the sharing of electrons between atoms

---

### Statement 2
A double bond consists of two shared pairs of electrons.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Correct definition of a double bond

---

### Statement 3
When simple molecular substances boil, the covalent bonds within molecules break.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** When simple molecular substances boil, only intermolecular forces break; covalent bonds within molecules remain intact

---

### Statement 4
Diamond and graphite are both made of carbon atoms only.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Both are allotropes of carbon

---

### Statement 5
Diamond conducts electricity because it has delocalised electrons.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Diamond does NOT conduct electricity because all 4 outer electrons per C are used in bonding

---

### Statement 6
Graphite is used as a lubricant because it is soft and slippery.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Weak forces between layers allow them to slide

---

### Statement 7
The boiling point of simple molecular substances increases with increasing molecular size.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Larger molecules have stronger Van der Waals forces

---

### Statement 8
Giant covalent structures have low melting points.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Giant covalent structures have HIGH melting points due to strong covalent bonds throughout

---

### Statement 9
In poly(ethene), the monomer is ethene (CH₂=CH₂).

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Correct monomer for addition polymerisation to poly(ethene)

---

### Statement 10
Thermosetting polymers soften when heated and can be reshaped.

**Answer: False**
**Correction:** Thermosetting polymers do NOT soften when heated (they have cross-links); thermosoftening polymers do

---

### Statement 11
Simple molecular substances do not conduct electricity because they have no free ions or delocalised electrons.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Correct explanation for non-conductivity

---

### Statement 12
Silicon dioxide has a similar structure to diamond.

**Answer: True**
**Explanation:** Both have tetrahedral network covalent structures

### Question 1
Draw dot and cross diagrams to show the bonding in the following molecules: (4 marks)

a) H₂ (hydrogen molecule)

b) H₂O (water molecule)

c) NH₃ (ammonia molecule)

d) CO₂ (carbon dioxide molecule)

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
a) H₂: Two H nuclei with one dot and one cross between them (shared pair); each H shows one electron in total (1 mark)
b) H₂O: O with 6 electrons (dots), each of 2 H atoms shares one electron (crosses); O should have 8 electrons total (2 bonds + 4 lone pairs) (1 mark)
c) NH₃: N with 5 electrons, shares with 3 H atoms; N should have 8 electrons total (3 bonds + 1 lone pair) (1 mark)
d) CO₂: C with 4 electrons, double bonded to 2 O atoms; each O has 8 electrons, C has 8 electrons (1 mark)

---

### Question 2
Explain the following observations: (6 marks)

a) Chlorine (Cl₂) is a gas at room temperature, but bromine (Br₂) is a liquid.

b) Water (H₂O) has a much higher boiling point than hydrogen sulfide (H₂S), even though H₂S has a larger molecular mass.

c) Methane (CH₄) does not conduct electricity.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
a) Cl₂ gas, Br₂ liquid: Both are simple molecular substances with weak intermolecular forces (1 mark); Br₂ has larger molecules with more electrons → stronger Van der Waals forces → higher boiling point (1 mark)
b) H₂O vs H₂S: H₂O has hydrogen bonding (strong intermolecular force) (1 mark); H₂S only has weaker Van der Waals/dipole-dipole forces; hydrogen bonding requires more energy to overcome → higher boiling point (1 mark)
c) CH₄ doesn't conduct: No free ions or delocalised electrons (1 mark); electrons are held in covalent bonds within molecules (1 mark)

---

### Question 3
Compare the structures and properties of diamond and graphite. (6 marks)

a) Describe the structure of diamond.

b) Describe the structure of graphite.

c) Explain why graphite is soft and slippery but diamond is hard.

d) Explain why graphite conducts electricity but diamond does not.

e) State one use of diamond and explain why it is suitable for this use.

f) State one use of graphite and explain why it is suitable for this use.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
a) Diamond structure: Each carbon bonded to 4 other carbons (1 mark); tetrahedral arrangement, giant 3D network (1 mark)
b) Graphite structure: Each carbon bonded to 3 other carbons (1 mark); layers of hexagonal rings, delocalised electrons between layers (1 mark)
c) Soft vs hard: Graphite - weak forces between layers allow them to slide (1 mark); Diamond - strong covalent bonds in all directions make it rigid (1 mark)
d) Conductivity: Graphite - each C has 1 delocalised electron that can move (1 mark); Diamond - all 4 outer electrons per C used in bonding, none free to move (1 mark)
e) Use of diamond: Cutting tools/drill bits (1 mark) - hardness allows it to cut other materials (1 mark)
f) Use of graphite: Pencils OR lubricant OR electrodes (1 mark) - slippery layers rub off / conduct electricity (1 mark)

### Question 4
Silicon dioxide (SiO₂) is a giant covalent substance. (4 marks)

a) Describe the structure of silicon dioxide.

b) Explain why silicon dioxide has a high melting point.

c) Explain why silicon dioxide does not conduct electricity.

d) State one use of silicon dioxide and explain why it is suitable.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
a) Structure: Each Si bonded to 4 O atoms, each O bonded to 2 Si atoms (1 mark); giant 3D covalent network (1 mark)
b) High melting point: Strong covalent bonds throughout structure (1 mark); large amounts of energy needed to break many bonds (1 mark)
c) No conductivity: No free ions or delocalised electrons (1 mark); all electrons held in covalent bonds (1 mark)
d) Use: Glass (1 mark) - transparent, hard, high melting point (1 mark)

Propene (CH₂=CH-CH₃) is an unsaturated hydrocarbon. (4 marks)

b) Draw the structure of propene, showing all atoms and bonds.

c) Draw the repeating unit of poly(propene).

d) Name the type of polymerisation that forms poly(propene).

**Mark Scheme Answer:**

d) Type of polymerisation: Addition polymerisation (1 mark)

| A | -183°C | No |
| B | 3550°C | No |

### Question 7
Explain the difference between: (4 marks)

a) Intermolecular forces and covalent bonds.

b) Thermosoftening and thermosetting polymers.

**Mark Scheme Answer:**
a) Intermolecular forces vs covalent bonds: Intermolecular forces - BETWEEN molecules, weak (1 mark); Covalent bonds - WITHIN molecules, strong (1 mark)
b) Thermosoftening vs thermosetting: Thermosoftening - linear chains, soften when heated, can be reshaped (1 mark); Thermosetting - cross-linked 3D network, do not soften when heated (1 mark)
