# IGCSE Chemistry Chapter 19: Energetics
## Synopsis (Quick Revision Summary)

---

## Key Definitions at a Glance

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Exothermic reaction** | Reaction that releases thermal energy to surroundings (ΔH is negative) |
| **Endothermic reaction** | Reaction that absorbs thermal energy from surroundings (ΔH is positive) |
| **Enthalpy change (ΔH)** | Overall energy change during a reaction (kJ/mol) |
| **Bond energy** | Energy required to break one mole of a specific covalent bond |
| **Activation energy (Ea)** | Minimum energy particles must have to react successfully |
| **Activated complex** | Temporary, unstable arrangement of atoms at the peak of Ea barrier |

---

## Quick Comparison

| Feature | Exothermic | Endothermic |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| ΔH sign | Negative (−) | Positive (+) |
| Energy flow | OUT to surroundings | IN from surroundings |
| Temperature of surroundings | Increases | Decreases |
| Products energy level | Lower than reactants | Higher than reactants |
| More energy released/absorbed? | Making bonds > Breaking bonds | Breaking bonds > Making bonds |

## Bond Breaking and Making

| Process | Energy | Type |
|---------|--------|------|
| **Breaking bonds** | ABSORBED (IN) | Endothermic (+) |
| **Making bonds** | RELEASED (OUT) | Exothermic (−) |

### The Formula
**ΔH = Σ(bonds broken) − Σ(bonds formed)**

Or: **ΔH = Energy IN − Energy OUT**

---

## Bond Energy Calculation Steps

1. Write balanced equation with **displayed formulae**
2. List all bonds **broken** (reactants)
3. List all bonds **formed** (products)
4. Calculate total energy for bonds broken
5. Calculate total energy for bonds formed
6. Apply: ΔH = Energy in − Energy out

## Key Examples

### Exothermic Reactions
- **Combustion**: CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O
- **Neutralisation**: HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O
- **Oxidation**: 2Mg + O₂ → 2MgO
- **Respiration**: C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂ → 6CO₂ + 6H₂O
- **Hand warmers**: Fe + O₂ → Fe₂O₃
- **Self-heating cans**: CaO + H₂O → Ca(OH)₂

### Endothermic Reactions
- **Photosynthesis**: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
- **Thermal decomposition**: CaCO₃ → CaO + CO₂
- **Cold packs**: NH₄NO₃ dissolving
- **Electrolysis**: 2H₂O → 2H₂ + O₂
- **Cracking**: Long hydrocarbons → Short hydrocarbons

---

## Activation Energy Key Points

- **ALL reactions** need activation energy
- Represents the **energy barrier** to overcome
- Supplied by: heat, light, spark, flame
- **Catalysts lower activation energy** (but don't change ΔH)

---

## Top 5 Exam Mistakes

1. ❌ "Breaking bonds releases energy" → ✅ Breaking bonds **absorbs** energy
2. ❌ "Exothermic has positive ΔH" → ✅ Exothermic has **negative** ΔH
3. ❌ "Exothermic doesn't need Ea" → ✅ **ALL** reactions need Ea
4. ❌ Forgetting units → ✅ Always include **kJ/mol**
5. ❌ Miscounting bonds → ✅ Draw **displayed formulae** and count carefully
