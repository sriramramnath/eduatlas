### Question 1
What type of reaction is esterification?
**Answer: C**
**Explanation**: Esterification is a condensation reaction because two molecules (carboxylic acid and alcohol) join together with the elimination of a small molecule (water).
---

B) Reduction  
C) Condensation  
D) Combustion

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: Esterification is a condensation reaction because two molecules (carboxylic acid and alcohol) join together with the elimination of a small molecule (water).

---

C) Ethanol ethanoate

### Question 7
What is the functional group of esters?
**Answer: C**
**Explanation**: The ester functional group is -COO- (a carbonyl group bonded to an oxygen that is bonded to an alkyl group). -OH is for alcohols, -COOH for carboxylic acids, and C=C for alkenes.
---
A) -OH  
B) -COOH  
C) -COO-  
D) C=C

**Answer: C**

**Explanation**: The ester functional group is -COO- (a carbonyl group bonded to an oxygen that is bonded to an alkyl group). -OH is for alcohols, -COOH for carboxylic acids, and C=C for alkenes.

### Question 8
Why do esters have lower boiling points than carboxylic acids of similar molecular mass?
**Answer: B**
**Explanation**: Carboxylic acids can form hydrogen bonds and dimers between molecules, requiring more energy to separate. Esters cannot form hydrogen bonds with each other, only weaker intermolecular forces, so they have lower boiling points.
---
A) Esters have smaller molecules  
B) Esters cannot form hydrogen bonds between molecules  
C) Esters are less dense  
D) Esters are more volatile

**Answer: B**

**Explanation**: Carboxylic acids can form hydrogen bonds and dimers between molecules, requiring more energy to separate. Esters cannot form hydrogen bonds with each other, only weaker intermolecular forces, so they have lower boiling points.

### Question 13
**Statement**: Esters are formed by the reaction between an alcohol and a carboxylic acid.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: This is correct. Esterification involves a carboxylic acid reacting with an alcohol in the presence of an acid catalyst to form an ester and water.

---

### Question 14
**Statement**: The formation of an ester is an irreversible reaction.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: The formation of an ester is a **reversible reaction** that reaches equilibrium. This is why the equilibrium symbol (⇌) is used instead of a forward arrow.

---

### Question 15
**Statement**: Ethyl ethanoate is more soluble in water than ethanoic acid.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Ethyl ethanoate is **less soluble in water** than ethanoic acid. Carboxylic acids can form hydrogen bonds with water, while esters cannot.

---

### Question 16
**Statement**: The ester functional group contains a carbon-oxygen double bond.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: The ester group (-COO-) contains a carbonyl group (C=O), which is a carbon-oxygen double bond, plus a C-O single bond.

---

### Question 17
**Statement**: When naming esters, the name of the carboxylic acid comes first.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: When naming esters, the name of the **alcohol** comes first (as the alkyl part), followed by the acid name (with -oate ending).

---

### Question 18
**Statement**: Concentrated sulfuric acid acts as a catalyst in esterification.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Concentrated sulfuric acid catalyzes the esterification reaction by providing H⁺ ions that help in the reaction mechanism.

---

### Question 19
**Statement**: Esters are excellent conductors of electricity.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Esters are **poor conductors of electricity** as they do not contain free ions and are not ionic compounds.

---

### Question 20
**Statement**: Hydrolysis of an ester using sodium hydroxide produces a carboxylic acid.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Alkaline hydrolysis produces a **carboxylate salt** (e.g., sodium ethanoate), not a carboxylic acid. The salt is formed because the base neutralizes the acid produced.

---

### Question 21
**Statement**: Methyl butanoate has a characteristic apple odor.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Methyl butanoate is one of the esters responsible for the characteristic smell of apples and is used to create artificial apple flavoring.

---

### Question 22
**Statement**: Water is a reactant in the formation of esters.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: Water is a **product** in ester formation, not a reactant. The reaction is: Carboxylic acid + Alcohol → Ester + Water.

---

### Question 23
**Statement**: Esters can be used as solvents in paints and inks.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation**: Esters such as ethyl ethanoate are commonly used as solvents in paints, varnishes, inks, and nail polish removers due to their ability to dissolve many organic compounds.

---

### Question 24
**Statement**: All esters are liquids at room temperature.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction**: **Most** simple esters are liquids at room temperature, but not all. Some esters with larger molecular masses can be solids. However, for IGCSE purposes, esters are generally described as colorless liquids.
