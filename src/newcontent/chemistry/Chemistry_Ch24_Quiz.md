# IGCSE Chemistry Chapter 24: Alkanes

## Assessment Quiz
**Pearson Edexcel IGCSE Chemistry**

---

## Section A: Multiple Choice Questions (12 marks)

Select the correct answer for each question.

---

### Question 1
What is the general formula for alkanes?
A) CₙH₂ₙ
B) CₙH₂ₙ₊₂
C) CₙH₂ₙ₊₁
D) CₙH₂ₙ₋₂

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** The general formula for alkanes is CₙH₂ₙ₊₂, where n is the number of carbon atoms. For example, methane (n=1) is CH₄.

---

### Question 2
Which statement describes alkanes?
A) They are unsaturated hydrocarbons
B) They contain only single bonds
C) They have the general formula CₙH₂ₙ
D) They are very reactive

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons containing only single bonds. They are relatively unreactive under normal conditions.

---

### Question 3
What is the molecular formula of pentane?
A) C₄H₁₀
B) C₅H₁₀
C) C₅H₁₂
D) C₆H₁₄

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Pentane has 5 carbon atoms (pent- = 5). Using CₙH₂ₙ₊₂: C₅H₂(5)+₂ = C₅H₁₂.

---

### Question 4
Which alkane is the main component of natural gas?
A) Ethane
B) Methane
C) Propane
D) Butane

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Natural gas is mainly methane (CH₄), which is used for domestic heating and cooking.

---

### Question 5
Which equation shows the complete combustion of methane?
A) CH₄ + O₂ → CO + 2H₂O
B) CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O
C) 2CH₄ + 3O₂ → 2CO + 4H₂O
D) CH₄ + O₂ → C + 2H₂O

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Complete combustion requires excess oxygen and produces CO₂ and H₂O. The balanced equation is CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O.

---

### Question 6
What happens to the boiling point of alkanes as chain length increases?
A) It decreases
B) It stays the same
C) It increases
D) It decreases then increases

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** As chain length increases, there are stronger van der Waals forces between molecules, requiring more energy (higher temperature) to overcome.

---

### Question 7
Which alkane is a liquid at room temperature (25°C)?
A) Methane
B) Ethane
C) Propane
D) Pentane

**Answer: D**

**Explanation:** Pentane has a boiling point of 36°C, so it is a liquid at room temperature. Methane, ethane, and propane are gases.

---

### Question 8
What is produced during incomplete combustion of alkanes?
A) Carbon dioxide only
B) Carbon monoxide and/or carbon
C) Only water
D) Oxygen and water

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Incomplete combustion (limited oxygen) produces carbon monoxide (CO) and/or carbon (soot), plus water. CO is toxic.

---

### Question 9
Propane is used in:
A) Natural gas pipelines
B) LPG (Liquefied Petroleum Gas)
C) Petrol for cars
D) Jet fuel

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Propane (C₃H₈) is used in LPG for portable heating, camping gas, and in areas without natural gas supply.

---

### Question 10
What condition is required for alkanes to react with chlorine?
A) High temperature
B) A catalyst
C) UV light
D) High pressure

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Alkanes undergo substitution reactions with halogens only in the presence of UV light (ultraviolet radiation).

---

### Question 11
Which statement about isomers is correct?
A) They have different molecular formulae
B) They have the same structure
C) They have the same molecular formula but different structures
D) They are only found in alkenes

**Answer: C**

**Explanation:** Isomers are compounds with the same molecular formula but different structural arrangements of atoms.

---

### Question 12
Why is carbon monoxide dangerous?
A) It causes explosions
B) It binds to haemoglobin preventing oxygen transport
C) It is highly acidic
D) It causes global warming

**Answer: B**

**Explanation:** Carbon monoxide (CO) binds to haemoglobin in red blood cells about 200 times more strongly than oxygen, preventing oxygen transport around the body.

---

## Section B: True/False Questions (12 marks)

State whether each statement is TRUE or FALSE. If FALSE, provide the correct statement.

---

### Question 13
Alkanes are saturated hydrocarbons.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Alkanes contain only single bonds between carbon atoms, making them saturated. They cannot add more atoms without breaking existing bonds.

---

### Question 14
The general formula for alkanes is CₙH₂ₙ.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** The general formula for alkanes is **CₙH₂ₙ₊₂**. (CₙH₂ₙ is the formula for alkenes.)

---

### Question 15
Methane has the molecular formula C₂H₆.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Methane has the molecular formula **CH₄**. (C₂H₆ is ethane.)

---

### Question 16
Alkanes are soluble in water.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Alkanes are **insoluble in water** because they are non-polar molecules while water is a polar solvent.

---

### Question 17
Complete combustion of alkanes produces a blue flame.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Complete combustion with excess oxygen produces a clean blue flame. Incomplete combustion produces a yellow/orange sooty flame.

---

### Question 18
Butane has a boiling point above room temperature.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Butane has a boiling point of **-0.5°C**, which is **below room temperature**, so it is a gas at room temperature.

---

### Question 19
Longer chain alkanes are more flammable than shorter chain alkanes.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** **Shorter chain** alkanes are more flammable than longer chain alkanes. Shorter chains vaporise more easily and mix better with oxygen.

---

### Question 20
Isomerism in alkanes starts from butane (C₄H₁₀).

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Butane is the smallest alkane that can have structural isomers (butane and 2-methylpropane). Methane, ethane, and propane have no isomers.

---

### Question 21
The products of complete combustion of ethane are CO₂ and H₂O.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Complete combustion of any alkane produces carbon dioxide and water. For ethane: 2C₂H₆ + 7O₂ → 4CO₂ + 6H₂O.

---

### Question 22
Alkanes react readily with most chemicals at room temperature.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** Alkanes are **relatively unreactive** at room temperature because they have strong, non-polar single bonds.

---

### Question 23
Propane is used as lighter fuel.

**Answer: FALSE**

**Correction:** **Butane** is used as lighter fuel. Propane is used in LPG for portable heating and camping gas.

---

### Question 24
Alkanes can undergo substitution reactions with halogens.

**Answer: TRUE**

**Explanation:** Alkanes react with halogens (Cl₂, Br₂) in a substitution reaction when exposed to UV light. One hydrogen is replaced by a halogen atom.

---

## Section C: Short Answer Questions (12 marks)

Answer each question clearly. Show your working where appropriate.

### Question 25
Write the molecular formula and name of the alkane with 6 carbon atoms.
*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Formula: C₆H₁₄ (using CₙH₂ₙ₊₂: n=6, so 2(6)+2=14) (1 mark)
- Name: Hexane (1 mark)

---

### Question 26
Draw the displayed formula of propane (C₃H₈).
*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Three carbon atoms in a chain (1 mark)
- Correct bonding: each C has 4 bonds (to H or C), each H has 1 bond (1 mark)

```
   H   H   H
   |   |   |
 H—C — C — C—H
   |   |   |
   H   H   H
```

---

### Question 27
Write a balanced equation for the complete combustion of propane (C₃H₈).
*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Correct products: CO₂ and H₂O (1 mark)
- Balanced equation: C₃H₈ + 5O₂ → 3CO₂ + 4H₂O (1 mark)

---

### Question 28
Explain why the boiling point of alkanes increases as the chain length increases.
*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Longer chain alkanes have more electrons/greater surface area (1 mark)
- Stronger van der Waals forces / intermolecular forces between molecules (1 mark)
- More energy required to overcome these forces, so higher boiling point (1 mark)

*Do NOT accept: "stronger bonds" or "stronger covalent bonds"*

---

### Question 29
State two differences between complete and incomplete combustion of alkanes.
*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
Any two from:
| Complete | Incomplete |
|----------|------------|
| Excess/plentiful oxygen | Limited/insufficient oxygen |
| Products: CO₂ + H₂O | Products: CO and/or C + H₂O |
| Blue flame | Yellow/orange sooty flame |
| Maximum energy released | Less energy released |

---

### Question 30
Calculate the molecular formula of the alkane with 10 carbon atoms. Name this alkane.
*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Calculation: C₁₀H₂(10)+₂ = C₁₀H₂₂ (1 mark)
- Name: Decane (1 mark)

---

### Question 31
Write the balanced equation for the complete combustion of butane (C₄H₁₀).
*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Identify products: CO₂ and H₂O (1 mark)
- Balance carbon: C₄H₁₀ + ... O₂ → 4CO₂ + ... H₂O (1 mark)
- Balance oxygen and hydrogen: 2C₄H₁₀ + 13O₂ → 8CO₂ + 10H₂O (1 mark)

*Alternative: C₄H₁₀ + 6½O₂ → 4CO₂ + 5H₂O (also acceptable)*

---

### Question 32
Explain why carbon monoxide is produced when a gas heater is used in a poorly ventilated room.
*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Limited/insufficient oxygen supply (poor ventilation) (1 mark)
- Incomplete combustion occurs, producing carbon monoxide instead of carbon dioxide (1 mark)

---

### Question 33
Give one use for each of the following alkanes:
a) Methane
b) Butane
*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
a) Methane: natural gas / domestic heating / cooking / power generation (1 mark)
b) Butane: LPG / lighter fuel / camping gas / aerosol propellant (1 mark)

---

### Question 34
Describe the trend in flammability of alkanes as chain length increases. Explain your answer.
*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Flammability decreases as chain length increases (1 mark)
- Longer chains vaporise less easily / are less volatile (1 mark)
- Vapour mixes less readily with oxygen / harder to ignite (1 mark)

---

### Question 35
Write an equation for the reaction of methane with chlorine in the presence of UV light. State the type of reaction.
*(2 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Equation: CH₄ + Cl₂ → CH₃Cl + HCl (1 mark)
- Reaction type: Substitution (1 mark)

---

### Question 36
Butane (C₄H₁₀) has two structural isomers. Draw the structure of the second isomer and give its name.
*(3 marks)*

**Mark Scheme:**
- Recognise that the isomer is branched (1 mark)
- Correct structure with CH₃ branch on middle carbon (1 mark)
- Name: 2-methylpropane (1 mark)

```
    CH₃
     |
CH₃—CH—CH₃
```
