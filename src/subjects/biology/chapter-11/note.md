# Chapter 11: Plants and Food

## Main Points

### 1. Photosynthesis - The Process
- **Word equation**: Carbon dioxide + Water → Glucose + Oxygen (in presence of light and chlorophyll)
- **Symbol equation**: 6CO₂ + 6H₂O → C₆H₁₂O₆ + 6O₂
- **Definition**: Process by which plants make glucose using light energy
- **Location**: Chloroplasts (contain chlorophyll)
- **Raw materials**: Carbon dioxide (from air), Water (from soil)
- **Products**: Glucose (stored as starch), Oxygen (waste product)

### 2. Structure of Leaf (Adaptations for Photosynthesis)

| Structure | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| Upper epidermis | Transparent, allows light through |
| Palisade mesophyll | Tightly packed cells with many chloroplasts |
| Spongy mesophyll | Loosely packed cells with air spaces for gas exchange |
| Lower epidermis | Contains stomata for gas exchange |
| Stomata | Pores controlled by guard cells |
| Veins (xylem/phloem) | Brings water, removes glucose |
| Chloroplasts | Contain chlorophyll, absorb light energy |

### 3. Testing for Starch in Leaves
1. Kill leaf in boiling water (stops all reactions)
2. Place leaf in boiling ethanol (removes chlorophyll)
3. Remove when pale/colorless
4. Wash in cold water (softens leaf)
5. Spread on tile, add iodine solution
6. Blue-black color = starch present

### 4. Requirements for Photosynthesis
- **Light**: Provides energy
- **Chlorophyll**: Green pigment that absorbs light energy
- **Carbon dioxide**: From air
- **Water**: From soil
- **Suitable temperature**: Enzymes need optimum temperature

### 5. Limiting Factors of Photosynthesis
- **Light intensity**: Low light = slow rate
- **Carbon dioxide concentration**: Low CO₂ = slow rate
- **Temperature**: Low temp = slow; above optimum = enzymes denature
- **Graph interpretation**: Plateau = another factor is limiting

### 6. Uses of Glucose in Plants
- **Immediate use**: Respiration, converted to other substances
- **Storage**: Converted to starch (insoluble, compact)
- **Other conversions**: Cellulose (cell walls), Proteins (with nitrates), Lipids

### 7. Mineral Ions Needed by Plants

| Mineral | Function | Deficiency |
|---------|----------|------------|
| Nitrates | Make proteins, DNA, chlorophyll | Stunted growth, yellow leaves |
| Phosphates | Make DNA, cell membranes, ATP | Poor root growth |
| Potassium | Enzyme activation, water balance | Yellow leaves with dead spots |
| Magnesium | Makes chlorophyll | Yellow leaves between veins |

## Things to Remember
- **Photosynthesis converts light energy to chemical energy**
- **Starch test requires removal of chlorophyll first** (boiling ethanol)
- **Light, CO₂, and temperature can all limit photosynthesis**
- **Plants respire all the time** (day and night)
- **Glucose is converted to starch for storage** (starch is insoluble)
