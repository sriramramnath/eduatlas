# Chapter 4: Food and Digestion

## Main Points

### 1. Biological Molecules

**Carbohydrates**:
- Elements: Carbon, Hydrogen, Oxygen
- Made from simple sugars (glucose)
- Starch and glycogen are storage forms
- Function: energy source, structural support

**Proteins**:
- Elements: Carbon, Hydrogen, Oxygen, Nitrogen (sometimes Sulfur)
- Made from amino acids
- Function: growth, repair, enzymes, hormones

**Lipids (Fats and Oils)**:
- Elements: Carbon, Hydrogen, Oxygen
- Made from glycerol and three fatty acids
- Function: energy storage, insulation, protection, cell membranes

### 2. Food Tests
- **Starch**: Iodine solution → blue-black colour
- **Glucose**: Benedict's solution + heat → brick red precipitate
- **Protein**: Biuret test (potassium hydroxide + copper sulfate) → purple/violet
- **Lipids**: Ethanol + water → milky white emulsion

### 3. Balanced Diet
- **Components**: Carbohydrates, proteins, lipids, vitamins, minerals, water, dietary fibre
- **Energy requirements vary** with activity level, age, pregnancy, gender

**Vitamins**:
- **Vitamin A**: Sources - liver, carrots; Function - vision, healthy skin
- **Vitamin C**: Sources - citrus fruits; Function - healthy skin, prevents scurvy
- **Vitamin D**: Sources - sunlight, fish; Function - calcium absorption, prevents rickets

**Minerals**:
- **Calcium**: Sources - milk, cheese; Function - strong bones and teeth
- **Iron**: Sources - red meat, spinach; Function - haemoglobin production, prevents anaemia

### 4. Digestive System Structure
- **Mouth**: Mechanical digestion (teeth), chemical digestion (amylase in saliva)
- **Oesophagus**: Peristalsis moves food to stomach
- **Stomach**: Stores food, secretes hydrochloric acid (kills bacteria, pH 2), pepsin digests proteins
- **Small intestine**:
  - **Duodenum**: Receives bile and pancreatic juices
  - **Ileum**: Site of absorption (villi)
- **Large intestine (Colon)**: Absorbs water, forms faeces
- **Rectum**: Stores faeces
- **Anus**: Egestion of faeces
- **Pancreas**: Produces digestive enzymes
- **Liver**: Produces bile
- **Gall bladder**: Stores bile

### 5. Digestion Process
- **Peristalsis**: Wave-like muscle contractions move food along gut
- **Mechanical digestion**: Physical breakdown (chewing, churning)
- **Chemical digestion**: Enzymes break down large molecules

**Enzymes**:
- **Amylase**: Starch → maltose (mouth, pancreas)
- **Maltase**: Maltose → glucose (small intestine wall)
- **Pepsin**: Proteins → peptides (stomach, pH 2)
- **Trypsin**: Proteins → peptides (pancreas)
- **Peptidases**: Peptides → amino acids (small intestine wall)
- **Lipase**: Lipids → fatty acids + glycerol (pancreas)

**Bile**: Produced by liver, stored in gall bladder
- Neutralizes stomach acid
- Emulsifies lipids (breaks into small droplets)

### 6. Absorption in Small Intestine
- **Villi adaptations**:
  - Large surface area (finger-like projections)
  - Thin walls (one cell thick epithelium)
  - Rich blood supply (capillaries)
  - Lacteal (lymph vessel) for lipid absorption
  - Microvilli on surface cells (increase surface area further)
  - Muscle fibres (keep villi moving)

- **Products of digestion absorbed**:
  - Glucose and amino acids → blood capillaries
  - Fatty acids and glycerol → lacteal (lymph system)
  - Vitamins, minerals, water → blood

## Things to Remember
- **Egestion ≠ Excretion**: Egestion is removal of undigested food (faeces); excretion is removal of metabolic waste
- **Digestion converts large insoluble molecules into small soluble molecules**
- **Bile does NOT contain enzymes** - it emulsifies and neutralizes
- **Villi are in constant motion** - maintains concentration gradient
- **Most absorption occurs in ileum** (small intestine)
- **Hydrochloric acid kills bacteria** - important protective function
