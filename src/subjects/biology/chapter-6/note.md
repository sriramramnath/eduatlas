# Chapter 6: Coordination

## Main Points

### 1. Stimulus and Response
- **Stimulus**: Change in environment (internal or external)
- **Receptor**: Detects stimulus (sense organ)
- **Effector**: Produces response (muscle or gland)
- **Coordinated response**: Stimulus → Receptor → CNS → Effector → Response

### 2. Nervous System
- **Central Nervous System (CNS)**: Brain and spinal cord
- **Peripheral nerves**: Connect CNS to sense organs and effectors
- **Function**: Rapid communication using electrical impulses

### 3. Neurones (Nerve Cells)
- **Sensory neurone**: Carries impulses from receptor to CNS
- **Motor neurone**: Carries impulses from CNS to effector
- **Relay neurone**: Connects sensory and motor neurones (in CNS)
- **Structure**: Cell body, axon (long fibre), dendrites (branches)

### 4. Reflex Actions
- **Reflex arc**: Automatic, rapid response to danger
- **Example**: Withdrawing hand from hot object
- **Pathway**: Stimulus → Receptor → Sensory neurone → Relay neurone (in spinal cord) → Motor neurone → Effector → Response
- **Advantages**: Protects body from harm, doesn't require conscious thought

### 5. Synapses
- **Gap** between neurones
- **Neurotransmitters**: Chemicals that cross synapse
- **Function**: Allows impulses to pass from one neurone to next
- **One-way transmission**: Prevents impulses going backwards

### 6. The Eye (Receptor)
- **Structure**:
  - **Cornea**: Transparent front, refracts light
  - **Iris**: Colored part, controls pupil size
  - **Pupil**: Hole in iris, allows light in
  - **Lens**: Focuses light onto retina
  - **Retina**: Contains light-sensitive cells (rods and cones)
  - **Optic nerve**: Carries impulses to brain
  - **Ciliary muscles**: Control lens shape
  - **Suspensory ligaments**: Hold lens in place

- **Focusing**:
  - **Near objects**: Ciliary muscles contract, suspensory ligaments slacken, lens becomes fatter (more curved)
  - **Distant objects**: Ciliary muscles relax, suspensory ligaments tighten, lens becomes thinner (less curved)

- **Response to light intensity**:
  - **Bright light**: Circular muscles contract, radial muscles relax, pupil constricts (smaller)
  - **Dim light**: Circular muscles relax, radial muscles contract, pupil dilates (larger)

## Things to Remember
- **Reflex actions are automatic** - don't involve brain (spinal cord only)
- **Synapses use chemicals** (neurotransmitters), not electrical impulses
- **Impulses travel in one direction** - due to synapses
- **Eye focuses by changing lens shape**, not position
- **Pupil size controls light entering**, not focusing
- **Rods detect light intensity**, **cones detect color**
