# Chapter 2: The Variety of Living Organisms

## Main Points

### 1. Classification of Organisms
- **Five Kingdoms**: Animals, Plants, Fungi, Protoctists, Bacteria
- **Eukaryotic organisms**: Have nucleus and membrane-bound organelles (animals, plants, fungi, protoctists)
- **Prokaryotic organisms**: No nucleus, simpler structure (bacteria)

### 2. Animals
- Multicellular
- No cell walls
- No chloroplasts (cannot photosynthesize)
- Store carbohydrate as glycogen
- Feed on other organisms
- Have nervous system for coordination

### 3. Plants
- Multicellular
- Have cell walls (cellulose)
- Have chloroplasts (photosynthesize)
- Store carbohydrate as starch or sucrose
- Examples: maize, peas, flowering plants

### 4. Fungi
- Can be multicellular (mushrooms, moulds) or unicellular (yeast)
- Have cell walls (chitin, not cellulose)
- No chloroplasts
- Feed by saprotrophic nutrition (absorb nutrients from dead/decaying matter)
- Examples: mushrooms, yeast, moulds

### 5. Protoctists
- Mostly unicellular
- Some have chloroplasts (algae), some don't (protozoa)
- Examples: Amoeba, Chlorella, Plasmodium (causes malaria)

### 6. Bacteria
- Prokaryotic (no nucleus)
- Single-celled, very small (1-5µm)
- Three shapes: spheres, rods, spirals
- Have cell wall (peptidoglycan, not cellulose)
- Some have flagella for movement
- Examples: Lactobacillus bulgaricus, Pneumococcus
- Some are pathogens (disease-causing)

### 7. Viruses
- **NOT considered living organisms**
- Much smaller than bacteria (0.01-0.1µm)
- Structure: protein coat containing genetic material (DNA or RNA)
- Cannot carry out life processes independently
- Reproduce by taking over host cell's genetic machinery
- Examples: influenza, HIV, tobacco mosaic virus

## Things to Remember
- **Viruses are NOT alive** - they don't feed, respire, excrete, move, grow, or respond (except reproduce parasitically)
- **Bacteria are prokaryotic** - no nucleus, simpler than eukaryotic cells
- **Fungi cell walls are made of chitin**, not cellulose
- **Protoctists** are a "dustbin kingdom" - organisms that don't fit elsewhere
- **Eukaryotic** = "true nucleus", **Prokaryotic** = "before nucleus"
- **Pathogen**: organism that causes disease
