# Chapter 3: Breathing and Gas Exchange

## Main Points

### 1. Structure of Gas Exchange System
- **Thorax**: chest cavity containing lungs
- **Trachea**: windpipe, splits into two bronchi
- **Bronchi**: lead to each lung, divide into bronchioles
- **Bronchioles**: smaller tubes ending at alveoli
- **Alveoli**: tiny air sacs where gas exchange occurs
- **Diaphragm**: muscular sheet separating thorax from abdomen
- **Intercostal muscles**: between ribs (external and internal)
- **Pleural membranes**: surround lungs, contain pleural fluid for lubrication
- **Cartilage rings**: keep airways open (C-shaped in trachea)

### 2. Ventilation (Breathing)

**Inhalation (breathing in)**:
- External intercostal muscles contract
- Ribs move up and out
- Diaphragm contracts and flattens
- Volume of thorax increases
- Pressure in thorax decreases
- Air moves into lungs

**Exhalation (breathing out)**:
- External intercostal muscles relax
- Internal intercostal muscles contract
- Ribs move down and in
- Diaphragm relaxes and becomes dome-shaped
- Volume of thorax decreases
- Pressure in thorax increases
- Air moves out of lungs

### 3. Gas Exchange in Alveoli

**Adaptations for efficient gas exchange**:
- Large surface area (millions of alveoli)
- Thin walls (one cell thick)
- Moist surface (for gas dissolution)
- Rich blood supply (capillaries)
- Short diffusion distance

**Gases exchanged**:
- Oxygen diffuses from alveoli into blood
- Carbon dioxide diffuses from blood into alveoli

### 4. Composition of Air
- **Atmospheric air**: 78% nitrogen, 21% oxygen, 0.04% carbon dioxide
- **Exhaled air**: 79% nitrogen, 16% oxygen, 4% carbon dioxide
- Exhaled air is warmer and saturated with water vapour

### 5. Effects of Smoking
- **Tar**: damages cilia, causes smoker's cough, increases risk of lung cancer
- **Nicotine**: addictive, increases heart rate and blood pressure
- **Carbon monoxide**: combines with haemoglobin, reduces oxygen-carrying capacity
- **Diseases caused**:
  - **Bronchitis**: inflammation of airways, excess mucus
  - **Emphysema**: breakdown of alveoli walls, reduced surface area
  - **Lung cancer**: uncontrolled cell division
  - **Coronary heart disease**: blocked arteries

## Things to Remember
- **Breathing ≠ Respiration**: Breathing is mechanical movement of air; respiration is chemical reaction
- **Lungs don't actively pump air** - pressure changes cause air movement
- **Alveoli have huge surface area** - about 70m² in humans
- **Smoking kills cilia** - prevents cleaning of airways
- **Carbon monoxide** binds more tightly to haemoglobin than oxygen
- **Emphysema is irreversible** - no cure
