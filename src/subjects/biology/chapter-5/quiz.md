# Quiz: Blood and Circulation

## Question 1
**Explain how red blood cells are adapted to transport oxygen.** (3 marks)

## Answer 1
- Biconcave disc shape increases surface area for gas exchange
- No nucleus provides more space for haemoglobin
- Contains haemoglobin which binds to oxygen in lungs
- Small size allows them to fit through narrow capillaries

---

## Question 2
**Describe the differences between arteries and veins.** (4 marks)

## Answer 2
- Arteries carry blood away from heart; veins carry blood towards heart
- Arteries have thick, muscular walls; veins have thin walls
- Arteries have elastic tissue; veins have less elastic tissue
- Arteries carry high-pressure blood; veins carry low-pressure blood
- Veins have valves; arteries don't (except semi-lunar valves)

---

## Question 3
**Explain how vaccination provides immunity.** (4 marks)

## Answer 3
- Vaccine contains weakened/dead pathogen or antigens
- Lymphocytes recognize antigens and produce antibodies
- Memory cells are produced and remain in blood
- On re-infection, memory cells produce antibodies faster and in greater quantity
- Prevents pathogen from causing disease
