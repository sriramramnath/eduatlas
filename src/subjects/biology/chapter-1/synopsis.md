# Chapter 1: Life Processes - Synopsis

## Quick Summary

This chapter introduces the fundamental characteristics that define all living organisms and explores the cellular basis of life.

## Key Concepts

1. **MRS GREN** - The seven characteristics all living organisms share
2. **Cell Structure** - The building blocks of life (plant vs animal cells)
3. **Enzymes** - Biological catalysts that speed up reactions
4. **Respiration** - How cells release energy from food
5. **Movement of Materials** - Diffusion, osmosis, and active transport

## Must Know

- MRS GREN stands for: Movement, Respiration, Sensitivity, Growth, Reproduction, Excretion, Nutrition
- Plant cells have: cell wall, chloroplasts, permanent vacuole (not in animal cells)
- Enzymes are **not used up** in reactions
- Respiration ≠ Breathing
- Osmosis only involves **water** molecules
- Active transport requires **energy** (ATP)

## Exam Tips

- Always distinguish between respiration and breathing
- Remember that enzymes work best at specific pH and temperature
- Know the difference between diffusion (passive) and active transport (requires energy)
