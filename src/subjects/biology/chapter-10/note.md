# Chapter 10: Transport in Plants

## Main Points

### 1. Need for Transport
- Plants need water and mineral ions from roots
- Leaves need water for photosynthesis
- Products of photosynthesis need to be transported

### 2. Plant Transport Tissues

**Xylem**: 
- Transports water and mineral ions (upwards)
- Dead cells, no cytoplasm
- Lignified walls (strength)
- Continuous tubes from roots to leaves

**Phloem**:
- Transports sugars (sucrose) and amino acids
- Bidirectional (up and down)
- Living cells (sieve tubes and companion cells)
- Translocation

### 3. Water Uptake by Roots
- **Root hairs**: Increase surface area for absorption
- **Osmosis**: Water moves from soil (dilute) to root cells (concentrated)
- **Active transport**: Mineral ions absorbed against concentration gradient

### 4. Transpiration
- **Definition**: Loss of water vapour from leaves
- **Process**: Water evaporates from mesophyll cells → diffuses through stomata
- **Factors affecting rate**:
  - Temperature (increases)
  - Humidity (decreases)
  - Wind speed (increases)
  - Light intensity (increases - stomata open)

- **Transpiration stream**: Water pulled up xylem from roots to leaves
- **Cohesion-tension theory**: Water molecules stick together (cohesion), pulled up by evaporation (tension)

### 5. Stomata
- **Structure**: Pores in leaf epidermis, surrounded by guard cells
- **Function**: Control gas exchange and water loss
- **Opening**: Guard cells take in water, become turgid, pore opens
- **Closing**: Guard cells lose water, become flaccid, pore closes

## Things to Remember
- **Xylem transports upwards only** (water and minerals)
- **Phloem transports both directions** (sugars and amino acids)
- **Transpiration is a consequence**, not a purpose (unavoidable water loss)
- **Root hairs increase surface area** for absorption
- **Stomata control water loss** - close in dry conditions
- **Cohesion** keeps water column continuous
