# Quiz: Transport in Plants

## Question 1
**Explain how water moves from roots to leaves.** (4 marks)

## Answer 1
- Water absorbed by root hairs via osmosis
- Enters xylem vessels
- Water evaporates from mesophyll cells in leaves
- Creates tension (pulling force)
- Water molecules cohere (stick together)
- Continuous column pulled up xylem
- Transpiration stream

---

## Question 2
**Describe how stomata control water loss.** (3 marks)

## Answer 2
- Guard cells control pore size
- When turgid (full of water), stomata open
- When flaccid (lose water), stomata close
- In dry conditions, guard cells lose water, stomata close
- Reduces water loss by transpiration

---

## Question 3
**Compare xylem and phloem.** (4 marks)

## Answer 3
- **Xylem**: Dead cells, transports water/minerals upwards only, lignified walls
- **Phloem**: Living cells, transports sugars/amino acids both directions, no lignification
- **Xylem**: No cytoplasm, continuous tubes
- **Phloem**: Sieve tubes with companion cells
